freeze

verb
become suddenly cold or formal
1.0
Pronunciation: / friz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
to suddenly become cold and formal in manner, especially as a reaction to shock, fear, or discomfort

Examples

To freeze in shock
He tends to freeze during awkward social situations, hardly saying a word.
She could only freeze when her boss unexpectedly criticized her in front of everyone.
The atmosphere in the room seemed to freeze after the argument started.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relax - to become less tense, tight, or formal
  • synonym: tense up - to become tense or stiff in response to a situation
