stockpile

noun
accumulated supply kept for future use
3.6 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈstɑːk.paɪl/ (AmE), /ˈstɒk.paɪl/ (BrE)
a large supply of something that is kept or stored for future use

Examples

A stockpile of food
Farmers created a stockpile of grain to prepare for the winter months.
The army's stockpile of weapons was inspected by international observers.
The government maintains a stockpile of medical supplies for emergencies.
He added more canned food to his stockpile before the winter storm hit.
The country maintained a stockpile of emergency medical supplies in case of disaster.
The government is worried about the size of its nuclear stockpile.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reserve - a supply of something kept for future use or emergencies
