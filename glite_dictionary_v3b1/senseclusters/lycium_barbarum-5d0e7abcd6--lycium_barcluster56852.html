Lycium barbarum

Pronunciation: /ˈlaɪ.siː.əm ˈbɑːr.bə.rəm/ /; / /ˈlaɪ.sɪ.əm ˈbɑːr.bə.rəm/ (AmE), /ˈlaɪ.siː.əm ˈbɑː.bə.rəm/ /; / /ˈlaɪ.sɪ.əm ˈbɑː.bə.rəm/ (BrE)
A deciduous shrub with spiny branches and violet-purple flowers, native from southeastern Europe to China, bearing orange-red berries often known as goji berries or wolfberries.

Examples

Lycium barbarum berries
Lycium barbarum is widely cultivated for its nutritious berries, commonly known as goji berries.
The bright orange-red berries of Lycium barbarum are valued both as food and for their medicinal properties.
Traditional Chinese medicine often uses Lycium barbarum as a herbal remedy for various ailments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Lycium chinense - related species of goji berry plant; similar shrub native to Asia
  • synonym: goji berry - the edible fruit of Lycium barbarum, also called wolfberry
