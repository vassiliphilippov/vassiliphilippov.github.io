moose-wood

noun
deciduous shrub of eastern North America
Pronunciation: / ˈmuːs ˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈmuːs ˌwʊd / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub of eastern North America with tough, flexible branches, pliable bark, and small yellow flowers.

Examples

Moose-wood shrub
In early spring, the moose-wood is known for its clusters of delicate yellow flowers.
The dense thicket of moose-wood made walking through the forest slow and difficult.
Wreaths can be woven from the flexible branches of moose-wood in the fall.
