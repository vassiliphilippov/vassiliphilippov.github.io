goal-kick

noun
(rugby) attempt to score by kicking at goal
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊl kɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊl kɪk / (BrE)
(in rugby) an attempt to score points by kicking the ball between the goalposts.

Examples

Rugby goal-kick
He missed the crucial goal-kick in the final minutes of the match.
Practicing the perfect goal-kick can make a big difference in the game's outcome.
The team was awarded a goal-kick after the foul.

Related Terms

  • synonym: penalty kick - a kick awarded after certain infringements to attempt a goal
