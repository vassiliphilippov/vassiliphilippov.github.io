disrelish

verb
to make dislike
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈrɛlɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪsˈrɛlɪʃ / (BrE)
to feel or develop a dislike for something, or to cause someone to dislike something

Examples

To disrelish criticism
The harsh feedback may disrelish students from participating in future projects.
Repeated failures can disrelish even the most passionate learners from pursuing their goals.
Negative experiences sometimes disrelish people from trying new activities.
Over time, he began to disrelish the routine tasks assigned to him at work.
Many children disrelish eating vegetables, especially if they are not cooked well.
She could not help but disrelish the idea of moving to a new city again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detest - to strongly dislike or hate
  • synonym: dislike - to not like or enjoy something
  • antonym: enjoy - to take pleasure in something
  • antonym: relish - to enjoy or take pleasure in something
