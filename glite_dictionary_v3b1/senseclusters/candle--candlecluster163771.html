candle

noun
stick of wax with a wick; for light
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkændl / (BrE)
A cylinder or block of wax or tallow with a central wick that is burned to provide light.

Examples

A birthday candle
He made a wish and blew out all the candles on his birthday cake.
She lit a candle when the power went out during the storm.
The room was filled with the soft glow of a single candle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lamp - device for giving light, commonly using oil, electricity, or gas
