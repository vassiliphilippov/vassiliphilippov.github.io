Garcinia gummi-gutta

noun
tree producing gamboge resin
Pronunciation: / ɡɑrˈsɪniə ˌɡʌmiˈɡʌtə / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈsɪniə ˌɡʌmiˈɡʌtə / (BrE)
A low, spreading tree native to Indonesia that produces an orange to brown gum resin called gamboge, used as a pigment.

Examples

Garcinia gummi-gutta tree resin
Botanists study Garcinia gummi-gutta for its unique gum resin used in dyes.
The leaves of Garcinia gummi-gutta are glossy green and oval-shaped.
The resin obtained from the Garcinia gummi-gutta tree is a valued pigment in traditional art.
