fade

verb
become less visible; disappear gradually
5.0
Pronunciation: / feɪd / (AmE), / feɪd / (BrE)
to gradually disappear, become less visible, or lose strength or freshness

Examples

Colors fade away
As the sun set, the landscape began to fade into darkness.
Her voice faded as she walked away down the long corridor.
The colors on the old painting faded over decades of sunlight.
His energy seemed to fade as the illness progressed.
She slowly faded away after years of poor health.
The old traditions have started to fade in this modern society.
As the years passed, memories of his childhood slowly faded.
The sound of the music faded as we walked away from the concert.
The words on the old sign have almost completely faded from view.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appear - to come into sight or become visible
  • synonym: disappear - to cease to be visible
  • antonym: revive - to become strong or lively again after fading or declining
  • antonym: strengthen - to make or become stronger
  • synonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
  • synonym: weaken - to become or make weaker
  • synonym: wilt - to become limp or droop, usually due to lack of water or vitality
  • synonym: wither - to dry up and lose freshness, often referring to plants
