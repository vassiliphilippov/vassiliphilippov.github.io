vena appendicularis

noun
vein of the appendix region
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪ.nə əˌpɛn.dɪˈkjʊ.lɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə əˌpɛn.dɪˈkjʊ.lə.rɪs / (BrE)
a vein that accompanies the appendicular artery and drains into the ileocolic vein, supplying the appendix

Examples

Vena appendicularis vein
During an appendectomy, the vena appendicularis must be carefully ligated to prevent bleeding.
Inflammation in the region can cause thrombosis of the vena appendicularis.
The vena appendicularis joins with the ileocolic vein near the base of the appendix.
