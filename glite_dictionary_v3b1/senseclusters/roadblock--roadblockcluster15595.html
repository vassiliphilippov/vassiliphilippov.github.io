roadblock

noun
barrier set up to stop/control road traffic
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊdˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌblɒk / (BrE)
a barrier erected, especially by police, to stop or control traffic on a road, usually to inspect vehicles or catch a suspect

Examples

Police roadblock
Protesters created a makeshift roadblock to stop traffic entering the city center.
The police set up a roadblock to catch the escaping suspects.
We were delayed for an hour because of a roadblock on the highway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barricade - a barrier across a street or road, often to block movement
  • synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress
