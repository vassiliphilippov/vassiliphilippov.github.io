roadblock

noun
barrier set up to stop/control road traffic
2.8 1.39/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆroÊŠdËŒblÉ‘k / (AmE), / ËˆrÉ™ÊŠdËŒblÉ’k / (BrE)
a barrier erected, especially by police, to stop or control traffic on a road, usually to inspect vehicles or catch a suspect

Examples

Police roadblock
Protesters created a makeshift roadblock to stop traffic entering the city center.
The police set up a roadblock to catch the escaping suspects.
We were delayed for an hour because of a roadblock on the highway.

Synonyms

  • checkpoint
    3.4 6.10/m
    checkpoint is a fixed control point where officials inspect travellers and vehicles, while roadblock is a temporary police barrier on roads.
  • barricade
    4.8 3.98/m
    barricade is a strong barrier put up to defend or block an area, while roadblock is placed mainly to stop and check vehicles.
  • obstruction
    4.4 2.61/m
    obstruction is any thing that blocks a path or view and may be accidental, while roadblock is a deliberate barrier by police.
  • blockage
    4.1 2.24/m
    blockage is a stoppage that prevents flow in pipes or roads, often accidental, while roadblock is an intentional police barrier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barricade - a barrier across a street or road, often to block movement
  • synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress
