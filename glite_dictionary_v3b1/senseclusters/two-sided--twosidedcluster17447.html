two-sided

adjective
having two sides or aspects
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtuˌsaɪdəd /; / ˈtuː ˌsaɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˌtuːˈsaɪdɪd /; / ˈtuː ˌsaɪdɪd / (BrE)
having two sides or surfaces, or able to be used on both sides (such as tape, fabric, or objects)

Examples

Two-sided tape
Please use two-sided tape to stick the posters to the wall.
She handed me a two-sided brochure about the museum's exhibitions.
Their relationship was complicated and two-sided, with both affection and rivalry.
She bought a two-sided jacket that she could wear inside out for a different look.
The puzzle has a two-sided design, so you get twice the fun in one box.
This two-sided tape is sticky on both surfaces for extra convenience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reversible - able to be used or worn with either side out
  • antonym: single-sided - having only one side
