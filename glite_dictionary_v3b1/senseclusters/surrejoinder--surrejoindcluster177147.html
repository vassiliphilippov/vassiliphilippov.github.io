surrejoinder

noun
plaintiff's reply to a rejoinder (law)
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜːrɪˈdʒɔɪndər / (AmE), / ˌsʌrɪˈdʒɔɪndə / (BrE)
(Law) A pleading made by the plaintiff in reply to the defendant's rejoinder.

Examples

The surrejoinder pleading
After receiving the defendant’s rejoinder, the plaintiff’s team prepared their surrejoinder to submit to the court.
In complex litigation, the sequence of reply, rejoinder, and surrejoinder is not uncommon.
The lawyer drafted a surrejoinder to address new issues raised in the defendant's rejoinder.
