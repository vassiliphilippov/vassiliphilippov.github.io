chain mail

noun
armor of interlinked metal rings
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn meɪl / (AmE), / tʃeɪn meɪl / (BrE)
A type of flexible armor made of small interlinked metal rings, used especially in the Middle Ages.

Examples

Chain mail armor
Archaeologists discovered remnants of chain mail at the ancient battlefield site.
In medieval times, chain mail was prized for its flexibility and durability.
The knight wore a suit of chain mail beneath his surcoat for protection in battle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plate armor - protective armor made from large metal plates
