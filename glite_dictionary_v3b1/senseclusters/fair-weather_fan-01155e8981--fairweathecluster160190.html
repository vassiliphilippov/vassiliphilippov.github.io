fair-weather fan

noun
person who supports a team only when it's winning
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛr ˌwɛðər ˈfæn / (AmE), / ˈfeə ˌwɛðə ˈfan / (BrE)
A person who only supports a sports team when it is successful or winning, and not during tough times.

Examples

A fair-weather fan
Even when the team was losing, he refused to be called a fair-weather fan.
She accused her brother of being a fair-weather fan after he stopped watching the games during their losing streak.
Unlike a fair-weather fan, a true supporter stands by the team in both good and bad times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bandwagoner - a person who joins a popular activity or cause only after it is successful
