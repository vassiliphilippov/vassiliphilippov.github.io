multiscale

adjective
involving multiple scales
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltiˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmʌltiˌskeɪl / (BrE)
involving or relating to multiple levels or scales, especially in analysis or study

Examples

A multiscale model
The scientists developed a multiscale model to better understand the complex system.
A multiscale analysis can reveal patterns that are not visible at a single level.
Engineers often use multiscale techniques to study materials from the atomic to the structural level.

Related Terms

  • synonym: multilevel - involving several different levels or layers
  • antonym: single-scale - involving only one level or scale
