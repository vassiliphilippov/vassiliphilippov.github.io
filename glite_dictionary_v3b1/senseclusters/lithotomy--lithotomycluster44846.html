lithotomy

noun
surgical removal of a bodily stone
7.0 9.0
Pronunciation: /lɪ.ˈθɑː.tə.mi/ /; / /laɪ.ˈθɑː.tə.mi/ (AmE), /lɪ.ˈθɒt.ə.mi/ /; / /laɪ.ˈθɒt.ə.mi/ (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove a stone (such as a calculus) from the bladder, kidney, or urinary tract

Examples

A lithotomy operation
A lithotomy is considered when less invasive treatments for stones have failed.
Historically, lithotomy was a common procedure before the advent of modern urology techniques.
The patient underwent a lithotomy to remove a large kidney stone.
