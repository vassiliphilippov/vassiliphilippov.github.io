cerebral peduncle

noun
bundle of nerve fibers in the brainstem
9.9
Pronunciation: / səˈriːbrəl pəˈdʌŋkəl / (AmE), / səˈriːbrəl pɪˈdʌŋkl̩ / (BrE)
either of two bundles of nerve fibers on each side of the brainstem that connect the cerebrum with the lower parts of the brain and spinal cord, transmitting signals between them

Examples

The cerebral peduncle bundle
Damage to the cerebral peduncle can result in motor coordination problems and muscle weakness.
MRI scans revealed a lesion in the left cerebral peduncle of the patient.
The surgeon carefully avoided the cerebral peduncle during the operation to prevent neurological deficits.
