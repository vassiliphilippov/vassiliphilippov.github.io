Juniperus communis depressa

noun
low-growing variety of common juniper
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒuː.nɪ.pə.rəs kə.ˈmjuː.nɪs dɪ.ˈpres.ə/ (AmE), /ˈdʒuː.nɪ.pə.rəs kə.ˈmjuː.nɪs dɪ.ˈpres.ə/ (BrE)
A prostrate or low-growing variety of the common juniper native to North America, characterized by its spreading, ground-hugging habit.

Examples

Juniperus communis depressa ground cover
Many gardeners use Juniperus communis depressa as ground cover in rocky or sandy soils.
The spreading branches of Juniperus communis depressa help prevent soil erosion on slopes.
Unlike upright junipers, Juniperus communis depressa forms a dense, mat-like cover close to the ground.
