homoousion

noun
Christian doctrine of same substance
9.9 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / Àåho ämo äÀàuÀêsiÀå…ín / (AmE), / Àåh…ím…ô äÀàuÀês…™…ín / (BrE)
the doctrine in Christian theology that the Son and the Father are of the same substance or essence

Examples

The homoousion doctrine
The Council of Nicaea affirmed the concept of homoousion to define the relationship between the Father and the Son.
Debates over homoousion shaped early Christian theology and the doctrine of the Trinity.
Many church leaders argued passionately about the meaning of homoousion during the fourth century.

Synonyms

  • consubstantiality
    9.9 0.05/m

  • Athanasianism
    9.9 0.00/m
    Athanasianism is the historical doctrine taught by Athanasius about Christ's nature, while homoousion names the same theological idea more generally.
