overstay

verb
stay too long
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsteɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsteɪ / (BrE)
to stay longer than is allowed, expected, or considered polite

Examples

To overstay a visit
If we overstay our time here, we might inconvenience the hosts.
We had to ask our guests to leave because they overstayed their welcome.
You should not overstay your visa in a foreign country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outstay - to stay longer than someone else or than is desirable
