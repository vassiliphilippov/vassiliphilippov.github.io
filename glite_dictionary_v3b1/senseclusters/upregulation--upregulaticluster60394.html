upregulation

noun
increase in activity
Pronunciation: / ˌʌpˌrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌpˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an increase in the level, amount, or activity of something, especially in a biological or cellular context

Examples

The upregulation of activity
The new policy resulted in the upregulation of safety checks throughout the company.
Researchers observed an upregulation of communication between departments after the reorganization.
There was a noticeable upregulation in customer feedback following the product launch.
The upregulation of certain genes can help the body respond to stress.
Researchers observed upregulation of immune receptors after the treatment.
Regular exercise may lead to the upregulation of enzymes involved in energy production.

Related Terms

  • synonym: activation - the process of making something active or operative
  • antonym: downregulation - a decrease in the level or activity of something
  • synonym: increase - a rise in amount or level
