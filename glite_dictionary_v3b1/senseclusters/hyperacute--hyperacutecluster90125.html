hyperacute

adjective
extremely rapid onset
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərəˈkjuːt / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərəˈkjuːt / (BrE)
extremely acute or intense, especially describing a very rapid onset or reaction, often in medical contexts

Examples

Hyperacute rejection
The patient experienced a hyperacute rejection of the transplanted organ within hours of surgery.
Doctors monitored for any hyperacute symptoms that could indicate a severe allergic reaction.
A hyperacute response requires immediate medical attention to prevent serious complications.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: acute - sharp or severe in effect; intense
  • antonym: chronic - persisting for a long time or constantly recurring
