soleus muscle

noun
muscle in the calf beneath gastrocnemius
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊli.əs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlɪəs ˈmʌsl / (BrE)
a broad, flat muscle located in the calf of the leg beneath the gastrocnemius, responsible for flexing the foot downward at the ankle

Examples

Soleus muscle injury
An injury to the soleus muscle can lead to pain or weakness in the leg.
During running, the soleus muscle helps propel the body forward by flexing the ankle.
The soleus muscle plays a crucial role in maintaining posture when standing.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: gastrocnemius - the chief muscle of the calf of the leg
