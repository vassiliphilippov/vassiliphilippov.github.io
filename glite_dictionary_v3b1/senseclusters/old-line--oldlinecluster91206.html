old-line

adjective
adhering to traditional or conservative principles
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊld laɪn /; / ˈoʊld ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊld laɪn /; / ˈəʊld ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
having traditional, long-established, or conservative views, methods, or history (especially in organizations or families)

Examples

An old-line company
He was known as an old-line executive who preferred established business methods.
Many old-line families in the city have traditions dating back centuries.
The old-line members of the party resisted any calls for reform.
He is considered an old-line member of the party, favoring traditional policies.
Many old-line companies are struggling to adapt to new technology.
She comes from an old-line aristocratic family with a distinguished history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views or values; resistant to change
  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
  • antonym: progressive - favoring or implementing social reform or new, liberal ideas
  • synonym: traditional - having long-established customs or beliefs
