clack

verb
talk rapidly about trivial matters
9.9 6.7
Pronunciation: /klæk/ (AmE), /klæk/ (BrE)
(of a person) to talk rapidly and continuously about trivial or unimportant matters.

Examples

They clack about gossip
He grew tired of listening to them clack about nothing in particular.
She tends to clack on the phone with her friends late into the night.
The office workers would clack about their weekends for hours before getting any work done.
