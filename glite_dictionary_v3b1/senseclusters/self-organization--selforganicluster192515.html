self-organization

noun
process of organizing oneself or one's group
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˌɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / sɛlf ˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / sɛlf ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of organizing oneself, especially with regard to forming one's own group or labor union without external direction

Examples

Community self-organization
Community self-organization is crucial for initiating lasting social change.
Effective self-organization helped the team coordinate their efforts without a manager.
The workers' self-organization led to the establishment of a new independent union.
Many grassroots movements rely on self-organisation to address issues in their communities.
The company's shift toward self-organisation empowered employees to take more initiative and responsibility for their work.
Training programs focus on self-organisation to help individuals manage their own professional development.
