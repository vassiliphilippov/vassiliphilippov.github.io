fermenter

noun
organism causing fermentation
5.3
Pronunciation: / fərˈmɛntər / (AmE), / fəˈmɛntə / (BrE)
a microorganism or biological agent that causes fermentation by converting sugars into alcohol or acids

Examples

A wild fermenter
The baker introduced a wild fermenter to the dough to develop a tangy sourdough flavor.
Scientists studied the fermenter responsible for turning milk into yogurt.
A new type of fermenter was discovered in the soil, capable of producing unique acids.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacteria - microorganisms that can ferment sugars into acids
  • synonym: yeast - a microorganism commonly used to ferment sugars in baking and brewing
