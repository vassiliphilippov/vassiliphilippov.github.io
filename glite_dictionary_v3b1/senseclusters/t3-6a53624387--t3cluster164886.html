T3

noun
thyroid hormone, more potent than thyroxine (T4)
8.9
Pronunciation: / ti ˈθri / (AmE), / ti ˈθri / (BrE)
a thyroid hormone similar to thyroxine but with one less iodine atom, produced in smaller quantities, and having more potent yet shorter-lasting effects

Examples

Low T3 levels
Low T3 levels can indicate hypothyroidism in some patients.
Research shows that T3 is more biologically active than T4.
The doctor measured his T3 and T4 levels to assess thyroid function.
