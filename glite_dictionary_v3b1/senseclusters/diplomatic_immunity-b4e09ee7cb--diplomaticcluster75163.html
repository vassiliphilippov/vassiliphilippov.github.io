diplomatic immunity

noun
legal exemption for diplomats in foreign states
8.0 5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
a special legal status granted to foreign diplomats that exempts them from certain laws and taxes in the host country

Examples

Diplomatic immunity status
Because of diplomatic immunity, foreign officials often cannot be prosecuted under local law.
Many countries negotiate agreements to clarify the extent of diplomatic immunity for their staff abroad.
The ambassador claimed diplomatic immunity when questioned by the police.
