ship-breaker

noun
one who dismantles ships for scrap
Pronunciation: / ʃɪp ˈbreɪkər / (AmE), / ʃɪp ˈbreɪkə / (BrE)
a person or company that buys old ships and dismantles them for scrap metal and other materials

Examples

A ship-breaker company
As a successful ship-breaker, he oversees the recycling of tons of steel each year.
The government is regulating the operations of every ship-breaker along the coast.
The port employs dozens of ship-breakers to dismantle decommissioned vessels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shipbreaking - the process of dismantling old ships for scrap
