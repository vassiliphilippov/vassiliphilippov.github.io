binding

noun
capacity to attract and hold something
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
the capacity of a substance to attract and hold another substance, especially by chemical or physical means.

Examples

Chemical binding
Magnetic binding allows nanoparticles to cluster together in specific patterns.
Researchers studied the binding capacity of the new drug to the target protein.
The antibody's binding to the virus prevents infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affinity - a natural attraction or capacity to combine, often used in chemistry or biology
