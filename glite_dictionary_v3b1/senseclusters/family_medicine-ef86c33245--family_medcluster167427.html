family medicine

noun
branch of medicine focused on family healthcare
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˈmɛdɪsən / (BrE)
A branch of medicine that provides comprehensive healthcare to individuals of all ages, placing special emphasis on the family unit.

Examples

Family medicine doctor
He chose to specialize in family medicine because he wanted to care for patients of all ages.
Many rural communities rely on family medicine doctors for their healthcare needs.
The clinic offers a range of services in family medicine, from immunizations to chronic disease management.

Related Terms

  • synonym: general practice - medical practice providing primary care for all ages and conditions
  • antonym: internal medicine - branch of medicine dealing with adult diseases
