Nouvelle Vague

noun
French film movement (1950s-60s)
9.3
Pronunciation: / nuːˌvɛl ˈvɑːɡ / (AmE), / nuːˌvɛl ˈvɑːɡ / (BrE)
A French film movement of the late 1950s and 1960s characterized by experimental techniques and innovative storytelling, often associated with directors like Jean-Luc Godard and François Truffaut.

Examples

The Nouvelle Vague movement
Directors such as Jean-Luc Godard were pioneers of the Nouvelle Vague movement.
Several film students analyzed the editing style of the Nouvelle Vague in their thesis projects.
The influence of the Nouvelle Vague can still be felt in contemporary independent cinema.
