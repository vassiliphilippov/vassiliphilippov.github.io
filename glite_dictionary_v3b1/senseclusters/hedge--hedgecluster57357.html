hedge

noun
financial risk-reducing strategy
6.6 5.5
Pronunciation: /hedʒ/ (AmE), /hedʒ/ (BrE)
a method or arrangement used in finance to protect against potential losses, often by taking offsetting positions to limit risk

Examples

A financial hedge
A hedge can reduce risk but might also limit potential gains.
Many investors use a hedge as insurance against falling stock prices.
To protect its investments from currency fluctuations, the company used a hedge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: speculation - investment involving risk for potential profit; opposite strategy to hedging
