semicolon

noun
punctuation mark (;) connecting clauses or list items
2.2 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈse.mɪ.koʊ.lən/ (AmE), /ˈse.mɪ.kəʊ.lən/ (BrE)
a punctuation mark (;) used to connect independent clauses or separate items in a list, indicating a stronger pause than a comma but weaker than a period.

Examples

A semicolon in a sentence
She struggled with proper semicolon usage in her essays.
The professor explained when using a semicolon is more appropriate than a comma.
Use a semicolon to connect two closely related independent clauses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colon - punctuation mark (:) used to introduce a list, explanation, or quotation
  • degree lower: comma - punctuation mark (,) indicating a pause or separation within a sentence
  • degree higher: period - punctuation mark (.) indicating a full stop at the end of a sentence
