drain resources

verb
to use up assets or funds
Pronunciation: / dreɪn rɪˈsɔrsɪz / (AmE), / dreɪn rɪˈsɔːsɪz / (BrE)
to steadily consume or exhaust the financial, material, or other assets of a person, group, or organization

Examples

To drain resources quickly
If we keep expanding too quickly, it might drain resources needed for core operations.
Repeated maintenance issues can drain resources from even the most profitable company.
Unexpected legal fees have begun to drain resources from the charitable foundation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exhaust - use up completely; consume entirely
  • antonym: replenish - restore to the former level or condition
