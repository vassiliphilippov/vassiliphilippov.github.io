beef

verb
to complain persistently
6.1
Pronunciation: / biːf / (AmE), / biːf / (BrE)
(informal) to complain or argue, especially in a persistent or hostile way

Examples

To beef about work
He's always beefing about the quality of the food in the cafeteria.
The employees beefed to management about the lack of air conditioning during the summer.
You shouldn't beef so much if you're not willing to help fix the problem.
The two friends started beefing over who should pay for dinner.
She told them to stop beefing and try to solve the problem calmly.
There has been a lot of beefing between the rival teams this season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion or not argue
  • synonym: argue - to speak angrily with someone because you disagree
  • synonym: gripe - to complain about something persistently
  • synonym: grumble - to complain about something in a quiet, slightly angry way
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • synonym: quarrel - to have an angry disagreement
