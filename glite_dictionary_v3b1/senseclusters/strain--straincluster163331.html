strain

noun
distinctive musical melody or sequence
Pronunciation: / streɪn / (AmE), / streɪn / (BrE)
a succession of musical notes forming a melody, especially in a song or piece of music (literary or archaic)

Examples

A strain of music
A gentle strain drifted from the old piano in the living room.
He recognized the strain of an old hymn as the choir began to sing.
The strains of a familiar folk song filled the air during the festival.
A sweet strain drifted from the open window, enchanting the passersby.
As the strain of the old hymn filled the church, the congregation stood in reverent silence.
With a joyful strain, the festival choir began their evening performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melody - a sequence of musical notes that are musically satisfying
  • antonym: silence - absence of sound or music
  • synonym: song - a short musical composition with words; singing
  • synonym: tune - a melody, especially one that characterizes a certain piece of music
