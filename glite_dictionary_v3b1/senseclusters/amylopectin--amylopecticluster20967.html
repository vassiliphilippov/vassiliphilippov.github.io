amylopectin

noun
branched starch component
Pronunciation: / ˌæmɪloʊˈpɛktɪn / (AmE), / ˌæmɪləʊˈpɛktɪn / (BrE)
a branched polysaccharide that is one of the main components of plant starch

Examples

Plant amylopectin
Rice contains a high amount of amylopectin, making it sticky when cooked.
Scientists study amylopectin to understand how plants store energy.
The texture of some potatoes is influenced by their amylopectin content.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amylose - a linear polysaccharide and another main component of starch
  • synonym: starch - a carbohydrate consisting of amylopectin and amylose, found in plants
