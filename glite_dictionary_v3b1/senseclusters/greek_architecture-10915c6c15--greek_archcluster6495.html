Greek architecture

noun
architecture of ancient Greece
Pronunciation: / ɡrik ˌɑrkəˈtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ɡriːk ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
the style and principles of building design characteristic of ancient Greece, especially from the 5th to 4th centuries BC, notable for its columns, symmetry, and decorative details

Examples

The Parthenon is a renowned example of classical Greek architecture.
