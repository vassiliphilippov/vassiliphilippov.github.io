photophone

noun
sound transmission by light
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfoʊ.tə.foʊn/ (AmE), /ˈfəʊ.tə.fəʊn/ (BrE)
a device that transmits sound by using a beam of light to carry audio signals

Examples

Bell's photophone invention
Alexander Graham Bell invented the photophone in the late nineteenth century.
The photophone was an early step toward modern fiber-optic communication.
Unlike the telephone, the photophone used light instead of wires to send sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radio - a device that transmits sound using electromagnetic waves
  • synonym: telephone - a device for transmitting sound over a distance using electrical signals
