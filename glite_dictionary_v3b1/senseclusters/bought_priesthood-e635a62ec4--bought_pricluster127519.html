bought priesthood

noun
priesthood acquired by purchase
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / b…ît ÀàpristÀåh äd / (AmE), / b…îÀêt ÀàpriÀêsth äd / (BrE)
A priesthood position acquired by payment or purchase rather than by merit or qualification, often implying corruption or illegitimacy.

Examples

The bought priesthood system
Historians note that church reforms were driven in part by widespread resentment against the bought priesthood.
Many criticized the bought priesthood for its obvious conflicts of interest and moral failings.
The legitimacy of the bought priesthood was challenged by those who believed only merit should grant such authority.
