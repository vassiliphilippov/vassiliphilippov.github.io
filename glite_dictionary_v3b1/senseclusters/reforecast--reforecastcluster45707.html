reforecast

noun
revised prediction
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfɔːrkæst / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɔːkɑːst / (BrE)
a revised or updated forecast, especially one made after receiving new information

Examples

Financial reforecast
The finance team prepared a reforecast after the quarterly sales figures were released.
Due to unexpected weather changes, the meteorologist issued a reforecast for the weekend.
Management requested a reforecast to reflect the impact of the new regulations.
