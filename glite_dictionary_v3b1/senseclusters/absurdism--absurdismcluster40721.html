absurdism

noun
philosophy of meaninglessness
8.9
Pronunciation: / əbˈsɝːdɪzəm / (AmE), / əbˈsɜːdɪzəm / (BrE)
a style or movement in art, literature, or theatre that presents human life as meaningless or illogical, often using bizarre or surreal elements

Examples

Absurdism in theatre
The play is a classic example of absurdism, with characters trapped in meaningless conversations.
Many artists turned to absurdism to express their feelings after the war.
Her interest in absurdism led her to study the works of Samuel Beckett and Eugène Ionesco.

Related Terms

  • synonym: existentialism - a philosophy emphasizing individual existence and freedom in a meaningless world
  • antonym: realism - an artistic movement focused on representing reality as it is
