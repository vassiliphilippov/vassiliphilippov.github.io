joltingly

adverb
suddenly or abruptly
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒoʊltɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊltɪŋli / (BrE)
in a sudden or shocking way, either causing abrupt movement or strong surprise

Examples

A joltingly quick stop
The bus came to a joltingly quick stop at the intersection.
He was joltingly awakened by the loud thunder outside his window.
The roller coaster moved joltingly as it turned the sharp corner.
The news was delivered joltingly, leaving everyone in the room speechless.
He joltingly revealed the truth, catching his friends completely off guard.
Her joltingly honest comments made the audience uncomfortable but attentive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abruptly - in a sudden or unexpected way
  • antonym: calmly - in a peaceful or undisturbed manner
  • synonym: shockingly - in a way that causes surprise or shock
  • antonym: smoothly - in a gentle or even way, without sudden changes
