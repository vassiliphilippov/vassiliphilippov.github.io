dawn

verb
begin to be understood; become clear
3.6 3.89/m
Pronunciation: / d…în / (AmE), / d…îÀên / (BrE)
to begin to be understood, to become clear, or to start to appear (of an idea, period, or feeling)

Examples

It dawns on me
It slowly dawned on me that I had made a mistake.
The importance of the discovery did not dawn on them until much later.
The truth finally dawned on her after she read the letter.
A new era of innovation dawned with the invention of the smartphone.
As understanding of the problem dawned, the team changed their approach.
It finally dawned on her that she had the skills to succeed.

Synonyms

  • dawn on
    3.7 2.98/m
    dawn on means an idea suddenly becomes clear to someone, while dawn means an idea or feeling begins to become clear.
  • come home
    5.0 3.29/m
    come home is a realization that feels deeply true and emotional, while dawn is when an idea or feeling begins to be understood.
  • click
    3.4 3.99/m
    click is a sudden 'I get it' moment in the mind, while dawn is a process of an idea starting to become clear.
  • fall into place
    3.9 2.09/m
    fall into place describes facts or events becoming organized and clear after confusion, while dawn means something begins to be gradually understood.

  • synonym: occur - to come into one's mind; be thought of
  • synonym: realize - to become aware or conscious of something
