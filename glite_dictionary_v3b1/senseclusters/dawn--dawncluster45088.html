dawn

verb
begin to be understood; become clear
9.6
Pronunciation: / dɔn / (AmE), / dɔːn / (BrE)
to begin to be understood, to become clear, or to start to appear (of an idea, period, or feeling)

Examples

It dawns on me
It slowly dawned on me that I had made a mistake.
The importance of the discovery did not dawn on them until much later.
The truth finally dawned on her after she read the letter.
A new era of innovation dawned with the invention of the smartphone.
As understanding of the problem dawned, the team changed their approach.
It finally dawned on her that she had the skills to succeed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dusk - the time when evening begins; opposite of dawn
  • synonym: emerge - to become apparent or prominent
  • synonym: occur - to come into one's mind; be thought of
  • synonym: realize - to become aware or conscious of something
