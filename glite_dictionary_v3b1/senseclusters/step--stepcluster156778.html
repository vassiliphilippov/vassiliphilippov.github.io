step

noun
part of a stairway for the foot
1.0 35/m
Pronunciation: / stÉ›p / (AmE), / stÉ›p / (BrE)
a flat horizontal surface on a stairway where you place your foot when moving up or down

Examples

A stair step
Please watch your step when entering the building; the stairs are narrow.
She placed her foot on the first step and began to climb the stairs.
The old wooden step creaked loudly as he descended into the basement.

Synonyms

  • stairstep
    2.1 0.01/m
    stairstep is a single step in a staircase you step on, while step is the flat part where you place your foot.
  • stair
    1.0 49/m
    stair is a flat part within a set of stairs you put your foot on, while step names that flat horizontal surface.
  • tread
    3.7 2.22/m
    tread is the surface that is stepped on and often has grip, while step is the flat stair surface for the foot.
