decliner

noun
one who declines
Pronunciation: / dɪˈklaɪnər / (AmE), / dɪˈklaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who refuses or rejects an offer, invitation, or request

Examples

The decliner of the invitation sent a polite note to the host.
As the economy worsened, the company became a steady decliner in the market.
He was known as a frequent decliner of social events, preferring to stay home.
The event organizers sent reminders to all decliners in case they changed their minds.
Only a handful of decliners did not attend the annual meeting.
The survey results showed that most decliners cited scheduling conflicts as their reason.
