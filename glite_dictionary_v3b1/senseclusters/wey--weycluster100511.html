wey

noun
archaic form of whey
Pronunciation: / weɪ / (AmE), / weɪ / (BrE)
an archaic spelling of whey, the watery part of milk that remains after butter has been churned.

Examples

Milk wey for animals
After churning, the milk separated into butter and wey, which farmers used as animal feed.
In old recipes, you may find instructions to use wey instead of water.
The dairymaid poured the wey into a trough for the pigs to drink.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whey - the liquid part of milk left after making cheese or butter
