reconsent

verb
to give permission again
7.8
Pronunciation: /rə.kən.ˈsent/ /; / /riː.kən.ˈsent/ (AmE), /rə.kən.ˈsent/ /; / /riː.kən.ˈsent/ (BrE)
to give or request permission or agreement again, especially after changes have been made

Examples

To reconsent to changes
After the policy was updated, all employees had to reconsent to the new terms.
The research team asked participants to reconsent before continuing the study.
If significant changes occur, you may be required to reconsent to the agreement.
After updating the privacy policy, the company had to reconsent all its users.
Researchers must reconsent participants if the study procedures change significantly.
The hospital will reconsent patients before starting the new treatment protocol.
