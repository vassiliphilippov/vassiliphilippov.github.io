anticodon

noun
tRNA base triplet
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈkoʊdɑn / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈkəʊdɒn / (BrE)
a sequence of three bases in transfer RNA that pairs with a complementary codon in messenger RNA during protein synthesis

Examples

Anticodon sequence
Each anticodon on tRNA matches a specific codon on the mRNA strand during translation.
The accuracy of protein synthesis depends on the correct pairing of the anticodon and codon.
Scientists study how mutations in the anticodon region can affect genetic translation.
