Allium vineale

noun
a pungent species of wild onion
Pronunciation: / ˈæliəm vaɪˈniːəli / (AmE), / ˈæliəm vɪˈniːɑːli / (BrE)
A species of wild onion with a strong, pungent smell, native to Europe and western Asia, commonly known as crow garlic or wild garlic.

Examples

Allium vineale plant
Allium vineale often invades lawns and fields, making it difficult to eradicate due to its persistent bulbs.
Gardeners may mistake Allium vineale for chives because of its slender leaves and similar growth habit.
The pungent smell of Allium vineale makes it easy to identify, even when not in flower.
