crutching

noun
walking with crutches
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrʌtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of walking or moving with the help of crutches, usually due to injury or disability

Examples

Crutching after injury
After his surgery, he became skilled at crutching around the hospital corridors.
Physical therapy sessions focused on improving her crutching technique for better mobility.
The doctor observed his crutching to ensure he was using the correct posture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambulation - the act of walking or moving from place to place
  • synonym: limping - walking with difficulty, typically because of a damaged or stiff leg or foot
