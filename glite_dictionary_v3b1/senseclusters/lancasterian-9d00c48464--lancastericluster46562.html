Lancasterian

adjective
education method using monitors
Pronunciation: / ˌlæŋkəˈstɪəriən / (AmE), / ˌlæŋkəˈstɪəriən / (BrE)
relating to a method of education that uses student monitors to teach others, developed by Joseph Lancaster

Examples

Lancasterian system
The Lancasterian approach allowed one teacher to educate hundreds of students at once.
Many nineteenth-century schools adopted Lancasterian principles to improve efficiency.
A Lancasterian classroom relied on older students to help instruct their peers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monitorial - relating to a system where advanced students teach others
