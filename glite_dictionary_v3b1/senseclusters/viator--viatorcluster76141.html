viator

noun
life insurance seller
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪətər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪətɔː / (BrE)
a person who sells their life insurance policy to a third party in a viatical settlement, usually due to serious illness

Examples

Viator sells policy
The viator decided to sell his policy to help cover his medical expenses.
A viator must meet certain criteria before entering a viatical settlement agreement.
After her diagnosis, she became a viator to access funds for her treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: buyer - a person who purchases something, especially in a transaction
  • synonym: policyholder - a person who owns an insurance policy
