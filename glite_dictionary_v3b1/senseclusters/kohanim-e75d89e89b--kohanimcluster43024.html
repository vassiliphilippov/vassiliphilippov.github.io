Kohanim

noun
Jewish priestly class
Pronunciation: / koʊˈhɑːnɪm / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɑːnɪm / (BrE)
members of the Jewish priestly class traditionally believed to be descendants of Aaron

Examples

Jewish Kohanim blessing
The Kohanim are given special honors during certain synagogue services.
Traditionally, only Kohanim may perform the priestly blessing in Jewish rituals.
Many families trace their ancestry to the Kohanim through generations of tradition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Levite - a member of the tribe of Levi, distinct from the priestly Kohanim
  • synonym: kohen - a Jewish priest descended from Aaron
