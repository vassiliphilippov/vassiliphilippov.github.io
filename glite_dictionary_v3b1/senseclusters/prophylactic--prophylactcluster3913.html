prophylactic

noun
preventive medicine or remedy
8.3 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊfləˈktɪk / (AmE), / ˌprɒfləˈktɪk / (BrE)
A medicine or treatment intended to prevent or slow the development of an illness or disease.

Examples

Prophylactic medicine
Doctors often prescribe a prophylactic during flu season to at-risk individuals.
Many travelers take a malaria prophylactic before visiting tropical regions.
The hospital implemented the use of a new prophylactic to prevent infection in surgical patients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: curative - a medicine or treatment that cures disease
  • synonym: preventative - something that prevents disease or harm
