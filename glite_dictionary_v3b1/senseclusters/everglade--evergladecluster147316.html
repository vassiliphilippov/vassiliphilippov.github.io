everglade

noun
swampy wetland area
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɛvərˌɡleɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛvəˌɡleɪd / (BrE)
a large area of low, swampy land that is often covered with water and thick vegetation

Examples

Florida everglade
Many rare birds can be found living in the everglade throughout the year.
The boat tour took us deep into the everglade, where we saw alligators and turtles.
Scientists study the unique ecosystem of the everglade to learn about wetland conservation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desert - a dry, barren area of land with little rainfall
  • synonym: marsh - a wetland area with grasses and reeds
  • synonym: swamp - an area of land permanently saturated with water
