Cornish heath

noun
bushy shrub native to Cornwall, pink/white flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrnɪʃ hiθ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnɪʃ hiːθ / (BrE)
a bushy shrub with pink to white flowers, native to the moors of Cornwall and southwestern Europe, sometimes cultivated elsewhere

Examples

Cornish heath flowers
During our trip to Cornwall, we saw vast stretches of Cornish heath thriving in the wild.
Gardeners in other regions often cultivate Cornish heath for its ornamental value.
The Cornish heath is well known for its delicate pink blooms covering the moors in summer.
