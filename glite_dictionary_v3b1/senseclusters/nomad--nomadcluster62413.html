nomad

noun
person with no permanent home, migrates for livelihood
7.6 4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊˌmæd / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌmad / (BrE)
a member of a community or people who move from place to place, rather than settling permanently in one location, often in search of food, pasture, or work

Examples

A nomad group
Historically, many nomad groups relied on herding animals for their livelihood.
Modern society has made it difficult for traditional nomad lifestyles to continue.
The nomad traveled across the desert in search of water and grazing lands.

Related Terms

  • antonym: settler - a person who moves to a new area and establishes a permanent residence
  • synonym: wanderer - person who moves without a fixed destination
