obturator vein

noun
vein draining hip joints and thigh muscles
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑb.tʃəˌreɪ.tər veɪn / (AmE), / ˈɒb.tjʊˌreɪ.tə veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that collects blood from the hip joints and thigh muscles and drains into the internal iliac vein

Examples

Obturator vein drains blood
An injury near the pelvis can sometimes damage the obturator vein and cause internal bleeding.
During the surgery, the doctor identified the obturator vein to prevent accidental bleeding.
The obturator vein plays a crucial role in draining blood from the thigh muscles.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: internal iliac vein - vein into which obturator vein empties
  • synonym: obturator artery - artery accompanying the obturator vein
