spiky

adjective
having sharp points or sharp, accentuated qualities
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪki / (AmE), / ˈspaɪki / (BrE)
having sharp points or, by extension, sharply accentuated or high-pitched qualities (as in sound or style)

Examples

A spiky hairstyle
Her hair was styled in a dramatic, spiky fashion for the concert.
The musician adjusted the equalizer to reduce the spiky highs in the recording.
The review criticized the album's spiky sound profile, calling it harsh and unnatural.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pointed - having a sharp or tapering end
  • antonym: smooth - having an even surface, not rough or sharp
