nonrecognition

noun
inability to recognize
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌrɛkəɡˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌrɛkəɡˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a clinical or neurological inability to recognize people, objects, sounds, or other stimuli, often due to brain injury or disorder

Examples

Nonrecognition of faces
The patient's nonrecognition of familiar faces was distressing for his family.
After the stroke, she experienced nonrecognition of everyday objects in her home.
Doctors diagnosed him with auditory nonrecognition after he could no longer identify common sounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agnosia - loss of ability to recognize objects, people, sounds, or smells
  • antonym: recognition - the ability to identify or remember someone or something
