stand on one's own two feet

verb
be independent; support oneself
Pronunciation: / stænd ɑn wʌnz oʊn tu fit / (AmE), / stænd ɒn wʌnz əʊn tuː fiːt / (BrE)
to be independent and able to support oneself without help from others

Examples

To stand on one's own two feet
After moving out, she had to stand on her own two feet for the first time.
He realized he could no longer rely on his parents and needed to stand on his own two feet.
It's important for children to learn how to stand on their own two feet as they grow up.
