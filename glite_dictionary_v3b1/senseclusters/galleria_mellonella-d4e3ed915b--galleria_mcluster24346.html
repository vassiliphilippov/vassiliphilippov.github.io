Galleria mellonella

noun
a moth species whose larvae eat bee honeycombs
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈlɪəriə ˌmɛləˈnɛlə / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɪəriə ˌmɛləˈnɛlə / (BrE)
a species of moth whose larvae, called waxworms, live in and feed on bee honeycombs, often considered a pest in beekeeping

Examples

Galleria mellonella larvae
Beekeepers must protect their hives from infestations of Galleria mellonella.
Researchers often use Galleria mellonella as a model organism to study bacterial infections.
The larvae of Galleria mellonella can cause significant damage in beehives.
