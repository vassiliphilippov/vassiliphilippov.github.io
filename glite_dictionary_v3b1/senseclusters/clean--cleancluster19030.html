clean

adjective
not causing pollution or contamination
1.0 59/m
Pronunciation: / klin / (AmE), / kliÀên / (BrE)
free from dirt, impurities, or harmful substances; not dirty or contaminated

Examples

A clean room
Electric cars are seen as a clean alternative to gasoline vehicles.
The new plant uses clean technology to reduce air pollution.
Wind power provides clean energy without toxic emissions.
After the storm, the air felt fresh and clean.
The laboratory requires clean water for every experiment.
We need to ensure all surfaces are clean before preparing food.
Hospitals need to ensure that all surgical instruments are completely clean before use.
It is important to keep a cut clean to prevent infection.
The doctor confirmed that the wound was clean and healing well.

Synonyms

  • uncontaminated
    5.2 0.29/m
    uncontaminated indicates nothing harmful or polluted is present, while clean is a general word for not dirty or contaminated.
  • sanitary
    2.8 2.20/m
    sanitary describes places or items kept to stop germs and protect health, while clean simply means not dirty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dirty - contaminated or causing pollution
  • synonym: pure - not mixed with any other substance; untainted
  • degree higher: spotless - completely clean; without any dirt or stains
  • synonym: sterile - free from living microorganisms
  • synonym: tidy - arranged neatly and in order
