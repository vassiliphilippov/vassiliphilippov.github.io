settle

verb
accept less than desired
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl / (BrE)
to accept or agree to something, especially something less than what was wanted or hoped for

Examples

To settle for less
After lengthy negotiations, they settled for a modest pay raise.
She didn't get the job she wanted and decided to settle for a different position.
We had to settle for a smaller apartment because the rent was too high.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compromise - to accept something less than hoped for by making concessions
