adrift

adjective, adverb
floating freely; not anchored
4.6
Pronunciation: / əˈdrɪft / (AmE), / əˈdrɪft / (BrE)
floating freely on water, not anchored or attached to anything

Examples

Boat adrift at sea
After the rope snapped, the raft floated adrift on the open sea.
Several buoys had come adrift during the storm and needed to be recovered.
The old fishing boat was found adrift miles from shore.
After the storm, several boats were adrift in the harbor.
The sailors spotted a small raft adrift near the rocky shore.
We rescued a fishing net that had been adrift for days.
