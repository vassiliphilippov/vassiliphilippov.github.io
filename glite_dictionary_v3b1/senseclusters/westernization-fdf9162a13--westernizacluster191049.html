Westernization

noun
adoption of Western culture
6.8 5.7
Pronunciation: /we.stər.nɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /we.stər.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /we.stə.nɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /we.stə.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of adopting or being influenced by the customs, values, practices, cultural, social, political, or economic norms of Western countries, especially in culture, politics, or technology

Examples

Westernization of culture
Some critics argue that Westernization has led to the loss of indigenous cultures.
The rapid Westernization of the city changed its traditional way of life.
Tourism played a significant role in the Westernization of the region.
Many urban areas underwent rapid Westernisation during the twentieth century.
Some scholars argue that Westernisation affects not only lifestyle but also educational systems worldwide.
The Westernisation of local traditions has sparked debates among cultural preservationists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Easternization - the adoption of Eastern cultural or social practices
  • synonym: modernisation - the process of adapting to modern needs or habits, especially by installing modern equipment or adopting modern ideas or methods
  • synonym: modernization - the process of adapting something to modern needs or habits
