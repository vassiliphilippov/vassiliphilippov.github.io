commensuration

noun
proportional relationship
9.9
Pronunciation: /kə.men.sə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kə.men.sjʊ.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kə.men.sjʊ.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kə.men.sə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of making things equal or proportional in size, degree, or extent

Examples

Commensuration of salaries
The new policy requires the commensuration of salaries with employees' experience and qualifications.
Effective justice depends on the commensuration of punishment to the severity of the crime.
Architects must ensure the commensuration of building materials to guarantee structural stability.
