stub

noun
small remaining part or piece
Pronunciation: / stʌb / (AmE), / stʌb / (BrE)
a small piece or part that remains after the main part has been used or removed, such as the end of a pencil, cigarette, or a short piece left on a plant

Examples

Pencil stub
He could barely write with the stub of his pencil.
She flicked the stub of her cigarette into the ashtray.
There was only a stub of candle left after the power outage.
After the storm, only a stub remained of the once-tall sapling.
Be careful not to injure the stub while pruning the tree.
The gardener left a stub where the old branch had been cut.
He flicked the stub of his cigarette into the ashtray before leaving the room.
She picked up the stub and threw it away, careful not to leave any mess behind.
There were several stubs of pencils and cigarette ends on the table.

Related Terms

  • synonym: butt - the end part of a cigarette after most has been smoked
  • synonym: nub - a small lump or piece; the crux of something
  • synonym: stump - the base part of a plant or tree left after the main part is cut or broken
