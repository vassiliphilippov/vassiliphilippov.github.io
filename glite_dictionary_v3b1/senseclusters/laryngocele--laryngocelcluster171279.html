laryngocele

noun
air-filled larynx swelling
Pronunciation: / ləˈrɪŋɡəˌsil / (AmE), / ləˈrɪŋɡəˌsiːl / (BrE)
an abnormal air-filled swelling in the larynx that can cause hoarseness or breathing difficulties

Examples

A laryngocele swelling
The surgeon removed the laryngocele to help the patient breathe more easily.
A large laryngocele can sometimes be seen as a swelling in the neck.
Hoarseness and difficulty breathing led to the discovery of a laryngocele in the patient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyst - a closed sac in the body filled with fluid or air
  • synonym: tumor - an abnormal mass of tissue
