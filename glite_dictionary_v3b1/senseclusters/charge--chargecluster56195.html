charge

noun
heraldic figure on a shield
9.3 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / t É…ërd í / (AmE), / t É…ëÀêd í / (BrE)
(heraldry) a figure, symbol, or image depicted on a shield as part of a coat of arms.

Examples

A charge on a coat of arms
A fleur-de-lis is a common charge in French heraldry.
Each charge on the coat of arms symbolizes a family trait or achievement.
The knight's shield featured a bold charge of a lion rampant.

Synonyms

  • heraldic bearing
    9.9 0.00/m
    heraldic bearing is a design on a shield used in heraldry, while charge names the specific symbol placed on a coat of arms.
  • bearing
    9.9 0.10/m
    bearing is a general design shown on a shield in heraldry, while charge refers to the particular image or figure on that shield.
  • armorial bearing
    9.9 0.01/m
    armorial bearing is a formal heraldry term identifying a family's design on a shield, while charge names the individual symbol within that design.
