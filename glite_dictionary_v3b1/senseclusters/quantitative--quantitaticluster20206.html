quantitative

adjective
of verse: based on syllable length
6.0 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkwɑːn.tɪ.tə.tɪv/ /; / /ˈkwɑːn.tə.tə.tɪv/ (AmE), /ˈkwɒn.tɪ.tə.tɪv/ /; / /ˈkwɒn.tə.tə.tɪv/ (BrE)
(of verse) having a metrical system based on the relative duration of syllables, rather than stress or accent.

Examples

Quantitative verse
Ancient Greek poets employed highly quantitative structures in their compositions.
Classical Latin poetry is written in quantitative meter, relying on syllable length.
The transition to accentual verse marked the end of the quantitative tradition in English poetry.
