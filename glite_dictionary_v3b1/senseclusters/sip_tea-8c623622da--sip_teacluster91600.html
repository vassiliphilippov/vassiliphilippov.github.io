sip tea

verb
enjoy gossip or drama passively
8.8
Pronunciation: / sɪp ti / (AmE), / sɪp tiː / (BrE)
(informal) to watch, enjoy, or passively follow gossip, controversy, or dramatic events (especially online) with calm amusement and without intervening.

Examples

To sip tea at drama
Online, fans began to sip tea as the celebrity's old messages were revealed.
She prefers to sip tea and watch other people argue rather than get involved herself.
At the forum, users were quietly sipping tea while moderators tried to calm them down.

Related Terms

  • synonym: observe - to notice or pay attention to something, especially events or behaviour
  • synonym: watch - to look at or observe events or actions, often without taking part
