confession

noun
public declaration of faith
5.3 3.6
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈfe.ʃən/ (AmE), /kən.ˈfe.ʃən/ (BrE)
a public or formal declaration of religious faith or the beliefs of a church

Examples

Public confession of faith
During the ceremony, she made a solemn confession of her faith before the entire congregation.
His personal confession as a Christian was a turning point in his life.
The Nicene Creed is an ancient confession recited by many Christian churches worldwide.
Many Presbyterian churches adhere to the Westminster Confession as a summary of their doctrine.
That denomination revised its confession in the 19th century to reflect new theological views.
The Augsburg Confession is one of the most important documents of the Lutheran tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catechism - a summary of religious doctrine often in question-and-answer form
  • synonym: creed - a formal statement of religious belief
  • antonym: denial - the act of declaring something to be untrue, especially faith
