incapacitation

noun
preventing further offenses
7.8
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kə.pæs.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.kə.pə.sɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.kə.pæs.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.kə.pə.sɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or policy of preventing someone from committing further offenses by restricting their ability, especially through imprisonment.

Examples

Incapacitation by prison
The judge argued that incapacitation was necessary to protect the public from repeat offenders.
Some experts question whether incapacitation alone is effective in reducing crime rates.
Long prison sentences are often justified by the goal of incapacitation rather than rehabilitation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deterrence - the use of punishment to discourage crime
  • antonym: rehabilitation - the process of helping offenders return to society
