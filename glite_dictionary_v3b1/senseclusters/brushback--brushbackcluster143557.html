brushback

noun
intimidating warning
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbrʌʃˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈbrʌʃˌbæk / (BrE)
an action, remark, or warning intended to intimidate or discourage someone from continuing a certain behavior

Examples

Brushback comment
The senator's speech was a clear brushback to critics questioning her policies.
After the mistake, his supervisor gave him a gentle brushback to remind him of the rules.
The team's early success received a brushback from rivals who warned them not to get overconfident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encouragement - words or actions that give someone confidence or support
  • synonym: rebuke - a sharp criticism or reprimand
