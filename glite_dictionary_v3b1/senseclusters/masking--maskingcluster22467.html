masking

noun
stage scenery that hides parts of the stage
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmæskɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of hiding or concealing something from view, especially by blocking it with an object or making it less noticeable

Examples

Masking the stage
He designed the masking so the offstage actors remained out of sight.
Proper masking is essential to maintain the illusion on stage.
The crew positioned additional masking to hide the set changes from the audience.
Heavy fog provided natural masking for the ship's approach to the harbor.
Proper masking is essential when painting to prevent color from reaching unwanted areas.
The soldiers used masking techniques to hide their positions from the enemy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concealment - the action of hiding or keeping something secret
  • antonym: exposure - the act of making something visible or uncovering it
