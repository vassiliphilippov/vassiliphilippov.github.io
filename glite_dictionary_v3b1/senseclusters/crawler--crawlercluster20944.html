crawler

noun
soil-burrowing worm; angling bait
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːlə / (BrE)
a terrestrial worm that burrows into the soil and helps to aerate it, often surfacing when the ground is cool or wet; commonly used as bait by anglers

Examples

A garden crawler
A healthy garden benefits from the burrowing actions of the humble crawler.
After the rain, dozens of crawlers could be seen wriggling across the sidewalk.
Anglers often use a crawler as live bait to catch fish such as bass and trout.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earthworm - a common worm that lives and aerates soil
