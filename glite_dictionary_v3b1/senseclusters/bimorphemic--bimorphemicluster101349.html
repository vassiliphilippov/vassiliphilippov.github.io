bimorphemic

adjective
consisting of two morphemes
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪˌmɔrˈfimɪk / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪˌmɔːˈfiːmɪk / (BrE)
(Linguistics) consisting of two morphemes.

Examples

A bimorphemic word
Many verbs in English are bimorphemic, such as 'walked', which consists of 'walk' and '-ed'.
Plural nouns like 'cats' are typically bimorphemic because they combine a base and a plural suffix.
The word bimorphemic itself is bimorphemic, composed of 'bi-' and 'morphemic'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monomorphemic - consisting of only one morpheme
