rudiment

noun
basic principle
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈruːdəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈruːdɪmənt / (BrE)
a basic or elementary principle, skill, or fundamental part of something

Examples

The rudiments of math
Before starting the project, she made sure everyone understood the rudiments of the process.
Children are taught the rudiments of reading and writing in their first years at school.
He only had a rudiment of knowledge about computers when he started the job.

Related Terms

  • antonym: detail - a small part of something, not the basic or main part
  • synonym: foundation - the underlying basis or principle of something
  • synonym: fundamental - a basic or essential part or principle
