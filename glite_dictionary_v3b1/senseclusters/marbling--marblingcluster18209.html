marbling

noun
swirled pattern
4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑːr.blɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈmɑː.blɪŋ/ (BrE)
a pattern of swirls or streaks resembling the appearance of marble, often found in food, art, or materials

Examples

Cake marbling
The steak was prized for its rich marbling, which made it tender and flavorful.
She admired the marbling on the handmade paper used for the invitation cards.
The chocolate cake had beautiful marbling from the swirled vanilla and chocolate batters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uniformity - the quality of being the same throughout, without variation
  • synonym: veining - lines or streaks resembling veins, especially in stone or food
