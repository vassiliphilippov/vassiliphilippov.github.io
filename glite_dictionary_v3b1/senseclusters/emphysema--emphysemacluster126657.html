emphysema

noun
lung disease causing breathlessness
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɛmfəˈsiːmə / (AmE), / ˌɛm.fɪˈsiː.mə / (BrE)
A chronic disease of the lungs in which the air sacs are damaged, causing breathlessness and reduced respiratory function.

Examples

Lung emphysema
Lung damage from emphysema cannot be reversed, but treatment can help manage the symptoms.
Smoking for many years can greatly increase your risk of developing emphysema.
The doctor explained that his shortness of breath was caused by emphysema.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronic bronchitis - long-term inflammation of the bronchi in the lungs
