white-pine rust

noun
fungal disease of pine trees
7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈwaɪt.paɪn rʌst/ /; / /ˈhwaɪt.paɪn rʌst/ (AmE), /ˈwaɪt.paɪn rʌst/ (BrE)
A disease of pine trees, caused by rust fungi of the genus Cronartium, characterized by destructive invasion of bark and sapwood and the appearance of external blisters.

Examples

White-pine rust disease
Efforts to control white-pine rust focus on eradicating alternate host plants nearby.
Many forests have suffered from white-pine rust, leading to significant losses of mature trees.
The presence of white-pine rust was confirmed by the telltale blisters on the trunks of several trees.
