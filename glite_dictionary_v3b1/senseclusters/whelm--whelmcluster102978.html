whelm

verb
to overpower or engulf
4.9
Pronunciation: / wɛlm / (AmE), / wɛlm / (BrE)
to overpower or engulf someone, especially with strong emotions or force

Examples

To whelm with emotion
A sense of gratitude whelmed him as he listened to the kind words.
She was whelmed by a sudden wave of nostalgia as she entered her childhood home.
The city was whelmed by the floodwaters after days of heavy rain.
She was suddenly whelmed by a flood of memories from her childhood.
Sometimes, even good news can whelm us with unexpected emotion.
The city was whelmed by rising waters after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engulf - to sweep over and cover completely
  • synonym: overwhelm - to overpower completely, especially emotionally or physically
