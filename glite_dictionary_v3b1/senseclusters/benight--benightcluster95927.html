benight

verb
make dark or obscure
8.8 9.2
Pronunciation: /biː.ˈnaɪt/ /; / /bɪ.ˈnaɪt/ (AmE), /bɪ.ˈnaɪt/ (BrE)
to make dark or obscure, or to be overtaken by night or darkness (literally)

Examples

Clouds benight the land
Clouds benight the landscape, hiding the mountains from view.
Her memory of that night was benighted by fear, making it hard to recall details.
The ancient forest was benighted by the setting sun, casting long shadows everywhere.
He feared the party would be benighted on the mountain trail.
The travelers were benighted in the forest after sundown.
We hurried to make camp before the valley was benighted.
