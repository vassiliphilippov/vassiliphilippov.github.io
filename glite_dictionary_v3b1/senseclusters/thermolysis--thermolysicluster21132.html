thermolysis

noun
tissue destruction by heat
Pronunciation: / θərˈmɑləsɪs / (AmE), / θɜːˈmɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the destruction or removal of living tissue by applying heat, especially as a medical treatment

Examples

Medical thermolysis
The dermatologist recommended thermolysis to treat the patient's persistent skin growth.
During the procedure, thermolysis was used to safely remove the unwanted tissue.
Advances in thermolysis have made certain medical treatments less invasive and more effective.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cryotherapy - the destruction of tissue using extreme cold
  • synonym: electrolysis - the destruction of tissue using electric current
