A nuclear weapon that generates explosive energy by fusing hydrogen nuclei at extremely high temperatures, resulting in the formation of helium.

Examples

A hydrogen bomb explosion
International treaties seek to prevent the spread of hydrogen bombs to new countries.
Scientists conducted tests to measure the power of the hydrogen bomb in the 1950s.
The detonation of a hydrogen bomb would cause massive destruction over a wide area.

Synonyms

  • thermonuclear bomb
    6.9 0.08/m
    thermonuclear bomb is the technical term used in science and policy, while hydrogen bomb is a common name for the same device.
  • fusion bomb
    6.3 0.03/m
    fusion bomb emphasizes the fusion process in its name, while hydrogen bomb often highlights hydrogen fusion in everyday language.
  • H-bomb
    4.0 0.18/m
    H-bomb is a short, informal label used in speech and headlines, while hydrogen bomb is the full technical name for the weapon.
