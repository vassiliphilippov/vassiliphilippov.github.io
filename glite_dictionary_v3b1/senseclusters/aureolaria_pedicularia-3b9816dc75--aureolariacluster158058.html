A multi-stemmed North American annual plant bearing solitary dark golden-yellow, foxglove-like flowers; commonly known as the foxglove false foxglove.

Examples

Aureolaria pedicularia plant
Aureolaria pedicularia is often found growing in dry, open woods throughout eastern North America.
Botanists sometimes place Aureolaria pedicularia in the genus Gerardia due to its floral characteristics.
The solitary yellow flowers of Aureolaria pedicularia closely resemble those of the true foxglove.
