subscript

verb
to write below line
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
to write or print a character slightly below the normal line of text, especially in scientific or mathematical notation

Examples

To subscript a digit
In chemical formulas, numbers are often subscripted to indicate the quantity of atoms.
To show the variable's index, simply subscript the number next to the letter.
The teacher explained how to subscript text in the word processor for math assignments.
