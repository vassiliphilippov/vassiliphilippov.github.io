impurely

adverb
in an unclean way
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpjʊrli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjʊəli / (BrE)
in a way that is morally, spiritually, or physically unclean or corrupt

Examples

Impurely obtained evidence
The judge ruled that the evidence had been obtained impurely and could not be used in court.
He was accused of acting impurely for personal gain rather than for the good of the community.
Some traditions warn against living impurely to maintain spiritual health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corruptly - in a dishonest or morally wrong way
  • antonym: purely - in a way that is clean or free from moral or physical corruption
