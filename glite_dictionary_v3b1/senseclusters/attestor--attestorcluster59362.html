attestor

noun
hardware/software that issues cryptographic attestations
Pronunciation: / əˈtɛstər / (AmE), / əˈtɛstə / (BrE)
(in computing and security) a hardware module or software component that produces cryptographic attestations — signed evidence asserting a system's integrity, configuration, or identity — for a verifier.

Examples

System attestor
The device's TPM acts as an attestor, attesting to the system's integrity to the verifier.
During remote attestation, the cloud service listed several attestors that provided hardware-based proofs.
Security engineers configured the firmware so the onboard secure element would be the primary attestor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attester - an entity (hardware or software) that performs attestation by producing cryptographic proof of state or identity
  • antonym: verifier - an entity that checks and validates attestations produced by an attestor
