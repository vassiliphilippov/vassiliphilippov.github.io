interrogation

noun
formal or official questioning
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɛrəˈɡeɪʃən /; / ˌɪn.təˈreɪ.ɡə.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛrəˈɡeɪʃən /; / ˌɪn.təˈreɪ.ɡə.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of formally and systematically asking someone questions, especially to get information or during an investigation

Examples

Police interrogation
After hours of interrogation, he finally confessed to the crime.
The detective's interrogation revealed inconsistencies in the witness's story.
The suspect was taken to the police station for interrogation after the incident.
After hours of intense interrogation, the suspect finally confessed.
Lawyers are present during an interrogation to protect the rights of the person being questioned.
The police carried out an interrogation to find out more about the incident.
During the interview, she responded calmly to every interrogation.
His careful phrasing turned the simple remark into an interrogation.
The detective's interrogation focused on the suspect's whereabouts that night.
