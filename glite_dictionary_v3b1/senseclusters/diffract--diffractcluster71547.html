diffract

verb
bend or spread out (waves) by diffraction
6.8 6.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈfrækt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈfrækt/ (BrE)
(of light, sound, or other waves) to bend or spread out as they pass around an obstacle or through a narrow opening, producing a characteristic pattern.

Examples

To diffract light waves
Sound waves can diffract around obstacles, allowing you to hear someone even if you can't see them.
The X-ray beams diffracted as they passed through the crystal structure, revealing its atomic arrangement.
When light waves diffract through a small slit, a distinctive pattern of bands is created.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reflect - to bounce back (waves, such as light or sound) from a surface
  • antonym: refract - to change direction when passing through a medium
