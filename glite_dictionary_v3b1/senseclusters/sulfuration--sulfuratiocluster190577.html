sulfuration

noun
treatment with sulfur
8.8
Pronunciation: /sʌl.fər.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sʌl.fə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of treating or combining a substance with sulfur; this includes the chemical process of adding sulfur to change a material's properties, treating materials with sulphur fumes or sulphur dioxide for disinfection, preservation, or bleaching, and reacting a substance (especially a metal or ore) with sulfur to form a sulfide layer.

Examples

The sulfuration process
The sulfuration of rubber improves its durability and resistance to wear.
Industrial sulfuration is essential in the production of certain chemicals.
Proper sulfuration can enhance the properties of various materials.
The sulphuration of rubber improves its durability and resistance to wear.
Industrial sulphuration is used to produce certain chemicals and materials.
Proper sulphuration can enhance the quality of some food products.
The sulphuration of dried fruits helps to preserve their color and prevent spoilage.
After the flood, sulphuration was used to disinfect the storage rooms.
Traditional winemaking sometimes involves the sulphuration of barrels to prevent bacterial growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bleaching - the process of making something white or lighter in color
  • synonym: fumigation - the use of smoke or fumes to disinfect or purify
  • antonym: oxidation - the process of combining with oxygen
  • synonym: sulfidation - the process of converting a material into a sulfide by reaction with sulfur
  • synonym: sulfonation - the introduction of a sulfonic acid group into a molecule
  • synonym: sulfurization - the process of treating or combining with sulfur
