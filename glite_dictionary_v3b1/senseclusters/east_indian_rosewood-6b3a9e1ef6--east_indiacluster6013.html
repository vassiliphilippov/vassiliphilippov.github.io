East Indian rosewood

noun
tree with valuable dark wood, native to India
Pronunciation: / iːst ˈɪndiən ˈroʊzˌwʊd / (AmE), / iːst ˈɪndiən ˈrəʊzˌwʊd / (BrE)
a large tree native to India, prized for its dense, dark purple wood used in fine furniture and musical instruments

Examples

Dense East Indian rosewood wood
Deforestation has made East Indian rosewood increasingly difficult to source sustainably.
Many high-quality guitars feature fingerboards made of East Indian rosewood for their resonance and beauty.
The cabinet was handcrafted from rare East Indian rosewood, giving it a rich, deep hue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Dalbergia latifolia - botanical name for East Indian rosewood
  • degree lower: rosewood - wood from any of several tropical trees, often dark-colored and fragrant
