bypass

verb
avoid dealing with (something unpleasant or difficult)
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪpɑːs / (BrE)
to avoid dealing with something, especially something unpleasant or difficult, by using a different method or route

Examples

To bypass a problem
Many travelers try to bypass long security lines by enrolling in fast-track programs.
New technology may allow us to bypass the need for manual checks entirely.
She found a way to bypass the complicated application process.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confront - to face up to and deal with something unpleasant
  • synonym: evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery
