flap

noun
movable tissue attached to the body
7.7
Pronunciation: / flæp / (AmE), / flæp / (BrE)
a piece of tissue partially detached from the body, used especially in surgery for covering a wound or defect and still attached at one side to maintain its blood supply

Examples

Surgical flap
An infection developed under the flap after the dental procedure.
During reconstructive surgery, a flap of skin was used to cover the damaged area.
The surgeon carefully repositioned the flap to ensure it remained attached to blood vessels.
