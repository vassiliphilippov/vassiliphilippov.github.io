bottleneck

noun
a narrowing that restricts flow
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑtəlnɛk /; / ˈbɑːt̬lˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈbɒt(ə)lnɛk /; / ˈbɒt.əl.nɛk / (BrE)
a narrow or blocked place (in a road, pipe, process, etc.) that slows or stops movement or progress

Examples

Traffic bottleneck
A bottleneck formed in the river, slowing the water's flow considerably.
The old bridge creates a bottleneck for traffic during rush hour.
There is a bottleneck on the highway where the lanes merge into one.
Her indecision created a bottleneck in the project, causing significant delays.
The delayed delivery of parts became a bottleneck that nearly halted production.
We need to address the bottleneck in our approval process to finish on time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constriction - a narrowing or tightening that restricts flow
  • synonym: impediment - a hindrance or obstruction
  • synonym: obstacle - something that blocks or impedes progress
  • antonym: smoothness - state of being free from obstruction
