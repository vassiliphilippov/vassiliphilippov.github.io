whitewasher

noun
one who conceals faults
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌwɑʃər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌwɒʃə / (BrE)
a person or thing that covers up faults or wrongdoings, or applies whitewash to surfaces

Examples

Political whitewasher
The journalist accused the official of being a whitewasher who hid the real problems.
As a whitewasher, he tried to make the company's actions seem harmless.
The old barn was painted by a skilled whitewasher every spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cover-up - an attempt to hide the truth about something
  • antonym: exposer - a person who reveals faults or wrongdoing
