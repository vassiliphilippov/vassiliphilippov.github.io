parry

verb
ward off or block (especially an attack)
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpæri / (AmE), / ˈpæri / (BrE)
to ward off or block the movement of an opponent or an object, especially in fencing or sports

Examples

To parry an attack
He parried the blow with his forearm during the martial arts demonstration.
She managed to parry the ball away from the goal with a quick movement.
The fencer skillfully parried his opponent's thrust.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block - to stop movement or progress, especially in sports or combat
  • antonym: dodge - to avoid, especially by a sudden quick movement
