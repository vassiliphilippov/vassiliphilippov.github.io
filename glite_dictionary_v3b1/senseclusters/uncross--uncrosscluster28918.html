uncross

verb
move from crossed to uncrossed position
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to move a part of the body or two objects from a crossed position to an uncrossed position

Examples

To uncross legs
He uncrossed his legs to stretch them out under the table.
Please uncross your fingers so we can start the exercise.
She nervously uncrossed her arms as she spoke.
She was uncrossing her legs when the teacher entered the room.
He quickly uncrossed his arms as he prepared to speak.
After sitting for a while, I needed to uncross my ankles to get comfortable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cross - to place one thing over another, especially forming an X shape
