promenade

verb
walk in public, often as a procession
9.9 4.4
Pronunciation: /prɑː.mə.ˈneɪd/ /; / /ˈprɑː.mə.nəd/ (AmE), /prɒ.mə.ˈneɪd/ /; / /ˈprɒ.mə.nɑːd/ (BrE)
to walk in a public place, especially in a slow or formal way for pleasure or display

Examples

To promenade along streets
Every Sunday, families would promenade along the town's main boulevard.
The couples promenade around the square, waving to the crowd.
The graduates promenaded through the hall as part of the ceremony.
During the festival, people would promenade around the main square in their finest clothes.
Every Sunday, couples promenade through the city park, enjoying the fresh air.
We promenaded along the boardwalk, watching the sunset over the ocean.

Related Terms

  • antonym: march - walk in a military manner with a regular measured tread
  • synonym: parade - to walk or march in public as part of a procession or display
  • synonym: stroll - walk in a slow, relaxed way
