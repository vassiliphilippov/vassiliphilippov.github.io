rush

verb
move or act quickly
3.3 1.0
Pronunciation: /rʌʃ/ (AmE), /rʌʃ/ (BrE)
to move or act quickly and with urgency, or to attack or move forcefully

Examples

To rush through work
She rushed through her homework so she could go outside and play.
The nurses rushed to help the injured man after the accident.
We need to rush if we want to catch the train on time.
Fans rushed the field after the final whistle sounded.
Protesters rushed the barricades during the demonstration.
The soldiers rushed the enemy position at dawn, taking them by surprise.
He rushed into the meeting, apologizing for being late.
She had to rush to catch the last train home.
Students often rush through their homework to finish early.

  • antonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw from an enemy
