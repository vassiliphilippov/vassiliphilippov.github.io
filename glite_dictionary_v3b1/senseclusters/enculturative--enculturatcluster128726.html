enculturative

adjective
relating to cultural learning
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌltʃəˌreɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌltʃərətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the process by which individuals learn and adopt the values, customs, and behaviors of their culture

Examples

Enculturative process
Parents play a significant enculturative role by teaching their children family traditions.
Schools often serve as important enculturative environments for young people.
The enculturative influence of media can shape attitudes and beliefs in society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acculturative - relating to the process of cultural change and adaptation
