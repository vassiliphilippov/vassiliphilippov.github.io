Ponca

noun
member of a Native American people from Nebraska
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːŋkə / (AmE), / ˈpɒŋkə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people of the Siouan language family, originally from the Missouri River valley in northeastern Nebraska

Examples

A Ponca leader
A Ponca elder spoke at the ceremony, sharing the tribe's history with the community.
In the nineteenth century, many Ponca were forcibly relocated to what is now Oklahoma.
The Ponca have a rich tradition of storytelling and ceremonial dance.
