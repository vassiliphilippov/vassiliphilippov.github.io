tontine

noun
a shared fund distributed among surviving members
9.4
Pronunciation: / tɑnˈtin / (AmE), / tɒnˈtiːn / (BrE)
an arrangement or investment scheme where people contribute to a common fund, and as members die or leave, their shares are given to the survivors, with the last survivor receiving the remaining fund

Examples

A tontine fund
A tontine was established among the group of investors, each hoping to outlive the others and claim the fund.
After her uncle passed away, she received a payout from the tontine he had joined as a young man.
The novel's plot centers on a family quarrel over a very large tontine.
After joining the tontine, she received larger dividends each year as older members passed away.
The concept of a tontine has inspired both financial systems and mystery novels.
The town's elders invested in a tontine as a way to provide for their families' future.
