thermal pollution

noun
harmful heating of water bodies by waste heat
4.0 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈθɜːr.məl pə.ˈluː.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈθɜː.məl pə.ˈluː.ʃən/ /; / /ˈθɜː.məl pəl.ˈjuː.ʃən/ (BrE)
The harmful increase in temperature of natural water bodies, such as lakes and rivers, caused by the discharge of heated industrial waste or other artificial heat sources.

Examples

Thermal pollution of lakes
Efforts to reduce thermal pollution include implementing cooling towers and recycling wastewater.
Many fish species are threatened by thermal pollution due to sudden changes in water temperature.
The power plant was fined for causing thermal pollution in the nearby river.

  • synonym: water pollution - contamination of water bodies by harmful substances
