trade union

noun
workers' organization for collective bargaining
2.8 3.27/m
Pronunciation: / tre…™d ÀàjuÀêni…ôn / (AmE), / tre…™d ÀàjuÀênj…ôn / (BrE)
an organization of workers formed to protect their rights and interests and to negotiate with employers regarding conditions of employment

Examples

A trade union member
He decided to join the trade union to ensure better working conditions.
Negotiations between the company and the trade union continued late into the night.
The trade union organized a strike to demand higher wages for its members.

Synonyms

  • labor union
    2.7 3.79/m
    a labor union negotiates pay and conditions for workers in common US speech, while a trade union is the British term.
  • trades union
    2.7 0.01/m
    a trades union focuses on workers in particular trades and is a less common variant, while a trade union is standard.
  • union
    2.3 71/m
    a union can mean any organized group or workers' body, while a trade union specifically means a workers' bargaining organization.
  • brotherhood
    3.5 15/m
    a brotherhood is often an older, fraternal group of workers, while a trade union is a formal workers' organization that negotiates with employers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: labor union - an association of workers formed to protect their rights; synonym for 'trade union' (especially in American English)
