seat

noun
center of authority or government
4.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /siːt/ (AmE), /siːt/ (BrE)
a city or location that serves as the center of authority, government, or administration; or the place where something important is centered (often metaphorical)

Examples

Seat of power
Florence became the seat of the Renaissance in the 15th century.
The bishop moved the seat of his diocese to a larger city.
Washington, D.C. is the seat of the U.S. federal government.
Ancient philosophers debated the true seat of the soul.
He rose to the seat of power after years of service.
The hippocampus is believed to be the seat of long-term memory in the brain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capital - the city that is the official seat of government
  • synonym: center - the main place where something happens or is concentrated
  • synonym: source - the place, person, or thing from which something originates
