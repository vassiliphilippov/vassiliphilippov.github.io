thrombolytic

noun
drug that dissolves blood clots
Pronunciation: / ˌθrɑm.boʊˈlɪ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌθrɒm.bəˈlɪ.tɪk / (BrE)
A drug or agent that dissolves blood clots, used especially to treat blocked blood vessels.

Examples

A thrombolytic drug
Doctors often administer a thrombolytic to restore blood flow in stroke patients.
Research into new thrombolytic agents has improved survival rates after heart attacks.
The patient was given a thrombolytic to dissolve the clot causing his heart attack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticoagulant - a drug that prevents blood clotting
