fine whiskey

noun
high-quality whiskey
2.1
Pronunciation: / faɪn ˈwɪski / (AmE), / faɪn ˈwɪski / (BrE)
A high-quality whiskey, especially one recognized for its superior taste, aroma, and craftsmanship.

Examples

A glass of fine whiskey
After dinner, they enjoyed a glass of fine whiskey by the fireplace.
Collectors often pay high prices for bottles of fine whiskey from rare distilleries.
He offered his guests a taste of his most fine whiskey during the celebration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheap whiskey - low-quality whiskey, often inexpensive and harsh
  • synonym: premium whiskey - whiskey of higher-than-average quality or price
