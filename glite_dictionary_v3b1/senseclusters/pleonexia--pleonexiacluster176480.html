pleonexia

noun
excessive greed
Pronunciation: / ˌpliːəˈnɛksiə / (AmE), / ˌpliːəˈnɛksiə / (BrE)
an excessive or insatiable desire to have more than one's fair share, especially of material possessions

Examples

Excessive pleonexia
The philosopher argued that pleonexia leads to social injustice.
Many believe that unchecked pleonexia can harm both individuals and society as a whole.
The novel's main character is driven by pleonexia, never satisfied with what he already has.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avarice - extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and giving to others
  • synonym: greed - an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
