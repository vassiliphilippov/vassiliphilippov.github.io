milk sickness

noun
disease from drinking toxic cow's milk
2.8 7.8
Pronunciation: / mɪlk ˈsɪknəs /; / ˈmɪlk ˌsɪk.nəs / (AmE), / mɪlk ˈsɪknəs /; / ˈmɪlk ˌsɪk.nəs / (BrE)
a disease in humans or animals caused by drinking milk from cows that have eaten certain poisonous plants (especially white snakeroot)

Examples

Milk sickness disease
During the 19th century, many settlers in the Midwest died from milk sickness.
Medical journals contain accounts of outbreaks of milk sickness among early American pioneers.
Scientists eventually discovered that milk sickness was caused by toxins in cows that ate white snakeroot.
Early settlers in the Midwest frequently lost cattle to milk sickness.
People who drank contaminated milk sometimes developed milk sickness themselves.
Prevention of milk sickness involved removing white snakeroot from pastures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tremetol poisoning - poisoning resulting from ingestion of tremetol, the toxin in snakeroot
