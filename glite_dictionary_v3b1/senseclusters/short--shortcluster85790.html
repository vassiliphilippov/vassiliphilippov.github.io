short

adjective
having little length
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt / (AmE), / ʃɔːt / (BrE)
measuring a small distance, height, or lasting only a short time; not long, tall, or lasting

Examples

Short hair
He decided to cut his hair short for the summer.
The model wore a short dress that ended above her knees.
The rope was too short to reach the ground.
Among all her siblings, Maria was always the short one in family photos.
Despite being short, he made a big impression on everyone he met.
The short chimney barely reached above the roofline.
After a short break, the students returned to their lessons.
The documentary was quite short, lasting less than half an hour.
We only had a short meeting this morning before heading to the conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brief - lasting only a short period; concise or short in duration
  • antonym: long - measuring a great distance from end to end; opposite of short
  • antonym: tall - of great or more than average height
