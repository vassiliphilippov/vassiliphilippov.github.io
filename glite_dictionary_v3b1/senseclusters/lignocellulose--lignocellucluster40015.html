lignocellulose

noun
plant cell wall material
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪɡnoʊˈsɛljəˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˌlɪɡnəʊˈsɛljʊləʊs / (BrE)
a complex plant material made of cellulose, hemicellulose, and lignin, found in plant cell walls and used as biomass for fuels or industrial products

Examples

Plant lignocellulose
Researchers are studying how to break down lignocellulose to produce renewable energy.
The structure of lignocellulose makes it resistant to decomposition in nature.
Many agricultural wastes are rich in lignocellulose, making them valuable for biofuel production.
Scientists are developing new methods to convert lignocellulose into renewable biofuels.
Many agricultural residues, such as corn stalks, are rich in lignocellulose and can be used for energy production.
The biorefinery processes lignocellulose from wood chips to create sustainable chemicals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biomass - organic material used as fuel or for industrial production
  • synonym: cellulose - a carbohydrate forming the main part of plant cell walls
  • antonym: fossil fuel - natural fuel such as coal or gas formed from the remains of living organisms
  • synonym: lignin - a complex organic polymer in plant cell walls
