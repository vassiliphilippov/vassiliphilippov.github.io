cofferdam

noun
watertight enclosure for construction under water
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔfərˌdæm / (AmE), / ˈkɒfəˌdæm / (BrE)
a watertight enclosure built to allow construction work below the waterline, such as on bridges or piers

Examples

A cofferdam enclosure
Installing a cofferdam allowed workers to pump out water and lay the building's foundation.
The engineers built a cofferdam to create a dry workspace for repairing the bridge pier.
The old cofferdam needed repairs before construction on the underwater tunnel could continue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dam - a barrier constructed to hold back water
