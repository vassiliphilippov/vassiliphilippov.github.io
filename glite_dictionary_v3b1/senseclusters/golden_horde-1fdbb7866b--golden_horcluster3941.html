Golden Horde

noun
Mongol army in eastern Europe, 13th-14th c.
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldən hɔrd / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən hɔːd / (BrE)
the Mongol army that invaded and controlled parts of eastern Europe and Russia in the 13th and 14th centuries

Examples

Golden Horde army
Historians often debate the long-term impact of the Golden Horde on eastern European development.
The Golden Horde established control over much of what is now Russia and Ukraine.
The armies of the Golden Horde were renowned for their speed and discipline.
