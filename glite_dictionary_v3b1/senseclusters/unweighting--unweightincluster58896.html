unweighting

noun
removing weight or pressure
7.3
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈweɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈweɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of reducing or removing weight or pressure from something, especially to improve movement or control

Examples

Unweighting a ski
Skiers use unweighting techniques to make quick and smooth turns on the slopes.
Proper unweighting of the front wheel helps cyclists ride over obstacles safely.
In physical therapy, unweighting can assist patients in relearning how to walk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lightening - the act of making something lighter in weight
  • antonym: loading - the act of adding weight or pressure to something
