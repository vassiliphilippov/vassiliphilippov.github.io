trifle with

verb
treat lightly or carelessly
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪfəl wɪð / (AmE), / ˈtraɪf(ə)l wɪð / (BrE)
to treat someone or something without seriousness, respect, or proper consideration; to handle something in a careless or frivolous manner.

Examples

To trifle with feelings
During negotiations, they realized the opponent did not trifle with important details.
He warned her not to trifle with tasks that could affect the entire team.
You shouldn't trifle with other people's emotions, as it can cause real harm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: take seriously - to treat something with appropriate importance or respect
  • synonym: toy with - to consider something or someone in a casual or non-serious way
