community property

noun
property jointly owned by a married couple
Pronunciation: / kəˈmjunəti ˈprɑpɚti / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəti ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
a system in which property and income acquired during marriage are owned jointly by both spouses, rather than individually

Examples

Divide community property
After the divorce, their community property was divided equally between them.
In states that follow community property laws, both spouses have equal rights to assets acquired during marriage.
Ownership of the house was considered community property because it was purchased after marriage.
