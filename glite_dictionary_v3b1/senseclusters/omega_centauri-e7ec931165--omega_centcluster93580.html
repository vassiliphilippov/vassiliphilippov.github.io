Omega Centauri

proper noun
globular cluster in Centaurus constellation
Pronunciation: / oʊˈmeɪɡə sɛnˈtɔːri / (AmE), / əʊˈmiːɡə sɛnˈtaʊərʌɪ / (BrE)
A large, bright globular cluster in the constellation Centaurus, visible to the naked eye from the Southern Hemisphere.

Examples

Omega Centauri cluster
Astronomers have long studied Omega Centauri because of its unusually large population of stars.
Some scientists believe Omega Centauri may be the core of a dwarf galaxy captured by the Milky Way.
With binoculars, you can spot Omega Centauri shining brightly in the southern night sky.
