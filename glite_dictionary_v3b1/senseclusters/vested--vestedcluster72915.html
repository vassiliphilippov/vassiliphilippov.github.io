vested

adjective
fixed and absolute (esp. in law)
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛstɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɛstɪd / (BrE)
(of a right, interest, or power) fixed, absolute, and not subject to dispute or contingency; guaranteed by law or contract.

Examples

Vested interest
Citizens have a vested interest in the community’s safety.
Once the employee reaches five years, the pension benefits become vested.
The company failed to honor her vested rights under the employment contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contingent - subject to chance or to fulfillment of a condition
