flimflam

verb
to cheat by deception
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪmˌflæm / (AmE), / ˈflɪmˌflæm / (BrE)
to trick or cheat someone, especially by using deception or dishonest methods

Examples

To flimflam people
The salesman flimflammed the customers with promises of quick riches.
She realized too late that she had been flimflammed by a clever scam artist.
Tourists are often flimflammed by people selling fake tickets outside the stadium.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property
