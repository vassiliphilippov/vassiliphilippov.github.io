pelvic arch

noun
bony support structure for lower limbs
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛlvɪk ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɛlvɪk ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the bony structure in vertebrates that supports the lower limbs in humans or the hind limbs in other animals

Examples

The pelvic arch bone
A fractured pelvic arch can severely affect mobility in mammals.
Birds have a differently shaped pelvic arch to support flight and perching.
The human pelvic arch is adapted for upright walking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pelvis - the large bony structure near the base of the spine to which the lower limbs are attached
