tenesmus

noun
painful urge to defecate; medical term
9.9 9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈten.ɪz.məs/ /; / /ˈtɪn.ɪz.məs/ (AmE), /ˈten.ɪz.məs/ /; / /ˈtɪn.ɪz.məs/ (BrE)
A painful spasm of the anal sphincter accompanied by an urgent and continuous urge to defecate, often without significant passage of stool.

Examples

Painful tenesmus episodes
After surgery, she reported episodes of tenesmus, which concerned her physician.
Chronic tenesmus can be a symptom of underlying rectal inflammation or disease.
The patient was admitted with severe tenesmus following several days of abdominal pain.
