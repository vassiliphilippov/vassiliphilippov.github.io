suffrage

noun
the right to vote in elections

Pronunciation: / ËˆsÊŒfrÉªdÊ’ / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒfrÉªdÊ’ / (BrE)
the right to vote in political elections, especially as granted by law or constitution

Examples

Universal suffrage
He became passionate about extending suffrage to all members of society, regardless of wealth or education.
The fight for women's suffrage was a major milestone in U.S. history.
Universal suffrage ensures that every adult citizen has a say in government decisions.

Synonyms

  • right to vote
    2.3 3.82/m
    a right to vote is the legal entitlement to cast a ballot in elections, while suffrage is the formal constitutional term for that right.
  • voting right
    3.3 1.08/m
    a voting right is the official legal permission to take part in a vote, while suffrage is the formal political term for that right.
  • franchise
    6.6 1.11/m
    a franchise is a formal legal privilege often used in law, while suffrage is the common political term for the right to vote.
  • vote
    1.0 20/m
    a vote is the act or ballot a person casts to decide something, while suffrage is the legal right to take part in elections.

Related Terms
