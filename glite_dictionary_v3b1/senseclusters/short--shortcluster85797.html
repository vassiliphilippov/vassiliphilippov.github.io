short

adjective
abrupt or rude in manner
Pronunciation: / ʃɔɹt / (AmE), / ʃɔːt / (BrE)
(of a person’s manner or response) abrupt and rude, showing impatience or lack of politeness.

Examples

Short reply
Don’t be short with your colleagues, even when you’re busy.
He grew short with the waiter when his food was delayed.
Her short replies made it clear she was annoyed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brusque - blunt or abrupt in manner or speech
  • synonym: curt - rudely brief in speech or manner
  • antonym: polite - having or showing good manners or respect for others
