Cockcroft-Walton accelerator

noun
a high-voltage particle accelerator
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑk krɑft ˈwɔl tən əkˌsɛl əˌreɪ tər / (AmE), / ˈkɒk krɒft ˈwɒl tən əkˌsɛl əˌreɪ tə / (BrE)
a type of high-voltage particle accelerator in which a network of rectifiers and capacitors generates high voltages to accelerate charged particles down a tube

Examples

Cockcroft-Walton accelerator device
A Cockcroft-Walton accelerator can generate the high voltages needed for certain types of nuclear experiments.
Many early particle physics labs constructed their own Cockcroft-Walton accelerators for research purposes.
The Cockcroft-Walton accelerator was crucial in some of the first experiments to split the atom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Van de Graaff generator - an electrostatic generator used to produce high voltages, often for particle acceleration
