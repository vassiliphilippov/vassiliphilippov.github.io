chthonic

adjective
of the underworld; subterranean (esp. mythological)
Pronunciation: / ˈθɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˈθɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or dwelling in the underworld or beneath the surface of the earth, especially in reference to deities or spirits in mythology.

Examples

Ancient chthonic gods
Archaeologists uncovered evidence of ancient chthonic cults in the region's subterranean tombs.
In Greek mythology, Hades is considered a chthonic god ruling the underworld.
The ritual was believed to appease the chthonic deities that dwelled beneath the earth.
