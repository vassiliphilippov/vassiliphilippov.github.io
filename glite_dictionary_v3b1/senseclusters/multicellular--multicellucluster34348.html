multicellular

adjective
having many cells
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌl.tiˈsɛl.jɚ / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈsɛl.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
(of an organism or structure) consisting of more than one cell.

Examples

Multicellular organism
Certain fungi are multicellular, forming structures such as hyphae and fruiting bodies.
Most animals and plants are multicellular organisms, unlike bacteria, which are usually unicellular.
The human body is an example of a highly complex multicellular system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unicellular - consisting of a single cell
