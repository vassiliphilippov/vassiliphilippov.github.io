seropositive

noun
person with antibodies
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪroʊˈpɑzətɪv / (AmE), / ˌsɪərəʊˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
a person or animal whose blood test shows the presence of specific antibodies, indicating infection or exposure to a disease

Examples

A seropositive patient
After the test, the doctor explained that he was seropositive for the virus.
Many seropositive patients require regular monitoring and medical support.
Being seropositive does not always mean a person has symptoms of the disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: seronegative - a person whose blood test does not show specific antibodies
