Maniraptora

noun
group of advanced theropod dinosaurs
Pronunciation: / ˌmænəˌræpˈtɔrə /; / ˌmænɪˈræptərə / (AmE), / ˌmænɪˈræptərə /; / ˌmanɪˈraptərə / (BrE)
a group of advanced theropod dinosaurs, including oviraptorids, dromaeosaurs, and possibly modern birds, known for having bird-like features

Examples

Maniraptora dinosaur group
Museum exhibits often highlight Maniraptora as key ancestors of modern birds.
Some paleontologists believe that birds evolved from within the Maniraptora clade.
The discovery of a new Maniraptora fossil shed light on the evolution of bird-like features.
Fossil evidence suggests that Maniraptora developed features similar to those of modern birds.
Paleontologists debate whether all Maniraptora are directly ancestral to modern birds.
The discovery of feathers in some Maniraptora supports the theory that birds evolved from this group.
