slogger

noun
person who works persistently, often monotonously
Pronunciation: / ˈslɔɡər / (AmE), / ˈslɒɡə / (BrE)
a person who works steadily and persistently, often at monotonous tasks for long hours

Examples

Hardworking slogger
Although the job isn't glamorous, the company relies on each slogger to keep things running smoothly.
He's a real slogger; he never quits until the work is finished, no matter how tedious.
The project demanded long hours, but being a natural slogger, she stuck with it until the end.
