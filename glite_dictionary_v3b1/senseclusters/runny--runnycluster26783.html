runny

adjective
liquid and able to flow easily
1.0 1.57/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÊŒni / (AmE), / ËˆrÊŒni / (BrE)
(of a substance) more liquid than usual; able to flow or drip easily.

Examples

A runny nose
After catching a cold, she had a runny nose for several days.
He prefers his eggs to be slightly runny in the middle.
The soup was too runny, so I added more vegetables to thicken it.

Synonyms

  • liquidy
    4.0 0.01/m
    liquidy is thin and watery in texture, while runny means more liquid than usual and easily drips or flows.
  • watery
    1.0 0.85/m
    watery is thin and tastes or feels like it has too much water, while runny describes something that easily flows or drips.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liquid - having a consistency like water or oil; able to flow freely
  • antonym: thick - having a relatively high viscosity; not flowing easily
