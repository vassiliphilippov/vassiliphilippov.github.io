Hubble's law

noun
the principle linking galaxy speed and distance
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌbəlz lɔ / (AmE), / ˈhʌbəlz lɔː / (BrE)
the astronomical principle stating that the speed at which a galaxy moves away from an observer is directly proportional to its distance from that observer

Examples

Hubble's law of expansion
Hubble's law explains why distant galaxies appear to move away faster than nearby ones.
According to Hubble's law, the farther a galaxy is, the greater its redshift.
The discovery of Hubble's law provided strong evidence for the expansion of the universe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: redshift - displacement of spectral lines toward longer wavelengths in light from distant galaxies
