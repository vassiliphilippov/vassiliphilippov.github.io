tannia

noun
tropical tuberous plant, edible roots
Pronunciation: / ˈtæniə / (AmE), / ˈtæniə / (BrE)
A tropical American plant with edible starchy tubers, cooked and eaten similarly to yams or potatoes.

Examples

Cooked tannia tubers
Farmers cultivate tannia for its nutritious, starchy tubers.
The roots of the tannia are often used as a staple food in the Caribbean.
When cooked, tannia has a texture similar to potatoes.
