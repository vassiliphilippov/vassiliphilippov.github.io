believe my ears

verb
to be surprised by what one hears
1.0
Pronunciation: / bɪˈliv maɪ ɪrz / (AmE), / bɪˈliːv maɪ ɪəz / (BrE)
to be very surprised or unable to accept what one has just heard

Examples

To believe my ears clearly
After hearing the results, we all stood in silence, unable to believe our ears.
She was so shocked by the news that she could hardly believe her ears.
When he told me he was moving abroad, I couldn't believe my ears.
