presetting

noun
advance adjustment
Pronunciation: / priːˈsɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / priːˈsɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or result of setting something in advance, especially before it is needed or used

Examples

The presetting of alarm
The presetting of the alarm ensures it rings at the correct time every morning.
Thanks to the presetting feature, the coffee machine starts brewing before I wake up.
He checked the presetting of the thermostat to make sure the house would be warm when he arrived.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjustment - the act of making something suitable for a particular purpose
  • synonym: preprogramming - the act of programming something in advance
  • antonym: resetting - the act of setting something again or differently
