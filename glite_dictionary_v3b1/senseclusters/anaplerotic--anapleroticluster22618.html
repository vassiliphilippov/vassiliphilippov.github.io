anaplerotic

adjective
replenishing metabolic intermediates
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænəpləˈrɑtɪk / (AmE), / ˌænəpləˈrɒtɪk / (BrE)
describing a process that replenishes intermediates in a metabolic cycle

Examples

Anaplerotic process
Certain reactions in the citric acid cycle are considered anaplerotic because they restore key intermediates.
The doctor explained how anaplerotic pathways help maintain energy production in cells.
Biochemistry students must understand the role of anaplerotic processes in metabolism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cataplerotic - describing a process that removes intermediates from a metabolic cycle
