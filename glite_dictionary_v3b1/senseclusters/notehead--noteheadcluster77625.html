notehead

noun
part of musical note
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈnoʊt.hed/ (AmE), /ˈnəʊt.hed/ (BrE)
the oval or round part of a written musical note that shows its pitch and/or duration

Examples

The notehead shape
The music teacher explained how the notehead indicates the length of each note.
If the notehead is filled in, it usually means a shorter note value.

The music teacher explained how the position of the notehead on the staff determines the note's pitch.
If the notehead is filled in, it usually means the note is a quarter note or shorter.
She erased the notehead and redrew it to make the melody clearer on the sheet.
