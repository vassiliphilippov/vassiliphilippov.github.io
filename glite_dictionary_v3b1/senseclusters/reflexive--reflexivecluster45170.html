reflexive

noun
a pronoun like 'myself' or 'himself'
8.8 3.9
Pronunciation: /rə.ˈflek.sɪv/ /; / /rɪ.ˈflek.sɪv/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈflek.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈflek.sɪv/ (BrE)
a pronoun formed with '-self' or '-selves' (such as 'myself', 'yourself'), used to show that the subject of the verb is also its object

Examples

A reflexive like 'myself'
English learners often confuse when to use a reflexive instead of a regular pronoun.
In the sentence 'She blamed herself,' the pronoun is a reflexive.
The word 'themselves' is a reflexive used for plural subjects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflexive pronoun - a pronoun referring back to the subject (e.g. himself, myself)
