intercardinal

adjective
between main compass points
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkɑːrdɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkɑːˈdɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to the directions that are halfway between the main compass points, such as northeast or southwest

Examples

Intercardinal directions
The hikers used the intercardinal directions to navigate through the dense forest.
On the compass rose, intercardinal points like southeast and northwest are clearly marked.
Sailors often rely on intercardinal bearings when plotting a precise course at sea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cardinal - relating to the four main compass points: north, south, east, and west
  • degree higher: ordinal - relating to the directions between cardinal and intercardinal points, such as north-northeast
