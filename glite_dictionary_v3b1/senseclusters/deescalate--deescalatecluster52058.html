deescalate

verb
to reduce intensity
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˈɛskəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈɛskəleɪt / (BrE)
to make a conflict or situation less intense, dangerous, or serious

Examples

To deescalate a conflict
The police tried to deescalate the situation before it turned violent.
Tensions between the two countries began to deescalate after the peace talks.
She used calm words to deescalate the argument with her friend.
The police officer managed to deescalate the confrontation before it became violent.
It is important to deescalate arguments at work to maintain a positive environment.
She used humor to deescalate the tense situation at the family dinner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calm - to make less tense or agitated
  • synonym: diminish - to make or become less
  • antonym: intensify - to become or make more intense
