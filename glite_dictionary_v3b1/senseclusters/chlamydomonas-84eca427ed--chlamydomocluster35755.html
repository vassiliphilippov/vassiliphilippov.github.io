Chlamydomonas

noun
genus of green algae with two flagella
9.9
Pronunciation: / kləˌmɪdoʊˈmoʊnəs / (AmE), / kləˌmɪdəʊˈməʊnəs / (BrE)
A genus of unicellular, plant-like green algae found in fresh water and damp soil, characterized by two flagella and noted for rapid multiplication.

Examples

Chlamydomonas green algae
Chlamydomonas species are used as model organisms in studies of cell biology and motility.

Microscopes reveal the distinctive flagella that help Chlamydomonas swim through fresh water.
