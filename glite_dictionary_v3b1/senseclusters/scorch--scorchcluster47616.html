scorch

noun
discoloration caused by heat
4.8 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / sk…îrt É / (AmE), / sk…îÀêt É / (BrE)
a mark or area where something has been burned or singed, usually showing discoloration or damage

Examples

A scorch mark
A scorch marred the edge of the table after the hot pan was set down carelessly.
The iron left a visible scorch on his favorite shirt.
There was a brown scorch on the rug from the dropped match.
After the fire, a dark scorch remained on the wooden floor.
He nervously tried to scrub the scorch from his favorite shirt.
The tablecloth had a scorch where the hot pan had touched it.

Synonyms

  • burn mark
    2.4 0.28/m
    a burn mark is a visible, often flat spot of discoloration on skin or a surface, while scorch usually shows deeper heat damage.
  • singe
    5.3 0.07/m
    a singe is a slight, quick surface burn from brief heat or flame, while scorch names a larger, darker heat mark.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burn - an injury or mark caused by fire, heat, or acid
  • synonym: scorching - state of being very hot or burned
