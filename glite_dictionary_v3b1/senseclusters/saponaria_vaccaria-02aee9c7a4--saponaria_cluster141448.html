Saponaria vaccaria

noun
species of European flowering plant
Pronunciation: / sæpəˈnɛriə væˈkɛriə / (AmE), / sapəˈnɑːriə vakˈkeəriə / (BrE)
A European annual plant with pale rose-colored flowers, cultivated as a flower or sometimes considered a weed, also known as cow soapwort or cowherb.

Examples

Saponaria vaccaria flowers
Saponaria vaccaria produces clusters of pale pink flowers that brighten summer gardens.
Farmers sometimes find Saponaria vaccaria growing as a weed among their grain crops.
In North America, Saponaria vaccaria has spread beyond ornamental gardens into wild fields.
