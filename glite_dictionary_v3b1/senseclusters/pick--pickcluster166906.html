pick

verb
to steal by picking pockets
Pronunciation: / pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
to steal or rob, especially by surreptitiously taking things from someone's pockets or bags

Examples

To pick a pocket
He was caught trying to pick my pocket on the crowded bus.
Skilled thieves often pick tourists' bags in busy markets.
The detective watched the suspect as he attempted to pick a woman's purse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: steal - to take something without permission or legal right; a synonym in some contexts
