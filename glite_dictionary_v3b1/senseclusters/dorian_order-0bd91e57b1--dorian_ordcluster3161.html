Dorian order

noun
Greek architectural style with plain columns
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔriən ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈdɔːriən ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
the oldest and simplest of the classical Greek architectural orders, characterized by columns with plain capitals and typically no base

Examples

The Dorian order columns
Many ancient temples in Greece feature columns designed in the Dorian order.
The Dorian order is known for its sturdy columns and lack of decorative bases.
The Parthenon is a prime example of the Dorian order in ancient Greek architecture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Corinthian order - a classical Greek architectural order noted for its ornate capitals
  • synonym: Ionic order - a classical Greek architectural order with scrolled capitals
