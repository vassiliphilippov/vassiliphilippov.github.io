eth

noun
two-carbon prefix
9.2
Pronunciation: /eθ/ (AmE), /eθ/ (BrE)
a chemical prefix in organic chemistry indicating a two-carbon group, as in ethane or ethanol

Examples

Eth group in chemistry
The prefix eth is used in the name ethane to show it has two carbon atoms.
In organic chemistry, eth appears in compounds like ethanol and ethylene.
Students learn that eth means a molecule contains a two-carbon chain.
