melodrama

noun
sensational, exaggerated dramatic work or play
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛləˌdrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈmɛləˌdrɑːmə / (BrE)
A dramatic work, especially a play, characterized by exaggerated emotions, sensational events, and strong contrasts between good and evil, often with less emphasis on character depth.

Examples

A melodrama play
She enjoys acting in melodramas because of their intense emotions and dramatic plots.
The movie was criticized for being an overblown melodrama instead of a realistic portrayal.
The play turned into a melodrama with villains, heroes, and improbable twists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: farce - a light dramatic work marked by broadly satirical comedy and improbable plot
  • antonym: tragedy - a serious dramatic work with sorrowful or disastrous conclusion
