Devonian

noun
period in Earth's history (Paleozoic Era)
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈvoʊ.niː.ən/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈvəʊ.niː.ən/ (BrE)
the geological period from about 419 million to 359 million years ago, marked by abundant fish and the emergence of amphibians and ammonites

Examples

The Devonian period
Amphibians first appeared in the late Devonian, marking a major evolutionary milestone.
Many important rock formations were laid down during the Devonian period.
The Devonian is often known as the 'Age of Fishes' due to the diversity of aquatic life.
