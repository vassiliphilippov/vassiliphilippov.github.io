autocratic decision

noun
decision made without input from others
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtəˈkrætɪk dɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈkrætɪk dɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
A decision made by a single person who exercises complete authority and does not consult others.

Examples

An autocratic decision
An autocratic decision may speed up processes, but it can also undermine morale.
His autocratic decision completely ignored the concerns of his advisers.
The manager's autocratic decisions created resentment among her team members.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarian decision - a decision made with strict authority and limited discussion
  • antonym: democratic decision - a decision made based on group discussion or vote
