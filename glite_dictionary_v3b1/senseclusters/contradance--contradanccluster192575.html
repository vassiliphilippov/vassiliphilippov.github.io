contradance

noun
folk dance with couples in lines or sets
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.trəˌdæns /; / ˈkɑːntrɪˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trəˌdɑːns /; / ˈkɒntrɪˌdɑːns / (BrE)
a type of folk dance in which couples are arranged in sets or lines and perform a sequence of figures, often facing one another

Examples

In a classic contradance, couples switch partners as they move up and down the set.
