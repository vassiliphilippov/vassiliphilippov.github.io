offset

noun
printing technique using plate and rubber blanket
8.1 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆÉ‘fËŒsÉ›t / (AmE), / ËˆÉ’fËŒsÉ›t / (BrE)
a printing technique in which an inked image is transferred from a plate to a rubber blanket and then onto the printing surface, typically paper.

Examples

Offset printing process
Many newspapers are produced using offset to achieve high-quality images on thin paper.
The printer recommended offset for the job because it was cost-effective for large volumes.
Unlike traditional letterpress, offset allows for faster production and greater flexibility in color.

Synonyms

  • offset printing
    4.9 0.03/m
    offset printing is the full technical name for the plate-to-blanket ink transfer process, while offset is a shorter, general label for the same method.
  • offset lithography
    6.2 0.00/m
    offset lithography is the formal industry term focusing on lithographic plates and mass printing, while offset is the general name for that transfer technique.
