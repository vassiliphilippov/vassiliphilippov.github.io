burgess

noun
municipal representative
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrdʒɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdʒɪs / (BrE)
a member of a municipal assembly or a representative in a legislative body, especially in historical contexts

Examples

Burgess in parliament
In medieval England, a burgess was chosen to represent the town in parliament.
The council meeting was attended by every burgess from the surrounding boroughs.
He traced his ancestry back to a burgess who served in the 16th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: citizen - an inhabitant of a town or city
