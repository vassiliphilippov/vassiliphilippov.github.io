round

adjective
having a circular or spherical shape
1.0 19/m
Pronunciation: / raÊŠnd / (AmE), / raÊŠnd / (BrE)
having the shape or form of a circle or sphere; not angular or flat

Examples

Round table
She bought a round table to fit in their small kitchen.
The children sat in a round circle on the floor for story time.
The moon was perfectly round and bright in the night sky.

Synonyms

  • circular
    2.8 9.87/m
    circular describes a flat two-dimensional outline like a plate or coin, while round can mean flat or spherical.
  • spherical
    4.2 3.08/m
    spherical means shaped like a ball or planet, while round can also describe flat circular shapes.
  • ball-shaped
    2.3 0.06/m
    ball-shaped describes objects that look like a ball in everyday speech, while round is more general and can be flat or spherical.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circular - having the shape of a circle
  • antonym: square - having the shape of a square (with four equal straight sides and four right angles)
