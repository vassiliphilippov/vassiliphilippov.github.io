Jakob Behmen

proper noun
German mystic and theosophist (1575–1624)
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑːkəp ˈbeɪmən / (AmE), / ˈjɑːkɒb ˈbeɪmən / (BrE)
(1575–1624) A German mystic and theosophist, considered the founder of modern theosophy; influential on later religious and philosophical thought.

Examples

Jakob Behmen the mystic
Jakob Behmen is often cited as a major influence on European mysticism and philosophy.
Historians acknowledge Jakob Behmen for shaping the development of theosophical thought in the 17th century.
Many of Jakob Behmen's writings explore the nature of God and the universe.
