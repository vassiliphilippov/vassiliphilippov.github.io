squat

verb
perform a bending exercise with knees
3.4 2.27/m
Pronunciation: / skwÉ‘t / (AmE), / skwÉ’t / (BrE)
to perform a movement or exercise where you bend your knees and lower your body close to the ground, then stand up again

Examples

To squat with weights
He goes to the gym every morning to squat heavy weights.
If you want to strengthen your legs, you should learn how to squat properly.
She injured her knee because she tried to squat with too much weight.
Many athletes squat during certain strength training exercises.
She squatted beside the campfire to warm her hands.
The farmer squatted down to examine the young plants.

Synonyms

  • crouch
    1.8 8.82/m
    crouch means you lower your body and lean forward, often to hide or steady yourself, while squat is an up-and-down exercise.
  • hunker
    7.5 2.62/m
    hunker means you sit or squat close on your heels to stay put or shelter, while squat is an exercise of bending and standing.
  • scrunch up
    4.3 0.08/m
    scrunch up means you pull your body down into a tight crouch to fit small space, while squat is a deliberate exercise.
  • scrunch
    6.0 0.44/m
    scrunch means you lower your body by folding knees and sitting near your heels, while squat is an exercise rising back up.
