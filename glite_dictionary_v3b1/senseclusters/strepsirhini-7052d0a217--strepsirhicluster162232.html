Strepsirhini

noun
suborder of primates (lemurs, lorises, etc.)
Pronunciation: / strɛpˈsɪrəˌhaɪnaɪ /; / ˌstrɛp.sɪˈraɪ.ni / (AmE), / strɛpˈsɪrɪniː /; / ˌstrɛp.sɪˈraɪ.ni / (BrE)
a suborder of primates that includes lemurs, lorises, and related animals, characterized by a wet nose and other primitive features

Examples

The Strepsirhini suborder
Lemurs, found only in Madagascar, belong to the suborder Strepsirhini.
Members of the Strepsirhini are known for their keen sense of smell.
The most primitive living primates are classified as Strepsirhini.
Lemurs are members of the Strepsirhini, a suborder distinct from monkeys and apes.
Research on Strepsirhini has provided insight into early primate evolution.
The Strepsirhini include several species with unique adaptations for nocturnal life.
