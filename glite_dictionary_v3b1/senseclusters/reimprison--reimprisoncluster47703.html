reimprison

verb
to imprison again
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪmˈprɪzən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˈprɪzən / (BrE)
to put someone back in prison after they have been released or escaped

Examples

To reimprison an offender
After violating his parole, the court decided to reimprison the offender for another year.
The authorities had no choice but to reimprison the suspect after he attempted to flee the country.
If a prisoner escapes and is caught, the police will reimprison them immediately.

Related Terms

  • antonym: release - to set someone free from prison
