scintigraphy

noun
nuclear imaging test
7.5
Pronunciation: /sɪn.ˈtɪ.ɡrə.fɪ/ (AmE), /sɪn.ˈtɪ.ɡrə.fɪ/ (BrE)
a medical imaging technique that uses radioactive substances to create images of organs or tissues inside the body

Examples

Scintigraphy scan
The doctor ordered a scintigraphy to check for any abnormalities in the patient's bones.
After the scintigraphy, the radiologist explained the results to the patient.
A thyroid scintigraphy can help diagnose certain gland disorders.
The doctor recommended a scintigraphy to evaluate the patient's bone health.
A scintigraphy can help detect problems in the thyroid gland.
After the accident, the hospital performed a scintigraphy to check for internal injuries.
