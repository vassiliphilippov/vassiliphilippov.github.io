ponderosa pine

noun
tall pine tree of western North America
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːndəˈroʊsə paɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɒndəˈrəʊsə paɪn / (BrE)
A tall, widely distributed pine tree (Pinus ponderosa) of western North America with dark green needles in bunches and thick, dark brown bark.

Examples

A tall ponderosa pine
A forest of tall ponderosa pines stretched as far as the eye could see.
Many cabins in the region are built with sturdy ponderosa pine logs.
The ponderosa pine is well adapted to survive wildfires due to its thick bark.

Related Terms

  • synonym: yellow pine - a group of pine species with yellowish wood, sometimes including the ponderosa pine
