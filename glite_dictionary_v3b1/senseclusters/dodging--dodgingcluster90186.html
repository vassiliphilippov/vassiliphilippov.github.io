dodging

noun
the act of intentionally avoiding
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of deliberately avoiding something or someone, or preventing something from happening

Examples

Quick dodging in sports
During the game, her quick dodging allowed her to avoid being tagged.
His constant dodging of difficult questions frustrated the interviewer.
Tax dodging is illegal and can lead to severe penalties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avoidance - the act of keeping away from something
  • antonym: confrontation - direct engagement or facing of a situation
