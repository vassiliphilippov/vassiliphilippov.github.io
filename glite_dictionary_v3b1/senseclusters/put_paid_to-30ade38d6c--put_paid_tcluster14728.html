put paid to

verb
to put an end to; to cancel
8.8
Pronunciation: / pʊt peɪd tu / (AmE), / pʊt peɪd tʊ / (BrE)
to bring something to an end or prevent it from happening or continuing

Examples

To put paid to plans
An unexpected injury put paid to his dream of running in the marathon.
The heavy rain put paid to their hopes of playing tennis that afternoon.
The new regulations may put paid to small businesses in the area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cancel - decide that something will not take place
  • synonym: end - bring to a conclusion; finish
  • antonym: initiate - to begin or start something
