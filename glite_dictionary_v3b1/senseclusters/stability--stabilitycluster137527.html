Synonyms

  • steadiness
    3.6 0.6/m
    steadiness means being reliably steady and consistent in action, while stability means a broader state of resistance to change.
  • invariability
    7.6 0.01/m
    invariability is the complete lack of change or variation, while stability names a practical, often gradual resistance to disturbance.
  • fixity
    8.6 0.2/m
    fixity means being firmly fixed or unmovable, while stability means being steady and unlikely to change but may be less absolute.
  • constancy
    5.3 0.6/m
    constancy is faithfulness or unchanging behaviour over time, while stability focuses on general steadiness and lack of sudden change.
  • equilibrium
    6.8 5/m
    equilibrium is a balance between opposing forces or pressures, while stability is a steady state less tied to opposing forces.

Related Terms

  • antonym: instability - the state of being likely to change, not stable
  • synonym: order - a state where rules or laws are obeyed and authority is respected
  • synonym: steadiness - the quality of being stable and not changing
