conventionalized

adjective
done according to convention, not natural
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛnʃənəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəlaɪzd /; / kənˈvɛnʃ(ə)nəlaɪzd / (BrE)
created or done according to accepted conventions, often in an artificial or non-spontaneous way; stylized.

Examples

Conventionalized images
The artist's latest series features conventionalized images of birds rather than realistic portraits.
The ceremony followed a conventionalized set of rituals that have changed little over centuries.
Their approach to the play was highly conventionalized, with formal gestures and stylized speech.
Her painting style is deliberately conventionalised, echoing centuries-old artistic techniques.
The play was performed in a highly conventionalised manner, emphasizing gesture over realism.
Traditional Japanese art often uses conventionalised patterns and motifs instead of depicting nature realistically.

Related Terms

  • antonym: natural - not following conventions; spontaneous and lifelike
  • synonym: stylized - designed in a particular style, not natural or realistic
