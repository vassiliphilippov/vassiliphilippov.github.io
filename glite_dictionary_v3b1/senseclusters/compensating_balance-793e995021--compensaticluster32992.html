compensating balance

noun
minimum required balance for a loan
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmpənˌseɪtɪŋ ˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpənˌseɪtɪŋ ˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
a minimum amount of money that a bank requires a borrower to keep in an account as a condition for granting a loan or line of credit

Examples

A compensating balance requirement
Because of the compensating balance, their effective borrowing rate was higher than expected.
Many lines of credit specify a compensating balance as part of the loan agreement.
The bank required a compensating balance before approving the business loan.
