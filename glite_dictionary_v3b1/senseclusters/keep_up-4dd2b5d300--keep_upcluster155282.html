keep up

verb
maintain the required pace or level
Pronunciation: / kip ʌp / (AmE), / kiːp ʌp / (BrE)
to continue or maintain the same level, activity, or knowledge as others or as required

Examples

To keep up with work
If you want to do well in this class, you must keep up with the assignments.
It's hard to keep up with all the changes in technology nowadays.
She walked so quickly that I struggled to keep up.
Despite the difficulties, they managed to keep up the conversation for hours.
He worked hard to keep up his performance throughout the entire season.
The doctor advised her to keep up the treatment for another week.
It's important to keep up with the latest developments in your field.
She tries to keep up on local news by reading online articles every morning.
They use newsletters to keep up with industry trends and innovations.
