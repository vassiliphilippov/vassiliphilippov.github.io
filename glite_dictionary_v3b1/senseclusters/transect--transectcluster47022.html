transect

noun
sampling line in research
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈtræn.sekt/ /; / /ˈtrɑːn.sekt/ (AmE), /ˈtræn.sekt/ /; / /ˈtrɑːn.sekt/ (BrE)
a straight line or path along which scientific measurements or observations are made, especially in ecological studies

Examples

A survey transect
The ecologists set up a transect to record the types of plants growing in the meadow.
Each student walked along the transect, noting every animal they observed within one meter.
A transect was used to measure changes in soil composition across the hillside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: survey line - a line used as a reference for measurement or observation in field studies
