couldn't help but drool

verb
unable to resist showing desire (esp. for food)
6.2 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkʊd.ənt help bʌt druːl/ (AmE), /ˈkʊd.ənt help bʌt druːl/ (BrE)
used to say that someone could not resist displaying strong desire, especially for food, often by salivating or showing longing.

Examples

He couldn't help but drool over dessert
He couldn't help but drool as he watched the chef prepare a decadent chocolate dessert on television.
She couldn't help but drool over the display of fresh pastries in the bakery window.
When the waiter brought out the sizzling steak, I couldn't help but drool at the aroma.

Related Terms

  • synonym: couldn't help but - used to say that someone is unable to stop themselves from doing something
  • synonym: salivate - to produce saliva in the mouth, often because of seeing or smelling food
