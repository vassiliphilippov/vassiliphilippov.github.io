al Qaeda

proper noun
Islamist militant terrorist organization
Pronunciation: / æl ˈkaɪdə / (AmE), / æl ˈkaɪdə / (BrE)
a militant Islamist organization founded by Osama bin Laden in 1988, known for carrying out terrorist attacks worldwide, including the September 11 attacks in the United States, and for supporting radical Islamic groups.

Examples

al Qaeda terrorist group
Several governments have taken measures to prevent al Qaeda from establishing cells within their borders.
The United States launched military operations in response to the al Qaeda attacks in 2001.
The documentary provided a historical overview of how al Qaeda was formed and evolved over the years.
