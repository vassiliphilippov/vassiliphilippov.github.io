non-official

noun
person without authority
5.9
Pronunciation: / nɑn əˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / nɒn əˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
a person who is present or acts in a situation without formal authority or official appointment

Examples

A non-official observer
The committee allowed a non-official to observe the proceedings but not to participate in the vote.
As a non-official, she could attend the conference but could not make any decisions.
Only one non-official was present at the closed-door meeting to take notes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: official - a person with formal authority or appointment
