typecasting

noun
repetitive casting practice
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈtaɪp.kæs.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈtaɪp.kɑː.stɪŋ/ /; / /ˈtaɪp.kæs.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
the practice of repeatedly casting an actor in similar roles based on their appearance or previous roles

Examples

Actor typecasting
After years of typecasting, she finally landed a role that showed her range as an actress.
Directors sometimes avoid typecasting to keep their films fresh and unpredictable.
He worried that early success in comedies would lead to typecasting for the rest of his career.

  • antonym: versatility - the ability to play a wide range of roles
