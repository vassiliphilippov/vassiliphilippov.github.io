digestive juice

noun
liquid aiding digestion in the body
1.0
Pronunciation: / daɪˈdʒɛstɪv dʒus / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛstɪv dʒuːs / (BrE)
a liquid produced by glands in the digestive system, such as saliva, gastric juice, or bile, which helps break down food during digestion

Examples

Digestive juices in stomach
A lack of certain digestive juices can lead to problems with nutrient absorption.
Doctors studied the composition of digestive juice in patients with chronic stomach issues.
The stomach releases digestive juice to help break down proteins in food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bile - digestive fluid produced by the liver
  • synonym: gastric juice - acidic digestive fluid produced in the stomach
  • synonym: pancreatic juice - digestive secretion from the pancreas
