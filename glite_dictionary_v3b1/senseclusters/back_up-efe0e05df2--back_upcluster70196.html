back up

verb
move backwards from a position
3.2
Pronunciation: / bæk ˈʌp /; / bæk ʌp / (AmE), / bæk ˈʌp /; / bæk ʌp / (BrE)
to move backwards, or to make a vehicle go in reverse

Examples

To back up the car
Could you please back up a little so I can get through the door?
The police officer signaled for everyone to back up from the scene.
When the car in front stopped suddenly, he had to back up to avoid a collision.
Before you install new software, remember to back up all your important files.
She asked him to back up the car so she could open the garage door.
You should always back up your photos to avoid losing them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward toward a position
  • synonym: copy - to make a duplicate, especially of digital data
  • synonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw from a position
  • synonym: reverse - to go backward, especially a vehicle
