deadrise

noun
boat hull angle
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌraɪz / (BrE)
the angle between the bottom of a boat and its side, which influences how the boat moves through water

Examples

Steep deadrise angle
A boat with a steep deadrise can handle rough waves more smoothly.
The designer increased the deadrise to improve the vessel's performance in choppy water.
Measuring the deadrise is important when choosing a boat for offshore fishing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beam - the width of a boat at its widest point
  • synonym: draft - the depth of a boat's hull below the waterline
