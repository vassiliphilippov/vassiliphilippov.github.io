pay

verb
to settle a debt or obligation
Pronunciation: / peɪ / (AmE), / peɪ / (BrE)
to give money in exchange for goods, services, or to settle a debt or obligation

Examples

To pay a bill
After months of saving, she was finally able to pay her student loans.
Once he receives the invoice, he will pay the balance in full.
You must pay your taxes by the deadline to avoid penalties.
She promised to pay him for his work on the project.
The company must pay damages to the injured workers.
You can't pay back a kindness like that with money alone.
After much arguing, she finally agreed to pay what she owed.
He refused to pay the bill until the repairs were finished.
You must pay your rent by the end of the month.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buy - to obtain in exchange for payment
  • antonym: charge - to ask for an amount of money for work, service or goods
  • synonym: compensate - to provide something (typically money) as a recompense for loss, suffering, or effort
  • antonym: owe - to be under obligation to pay or repay
  • antonym: receive - to be given or paid something
  • synonym: repay - to pay back money that was borrowed or owed
  • synonym: reward - to give something (usually money) in return for good work or services
  • synonym: settle - to pay an amount that is owed; to resolve a debt or account
