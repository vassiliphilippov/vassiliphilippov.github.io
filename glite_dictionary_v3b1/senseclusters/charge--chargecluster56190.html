charge

verb
move forward quickly to attack
3.3
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to move forward quickly and forcefully, often to attack

Examples

To charge at the enemy
Suddenly, the bull charged at the red cloth the matador held.
The soldiers charged across the battlefield toward the enemy lines.
With a roar, she charged into the crowd, pushing her way to the front.
During the game, the players charged down the field toward the goal.
She charged through the door, demanding answers.
The bull began to charge at the startled crowd.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against someone or something
  • synonym: bolt - move or run away suddenly and quickly
  • antonym: retreat - to withdraw from an enemy or attack
  • synonym: rush - move with urgent haste
