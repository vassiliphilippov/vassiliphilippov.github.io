presumptive

adjective
affording reasonable grounds for belief
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzʌmptɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈzʌmptɪv / (BrE)
likely to be considered true or accepted based on reasonable grounds or available evidence, but not certain

Examples

The presumptive nominee
She is the presumptive nominee according to the latest polls.
The defendant's guilt was supported by presumptive facts but not proved beyond doubt.
There was presumptive evidence of fraud in the company’s financial records.
He is the presumptive winner based on the current vote count.
The committee named her the presumptive nominee for the upcoming election.
The will identified Daniel as the presumptive heir to the estate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conclusive - Serving to prove a case; decisive or convincing
  • synonym: probable - Likely to be true or to happen
  • antonym: unlikely - not likely to happen or be true
