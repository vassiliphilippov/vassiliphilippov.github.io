summer haw

noun
southern US hawthorn with scarlet fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌmɚ hɔ / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə hɔː / (BrE)
a hawthorn tree of the southern United States with juicy, acidic, scarlet fruit, often used in jellies or preserves

Examples

The summer haw tree
Grandmother made delicious jelly from the tart berries of the summer haw.
Local farmers sell jars of summer haw preserves at the market every spring.
The old orchard was filled with summer haw trees, their branches heavy with ripe, red fruit.
