artery

noun
blood vessel carrying blood from heart
1.9 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.tə.riː/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.tə.riː/ (BrE)
A blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart to other parts of the body.

Examples

Coronary artery
Each artery supplies oxygen-rich blood to specific tissues in the body.
Plaque buildup in an artery can lead to serious heart problems.
The surgeon repaired the damaged artery to restore normal blood flow.
