stark

adjective
complete or extreme (emphasizing intensity)
5.6 7.33/m
Pronunciation: / st…ërk / (AmE), / st…ëÀêk / (BrE)
complete or extreme, often used to emphasize something unpleasant, clear, or absolute

Examples

Stark contrast
After hearing his stark warning, everyone fell silent.
She was stark naked when she ran out of the bathroom.
The early morning revealed the stark reality of the devastation.
After the recession, the city faced stark poverty in many neighborhoods.
The difference between the two candidates' policies was a stark contrast.
The once bustling town now lies in stark silence after the disaster.
His stark warning left everyone in the room silent.
The report presented stark facts about climate change, with no attempt to soften the message.
We had to face the stark reality that the company was going to close.

Synonyms

  • utter
    3.7 9.67/m
    utter is absolute and used for strong emphasis in many contexts, while stark stresses a harsh, clear, often unpleasant reality.
  • complete
    2.8 13/m
    complete means whole or total in a neutral way, while stark often adds a sharp, bare, or unpleasant quality.
  • unadulterated
    6.9 0.40/m
    unadulterated describes something pure and undiluted, while stark focuses on plain extremeness or a harsh, often unpleasant, quality.
  • arrant
    9.9 0.10/m
    arrant is a formal, strongly negative word used to stress badness, while stark can mean extreme or simply plain and harsh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolute - not qualified or diminished in any way; total
  • synonym: bare - lacking any covering, adornment, or disguise
  • synonym: blunt - direct and unadorned in speech or manner
  • antonym: moderate - not extreme; average in amount or degree
  • antonym: partial - not complete; existing only in part
  • antonym: subtle - not obvious or conspicuous; delicately complex
