poetaster

noun
inferior poet
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊɪˌtæstər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊɪˌtɑːstə /; / ˈpəʊɪˌtæstə / (BrE)
a person who writes inferior, trivial, or poorly crafted poetry

Examples

A poetaster writes bad poems
The critics labeled him a poetaster because his verses lacked originality and depth.
Despite his ambition, his work was dismissed as the effort of a poetaster by literary scholars.
She feared being called a poetaster if her poems were not well received.
The literary magazine refused to publish the work of a known poetaster.
Critics often label those who imitate famous poets poorly as poetasters.
He was determined not to be remembered as a mere poetaster in literary circles.
