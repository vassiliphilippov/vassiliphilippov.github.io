oogonium

noun
female reproductive cell
9.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.ə.ˈgoʊ.niː.əm/ /; / /uː.ˈgoʊ.niː.əm/ (AmE), /əʊ.ə.ˈgəʊ.niː.əm/ /; / /uː.ˈgəʊ.niː.əm/ (BrE)
a female reproductive structure in certain algae, fungi, and oomycetes that produces and contains the egg cell before fertilization

Examples

Algae oogonium
The scientist observed the oogonium under the microscope to study its development.
In some algae, the oogonium contains a single large egg cell ready for fertilization.
After fertilization, the oogonium develops into an oospore that can survive harsh conditions.
