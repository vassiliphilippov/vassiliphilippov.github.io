mandible

noun
lower jawbone
Pronunciation: / ˈmændəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmændɪbəl / (BrE)
the lower jaw or jawbone, especially in insects or animals, used for biting or holding food

Examples

Ant mandible
The ant used its mandible to carry a piece of leaf.
A crocodile's powerful mandible helps it catch and hold onto prey.
Scientists studied the fossilized mandible to learn more about the ancient animal's diet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jaw - the bones that form the framework of the mouth
  • antonym: maxilla - the upper jawbone in vertebrates
