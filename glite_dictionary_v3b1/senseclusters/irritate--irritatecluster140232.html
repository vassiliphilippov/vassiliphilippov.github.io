irritate

verb
stimulate a biological reaction
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪteɪt / (BrE)
to cause a part of the body to become sore, inflamed, or uncomfortable, or to produce a physical reaction

Examples

To irritate the skin
Applying salt can irritate the surface cells of the tongue, causing a sharp sensation.
Certain chemicals irritate nerve endings in the skin, producing a reaction.
If pollen irritates the lining of the nose, it may trigger sneezing.
Certain chemicals in cosmetics may irritate sensitive skin.
Continuous rubbing of the shoe can irritate your heel, causing blisters.
The spicy food can irritate the lining of your stomach if eaten in excess.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to reduce activity or irritation in biological tissues
  • synonym: inflame - to cause inflammation in a part of the body
  • synonym: stimulate - encourage a biological or physiological activity
