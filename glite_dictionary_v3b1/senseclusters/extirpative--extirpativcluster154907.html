extirpative

adjective
removing completely
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkstərˌpeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛkstɜːpətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving the removal or complete destruction of something, especially tissue or an organ

Examples

An extirpative surgery
The surgeon chose an extirpative approach to ensure all cancerous cells were removed.
An extirpative procedure may be necessary when less invasive treatments are ineffective.
Modern medicine sometimes favors extirpative techniques to prevent the spread of disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ablative - relating to the removal of tissue
  • antonym: conservative - intended to preserve rather than remove tissue
