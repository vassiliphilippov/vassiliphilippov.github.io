forced feeding

noun
feeding by artificial means when refusing or unable to eat
4.4 5.1
Pronunciation: /fɔːrst ˈfiː.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /fɔːst ˈfiː.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of feeding someone by delivering a nutrient solution, often through a tube, when they cannot or refuse to eat

Examples

Forced feeding in hospital
Forced feeding is sometimes used in hospitals when patients are unable to swallow.
The controversial practice of forced feeding of hunger strikers has sparked international debate.
The patient was subjected to forced feeding after refusing to eat for several days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intravenous feeding - delivering nutrients directly into a vein
  • synonym: tube feeding - feeding by delivering food through a tube to the stomach or intestine
