non-language

adjective
not using language
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑnˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
not expressed in or based on spoken or written language; relating to nonverbal forms such as gestures, images, or sounds

Examples

Non-language signals
The therapist used non-language communication, such as hand signals, to help the child express feelings.
Art and music are powerful forms of non-language expression that can convey deep emotions.
Researchers studied how infants respond to non-language cues like facial expressions and tone of voice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: linguistic - relating to language or linguistics
  • synonym: nonverbal - not involving or using words or speech
