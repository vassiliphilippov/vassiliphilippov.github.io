microcirculation

noun
small vessel blood flow
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˌsɜːrkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˌsɜːkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the circulation of blood through the smallest blood vessels, such as capillaries, arterioles, and venules, in the body

Examples

Blood microcirculation
Diabetes can cause problems with microcirculation, leading to poor wound healing.
Researchers study microcirculation to understand how nutrients reach body tissues.
Improved microcirculation can help reduce the risk of tissue damage in certain diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circulation - the movement of blood through the body
  • antonym: macrovascular - relating to the large blood vessels in the body
