dysregulation

noun
impaired self-control
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
difficulty or inability to control or manage emotional or physiological responses properly

Examples

Emotional dysregulation
Children with emotional dysregulation may have trouble calming down after getting upset.
Researchers are studying how stress can lead to hormonal dysregulation in the body.
Therapy often focuses on teaching skills to manage dysregulation more effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imbalance - a lack of balance or stability
  • antonym: regulation - the act of controlling or managing something
