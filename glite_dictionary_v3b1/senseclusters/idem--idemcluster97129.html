same author/source
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪdɛm / (AmE), / ˈiːdɛm / (BrE)
used in citations to indicate that the same author or source as previously mentioned is being referred to

Examples

Idem in citations
In the bibliography, idem is used to show that the same author wrote several works.
The legal document referenced multiple cases by Johnson; idem indicated repeated authorship.
When citing several articles by the same person, scholars often use idem to avoid repetition.

Related Terms
