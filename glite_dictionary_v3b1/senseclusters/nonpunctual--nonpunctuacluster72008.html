nonpunctual

adjective
extended in time
7.2
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈpʌŋktʃuəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈpʌŋktjʊəl / (BrE)
not occurring at a single point in time; lasting or continuing over a period of time

Examples

Nonpunctual verb
In linguistics, a nonpunctual verb describes an action that continues over time.
The teacher explained that 'sleep' is a nonpunctual event because it lasts for hours.
Unlike a sudden noise, a nonpunctual sound, like music, can be heard for a long duration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: durative - lasting or continuing over a period of time
  • antonym: punctual - occurring at a single point in time
