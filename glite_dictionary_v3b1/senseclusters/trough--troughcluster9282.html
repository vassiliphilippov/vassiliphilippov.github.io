trough

noun
government treasury or repository for funds
9.1 0.94/m
Pronunciation: / trÉ‘f / (AmE), / trÉ’f / (BrE)
a place or institution used as a treasury for government funds, especially regarded as a source of money for those in power

Examples

Government trough
Corruption allegations centered on officials with access to the government trough.
He criticized politicians for lining up at the trough rather than serving the public.
Lobbyists are often accused of trying to dip into the public trough for contracts.

Synonyms

  • public treasury
    4.1 0.15/m
    public treasury holds government funds and pays bills, while trough suggests a treasury used as a private money source for those in power.
  • treasury
    5.3 1.80/m
    treasury refers to a government's funds or department, while trough implies that same treasury is treated as a private cash source for officials.
