nanofiber

noun
very thin fiber
Pronunciation: / ˈnænoʊˌfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈnænəʊˌfaɪbə / (BrE)
an extremely thin fiber with a diameter measured in nanometers, used in materials for filtration, textiles, or biomedical applications

Examples

Nanofiber filter
Researchers are developing nanofiber filters to improve air and water purification systems.
A single nanofiber is invisible to the naked eye due to its tiny diameter.
Medical implants made from nanofiber materials can help tissues heal more effectively.
The new air filter uses a nanofiber layer to trap even the smallest particles.
Researchers are exploring nanofiber scaffolds to help regenerate damaged tissues.
A nanofiber membrane can improve the efficiency of water purification systems.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: fiber - a thread or filament forming part of a material
  • degree lower: microfiber - a very fine synthetic fiber, larger than a nanofiber
