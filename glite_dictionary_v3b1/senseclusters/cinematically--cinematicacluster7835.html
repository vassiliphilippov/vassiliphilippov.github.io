cinematically

adverb
in film style
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪnəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌsɪnɪˈmætɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is like a movie, especially in terms of visual style or dramatic effect

Examples

Told cinematically
The story was told cinematically, with sweeping camera movements and dramatic lighting.
Her novel is structured cinematically, almost as if it were a screenplay.
The city skyline was captured cinematically in the opening scene of the film.
The landscape was filmed cinematically to highlight its breathtaking beauty.
She described the event cinematically, making it easy to imagine as a movie scene.
The commercial was shot cinematically to capture viewers' attention with dramatic visuals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: theatrically - in a way that relates to theater or stage performance
  • synonym: visually - in a way that relates to seeing or sight
