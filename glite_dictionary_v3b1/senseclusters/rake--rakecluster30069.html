rake

verb
examine hastily or superficially
Pronunciation: / reɪk / (AmE), / reɪk / (BrE)
to glance over or examine something quickly and superficially

Examples

To rake the shelves
He raked the shelves with his eyes, searching for the missing book.
His gaze raked the room, taking in the scene in a matter of seconds.
She raked through the document, hoping to spot any obvious errors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scan - to look at something quickly to get the main points
  • degree higher: scrutinize - to examine very closely or critically
