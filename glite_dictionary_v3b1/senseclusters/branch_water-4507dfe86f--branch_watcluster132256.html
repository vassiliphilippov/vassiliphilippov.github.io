branch water

noun
natural water from a stream or brook
Pronunciation: / bræntʃ ˈwɑtər / (AmE), / brɑːntʃ ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
pure natural water from a stream or brook, especially as used to dilute spirits and distinguished from soda water

Examples

A splash of branch water
He liked his whiskey with a splash of branch water instead of soda.
Some people insist that only branch water should be used to dilute fine bourbon.
The bartender offered him branch water on the side with his drink.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soda water - water carbonated with carbon dioxide
