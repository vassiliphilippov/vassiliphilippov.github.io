freedom from cruel and unusual punishment

noun
constitutional right against harsh punishment
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːdəm frəm ˈkruːəl ənd ʌnˈjuːʒuəl ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm frəm ˈkruːəl ənd ʌnˈjuːʒʊəl ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
The right, guaranteed by the Eighth Amendment to the United States Constitution, not to be subjected to torture or to excessively harsh or arbitrary penalties by the government.

Examples

Freedom from cruel and unusual punishment right
Legal challenges often cite freedom from cruel and unusual punishment when addressing conditions in jails and prisons.
Prisoners argued that their treatment violated their freedom from cruel and unusual punishment.
The Eighth Amendment protects the freedom from cruel and unusual punishment in the United States.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cruel and unusual punishment - punishment considered inhumane or too severe for the crime
