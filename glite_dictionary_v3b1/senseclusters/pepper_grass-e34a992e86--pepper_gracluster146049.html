pepper grass

noun
herb used as salad green or garnish
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛpər ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A small annual herb with pungent leaves, often used as a salad green or garnish.

Examples

Fresh pepper grass leaves
Gardeners often grow pepper grass because it is fast-sprouting and easy to harvest.
She sprinkled fresh pepper grass over the salad for extra flavor.
The chef used pepper grass as a garnish to brighten up the dish.
