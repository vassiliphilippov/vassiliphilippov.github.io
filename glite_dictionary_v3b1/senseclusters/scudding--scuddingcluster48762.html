scudding

noun
swift, smooth movement, esp. by wind
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈskʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of moving swiftly and smoothly, especially driven by the wind or while avoiding obstacles, often used to describe clouds or ships

Examples

Scudding clouds
Geese in scudding flight passed quickly overhead, disappearing into the distance.
The scudding of low clouds across the moon made the night seem mysterious.
The sailors watched the scudding of the waves before the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scud - to move fast in a straight line driven by the wind
