unspool

verb
to unfold gradually
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈspuːl / (AmE), / ʌnˈspuːl / (BrE)
to unfold, develop, or be revealed gradually, especially in the context of a story or sequence of events

Examples

A story unspools
As the documentary progressed, the full story began to unspool before the audience.
Details of the plan unspooled slowly during the meeting, keeping everyone in suspense.
The mystery unspooled over several chapters, keeping readers eager to learn more.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something hidden or secret
  • synonym: reveal - to make something known that was secret or hidden
  • synonym: unfold - to develop or become clear gradually
