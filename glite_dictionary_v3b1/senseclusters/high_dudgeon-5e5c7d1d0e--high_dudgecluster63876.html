high dudgeon

noun
intense indignation or anger
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈdʌdʒən / (AmE), / haɪ ˈdʌdʒən / (BrE)
a state of strong anger or indignation, especially shown in a proud or formal way (usually used in the phrase 'in high dudgeon')

Examples

Leave in high dudgeon
She left the meeting in high dudgeon after being criticized by her colleagues.
The actor stormed off the stage in high dudgeon when the director rejected his ideas.
Whenever things don't go his way, he tends to respond in high dudgeon.
After the meeting, she left the office in high dudgeon, refusing to speak to anyone.
He responded to the criticism with high dudgeon, insisting he had done nothing wrong.
Several employees resigned in high dudgeon over the proposed company changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • antonym: contentment - a state of happiness and satisfaction
  • synonym: indignation - anger or annoyance provoked by what is perceived as unfair treatment
