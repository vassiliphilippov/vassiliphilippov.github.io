mutilate

verb
to damage or injure severely
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪleɪt / (BrE)
to damage or change something so badly that it is ruined, disfigured, or hard to recognize, often by cutting or destroying parts

Examples

To mutilate the body
Careless editing can sometimes mutilate the meaning of an original text.
The artist was devastated when vandals mutilated his sculpture in the park.
The rescue team found that the animal had been severely mutilated by a trap.
He managed to mutilate her name so badly that nobody recognized it.
Improper handling can mutilate a historical artifact beyond identification.
The editor refused to mutilate the original text for the abridged edition.
He was convicted of attempting to mutilate evidence to hinder the investigation.
The ancient statue was mutilated during the war, losing both its arms.
The document was so badly mutilated that it was nearly impossible to read.

  • antonym: repair - to restore something that is damaged to its original state
