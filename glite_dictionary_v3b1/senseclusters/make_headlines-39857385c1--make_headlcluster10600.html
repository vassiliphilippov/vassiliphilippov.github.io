make headlines

verb
receive media publicity or attention
1.5
Pronunciation: / meɪk ˈhɛdlaɪnz / (AmE), / meɪk ˈhɛdlaɪnz / (BrE)
to receive prominent or widespread attention in the news media; to become a subject of news reports.

Examples

To make headlines worldwide
Her scientific discovery quickly made headlines in major newspapers around the world.
The mayor's sudden resignation made headlines across the country.
When the actor donated millions to charity, the generous act made headlines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attract attention - to become the focus of interest or notice
  • antonym: go unnoticed - to not receive attention or be ignored by the public or media
