abnegator

noun
a person who renounces something
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.nəˌɡeɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˈæb.nɪˌɡeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who renounces or relinquishes something, especially personal desires or interests

Examples

An abnegator of desires
As an abnegator of personal wealth, she donated her fortune to charity and lived modestly.
In literature, the hero is often depicted as an abnegator, sacrificing personal happiness for a greater cause.
The monk was considered a true abnegator by his peers, renouncing worldly pleasures for spiritual gain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascetic - a person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention
  • antonym: hedonist - a person who pursues pleasure as a primary goal
