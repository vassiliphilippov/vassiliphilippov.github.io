short-spurred

adjective
having a short projection at the flower's base
6.3
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt spɜrd / (AmE), / ʃɔːt spɜːd / (BrE)
(of a flower) having a short spur or projection at the base of the corolla.

Examples

Short-spurred flower
Some violets have short-spurred petals, making them easy to identify.
The columbine is often classified as short-spurred or long-spurred depending on the variety.
This orchid species is distinguished by its short-spurred flowers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: long-spurred - (of a flower) having a long spur at the base of the corolla
