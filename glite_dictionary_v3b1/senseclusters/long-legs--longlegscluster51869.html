long-legs

noun
a wading bird with long legs
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔŋˌlɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌlɛɡz / (BrE)
a long-legged black-and-white wading bird, found near inland ponds, marshes, or brackish lagoons

Examples

The long-legs bird
The distinctive long-legs waded gracefully through the shallow waters, searching for food.
Unlike herons, long-legs often keep to smaller inland ponds.
We spotted several long-legs near the edge of the marsh during our walk.
