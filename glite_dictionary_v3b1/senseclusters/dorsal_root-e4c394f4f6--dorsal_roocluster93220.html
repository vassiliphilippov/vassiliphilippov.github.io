dorsal root

noun
sensory branch of a spinal nerve
8.1 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…îrs…ôl ruÀêt / (AmE), / Ààd…îÀês…ôl ruÀêt / (BrE)
either of the two roots of a spinal nerve that passes toward the back (dorsally) from the spinal cord and contains sensory nerve fibers

Examples

Dorsal root nerve
Damage to the dorsal root can cause numbness or loss of sensation in the affected area.
Sensory information from the skin enters the spinal cord through the dorsal root.
The surgeon carefully identified the dorsal root before proceeding with the procedure.

Synonyms

  • posterior root
    9.5 0.04/m
    posterior root is the rear branch of a spinal nerve, while dorsal root is the anatomical name for that same sensory branch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ventral root - the root of a spinal nerve that passes ventrally and contains motor fibers
