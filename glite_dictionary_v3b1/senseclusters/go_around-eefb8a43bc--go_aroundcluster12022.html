go around

verb
move around an army's flank
3.6 4.1
Pronunciation: /goʊ ə.ˈraʊnd/ /; / /goʊ ə.ˈraʊn/ (AmE), /gəʊ ə.ˈraʊnd/ /; / /gəʊ ə.ˈraʊn/ (BrE)
to move around the side of an opposing army, especially to avoid direct confrontation or to attack from the flank

Examples

To go around the enemy
Instead of attacking head-on, they chose to go around and surprise the opponent from behind.
The cavalry managed to go around the main force and cut off their supply lines.
The general ordered his troops to go around the enemy's left flank during the battle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outflank - to move around the side of an enemy, especially to gain an advantage
