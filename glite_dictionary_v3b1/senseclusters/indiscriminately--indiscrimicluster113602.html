indiscriminately

adverb
without careful choice or selection
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnətli / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnətli /; / ˌɪndɪsˈkrɪmɪnətli / (BrE)
in a way that does not show careful choice or distinction; randomly or without considering differences

Examples

To act indiscriminately
She eats indiscriminately, without regard for nutrition or taste.
Social media can spread rumors indiscriminately, reaching people all over the world.
The soldiers fired indiscriminately into the crowd, causing panic and chaos.
Rain poured indiscriminately over the city, flooding both wealthy and poor neighborhoods alike.
The company sent advertisements indiscriminately to thousands of email addresses, not targeting any specific group.
The protesters were arrested indiscriminately by the authorities, regardless of their actual involvement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: randomly - lacking a definite plan, purpose, or pattern
  • antonym: selectively - with careful selection or discrimination
  • antonym: systematically - in a methodical or organized manner
