radiation sickness

noun
acute illness from exposure to ionizing radiation
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən ˈsɪknəs / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən ˈsɪknɪs / (BrE)
illness caused by exposure to a large dose of ionizing radiation, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, hair loss, and potentially severe organ damage or death

Examples

Severe radiation sickness
Doctors treated the workers for severe radiation sickness after the reactor leak.
Many survivors of the nuclear accident developed radiation sickness soon after the event.
Symptoms of radiation sickness can begin within hours of exposure to high radiation levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radiation poisoning - illness caused by excessive exposure to radiation
