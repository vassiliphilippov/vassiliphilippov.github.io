coarseness

noun
roughness or looseness in texture
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrsnəs /; / ˈkɔr·snəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsnəs /; / ˈkɔːs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being rough or not smooth in texture or material

Examples

Coarseness of fabric
He disliked the coarseness of the fabric and preferred something much softer.
The coarseness of the wool made the sweater uncomfortable against her skin.
The carpet's coarseness was apparent as soon as you touched it.
The coarseness of the fabric was obvious when compared to the silk.
The coarseness of the sand made it uncomfortable to walk on barefoot.
You can adjust the coarseness of the grind for your coffee beans depending on your preference.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fineness - the quality of being very small or fine in size or texture
  • synonym: roughness - the quality of being rough to the touch or not smooth
