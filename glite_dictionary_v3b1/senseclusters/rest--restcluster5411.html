rest

noun
pause for relaxation or recovery
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /rest/ (AmE), /rest/ (BrE)
a period or state of stopping activity, relaxing, or sleeping in order to recover strength or energy

Examples

A rest after work
After a long hike, we sat down for a much-needed rest under the trees.
Doctors recommend that you get plenty of rest when you are recovering from illness.
She took a short rest before going back to her studies.
According to Newton's first law, an object at rest will remain at rest unless acted upon by a force.
After the excitement, the city returned to a deep rest, with empty streets and quiet buildings.
The antique clock sat in perfect rest on the mantelpiece, its hands unmoving.
After a long day of hiking, she needed a rest before starting dinner.
Doctors recommend getting enough rest to recover from illness.
He took a short rest in the shade to regain his strength.
