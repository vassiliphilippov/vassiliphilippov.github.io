bluff

noun
an act of deception or pretense
5.3 4.5
Pronunciation: /blʌf/ (AmE), /blʌf/ (BrE)
an attempt to deceive someone by pretending to have a stronger position or more confidence than you actually have, especially in games like poker

Examples

Poker bluff
He called her bluff and discovered she was not holding a strong hand.
Sometimes a well-timed bluff can win a game of poker.
The threat was just a bluff to make us retreat.
He made a bold bluff, pretending to have a much stronger hand than he did.
Her bluff was so convincing that everyone folded their cards immediately.
Sometimes a well-timed bluff can win you the entire pot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or tricking someone
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere
