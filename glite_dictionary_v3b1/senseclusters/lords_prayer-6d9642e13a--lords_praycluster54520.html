Lord's Prayer

noun
the Christian prayer beginning 'Our Father'
Pronunciation: / lɔrdz ˈprɛr / (AmE), / lɔːdz ˈpreə / (BrE)
the prayer taught by Jesus to his disciples, beginning with 'Our Father,' and found in the New Testament (Matthew 6:9-13)

Examples

The Lord's Prayer
Many Christians recite the Lord's Prayer during religious services.
She taught her children to say the Lord's Prayer before going to bed.
The Lord's Prayer is often memorized by young children in Sunday school.

  • synonym: Our Father - another name for the Lord's Prayer
