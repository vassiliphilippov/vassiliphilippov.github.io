gong

noun
large percussion instrument (metal disc)
Pronunciation: / ɡɑŋ / (AmE), / ɡɒŋ / (BrE)
a metal percussion instrument that makes a resonant sound when struck, either as a single large disc or a set of tuned discs or bells

Examples

A large gong
After striking the gong, the meditation session began in silence.
He bought a brass gong during his travels in Southeast Asia.
The orchestra featured a massive gong that resonated through the concert hall.
Several gongs were arranged by pitch for the performance.
She struck the largest gong to produce a deep, resonant sound.
The composer included a gong in the percussion section of the orchestra.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bell - a hollow instrument that makes a ringing sound when struck
  • synonym: chime - a set of tuned bells
  • synonym: cymbal - a percussion instrument consisting of a thin, round metal plate, typically played in pairs
  • degree lower: xylophone - a percussion instrument with wooden bars struck by mallets
