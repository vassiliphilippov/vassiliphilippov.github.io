bucker

noun
one who bucks or loads
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkər / (AmE), / ˈbʌkə / (BrE)
a person or animal that bucks (jumps or resists), or a worker who moves or cuts logs

Examples

A wild bucker
The rodeo featured a wild bucker that threw off every rider.
He worked as a bucker in the logging camp, moving heavy logs all day.
The horse proved to be a strong bucker, making the competition exciting.
The experienced bucker made quick work of the fallen pine tree.
A powerful bucker can turn a large log into firewood in minutes.
The rodeo featured a fierce bucker that threw every rider off its back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bronco - an untrained or wild horse that bucks
  • synonym: loader - a person or machine that loads goods
  • synonym: logger - a person who fells trees and prepares timber
  • synonym: sawyer - a person who saws timber into planks or boards
  • antonym: unloader - a person who removes goods from a vehicle or place
