nonconvertible

adjective
not able to change
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈvɝːtəbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈvɜːtəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be changed or exchanged into a different form, type, or value

Examples

Nonconvertible currency
The country uses a nonconvertible currency that cannot be exchanged freely.
Some bonds are nonconvertible, meaning they cannot be exchanged for company stock.
Because the ticket is nonconvertible, you cannot change it for another event.
The company issued nonconvertible bonds that paid higher interest but could not be turned into stock.
Because the certificate is nonconvertible, it cannot be exchanged for company shares.
Investors often compare nonconvertible securities with convertible ones before making a decision.
