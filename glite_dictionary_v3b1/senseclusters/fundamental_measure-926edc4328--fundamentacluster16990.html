fundamental measure

noun
basic quantity in measurement systems
1.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /fʌn.də.ˈmen.təl ˈmɛʒ.ər/ (AmE), /fʌn.də.ˈmen.təl ˈmɛʒ.ə/ (BrE)
one of the basic quantities used as a foundation for systems of measurement, such as length, mass, time, and electric current

Examples

A fundamental measure of length
Length, mass, and time are each considered a fundamental measure in physics.
The SI system defines seven fundamental measures upon which all other units are based.
Without a clear fundamental measure, it is difficult to establish a consistent standard.
