trichomonad

noun
parasitic protozoan (genus Trichomonas)
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɪkəˈmoʊnæd / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈməʊnæd / (BrE)
A single-celled parasitic protozoan of the genus Trichomonas, some species of which can cause infections in humans and animals.

Examples

A trichomonad infection
Trichomonad infections are commonly found in the reproductive tracts of birds and mammals.
A sample was examined under a microscope to check for the presence of trichomonads.
The veterinarian explained that the cattle's illness was likely caused by a type of trichomonad.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trichomoniasis - an infection caused by trichomonads
