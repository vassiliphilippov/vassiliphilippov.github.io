Synonyms

    9.9 0.01/m
    requital is a formal, rare word for a deserved consequence in writing, while retribution is the common moral punishment term.
  • comeuppance
    9.1 0.50/m
    comeuppance is an informal word for someone getting what they deserve, while retribution sounds more formal and like justified punishment.
  • punishment
    1.3 33/m
    punishment is the general act of punishing someone for an offense, while retribution stresses deserved moral payback.
  • penalty
    2.5 22/m
    penalty is a legal or rule-based punishment for breaking rules, while retribution emphasizes moral payback for wrongdoing.
  • vengeance
    6.8 9.36/m
    vengeance is a personal, often emotional act of punishing someone, while retribution is a colder, socially accepted idea of deserved punishment.
  • revenge
    4.2 0.25/m
    revenge is doing harm to hurt someone back out of anger, while retribution is seen as deserved or morally right punishment.
  • payback
    5.5 2.54/m
    payback is an informal, often violent return for a wrong, while retribution implies a justified, measured punishment.
  • reprisal
    8.3 1.94/m
    reprisal is a military or political act of revenge after an attack, while retribution is broader and often legally or morally framed.
  • retaliation
    5.2 7.00/m
    retaliation is taking harmful action back against someone quickly, while retribution is more about deserved, often formal punishment.
