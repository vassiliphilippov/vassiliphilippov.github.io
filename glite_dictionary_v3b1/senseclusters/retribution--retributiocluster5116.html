retribution

noun
punishment or vengeance for wrongdoing
5.9 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtrɪˈbjuːʃən /; / ˌrɛtrəˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
punishment or penalty given as payback for wrongdoing, often seen as deserved or morally right

Examples

Swift retribution
He feared swift retribution after betraying his allies.
Many believe that justice requires both retribution and rehabilitation.
The community demanded retribution for the crimes committed against them.
He feared retribution from his former partners after testifying in court.
Many believe that the disaster was divine retribution for their actions.
The angry crowd saw the harsh sentence as retribution for the terrible crimes committed.
He feared that speaking out would bring retribution upon his family.
Many believed the natural disaster was an act of retribution for the town's past misdeeds.
Some stories warn of divine retribution for those who act unjustly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forgiveness - the act of forgiving or being forgiven
  • synonym: punishment - the infliction of a penalty for an offense
  • synonym: revenge - the action of inflicting hurt or harm on someone for an injury or wrong
  • antonym: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement; the opposite of punishment
  • synonym: vengeance - punishment inflicted in return for a wrong
