bear-baiting

verb
to provoke deliberately
9.5
Pronunciation: /ber ˈbeɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /beər ˈbeɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /beə ˈbeɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /bɪə ˈbeɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
to deliberately provoke or harass a person, group, or authority in order to get a strong reaction

Examples

To bear-bait a politician
The journalist was accused of bear-baiting the politician during the heated interview.
Some protesters resorted to bear-baiting the police to draw attention to their cause.
He enjoyed bear-baiting his rivals with sharp questions at every meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harass - to repeatedly annoy or attack someone
  • synonym: taunt - to provoke or challenge someone with insulting remarks
