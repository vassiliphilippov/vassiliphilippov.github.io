settle down

verb
begin a stable or permanent life
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈset.əl daʊn/ (AmE), /ˈset.əl daʊn/ (BrE)
to start living a quiet and steady life, often by getting a regular job, marrying, or establishing a home

Examples

To settle down and marry

She bought a house because she was ready to settle down in one place.

He hopes to settle down in the countryside when he retires.
The children finally settled down after the excitement of the party.
