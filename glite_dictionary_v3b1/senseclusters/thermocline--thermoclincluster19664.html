thermocline

noun
water temperature layer
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈθɜːrmoʊˌklaɪn / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməʊˌklaɪn / (BrE)
a distinct layer in a body of water where the temperature changes rapidly with increasing depth

Examples

A thermocline layer
During summer, a sharp thermocline can form in deep lakes, separating warm surface water from colder layers below.
Scuba divers often notice the sudden drop in temperature when they cross the thermocline.
Many species of fish are found just above the thermocline where the water is warmer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: halocline - a layer in water where the salinity changes rapidly with depth
  • synonym: pycnocline - a layer in water where the density changes rapidly with depth
