deviation

noun
variation from standard or norm
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən /; / ˌdiːvɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a difference or departure from what is usual, expected, or accepted, especially from a standard, rule, or course

Examples

A deviation from rules
Any significant deviation from the company's safety protocol must be reported immediately.
Standard deviation is an important concept in statistics.
The scientist noted a minor deviation in the experimental results.
Any deviation from the company's code of conduct will not be tolerated.
The architect allowed a small deviation in the design to suit the client's preference.
The scientist noticed a significant deviation from the expected results in her experiment.
Her speech included an unexpected deviation into political topics.
Strict adherence to the plan was required; any deviation could lead to confusion.
The plane had to make a deviation from its original flight path due to bad weather.

  • synonym: departure - an act of leaving, especially from a standard or norm
  • synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing
  • synonym: divergence - the process or state of diverging or branching apart, especially from a standard
  • synonym: diversion - an instance of turning something aside from its course
  • antonym: norm - a standard or pattern, especially of social behavior
