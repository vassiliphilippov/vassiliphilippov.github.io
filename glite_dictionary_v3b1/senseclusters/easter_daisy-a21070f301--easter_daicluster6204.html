Easter daisy

noun
plant with white-rayed flower heads
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈiːstɚ ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈiːstə ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
A small, nearly stemless herbaceous plant with a rosette of woolly leaves and large white-rayed flower heads, native to central Canada and the western United States.

Examples

The Easter daisy flower
Botanists studied the Easter daisy in its natural prairie habitat.
She planted an Easter daisy in her garden, attracted by its delicate white petals.
The Easter daisy blooms early in spring, often before most other flowers appear.
