bitterweed

noun
a North American weed producing allergenic pollen
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪt̬ɚˌwid / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəˌwiːd / (BrE)
Any of several weedy plants, primarily found in North America, of the genus Ambrosia, known for producing allergenic pollen that causes hay fever and asthma.

Examples

Allergy bitterweed pollen
Many allergy sufferers dread the annual blooming of bitterweed in the late summer.
Scientists have identified bitterweed as a major source of allergenic pollen in North America.
The farmer tried to clear bitterweed from his fields to reduce hay fever symptoms in the community.
