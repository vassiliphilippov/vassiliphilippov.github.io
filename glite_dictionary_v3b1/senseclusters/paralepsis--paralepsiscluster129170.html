paralepsis

noun
rhetorical device: emphasizing by omission
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈlepsɪs / (AmE), / ˌparəˈlɛpsɪs / (BrE)
A rhetorical device in which an idea is emphasized by pretending to pass over it or mentioning it briefly, thus suggesting much more is being left unsaid.

Examples

Paralepsis in speech
In his speech, he relied on paralepsis to make insinuations without stating them outright.
The politician used paralepsis to draw attention to his opponent’s mistakes while claiming to ignore them.
Writers often employ paralepsis to highlight controversial topics while maintaining plausible deniability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paralipsis - rhetorical device of emphasizing something by professing not to mention it
