Pulitzer

proper noun
journalism award
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊlɪtsər / (AmE), / ˈpjuːlɪtsə / (BrE)
a prestigious award given annually for achievements in journalism, literature, or music in the United States

Examples

A Pulitzer Prize
She was thrilled to win a Pulitzer for her groundbreaking novel.
The newspaper received a Pulitzer for its investigative reporting on corruption.
Many journalists dream of earning a Pulitzer during their careers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Booker Prize - a major literary award for the best original novel written in English
  • synonym: Nobel Prize - an international award for outstanding achievements in various fields
