chiru

noun
Tibetan antelope
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃiːruː / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːruː / (BrE)
a wild antelope native to the Tibetan Plateau, valued for its fine wool and endangered status

Examples

Wild chiru antelope
The chiru is protected due to its endangered status.
Poachers once hunted the chiru for its valuable wool, threatening its survival.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of the chiru in recent years.

Related Terms
