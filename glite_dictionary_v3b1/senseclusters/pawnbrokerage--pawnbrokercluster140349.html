pawnbrokerage

noun
pawn shop business
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔnˌbroʊkərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːnˌbrəʊkərɪdʒ / (BrE)
a business or shop where money is lent to people in exchange for personal property as security (collateral)

Examples

The pawnbrokerage shop
He visited the pawnbrokerage to get a loan using his guitar as collateral.
The old pawnbrokerage on Main Street has been in business for decades.
She found a rare necklace at the local pawnbrokerage while searching for antiques.
He opened a successful pawnbrokerage after years of working in finance.
The city council discussed new regulations for local pawnbrokerage businesses.
Many people turn to pawnbrokerage when they need quick cash in emergencies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bank - an institution for receiving, lending, and safeguarding money
  • synonym: pawnshop - a shop where personal items are exchanged for loans
  • antonym: usury - the practice of lending money at unreasonably high interest rates
