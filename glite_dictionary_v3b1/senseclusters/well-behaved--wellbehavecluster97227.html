well-behaved

adjective
reliable or predictable in technical contexts
5.5
Pronunciation: /wel bɪ.ˈheɪvd/ /; / /wəl bɪ.ˈheɪvd/ (AmE), /wel bɪ.ˈheɪvd/ /; / /wəl bɪ.ˈheɪvd/ (BrE)
In technical contexts (computing, mathematics, engineering): behaving reliably and predictably; conforming to documented specifications, expected properties, or standard assumptions.

Examples

Well-behaved function
The new library is well-behaved, returning consistent results across edge cases.
Our API remains well-behaved under heavy load, handling requests without unexpected failures.
A well-behaved algorithm scales predictably with input size and avoids pathological slowdowns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erratic - not behaving in a regular or expected way; prone to failures or unexpected results
  • synonym: predictable - behaving in a way that can be accurately anticipated
  • synonym: reliable - consistently performing correctly or dependably
