sublayer

noun
underlying layer
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌleɪər / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌleɪə / (BrE)
a layer that is beneath or directly under another layer

Examples

The sublayer below
The soil's sublayer contains important minerals for plants.
Engineers examined the concrete sublayer before laying the final road surface.
Beneath the paint, a thin sublayer of primer helps the color adhere better.
The archaeologists carefully removed each sublayer of soil to uncover the ancient artifacts.
A thin sublayer of cheese was hidden beneath the vegetables on the pizza.
Engineers tested the strength of the road's sublayer before laying the asphalt.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: layer - a sheet or thickness of material, typically one of several
  • synonym: substratum - an underlying layer or base
  • antonym: surface - the outside or uppermost layer of something
