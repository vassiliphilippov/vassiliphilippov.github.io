reconverge

verb
to come together again
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈvɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to come together again after being separated or apart

Examples

To reconverge at the meeting
After the storm, the hikers decided to reconverge at the main trailhead.
The two research teams will reconverge next month to share their findings.
Once the separate groups finish their tasks, they will reconverge for a final discussion.
After exploring different parts of the museum, the students will reconverge in the lobby.
The two hiking trails split for a while but reconverge near the mountain summit.
Scientists from various fields often reconverge at conferences to share their latest research.

  • antonym: disperse - to go in different directions or spread apart
  • antonym: separate - to move apart or go in different directions
