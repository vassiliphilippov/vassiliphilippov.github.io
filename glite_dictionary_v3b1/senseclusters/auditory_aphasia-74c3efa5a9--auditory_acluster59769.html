auditory aphasia

noun
inability to understand spoken language despite normal hearing
Pronunciation: / ˈɔː.dɪˌtɔːr.i əˈfeɪ.ʒə / (AmE), / ˈɔː.dɪ.tər.i əˈfeɪ.zi.ə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which a person has difficulty understanding spoken language, despite normal hearing ability

Examples

Auditory aphasia diagnosis
After his stroke, the neurologist diagnosed him with auditory aphasia, explaining his trouble following conversations.
People with auditory aphasia can hear sounds clearly but struggle to understand spoken words.
Speech therapy can help individuals with auditory aphasia improve their language comprehension skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphasia - loss or impairment of the power to use or comprehend words
