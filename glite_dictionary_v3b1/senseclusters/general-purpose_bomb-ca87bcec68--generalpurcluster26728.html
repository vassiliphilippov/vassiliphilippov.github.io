general-purpose bomb

noun
an explosive bomb with blast and fragmentation effects
2.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒen.ər.əl ˈpɜːr.pəs bɑːm/ /; / /ˈdʒen.ər.əl ˈpɜːr.pəs bɔːm/ (AmE), /ˈdʒen.ər.əl ˈpɜː.pəs bɒm/ (BrE)
a large explosive bomb designed to create both a blast effect and fragmentation, typically weighing between 500 and 2,000 pounds and containing about 50% explosive material

Examples

A general-purpose bomb
A 1,000-pound general-purpose bomb was dropped on the target during the air raid.
Modern general-purpose bombs are often equipped with guidance kits for improved accuracy.
The Air Force used a general-purpose bomb to destroy the enemy’s ammunition depot.
