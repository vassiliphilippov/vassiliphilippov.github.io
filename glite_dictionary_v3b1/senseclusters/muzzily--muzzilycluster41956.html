muzzily

adverb
unclearly or indistinctly
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌzɪli / (AmE), / ˈmʌzɪli / (BrE)
in a muffled, indistinct, or unclear manner, especially referring to sound or speech.

Examples

Speaking muzzily
The instructions were read muzzily over the old loudspeaker, making them hard to follow.
After waking up from surgery, she spoke muzzily and struggled to form clear sentences.
He could hear music playing muzzily from the apartment next door.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearly - in a way that is easy to see, hear, or understand
  • synonym: unclearly - in a way that is not clear or easy to understand
