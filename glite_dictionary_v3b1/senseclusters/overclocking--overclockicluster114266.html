overclocking

noun
increasing computer speed
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈklɑːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈklɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making a computer component, such as a processor, run at a higher speed than its official rating

Examples

CPU overclocking
Many gamers use overclocking to get better performance from their computers.
Improper overclocking can lead to hardware damage if cooling is not sufficient.
He read several guides before attempting overclocking his graphics card.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underclocking - the act of making a computer component run slower than its standard speed
