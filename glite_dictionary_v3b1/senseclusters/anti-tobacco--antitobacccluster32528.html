anti-tobacco

noun
tobacco opposition movement
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌænti təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / ˌænti təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
a movement, campaign, or policy aimed at discouraging or restricting the use of tobacco products

Examples

Anti-tobacco campaign
The government launched a new anti-tobacco campaign to reduce smoking among teenagers.
Many health organizations support anti-tobacco initiatives to protect public health.
The city council passed strict anti-tobacco laws to limit smoking in public places.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anti-smoking - opposed to or aimed at preventing smoking
  • antonym: pro-tobacco - supporting or promoting the use of tobacco
