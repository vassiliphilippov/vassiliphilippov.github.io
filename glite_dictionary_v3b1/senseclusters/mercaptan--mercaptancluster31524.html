mercaptan

noun
sulfur compound with odor
Pronunciation: / mərˈkæptən / (AmE), / mɜːˈkæptən / (BrE)
a chemical compound (also called a thiol) containing sulfur and a strong, unpleasant odor, often used to help detect gas leaks

Examples

A mercaptan compound
A small amount of mercaptan is added to natural gas to help people detect leaks by smell.
The strong odor in the laboratory was caused by a spill of mercaptan during the experiment.
Workers must wear protective gear when handling mercaptan because of its intense smell and chemical properties.
The strong smell in natural gas comes from added mercaptan for safety reasons.
Chemists detected the presence of mercaptan by its distinctive odor in the laboratory.
Exposure to mercaptan can cause irritation, so proper ventilation is important during experiments.
