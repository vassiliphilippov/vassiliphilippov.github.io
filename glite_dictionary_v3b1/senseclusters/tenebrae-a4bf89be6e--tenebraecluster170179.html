Tenebrae

noun
Holy Week church service
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈten.ə.breɪ/ /; / /ˈten.ə.briː/ (AmE), /ˈten.ə.breɪ/ /; / /ˈten.ə.briː/ (BrE)
a Christian religious service during Holy Week, or the music and texts performed at this service, marked by the gradual extinguishing of candles

Examples

Tenebrae service
The congregation gathered for the solemn Tenebrae service on the evening of Good Friday.
During Tenebrae, each candle was extinguished one by one, leaving the church in darkness.
Many churches observe Tenebrae to reflect on the suffering and death of Jesus.
The choir performed a moving Tenebrae service on Good Friday evening.
Composers throughout history have written Tenebrae settings for use during Holy Week.
The cathedral echoed with the solemn music of Tenebrae as candles were gradually extinguished.
