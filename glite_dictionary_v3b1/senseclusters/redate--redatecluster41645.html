redate

verb
to assign new date
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to assign a new or different date to something, often because of new information or to avoid a conflict

Examples

To redate a document
The lawyer asked her assistant to redate the contract before sending it to the client.
We need to redate the invitation cards because the event has been postponed.
After the error was found, the team decided to redate all the official documents.
The event was redated to avoid conflicting with the holiday weekend.
After the error was discovered, the contract had to be redated and signed again.
They decided to redate the letter to reflect the actual day it was sent.
After new research, the scientists decided to redate the ancient fossils.
The historian had to redate several documents based on the latest findings.
The museum will redate the artifact to reflect the updated analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: backdate - to put an earlier date on something than the actual one
  • synonym: date - to determine or record the date of something
  • synonym: postpone - to delay something to a later date
  • synonym: reassess - to evaluate something again, especially with new information
  • synonym: reclassify - to assign to a new category or class
  • synonym: reschedule - to arrange for something to take place at a different time or date
