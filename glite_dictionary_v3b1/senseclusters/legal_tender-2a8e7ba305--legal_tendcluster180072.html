legal tender

noun
official money accepted by law for payments
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈliɡəl ˈtɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈliːɡl̩ ˈtɛndə / (BrE)
coins or banknotes that must be accepted if offered in payment of a debt, according to the law of the country.

Examples

Legal tender coins
Coins and banknotes are the only forms of legal tender in most countries.
Old currency notes are no longer recognized as legal tender after the government's announcement.
The shopkeeper refused to accept checks as legal tender for the purchase.

Related Terms

  • synonym: currency - system of money in general use in a country
  • synonym: fiat money - money that has no intrinsic value but is accepted as legal tender by government decree
