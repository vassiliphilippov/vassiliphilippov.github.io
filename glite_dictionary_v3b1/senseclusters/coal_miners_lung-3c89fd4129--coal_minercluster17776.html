coal miner's lung

noun
lung disease from coal dust exposure
Pronunciation: / koʊl ˈmaɪnərz lʌŋ / (AmE), / kəʊl ˈmaɪnəz lʌŋ / (BrE)
A lung disease caused by prolonged inhalation of coal dust, particularly affecting coal miners; also known as black lung disease or pneumoconiosis.

Examples

Coal miner's lung disease
Early diagnosis of coal miner's lung is essential for preventing further respiratory damage.
Many retired miners suffer from coal miner's lung due to years spent underground.
Prolonged exposure to coal dust can eventually cause coal miner's lung in workers.
