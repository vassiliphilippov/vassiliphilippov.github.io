individuate

verb
to make distinct or individual
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdjʊeɪt /; / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdjueɪt / (BrE)
to give something a unique or individual character; to make it distinct from others

Examples

To individuate personal traits
Education should help individuate students by nurturing their personal strengths.
The artist sought to individuate each sculpture by adding unique details.
The therapist worked to individuate her clients' identities apart from family expectations.
Careful observation helps teachers individuate the needs of every student in a diverse classroom.
Psychological theories often seek to individuate each person’s experience within a broader framework.
The sculptor aimed to individuate each statue by giving it a unique expression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: differentiate - to recognize or create differences between things
  • synonym: identify - to recognize someone or something as being a particular person or thing
