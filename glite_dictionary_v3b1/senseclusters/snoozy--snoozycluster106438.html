snoozy

adjective
sleepy or dull
Pronunciation: / ˈsnuːzi / (AmE), / ˈsnuːzi / (BrE)
feeling sleepy or causing sleepiness; drowsy or dull

Examples

A snoozy afternoon
After lunch, I always feel a bit snoozy and find it hard to concentrate.
The movie was so snoozy that half the audience started yawning.
He gave a snoozy presentation that failed to capture anyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drowsy - feeling tired and likely to fall asleep
  • antonym: lively - full of energy and excitement
  • synonym: sleepy - feeling ready to sleep
