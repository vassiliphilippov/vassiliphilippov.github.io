snoozy

adjective
sleepy or dull
4.6 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsnuÀêzi / (AmE), / ÀàsnuÀêzi / (BrE)
feeling sleepy or causing sleepiness; drowsy or dull

Examples

A snoozy afternoon
After lunch, I always feel a bit snoozy and find it hard to concentrate.
The movie was so snoozy that half the audience started yawning.
He gave a snoozy presentation that failed to capture anyone's attention.

Synonyms

  • sleepy
    1.0 8.04/m
    sleepy feels ready to fall asleep and shows tiredness, while snoozy often also means dull or causes sleepiness.
  • drowsy
    2.7 1.74/m
    drowsy means feeling heavy and not fully awake from tiredness or boredom, while snoozy can also suggest being dull.
  • dozy
    4.3 0.03/m
    dozy is slightly half awake and informal, often sounding silly, while snoozy focuses more on sleepiness and dullness.
