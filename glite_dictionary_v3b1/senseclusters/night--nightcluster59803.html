night

noun
figurative: period of ignorance or gloom
2.8
Pronunciation: / naɪt / (AmE), / naɪt / (BrE)
A period characterized by ignorance, backwardness, or general gloom, used figuratively.

Examples

A long night period
He spoke of the long night under dictatorship, when knowledge was suppressed.
Many view the Middle Ages as a night from which Europe eventually awoke.
The discovery ended the night of confusion that had shrouded the field for years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: darkness - a state of ignorance or lack of enlightenment
  • antonym: enlightenment - a period of increased knowledge or understanding
