hine

noun
farm laborer (archaic)
Pronunciation: / haɪn / (AmE), / haɪn / (BrE)
an old term for a farm servant or laborer, especially one working on a rural estate

Examples

Estate hine
The wealthy landowner employed a loyal hine to tend the fields year-round.
In medieval times, a hine would often live on the estate where he worked.
The duties of a hine included caring for livestock and maintaining the crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: farmhand - a person who works on a farm
  • synonym: laborer - a person doing unskilled manual work
  • antonym: landowner - a person who owns land, especially a large amount
