elide

verb
omit a sound or syllable
5.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlaɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈlaɪd / (BrE)
to omit or leave out a sound, syllable, or letter when speaking or writing a word

Examples

To elide sounds
In casual speech, people often elide the "d" in "handbag".
Linguists note that speakers may elide certain sounds for ease of pronunciation.
Some English poets elided unstressed vowels to fit the meter of their poems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insert - to put in or include something; to add
  • synonym: omit - to leave out or exclude something
