Quercus laurifolia

noun
species of semi-evergreen oak tree in the southeastern US
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜrkəs lɔːˈrɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs lɔːˈrɪfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A large, nearly semi-evergreen oak tree of the southeastern United States that thrives in damp soil; commonly known as the laurel oak.

Examples

The Quercus laurifolia tree
Botanists value Quercus laurifolia for its resilience and distinctive laurel-like foliage.
Many parks in Florida feature mature Quercus laurifolia trees providing shade and habitat for wildlife.
The Quercus laurifolia is well adapted to swampy areas and urban landscaping in the southeastern United States.
