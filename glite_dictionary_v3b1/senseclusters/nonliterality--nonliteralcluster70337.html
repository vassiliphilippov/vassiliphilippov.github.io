nonliterality

noun
lack of literary style
9.9
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.lɪt.ə.ˈræl.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /nɒn.lɪt.ə.ˈræl.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality or state of not having literary style, characteristics, or artistic qualities associated with literature

Examples

Nonliterality in writing
The report's nonliterality made it easy to understand but less engaging to read.
Critics noted the nonliterality of the biography, saying it lacked artistic expression.
Some readers prefer the nonliterality of straightforward news articles over poetic novels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: literalness - the quality of being strictly factual or non-artistic
  • antonym: literariness - the quality of having literary style or artistic qualities
