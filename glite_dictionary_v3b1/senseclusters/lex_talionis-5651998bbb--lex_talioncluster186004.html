lex talionis

noun
law of retaliation
Pronunciation: / lɛks ˌtæliˈoʊnɪs / (AmE), / lɛks ˌtalɪˈəʊnɪs / (BrE)
the legal principle that the punishment should be equal to the offense, often expressed as 'an eye for an eye'

Examples

Lex talionis principle
The ancient code of Hammurabi is often cited as an example of lex talionis in practice.
Some argue that lex talionis is too harsh for modern justice systems.
The company adopted a policy of lex talionis in response to unfair trade practices.
The ancient code was based on the principle of lex talionis, demanding equal retribution for wrongdoing.
Many legal scholars debate whether lex talionis is a just form of punishment in modern society.
The phrase 'an eye for an eye' is a classic example of lex talionis in action.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forgiveness - the act of letting go of resentment or revenge
  • synonym: retaliation - the act of returning an injury or wrong with a similar action
  • synonym: retribution - punishment considered to be morally right and fully deserved
  • synonym: revenge - the act of harming someone in return for a perceived wrong
