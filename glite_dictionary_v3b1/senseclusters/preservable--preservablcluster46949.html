preservable

adjective
able to be preserved
4.1
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzɝːvəbəl / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːvəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being preserved, especially without losing quality or usefulness.

Examples

Preservable foods
Only preservable foods can be shipped long distances without spoiling.
The artwork is highly preservable due to the materials used by the artist.
They invested in developing preservable vaccines for distribution in remote areas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: perishable - liable to spoil or decay
