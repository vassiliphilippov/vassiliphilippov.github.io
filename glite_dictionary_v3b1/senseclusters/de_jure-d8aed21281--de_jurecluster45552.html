de jure

adverb
by law; legally recognized
8.5
Pronunciation: / deɪ ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / deɪ ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
According to law; by right; legally recognized, regardless of whether the situation exists in fact.

Examples

De jure recognized
Although he was the de jure owner of the company, his partner managed all its operations.
Racial segregation was abolished de jure, but remained in practice for many years.
Their marriage was not recognized de jure until new laws were passed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: de facto - in fact; in reality, even if not legally recognized
