common iliac vein

noun
major pelvic vein forming part of the inferior vena cava
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ɪˈlaɪ.ək veɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ɪˈlaɪ.ək veɪn / (BrE)
either of the two large veins formed by the joining of the internal and external iliac veins on each side of the body, which unite to form the inferior vena cava.

Examples

The common iliac vein
A blood clot in the common iliac vein can cause serious complications if not treated promptly.
Both left and right common iliac veins join to form the inferior vena cava.
The surgeon located the common iliac vein before proceeding with the operation.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: external iliac vein - the vein that joins with the internal iliac vein to form the common iliac vein
  • degree higher: inferior vena cava - the large vein formed by the union of the two common iliac veins
