minor role

noun
small or less important part or function
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnɚ roʊl / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə rəʊl / (BrE)
a small or less important part in a play, film, or other activity or situation

Examples

A minor role in film
Despite her minor role, she made a lasting impression on the audience.
He was cast in a minor role for his first film appearance.
Many actors start their careers with a minor role before moving on to larger parts.
Despite her minor role in politics, her opinions were widely respected.
He was disappointed to only have a minor role in the school play.
The actor gained experience by accepting several minor roles early in his career.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lead role - the most important part in a play or performance
  • antonym: major role - a large or important part in a play, film, etc.
  • degree higher: supporting role - a role more important than a minor role but less than the lead
