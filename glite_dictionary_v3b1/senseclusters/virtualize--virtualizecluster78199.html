virtualize

verb
to create digital version
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝːtʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtʃuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to create a virtual (digital) version of something, especially computer hardware, systems, or resources, using software

Examples

To virtualize computer systems
Many companies virtualize their servers to reduce hardware costs and improve flexibility.
The IT department decided to virtualize the desktop environment for easier management.
By virtualizing storage, organizations can use their resources more efficiently.
Many companies virtualize their servers to save costs and increase flexibility.
The IT department decided to virtualize the old hardware to improve system management.
By virtualizing desktops, employees can access their work environment from anywhere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emulate - to imitate the function of another system, often in software
  • antonym: physicalize - to make something physical or tangible
