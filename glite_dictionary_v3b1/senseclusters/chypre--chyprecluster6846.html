chypre

adjective
perfume style
Pronunciation: / ˈʃiːprə / (AmE), / ˈʃiːprə / (BrE)
belonging to or characteristic of the chypre family of perfumes, typically with citrus top notes and a mossy, woody base.

Examples

Chypre scent style
She preferred a chypre fragrance for its elegant blend of citrus and earthy notes.
The new cologne has a distinctly chypre character, making it stand out from sweeter scents.
Many classic perfumes are described as chypre because of their mossy, woody undertones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: floral - having the scent or characteristics of flowers
  • synonym: woody - having the scent or characteristics of wood
