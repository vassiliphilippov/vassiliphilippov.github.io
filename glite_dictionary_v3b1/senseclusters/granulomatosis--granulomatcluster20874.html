granulomatosis

noun
disease with granulomas
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɡræn.jə.loʊˈmeɪ.t̬oʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌɡran.jʊ.ləʊˈməʊ.təʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by the formation of granulomas, which are small areas of inflammation in tissues

Examples

Granulomatosis disease
The doctor diagnosed the patient with granulomatosis after reviewing the biopsy results.
Treatment for granulomatosis often involves medications to reduce inflammation.
Researchers are studying the causes of granulomatosis to develop better therapies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sarcoidosis - a disease involving abnormal collections of inflammatory cells called granulomas
  • synonym: tuberculosis - an infectious disease that often causes granulomas in the lungs
