fail to impress

verb
not make a favorable impression
Pronunciation: / feɪl tə ɪmˈprɛs / (AmE), / feɪl tʊ ɪmˈprɛs / (BrE)
to not succeed in making someone feel admiration or approval; to not make a noticeable or favorable impression

Examples

To fail to impress judges
Despite his expensive suit, his presentation failed to impress the hiring committee.
Her performance was professional but failed to impress the judges enough to advance.
The film received good reviews but ultimately failed to impress audiences at the box office.

  • antonym: impress - to make someone feel admiration and respect
