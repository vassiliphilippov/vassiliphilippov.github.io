hardship

noun
severe suffering or difficulty
3.4 10/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ërdÀå É…™p / (AmE), / Ààh…ëÀêd É…™p / (BrE)
a situation of severe suffering or difficulty, especially because of lack of money, necessities, or because of misfortune

Examples

Severe hardships
Despite the hardships they faced, the community remained united and hopeful.
Many families experienced great hardship during the long winter months.
She remembers her childhood as a time marked by hardship and perseverance.
After the factory closed, many families faced unexpected hardship in the months that followed.
Despite the hardship of living in a foreign country, she remained optimistic and determined to succeed.
The community came together to support those experiencing financial hardship during the crisis.
After losing his job, he experienced great hardship while trying to make ends meet.
Many people face financial hardship during a recession.
She overcame every hardship through determination and hard work.

  • dire straits

    dire straits describe a very serious financial or personal crisis, while hardship names a situation of suffering from need or misfortune.
  • plight
    6.5 7.58/m

  • deprivation
    6.2 7.08/m

  • predicament
