raffia

noun
a palm tree grown for its strong leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈræfiə / (AmE), / ˈræfiə / (BrE)
a tropical palm tree of Africa, Madagascar, and the Americas, cultivated commercially for its strong, fibrous leaves

Examples

Raffia palm tree
Artisans often use dried raffia leaves to make baskets and mats.
The raffia is widely cultivated for its tough, fibrous leaves used in weaving.
The plantation focused on growing raffia for export to the textile industry.
