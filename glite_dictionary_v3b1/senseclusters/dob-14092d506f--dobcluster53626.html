DOB

noun
informer, tattletale
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / dÉ’b / (BrE)
an informer; a person who reports others' wrongdoing to the authorities, especially in British and Australian contexts

Examples

Called a DOB
Nobody trusted him after he was called a DOB by his classmates.
The gang was caught because a DOB told the police about their plans.
In some communities, being labeled a DOB can lead to social exclusion.

Synonyms

  • whistle-blower
    5.0 2.26/m
    whistle-blower reports serious wrongdoing to bosses or the public for safety, while DOB is an informal, often negative term for someone who tattles to authorities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime
  • synonym: informer - a person who secretly gives information about someone to the authorities
  • synonym: snitch - someone who tells the authorities about others' wrongdoing
