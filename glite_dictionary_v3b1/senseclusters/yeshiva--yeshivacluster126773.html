yeshiva

noun
Jewish religious academy
Pronunciation: / jəˈʃivə / (AmE), / jəˈʃiːvə / (BrE)
an academy for the advanced study of Jewish texts, especially the Talmud, typically for the training of rabbis

Examples

A yeshiva academy
After graduating high school, he spent a year studying at a yeshiva in Jerusalem.
Many rabbis begin their formal training in a yeshiva environment.
The yeshiva offers intensive courses in Talmudic law and Jewish philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seminary - a college for training clergy
