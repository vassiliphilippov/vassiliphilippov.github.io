scull

noun
racing boat propelled by sculls
8.0
Pronunciation: / skʌl / (AmE), / skʌl / (BrE)
a narrow, light racing boat that is propelled using pairs of oars called sculls, usually by one or more rowers

Examples

A racing scull
Each scull at the regatta was built for speed and agility.
He repaired his cracked scull before the next big race.
The rower took her scull out on the river for early morning practice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shell - a long, narrow racing boat used in competitive rowing
