tired and emotional

adjective
euphemism implying intoxicated
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtaɪ.ərd ənd ɪ.ˈmoʊ.ʃən.əl/ /; / /taɪrd ənd ɪ.ˈmoʊ.ʃən.əl/ (AmE), /ˈtaɪ.əd ənd ɪ.ˈməʊ.ʃən.əl/ /; / /taɪəd ənd ɪ.ˈməʊ.ʃən.əl/ (BrE)
used euphemistically (especially in British English) to suggest that someone is intoxicated and behaving emotionally, without saying they were drunk.

Examples

Tired and emotional after drinking
The tabloid described the celebrity as tired and emotional after leaving the party late.
At the hearing, witnesses said he seemed tired and emotional, implying he had been drinking earlier.
Reporters used the phrase tired and emotional to avoid explicitly saying the politician was intoxicated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drunk - intoxicated with alcohol; unable to behave normally because of drinking.
  • antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not intoxicated.
