keepable

adjective
suitable for captivity
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːpəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkiːpəbəl / (BrE)
suitable to be kept, especially in captivity or as a pet, and able to live under human care

Examples

Keepable animals
Some wild animals are not keepable because they cannot adapt to life in captivity.
The zookeeper explained which species are considered keepable as pets and which are not.
After careful assessment, the rescued bird was found to be keepable and placed in a sanctuary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: domesticable - able to be tamed or adapted for living with humans
  • antonym: unmanageable - difficult or impossible to control or keep
