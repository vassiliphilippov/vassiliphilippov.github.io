deputize

verb
appoint as substitute or representative
6.0 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdep.jə.taɪz/ /; / /ˈdep.juː.taɪz/ (AmE), /ˈdep.jə.taɪz/ /; / /ˈdep.juː.taɪz/ (BrE)
to appoint someone to act as a substitute or to act as a substitute for someone, especially in an official or temporary way

Examples

To deputize an assistant
In his absence, he will deputize his assistant to run the meeting.
She was deputized to represent her manager at the conference.
The sheriff decided to deputize several townspeople during the emergency.
If the team leader is absent, someone else must deputize at the meeting.
She will deputize for the principal at tomorrow's assembly.
The assistant manager was asked to deputize for the boss during her vacation.
During the concert, Ben agreed to deputise for the lead guitarist who was ill.
The teacher needed someone to deputise in her absence the following week.
When the manager was away, Jane was asked to deputise for him at the meeting.
