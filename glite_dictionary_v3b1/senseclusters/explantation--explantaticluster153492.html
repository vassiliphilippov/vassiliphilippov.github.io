explantation

noun
removal of tissue
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksplænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing tissue or an organ from a living organism for study, culture, or transplantation

Examples

Explantation of tissue
The surgeon performed an explantation to remove the damaged heart valve for further analysis.
After the explantation of the tissue sample, researchers placed it in a nutrient solution for culture.
Successful explantation is essential for studying the effects of drugs on living cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excision - the act of cutting out or removing tissue
  • antonym: implantation - the process of inserting tissue or an organ into a body
