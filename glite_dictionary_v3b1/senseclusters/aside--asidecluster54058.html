aside

noun
theatrical remark to audience
9.1
Pronunciation: / əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
a remark or comment that is not part of the main discussion, especially in a play or conversation

Examples

A quick aside
In Shakespeare's plays, characters often speak an aside to reveal their true feelings.
She addressed the audience with an aside, sharing her secret intentions.
The actor delivered a witty aside that made the audience laugh.
During the meeting, she made an aside about the weather that made everyone laugh.
He couldn't resist a humorous aside during his otherwise serious speech.
The play features several asides that reveal the main character's true feelings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing
  • synonym: soliloquy - an act of speaking one's thoughts aloud when by oneself or regardless of any hearers, especially by a character in a play
