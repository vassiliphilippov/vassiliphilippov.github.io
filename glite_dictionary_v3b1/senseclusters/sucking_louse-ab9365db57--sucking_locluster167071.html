sucking louse

noun
parasitic bloodsucking insect
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌkɪŋ laʊs / (AmE), / ˈsʌkɪŋ laʊs / (BrE)
A small, wingless, often flattened insect that lives as a parasite on warm-blooded animals and feeds on their blood.

Examples

A sucking louse parasite
A sucking louse can cause irritation and anemia in livestock if not controlled.
Birds and mammals are common hosts for the sucking louse due to their warm-blooded nature.
The veterinarian identified the parasite as a sucking louse, which requires immediate treatment.
