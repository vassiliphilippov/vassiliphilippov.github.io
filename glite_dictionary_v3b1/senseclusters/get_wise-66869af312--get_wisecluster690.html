get wise

verb
come to understand, often after difficulty
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt waɪz / (AmE), / ɡɛt waɪz / (BrE)
to come to understand or realize something, especially after being unaware or deceived at first

Examples

To get wise quickly
After getting scammed once, he got wise and now double-checks everything.
It took her a while, but she finally got wise to his tricks.
You'd better get wise before it's too late and someone takes advantage of you.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catch on - to understand or realize something, often after a delay
  • synonym: wise up - to become aware of the truth about a situation
