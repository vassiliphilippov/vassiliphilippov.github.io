orthochromatic film

noun
photographic film sensitive to blue and green light
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθoʊkroʊˈmætɪk fɪlm / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊkrəʊˈmætɪk fɪlm / (BrE)
a type of photographic film that is sensitive to blue, violet, and green light, but not to red light

Examples

Orthochromatic film sensitivity
Because orthochromatic film isn't sensitive to red light, skies appear very bright in black-and-white prints.
Early 20th-century landscapes often used orthochromatic film, which rendered foliage with enhanced contrast.
Photographers once relied on orthochromatic film before the invention of modern panchromatic emulsions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: panchromatic film - photographic film sensitive to all visible wavelengths of light
