mountain

noun
large natural elevation of land
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊntn̩ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn / (BrE)
A very large natural elevation of the earth's surface rising abruptly from the surrounding level; higher and steeper than a hill.

Examples

Climb a mountain
After hours of hiking, they finally reached the summit of the mountain.
She dreamed of climbing a famous mountain one day.
Snow covered the peaks of every mountain visible from the village.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high or steep as a mountain
