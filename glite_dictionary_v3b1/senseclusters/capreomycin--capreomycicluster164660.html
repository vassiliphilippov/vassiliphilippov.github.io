capreomycin

noun
antibiotic for tuberculosis
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌkæpriːoʊˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌkæpriːəʊˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic medication used to treat certain types of tuberculosis, especially when other drugs are ineffective

Examples

Capreomycin treatment
The patient received capreomycin as part of a treatment plan for drug-resistant tuberculosis.
Doctors may prescribe capreomycin when other antibiotics fail to cure the infection.
Side effects of capreomycin can include kidney problems and hearing loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isoniazid - a medication commonly used to treat tuberculosis
  • synonym: streptomycin - an antibiotic used to treat tuberculosis and other infections
