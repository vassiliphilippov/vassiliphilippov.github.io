Pizarro

proper noun
Spanish conquistador; conqueror of the Incas

(Francisco Pizarro) A Spanish conquistador (1475–1541) who conquered the Inca Empire in Peru and founded the city of Lima.

Examples

Pizarro the conquistador
Pizarro led the expedition that ultimately destroyed the Inca Empire in South America.
After defeating Atahualpa, Pizarro founded the city of Lima in 1535.
Many historians consider Pizarro one of the most influential conquistadors of the Age of Exploration.
