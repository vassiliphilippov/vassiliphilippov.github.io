Cnidaria

noun
phylum of stinging aquatic animals
Pronunciation: / naɪˈdɛriə / (AmE), / naɪˈdɛːriə / (BrE)
A phylum of aquatic animals that includes hydras, jellyfish, sea anemones, and corals, characterized by the presence of specialized stinging cells.

Examples

The Cnidaria phylum
Jellyfish, which belong to the Cnidaria phylum, drift gracefully through the ocean.
Scientists study Cnidaria to learn more about nervous system evolution.
The coral reefs are formed by colonies of Cnidaria known as corals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coelenterate - an older term for animals of the phylum Cnidaria
