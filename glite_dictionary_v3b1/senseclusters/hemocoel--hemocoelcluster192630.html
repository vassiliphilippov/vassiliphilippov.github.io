hemocoel

noun
blood-filled cavity
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːməˌsiːl / (AmE), / ˈhiːməˌsiːl / (BrE)
an abnormal cavity in the body filled with blood, usually formed by injury or disease rather than as a normal body cavity

Examples

Abnormal hemocoel
The surgeon drained the haemocoel to prevent further complications after the accident.
A large haemocoel developed in the tissue following the traumatic injury.
Doctors monitored the patient's haemocoel closely to ensure it did not become infected.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abscess - a swollen area in body tissue filled with pus
  • synonym: hematoma - a localized collection of blood outside blood vessels
