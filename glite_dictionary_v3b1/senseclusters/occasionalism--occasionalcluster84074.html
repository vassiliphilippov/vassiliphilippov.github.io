occasionalism

noun
theory of divine causation
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.keɪ.ʒə.ˈnæl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ə.ˈkeɪ.ʒə.nə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ə.keɪ.ʒə.ˈnæl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ə.ˈkeɪ.ʒə.nə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a philosophical theory that claims God is the only true cause of all events, with created things serving only as occasions for divine action

Examples

Occasionalism theory
According to occasionalism, natural causes are only occasions for God's direct intervention.
Some philosophers debated occasionalism as an explanation for the relationship between mind and body.
The doctrine of occasionalism was influential in 17th-century philosophical discussions about causation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: determinism - the belief that all events are determined by previously existing causes
  • synonym: theism - belief in the existence of a god or gods, especially a creator
