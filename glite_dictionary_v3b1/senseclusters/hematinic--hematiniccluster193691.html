hematinic

noun
medicine that boosts blood hemoglobin
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛməˈtɪnɪk /; / hiːməˈtɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈtɪnɪk /; / hiːməˈtɪnɪk / (BrE)
a medicine that increases the hemoglobin content of the blood, used to treat iron-deficiency anemia or other blood disorders

Examples

A hematinic medicine
Long-term use of a hematinic can significantly improve red blood cell counts in anemic individuals.
Many iron supplements act as effective hematinics for patients with low hemoglobin levels.
The doctor prescribed a hematinic to help correct her iron-deficiency anemia.
After several weeks of taking a haematinic, his blood tests showed significant improvement.
Some patients require a daily haematinic to maintain healthy hemoglobin levels.
The doctor prescribed a haematinic to help with her iron-deficiency anemia.
