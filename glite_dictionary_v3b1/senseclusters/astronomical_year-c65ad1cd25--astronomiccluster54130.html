astronomical year

noun
year measured from vernal equinox to equinox
7.6 4.7
Pronunciation: /æ.strə.ˈnɑː.mɪ.kəl jɪr/ (AmE), /æ.strə.ˈnɒ.mɪ.kəl jɪə/ (BrE)
The period of time it takes the Earth to complete one revolution around the Sun, measured from one vernal equinox to the next.

Examples

The astronomical year length
Ancient astronomers used the astronomical year to calculate the calendar.
The difference between a calendar year and an astronomical year can affect the timing of seasons.
The length of an astronomical year is approximately 365.2422 days.
