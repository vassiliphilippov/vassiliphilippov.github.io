ruderal

adjective
growing in disturbed areas
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈruːdərəl / (AmE), / ˈruːdərəl / (BrE)
describing plants or species that grow in disturbed or waste areas, especially where the soil has been disrupted

Examples

Ruderal plants in cities
Many ruderal species thrive on roadsides and in abandoned construction sites.
The garden was quickly overtaken by ruderal plants after the soil was disturbed.
Urban ecologists study how ruderal vegetation colonizes empty lots in cities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: weedy - growing like a weed, especially in disturbed places
