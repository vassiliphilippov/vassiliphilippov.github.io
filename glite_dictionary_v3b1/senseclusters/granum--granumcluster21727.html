granum

noun
stack of thylakoids
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪnəm / (BrE)
a stack of thylakoids within the chloroplast of plant cells, important in photosynthesis

Examples

Chloroplast granum
A granum is essential for capturing light energy during photosynthesis in plant cells.
Scientists observed that each granum consists of several stacked thylakoids.
Damage to a granum can reduce the efficiency of photosynthesis in leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thylakoid - a membrane-bound compartment inside chloroplasts
