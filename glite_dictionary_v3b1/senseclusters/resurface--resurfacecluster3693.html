resurface

verb
put a new surface on something
3.9 0.41/m
Pronunciation: / riÀàs…úrf…™s / (AmE), / riÀàs…úÀêf…™s / (BrE)
to put a new surface on something, especially a road or floor

Examples

To resurface a road
After years of wear, the wooden floor had to be resurfaced to look new again.
The city plans to resurface the main road next summer.
They decided to resurface the tennis court before the tournament.

Synonyms

  • repave
    3.5 0.20/m
    repave means replacing a road's surface with new asphalt, while resurface can mean renewing floors or roads more broadly.
  • refinish
    6.1 0.66/m
    refinish means stripping an old finish and applying a new coat to restore floors or furniture, while resurface refers to renewing many surfaces.
  • reface
    6.7 0.02/m
    reface means covering a front surface with new material like thin wood to change the look, while resurface usually means renewing floors or roads.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pave - to cover a surface, especially a road, with flat stones, bricks, or concrete
