Lilith

noun
female demon in Semitic folklore
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈlɪlɪθ / (BrE)
In ancient Semitic folklore, a female demon believed to attack children, often depicted as a night spirit or the first wife of Adam in later traditions.

Examples

Lilith demon legend
According to legend, Lilith haunted the nights and preyed upon newborns.
Some stories describe Lilith as Adam's rebellious first wife who became a demon.
The myth of Lilith has influenced many works of literature and art.

Related Terms
