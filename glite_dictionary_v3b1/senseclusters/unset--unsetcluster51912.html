unset

adjective
not fixed or assigned
5.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɛt / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛt / (BrE)
not fixed, attached, established, or assigned; not yet made firm, mounted, or given a value.

Examples

An unset stone
The jeweller showed her an expensive, unset stone waiting to be placed in a ring.
The value for the password field remains unset until the user enters information.
After the storm, several fence posts were left unset and needed to be secured.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fixed - firmly attached or established
  • synonym: unattached - not fastened or connected
