grosbeak

noun
type of finch with a large bill
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡroʊsˌbik / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊsˌbiːk / (BrE)
a type of finch found in Europe or America, with a large, strong bill for cracking seeds

Examples

A grosbeak bird
Because of its strong bill, the grosbeak can crack open tough seeds with ease.
The evening grosbeak is known for its bright yellow markings.
We spotted a rose-breasted grosbeak near the bird feeder yesterday.
A grosbeak used its powerful beak to crack open sunflower seeds at the feeder.
Several species of grosbeaks migrate long distances in search of food.
The evening grosbeak is known for its striking yellow and black plumage.
