obelisk

noun
symbol for second footnote or reference
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊbəlɪsk / (AmE), / ˈɒbəlɪsk / (BrE)
A symbol (†) used in printing or writing to indicate a second footnote or reference, usually after an asterisk has been used.

Examples

Obelisk symbol in text
The obelisk in the text shows that there is a secondary footnote with more information.
When the asterisk is already used, editors often rely on the obelisk for additional references.
You will see an obelisk next to terms that require further explanation in the appendix.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dagger - the typographical symbol (†) used for footnotes
