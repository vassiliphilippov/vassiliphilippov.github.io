the philosophical doctrine that reality is fundamentally composed of substances or material things rather than processes or relations

Examples

Substantialism in philosophy
In classical philosophy, substantialism argues that objects exist independently of our perception.
Some philosophers reject substantialism in favor of theories that focus on change and process.
The debate between substantialism and process philosophy continues in modern metaphysics.

Synonyms

  • materialism
    7.4 1.05/m
    materialism claims only physical matter exists and explains minds by matter, while substantialism sees reality as substances, not processes.
  • physicalism
    9.9 1.99/m
    physicalism holds everything is physical and may include structures or laws, while substantialism insists reality is made of individual substances, not relations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: materialism - the belief that only physical matter truly exists
  • antonym: process philosophy - the view that processes, not substances, are the fundamental elements of reality
