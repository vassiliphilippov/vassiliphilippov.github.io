ghatti

noun
gum from the Indian dhawa tree
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈt̬i / (AmE), / ɡəˈtiː / (BrE)
a gum obtained from the Indian dhawa tree, used as a substitute for gum arabic in food and industry

Examples

Ghatti gum
Many manufacturers use ghatti as a thickener in candies and soft drinks.
Researchers found that ghatti can effectively replace gum arabic in many recipes.
The export of ghatti from India has increased due to global demand for natural gums.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gum arabic - an edible gum from the acacia tree, widely used in food and industry
