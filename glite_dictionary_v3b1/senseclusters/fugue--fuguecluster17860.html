fugue

noun
a contrapuntal musical form
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːɡ / (AmE), / ˈfjuːɡ / (BrE)
A musical composition in which a short melody or phrase (the subject) is introduced by one part and successively taken up by others and developed by interweaving the parts.

Examples

A Bach fugue
Bach composed some of his greatest works in the form of a fugue.
She studied the structure of a fugue for her music theory class.
The orchestra performed a complex fugue during the concert, captivating the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canon - a musical form in which a melody is imitated by one or more voices at a fixed interval
