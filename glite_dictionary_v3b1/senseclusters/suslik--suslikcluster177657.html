suslik

noun
Eurasian ground squirrel
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːslɪk / (AmE), / ˈsuːslɪk / (BrE)
a large ground squirrel native to central Eurasia, known for living in burrows and hibernating in winter

Examples

A suslik in burrow
Farmers sometimes consider the suslik a pest because it can damage crops with its burrowing.
In central Asia, the suslik is often seen sitting upright near its burrow, keeping watch for predators.
The suslik spends much of the winter hibernating underground to survive the cold months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ground squirrel - a burrowing rodent of the squirrel family
