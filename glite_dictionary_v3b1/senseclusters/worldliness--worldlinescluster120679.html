worldliness

noun
sophistication and practical experience in worldly affairs
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːld.li.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːld.li.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being experienced, sophisticated, and knowledgeable about life and worldly matters, often as a result of education or experience

Examples

Urban worldliness
Despite his youth, his worldliness was evident in how he handled complex situations.
Her worldliness made her an insightful advisor to those less experienced.
The novel captures the worldliness of someone who has traveled extensively and seen much of life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • synonym: sophistication - having a refined knowledge of the world
