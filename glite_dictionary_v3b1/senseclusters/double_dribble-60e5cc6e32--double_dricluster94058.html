double dribble

noun
illegal basketball move (dribbling)
5.5 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdʌb.əl ˈdrɪb.əl/ (AmE), /ˈdʌb.əl ˈdrɪb.əl/ (BrE)
an illegal move in basketball where a player either dribbles the ball with both hands at once or starts dribbling again after stopping

Examples

A double dribble foul
A double dribble occurs when a player dribbles, stops, and then starts dribbling again.
Coaches teach young players the rules to avoid committing a double dribble during games.
The referee called a double dribble on the point guard, and possession was turned over.
