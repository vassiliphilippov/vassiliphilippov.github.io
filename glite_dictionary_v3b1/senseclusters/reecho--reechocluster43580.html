reecho

verb
to echo or be echoed again
7.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / riÀà…õko ä / (AmE), / riÀêÀàek…ô ä /; / riÀêÀà…õk…ô ä / (BrE)
(of a sound) to be echoed again or repeatedly; to be reflected back like an echo

Examples

To reecho loudly
Her laughter reechoed down the marble hallway, lingering long after she was gone.
The cheers in the stadium reechoed throughout the night.
Thunder reechoed between the mountains, making the whole valley vibrate.
His words reechoed in her mind as she walked through the silent house.
The mountains reecho every peal of thunder during the summer storms.
Their laughter reechoed through the empty hallways long after they had left.
Her laughter reechoes in my mind whenever I enter that room.
The old halls reechoed with memories of celebrations past.
The sound of the gunshot reechoed through the valley long after it was fired.

Synonyms

  • resound
    5.3 1/m

  • resonate
    5.1 2/m
