Chanticleer

proper noun
fabled rooster name
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæntɪˌklɪr / (AmE), / ˈʃæntɪklɪə / (BrE)
the name of a proud rooster character in medieval fables, especially in Chaucer's 'The Nun's Priest's Tale'

Examples

Chanticleer the rooster
In Chaucer's tale, Chanticleer is known for his beautiful crowing and cleverness.
The fable describes how Chanticleer outsmarts the cunning fox.
Many medieval stories feature Chanticleer as a symbol of pride and wit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rooster - an adult male chicken
