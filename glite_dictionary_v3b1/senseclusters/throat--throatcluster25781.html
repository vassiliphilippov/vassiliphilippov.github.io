throat

noun
narrow passage in a structure or object
Pronunciation: / θroʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊt / (BrE)
a narrow passage in a structure or object that resembles the human throat in shape or function

Examples

Tunnel throat
Engineers examined the throat of the tunnel for potential blockages.
Smoke poured quickly through the throat of the chimney as the fire burned.
The narrow throat of the bottle made it difficult to pour the liquid.
