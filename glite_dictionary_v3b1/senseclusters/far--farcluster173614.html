far

adjective
at a great distance; remote
1.0 23/m
Pronunciation: / f…ër / (AmE), / f…ëÀê / (BrE)
located at a great distance in space, time, or degree; remote or distant

Examples

A far country
After a far journey, they finally reached the campsite at sunset.
The mountain peak seemed impossibly far for us to reach before nightfall.
The village was so far from the city that it took hours to drive there.
The far side of the river was thick with trees.
They lived in a far village, several miles from the nearest town.
We will not see real change until the far future.

Synonyms

  • distant
    2.8 24/m
    distant is used for things far away in space or time, while far is a more general word for distance.
  • far-off
    4.2 2.77/m
    far-off describes something very distant in space or time, while far is more general and common in speech.
  • faraway
    2.8 2.73/m
    faraway paints a picture of places far from here or long ago, while far is a plain, flexible term.
  • remote
    3.6 20/m
    remote means isolated and often hard to reach, while far simply describes great distance in space, time, or degree.
  • far-flung
    6.3 1.91/m
    far-flung suggests things spread over a very wide area, while far just refers to great distance.
