cark

verb
disturb or worry (archaic)
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / k…ërk / (AmE), / k…ëÀêk / (BrE)
(archaic) To disturb, worry, or cause unease in someone's mind.

Examples

Troubles cark someone
Financial troubles cark him night and day, leaving him restless.
It did cark her heart to hear such dreadful news.
The burden of secrecy began to cark the young squire's conscience.
