bloc

noun
allied group of countries or parties
4.8 8.05/m
Pronunciation: / blÉ‘k / (AmE), / blÉ’k / (BrE)
A group of countries, political parties, or groups that have joined together for a common purpose or in an alliance.

Examples

A bloc of countries
After World War II, Eastern European countries formed a Communist bloc aligned with the Soviet Union.
Several nations created a new economic bloc to increase cooperation and competitiveness.
The European Union is considered a powerful bloc in international trade negotiations.

Synonyms

  • coalition
    4.3 42/m
    a coalition brings different groups together for a specific goal and is often temporary, while a bloc is a more stable alliance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alliance - a union or association formed for mutual benefit, especially between countries or organizations
  • synonym: coalition - a temporary alliance for combined action, especially of political parties forming a government
