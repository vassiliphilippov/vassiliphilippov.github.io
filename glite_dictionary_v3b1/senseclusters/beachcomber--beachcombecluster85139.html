beachcomber

noun
person living idly on a beach
Pronunciation: / ˈbitʃˌkoʊmər / (AmE), / ˈbiːtʃˌkəʊmə / (BrE)
a person who lives or spends time idly on a beach, often searching for items of value among things washed up by the sea; historically, a vagrant living on a beach

Examples

A beachcomber on shore
During the 19th century, many beachcombers found themselves living on remote South Sea islands.
He lived as a beachcomber for years, surviving on whatever the tide brought in.
The old beachcomber spent his days collecting shells and driftwood along the shore.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vagrant - a person with no settled home or regular work who wanders from place to place
