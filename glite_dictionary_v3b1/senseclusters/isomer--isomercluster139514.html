isomer

noun
compound with same formula, different structure
4.5 6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪ.sə.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sə.mə / (BrE)
a chemical compound with the same molecular formula as another but with a different arrangement of atoms in the molecule

Examples

Chemical isomers
Glucose and fructose are isomers because they share the same molecular formula but have different structures.
Some plastics are made by combining different isomers of hydrocarbons.
The drug's effectiveness depends on which isomer is present in the medication.
