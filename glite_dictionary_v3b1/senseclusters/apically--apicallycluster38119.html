apically

adverb
with tongue tip
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪpɪkli / (AmE), / ˈapɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner involving or using the tip (apex) of the tongue, especially in phonetics.

Examples

Spoken apically
The sound is articulated apically, with the tip of the tongue touching the alveolar ridge.
Some languages distinguish between apically and laminally produced consonants.
The linguist noted that the 't' in this dialect is pronounced apically.

Related Terms

  • antonym: laminally - in a manner using the blade of the tongue
