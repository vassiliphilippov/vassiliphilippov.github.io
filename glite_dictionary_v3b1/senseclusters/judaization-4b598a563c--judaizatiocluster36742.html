Judaization

noun
making more Jewish
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒuːdəaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːdəaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more Jewish in character, culture, or influence

Examples

Judaization of culture
The Judaization of the neighborhood was evident in the new cultural centers and synagogues.
Some historians debate the extent of Judaization in ancient societies influenced by Jewish traditions.
The Judaization of local customs led to significant changes in holiday celebrations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assimilation - the process of becoming similar to another group or culture
