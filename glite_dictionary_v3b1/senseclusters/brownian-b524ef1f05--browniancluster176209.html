Brownian

adjective
random particle movement
Pronunciation: / ˈbraʊniən / (AmE), / ˈbraʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the random movement of small particles (Brownian motion) in science or mathematics

Examples

Brownian motion
The scientist observed Brownian motion under the microscope as tiny particles moved unpredictably.
Many financial models use Brownian processes to represent the random behavior of stock prices.
The Brownian movement of dust in the air can be seen in a beam of sunlight.
The Brownian movement of pollen grains was visible under the microscope.
Physicists study Brownian motion to understand the behavior of particles in liquids.
The unpredictable path of dust in water is an example of Brownian motion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deterministic - not involving randomness; fully determined by initial conditions
  • synonym: random - happening without a predictable pattern or plan
  • synonym: stochastic - involving a random probability or process
