view

verb
look at or examine carefully
2.4
Pronunciation: / vjuː /; / vju / (AmE), / vjuː / (BrE)
to look at, observe, or watch something carefully

Examples

To view a house
Before buying a house, it's important to view all your options carefully.
She likes to view the situation from multiple perspectives before acting.
The panel will view the proposals before making a final decision.
Millions of people view the Olympics on television every four years.
She viewed the new art exhibition at the gallery last weekend.
You can view the recorded lecture online at your convenience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: examine - inspect or look at something very carefully
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to
  • synonym: see - to perceive with the eyes; to look at
  • synonym: watch - to look at something attentively, especially for some period of time
