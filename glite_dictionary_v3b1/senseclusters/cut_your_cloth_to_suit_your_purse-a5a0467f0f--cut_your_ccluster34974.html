cut your cloth to suit your purse

verb
adjust spending to fit finances
8.1 6.3
Pronunciation: /kʌt jʊr klɔːθ tə suːt jʊr pɜːrs/ /; / /kʌt jər klɔːθ tə suːt jər pɜːrs/ (AmE), /kʌt jɔː klɒθ tə sjuːt jɔː pɜːs/ /; / /kʌt jə klɒθ tə sjuːt jə pɜːs/ (BrE)
to spend or live within the limits of one’s financial resources; to make decisions or plans based on the amount of money one has available.

Examples

To cut your cloth to suit your purse
After losing his job, he had to cut his cloth to suit his purse and make many sacrifices.
It’s important to cut your cloth to suit your purse to avoid getting into debt.
They were wise enough to cut their cloth to suit their purse when business was slow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: live within your means - to spend only as much money as you have; to not live beyond your financial capabilities
  • antonym: overspend - to spend more than you can afford or more than you intended
