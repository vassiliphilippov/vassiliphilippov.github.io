hypergolic

noun
spontaneous-ignition propellant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈɡɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈɡɒlɪk / (BrE)
a propellant or chemical that ignites spontaneously when it comes into contact with an oxidizer or another reactive substance

Examples

A hypergolic propellant
The engineers handled the hypergolic with extreme caution due to its dangerous reactivity.
A small amount of hypergolic can ignite instantly when mixed with the right oxidizer.
Spacecraft often use hypergolic fuels for their reliability in starting engines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oxidizer - a chemical that provides oxygen to support the combustion of fuel
  • synonym: propellant - a substance used to drive forward or propel, especially in rockets
