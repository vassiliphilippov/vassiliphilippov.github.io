autosome

noun
non-sex chromosome
Pronunciation: / ˈɔtoʊˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəsəʊm / (BrE)
any chromosome that is not a sex chromosome, appearing in pairs in body cells and singly in gametes such as sperm or egg cells

Examples

An autosome chromosome
Genetic disorders can sometimes be traced to mutations on an autosome.
Humans typically have 22 pairs of autosomes and one pair of sex chromosomes.
The study compared the structure of a specific autosome across several mammalian species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sex chromosome - a chromosome that determines the sex of an organism
