deflator

noun
inflation-adjustment factor in economics
5.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfleɪtər / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪtə / (BrE)
a statistical measure used to adjust monetary values to eliminate the effects of inflation, allowing comparison in constant dollars

Examples

A deflator index
By applying a deflator, economists can compare GDP figures across different years accurately.
The GDP deflator provides a comprehensive measure of price inflation in the economy.
The report used a deflator to convert nominal wages into real terms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inflator - a statistical factor used to increase values to account for inflation or other factors
