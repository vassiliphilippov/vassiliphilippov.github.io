instinctive reflex

noun
automatic, unlearned response to a stimulus
2.0 3.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈstɪŋktɪv riˈflɛks / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɪŋktɪv ˈriːflɛks / (BrE)
a natural, automatic response to a stimulus that does not require learning or conscious thought

Examples

An instinctive reflex response
Infants are born with several instinctive reflexes, such as grasping a finger.
The doctor tested his knee-jerk instinctive reflex with a small hammer.
Turning your head when you hear a loud noise is an instinctive reflex.
