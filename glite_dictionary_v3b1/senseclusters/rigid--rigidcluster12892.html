rigid

adjective
not adaptable or flexible
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
not willing or able to change or adapt; inflexible in attitude, rule, or behaviour

Examples

Rigid rules
Despite suggestions for improvement, the system remained rigid and outdated.
His rigid approach to management left no room for creative solutions.
The company's rigid policies made it difficult for employees to feel valued.
He remained rigid in his beliefs, refusing to consider any alternatives.
Her rigid attitude made it impossible to reach any agreement.
The company's rigid policies left little room for innovation or creativity.
