ultrasonic flaw detector

noun
device for detecting internal flaws using ultrasound
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌʌltrəˈsɑnɪk flɔː dɪˈtɛktər / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈsɒnɪk flɔː dɪˈtɛktə / (BrE)
A device that uses ultrasonic waves to detect hidden cracks, voids, and other internal irregularities in metals, plastics, ceramics, and composites.

Examples

An ultrasonic flaw detector device
Routine inspections involved passing an ultrasonic flaw detector over the welds to ensure structural integrity.
The engineer used an ultrasonic flaw detector to check for internal cracks in the metal beam.
With an ultrasonic flaw detector, technicians can locate imperfections that are invisible to the naked eye.
