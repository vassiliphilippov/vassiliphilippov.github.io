sand sage

noun
silver-haired shrub of US and Mexico
Pronunciation: / sænd seɪdʒ / (AmE), / sand seɪdʒ / (BrE)
A silver-haired shrub native to central and southern United States and Mexico, often considered a troublesome weed on rangelands.

Examples

Sand sage shrub
Large areas of the prairie were covered in sand sage and other native grasses.
Ranchers often struggle to control the spread of sand sage on their grazing lands.
The delicate silver leaves of sand sage shimmered in the early morning light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sagebrush - a common term for similar aromatic shrubs of arid regions
