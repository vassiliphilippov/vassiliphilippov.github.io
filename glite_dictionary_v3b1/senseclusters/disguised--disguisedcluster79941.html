disguised

adjective
concealed or hidden to mislead
7.1
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɡaɪzd / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡaɪzd / (BrE)
having its true nature or intentions concealed, especially to deceive or mislead

Examples

Disguised insult
His anger took the form of a disguised insult during the meeting.
The company's true intentions were disguised in the official statement.
The spy entered the building in a disguised uniform to avoid detection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hidden - kept out of sight or concealed
  • antonym: overt - done or shown openly; not secret or hidden
