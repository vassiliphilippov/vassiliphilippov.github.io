speculativeness

noun
quality of being speculative or based on conjecture
Pronunciation: / spəˈkjuleɪtɪvnəs / (AmE), / spɛkˈjuːlətɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being based on conjecture or supposition rather than fact or investigation

Examples

Academic speculativeness
Her argument was marked by a surprising degree of speculativeness despite her usually rigorous methods.
Many critics objected to the speculativeness of the proposal, citing a lack of concrete data.
The speculativeness of his theory made it difficult for others to accept.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - the quality of being reliably true; known for sure
  • synonym: speculation - the forming of a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
