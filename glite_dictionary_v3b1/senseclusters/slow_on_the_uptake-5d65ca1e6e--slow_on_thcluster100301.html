slow on the uptake

adjective
slow to understand or learn
Pronunciation: / sloʊ ɑn ði ˈʌpˌteɪk / (AmE), / sləʊ ɒn ði ˈʌp.teɪk / (BrE)
not quick to understand or realize something; slow to grasp or learn new information

Examples

Slow on the uptake with ideas
He can be slow on the uptake with technical concepts but is otherwise very clever.
I felt slow on the uptake during the meeting and missed the main point.
Some students are slow on the uptake and need extra time to process new material.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quick on the uptake - able to understand or learn things quickly
