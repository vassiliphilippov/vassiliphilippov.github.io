feel like a sap

verb
to feel foolish or deceived
Pronunciation: / fil laɪk ə sæp / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə sæp / (BrE)
to feel foolish or easily deceived, especially after being misled or tricked by someone

Examples

To feel like a sap after prank
After her friends played a harmless prank, Anna felt like a sap for believing their story.
He felt like a sap when he realized the email was just a scam.
Whenever I fall for a marketing gimmick, I end up feeling like a sap.
