interconnected

adjective
mutually connected
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərkəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
having parts or things connected with each other so that they affect or depend on each other

Examples

Interconnected parts

These ecosystems are interconnected, so a change in one affects the other.
Global economies have become increasingly interconnected over the past few decades.
The nervous system is a complex network of interconnected cells.
The new software allows all the devices to be interconnected for better efficiency.
