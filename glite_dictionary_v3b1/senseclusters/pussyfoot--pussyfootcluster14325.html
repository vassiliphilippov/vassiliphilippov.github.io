pussyfoot

verb
to avoid decisiveness
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊsiˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈpʊsiˌfʊt / (BrE)
to avoid making a clear decision or statement, often by being overly cautious or indirect

Examples

To pussyfoot around decisions
The manager was pussyfooting around the issue instead of giving a direct answer.
If you keep pussyfooting, we will never reach a decision on this project.
She tends to pussyfoot when asked difficult questions in meetings.
