Salmonella enteritidis

noun
bacterium causing human gastroenteritis
Pronunciation: / sælməˈnɛlə ˌentəˈrɪdɪtɪs / (AmE), / sælməˈnɛlə ˌentəˈrɪdɪtɪs / (BrE)
A species of Salmonella bacterium that commonly causes gastroenteritis in humans, often through contaminated food.

Examples

Salmonella enteritidis infection
Doctors recommend proper handling and cooking of food to prevent Salmonella enteritidis infection.
Many cases of food poisoning are caused by Salmonella enteritidis found in undercooked eggs.
The outbreak was traced to Salmonella enteritidis contaminating a batch of poultry products.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gastroenteritis - inflammation of the stomach and intestines, causing vomiting and diarrhea
