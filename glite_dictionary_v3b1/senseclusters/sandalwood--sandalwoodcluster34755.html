sandalwood

noun
fragrant wood used for carving and scent
Pronunciation: / ˈsæn.dəlˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsændlˌwʊd / (BrE)
The close-grained, fragrant yellowish wood of the sandalwood tree, often used for carving, cabinetmaking, and in perfumes due to its pleasant scent and insect-repelling properties.

Examples

Fragrant sandalwood wood
Ancient temples often burn sandalwood during religious ceremonies for its calming scent.
He bought a box with intricate designs made entirely of sandalwood.
The artist skillfully carved the statue from sandalwood, giving it a rich, sweet aroma.
