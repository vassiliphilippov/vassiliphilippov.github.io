break

verb
to end an activity or state
1.0 113/m
Pronunciation: / breÉªk / (AmE), / breÉªk / (BrE)
to stop, interrupt, or put an end to an activity, process, state, habit, or relationship

Examples

To break a habit
After hours of discussion, they finally decided to break the silence and speak openly.
The referee had to break up the fight before it got out of hand.
We need to break this unhealthy cycle before it continues any longer.
A thick hedge can break the force of a strong wind.
She put her arms out to break her fall when she tripped.
The airbags helped to break the impact of the collision.
An avenue of statues breaks the monotony of the garden.
The coastline is broken by small inlets and coves.
The silence was broken by a sudden clap of thunder.

  • quit

  • give up
    1.2 65/m

  • break off
    3.2 3.16/m

  • break up
    3.3 0.30/m
    break up brings an end or pause to an event or relationship, often permanently, while break is a general stop or interruption.
  • break short
    5.4 0.00/m

  • break away
    3.9 0.58/m

  • interrupt
    2.5 39/m
    interrupt stops or breaks the flow of action or speech briefly, while break can mean a full stop or a temporary interruption.
  • cut off
    3.8 24/m

  • pause
    1.0 44/m
