thermophile

noun
heat-loving microorganism
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈθɜːrməˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməˌfaɪl / (BrE)
a microorganism that grows best at high temperatures, often found in hot environments like hot springs

Examples

A thermophile microbe
Scientists discovered a new species of thermophile living in the boiling waters of the geyser.
A thermophile can survive temperatures that would kill most other organisms.
Many industrial processes use enzymes from thermophiles because they remain active at high heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extremophile - an organism that thrives in extreme environmental conditions
  • antonym: psychrophile - a microorganism that grows best at low temperatures
