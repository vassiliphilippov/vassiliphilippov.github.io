overhandle

verb
to control excessively
7.7
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈhæn.dəl/ /; / /ə.vər.ˈhæn.dəl/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈhæn.dəl/ /; / /ə.və.ˈhæn.dəl/ (BrE)
to manage, control, or interfere with something or someone too much, often reducing effectiveness or independence

Examples

To overhandle a project
If you overhandle your child's homework, they may not learn to solve problems on their own.
The coach was criticized for overhandling the team and not letting players make decisions.
Try not to overhandle the situation; sometimes it's best to step back and observe.
