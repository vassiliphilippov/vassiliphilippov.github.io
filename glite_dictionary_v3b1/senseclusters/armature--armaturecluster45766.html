armature

noun
digital skeleton/rig for animation
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.mə.tʃər/ /; / /ˈɑːr.mə.tjʊr/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.mə.tʃə/ /; / /ˈɑː.mə.tjʊə/ (BrE)
(in computer graphics and 3D animation) a digital skeleton or hierarchical set of bones and controls used to pose, deform, and animate a character or object.

Examples

3D armature
The animator adjusted the armature to correct the model's movements in the scene.
Before exporting, she renamed several armatures to keep different character rigs organized.
Using inverse kinematics, the lead artist posed the armature to test realistic joint movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skeleton - a hierarchical bone structure used to define and drive a model's movements
