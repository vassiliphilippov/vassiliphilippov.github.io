proteolysis

noun
breakdown of proteins into peptides or amino acids
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtiˈɑləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtiˈɒləsɪs / (BrE)
the breakdown of proteins into smaller peptides or amino acids by the cleavage of peptide bonds, usually by enzymes

Examples

Proteolysis by enzymes
Abnormal proteolysis can contribute to diseases such as muscle wasting.
Scientists study proteolysis to understand how cells regulate protein levels.
The digestive enzyme pepsin initiates proteolysis in the stomach.

  • synonym: hydrolysis - chemical breakdown caused by reaction with water
