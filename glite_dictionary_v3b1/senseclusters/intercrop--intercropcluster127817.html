intercrop

verb
to grow mixed crops
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkrɑp / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈkrɒp / (BrE)
to grow two or more different crops together in the same field at the same time

Examples

To intercrop maize and beans
Farmers often intercrop maize and beans to improve soil fertility and yields.
In sustainable agriculture, many experts recommend intercropping to reduce pests and diseases.
The agricultural project taught villagers how to intercrop vegetables and grains for better harvests.
Many farmers intercrop maize and beans to make better use of their land.
By intercropping, they reduced the risk of losing all crops to pests or disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crop rotation - the practice of growing different crops in succession on the same land
  • antonym: monocrop - to grow only one type of crop in a field
  • synonym: polyculture - the practice of growing multiple crops together
