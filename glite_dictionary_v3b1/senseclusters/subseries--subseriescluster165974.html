subseries

noun
partial mathematical series
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌsɪriːz / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌsɪəriːz / (BrE)
a series formed by selecting some, but not necessarily all, terms from a given mathematical series

Examples

Mathematical subseries
By removing the negative terms, he obtained a convergent subseries from the original divergent series.
The mathematician analyzed a subseries to determine if it would converge independently.
Selecting every other term from the original sequence creates a new subseries for study.

Related Terms

  • synonym: series - a sum of a sequence of terms in mathematics
  • synonym: subsequence - a sequence formed from another by deleting some elements without changing the order
