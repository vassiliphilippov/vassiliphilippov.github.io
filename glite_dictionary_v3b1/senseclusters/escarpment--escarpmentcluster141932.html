escarpment

noun
steep defensive slope in front of a fortification
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskɑːrpmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈskɑːpmənt / (BrE)
a steep artificial slope built in front of a fortification as a defensive measure

Examples

Fortress escarpment
Engineers constructed an escarpment around the fortress to strengthen its defenses.
Soldiers struggled to scale the steep escarpment during the siege.
The castle was protected by a massive escarpment that deterred invaders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moat - a deep, wide ditch surrounding a fort
  • synonym: rampart - a defensive wall or bank around a fort; synonym in fortification
