unhulled

adjective
with husk intact
6.3
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈhʌld / (AmE), / ʌnˈhʌld / (BrE)
not having had the outer covering or husk removed, especially from seeds or grains

Examples

Unhulled grains
The bakery uses unhulled grains to make their rustic bread.
You can see the difference in color between unhulled and hulled sesame seeds.
Some people prefer unhulled rice for its higher fiber content.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hulled - having had the outer covering or husk removed
  • synonym: whole - not processed or refined; in a complete state
