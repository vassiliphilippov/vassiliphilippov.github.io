post-processing

noun
finishing treatments
4.4
Pronunciation: /poʊst ˈprɑː.ses.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /pəʊst ˈprəʊ.ses.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of editing, refining, or finishing something (such as an object, image, video, or data) after its initial creation or production to improve its quality or appearance

Examples

Post-processing images
The 3D-printed parts required extensive post-processing to remove support material and achieve a smooth surface.
After casting, the sculpture went through post-processing steps like sanding and painting to enhance its appearance.
Proper post-processing can greatly improve the durability and look of manufactured products.
The film's stunning visuals were achieved through careful post-processing of each scene.
Photographers often use post-processing to adjust colors and lighting in their images.
Advanced post-processing techniques can make video games look more realistic and immersive.
The film required extensive post-processing to achieve its unique visual effects.
Modern cameras often include automatic post-processing to improve image quality.
After collecting the data, the team performed post-processing to remove any errors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: editing - the process of making changes or improvements to images or video
  • synonym: finishing - the final processes to complete or perfect a manufactured item
  • antonym: pre-processing - preparatory steps taken before the main production process
  • antonym: preprocessing - the process of preparing or modifying data before its main use
  • antonym: rendering - the process of generating an image from a model in computer graphics
