unpeg

verb
to remove a fixed value
6.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈpɛɡ / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɛɡ / (BrE)
to remove a fixed or set value, especially detaching a currency or price from another standard or benchmark

Examples

To unpeg a currency
The central bank decided to unpeg the national currency from the euro.
After years of stability, the government chose to unpeg fuel prices from international rates.
Many economists debated whether to unpeg the exchange rate to encourage market flexibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detach - to separate or remove from something to which it is attached
  • antonym: fix - to set or establish firmly
