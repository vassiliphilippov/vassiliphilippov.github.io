artificial satellite

noun
man-made object in orbit
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːr.t̬əˈfɪʃ.əl ˈsæt.əl.aɪt / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl ˈsæt.əl.aɪt / (BrE)
A man-made object that has been deliberately placed to orbit the earth, the moon, or another celestial body.

Examples

An artificial satellite orbiting Earth
An artificial satellite can transmit television signals across great distances.
Scientists use artificial satellites to gather important data about weather patterns.
The first artificial satellite, Sputnik 1, was launched by the Soviet Union in 1957.

Related Terms

  • synonym: satellite - an object that orbits a planet or star
