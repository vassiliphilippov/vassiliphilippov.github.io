magnesium

noun
a metallic element (Mg) with a bright white flame
Pronunciation: / mægˈniːziəm / (AmE), / maɡˈniːziəm / (BrE)
a light, silver-white, ductile metallic element that burns with a bright white flame and is found naturally in compounds

Examples

Pure magnesium metal
Dietary supplements often contain magnesium to support healthy muscle and nerve function.
Spinach and almonds are both excellent sources of magnesium.
When magnesium is ignited, it produces an intense white light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calcium - a soft, silvery-white metallic element; chemically similar to magnesium
