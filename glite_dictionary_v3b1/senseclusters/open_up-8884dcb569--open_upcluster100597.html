open up

verb
make available or possible
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ʌp / (BrE)
to make something available, possible, or accessible, especially something new or previously unavailable

Examples

Open up new opportunities
Digital platforms open up opportunities for businesses to reach international markets.
Her research helped open up new avenues in the field of medicine.
The new highway will open up remote areas to tourism and trade.
New possibilities opened up for her after she moved to the city.
Scholarship options often open up as your grades improve.
When the director retired, a position finally opened up in the department.
She worked hard to open up new opportunities for local businesses.
The discovery of gold helped open up the West for settlers.
The new railway line will open up the region to tourism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: become available - to start being accessible or possible to use
  • antonym: close off - to make something inaccessible or unavailable
  • synonym: pioneer - to initiate or be among the first to develop something
  • synonym: unlock - to open or make something accessible
