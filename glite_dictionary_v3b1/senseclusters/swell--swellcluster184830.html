swell

verb
cause to become swollen or enlarged
2.2
Pronunciation: / swɛl / (AmE), / swɛl / (BrE)
to increase in size, amount, number, or intensity; to become larger or rounder, especially due to pressure, injury, or absorption

Examples

To swell up after injury
Humidity can swell a door, making it difficult to close.
If you leave dried beans in water overnight, they will swell considerably.
The rain caused the wood to swell and warp.
Her pride swelled when she heard the news of her promotion.
The crowd began to swell as more people arrived for the concert.
The river swelled rapidly after the heavy rainstorm.
After the insect bite, her ankle began to swell painfully and turned red.
His eyes would swell shut whenever he had a severe allergic reaction.
The river can swell rapidly after heavy rainfall, causing localized flooding.
