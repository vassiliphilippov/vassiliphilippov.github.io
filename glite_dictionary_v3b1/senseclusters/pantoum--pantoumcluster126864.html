pantoum

noun
fixed-form poem
Pronunciation: / pænˈtuːm / (AmE), / pænˈtuːm / (BrE)
a poem with a fixed form in which lines are repeated in a set pattern and follow a specific rhyme scheme

Examples

A pantoum poem
The poet composed a pantoum to experiment with repeating lines and intricate rhyme.
In her literature class, they analyzed a nineteenth-century pantoum by a French writer.
Writing a pantoum requires careful planning to ensure the repeated lines make sense.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free verse - poetry without a fixed form or rhyme scheme
  • synonym: villanelle - a poem with a fixed form and repeated lines
