lighthouse

noun
tower with a warning light for ships
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪthaʊs / (BrE)
a tall structure built on or near the coast, equipped with a powerful light to warn or guide ships at sea

Examples

A lighthouse tower
During the storm, the lighthouse was the only thing visible through the darkness.
Many tourists visit the coastal lighthouse to enjoy its panoramic views.
The old lighthouse stood proudly on the rocky shore, guiding ships safely into harbor.
