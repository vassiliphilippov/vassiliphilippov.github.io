appellate court

noun
court that reviews lower court decisions
7.4 1.35/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàp…õl…ôt k…îrt / (AmE), / …ôÀàp…õl…ôt k…îÀêt / (BrE)
A court with the authority to review and potentially revise the decisions of lower courts or agencies.

Examples

The appellate court decision
Cases are submitted to the appellate court if one party believes a legal error was made.
She argued her case before the state's highest appellate court last week.
The decision was overturned by the appellate court after a thorough review.

Synonyms

  • court of appeals
    5.0 1.90/m
    a court of appeals is a specific formal court that reviews lower decisions, while an appellate court is a general term for such courts.
  • appeals court
    4.4 2.49/m
    an appeals court is a common spoken name for a court that hears appeals, while an appellate court is the formal legal term.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trial court - a court where cases are initially tried, as opposed to being reviewed
