maneuvering

noun
skillful planning
4.3
Pronunciation: / məˈnuːvərɪŋ / (AmE), / məˈnjuːvərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of planning or moving skillfully, often to achieve a particular goal or advantage

Examples

Financial maneuvering
The company's financial maneuvering allowed it to survive during the economic crisis.
Political maneuvering behind the scenes influenced the outcome of the election.
Her careful maneuvering in negotiations led to a successful agreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair
  • synonym: manipulation - controlling or influencing something or someone cleverly or unfairly
  • synonym: scheming - the act of making secret plans to achieve something
