tailback

noun
traffic jam line
4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈteɪl.bæk/ (AmE), /ˈteɪl.bæk/ (BrE)
a long line of vehicles that are stopped or moving slowly, especially because of traffic congestion

Examples

A long tailback
A serious accident caused a long tailback on the main road into the city.
Drivers faced a two-mile tailback during the morning rush hour.
The roadworks resulted in a tailback that stretched as far as the next town.
There was a long tailback on the highway after the accident.
We missed our appointment because we got stuck in a tailback during rush hour.
The radio warned drivers about a five-mile tailback near the city center.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free flow - a situation where traffic moves smoothly without delays
  • synonym: gridlock - a severe traffic jam where vehicles cannot move in any direction
  • synonym: traffic jam - a situation where vehicles are unable to move or move very slowly due to congestion
