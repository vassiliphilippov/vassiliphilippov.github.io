ravine

noun
deep, narrow, steep-sided valley
Pronunciation: / rəˈvin / (AmE), / rəˈviːn / (BrE)
a deep, narrow valley with steep sides, especially one formed by running water

Examples

Steep ravine
A narrow wooden bridge stretched across the steep ravine, connecting the two villages.
The stream carved a ravine through the mountains over thousands of years.
They carefully descended into the ravine to explore the rocky bottom and observe the wildlife.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canyon - a deep gorge, typically one with a river flowing through it
  • synonym: gorge - a deep, narrow passage with steep rocky sides, often with a stream
