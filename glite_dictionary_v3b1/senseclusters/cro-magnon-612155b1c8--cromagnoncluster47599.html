Cro-Magnon

noun
extinct Upper Paleolithic human in Europe
9.7
Pronunciation: / kroʊ ˈmæɡ.nɑn / (AmE), / krəʊ ˈmæɡ.nɒn / (BrE)
an extinct human of the Upper Paleolithic period in Europe, regarded as an early form of modern Homo sapiens

Examples

Cro-Magnon people
Compared to Neanderthals, Cro-Magnon people had more advanced tools and artwork.
Many anthropologists study Cro-Magnon art to learn about early human culture.
The remains of a Cro-Magnon were discovered in a cave in France.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Neanderthal - an extinct species of human that lived in Europe and western Asia until about 40,000 years ago
