corundum

noun
very hard mineral, aluminum oxide
9.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌndəm / (AmE), / kəˈrʌndəm / (BrE)
A very hard mineral, aluminum oxide in crystalline form, used as an abrasive and as the basis for gemstones such as ruby and sapphire.

Examples

Corundum gemstones
Both ruby and sapphire are types of corundum prized as gemstones.
Industrial uses of corundum include making sandpaper and other abrasive tools.
The hardness of corundum makes it valuable for grinding metals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ruby - a red gemstone variety of corundum
  • synonym: sapphire - a blue gemstone variety of corundum
