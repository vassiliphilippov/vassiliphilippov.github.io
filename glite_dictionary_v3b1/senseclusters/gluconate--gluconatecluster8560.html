gluconate

noun
salt of gluconic acid
Pronunciation: / ˈɡluːkəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡluːkəneɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester formed from gluconic acid, commonly used in supplements or medicine

Examples

Gluconate supplements
Doctors may prescribe gluconate supplements to treat mineral deficiencies.
Calcium gluconate is often given to patients with low blood calcium levels.
The pharmacist explained how to take the gluconate tablets safely.
