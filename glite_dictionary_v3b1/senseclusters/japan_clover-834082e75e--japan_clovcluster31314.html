Japan clover

noun
an annual Asian plant, naturalized in the U.S.
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæn ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
An annual plant native to tropical Asia and naturalized in the United States, often used as forage or ground cover.

Examples

Japan clover plant
Japan clover grows well in poor soils, making it valuable as a ground cover in the southeastern United States.
After being introduced from Asia, Japan clover quickly spread across several American states.
Farmers often plant Japan clover to improve pasture quality for livestock.
