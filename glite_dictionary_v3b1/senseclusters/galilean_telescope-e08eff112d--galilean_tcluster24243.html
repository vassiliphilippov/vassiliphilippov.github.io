Galilean telescope

noun
historical refracting telescope designed by Galileo
7.2 7.0
Pronunciation: /gæl.ɪ.ˈliː.ən ˈtel.ə.skoʊp/ /; / /gæl.ə.ˈliː.ən ˈtel.ə.skoʊp/ (AmE), /gæl.ɪ.ˈliː.ən ˈtel.ɪ.skəʊp/ /; / /gæl.ə.ˈliː.ən ˈtel.ɪ.skəʊp/ (BrE)
a type of refracting telescope, invented by Galileo, consisting of a convex objective lens and a concave eyepiece, and no longer used in modern astronomy

Examples

A Galilean telescope
Galileo used a Galilean telescope to discover the four largest moons of Jupiter.
The Galilean telescope revolutionized early astronomy by allowing distant objects to be observed in detail.
Unlike the Keplerian design, the Galilean telescope produced an upright image.
