garishly

adverb
in a tastelessly bright or showy manner
7.6 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°…õr…™ Éli / (AmE), / Àà…°…ëÀê.r…™ É.li / (BrE)
In a way that is excessively bright or showy, and lacking in good taste.

Examples

To be garishly bright
At night, the city square is garishly lit by dozens of blinking signs.
She wore a garishly colored dress that drew everyone's attention at the party.
The living room was garishly painted with neon stripes and geometric patterns.

Synonyms

  • gaudily
    6.3 0.06/m
    gaudily looks showy and tasteless, often with bright or ornate decoration, while garishly stresses excessive brightness and bad taste.
  • tawdrily
    9.2 0.00/m
    tawdrily appears cheap and showy, often with low-cost glitter or fabric, while garishly means overly bright and in bad taste.
  • tackily
    7.6 0.01/m
    tackily looks cheap or poorly made and shows bad taste, while garishly points to colors or lights that are too bright.
  • flashily
    7.0 0.01/m
    flashily uses bright or glittery details to attract attention, while garishly always feels excessive and lacking in good taste.
  • showily
    7.2 0.04/m
    showily is meant to attract attention and can be stylish or bold, while garishly specifically suggests ugly, tasteless brightness.
  • splashily
    6.3 0.00/m
    splashily uses bold, attention-grabbing effects or headlines and can be positive, while garishly stresses ugly, excessive brightness and bad taste.
