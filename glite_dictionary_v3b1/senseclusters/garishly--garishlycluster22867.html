garishly

adverb
in a tastelessly bright or showy manner
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɛrɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈɡɑː.rɪʃ.li / (BrE)
In a way that is excessively bright or showy, and lacking in good taste.

Examples

To be garishly bright
At night, the city square is garishly lit by dozens of blinking signs.
She wore a garishly colored dress that drew everyone's attention at the party.
The living room was garishly painted with neon stripes and geometric patterns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gaudily - in a brilliantly or excessively bright or showy manner
  • antonym: tastefully - in a way that shows good aesthetic judgment or taste
