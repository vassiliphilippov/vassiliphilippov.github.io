journalize

verb
to record transactions
7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒɜːr.nə.laɪz/ /; / /ˈdʒɜːr.nəl.aɪz/ (AmE), /ˈdʒɜː.nəl.aɪz/ /; / /ˈdʒɜː.nə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to record financial transactions or information in a journal as part of bookkeeping or accounting

Examples

To journalize a transaction
Accountants must journalize every transaction to ensure the books are accurate.
Before preparing the financial statements, she carefully journalized all the company's expenses.
It is important to journalize receipts and payments daily in the accounting system.
The accountant journalized all the transactions at the end of the day.
It is important to journalize each entry accurately in the company records.
She journalizes her daily expenses to keep track of her budget.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erase - to remove or delete written information
  • synonym: log - to make an official or systematic record of something
  • synonym: post - to transfer an entry from a journal to a ledger in accounting
  • synonym: record - to write down or enter information for future reference
