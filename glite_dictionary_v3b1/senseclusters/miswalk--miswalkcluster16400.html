miswalk

verb
walk incorrectly or into the wrong place
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈwɔk / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈwɔːk / (BrE)
to walk incorrectly, in a distracted or mistaken manner, or to enter the wrong place while walking by accident

Examples

To miswalk into room
During the interview, she miswalked into the wrong meeting room by mistake.
He miswalked down the street, lost in thought and barely noticing his surroundings.
If you're not paying attention, you might miswalk and bump into someone on the sidewalk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps
  • synonym: wander - to walk or move in a leisurely, aimless way
