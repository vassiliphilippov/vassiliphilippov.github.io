quell

verb
suppress or overcome (emotion or situation)
7.7 2.75/m
Pronunciation: / kwÉ›l / (AmE), / kwÉ›l / (BrE)
to stop or suppress something, especially a feeling, situation, or disturbance

Examples

To quell a feeling
He drank water to quell his thirst after the long run.
Police were called in to quell the violent protest.
She tried to quell her anxiety before the big presentation.
Her calm words helped to quell the rising panic in the room.
The police were called in to quell the violent protests downtown.
Troops were sent to quell the uprising before it could spread further.
