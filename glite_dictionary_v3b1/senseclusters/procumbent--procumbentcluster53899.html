procumbent

adjective
stems trailing along the ground (botany)
9.1 8.7
Pronunciation: / proʊˈkʌmbənt / (AmE), / prəˈkʌmbənt / (BrE)
(of a plant) having stems that lie or trail along the ground without rooting at the nodes.

Examples

Procumbent stems lie
Some succulents have procumbent growth, allowing them to spread widely in rocky areas.
The procumbent stems of the plant helped it cover the forest floor quickly.
Unlike climbing vines, procumbent species do not attach themselves to other plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creeping - growing along the ground and potentially rooting at nodes
  • antonym: erect - (in botany) standing upright
  • synonym: prostrate - lying flat on the ground; in botany, similar trailing habit
