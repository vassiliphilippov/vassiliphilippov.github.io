Pterocarpus

noun
genus of tropical trees with winged pods
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrəˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical trees or climbers, known for their usually broadly winged pods, including species valued for hardwood and dyes

Examples

Pterocarpus tree species
Botanists conducted a study on the seed dispersal mechanisms of Pterocarpus species in West Africa.
Many traditional dyes are derived from the heartwood of Pterocarpus plants.
The wood from Pterocarpus trees is highly valued for making furniture and musical instruments.
