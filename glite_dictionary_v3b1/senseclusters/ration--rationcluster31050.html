ration

verb
restrict or limit usage (esp. in scarcity)
6.3 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈræ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈræ.ʃən/ (BrE)
to restrict or distribute a fixed amount of something, especially when there is not enough for everyone

Examples

To ration water
During the drought, the government decided to ration water to ensure there was enough for everyone.
Parents sometimes ration their children's screen time to encourage other activities.
When fuel supplies ran low, the army rationed gasoline to its vehicles.
During the war, food supplies were rationed to make sure everyone had enough.
Each family was rationed only two gallons of water per week.
The government decided to ration electricity during the energy crisis.

  • degree lower: hoard - accumulate and store goods
  • synonym: limit - set or serve as a restriction
  • synonym: withhold - refuse to give or allow something
