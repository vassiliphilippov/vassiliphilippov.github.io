fibrous joint

noun
immovable joint joined by fibrous tissue
4.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.brəs dʒɔɪnt/ /; / /ˈfaɪ.bər.əs dʒɔɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.brəs dʒɔɪnt/ /; / /ˈfaɪ.bə.rəs dʒɔɪnt/ (BrE)
an immovable joint in which the bones are joined by fibrous tissue, especially those between the bones of the skull

Examples

A fibrous joint in skull
A fibrous joint is designed for stability rather than flexibility.
In infants, some fibrous joints of the skull are temporarily flexible to allow for brain growth.
The bones of the skull are connected by fibrous joints that do not allow movement.
