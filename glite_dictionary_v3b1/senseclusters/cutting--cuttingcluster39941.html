cutting

noun
film editing process
3.1 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkʌ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
a technical or specialized act of cutting, such as film editing, dividing cards, diluting substances, or creating patterns in materials

Examples

Film cutting
After the initial shoot, the director supervised the cutting to ensure the story flowed smoothly.
Innovative cutting techniques gave the movie its unique style.
The cutting of the documentary took several months to complete.
He asked for the cutting to be done in a specific way every time.
In some games, the cutting of the cards is an important ritual.
The dealer performed the cutting of the deck before handing out the cards.
Paint manufacturers recommend the cutting of paint with thinners for easier application.
Some people object to the cutting of gasoline with cheaper additives.
The cutting of spirits with water was a common practice to make them more palatable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dilution - the process of making a liquid thinner or weaker by adding water or another solvent
  • synonym: editing - the process of selecting and arranging material for a film, book, etc.
  • synonym: engraving - the act of carving a design onto a hard surface
