stigmatize

verb
to mark as disgraceful or disapproved
5.8 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪɡ.mə.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɪɡ.mə.taɪz / (BrE)
to treat or describe someone or something as disgraceful, unacceptable, or not worthy of respect

Examples

To stigmatize a person
He feared his background would stigmatize him in the new community.
Society should not stigmatize people with mental health conditions.
The campaign aims to stop schools from stigmatizing students with learning difficulties.
It is unfair to stigmatize children because of their family's background.
People often stigmatize those who struggle with addiction, making it harder for them to seek help.
The organization works to reduce the ways society stigmatizes mental health conditions.
Policies that stigmatise certain groups can increase discrimination.
She felt stigmatised because of her background.
Society should not stigmatise people living with mental illness.
