temperament

noun
excessive emotionalism or excitability
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (BrE)
a person's usual mood or emotional nature, especially as it affects how they behave

Examples

Calm temperament
Due to his sensitive temperament, he easily became upset by criticism.
Her temperament often led to dramatic outbursts in stressful situations.
The actor's temperament made him difficult to work with during rehearsals.
His artistic temperament made him sensitive to beauty and easily moved by emotion.
She has a calm temperament and rarely gets upset, even under pressure.
Teachers need patience and a balanced temperament to handle young children effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disposition - a person's inherent qualities of mind and character
  • degree lower: mood - a temporary state of mind or feeling
  • synonym: personality - the combination of characteristics or qualities that form an individual's character
  • synonym: temper - a person's state of mind seen in terms of their being angry or calm
