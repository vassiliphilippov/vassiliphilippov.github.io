ride up

verb
(of clothing) move up on the body
Pronunciation: / raɪd ʌp / (AmE), / raɪd ʌp / (BrE)
(of a piece of clothing) to move up from its intended position on the body, often causing discomfort or awkwardness.

Examples

Jeans ride up
After sitting down, he noticed his jeans had ridden up above his ankles.
Her shirt kept riding up as she stretched to reach the top shelf.
These tights always ride up when I walk for long periods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: slip down - of clothing, to move down from intended position
