commonsensical

adjective
showing good sense; sensible
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmənˈsɛnsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒmənˈsɛnsɪkəl / (BrE)
Showing good sense and practical judgment based on simple perception of the situation or facts; sensible.

Examples

A commonsensical idea
Her commonsensical approach to solving the problem impressed the entire team.
It would be commonsensical to bring an umbrella if the forecast calls for rain.
The manager appreciated his employee's commonsensical decision-making in stressful situations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment; unwise
  • synonym: practical - adapted or adaptable for use; involving or resulting from practice or action; guided by practical experience and observation rather than theory
  • synonym: sensible - having, using, or showing good sense or sound judgment
