cosmozoan

noun
organism from space
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːzməˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməˈzəʊən / (BrE)
an organism that exists in or comes from outer space, especially one believed to have seeded life on Earth

Examples

A cosmozoan organism
Some scientists have speculated that a cosmozoan could have brought the first life to our planet.
The theory of cosmozoan origins challenges traditional ideas about how life began on Earth.
In science fiction, a cosmozoan species might travel between planets on comets or asteroids.
Scientists debated whether the strange signals came from a cosmozoan traveling through the galaxy.
The science fiction novel described a cosmozoan that could survive the vacuum of space.
Some researchers believe that a cosmozoan might one day be discovered on a distant asteroid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alien - a creature from another planet or outer space
  • synonym: extraterrestrial - a being from outside the Earth or its atmosphere
  • synonym: panspermia - the theory that life exists throughout the universe and can be spread by space dust or meteoroids
  • antonym: terrestrial - originating from Earth
