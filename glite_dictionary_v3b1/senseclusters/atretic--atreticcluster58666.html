atretic

adjective
degenerating tissue
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈtrɛtɪk / (AmE), / əˈtrɛtɪk / (BrE)
describing tissue, cells, or an organ that is failing to develop or is degenerating, especially due to atresia

Examples

Atretic tissue
Most ovarian follicles become atretic during the reproductive cycle and never release an egg.
The doctor explained that the tissue was atretic and could not function normally.
Microscopic examination revealed several atretic cells in the sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym: degenerate - to decline or deteriorate in quality or function
  • antonym: developed - fully grown or matured
