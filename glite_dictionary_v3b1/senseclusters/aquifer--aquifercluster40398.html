aquifer

noun
underground water-bearing layer
Pronunciation: / ˈækwɪfər / (AmE), / ˈakwɪfə / (BrE)
A layer of rock, sand, or earth that contains or can transmit groundwater, supplying wells and springs.

Examples

An aquifer layer
Overuse of the aquifer has led to a significant drop in groundwater levels.
Scientists monitor the quality of water in the local aquifer to ensure safety.
The city relies on a deep aquifer for its drinking water supply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: water table - the upper level of groundwater in an aquifer
