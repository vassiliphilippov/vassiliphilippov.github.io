mechanist

noun
philosopher who believes in mechanism
3.7 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈme.kə.nɪst/ (AmE), /ˈme.kə.nɪst/ (BrE)
a philosopher who believes that natural processes can be explained entirely by physical causes, especially according to the doctrine of mechanism

Examples

A mechanist philosopher
As a committed mechanist, she argued that mental states are the result of physical processes in the brain.
Many 17th-century philosophers were known as mechanists because they believed everything followed the laws of physics.
The debate between the mechanist and vitalist views was central to the development of modern biology.
