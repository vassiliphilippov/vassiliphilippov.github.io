wherein

adverb
in what way
6.5
Pronunciation: / wɛrˈɪn / (AmE), / wɛərˈɪn /; / weərˈɪn / (BrE)
in which (place, situation, or way); used to refer to something happening or being true within a particular context (formal)

Examples

The place wherein
The report details the process wherein the new policy will be implemented.
Can you explain wherein this method is more effective than the previous one?
She described the situation wherein the two companies decided to merge.
The report outlines the process wherein the data was collected and analyzed.
They reached an agreement wherein both parties would share the profits equally.
The novel describes a world wherein technology controls every aspect of life.
