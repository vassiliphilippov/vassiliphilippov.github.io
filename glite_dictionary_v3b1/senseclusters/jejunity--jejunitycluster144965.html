jejunity

noun
lack of nutritive value or substance
Pronunciation: / dʒɪˈdʒunəti / (AmE), / dʒɪˈdʒuːnɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking in nutritive value or substance; emptiness or insubstantiality

Examples

Jejunity of the meal
Despite the colorful packaging, the cereal's jejunity left consumers unsatisfied and hungry soon after breakfast.
Scholars debated the jejunity of the new curriculum, arguing it offered little substantive value.
The critic remarked on the jejunity of the novel, noting its lack of intellectual nourishment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jejuneness - the quality of being jejune; lack of substance or interest
