group therapy

noun
psychotherapy involving group sessions
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡruːp ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ɡruːp ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a form of psychotherapy in which a small group of people meet regularly with a therapist to discuss and resolve their problems together

Examples

Group therapy session
After his accident, Tom joined group therapy to help him cope with trauma and anxiety.
Many patients find group therapy more supportive than individual counseling due to shared experiences.
Their clinic offers group therapy sessions for people dealing with loss or depression.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individual therapy - psychotherapy conducted one-on-one between a therapist and a patient
  • synonym: psychotherapy - the treatment of mental disorders by psychological rather than medical means
