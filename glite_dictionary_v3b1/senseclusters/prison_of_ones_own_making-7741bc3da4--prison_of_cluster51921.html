prison of one's own making

noun
self-imposed restrictions or limitations
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪzən əv wʌnz oʊn ˈmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪzən əv wʌnz əʊn ˈmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
a situation in which a person is restricted or trapped by limitations, rules, or problems they have created for themselves

Examples

A prison of one's own making
After years of refusing help, she realized she was living in a prison of her own making.
He blamed others, but his isolation was truly a prison of his own making.
Many people struggle to break free from the prison of their own making.
