throstle

noun
Old World song thrush
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɑːsəl / (AmE), / ˈθrɒsəl / (BrE)
A common Old World songbird, the song thrush, known for its melodious song.

Examples

A singing throstle
In the quiet woodland, a throstle sang sweetly atop a mossy branch.
Many poets have written about the beauty of the throstle's song in springtime.
The garden was filled with the melodious notes of a throstle at dawn.
