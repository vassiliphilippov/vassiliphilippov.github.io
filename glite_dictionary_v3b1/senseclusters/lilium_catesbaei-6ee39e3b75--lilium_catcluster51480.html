Lilium catesbaei

noun
lily with yellow to scarlet recurved petals
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪliəm kætzˈbi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪliəm kætzˈbi.aɪ / (BrE)
A lily native to the southeastern United States, known for its cup-shaped flowers with recurved petals that range in color from deep yellow to scarlet.

Examples

Lilium catesbaei flower
Botanists found a thriving population of Lilium catesbaei in the damp pine flatwoods of Florida.
Gardeners admire how the cup-shaped flowers of Lilium catesbaei attract pollinators throughout the summer.
The Lilium catesbaei is easily recognized by its striking scarlet petals and long slender stem.
