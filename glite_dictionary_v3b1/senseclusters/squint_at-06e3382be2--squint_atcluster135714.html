squint at

verb
look with narrowed eyes
Pronunciation: / skwɪnt æt / (AmE), / skwɪnt ət / (BrE)
to look at something with partly closed eyes, often because of bright light or difficulty seeing clearly

Examples

To squint at bright light
He squinted at the distant object, trying to make out what it was.
I often squint at my computer screen when the sun is shining.
She had to squint at the tiny writing on the label.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glance at - to look quickly at something
  • degree higher: stare at - to look at something for a long time
