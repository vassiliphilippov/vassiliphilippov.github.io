toft

noun
medieval house plot
Pronunciation: / tɑft /; / tɔft / (AmE), / tɒft / (BrE)
an old term for a plot of land, especially in medieval times, used for building a house or homestead

Examples

A toft plot
In the medieval village, each family was granted a toft for their home and garden.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a stone wall on the old toft near the river.
The lord divided the land into several tofts for new settlers to build their houses.
Each family in the village owned a toft where they built their home and kept animals.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a medieval toft near the old church.
The map showed several tofts and crofts scattered across the countryside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: croft - a small enclosed field or farm, especially in Scotland
  • synonym: homestead - a house and the land surrounding it, especially in the past
  • synonym: plot - a small piece of land for building or gardening
