unbearably

adverb
to an intolerable degree
4.9 0.94/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàb…õr…ôbli / (AmE), /  ånÀàb…õÀêr…ôbli / (BrE)
to a degree that is impossible to tolerate; extremely or excessively

Examples

An unbearably cold day
It became unbearably cold after the sun went down.
She found the pain after the surgery unbearably intense, even with medication.
The noise from the construction site was unbearably loud all day long.

Synonyms

  • intolerably
    6.3 0.20/m
    intolerably describes something that cannot be tolerated in serious or formal situations, while unbearably is more common and general.
  • insufferably
    8.2 0.18/m
    insufferably means extremely unpleasant or annoying to others, while unbearably covers any strong degree that cannot be tolerated.
  • unendurably
    7.9 0.03/m
    unendurably describes something impossible to endure physically or mentally, while unbearably is the everyday word for extreme hardship.
  • unsufferably
    9.1 0.00/m
    unsufferably means extremely hard to endure in practice, while unbearably is a common adverb for things people cannot tolerate.
