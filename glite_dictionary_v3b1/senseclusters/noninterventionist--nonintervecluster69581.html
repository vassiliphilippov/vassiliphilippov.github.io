noninterventionist

noun
opponent of foreign involvement
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌɪntərˈvɛnʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who opposes becoming involved in the affairs or conflicts of other countries

Examples

A noninterventionist leader
The president was described as a noninterventionist who preferred diplomacy over military action.
Many voters supported the noninterventionist stance during the international crisis.
As a noninterventionist, she argued against sending troops abroad.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interventionist - a person who supports becoming involved in other countries' affairs
  • synonym: isolationist - a person who favors staying out of international affairs
