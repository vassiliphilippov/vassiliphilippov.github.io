gray

noun
horse with a light gray or whitish coat
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ / (BrE)
a horse with a light gray or whitish coat, whose coat typically whitens with age.

Examples

A gray horse
Among the horses at the stable, the gray stood out for its elegance.
She has trained a beautiful gray to compete in jumping events.
The young gray galloped across the field, its coat shining in the sun.
She decided to purchase a young grey that would likely become whiter as it aged.
The grey trotted elegantly around the show ring, drawing everyone's attention.
The champion grey was known for its gentle temperament and striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bay - a brown horse with black mane and tail
  • synonym: chestnut - a horse of a reddish-brown color
  • degree lower: dapple - a horse with a mottled or spotted coat pattern
