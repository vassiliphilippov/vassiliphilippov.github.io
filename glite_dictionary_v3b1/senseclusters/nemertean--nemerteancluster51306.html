nemertean

noun
marine worm of the phylum Nemertea
Pronunciation: / nəˈmɜrtiən / (AmE), / nɪˈmɜːtiən / (BrE)
a soft, unsegmented marine worm with a long, threadlike proboscis, belonging to the phylum Nemertea, known for its ability to stretch and contract its body

Examples

A nemertean worm
Among marine invertebrates, nemerteans stand out because of their unique method of prey capture.
Researchers discovered a new species of nemertean living in the deep-sea vent community.
The nemertean is remarkable for its ability to extend its proboscis several times its body length.
