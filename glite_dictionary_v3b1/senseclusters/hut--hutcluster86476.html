hut

noun
small, simple shelter or dwelling
3.6
Pronunciation: / hʌt / (AmE), / hʌt / (BrE)
a small, simple shelter or dwelling, often made from basic materials

Examples

A small hut
During the storm, the travelers took refuge in a hut at the edge of the forest.
The shepherd lived in a simple hut on the hillside, surrounded by his sheep.
Workers built a temporary hut to store their equipment during the construction project.
After a long march, the troops welcomed the warmth of the hut on the base.
During the war, each unit was assigned a hut for sleeping and storing their gear.
The soldiers constructed a hut in the woods as a makeshift headquarters.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: barracks - a building or group of buildings used to house soldiers
  • synonym: cabin - a small, simple house, especially one built of wood
  • synonym: shack - a roughly built hut or cabin, often dilapidated
