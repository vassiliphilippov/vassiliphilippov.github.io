hut

noun
small, simple shelter or dwelling
1.0 12/m
Pronunciation: / hÊŒt / (AmE), / hÊŒt / (BrE)
a small, simple shelter or dwelling, often made from basic materials

Examples

A small hut
During the storm, the travelers took refuge in a hut at the edge of the forest.
The shepherd lived in a simple hut on the hillside, surrounded by his sheep.
Workers built a temporary hut to store their equipment during the construction project.
After a long march, the troops welcomed the warmth of the hut on the base.
During the war, each unit was assigned a hut for sleeping and storing their gear.
The soldiers constructed a hut in the woods as a makeshift headquarters.

Synonyms

  • shack
    1.8 7.42/m
    a shack is a roughly built small dwelling using cheap or salvaged materials, while a hut is a simple shelter made from basic materials.
  • shanty
    6.8 3.11/m
    a shanty is a crudely built shelter made from discarded materials and often found in poor areas, while a hut is simpler.
  • hutch
    6.8 0.03/m
    a hutch is a small, makeshift shelter often temporary and cramped, whereas a hut is a basic dwelling used for everyday shelter.
  • hovel
    7.1 0.94/m
    a hovel is a dirty, poorly kept dwelling that people view as unfit to live in, while a hut is simply a small, basic shelter.
