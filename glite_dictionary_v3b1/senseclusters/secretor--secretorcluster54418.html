secretor

noun
one who releases substances
7.8
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkriːtər / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːtə / (BrE)
a person or organism that produces and releases a particular substance, especially a bodily fluid, into bodily fluids such as saliva or mucus

Examples

A secretor person
Some people are classified as secretors because they release blood group antigens into their saliva.
The test determined whether the patient was a secretor or a nonsecretor.
Researchers studied how secretors and nonsecretors differ in their immune responses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonsecretor - a person who does not release certain substances into bodily fluids
