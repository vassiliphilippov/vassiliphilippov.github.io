scalding

noun
burn from hot liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈskɔldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɔːldɪŋ / (BrE)
an injury to the skin caused by contact with very hot liquid or steam

Examples

A scalding injury
The child suffered a scalding after accidentally spilling boiling water on his hand.
Immediate treatment is important to reduce the pain of a scalding from hot soup.
She wore gloves to protect her skin from a possible scalding while working in the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burn - an injury to the skin caused by heat, fire, or chemicals
  • antonym: frostbite - an injury to the skin caused by extreme cold
