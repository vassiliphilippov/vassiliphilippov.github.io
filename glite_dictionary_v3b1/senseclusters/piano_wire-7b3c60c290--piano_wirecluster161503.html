piano wire

noun
strong steel wire for piano strings
1.8
Pronunciation: / piˈænoʊ waɪɚ / (AmE), / pɪˈænəʊ waɪə / (BrE)
A thin, high-tensile steel wire typically used for the strings in pianos and for other specialized purposes requiring strong, flexible wire.

Examples

Piano wire string
Piano wire is valued for its strength and flexibility in both musical instruments and mechanical devices.
The craftsman used a length of piano wire to replace the broken string on the grand piano.
To create a precise spring mechanism, engineers often rely on high-quality piano wire.
