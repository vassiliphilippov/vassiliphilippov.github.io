plate

verb
coat with a layer of metal

to coat an object with a thin layer of metal, often for protection or decoration

Examples

To plate jewelry
Jewelry makers often plate pieces with rhodium to prevent tarnishing.
The factory plates cutlery with silver to make it more attractive.
They decided to plate the old candlesticks with gold for the anniversary gift.
The jeweler plated the ring with gold to give it a shiny finish.
Old coins are sometimes plated with silver to increase their value.
They plated the metal parts to protect them from rust and corrosion.

Synonyms

  • metallize
    8.4 0.04/m
    metallize is a technical verb for applying a metal layer in industry or labs, while plate is the common term.
  • clad
    6.7 1.39/m
    clad means to cover something with a material, often a thicker outer layer on structures, while plate means a thin metal coating.
