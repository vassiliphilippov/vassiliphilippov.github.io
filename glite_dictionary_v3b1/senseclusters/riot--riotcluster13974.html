riot

noun
violent public disturbance
8.3 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈraɪ.ət/ (AmE), /ˈraɪ.ət/ (BrE)
a violent public disturbance by a crowd, often involving disorder and property damage

Examples

A violent riot
Police struggled to control the riot as violence broke out among the crowd.
Several cities experienced a riot after the controversial court verdict was announced.
The government imposed a curfew to restore order following a night of riot in the capital.
Police struggled to contain the riot as the violence escalated through the night.
Several people were injured during the riot that erupted following the controversial verdict.
The city declared a state of emergency after a riot broke out downtown.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disturbance - a disruption of public order; commotion
  • antonym: peace - a state of calm or absence of violence
  • degree lower: protest - a public demonstration expressing strong objection
  • synonym: rebellion - an act of open resistance against authority
  • degree higher: uprising - an act of resistance or rebellion, often armed, against authority
  • synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion or protest
