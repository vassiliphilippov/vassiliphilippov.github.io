escapism

noun
the tendency to escape from reality through fantasy or activity
4.8 5.1
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈskeɪ.pɪ.zəm/ /; / /ə.ˈskeɪ.pɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈskeɪ.pɪ.zəm/ /; / /ə.ˈskeɪ.pɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the tendency to seek distraction and relief from unpleasant realities, especially by engaging in fantasy or entertainment

Examples

Escapism through fantasy
His constant daydreaming was a clear sign of escapism from his problems.
She turned to escapism by watching fantasy movies when life became overwhelming.
Video games offer a form of escapism for many people after a stressful day.
