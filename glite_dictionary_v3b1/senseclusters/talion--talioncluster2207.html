talion

noun
matching retaliation
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪliən / (AmE), / ˈteɪliən /; / ˈtæliən / (BrE)
the principle or practice of punishment where the penalty is equal to the harm or crime done (for example, 'an eye for an eye')

Examples

Law of talion
The ancient law of talion demanded an eye for an eye as punishment.
Some societies rejected talion in favor of more rehabilitative justice systems.
Debates about talion often arise when discussing the fairness of strict punishments.
The ancient code of talion demanded that punishments directly mirrored the crimes committed.
Many legal systems have moved away from the strict application of talion in favor of rehabilitation.
The concept of talion is often summarized by the phrase 'an eye for an eye'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forgiveness - the act of letting go of resentment or punishment
  • antonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone
  • synonym: retaliation - the act of returning an injury or wrong
  • synonym: retribution - punishment given in return for a wrong
  • synonym: revenge - the act of harming someone in return for a wrong
