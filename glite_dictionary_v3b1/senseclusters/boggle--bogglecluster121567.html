boggle

verb
hesitate or be confused
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑɡəl /; / ˈbɑːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbɒɡl /; / ˈbɒɡəl / (BrE)
to be so surprised, confused, or amazed that you hesitate or cannot think clearly

Examples

To boggle the mind
His mind would boggle whenever he thought about the vastness of the universe.
I saw him boggle in fear before stepping onto the stage.
She began to boggle at the complexity of the instructions given to her.
It still boggles me how quickly technology has advanced in the past decade.
The sheer size of the universe will boggle your mind.
Their generosity boggled everyone at the charity gala.
His story was so incredible that it would boggle anyone's mind.
It boggles me how anyone could solve this in an hour.
The complexity of the puzzle made my mind boggle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to believe or receive something as true without surprise

  • synonym: astonish - to surprise or amaze someone a lot
  • synonym: hesitate - pause before doing something, often due to uncertainty
  • synonym: stagger - to astonish or deeply shock someone
  • synonym: waver - show indecision or hesitation
