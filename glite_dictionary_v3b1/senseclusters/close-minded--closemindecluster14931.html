close-minded

adjective
unwilling to consider new ideas
Pronunciation: / kloʊs ˈmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / kləʊs ˈmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
Unwilling to consider new or different ideas; having or showing a rigid or narrow outlook.

Examples

A close-minded person
Being close-minded can prevent people from learning and growing.
His close-minded attitude made it difficult for him to accept alternative solutions.
She refused to join the discussion, appearing close-minded to differing perspectives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: narrow-minded - having or showing a limited outlook
  • antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas
