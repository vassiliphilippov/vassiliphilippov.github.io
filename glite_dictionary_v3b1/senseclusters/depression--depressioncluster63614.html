depression

noun
area of low atmospheric pressure
5.4 4.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈpre.ʃən/ /; / /də.ˈpre.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈpre.ʃən/ (BrE)
An area where the atmospheric pressure is lower than that of the surrounding region, often associated with bad weather such as rain or snow.

Examples

A weather depression
A deep depression in the Atlantic resulted in several days of stormy weather.
As the depression passed over the region, temperatures dropped and winds picked up.
The weather forecast predicted a depression approaching from the west, likely to cause heavy rain.
