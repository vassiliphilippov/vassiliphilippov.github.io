interzone

proper noun
fictional liminal space
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌzoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌzəʊn / (BrE)
a fictional or symbolic place representing a liminal, lawless, or ambiguous space, especially in the works of William S. Burroughs

Examples

Burroughs's Interzone
In Burroughs's novel, the interzone is a surreal city where reality and fantasy blend together.
The artist described his studio as an interzone between chaos and creativity.
Many readers interpret the interzone as a metaphor for cultural and personal transition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: borderland - a region near a border, often with unclear rules or identity
  • synonym: no-man's-land - an area between two opposing forces or boundaries, often lawless or unclaimed
