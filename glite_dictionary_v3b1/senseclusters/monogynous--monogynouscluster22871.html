monogynous

adjective
having only one chief wife at a time
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑːdʒɪnəs / (AmE), / məˈnɒdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
having or relating to a system of marriage or relationship in which a man has only one wife at a time (though he may have concubines)

Examples

A monogynous marriage
A monogynous household had clear rules about inheritance and succession.
Historically, some societies practiced monogynous marriage, distinguishing the legal wife from secondary partners.
The tribal leader adhered to a monogynous system, taking only one chief wife but several concubines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monogamous - having only one mate, spouse, or partner at a time
  • antonym: polygynous - having more than one wife at a time
