for the plot's sake

adverb
for the benefit of a story's plot
6.1
Pronunciation: / fər ðə plɑts seɪk / (AmE), / fə ðə plɒts seɪk / (BrE)
for the purpose of improving or advancing the storyline of a book, film, or other narrative work, often used when an element is added primarily to serve the story rather than realism or character development.

Examples

For the plot's sake
He introduced a dramatic twist for the plot's sake, keeping the audience guessing until the end.
Some scenes in the movie felt unnatural, as if they were there for the plot's sake rather than logic.
The author changed the character's background for the plot's sake, making the story more engaging.

Related Terms

  • synonym: for the sake of - for the purpose or benefit of something
