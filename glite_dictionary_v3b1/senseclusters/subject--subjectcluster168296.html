Sentence subject
In formal logic, the subject and predicate play distinct roles.
In the proposition 'All dogs are mammals,' 'dogs' is the subject.
The subject of the statement is what the proposition asserts something about.
'Happiness' is the subject in the phrase 'Happiness is important.'
Every complete sentence must have a subject and a predicate.
In the sentence 'The cat slept,' subject is 'The cat.'
Can you identify the subject?
In English, the subject usually comes before the verb.
