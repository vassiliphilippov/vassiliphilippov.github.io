turgidity

noun
pompous or inflated language
9.9 8.2
Pronunciation: /tɜːr.ˈɡɪ.dɪ.tiː/ /; / /tɜːr.ˈɡɪ.də.tiː/ (AmE), /tɜː.ˈɡɪ.dɪ.tiː/ /; / /tɜː.ˈɡɪ.də.tiː/ (BrE)
language or style that is pompous, inflated, or overly elaborate, often to the point of being difficult to understand or insincere

Examples

The turgidity of language
Despite the turgidity of the prose, some readers admired its elaborate style.
His speech was criticized for its turgidity and lack of clear meaning.
The novel suffers from episodes of turgidity that obscure its main message.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bombast - speech or writing that is meant to sound important or impressive but is not sincere or meaningful
  • synonym: inflation - the use of overly elaborate or grand language
  • antonym: simplicity - the quality of being plain and uncomplicated in language or style
