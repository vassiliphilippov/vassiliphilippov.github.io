false tamarisk

noun
Eurasian shrub resembling tamarisk
Pronunciation: / fɔls ˈtæmərɪsk / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈtæmərɪsk / (BrE)
A Eurasian shrub that resembles the tamarisk, often found in dry regions.

Examples

The false tamarisk shrub
Although called false tamarisk, this shrub belongs to a different genus than true tamarisks.
Gardeners often plant false tamarisk for its feathery foliage and resilience.
The false tamarisk thrives in sandy soils along riverbanks in its native Eurasia.
