uneasy

adjective
anxious or uncomfortable
3.2 9/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàizi / (AmE), /  ånÀàiÀêzi / (BrE)
feeling or causing anxiety, discomfort, or a lack of confidence; not relaxed or at ease

Examples

Uneasy feeling
He gave an uneasy smile when asked about the missing files.
She felt uneasy about walking home alone after dark.
The quiet in the office made everyone feel uneasy.
He gave an uneasy smile as he entered the silent room.
Many students are uneasy when asked to speak in public for the first time.
She always feels uneasy meeting new people at large gatherings.
After eating the unfamiliar meal, she felt uneasy for hours.
He shifted in his chair, feeling uneasy as the interview continued.
There was an uneasy silence in the room that made everyone uncomfortable.

Synonyms

    2.7 12/m
    anxious is a strong feeling of worry about a future event, while uneasy is a milder, general discomfort.
  • uncomfortable
    1.7 30/m
    uncomfortable often describes physical or social pain, while uneasy focuses on a mental lack of calm or confidence.
  • worried
    1.0 60/m
    worried is active concern about a specific problem, while uneasy is a broader, low-level feeling of not being at ease.
  • nervous
    1.3 50/m
    nervous is a shaky, high-energy worry often with physical signs, while uneasy is quieter and more about inner discomfort.
  • apprehensive
    5.0 3/m
    apprehensive feels like fear that something bad will happen, while uneasy is more general worry or lack of comfort.
  • ill at ease
    5.5 0.5/m
    ill at ease describes social awkwardness or self-consciousness, while uneasy is a wider discomfort not only in social settings.
