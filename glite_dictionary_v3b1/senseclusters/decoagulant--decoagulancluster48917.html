decoagulant

noun
medicine that prevents blood clotting
8.1
Pronunciation: / diˈkoʊ.æɡ.jə.lənt / (AmE), / diˈkəʊ.æɡ.jʊ.lənt / (BrE)
a substance or medicine that prevents or slows the clotting of blood

Examples

A decoagulant medicine
Patients taking a decoagulant should have their blood monitored regularly.
Research is ongoing to develop safer decoagulant medications for people at risk of thrombosis.
The doctor administered a decoagulant to prevent further blood clots after the surgery.
