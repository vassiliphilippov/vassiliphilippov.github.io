molting

noun
periodic shedding of skin or covering
4.8 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊl.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈməʊl.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process by which an animal periodically sheds its skin, feathers, or exoskeleton in order to allow for growth or renewal

Examples

Bird molting season
Birds usually go through molting once a year to replace old feathers.
During molting, crabs are especially vulnerable to predators.
The snake began its molting just before the start of summer.
During moulting, birds often lose many feathers and temporarily look scruffy.
Many insects go through several phases of moulting before reaching adulthood.
The snake began moulting after outgrowing its old skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ecdysis - the scientific term for the process of molting in arthropods and reptiles
  • synonym: shedding - the act of casting off or letting something fall away (e.g., skin or hair)
