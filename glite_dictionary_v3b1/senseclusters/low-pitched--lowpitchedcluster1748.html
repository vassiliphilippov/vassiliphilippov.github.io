low-pitched

adjective
set at a low angle or slant (e.g., roof)
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ pɪtʃt / (AmE), / ˈləʊ pɪtʃt / (BrE)
(of a roof or surface) set at a low angle or slant, not steep.

Examples

Low-pitched roof
A low-pitched ceiling can make a room feel more intimate but less spacious.
The architect designed the building with low-pitched eaves to match the traditional style.
The cottage has a low-pitched roof that gives it a quaint appearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steep - having a sharp or high angle or incline
