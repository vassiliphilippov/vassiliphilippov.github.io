oratorio

noun
large musical composition based on a religious theme
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrəˈtɔrioʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒrəˈtɔːriəʊ / (BrE)
a large musical composition for orchestra, choir, and soloists, typically based on a religious theme and performed without theatrical staging

Examples

A oratorio performance
Handel's oratorio 'Messiah' is performed by choirs around the world every year.
The choir rehearsed the difficult passages in the new oratorio for several weeks.
Unlike an opera, an oratorio is performed without costumes or scenery.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cantata - a shorter vocal composition with instrumental accompaniment, often religious
  • degree higher: opera - a staged dramatic composition with orchestral accompaniment, usually secular
