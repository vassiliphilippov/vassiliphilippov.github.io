perimysium

noun
connective tissue covering a muscle bundle
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪˈmɪziəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈmɪzɪəm / (BrE)
The sheath of connective tissue that surrounds and covers a bundle of muscle fibers (fascicle) in skeletal muscle.

Examples

The perimysium sheath
Injury to the perimysium may disrupt normal muscle function and healing.
The perimysium contains blood vessels that supply nutrients to muscle fibers.
Under the microscope, the perimysium appears as a distinct connective layer between muscle fascicles.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: endomysium - the thin connective tissue surrounding individual muscle fibers
  • degree higher: epimysium - the outermost layer of connective tissue surrounding an entire muscle
