inkhorn

noun
old ink container
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋkˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkˌhɔːn / (BrE)
a small container, often made of horn, used to hold ink, especially in the past

Examples

An old inkhorn
The old desk still had an inkhorn set into the corner for writing letters.
Before fountain pens, writers would refill their quills from an inkhorn on the table.
The museum displayed a medieval inkhorn alongside ancient manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inkwell - a small container for ink used in writing with a pen
