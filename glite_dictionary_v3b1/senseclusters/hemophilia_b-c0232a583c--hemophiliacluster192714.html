hemophilia B

noun
genetic bleeding disorder lacking factor IX
6.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåhiÀêm…ôÀàf…™li…ô ÀàbiÀê /; / hiÀêm…ôÀàf…™li…ô bi / (AmE), / ÀåhiÀêm…ôÀàf…™li.…ô ÀàbiÀê /; / hiÀêm…ôÀàf…™l…™…ô biÀê / (BrE)
A rare hereditary disorder in which the blood does not clot properly because of a congenital deficiency of clotting factor IX, resulting in prolonged bleeding; similar to hemophilia A.

Examples

Hemophilia B disorder
Many cases of hemophilia B are inherited and detected early in childhood.
The patient was diagnosed with hemophilia B after experiencing severe bleeding after a minor injury.
Treatment for hemophilia B often involves regular infusions of clotting factor IX.
Carriers of the haemophilia B gene may not show symptoms but can pass the disorder to their children.
The boy was diagnosed with haemophilia B after experiencing severe bruising and unexplained bleeding.
Treatment for haemophilia B often involves regular infusions of clotting factor IX.

Synonyms

  • Christmas disease
    8.2 0.00/m
    Christmas disease is the traditional name used in medical texts for factor IX deficiency, while hemophilia B is the current clinical term.
