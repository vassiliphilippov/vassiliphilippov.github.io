formalistic

adjective
strict adherence to established forms
3.6 7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔːr.məˈlɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɔː.məˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
concerned with or characterized by strict adherence to prescribed forms or conventions, especially in art or religion.

Examples

A formalistic style
Her approach to poetry was highly formalistic, focusing on structure over personal expression.
Some critics claim his paintings are too formalistic and lack emotional depth.
The ceremony followed a formalistic script, leaving little room for improvisation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conventional - based on or in accordance with what is generally done or believed
  • antonym: innovative - featuring new methods; advanced and original, opposite of formalistic
