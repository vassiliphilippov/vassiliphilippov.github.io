primitive art

noun
art by untrained, self-taught creators
3.4 5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪmətɪv ɑrt / (AmE), / ˈprɪmɪtɪv ɑːt / (BrE)
A genre of art created by artists without formal training, often characterized by a simple, unschooled style and typically made by individuals who do not view themselves as professional artists.

Examples

Primitive art style
Collectors value primitive art for its raw creativity and unique perspective.
Many critics admire the authenticity found in primitive art compared to academic styles.
The museum features an impressive collection of primitive art from rural communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outsider art - art created outside the boundaries of official culture, often by self-taught or unconventional artists
