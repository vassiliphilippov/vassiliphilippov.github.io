up to your neck

adjective
deeply involved in a situation
5.0
Pronunciation: / ʌp tə jʊr nɛk / (AmE), / ʌp tə jɔː nɛk / (BrE)
very deeply involved in a difficult, unpleasant, or demanding situation, often with little hope of escape or relief.

Examples

Be up to your neck in work
By the end of the semester, she found herself up to her neck in assignments.
Ever since the company merged, I've been up to my neck in paperwork.
They were up to their necks in debt after buying the new house.
