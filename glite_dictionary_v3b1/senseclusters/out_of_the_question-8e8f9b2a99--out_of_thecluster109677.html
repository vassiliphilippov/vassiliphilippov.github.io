out of the question

adjective
not possible; totally unlikely
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv ðə ˈkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə ˈkwɛstʃən / (BrE)
not possible, not allowed, or not to be considered

Examples

Out of the question idea
A holiday abroad this year is out of the question because we can't afford it.
Asking for a pay rise right now is simply out of the question.
It was out of the question for them to leave the house at night due to the storm.
Another loan from the bank is out of the question given our current debt.
For many families, moving to the city is simply out of the question due to high costs.
Skipping the meeting is out of the question if you want to keep your job.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impossible - not able to be done or achieved
  • antonym: possible - able to be done or achieved
