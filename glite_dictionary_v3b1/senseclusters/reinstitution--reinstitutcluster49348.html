reinstitution

noun
return to institution
9.0
Pronunciation: /riː.ɪn.stɪ.ˈtuː.ʃən/ /; / /riː.ɪn.stɪ.ˈtjuː.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.ɪn.stɪ.ˈtjuː.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.ɪn.stɪ.ˈtjuː.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of placing someone back into institutional care, such as a hospital, prison, or psychiatric facility

Examples

Reinstitution of a patient
Following his relapse, the court ordered the reinstitution of the patient in a psychiatric hospital.
The reinstitution of the program's strict rules was necessary after several incidents.
Social workers debated whether reinstitution was the best option for the young offender.

Related Terms

  • synonym: readmission - the act of being admitted again to an institution
  • antonym: release - the act of allowing someone to leave an institution
