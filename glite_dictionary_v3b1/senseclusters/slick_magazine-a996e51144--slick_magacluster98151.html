slick magazine

noun
glossy, high-quality magazine
5.9
Pronunciation: / slɪk məˈɡæzɪn / (AmE), / slɪk məˈɡæzɪn / (BrE)
A magazine printed on high-quality, glossy paper, often featuring photographs and sophisticated design.

Examples

A slick magazine
She picked up a slick magazine from the coffee table to browse through the latest fashion trends.
The photographs in the slick magazine looked vibrant because of the high-quality paper.
Unlike old pulp publications, a slick magazine feels substantial and looks visually stunning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pulp magazine - magazine printed on cheap, low-quality paper
