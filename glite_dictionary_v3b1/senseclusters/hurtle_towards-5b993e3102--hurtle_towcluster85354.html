hurtle towards

verb
move rapidly and forcefully toward something
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜr.tl̩ təˈwɔrdz / (AmE), / ˈhɜː.tl̩ təˈwɔːdz / (BrE)
to move quickly and violently in the direction of something, often with a sense of being out of control or unstoppable

Examples

To hurtle towards Earth
He watched the asteroid hurtle towards Earth on the computer screen.
The train began to hurtle towards the station at an alarming speed.
With brakes failing, the car hurtled towards the intersection, unable to stop.
