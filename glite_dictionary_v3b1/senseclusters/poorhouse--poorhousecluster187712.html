poorhouse

noun
housing for the poor provided by public funds
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔrˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɔːˌhaʊs / (BrE)
an establishment, formerly maintained at public expense, to house and support people who were poor or homeless

Examples

The poorhouse building
After losing his job and savings, he was forced to live in the poorhouse.
Many communities established a poorhouse to take care of their destitute citizens.
The novel describes the harsh conditions faced by residents of a nineteenth-century poorhouse.

  • synonym: workhouse - an institution where those unable to support themselves were offered accommodation and employment
