repercussion

noun
movement back after an impact

a movement back caused by an impact; a rebound or reflection

Examples

Physical repercussion
The repercussion of the dropped ball sent it bouncing across the floor.
The scientist measured the repercussion of the shock wave on the metal plate.
The sudden repercussion from the collision damaged the fragile equipment.

Synonyms

  • rebound
    3.3 1.13/m
    rebound is a sudden return after a hit, while repercussion is the movement back caused by that impact.
  • bounce-back
    3.0 0.11/m
    bounce-back is the quick return of an object like a ball after it hits, while repercussion describes the resulting backward movement.
  • recoil
    6.3 3.28/m
    recoil is a sharp backward jerk often from a strong blow or gunfire, while repercussion is a general rebound.
  • backlash
    7.0 0.59/m
    backlash is a mechanical or forceful reaction often in machines or politics, while repercussion names the physical rebound.
