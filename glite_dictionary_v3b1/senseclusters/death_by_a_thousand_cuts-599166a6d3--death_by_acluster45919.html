death by a thousand cuts

noun
harm from many small problems
Pronunciation: / dɛθ baɪ ə ˈθaʊzənd kʌts / (AmE), / dɛθ baɪ ə ˈθaʊzənd kʌts / (BrE)
a gradual and cumulative worsening or harm caused by many small, seemingly insignificant problems or actions

Examples

Death by a thousand cuts effect
For small businesses, new regulations often result in death by a thousand cuts rather than a single catastrophic failure.
Over years, rising fees and declining benefits caused employees to experience death by a thousand cuts.
The constant changes to the project plan felt like death by a thousand cuts to the team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slow death - gradual weakening or demise
  • antonym: sudden collapse - a rapid or abrupt failure or end
