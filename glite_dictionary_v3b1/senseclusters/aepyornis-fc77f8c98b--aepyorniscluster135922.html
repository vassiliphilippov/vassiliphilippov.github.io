Aepyornis

noun
genus of extinct giant birds (elephant birds)
Pronunciation: / ˌiːpiˈɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˌiːpɪˈɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, extinct flightless birds, known as elephant birds, that were native to Madagascar.

Examples

The extinct Aepyornis
Legends of the roc may have been inspired by stories of the extinct Aepyornis.
Researchers discovered new fossils attributed to the Aepyornis genus.
The Aepyornis was once the largest bird on earth, native to Madagascar.
