serve two masters

verb
try to satisfy conflicting authorities
3.6 6.6
Pronunciation: / sɝv tu ˈmæstɚz / (AmE), / sɜːv tuː ˈmɑːstəz / (BrE)
to attempt to satisfy two authorities or groups with conflicting interests, making it impossible to please both fully

Examples

To serve two masters carefully
If you serve two masters, you risk letting down both sides.
She realized she couldn't serve two masters when her loyalties were divided between her boss and her family.
Trying to serve two masters left him stressed and ultimately dissatisfied both at work and home.
