hop off

verb
leave or get off quickly
3.1 0.47/m
Pronunciation: / hÉ‘p É”f / (AmE), / hÉ’p É’f / (BrE)
to leave or get off a vehicle or place quickly and often lightly

Examples

To hop off the bus
I need to hop off the bus at the next stop.
Let me hop off the call; I'll talk to you later.
She hopped off her bike and rushed inside.

Synonyms

  • hop out
    2.8 0.78/m
    hop out means getting out quickly and energetically, while hop off suggests leaving a vehicle or place more lightly and briefly.
  • get off
    2.5 5.31/m
    get off means leaving or dismounting a vehicle in a neutral way, while hop off implies doing this quickly and lightly.

Related Terms
