countercurrent

noun
opposing tendency; movement contrary to the main flow
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊn.tɚˌkɝ.ənt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.təˌkʌr.ənt / (BrE)
a tendency, movement, or action that runs counter to the prevailing direction or main activity, especially within a group or society

Examples

Countercurrent of opinion
Although most of the team supported the merger, a countercurrent of opposition emerged during discussions.
Social reform often faces a countercurrent of traditional values and resistance.
There was a strong countercurrent of dissent among the younger members of the organization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crosscurrent - a conflicting tendency or movement (especially within a group); similar to 'countercurrent'
