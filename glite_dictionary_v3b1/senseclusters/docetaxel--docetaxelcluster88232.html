docetaxel

noun
chemotherapy drug
Pronunciation: / ˌdoʊsəˈtæksəl / (AmE), / ˌdəʊsəˈtæksəl / (BrE)
a chemotherapy drug used to treat certain types of cancer, such as breast, lung, and prostate cancer

Examples

Docetaxel chemotherapy drug
The oncologist recommended docetaxel as part of the patient's treatment plan for lung cancer.
After several cycles of docetaxel, her tumor began to shrink noticeably.
Some patients experience hair loss and fatigue while receiving docetaxel chemotherapy.
