range animal

noun
grazing livestock of open grasslands
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪndʒ ˌænɪml̩ / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒ ˌænɪml̩ / (BrE)
an animal, such as a horse, cow, or sheep, that lives and grazes in the open grasslands, especially in western North America

Examples

A range animal grazes
Sheep are often managed as a range animal on large, open ranchlands.
The cattleman checked on every range animal after last night's storm.
Wild horses are classic examples of a range animal found in the American West.
