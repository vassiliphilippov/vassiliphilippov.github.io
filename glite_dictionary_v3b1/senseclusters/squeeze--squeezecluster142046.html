squeeze

verb
hold someone closely in your arms
1.0 4.89/m
Pronunciation: / skwiÀêz / (AmE), / skwiÀêz / (BrE)
to hold someone tightly in your arms, usually to show affection

Examples

To squeeze a friend
He squeezed his friend goodbye at the airport.
She gave her son a big squeeze before he went to school.
Whenever they meet, they squeeze each other tightly in a warm hug.

Synonyms

  • hug
    1.0 14/m
    hug is a warm, open holding to show affection, while squeeze is a tighter, often brief pressure in the arms.
  • embrace
    2.8 11/m
    embrace is a more formal, poetic tight holding to show feeling, while squeeze is a more casual tight pressure.
  • wrap arms around
    2.8 3.90/m
    wrap arms around means putting your arms around someone protectively and fully, while squeeze focuses on tight pressure.
  • cuddle
    1.0 2.80/m
    cuddle is holding someone close for comfort, warmth, or affection, while squeeze stresses a quick, tight pressure.
  • hold close
    1.4 0.03/m
    hold close is keeping someone firmly near for safety or support, while squeeze implies a tighter, often shorter affectionate grip.
  • clasp
    2.6 5.14/m
    clasp is fastening or holding something tightly with hands, while squeeze describes an affectionate tight hold in someone's arms.
  • clinch
    4.8 0.18/m
    clinch is holding tightly to stop movement in sport or during a kiss, while squeeze is mainly an affectionate arm hold.
