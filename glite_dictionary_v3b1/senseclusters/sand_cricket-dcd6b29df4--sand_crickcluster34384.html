sand cricket

noun
large wingless nocturnal grasshopper of the Pacific US
6.3
Pronunciation: / sænd ˈkrɪkɪt / (AmE), / sand ˈkrɪkɪt / (BrE)
a large, wingless, nocturnal grasshopper that burrows in loose soil along the Pacific coast of the United States

Examples

The sand cricket burrows
At night, the sand cricket emerges to search for food along the Pacific shoreline.
Researchers study the sand cricket to understand its unique adaptations to sandy environments.
The sand cricket is known for its ability to burrow deep into the loose coastal soil.
