supraspinatus

noun
shoulder muscle
8.2
Pronunciation: /suː.prə.spɪ.ˈneɪ.təs/ /; / /suː.prə.spɪ.ˈnæ.təs/ (AmE), /suː.prə.spɪ.ˈneɪ.təs/ /; / /suː.prə.spɪ.ˈnæ.təs/ (BrE)
a muscle in the upper back/shoulder (part of the rotator cuff) that helps lift the arm and stabilize the shoulder joint

Examples

The supraspinatus muscle
The physical therapist showed exercises to strengthen the supraspinatus after the injury.
A tear in the supraspinatus can make it difficult to lift your arm overhead.
The MRI revealed inflammation in the patient's supraspinatus muscle.
The supraspinatus is often injured in athletes who perform repetitive overhead motions.
Physical therapy can help strengthen the supraspinatus after a shoulder injury.
A torn supraspinatus may cause pain when lifting the arm above the head.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deltoid - the large muscle covering the shoulder joint
  • synonym: infraspinatus - a muscle below the spine of the scapula, also part of the rotator cuff
