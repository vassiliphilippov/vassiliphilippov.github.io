humanitarian

adjective
concerned with promoting human welfare
3.7 3.4
Pronunciation: /hjuː.mæn.ə.ˈte.riː.ən/ /; / /juː.mæn.ə.ˈte.riː.ən/ (AmE), /hjuː.mæn.ə.ˈteə.riː.ən/ /; / /juː.mæn.ə.ˈteə.riː.ən/ (BrE)
concerned with or seeking to promote human welfare, compassion, and social reform

Examples

Humanitarian aid work
Many charities are driven by humanitarian principles rather than profit.
She took a humanitarian approach to the refugee crisis, advocating for their rights.
The flood victims received humanitarian aid from several countries.
Many organizations provide humanitarian aid to countries affected by war and natural disasters.
She has always been involved in humanitarian work, focusing on improving healthcare in developing nations.
The government launched a humanitarian mission to deliver food and medical supplies to refugees.

  • antonym: selfish - lacking concern for others; concerned chiefly with one's own personal profit or pleasure
