Wyclif

proper noun
English theologian and reformer (1328–1384)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪklɪf / (AmE), / ˈwɪklɪf / (BrE)
(1328–1384) English theologian who criticized Roman Catholic doctrine; his ideas anticipated the Protestant Reformation.

Examples

Wyclif Bible translation
Wyclif translated the Bible into English, making it accessible to the common people.
As a critic of church practices, Wyclif faced opposition from both religious and political authorities.
The teachings of Wyclif influenced later reformers such as Jan Hus and Martin Luther.
