wakeful

adjective
alert and watchful
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪkfəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪkfʊl / (BrE)
Alert and watchful, especially in a careful or cautious way; on the lookout for possible danger.

Examples

Wakeful guard
A wakeful parent kept a close eye on the playground from the bench.
Her wakeful attitude helped her notice the subtle change in her surroundings.
The guards remained wakeful throughout the night, alert for any sign of movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inattentive - not paying attention or being watchful
  • synonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
  • synonym: watchful - being alert and observing closely
