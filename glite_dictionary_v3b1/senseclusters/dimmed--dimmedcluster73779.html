dimmed

adjective
made less bright
3.1
Pronunciation: / dɪmd / (AmE), / dɪmd / (BrE)
(of a light or brightness) made less bright or intense; having had brightness reduced.

Examples

The dimmed lights
During the performance, the theater's dimmed lights signaled the show was about to begin.
She prefers dimmed headlights when driving at night to reduce glare.
The room was relaxing with its dimmed lamps and soft music.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting much light; opposite of dim
  • degree lower: dim - not bright; lacking in light or brightness
