visualize

verb
make visible; render perceptible
3.5 3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʒuəˌlaɪz /; / ˈvɪʒəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəlaɪz /; / ˈvɪʒʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something visible to the eye, especially by using technology or special techniques (such as medical imaging)

Examples

To visualize a tumor
Advanced telescopes allow astronomers to visualize distant galaxies previously hidden from view.
The special dye helps visualize blood flow in the brain during the scan.
With this new imaging software, doctors can visualize organs in remarkable detail.
A contrast agent is sometimes used to help visualize blood vessels during the procedure.
Doctors visualized the tumor in her kidney using advanced imaging techniques.
The technician was able to visualize the blockage in the artery with a special X-ray scan.
Modern MRI machines can visualise areas of the brain in great detail.
The doctor was able to visualise the tumour using a CT scan.
The procedure allows surgeons to visualise blood vessels before operating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something from being seen; to hide
  • synonym: reveal - to make something known or visible that was hidden
  • synonym: visualize - to make an internal part of the body visible, especially by means of an X-ray or similar scanning technique (American spelling)
