codifiability

noun
ability to be codified
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊdəˌfaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌkəʊdɪfəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be organized or expressed in a systematic code or set of rules

Examples

The codifiability of rules
The codifiability of scientific procedures helps ensure consistency across different laboratories.
Some traditions lack codifiability and are passed down informally through generations.
Legal experts debated the codifiability of international human rights standards.
