diddle

verb
to waste time
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdɪd.əl/ (AmE), /ˈdɪd.əl/ (BrE)
to waste time or behave in a silly or playful way instead of being serious or productive

Examples

To diddle around all day
He spent the afternoon diddling around the house instead of cleaning.
Stop diddling and help me set the table for dinner.
They were diddling with their phones instead of paying attention in class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dawdle - to move or act slowly and waste time
  • synonym: fiddle - to play or tinker aimlessly
  • antonym: focus - to concentrate attention or effort
