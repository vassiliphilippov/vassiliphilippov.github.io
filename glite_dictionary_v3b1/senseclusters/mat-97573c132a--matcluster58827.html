MAT

noun
medication-assisted treatment for addiction
4.9 0.26/m

(in medicine) medication-assisted treatment: a clinical approach for treating substance use disorders that combines prescription medications with counseling and behavioral therapies.

Examples

MAT for addiction
Hospitals increasingly use MAT to treat patients with opioid dependence.
As part of his recovery plan, he started MAT and attended weekly counseling sessions.
Public health programs recommend MAT alongside psychosocial support to reduce overdose risk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: medication-assisted therapy - Different phrasing sometimes used for the same clinical approach combining medication and therapy.
  • synonym: opioid substitution therapy - A related treatment approach in which safer opioid medications are prescribed to reduce harm and withdrawal symptoms.
