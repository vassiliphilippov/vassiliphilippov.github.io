Jesse Owens

proper noun
U.S. Olympic athlete (1913–1980)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛsi ˈoʊwɪnz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛsi ˈəʊɪnz / (BrE)
(1913–1980) A Black American track and field athlete who won four gold medals at the 1936 Berlin Olympic Games, challenging Nazi ideas of racial superiority.

Examples

Jesse Owens Olympic winner
Jesse Owens won four gold medals at the 1936 Olympic Games in Berlin.
Many people see Jesse Owens as a symbol of courage and equality in sports.
The achievements of Jesse Owens inspired generations of athletes worldwide.
