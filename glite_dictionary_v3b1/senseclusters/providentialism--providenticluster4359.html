providentialism

noun
belief in divine guidance
9.9
Pronunciation: / prəˌvɪdənˈʃəlɪzəm / (AmE), / prəˌvɪdənˈʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that events in life are controlled or guided by divine intervention or fate

Examples

Providentialism belief
Many people found comfort in providentialism during times of uncertainty and hardship.
The idea of providentialism influenced her interpretation of historical events.
His belief in providentialism led him to trust that everything happened for a reason.

Related Terms

  • antonym: atheism - the belief that there is no deity or divine intervention
  • synonym: determinism - the doctrine that all events are determined by causes external to the will
  • synonym: fatalism - the belief that all events are predetermined and inevitable
