gnaw at

verb
gradually wear down or erode
9.2 5.0
Pronunciation: / nɔ æt / (AmE), / nɔː æt / (BrE)
to gradually reduce, weaken, or wear down something, especially someone's feelings, confidence, or resolve

Examples

Doubt gnaws at confidence
Doubt began to gnaw at her confidence as the competition drew near.
Regret can gnaw at a person long after a mistake is made.
Worry about his job security continued to gnaw at him for months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erode - to gradually wear away or be gradually worn away
