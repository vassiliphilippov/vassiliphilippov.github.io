lumbar puncture

noun
medical procedure to remove spinal fluid
6.6 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈlʌm.bɑːr ˈpʌŋk.tʃər/ /; / /ˈlʌm.bər ˈpʌŋk.tʃər/ (AmE), /ˈlʌm.bɑː ˈpʌŋk.tʃə/ /; / /ˈlʌm.bə ˈpʌŋk.tʃə/ (BrE)
a medical procedure in which fluid is removed from the lower part of the spinal cord for diagnostic or therapeutic purposes

Examples

A lumbar puncture procedure
A lumbar puncture can provide important information about infections or bleeding in the brain.
After the lumbar puncture, she was instructed to remain lying down for an hour to prevent complications.
The doctor performed a lumbar puncture to help diagnose the cause of the patient's headaches.
