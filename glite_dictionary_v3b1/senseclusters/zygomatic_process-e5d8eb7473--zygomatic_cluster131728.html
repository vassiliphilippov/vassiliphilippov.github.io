zygomatic process

noun
bony projection of the temporal bone in the skull
8.5
Pronunciation: / zaɪɡəˈmætɪk ˈprɑsɛs / (AmE), / zaɪɡəˈmatɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a slender projection of the temporal bone that helps form and strengthen the zygomatic arch in the skull

Examples

The zygomatic process bone
A well-developed zygomatic process provides structural support to the face.
In anatomy class, we learned about the role of the zygomatic process in forming the cheekbone.
The surgeon examined the zygomatic process to assess the extent of the fracture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: zygomatic arch - the bony arch of the cheek formed by parts of the zygomatic and temporal bones
