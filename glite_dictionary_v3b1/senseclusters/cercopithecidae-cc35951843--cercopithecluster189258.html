Cercopithecidae

noun
family of Old World monkeys
Pronunciation: / sɜrˌkoʊpɪˈθiːdəˌi / (AmE), / sɜːˌkɒpɪˈθiːdɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of Old World monkeys native to Africa and Asia, including baboons, macaques, colobus monkeys, and related species.

Examples

The Cercopithecidae family
Scientists study the social behaviors of Cercopithecidae to better understand primate evolution.
The Cercopithecidae family includes both baboons and macaques, two of the most recognizable monkey groups.
Unlike New World monkeys, members of the Cercopithecidae have narrow, downward-facing nostrils.
