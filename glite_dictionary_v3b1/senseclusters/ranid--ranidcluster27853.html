ranid

noun
frog of the family Ranidae
Pronunciation: / ˈrænɪd / (AmE), / ˈrænɪd / (BrE)
Any frog belonging to the family Ranidae, typically characterized by smooth, moist skin and long, powerful hind legs adapted for swimming and jumping.

Examples

A ranid frog
The green frog is a common ranid found throughout North America.
The researchers studied a population of ranids in the wetland ecosystem.
Unlike toads, ranids usually have smooth, moist skin and spend much of their time near water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frog - A tailless amphibian with a short squat body and long hind legs for leaping, related to the family Ranidae
