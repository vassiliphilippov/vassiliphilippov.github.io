decon

verb
to remove contaminants
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːkɑn / (AmE), / ˈdiːkɒn / (BrE)
to clean or remove hazardous substances, such as chemicals or biological agents, from something thoroughly

Examples

To decon equipment
After handling hazardous materials, workers must decon their protective suits before leaving the area.
The emergency team quickly deconned the equipment to prevent the spread of chemicals.
It is important to decon all surfaces after a laboratory spill.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contaminate - to make something impure or unsafe by adding a harmful substance
  • synonym: decontaminate - to remove dangerous substances from something
  • synonym: sanitize - to make something clean and free from bacteria
