first strike

noun
pre-emptive nuclear attack
2.4 6.9
Pronunciation: /fɜːrst straɪk/ (AmE), /fɜːst straɪk/ (BrE)
a pre-emptive attack using nuclear weapons against an enemy capable of nuclear retaliation, intended to destroy their ability to respond

Examples

Nuclear first strike
A policy of no first strike is meant to reassure other nuclear powers.
During the Cold War, both sides feared the possibility of a first strike scenario.
The president warned that any first strike would be met with devastating retaliation.
