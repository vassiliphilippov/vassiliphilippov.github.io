chain stitch

noun
looped embroidery or sewing stitch
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn stɪtʃ / (AmE), / tʃeɪn stɪtʃ / (BrE)
a stitch used in embroidery, sewing, or crochet that forms a chain-like pattern by looping thread or yarn through previous loops

Examples

Chain stitch embroidery
She learned how to do a chain stitch in her embroidery class.
The border of the napkin was decorated with a delicate chain stitch.
Using a chain stitch makes the pattern stand out beautifully on the fabric.
A neat chain stitch gives the edge a clean, decorative finish.
I started the scarf by making a row of chain stitches with blue yarn.
The pattern requires you to use a chain stitch for the border.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backstitch - a sewing stitch for solid lines, often used to strengthen seams
  • degree higher: single crochet - a basic crochet stitch building on the chain
