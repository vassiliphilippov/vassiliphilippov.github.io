Reaganomics

noun
U.S. economic policy 1980s
8.1
Pronunciation: /reɪ.gə.ˈnɑː.mɪks/ (AmE), /reɪ.gə.ˈnɒ.mɪks/ (BrE)
the economic policies promoted by U.S. President Ronald Reagan in the 1980s, focusing on tax cuts, deregulation, and reduced government spending

Examples

Reaganomics economic policies
Many supporters of Reaganomics believe it helped stimulate economic growth in the 1980s.
Critics argue that Reaganomics increased income inequality in the United States.
The principles of Reaganomics continue to influence American economic policy debates today.
Many economists debate the long-term effects of Reaganomics on income inequality in the United States.
Supporters of Reaganomics argue that lower taxes encourage investment and economic growth.
The policies known as Reaganomics shaped American economic thinking throughout the 1980s.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Keynesianism - an economic theory advocating government intervention to manage demand
  • synonym: supply-side economics - an economic theory focusing on boosting supply through tax cuts and deregulation
