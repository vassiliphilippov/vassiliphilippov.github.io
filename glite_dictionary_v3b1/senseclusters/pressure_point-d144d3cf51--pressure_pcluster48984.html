pressure point

noun
point on body to control bleeding
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
an area on the body that is especially sensitive to pressure, where pressing can have a strong effect (such as controlling bleeding, or used in massage, acupuncture, or martial arts)

Examples

Arm pressure point
Paramedics are trained to identify each pressure point in case of emergencies.
She pressed firmly on the pressure point in his arm until the bleeding stopped.
When treating a severe cut, apply direct pressure over the pressure point to slow the bleeding.
During the exam, the doctor pressed a pressure point to check for tenderness.
Knowing where each pressure point is can help in both healing and self-defense techniques.
The therapist applied gentle pressure to a pressure point on my shoulder to relieve tension.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acupressure point - a spot on the body targeted in acupressure therapy
