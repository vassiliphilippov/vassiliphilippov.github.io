foolhardy

adjective
recklessly bold or rash
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfulˌhɑrdi / (AmE), / ˈfuːlhɑːdi / (BrE)
recklessly bold or rash; showing a disregard for danger or consequences

Examples

A foolhardy choice
Her foolhardy decision to climb without safety gear alarmed her friends.
It was foolhardy of him to swim across the river during a storm.
Only a foolhardy investor would risk all their savings on one stock.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential danger or harm
  • synonym: reckless - without thinking or caring about the consequences
