maar

noun
broad volcanic crater, often water-filled
Pronunciation: / mɑr / (AmE), / mɑː / (BrE)
a broad, shallow volcanic crater, typically with a flat floor and often filled with water, formed by an explosive eruption.

Examples

A maar crater
A maar was formed in the valley after a violent volcanic eruption thousands of years ago.
Many tourists visit the maar because of its striking blue water and unique geological history.
Scientists took water samples from the lake that filled the ancient maar.
