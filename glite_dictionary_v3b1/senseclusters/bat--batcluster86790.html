bat

verb
take a turn hitting in baseball or cricket
5.5
Pronunciation: / bæt / (AmE), / bæt / (BrE)
to hit or try to hit a ball in sports like baseball or cricket, or to strike something with or as if with a bat

Examples

To bat a ball
It's your turn to bat now, so get ready.
Martinez will bat third in today's lineup.
The team chose to bat first after winning the toss.
After warming up, the player stepped up to bat in the last inning.
He loves to bat whenever he gets the chance at the park.
She will bat first for her team in the cricket match.
He skillfully batted the ball over the fence to score a home run.
She batted at the balloon, laughing as it floated away.
The cat batted the toy mouse across the kitchen floor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: field - to play in the field (not batting) in sports like cricket or baseball
  • synonym: hit - to strike the ball in sports
  • antonym: pitch - to throw the ball to the batter in baseball
  • synonym: strike - to hit the ball with a bat or similar object
