opportune

adjective
at a suitable or advantageous time
6.8 1.13/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêp…ôrÀåtun / (AmE), / Àà…íp…ôtjuÀên / (BrE)
happening or done at a particularly suitable or advantageous time; well-timed

Examples

An opportune moment
Her suggestion came at an opportune moment and helped us avoid a major problem.
Their arrival proved opportune, as the meeting was about to begin.
We made an opportune stop at the store just before it closed for the night.

Synonyms

  • well-timed
    3.7 0.04/m
    well-timed is arranged to happen at the best possible moment for effect, while opportune means happening at a suitably advantageous time.
  • timely
    3.0 11/m
    timely describes something that happens at the right moment or before problems grow, while opportune stresses a particularly advantageous time.
  • apropos
    7.7 0.00/m
    apropos appears in formal or literary speech to mean suitably timed or relevant, while opportune is a common adjective for well-timed moments.
