orthicon

noun
obsolete television camera tube
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrθɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈɔːθɪˌkɒn / (BrE)
a type of television camera tube, now obsolete, in which electrons released from a photoemissive surface are focused onto a target, allowing the conversion of an optical image into an electronic signal

Examples

An orthicon camera tube
Engineers had to calibrate the orthicon before each live event to ensure accurate image capture.
Historians restored an old television using a rare orthicon to demonstrate 1940s technology.
The studio's original cameras were equipped with orthicon tubes, giving a distinct look to early broadcasts.
