haptoglobin

noun
plasma protein that binds free hemoglobin
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhæp.təˈɡloʊ.bɪn / (AmE), / ˌhæp.təˈɡləʊ.bɪn / (BrE)
a protein in blood plasma that binds free hemoglobin, aiding its removal from the bloodstream

Examples

Haptoglobin protein
A decrease in haptoglobin can indicate that red blood cells are being destroyed faster than normal.
Doctors measured the patient's haptoglobin levels to evaluate possible hemolysis.
Laboratory tests showed normal haptoglobin despite mild anemia symptoms.
