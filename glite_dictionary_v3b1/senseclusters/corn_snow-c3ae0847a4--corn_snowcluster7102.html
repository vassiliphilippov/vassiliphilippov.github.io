corn snow

noun
granular, refrozen snow type
9.9 7.5
Pronunciation: / kɔrn snoʊ / (AmE), / kɔːn snəʊ / (BrE)
Granular snow that forms through repeated cycles of melting during the day and refreezing at night, resulting in coarse, grainy crystals.

Examples

Patches of corn snow
After several freeze-thaw cycles, the mountain was covered in patches of corn snow.
By midday, the surface had transformed into soft corn snow under the warm sun.
Skiers often prefer corn snow in the spring because of its smooth, forgiving texture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: powder snow - fresh, loose, dry snow with a powdery texture
