ketone group

noun
chemical functional group of ketones
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːtoʊn ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈkiːtəʊn ɡruːp / (BrE)
a functional group in organic compounds, consisting of a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom and bonded to two other carbon atoms, characteristic of ketones

Examples

The ketone group structure
Spectroscopic analysis can be used to identify the ketone group within a compound.
The ketone group is a defining feature of many organic molecules used in pharmaceuticals.
When oxidized, the secondary alcohol forms a ketone group in the molecule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carbonyl group - a functional group consisting of a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom
