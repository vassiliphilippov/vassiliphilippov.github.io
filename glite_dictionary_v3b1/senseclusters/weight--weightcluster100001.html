weight

noun
importance coefficient in statistics
5.0
Pronunciation: / weɪt / (AmE), / weɪt / (BrE)
a numerical value or parameter that shows the importance or influence of something in calculations or models

Examples

Statistical weight
Assigning a higher weight to recent data can improve prediction accuracy.
Each observation in the survey was given a weight based on its relevance.
Statistical analyses often use weight to adjust for sampling biases.
During training the weight for that input feature was adjusted to reduce the model's error.
The neural network's learned weights determine how strongly each feature contributes to the final prediction.
Regularization encourages smaller weights, preventing the model from relying too heavily on any single feature.
