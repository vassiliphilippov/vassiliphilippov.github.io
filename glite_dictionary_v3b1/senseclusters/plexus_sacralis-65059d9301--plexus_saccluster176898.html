plexus sacralis

noun
nerve network in the pelvis and lower limbs
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈplek.səs ˈseɪ.krə.lɪs/ /; / /ˈplek.səs ˈsæk.rə.lɪs/ (AmE), /ˈplek.səs ˈseɪ.krə.lɪs/ /; / /ˈplek.səs ˈsæk.rə.lɪs/ (BrE)
A nerve plexus formed by the fourth and fifth lumbar nerves and the first, second, and third sacral nerves, supplying the pelvic region and lower limbs.

Examples

plexus sacralis nerves
Damage to the plexus sacralis can result in loss of sensation in the lower limbs.
Surgical procedures near the pelvis must avoid injuring the plexus sacralis to prevent complications.
The plexus sacralis is essential for motor and sensory function in the pelvic region.
