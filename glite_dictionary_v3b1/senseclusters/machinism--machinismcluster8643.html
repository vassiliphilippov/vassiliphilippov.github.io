machinism

noun
belief in machines
9.4
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈʃiː.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /mə.ˈʃiː.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the use of machines or the belief in the central importance of machines in society or industry

Examples

Modern machinism
Some critics argue that modern society is dominated by machinism.
The rise of machinism changed the way factories operated in the 20th century.
Debates about machinism often focus on its impact on employment and culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: automation - the use of machines to perform tasks without human intervention
  • antonym: humanism - a belief in the value and agency of human beings
  • synonym: mechanization - the process of introducing machines into an activity or industry
