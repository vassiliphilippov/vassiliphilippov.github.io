myocyte

noun
muscle cell
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪəʊˌsaɪt /; / ˈmaɪəʊsaɪt /; / ˈmaɪəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a muscle cell that forms muscle tissue and is responsible for contraction and movement in the body

Examples

A myocyte cell
Each myocyte contracts to help move parts of the body.
Damage to a myocyte can affect the function of the entire muscle.
Scientists study myocytes to better understand muscle diseases.
A myocyte is responsible for contracting and relaxing to help the heart pump blood.
Damage to a myocyte can affect the overall function of muscle tissue.
Researchers studied how each myocyte responded to electrical stimulation in the laboratory.
A single myocyte can contract to help regulate blood flow in the body.
Damage to heart myocytes can lead to serious cardiac conditions.
Researchers studied how each myocyte responds to different types of exercise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cardiomyocyte - a muscle cell of the heart
  • synonym: muscle cell - a cell that makes up muscle tissue
  • synonym: muscle fiber - an elongated cell that makes up muscle tissue
  • antonym: neuron - a nerve cell that transmits electrical signals
