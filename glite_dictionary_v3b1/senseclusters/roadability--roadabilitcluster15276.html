roadability

noun
suitability for vehicles
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌroʊdəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or condition of a road, path, or surface that determines whether vehicles can travel on it safely and easily

Examples

Roadability of a track
The roadability of the dirt track was poor after the storm, making travel difficult.
Engineers assessed the roadability of the new highway before opening it to traffic.
Farmers depend on the roadability of rural roads to transport their crops to market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impassability - the state of being impossible to travel through
  • synonym: passability - the state of being able to be passed or traveled through
