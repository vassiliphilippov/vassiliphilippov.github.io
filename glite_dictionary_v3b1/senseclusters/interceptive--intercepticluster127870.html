interceptive

adjective
preventive treatment
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
relating to early treatment intended to prevent the development or worsening of a disorder or condition

Examples

Interceptive treatment

Early interceptive therapy can reduce the need for more complex treatments later.
Doctors often use interceptive strategies to manage conditions before they become severe.
