rebut

verb
prove something is false; refute
5.2 5.6
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈbʌt/ /; / /riː.ˈbʌt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈbʌt/ (BrE)
to argue against or prove that a statement or accusation is false

Examples

To rebut an argument
In his article, he skillfully rebuts the popular misconceptions about the topic.
She quickly rebutted their claims during the interview.
The lawyer attempted to rebut the witness’s testimony with new evidence.
During the debate, she rebutted his claims with solid evidence and clear reasoning.
The lawyer skillfully rebutted the prosecution's accusations during the trial.
Whenever challenged, he is quick to rebut any criticism with facts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confirm - to establish the truth or correctness of something
  • synonym: contradict - to assert the opposite of a statement made by someone
  • synonym: refute - to prove a statement, theory, or person to be wrong or false
