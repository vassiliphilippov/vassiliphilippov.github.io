falcata

noun
ancient Spanish sword
9.9
Pronunciation: / fælˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / fælˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
a curved, single-edged sword used in ancient Spain, especially by Iberian warriors

Examples

Ancient falcata sword
The ancient warrior gripped his falcata tightly as he prepared for battle.
Archaeologists discovered a beautifully decorated falcata in the ruins of an Iberian settlement.
The distinctive curve of the falcata made it effective for both cutting and thrusting.
