gastrolobium

noun
Australian poisonous shrub genus
Pronunciation: / ˌɡæstrəˈloʊbiəm / (AmE), / ˌɡastrəˈləʊbɪəm / (BrE)
Any of various Australian evergreen shrubs of the genus Gastrolobium, with whorled compound leaves poisonous to livestock and showy yellow to reddish-orange flowers.

Examples

The gastrolobium shrub
Botanists documented the unusually bright flowers of the gastrolobium in Western Australia.
The farmer lost several sheep after they grazed on gastrolobium plants in the pasture.
Wildlife experts are careful to identify gastrolobium because of its toxic effects on livestock.
