double-entry bookkeeping

noun
method recording every transaction as both debit and credit
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl ˌɛntri ˈbʊkˌkipɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˌɛntri ˈbʊkkiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
A system of bookkeeping in which each financial transaction is entered twice, once as a debit in one account and once as a credit in another, ensuring that the accounts remain balanced.

Examples

Double-entry bookkeeping system
Errors are easier to spot when using double-entry bookkeeping compared to a simpler system.
Modern businesses rely on double-entry bookkeeping to ensure their financial records are accurate and balanced.
She learned the basics of double-entry bookkeeping during her first accounting class at university.

Related Terms

  • antonym: single-entry bookkeeping - a simpler system recording only one side of transactions
