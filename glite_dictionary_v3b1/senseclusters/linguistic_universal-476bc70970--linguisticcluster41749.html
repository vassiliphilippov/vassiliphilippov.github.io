linguistic universal

noun
feature or rule found in every language
Pronunciation: / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk juːˈnɪvɚsl̩ / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk juːˈnɪvəsəl / (BrE)
a grammatical rule or other linguistic feature that is found in all languages

Examples

A linguistic universal rule
Many researchers have debated whether the existence of a linguistic universal implies an innate language faculty.
Subject-verb-object word order is sometimes discussed as a possible linguistic universal.
The search for a linguistic universal has deeply influenced the field of linguistics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: universal grammar - the theory that the ability to acquire language is innate to humans and languages share a common structure
