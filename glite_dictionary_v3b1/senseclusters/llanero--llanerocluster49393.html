llanero

noun
South American cavalryman
Pronunciation: / jɑˈnɛɾoʊ / (AmE), / jəˈnɛərəʊ / (BrE)
a member of the historical cavalry from the plains of Venezuela and Colombia, known for fighting in independence-era conflicts

Examples

Llanero cavalry
The llanero played a crucial role in the fight for independence in Venezuela.
Dressed in traditional clothing, the llanero rode across the vast plains with skill and pride.
Many songs and stories celebrate the bravery of the llanero in South American history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cowboy - a person who herds cattle on horseback, especially in North America
  • synonym: gaucho - a skilled horseman from the South American pampas
