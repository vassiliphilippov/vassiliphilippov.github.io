lenticularis

noun
lens-shaped cloud
9.9
Pronunciation: /len.tɪ.kjʊ.ˈlær.ɪs/ /; / /len.tɪ.kjuː.ˈlær.ɪs/ (AmE), /len.tɪ.kjuː.ˈlær.ɪs/ /; / /len.tɪ.kjʊ.ˈlær.ɪs/ (BrE)
a lens-shaped cloud that forms at high altitudes, typically near mountains, often indicating atmospheric instability

Examples

A lenticularis cloud
A large lenticularis appeared above the ridge, resembling a flying saucer in the sky.
Photographers often seek out lenticularis clouds for their dramatic and unusual shapes.
The presence of a lenticularis can signal strong winds and turbulence for pilots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altocumulus - a type of mid-level cloud forming white or gray patches
  • antonym: cumulus - a type of puffy, white cloud with a flat base
