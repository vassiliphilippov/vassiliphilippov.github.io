overclocking

verb
to push beyond limits
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈklɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈklɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
to push a person, team, or system to operate beyond normal or sustainable limits, often causing stress or exhaustion.

Examples

Coach overclocking the team
The coach was accused of overclocking the players during the intense training camp.
If you keep overclocking your team, you might see a drop in morale and productivity.
The company suffered when management tried overclocking its staff to meet unrealistic goals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exhaust - to use up all of someone's energy or resources
  • synonym: overwork - to make someone work too hard or too much
  • antonym: underwork - to give someone too little work or effort
