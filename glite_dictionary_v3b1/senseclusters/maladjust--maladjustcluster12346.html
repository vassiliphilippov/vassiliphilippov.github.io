maladjust

verb
to fail to adapt
Pronunciation: / ˌmæl.əˈdʒʌst / (AmE), / ˌmæl.əˈdʒʌst / (BrE)
to fail to adapt properly to one's environment or circumstances

Examples

To maladjust to change
Children who move frequently may maladjust to new schools without proper support.
If employees maladjust to changes in the workplace, productivity can suffer.
Some people maladjust after major life events, leading to stress or anxiety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adapt - to adjust to new conditions or environments
  • antonym: adjust - to change or modify to fit new conditions
