pound

noun
apothecary weight, equals 12 troy ounces
Pronunciation: / paʊnd / (AmE), / paʊnd / (BrE)
a unit of weight (equal to 16 ounces or about 0.45 kilograms) or a unit of force

Examples

A pound of apples
Historical documents often reference the pound when listing ingredients for remedies.
In 18th-century pharmacies, a pound was divided into twelve ounces troy.
The recipe called for a pound of medicinal herbs, measured in the old apothecary system.
A torque of 10 foot-pounds was applied to the bolt.
For this calculation, express the answer in pounds of force rather than newtons.
The force required was measured in pounds using traditional equipment.
She managed to lose ten pounds over the summer.
The box weighs just over one pound.
The recipe calls for two pounds of flour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kilogram - the metric unit of mass equal to 1,000 grams
  • synonym: newton - the SI unit of force; equal to the force needed to accelerate one kilogram of mass by one metre per second squared
  • degree lower: ounce - a unit of weight equal to 1/16 of a pound
  • degree higher: ton - a unit of weight equal to 2,000 pounds (short ton) or 1,016 kg (long ton)
