octane

noun
a hydrocarbon used in fuel and solvents
5.7 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːk.teɪn/ (AmE), /ɒk.ˈteɪn/ (BrE)
a saturated hydrocarbon (C8H18) found in petroleum, used as a fuel and solvent, especially in reference to its role in gasoline quality

Examples

Octane rating
Chemical engineers often analyze the different octane isomers in refining processes.
Pure octane is rarely used alone as a fuel but serves as a benchmark for gasoline.
The fuel's resistance to knocking is rated by its octane number.
