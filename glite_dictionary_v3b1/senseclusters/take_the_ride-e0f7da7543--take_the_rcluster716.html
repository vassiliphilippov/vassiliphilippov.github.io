take the ride

verb
accept an uncertain experience
3.2
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə raɪd / (AmE), / teɪk ðə raɪd / (BrE)
to accept or undergo an experience, especially when the outcome is uncertain or out of one's control

Examples

To take the ride bravely
She finally chose to take the ride and see where the new job would lead her.
Sometimes life asks you to take the ride without knowing the destination.
We can't control everything—just take the ride and enjoy the journey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: go along for the ride - to be present for an experience without controlling it
