disentanglement

noun
ML: separating independent factors in representations
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ən.ˈtæŋ.gəl.mənt/ (AmE), /dɪs.ən.ˈtæŋ.gəl.mənt/ (BrE)
(in machine learning) the property or process by which a model's learned representation separates independent generative factors of variation, producing interpretable, independent features.

Examples

Disentanglement in models
Researchers explored disentanglement in latent representations to improve model interpretability and robustness.
Achieving meaningful disentanglement often requires inductive biases or carefully designed training objectives.
They measured disentanglement by assessing whether single latent dimensions corresponded to single generative factors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: entanglement - a state where learned features remain mixed together rather than separated; opposite of disentanglement
  • synonym: representation learning - learning useful feature representations of data in machine learning models
