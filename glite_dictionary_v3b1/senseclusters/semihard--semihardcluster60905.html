semihard

adjective
moderately difficult negative example (ML)
Pronunciation: / c8s5bmicch51rd / (AmE), / c8s5bmicch51d0d / (BrE)
(in machine learning) (of a negative training example) moderately difficult to distinguish from a positive example; used in training methods (e.g., semihard negative mining) to improve model discrimination.

During training, the model sampled semihard negatives to help the embedding separate similar classes.

The triplet loss benefits when each batch contains at least one semihard pair alongside easy and hard pairs.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: hard negative - a negative example that is very similar to the positive example and thus harder to distinguish
