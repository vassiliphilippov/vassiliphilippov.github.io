TDR

noun
cable fault detector
8.8
Pronunciation: /tiː diː ɑːr/ (AmE), /tiː diː ɑː/ (BrE)
an instrument or method (time-domain reflectometry) used to detect faults or breaks in electrical or telecommunications cables by sending pulses and measuring reflections

Examples

TDR test
The technician used a TDR to pinpoint the exact location of the cable fault.
Before repairing the underground line, they checked it with a TDR for any breaks.
A TDR can quickly identify issues in long stretches of fiber-optic cable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cable tester - a device used to test the integrity of cables
