pressure point

noun
place where problems may arise
3.9 4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a place, situation, or aspect that is likely to cause problems or difficulties, especially in a process or system

Examples

The introduction of new technology became a major pressure point for the management team.
