eme

noun
linguistic unit
Pronunciation: / ˈiːm /; / iːm / (AmE), / ˈiːm /; / iːm / (BrE)
a minimal unit or element in the structure of a language, such as in 'phoneme' or 'morpheme'; also used as a suffix in technical linguistic terms

Examples

A eme in linguistics
In linguistics, a eme refers to a basic unit like a phoneme or morpheme.

The suffix eme is found in many technical linguistic terms.
In the word 'phoneme', eme indicates a basic sound unit.
