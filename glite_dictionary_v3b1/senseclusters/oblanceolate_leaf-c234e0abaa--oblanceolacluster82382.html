oblanceolate leaf

noun
leaf broader at tip, tapers to base
Pronunciation: / oʊˈblænsiəˌleɪt liːf / (AmE), / əʊˈblænsɪəˌleɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf that is broader at the tip and tapers toward the base, with a rounded apex and a narrowing base.

Examples

An oblanceolate leaf
Botanists categorized the specimen by its distinctive oblanceolate leaf, broader near the tip than at the base.
Some wildflowers have an oblanceolate leaf that makes them easy to identify in the field.
The desert plant is notable for its oblanceolate leaf structure, which helps reduce water loss.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lanceolate leaf - leaf shape that tapers to a point at the apex and is broader at the base
