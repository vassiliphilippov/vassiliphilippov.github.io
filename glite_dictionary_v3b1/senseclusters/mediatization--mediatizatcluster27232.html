mediatization

noun
loss of sovereignty
Pronunciation: / ˌmiːdiətəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmiːdiətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the historical process by which smaller sovereign territories are absorbed into larger states and lose their independence

Examples

Mediatization of small states
The mediatization of small German states changed the political landscape of Europe in the nineteenth century.
After mediatization, many former rulers lost their titles and privileges.
Historians study the effects of mediatization on regional identities and governance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annexation - the act of adding a territory to a larger state
  • antonym: sovereignty - the authority of a state to govern itself
