Apgar score

noun
test assessing newborn's physical condition
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæpɡɑr ˌskɔr / (AmE), / ˈapɡɑː ˌskɔː / (BrE)
a quick test performed on a newborn baby to assess its physical condition, based on heart rate, muscle tone, breathing effort, skin color, and reflexes

Examples

The Apgar score test
A low Apgar score may indicate a baby needs extra help breathing or stimulation.
Doctors carefully evaluate the Apgar score to decide if immediate medical attention is needed.
The nurse recorded an Apgar score of 8 for the newborn shortly after delivery.
