lithotripsy

noun
stone-breaking procedure
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪ.θə.trɪp.siː/ /; / /ˈlɪ.θə.trɪp.sɪ/ (AmE), /ˈlɪ.θə.trɪp.siː/ /; / /ˈlɪ.θə.trɪp.sɪ/ (BrE)
a medical procedure that uses sound waves or other methods to break up stones in the kidney, bladder, or ureter

Examples

Lithotripsy treatment
The doctor recommended lithotripsy to treat the patient's kidney stones without surgery.
After undergoing lithotripsy, she was able to pass the broken stones naturally.
Modern lithotripsy techniques often use ultrasound to target and shatter the stones.
