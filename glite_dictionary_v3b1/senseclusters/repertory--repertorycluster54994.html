repertory

noun
collection of works performed by an artist or group
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛpərˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈrɛpətəri / (BrE)
a collection of plays, dances, or musical works that a performer or group can perform and regularly presents as part of their schedule

Examples

Repertory of plays
Her repertory includes classical piano pieces and contemporary compositions.
The ballet troupe performs different works from their repertory each week.
The theater company added a new play to its repertory this season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repertoire - all the works a performer is prepared to present
