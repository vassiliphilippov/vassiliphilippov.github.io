refraction

noun
bending of a wave as it passes through a medium
2.5 5.4
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈfræk.ʃən/ /; / /rə.ˈfræk.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈfræk.ʃən/ /; / /rə.ˈfræk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the change in direction of a wave, such as light or sound, as it passes from one medium to another

Examples

Light refraction
Eyeglasses work by correcting the refraction of light as it enters your eyes.
Scientists measured the refraction of the sound wave as it traveled from air into water.
The angle of refraction depends on the properties of the materials the light passes through.
Glasses correct vision by taking advantage of the refraction of light through lenses.
Scientists study refraction to better understand how sound travels through air and water.
The refraction of light in water causes objects to appear bent or distorted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diffraction - the bending of waves around obstacles or through openings
  • antonym: reflection - bouncing back of a wave from a surface
