wild service tree

noun
European tree with edible brownish fruit
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈsɜːrvɪs triː / (AmE), / waɪld ˈsɜːvɪs triː / (BrE)
A European tree (Sorbus torminalis) with lobed leaves and small, speckled brown edible fruits.

Examples

A wild service tree fruit
A wild service tree can be identified by its distinctively lobed leaves and brown, speckled fruits.
Conservationists are working to protect ancient wild service trees in European woodlands.
The wild service tree produces fruit that was once popular in traditional English country diets.
