catchy hook

noun
attention-grabbing opening in writing/media
3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkætʃ.iː hʊk/ /; / /ˈke.tʃiː hʊk/ (AmE), /ˈkætʃ.iː hʊk/ /; / /ˈkætʃ.ɪ hʊk/ (BrE)
an attention-grabbing opening line or element in writing, advertising, or media intended to quickly interest the audience.

Examples

A catchy hook in writing
The article opens with a catchy hook that made me read the whole piece.
Her marketing email used a catchy hook to increase clicks and engagement.
Speakers often start with a catchy hook to win the audience's attention quickly.
