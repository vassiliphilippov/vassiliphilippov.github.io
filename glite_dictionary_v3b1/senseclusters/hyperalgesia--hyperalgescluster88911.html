hyperalgesia

noun
increased pain sensitivity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərælˈdʒiːziə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərælˈdʒiːziə / (BrE)
an increased sensitivity to pain, causing a stronger than normal response to painful stimuli

Examples

Severe hyperalgesia pain
After the surgery, the patient developed hyperalgesia and found even light touches painful.
Doctors monitor for hyperalgesia in patients taking certain pain medications long-term.
Nerve injuries can sometimes lead to hyperalgesia, making recovery more difficult.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analgesia - absence of pain in response to stimulation
