thrombolytic therapy

noun
treatment dissolving blood clots with drugs
7.7 4.3
Pronunciation: /θrɑːm.bə.ˈlɪt.ɪk ˈθer.ə.piː/ (AmE), /θrɒm.bə.ˈlɪt.ɪk ˈθe.rə.piː/ (BrE)
the use of drugs to dissolve abnormal blood clots in the body, especially to treat conditions like heart attack or stroke

Examples

Thrombolytic therapy treatment
After reviewing his medical history, the team decided that thrombolytic therapy was the most appropriate treatment.
Early thrombolytic therapy can significantly improve outcomes for stroke patients.
The doctors administered thrombolytic therapy to quickly dissolve the clot causing the heart attack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticoagulant - a drug that prevents blood clotting
  • degree lower: thrombosis - formation of a blood clot inside a blood vessel
