Nicholas II

proper noun
last czar of Russia (1868–1918)
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪkələs ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈnɪkələs ði ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
the last Emperor of Russia (reigned 1894–1917), who was forced to abdicate during the Russian Revolution and was executed with his family by the Bolsheviks.

Examples

Nicholas II Emperor of Russia
Nicholas II was forced to abdicate the throne at the height of the Russian Revolution.
Historians continue to debate the legacy of Nicholas II in Russian history.
The tragic fate of Nicholas II and his family shocked the world in 1918.
