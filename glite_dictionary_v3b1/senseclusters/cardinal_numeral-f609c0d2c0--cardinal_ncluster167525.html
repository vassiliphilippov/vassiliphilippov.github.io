cardinal numeral

noun
numeral expressing a quantity
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdn̩l ˈnuːmərəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnəl ˈnjuːmərəl / (BrE)
A numeral that expresses a countable quantity, such as one, two, three, etc.

Examples

A cardinal numeral one
A cardinal numeral tells us how many objects are in a group.
Teachers often ask students to write cardinal numerals in both digits and words.
The numbers 'three' and 'seven' are both cardinal numerals in English.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ordinal numeral - a numeral expressing position or order (first, second, etc.)
