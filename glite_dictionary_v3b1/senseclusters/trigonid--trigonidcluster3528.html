trigonid

noun
front molar cusp group
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪɡənɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡənɪd / (BrE)
the group of three main cusps at the front inner part of a lower molar tooth in mammals

Examples

The trigonid cusps
The paleontologist identified the trigonid on the fossilized molar.
In mammals, the trigonid is important for slicing food during chewing.
Damage to the trigonid can affect how efficiently an animal processes its food.
