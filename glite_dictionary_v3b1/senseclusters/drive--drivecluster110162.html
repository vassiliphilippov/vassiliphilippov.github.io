drive

verb
operate or control a vehicle
Pronunciation: / draɪv / (AmE), / draɪv / (BrE)
to operate or travel in a vehicle, or to give someone a ride in a vehicle

Examples

To drive a car
Have you ever driven a manual transmission car before?
On weekends, I drive my kids to soccer practice across town.
She learned to drive when she was 18 and passed her test on the first try.
It was relaxing to drive along the coast with the windows down.
They decided to drive through the night to reach their destination.
We drive down this highway every morning to get to school.
After losing his office job, he started to drive a taxi to pay the bills.
Many people drive buses or trucks as their main occupation.
She used to drive a delivery van for a local bakery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ride - to be carried or transported by a vehicle, usually as a passenger
  • synonym: steer - to control the direction of a vehicle
