skiddoo

interjection
telling someone to leave
9.9
Pronunciation: / skɪdˈduː / (AmE), / skɪdˈduː / (BrE)
an informal exclamation telling someone to leave or go away immediately

Examples

Skiddoo now
When the police arrived, someone yelled skiddoo and everyone scattered.
If you don't want trouble, you better skiddoo right now.
Hearing the angry dog bark, the kids decided to skiddoo from the yard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scram - an informal command to leave quickly
  • antonym: stay - to remain in a place
