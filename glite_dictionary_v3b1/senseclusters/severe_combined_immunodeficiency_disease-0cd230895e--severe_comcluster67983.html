severe combined immunodeficiency disease

noun
rare genetic immune deficiency disorder
8.0
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈvɪr kəm.ˈbaɪnd ɪm.juː.noʊ.dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /sɪ.ˈvɪr kəm.ˈbaɪnd ɪm.juː.noʊ.dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː dɪ.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈvɪə kəm.ˈbaɪnd ɪm.juː.nəʊ.dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /sə.ˈvɪə kəm.ˈbaɪnd ɪm.juː.nəʊ.dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː dɪ.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
a rare, inherited disorder of the immune system in which affected individuals lack normal T cells and are highly susceptible to infections, typically fatal unless treated early

Examples

Severe combined immunodeficiency disease
Bone marrow transplantation can offer a cure for children diagnosed with severe combined immunodeficiency disease.
If left untreated, severe combined immunodeficiency disease is usually fatal within the first two years of life.
Infants with severe combined immunodeficiency disease require protective isolation to avoid life-threatening infections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autoimmune disease - a disease in which the body's immune system attacks healthy cells
  • synonym: immunodeficiency - a state in which the immune system's ability to fight infectious disease is compromised
