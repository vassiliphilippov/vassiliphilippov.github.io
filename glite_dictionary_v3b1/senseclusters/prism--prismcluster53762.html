prism

noun
optical device for deviating light
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
an optical device with a triangular shape, made of glass or quartz, that is used to deviate a beam of light or invert an image

Examples

Glass prism splits light
A prism can invert an image as well as bend light.
Holding the prism up to the sunlight created a beautiful rainbow on the wall.
The scientist used a prism to split the beam of white light into its component colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lens - piece of transparent material used to focus or disperse light
  • antonym: mirror - device that reflects light to form an image
