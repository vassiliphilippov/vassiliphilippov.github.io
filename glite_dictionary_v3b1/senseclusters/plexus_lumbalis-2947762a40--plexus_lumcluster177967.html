plexus lumbalis

noun
nerve network in the lower back
Pronunciation: / ˌplɛksəs lʌmˈbeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌplɛksəs lʌmˈbɑːlɪs / (BrE)
a network of nerves formed by the ventral branches of the first four lumbar spinal nerves

Examples

Plexus lumbalis nerves
Damage to the plexus lumbalis can result in weakness or loss of sensation in the legs.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully avoided the plexus lumbalis to prevent nerve injury.
The plexus lumbalis plays a crucial role in innervating the lower limbs.
