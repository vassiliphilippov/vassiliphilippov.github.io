conscience

noun
inner sense of right and wrong
1.4 4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.ʃəns /; / ˈkɑːnʃəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ʃəns /; / ˈkɒnʃəns / (BrE)
a person's inner sense or guide that helps them know what is right or wrong and feel guilty if they do something bad

Examples

A guilty conscience
A guilty conscience often keeps people awake at night.
He couldn't escape his conscience after lying to his best friend.
Her strong conscience prevented her from cheating on the exam.
A clear conscience is often considered more valuable than gold.
He could not ignore his conscience and decided to tell the truth.
Her conscience bothered her after she lied to her best friend.
He struggled with his conscience after making a difficult decision at work.
Her conscience told her it was wrong to lie to her friend.
I couldn't ignore my conscience any longer and confessed the truth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guilt - a feeling of responsibility or remorse for a real or imagined offense
  • synonym: morality - principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong
  • synonym: scruple - a feeling of doubt or hesitation about the morality of an action
  • antonym: shamelessness - lack of shame or moral restraint
