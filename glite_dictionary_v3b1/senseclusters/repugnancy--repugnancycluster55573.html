repugnancy

noun
strong disgust
9.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpʌɡnənsi / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌɡnənsi / (BrE)
a strong feeling of disgust or intense incompatibility with something

Examples

His repugnancy at cheating
He expressed his repugnancy at the idea of cheating to win the competition.
There was a clear repugnancy between the two statements made by the witness.
Her repugnancy toward violence was evident in her reaction to the news.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attraction - a feeling of being drawn to something
  • synonym: disgust - a strong sense of dislike or distaste
  • synonym: revulsion - a strong feeling of dislike or disgust
