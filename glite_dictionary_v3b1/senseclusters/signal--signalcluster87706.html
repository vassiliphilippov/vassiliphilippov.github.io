signal

verb
to indicate or be a sign of
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
to be a sign that something exists or is likely to happen; to indicate or suggest something

Examples

To signal a change
His sudden silence signaled his discomfort with the topic.
Rising sea levels signal the urgent need for action on climate change.
The slowdown in hiring signals a possible recession in the economy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imply - to suggest that something is true without saying so directly
  • synonym: indicate - to show that something exists or is true
