pressure unit

noun
unit for measuring pressure
4.9 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpreʃ.ər ˈjuː.nɪt/ /; / /ˈpreʃ.ər ˈjuː.nət/ (AmE), /ˈpreʃ.ə ˈjuː.nɪt/ /; / /ˈpreʃ.ə ˈjuː.nət/ (BrE)
a standard unit used to measure force applied per unit area

Examples

A pressure unit measure
Different fields may use different pressure units, such as psi in mechanics and millimeters of mercury in medicine.
In meteorology, the millibar is a traditional pressure unit for expressing atmospheric pressure.
The SI pressure unit is the pascal, commonly used in science and engineering.
