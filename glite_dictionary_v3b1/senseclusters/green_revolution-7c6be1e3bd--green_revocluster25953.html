green revolution

noun
major agricultural innovation period (1960s–1970s)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡrin ˌrɛvəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a period during the 1960s and 1970s marked by the introduction of new agricultural technologies, including high-yield crop varieties, chemical fertilizers, and pesticides, which greatly increased food production worldwide

Examples

Green revolution in farming
Some critics argue that the green revolution led to environmental and social challenges in rural communities.
Thanks to the green revolution, crop yields increased dramatically in many developing countries.
The green revolution transformed agriculture in India, helping avert widespread famine in the 20th century.
