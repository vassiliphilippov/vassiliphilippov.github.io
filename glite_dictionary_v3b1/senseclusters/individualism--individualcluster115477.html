individualism

noun
belief in minimal government interference in commerce
1.5 4.3
Pronunciation: /ɪn.dɪ.vɪd.ˈuː.ə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ɪn.dɪ.vɪd.ˈjuː.ə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /ɪn.dɪ.vɪd.ˈuː.ə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The belief or doctrine that government should not interfere in commercial or economic affairs, emphasizing personal and economic freedom.

Examples

Economic individualism
Critics claim that unchecked individualism can increase social inequality.
Many economists argue that individualism leads to greater innovation and prosperity in the marketplace.
The political party's platform was strongly rooted in individualism and free enterprise.
