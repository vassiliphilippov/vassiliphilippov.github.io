SVC

noun
grammar sentence pattern
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs vi ˈsi / (AmE), / ˌɛs viː ˈsiː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'subject–verb–complement', a sentence structure in which the verb links the subject to a complement

Examples

SVC sentence structure
In English grammar, the SVC structure is common in sentences like 'She is happy.'
Teachers often ask students to identify the SVC pattern in simple sentences.
Understanding the SVC structure helps learners build correct sentences in English.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: SV - a sentence structure with subject and verb only
  • synonym: SVO - a sentence structure with subject, verb, and object
