distinct

adjective
clearly different; separate
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɪŋkt / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋkt / (BrE)
clearly different or separate from others; not the same as something else

Examples

Distinct from others
Her accent is distinct from any I have heard before.
The twins have very distinct personalities and interests.
There is a distinct difference between these two proposals.
Each region has its own distinct dialect.
Her voice had a distinct tone that set her apart from the others.
There was a distinct smell of smoke in the kitchen.
As the fog lifted, the outline of the mountain became distinct.
His voice was distinct in the silence, clear and commanding everyone's attention.
There is a distinct smell of smoke in the air, which suggests a fire nearby.
