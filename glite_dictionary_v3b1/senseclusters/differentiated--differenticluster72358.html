differentiated

adjective
biologically specialized; adapted for a function
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or tissue) having become specialized for a particular function during development.

Examples

Differentiated cells
Cancer can develop when formerly differentiated cells lose their specialized functions.
Highly differentiated cells perform specific roles within an organ system.
The tissue sample contained both differentiated nerve cells and undifferentiated stem cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: undifferentiated - (of a cell or tissue) not specialized; retaining the ability to develop into various cell types
