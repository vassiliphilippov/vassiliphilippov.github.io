paint (oneself) into a corner

verb
put in a difficult situation with no escape
4.6
Pronunciation: / peɪnt ɪntu ə ˈkɔrnɚ / (AmE), / peɪnt ɪntu ə ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
to put oneself in a difficult situation from which there is no easy or good way out

Examples

To paint oneself into a corner
After refusing to compromise, the company painted itself into a corner during negotiations.
If you keep lying, you'll soon paint yourself into a corner with no good options left.
She realized that she had painted herself into a corner by taking on so many commitments.
