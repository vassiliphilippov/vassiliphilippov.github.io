pneumocystis carinii pneumonia

noun
fungal pneumonia in immunocompromised patients
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːmoʊˈsɪstɪs kəˈraɪni pnuˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməʊˈsɪstɪs kəˈraɪniː njʊˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A form of pneumonia caused by the fungus Pneumocystis carinii, mainly affecting infants and people with weakened immune systems, such as those with AIDS.

Examples

pneumocystis carinii pneumonia infection
Early detection of pneumocystis carinii pneumonia can significantly improve the prognosis for immunocompromised patients.
Individuals with advanced HIV infection are at high risk for developing pneumocystis carinii pneumonia.
The patient was diagnosed with pneumocystis carinii pneumonia after presenting with severe respiratory distress.
