oboe

noun
a double-reed woodwind instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊboʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊbəʊ / (BrE)
a woodwind musical instrument with a slender conical bore and a double-reed mouthpiece, known for its clear, penetrating tone

Examples

The oboe instrument
An oboe is often featured in classical symphonies and chamber music.
She played the oboe in the school orchestra for several years.
The solo oboe introduced the melody with a sweet, haunting sound.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bassoon - a large double-reed woodwind instrument with a lower pitch than the oboe
  • synonym: clarinet - a single-reed woodwind instrument with a cylindrical bore
