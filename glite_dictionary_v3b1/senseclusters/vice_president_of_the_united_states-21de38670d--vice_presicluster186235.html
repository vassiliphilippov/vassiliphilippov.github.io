Vice President of the United States

noun
US government official; second in command to the President
Pronunciation: / ˌvaɪs ˌprɛzəˈdɛnt əv ðə ˌjuːˈnaɪtɪd ˈsteɪts / (AmE), / ˌvaɪs ˌprɛzɪˈdɛnt əv ðə ˌjuːˈnaɪtɪd ˈsteɪts / (BrE)
the second-highest executive officer of the United States government, who presides over the Senate and succeeds the President if necessary

Examples

The Vice President of the United States
In the event of a presidential vacancy, the Vice President of the United States assumes office.
Kamala Harris currently serves as the Vice President of the United States.
The Vice President of the United States presides over Senate sessions when present.
