chopine

noun
historical women's shoe with high thick sole
Pronunciation: / tʃəˈpiːn / (AmE), / tʃəˈpiːn / (BrE)
a woman's shoe with a very high, thick sole, popular in 16th and 17th century Europe, especially in Venice

Examples

A chopine shoe
Because chopine soles were so high, walking in them required assistance.
The museum displayed a pair of ornate chopines from Renaissance Venice.
We learned that noblewomen wore chopines to appear taller and more distinguished.
