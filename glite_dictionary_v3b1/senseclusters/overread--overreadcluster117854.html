overread

verb
to read past limit
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈriːd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈriːd / (BrE)
to read more data or bytes than are available or allowed, often causing errors in computing

Examples

To overread data
If you overread the buffer, the program may crash or behave unpredictably.
The developer fixed a bug where the function would overread the end of the data array.
Careful programming is needed to avoid overreading files in low-level code.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underread - to read less data than is available or required
