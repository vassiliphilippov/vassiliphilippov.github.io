babble

verb
talk foolishly or incoherently
Pronunciation: / ˈbæbəl /; / ˈbæb.əl / (AmE), / ˈbabəl /; / ˈbæb.əl / (BrE)
to talk quickly and in a way that is silly, foolish, or hard to understand; to make meaningless or incoherent sounds

Examples

To babble nonsense
He quietly babbled nonsense to himself as he worked.
The child began to babble about her dreams during breakfast.
Tourists often babble excitedly when they see famous landmarks for the first time.
She couldn't hear what he was saying because he started to babble after the accident.
The baby began to babble excitedly as he played with his toys.
The brook seemed to babble along the rocks, making a pleasant sound.

Related Terms

  • antonym: articulate - to express an idea or feeling fluently and coherently
  • synonym: mumble - to speak quietly and not clearly enough so that the words are difficult to understand
  • synonym: mutter - to speak in a low, indistinct voice, often in a complaining or discontented manner
  • synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • antonym: speak clearly - to talk in a way that is easy to understand
