bimetallistic

adjective
pertaining to bimetallic monetary systems
9.7 8.5
Pronunciation: /baɪ.mə.ˈtæl.ɪ.stɪk/ /; / /baɪ.mɪ.ˈtæl.ɪ.stɪk/ (AmE), /baɪ.mə.ˈtæl.ɪ.stɪk/ /; / /baɪ.mɪ.ˈtæl.ɪ.stɪk/ (BrE)
relating to a monetary system in which the standard economic unit of account is based on the value of two different metals, typically gold and silver.

Examples

Bimetallistic currency system
Economists studied the effects of bimetallistic currency systems on price stability and international trade.
France operated under a bimetallistic system, using both silver and gold coins as legal tender.
The late 19th-century debate over adopting a bimetallistic standard shaped U.S. monetary policy for decades.
