monocratic

adjective
ruled by one person
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnəˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of rule by a single person with absolute power

Examples

Monocratic government
The new leader established a monocratic government, concentrating all authority in his own hands.
Critics argued that the reforms would lead to a more monocratic style of leadership.
After years of collective rule, the country became increasingly monocratic under the president.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autocratic - relating to a ruler with absolute power
  • antonym: democratic - relating to rule by the people or elected representatives
