toggle

noun
two-position electrical switch
9.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑː.gəl/ (AmE), /ˈtɒ.gəl/ (BrE)
a switch, command, or feature that alternates between two states, such as on and off (can be physical or in software)

Examples

Toggle switch
Each machine has a safety toggle to quickly cut the power.
She installed a new toggle in the living room to control the ceiling fan.
The lamp is turned on by flipping the toggle up.
The mute toggle works by pressing the same button to mute and unmute the audio.
This toggle lets you switch the subtitles on and off during playback.
You can enable dark mode with the toggle in the settings menu.
The team added a toggle to enable the experimental UI without redeploying.
During rollout, engineers used a toggle to enable the feature for five percent of users.
If a bug appears, the operations team can switch off the new feature by turning the toggle in staging.
