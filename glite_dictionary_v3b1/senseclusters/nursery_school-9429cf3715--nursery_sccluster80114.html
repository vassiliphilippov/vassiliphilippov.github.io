nursery school

noun
school for young children before primary school
1.6 1.12/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…ùÀês…ôri skuÀêl / (AmE), / Ààn…úÀês…ôri skuÀêl / (BrE)
A school or institution for very young children, typically between the ages of three and five, that prepares them for primary education.

Examples

A nursery school
Many parents send their children to a nursery school before they start primary education.
She works as a teacher at the local nursery school.
The nursery school held a spring concert to showcase the children's progress.

Synonyms

  • preschool
    1.3 12/m
    preschool offers group learning for three- to five-year-olds in play-based classes, while nursery school focuses on preparing children for primary school.
  • preprimary
    3.9 0.01/m
    preprimary is a formal program for children just before primary school, while nursery school is a local early school preparing them.
  • nursery
    1.0 2.96/m
    nursery is a childcare setting that often emphasizes care and day routines, while nursery school focuses on formal preschool education.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kindergarten - a class or school for children usually five years old, preparing for primary education
  • synonym: preschool - a school for children too young for kindergarten
