black fly

noun
small bloodsucking fly from the Simuliidae family
5.0
Pronunciation: / blæk flaɪ / (AmE), / blæk flaɪ / (BrE)
A small, dark-colored biting fly of the family Simuliidae, known for its stout body and bloodsucking habits, often affecting birds, humans, and other mammals.

Examples

A black fly bite
In the northern forests, hikers often have to protect themselves from black flies during the summer months.
Scientists studied the population of black flies to track the spread of certain diseases.
The bite of a black fly can cause itchy swelling and irritation.
