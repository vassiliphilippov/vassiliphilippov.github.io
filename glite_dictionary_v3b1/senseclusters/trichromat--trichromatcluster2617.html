trichromat

noun
normal color vision
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪkroʊˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkrəʊˌmat / (BrE)
a person or animal with normal color vision, having three types of color receptors in the eyes

Examples

A trichromat person
Most humans are trichromats and can distinguish a wide range of colors.
Scientists study how trichromat vision differs from other types of color perception in animals.
Being a trichromat allows people to see subtle differences between shades of green and red.

Related Terms

  • synonym: color vision - the ability to distinguish different colors
  • antonym: dichromat - a person or animal with only two types of color receptors
