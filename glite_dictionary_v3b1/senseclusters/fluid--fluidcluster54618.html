fluid

adjective
likely to change; not stable
5.1 3.56/m

likely to change, not fixed or stable; easily changed or adaptable (used for situations, systems, or assets)

Examples

Fluid situation
After the merger, roles within the organization became increasingly fluid.
Negotiations are still at a fluid stage, with no final agreement in sight.
The company's strategy remains fluid as the market conditions shift rapidly.
A fluid society makes it possible for people to improve their social standing.
Her career path was very fluid, moving between industries and roles.

Banks must ensure they maintain enough fluid assets to meet withdrawal demands.

The company's fluid assets allowed it to handle sudden expenses with ease.

Synonyms

  • adaptable
    3.4 2.40/m
    adaptable is able to change to suit different conditions or uses, while fluid describes situations or assets that change easily.
  • changeable
    3.5 0.90/m
    changeable is likely to alter or not stay the same, while fluid describes systems or situations that shift easily.
  • mutable
    7.7 0.51/m
    mutable is a formal word for things that can be changed easily, while fluid feels more everyday and flexible for situations.
  • versatile
    6.5 0.04/m
    versatile is often used for people or tools with many different uses, while fluid focuses on change and instability in situations.
