rhaphe

noun
anatomical seam or ridge
Pronunciation: / ˈræfi / (AmE), / ˈreɪfi / (BrE)
A ridge or seam formed by the joining of two parts, especially along the midline of an organ or structure in anatomy.

Examples

The rhaphe line
A malformed rhaphe can lead to congenital abnormalities.
The structure is divided by a visible rhaphe running its entire length.
The surgeon noted a clear rhaphe along the midline of the tongue.
