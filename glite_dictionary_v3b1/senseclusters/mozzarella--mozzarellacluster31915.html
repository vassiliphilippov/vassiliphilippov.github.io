mozzarella

noun
soft white Italian cheese
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːtsəˈrɛlə / (AmE), / ˌmɒtsəˈrɛlə / (BrE)
a mild, soft white Italian cheese, traditionally made from buffalo or cow's milk, often used in cooking, especially on pizzas and in salads

Examples

Fresh mozzarella cheese
He prefers mozzarella cheese because of its mild flavor and soft texture.
She sprinkled fresh mozzarella over the homemade pizza before baking it.
The caprese salad featured slices of ripe tomato, basil, and creamy mozzarella.
