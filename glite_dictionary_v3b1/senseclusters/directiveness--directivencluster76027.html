directiveness

noun
the quality of giving guidance or directions
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛktɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of tending to give instructions or guidance; inclination to direct others

Examples

Her directiveness as manager
Her directiveness as a manager ensured that every team member knew exactly what to do.
Some teachers use a high level of directiveness to keep students on task.
The therapist's directiveness was balanced with empathy to support the client's autonomy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: directive - giving instructions or guidance
  • antonym: permissiveness - the quality of allowing freedom or leniency
