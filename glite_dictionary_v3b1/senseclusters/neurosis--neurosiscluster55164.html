neurosis

noun
mild mental disorder without organic cause
6.1 6.6
Pronunciation: /nʊ.ˈroʊ.sɪs/ /; / /njuː.ˈroʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /njʊə.ˈrəʊ.sɪs/ /; / /njuː.ˈrəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A mild mental disorder involving distress but not loss of touch with reality, without a known neurological or organic cause.

Examples

A neurosis disorder
His neurosis manifested in obsessive behaviors and chronic indecision.
Modern therapy can help people manage their neurosis and improve their daily functioning.
She was diagnosed with neurosis after experiencing intense anxiety and worry for several months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: psychosis - a severe mental disorder with loss of contact with reality
