lensing

noun
altering light paths
7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈlen.sɪŋ/ /; / /ˈlen.zɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈlen.sɪŋ/ /; / /ˈlen.zɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process by which a lens or gravitational field bends or alters the appearance of light from objects

Examples

Gravitational lensing effect
Gravitational lensing allows astronomers to observe galaxies that would otherwise be too faint to see.
The lensing effect can create multiple images of the same distant star.
Scientists study lensing to better understand the distribution of dark matter in the universe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magnification - the process of making something appear larger
