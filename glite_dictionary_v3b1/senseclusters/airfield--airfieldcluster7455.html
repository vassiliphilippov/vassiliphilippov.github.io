airfield

noun
area for aircraft takeoff and landing
3.8 3.16/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…õrÀåfild / (AmE), / Ààe…ôÀåfiÀêld / (BrE)
an area of land designed for the takeoff and landing of aircraft, often without full airport facilities

Examples

A small airfield
After a long flight, the pilot finally landed at the remote airfield.
Several planes were parked along the edge of the airfield.
The small military airfield was hidden among the hills.

Synonyms

  • airstrip
    3.5 1.69/m
    an airstrip is a short runway used without full airport services, while an airfield can be larger and sometimes has more facilities.
  • landing strip
    3.5 0.34/m
    a landing strip is a runway where planes land and take off with few facilities, while an airfield may be larger or offer some services.
  • strip
    7.4 3.11/m
    a strip is a simple airfield often used for temporary or emergency landings, while an airfield is a general site for aircraft operations.
  • flight strip
    7.2 0.00/m
    a flight strip is a basic field often used for military or emergency landings, while an airfield is a broader place for aircraft.
  • flying field
    3.7 0.00/m
    a flying field is an area prepared for takeoff and landing, usually with few facilities, while an airfield covers similar ground.
  • landing field
    3.7 0.14/m
    a landing field is a smaller, less developed place for planes to land, while an airfield is any area designed for aircraft takeoff and landing.
  • field
    3.6 4.78/m
    a field is flat open land used for takeoffs or landings in general, while an airfield is a specific area made for aircraft.
  • aerodrome
    8.4 0.15/m
    an aerodrome is an airfield with control towers and hangars, while an airfield often lacks those full airport facilities.
