swear

verb
make a solemn promise or oath
1.0 3.8
Pronunciation: /sweər/ /; / /swer/ (AmE), /sweə/ (BrE)
to make a solemn or formal promise or statement, especially by taking an oath or declaring something is true (often in court or religious settings)

Examples

To swear an oath
After hearing the evidence, the witness swore an oath in court.
I swear to tell the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth.
She swore never to reveal the secret to anyone.
He placed his hand on the Bible and swore to tell the truth.
I swear I had nothing to do with the accident.
They swore that they would keep the secret forever.
All witnesses are required to swear before giving testimony in court.
He had to swear that his statement was true under penalty of perjury.
She swore in front of the judge that the documents were authentic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affirm - to state something firmly and publicly
  • antonym: deny - to declare that something is not true
  • antonym: perjure - to knowingly lie after swearing an oath
  • synonym: vow - to promise solemnly
