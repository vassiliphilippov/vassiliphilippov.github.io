Robinson Crusoe

proper noun
hero of Defoe's novel; castaway on a deserted island
Pronunciation: / ˌrɑːbɪnsən kruˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌrɒbɪnsən kruˈsəʊ / (BrE)
the main character in Daniel Defoe's novel, an English sailor shipwrecked on a deserted island, noted for his resourcefulness and survival skills

Examples

Robinson Crusoe character
He felt like Robinson Crusoe during his remote camping trip.
Like Robinson Crusoe, she had to rely on her ingenuity to survive in isolation.
The story of Robinson Crusoe has inspired countless adventure tales and films.

Related Terms

  • synonym: castaway - a person who has been shipwrecked and stranded on an island or coast
