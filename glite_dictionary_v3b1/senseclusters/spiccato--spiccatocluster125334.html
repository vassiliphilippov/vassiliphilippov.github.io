spiccato

noun
bouncing bow technique on strings
Pronunciation: / spɪˈkɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / spɪˈkɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a style of playing stringed instruments in which the bow bounces lightly off the strings, producing a short, detached sound

Examples

Spiccato bowing style
Learning to master spiccato can greatly enhance a string player's technique.
The conductor requested a light spiccato from the cellos in the second movement.
The violinist played with a crisp spiccato during the lively passage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legato - smooth and connected musical style
