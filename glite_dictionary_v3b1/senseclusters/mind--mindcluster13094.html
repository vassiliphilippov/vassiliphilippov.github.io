mind

noun
intellect; mental ability
Pronunciation: / maɪnd / (AmE), / maɪnd / (BrE)
the part of a person that thinks, feels, reasons, and remembers; a person's intellect or consciousness

Examples

A sharp mind
Reading challenging books stimulates the mind.
She has a brilliant mind and solves problems quickly.
The university attracts some of the brightest minds in the world.
He has a sharp mind and learns new things quickly.
Her mind was full of questions after the meeting.
When she meditates, she tries to clear her mind of all worries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: body - the physical structure of a person or animal, as opposed to the mind
  • synonym: brain - the organ inside the head that controls thought, memory, and feeling
  • synonym: intellect - the faculty of reasoning and understanding objectively
