saccharin

noun
artificial calorie-free sweetener
Pronunciation: / ˈsækərɪn / (AmE), / ˈsakərɪn / (BrE)
a white, crystalline, artificial sweetener that is several hundred times sweeter than sugar and contains no calories

Examples

Artificial saccharin sweetener
Many diet sodas contain saccharin instead of sugar to reduce calories.
Researchers tested the sweetness of saccharin compared to regular table sugar.
Some people are sensitive to the taste of saccharin in their coffee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aspartame - an artificial, low-calorie sweetener
  • antonym: sugar - a sweet crystalline substance used as food
