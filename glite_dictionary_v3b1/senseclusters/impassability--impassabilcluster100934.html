impassability

noun
state of being blocked
8.3
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpæsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpæsəˈbɪlɪti /; / ɪmˌpɑːsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to pass through, over, or overcome

Examples

The impassability of roads
The impassability of the flooded road forced drivers to find another route.
During winter, the impassability of the mountain pass can isolate entire villages.
Thick mud contributed to the impassability of the hiking trail after the storm.
The mountain's impassability made it a legendary challenge for climbers.
After ten straight championships, the coach's record achieved a level of impassability that few teams could challenge.
The thick jungle added to the impassability of the route, forcing the explorers to turn back.
