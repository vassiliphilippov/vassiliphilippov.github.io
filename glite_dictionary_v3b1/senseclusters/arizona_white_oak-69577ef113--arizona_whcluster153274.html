Arizona white oak

noun
oak species native to Arizona and New Mexico
Pronunciation: / əˈrɪzoʊnə waɪt oʊk / (AmE), / əˈrɪzənə waɪt əʊk / (BrE)
a semi-evergreen shrub or small tree native to Arizona and New Mexico, with acorns that have hemispherical cups

Examples

The Arizona white oak tree
The Arizona white oak thrives in the dry hills of southern Arizona.
The leaves of the Arizona white oak remain on the tree throughout much of the winter.
We identified several Arizona white oaks during our nature hike.
