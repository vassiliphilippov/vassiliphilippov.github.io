olm

noun
blind cave-dwelling salamander
Pronunciation: / oʊlm / (AmE), / ɒlm / (BrE)
a blind aquatic salamander native to European caves, with permanent external gills and a pale, elongated body

Examples

The olm salamander
Biologists study the olm to understand adaptations to life in total darkness.
The olm is rarely seen because it lives deep in underwater cave systems.
Unlike most amphibians, the olm retains its gills throughout its life.
