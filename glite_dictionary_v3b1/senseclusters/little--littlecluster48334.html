little

adjective, adverb
to a small extent; not much
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl / (BrE)
to a small extent or degree; a small amount or almost none

Examples

A little bit
He spoke little during the meeting, preferring to listen to others' opinions.
She had little to say after hearing the unexpected news.
They slept little that night, worrying about their journey in the morning.
During the drought, little rain fell for several weeks.
He has little patience for unnecessary delays.
There was little interest in the proposal during the meeting.
