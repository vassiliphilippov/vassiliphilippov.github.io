genus Rhododendron

noun
genus of flowering shrubs
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌroʊ.dəˈdɛn.drən / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌrəʊ.dəˈdɛn.drən / (BrE)
A large genus of evergreen shrubs native to cooler regions of the Northern Hemisphere, known for their showy and often colorful flowers.

Botanists have identified over a thousand species within the genus Rhododendron.
Gardeners often choose plants from the genus Rhododendron to add color to their landscapes in spring.
The genus Rhododendron includes both evergreen and deciduous species prized for their vivid blossoms.
