nonobservation

noun
lack of observation
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌɑbzɚˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɒbzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the absence of observation or the failure to detect or record something, especially in scientific or investigative contexts

Examples

Nonobservation of results
The nonobservation of the expected results led the researchers to question their hypothesis.
Due to nonobservation of the rare event, the experiment was repeated several times.
The report highlighted the nonobservation of safety protocols during the investigation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: detection - the process of discovering or identifying something
  • antonym: observation - the act of noticing or recording something
