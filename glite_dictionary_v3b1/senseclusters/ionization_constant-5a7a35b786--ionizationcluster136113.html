ionization constant

noun
equilibrium constant for electrolyte ionization
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪəˈnaɪzeɪʃən ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ˌaɪəˈnaɪzɪeɪʃən ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
the equilibrium constant that quantifies the degree to which a weak electrolyte dissociates into ions in solution.

Examples

The ionization constant value
Changes in temperature can affect the ionization constant of an electrolyte in water.
The ionization constant of acetic acid is much lower than that of hydrochloric acid.
Understanding the ionization constant is crucial when calculating pH in weak acid solutions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissociation constant - a constant that measures the extent to which a substance dissociates in solution
