repose

verb
place or confide (trust, etc.) in someone/something
4.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpoʊz / (AmE), / rɪˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to place or put trust, confidence, or responsibility in someone or something

Examples

Repose trust in someone
As a leader, she reposes great trust in her team.
He reposes his hopes in the success of the new initiative.
Many beliefs are reposed in tradition rather than in evidence.
In moments of crisis, citizens often repose their hopes in strong leadership.
Investors repose confidence in the stability of established markets.
She reposed her faith in her closest friends during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entrust - to assign the responsibility for doing something to someone
  • synonym: trust - firm belief in the reliability or ability of someone or something
