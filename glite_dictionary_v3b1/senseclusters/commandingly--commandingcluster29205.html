commandingly

adverb
with authority
Pronunciation: / kəˈmændɪŋli / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that shows authority, confidence, or dominance

Examples

To speak commandingly
The coach spoke commandingly to motivate the team before the big game.
She entered the meeting room commandingly, capturing everyone's attention immediately.
He stood commandingly at the front of the class, ready to begin his presentation.
She spoke commandingly to the team, inspiring everyone to do their best.
The chess champion played commandingly and won the match in just twenty moves.
He led the meeting commandingly, making sure every decision was clear and final.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritatively - in a confident and commanding way
  • synonym: decisively - in a way that produces a clear and definite result
  • antonym: timidly - in a shy or lacking confidence way
  • antonym: weakly - in a way that lacks strength or dominance
