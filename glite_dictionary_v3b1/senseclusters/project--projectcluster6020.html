Synonyms

  • stick out
    3.2 4.64/m
    stick out is an everyday phrase used when something extends beyond a surface, while project sounds more formal and technical.
  • jut
    4.8 2.61/m
    jut often describes a sharp, visible part that sticks out from a shape, while project is a general verb for extending.
  • jut out
    3.4 1.22/m
    jut out means to extend beyond the usual line in a clear way, while project covers many kinds of extension.
  • protrude
    5.8 4.35/m
    protrude is a more formal verb for something pushing out slightly, while project is more neutral everyday language.
  • poke out
    3.5 1.56/m
    poke out suggests a small part is pushed or forced outward, while project describes general extending beyond a surface.
  • overhang
    3.6 1.16/m
    overhang means to hang or extend over an edge and often implies weight, while project is any simple outward extension.
