immobilization

noun
preventing movement to aid healing
1.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ɪˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of preventing movement of a body part, usually by using a cast or splint, to promote proper healing

Examples

Immobilization of an arm
After the accident, immobilization of her arm was achieved with a splint.
Proper immobilization is crucial to ensure bones heal in the correct position.
The doctor recommended immediate immobilization of the ankle to prevent further injury.
After the fracture, immobilisation was achieved with a plaster cast.
Prolonged immobilisation can sometimes lead to muscle weakness and stiffness.
The doctor recommended immobilisation of the ankle to ensure a full recovery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fixation - the process of making something immobile or fixed in position, especially in medical treatment
  • synonym: immobilization - act of preventing movement (US spelling)
