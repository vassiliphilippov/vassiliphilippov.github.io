petit jury

noun
trial jury of twelve people
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛti ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / ˈpeti ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
A jury of twelve people chosen to decide the facts and verdict in a civil or criminal trial.

Examples

A petit jury decides
In most criminal cases, a petit jury consists of twelve members.
Jury selection is a crucial step before a petit jury is sworn in for trial.
The petit jury deliberated for hours before reaching a verdict.

Related Terms
