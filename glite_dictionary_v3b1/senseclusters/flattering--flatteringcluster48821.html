flattering

adjective
excessively complimentary, insincere praise
Pronunciation: / ˈflæt̬ɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflætərɪŋ / (BrE)
giving excessive or insincere praise, often to gain favor; obsequiously complimentary.

Examples

Flattering comments
He listened to the flattering remarks with a polite smile, unsure of their sincerity.
She gave him a flattering compliment that seemed more manipulative than genuine.
The salesman adopted a flattering tone to persuade the clients to buy more products.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fawning - excessively eager to please or praise
  • antonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings
