teasle

noun
spiny-flowered plant of the Dipsacus genus
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtiːzəl / (AmE), / ˈtiːz(ə)l / (BrE)
any of several tall herbs of the genus Dipsacus, native to the Old World, with dense, spiny flower heads often used historically in textile processing

Examples

A teasle plant
A field of wild teasle attracts many species of insects and birds.
The teasle is easily recognized by its tall stems and spiny flower heads.
The dried heads of the teasle were once used in textile mills to raise the nap on cloth.
