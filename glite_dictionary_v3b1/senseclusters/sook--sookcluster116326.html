sook

verb
to sulk or mope
Pronunciation: / sʊk / (AmE), / sʊk / (BrE)
to behave in a sulky or overly emotional way, often by complaining or moping.

Examples

To sook about homework
He tends to sook whenever things do not go his way.
Instead of trying to solve the problem, she just sooked in the corner.
If you sook about every little setback, you will never move forward.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheer up - to become happier or less upset
  • synonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something
  • synonym: sulk - to be silent and bad-tempered out of annoyance or disappointment
