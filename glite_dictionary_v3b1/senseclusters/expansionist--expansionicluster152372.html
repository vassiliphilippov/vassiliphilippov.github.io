expansionist

noun
supporter of expansion
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspænʃənɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈspænʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person or group that supports increasing a country's territory, power, or influence

Examples

An expansionist leader
The expansionist believed that acquiring new territories would strengthen the nation.
Many expansionists in the 19th century supported westward movement across the continent.
Critics argued that the expansionists ignored the rights of indigenous peoples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imperialist - a person who supports extending a country's power through colonization or force
  • antonym: isolationist - a person who favors remaining apart from the affairs of other countries
