undyed

adjective
not treated with artificial color
6.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈdaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈdaɪd / (BrE)
(of cloth, hair, etc.) not having been treated with artificial color; in its natural color.

Examples

Undyed natural fabric
She preferred to wear undyed linen shirts in the summer.
The artist chose undyed wool for her tapestry to achieve a natural look.
You can tell the difference between undyed and bleached cotton by the color and texture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dyed - having been colored with a dye
