major

adjective
having full legal age
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
(of a person) having reached the age at which one has full legal rights and responsibilities; of full legal age.

Examples

Major age limit
As a major, she was legally allowed to sign contracts on her own.
Once you turn eighteen, you are considered a major in most countries.
The court recognized him as a major and granted him full civil rights.

Related Terms

  • antonym: minor - a person under the age of legal adulthood
