towboat

noun
powerful small boat for towing ships
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈtəʊˌbəʊt / (BrE)
A small but powerful boat designed especially for pulling or pushing larger vessels, such as barges, usually on rivers or canals.

Examples

A towboat pulls barges
At dawn, a towboat arrived to help maneuver the stranded vessel into port.
Many towboats operate along the Mississippi River, moving goods between cities.
The towboat guided the massive barge safely down the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tugboat - A strong, powerful boat for towing or pushing ships, especially in harbors
