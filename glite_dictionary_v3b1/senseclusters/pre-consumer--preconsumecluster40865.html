pre-consumer

adjective
before consumer use
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː kənˈsuːmər / (AmE), / ˌpriː kənˈsjuːmə / (BrE)
describing materials or waste produced during manufacturing, before a product is sold to consumers

Examples

Pre-consumer material
Many companies collect pre-consumer scraps from factories to recycle into new products.
The fabric was made from pre-consumer waste generated during the clothing manufacturing process.
Using pre-consumer materials helps reduce the amount of industrial waste sent to landfills.

Related Terms

  • antonym: post-consumer - relating to materials or waste after a product has been used by consumers
