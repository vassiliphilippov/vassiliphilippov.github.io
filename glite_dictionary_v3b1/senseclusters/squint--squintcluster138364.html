squint

adjective
looking sideways, especially with suspicion or envy
5.5
Pronunciation: / skwɪnt / (AmE), / skwɪnt / (BrE)
(of the eyes or a glance) looking sideways, often with doubt, suspicion, or envy.

Examples

He shot a squint glance at the stranger, unsure if he could be trusted.
The child watched his friend open the gift with a squint expression of envy.
With a squint look, she signaled her doubts about the plan.
