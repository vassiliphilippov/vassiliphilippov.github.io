out

adjective
no longer burning or shining
Pronunciation: / aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
(of a fire, light, or similar) extinguished or no longer burning or shining.

Examples

The fire is out
By the time we arrived, the campfire was already out.
She waited until the candles were completely out before going to bed.
The streetlights go out every night at midnight to save energy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alight - (of a fire or light) burning or shining, opposite of out
  • synonym: extinct - (of a fire or light) no longer burning, extinguished
