outrigger

noun
a stabilizing float attached to a boat
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌrɪɡə / (BrE)
a beam or framework fixed to the side of a boat, especially a canoe, with a float attached to provide stability

Examples

Boat outrigger float
A single outrigger can greatly reduce the risk of capsizing in rough water.
He built a small outrigger to attach to his kayak for extra support.
The Polynesian sailors used an outrigger to stabilize their canoes during long ocean voyages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canoe - a light, narrow boat, pointed at both ends
