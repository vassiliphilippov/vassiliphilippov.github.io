blitz

noun
sudden intense effort
4.4
Pronunciation: / blɪts / (AmE), / blɪts / (BrE)
a sudden, intense effort or attack carried out quickly and with great force

Examples

A media blitz
The marketing team launched a blitz of social media posts to promote the new product.
After a blitz of questions from reporters, the spokesperson ended the press conference.
The coach called for a defensive blitz to pressure the opposing quarterback.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assault - a sudden violent attack
  • synonym: onslaught - a fierce or destructive attack
  • antonym: pause - a temporary stop or break
