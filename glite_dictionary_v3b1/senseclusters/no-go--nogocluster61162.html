no-go

adjective
not functioning; not permitted or possible
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊˈɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌnəʊˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
not functioning properly or not in a suitable condition for proceeding; not permitted or possible to proceed

Examples

A no-go situation
Due to technical issues, the flight was declared no-go at the last minute.
If the system is no-go, please report it to the supervisor immediately.
The project is still no-go until we secure more funding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: go - proceeding or functioning properly; the opposite condition
