Mentha pulegium

noun
a perennial mint, also called pennyroyal
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛnθə pjuˈliːdʒiəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛnθə pjuːˈliːdʒiəm / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial mint with small lilac-blue flowers and ovate leaves, commonly known as pennyroyal, yielding an aromatic oil

Examples

Mentha pulegium plant
In the wild, Mentha pulegium can be found growing along damp field margins in southern Europe.
Many herbalists caution against using Mentha pulegium oil due to its potential toxicity.
The leaves of Mentha pulegium have been historically used to flavor dishes and teas.
