terraforming

noun
making planets habitable
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈte.rə.fɑːr.mɪŋ/ /; / /ˈte.rə.fɔːr.mɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈte.rə.fɔː.mɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of changing or modifying the environment of a planet or moon to make it suitable for human life

Examples

Terraforming a planet
The idea of terraforming Mars has inspired many scientists and science fiction writers.
Successful terraforming would require advanced technology to create a breathable atmosphere.
Some experts question whether terraforming other planets is ethical or even possible.
Scientists are researching terraforming as a way to make Mars habitable for future generations.
The concept of terraforming is popular in science fiction novels and movies about space exploration.
Successful terraforming would require advanced technology to change a planet's atmosphere and climate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonization - the act of establishing control over a new area or territory
  • synonym: geoengineering - the deliberate large-scale intervention in the Earth's climate system
  • synonym: planetary engineering - the large-scale modification of a planet's environment
