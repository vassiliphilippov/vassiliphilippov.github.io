prejudice

verb
to influence opinion in advance, unfairly
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛdʒ.ə.dɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛdʒ.ɪ.dɪs / (BrE)
to influence someone so that they form an opinion about something before having enough information or without considering it fairly

Examples

To prejudice a jury
His earlier statements may have prejudiced many people against the new proposal.
Try not to prejudice the jury with your comments before the trial begins.
We must be careful not to prejudice the outcome by making assumptions too soon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - to influence someone so they support a particular point of view unfairly
