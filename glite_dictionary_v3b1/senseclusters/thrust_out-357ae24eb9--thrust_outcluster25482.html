thrust out

verb
push or force outward
5.9
Pronunciation: / θrʌst aʊt / (AmE), / θrʌst aʊt / (BrE)
to push or force something outward with sudden or strong movement

Examples

To thrust out a hand
He thrust out his hand to catch the falling glass.
She thrust out her chin defiantly, refusing to back down.
The snake thrust out its tongue when it sensed danger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pull in - to draw something inward or toward oneself
  • synonym: thrust - to push something suddenly or violently in a certain direction
