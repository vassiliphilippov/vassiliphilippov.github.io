attenuated

adjective
reduced in strength or effect
7.0
Pronunciation: / əˈtɛnjuˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnjueɪtɪd /; / əˈtɛnjʊeɪtɪd / (BrE)
weakened or reduced in force, effect, or intensity (can refer to signals, effects, or other things)

Examples

An attenuated signal
Our Wi-Fi signal became attenuated as we moved farther from the router.
The lawyer argued that stress had attenuated his client’s ability to make good decisions.
The scientist worked with an attenuated virus to develop a safer vaccine.
After passing through several walls, the Wi-Fi signal became noticeably attenuated.
The attenuated signal was still detectable by the receiver despite its low strength.
To avoid interference, the audio cable uses an attenuated signal over long distances.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplified - made greater in volume or strength (especially of a signal)
  • antonym: strengthened - made stronger
  • synonym: weakened - made weaker
