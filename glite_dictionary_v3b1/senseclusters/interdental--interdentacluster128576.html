interdental

adjective
between the teeth
7.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈden.təl/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈden.təl/ (BrE)
produced with the tongue placed between the teeth, especially referring to certain speech sounds in phonetics

Examples

Interdental sound
The English 'th' in 'think' is an interdental sound produced with the tongue between the teeth.
Some languages do not have any interdental consonants in their phonetic inventory.
Speech therapists often teach children how to pronounce interdental sounds correctly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alveolar - produced with the tongue against the ridge behind the teeth
  • synonym: dental - produced with the tongue against the teeth
