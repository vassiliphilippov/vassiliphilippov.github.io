Mandean language

noun
Aramaic dialect of the Mandeans
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmænˈdiːən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmænˈdiːən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the dialect of Aramaic traditionally spoken and written by the Mandean religious community, primarily in southern Iraq and Iran.

Examples

Mandean language texts
Classical texts of the Mandean language are vital for understanding the beliefs of the Mandean people.
Few scholars today are fluent in the Mandean language, as it is considered endangered.
The rituals of Mandaeism are traditionally conducted in the Mandean language.
