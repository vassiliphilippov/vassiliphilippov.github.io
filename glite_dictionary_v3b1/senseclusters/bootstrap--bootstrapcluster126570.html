bootstrap

verb
improve by one's own efforts
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌstræp / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌstræp / (BrE)
to improve one's situation by one's own efforts, often using minimal resources or improvised methods

Examples

To bootstrap a business
After losing his job, he decided to bootstrap a new business from his garage.
She had to bootstrap her education, taking free online courses and studying late into the night.
With limited funding, the team managed to bootstrap their project to completion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: improvise - to create or perform without preparation or with what is available
  • synonym: self-help - the practice of improving oneself without outside assistance
