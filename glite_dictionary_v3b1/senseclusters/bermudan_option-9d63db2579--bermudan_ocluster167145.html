Bermudan option

noun
option exercisable at set times
Pronunciation: / bɚˈmjuːdən ˈɑːpʃən / (AmE), / bəˈmjuːdən ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
A type of financial option that can be exercised at specific discrete times during its life, not just at expiry or at any time.

Examples

A Bermudan option contract
Many structured financial products use the Bermudan option as a flexible compromise between American and European options.
The Bermudan option allows the holder to exercise rights on specific dates within the contract period.
Unlike an American option, a Bermudan option can only be exercised at certain prescribed times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: American option - an option exercisable at any time before expiry
  • synonym: European option - an option exercisable only at expiry
