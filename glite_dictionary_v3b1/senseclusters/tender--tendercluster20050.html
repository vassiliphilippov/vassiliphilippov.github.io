tender

noun
formal offer to buy at a set price
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛndər / (AmE), / ˈtɛndə / (BrE)
an official written offer to buy something at a stated price, especially as part of a formal process for contracts or procurement

Examples

A tender for a contract
All tenders must be received by the closing date.
The company submitted a tender for the construction project.
Their tender was the lowest, so they won the contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bid - offer to pay a particular price for something, especially at auction or in business
