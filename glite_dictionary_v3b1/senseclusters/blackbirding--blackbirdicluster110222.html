blackbirding

noun
forced labor kidnapping
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌbɜrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbɜːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of kidnapping or forcibly recruiting people, often by deception, to work as laborers (especially in the 19th century Pacific islands)

Examples

Blackbirding of workers
During the 19th century, blackbirding was used to supply labor for plantations in Australia and Fiji.

Modern blackbirding operations often target vulnerable migrant workers seeking better opportunities.
Laws were introduced to combat blackbirding and protect people from forced labor.
