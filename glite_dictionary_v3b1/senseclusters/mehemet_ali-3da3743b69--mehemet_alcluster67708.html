Mehemet Ali

proper noun
Albanian viceroy of Egypt (1769–1849)
Pronunciation: / məˈhɛmət ɑːˈliː / (AmE), / məˈhɛmɪt ˈɑːli / (BrE)
(1769–1849) An Albanian soldier who became the viceroy of Egypt, overthrew Ottoman control, and established Egypt as a modern state.

Examples

Mehemet Ali of Egypt
Mehemet Ali is often credited with founding modern Egypt through a series of ambitious reforms.
Historians agree that Mehemet Ali asserted Egypt's independence from Ottoman rule in the nineteenth century.
The dynasty established by Mehemet Ali ruled Egypt until the mid-twentieth century.
