dehydrogenation

noun
removal of hydrogen
8.8
Pronunciation: / diˌhaɪdrədʒəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌhaɪdrɒdʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical process in which hydrogen is removed from a molecule, often by an enzyme in biochemical reactions

Examples

The dehydrogenation process
The dehydrogenation of hydrocarbons is a key step in producing certain chemicals.
Industrial dehydrogenation processes often require high temperatures and special catalysts.
Scientists studied the dehydrogenation reaction to improve fuel cell efficiency.
The dehydrogenation of alcohols is a key step in many metabolic pathways.
Industrial dehydrogenation processes are used to produce alkenes from alkanes.
Enzymes that catalyze dehydrogenation play an important role in cellular respiration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrogenation - a chemical process in which hydrogen is added to a molecule
  • synonym: oxidation - a chemical reaction involving the loss of electrons or increase in oxidation state
