skeeter hawk

noun
insect that feeds on mosquitoes; dragonfly
Pronunciation: / ˈskiːtər hɑk / (AmE), / ˈskiːtə hɔːk / (BrE)
A slender-bodied insect with iridescent wings, also known as a dragonfly, that does not sting and is known for feeding on mosquitoes.

Examples

A skeeter hawk insect
A skeeter hawk darted over the pond, catching mosquitoes just above the water's surface.
Children watched as the brilliant blue skeeter hawk hovered near the reeds.
My grandmother always called dragonflies skeeter hawks because they eat so many bugs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dragonfly - a large insect with a long slender body and two pairs of large, transparent wings
