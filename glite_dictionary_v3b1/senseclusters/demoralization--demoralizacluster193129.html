demoralization

noun
loss of morale; discouragement
Pronunciation: / dɪˌmɔrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / dɪˌmɔrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌmɒrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of discouragement, depression, or confusion caused by loss of morale, especially in a person or group

Examples

Team demoralization
Continuous criticism can result in demoralization and reduced productivity.
The team's repeated losses led to a sense of demoralization among the players.
There was demoralization in the office after the company announced major layoffs.
Continued management failures resulted in the demoralization of the entire department.
The sudden loss of leadership created demoralization and chaos in the organization.
The team's crushing defeat led to a sense of demoralization among the players.
The sudden policy changes caused confusion and demoralisation among the staff.
The team's repeated losses led to complete demoralisation within the locker room.
Widespread layoffs created a sense of demoralisation across the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demoralization - loss of confidence or morale; same meaning
  • synonym: disorganization - a lack of order or structure
  • antonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope
  • antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged appropriately
