Roman pace

noun
ancient Roman unit of length
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.mən peɪs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən peɪs / (BrE)
an ancient Roman unit of length equal to about 4.85 English feet, measured as the distance from the heel of one foot to the heel of the same foot the next time it touches the ground

Examples

One Roman pace length
A distance of one thousand Roman paces was marked as a milestone along the ancient road.
Historians estimate that a Roman pace measured just under five English feet.
The soldiers were trained to march exactly one Roman pace between steps.
