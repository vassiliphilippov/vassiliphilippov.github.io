drift

verb
move slowly from a fixed point or course
6.5
Pronunciation: / drɪft / (AmE), / drɪft / (BrE)
to move slowly or be carried along by a current of air or water, or to move aimlessly or without a clear direction or purpose (including people, thoughts, or things)

Examples

To drift away
Her attention would often drift during long meetings.
Over time, the boat began to drift away from the shore.
Stock prices can drift higher or lower based on economic news.
During the desert storm, sand would drift into high mounds near the dunes.
Fresh snow can drift across the road after a windy night.
Leaves drifted against the back fence, piling up over time.
If we don't pay attention, we might drift off course.
Over time, his mind would drift during long meetings.
The boat began to drift away from the shore as the wind picked up.
