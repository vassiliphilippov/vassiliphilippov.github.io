megabyte

noun
unit of digital information (1,000,000 bytes)
5.0 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛɡəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəˌbaɪt /; / ˈmɛɡəbaɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information used to measure data size or computer memory (about one million bytes)

Examples

A megabyte of data
My phone's storage capacity is 64 megabytes.
The document was too large to email because it was over five megabytes.
This photo only takes up two megabytes of space on my computer.
A single high-resolution photo can take up several megabytes of memory.
Downloading the update requires at least 100 megabytes of space on your device.
My phone only has 500 megabytes of free storage left.
