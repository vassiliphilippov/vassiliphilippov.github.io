udon

noun
Japanese wheat noodles
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈuːdɑn / (AmE), / ˈuːdɒn / (BrE)
a thick Japanese wheat noodle, or a dish made with these noodles, usually served in soup or with various toppings

Examples

A bowl of udon
For lunch, she ordered a steaming bowl of udon topped with tempura shrimp.
He prefers his udon cold with a soy-based dipping sauce during the summer.
The restaurant is famous for its handmade udon noodles and flavorful broths.
For dinner, he enjoyed a steaming bowl of udon topped with green onions and mushrooms.
Many Japanese restaurants offer udon in both hot and cold dishes.
She learned how to make homemade udon noodles from scratch during her trip to Japan.
