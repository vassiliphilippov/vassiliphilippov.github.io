has-been

noun
person formerly famous or successful
Pronunciation: / ˈhæz ˌbɪn /; / ˈhæzˌbɪn / (AmE), / ˈhæz ˌbiːn /; / ˈhæzˌbɪn / (BrE)
a person or thing that was once famous, important, or successful but is no longer

Examples

A has-been star
After his third flop movie, many began to call him a has-been.
Although some view him as a has-been, he still draws large crowds to his concerts.
She refuses to let people treat her like a has-been after years on television.
Many critics dismissed the aging actor as a has-been, but he made a remarkable comeback.
She hated being referred to as a has-been after her business failed.
The band's new album proved they were more than just has-beens from the past.

Related Terms

  • antonym: celebrity - a famous person
  • antonym: nobody - a person of no importance or influence
  • antonym: star - a person who is very famous, especially in entertainment
  • antonym: up-and-comer - a person who is rising to prominence or success
