arcus

noun
arc-shaped deposit in the cornea
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrkəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːkəs / (BrE)
A whitish, arc-shaped deposit that appears at the edge of the cornea, often associated with aging or certain medical conditions.

Examples

An arcus corneae
An arcus can be a sign of elevated cholesterol, especially in younger individuals.
The ophthalmologist noticed an arcus around the patient's cornea during the eye exam.
While arcus is common in older adults, it may warrant investigation if seen in someone under 40.
