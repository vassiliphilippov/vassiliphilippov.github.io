mid-sentence

adjective, adverb
in the middle of a sentence
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌsɛntəns /; / ˌmɪdˈsɛntəns / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌsɛntəns /; / ˌmɪdˈsɛntəns / (BrE)
happening or occurring in the middle of a sentence, or while speaking or writing before the sentence is finished

Examples

Mid-sentence interruption
She was interrupted mid-sentence by a sudden loud noise from outside.
He realized mid-sentence that he had made a mistake in his explanation.
The teacher corrected the student mid-sentence to help clarify the answer.
He stopped mid-sentence when he realized he had made a mistake.
The phone rang mid-sentence, interrupting their conversation.
She laughed mid-sentence because she remembered something funny.

Related Terms

  • antonym: end - the final part of something
  • synonym: interruption - a break or pause in speech or action
  • synonym: midway - at or in the middle of something
