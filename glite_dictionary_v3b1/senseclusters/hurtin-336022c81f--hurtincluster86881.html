hurtin'

verb
to need badly
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜrtɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɜːtɪn / (BrE)
to be in great need of something or to want something badly, especially in a difficult situation (informal, chiefly American English).

Examples

Hurtin' for money
After the layoffs, the family was hurtin' for money to pay rent this month.
We're really hurtin' for volunteers to help with the event this weekend.
By the end of the hike, everyone was hurtin' for some cold water and a rest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abundant - having plenty of something
  • synonym: desperate - needing or wanting something very much
  • synonym: lacking - not having enough of something
