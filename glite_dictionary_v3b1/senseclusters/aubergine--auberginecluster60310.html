aubergine

noun
plant and its edible fruit (eggplant)
9.8 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.bər.ʒiːn/ /; / /ˈoʊ.bər.dʒɪn/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.bə.ʒiːn/ /; / /ˈəʊ.bə.dʒiːn/ (BrE)
A hairy, upright plant native to Southeast Asia, widely cultivated for its large, glossy, edible fruit, which is commonly used as a vegetable.

Examples

Aubergine plant
In the UK, people often refer to eggplant as aubergine.
She roasted slices of aubergine with olive oil and herbs for dinner.
The recipe calls for a large aubergine to be cut into cubes and fried.
