plain

verb
to complain or express dissatisfaction (archaic)
9.9
Pronunciation: / pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
to express complaints, discontent, or unhappiness; to complain, especially in a persistent or whining manner

Examples

To plain about work
He plained day and night, but no one listened to his woes.
She would often plain about her duties at court.
The villagers began to plain to the lord about the new taxes.
