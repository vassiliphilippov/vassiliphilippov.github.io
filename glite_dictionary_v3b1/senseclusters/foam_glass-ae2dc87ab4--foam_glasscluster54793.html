foam glass

noun
lightweight cellular glass material
Pronunciation: / foʊm ɡlæs / (AmE), / fəʊm ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A lightweight, opaque, cellular glass made by expanding crushed glass, used primarily for insulation and as a building material.

Examples

Foam glass insulation
Foam glass is valued for its resistance to moisture and fire.
Researchers are developing new methods for recycling waste glass into foam glass products.
The contractor recommended using foam glass for thermal insulation in the new building.
