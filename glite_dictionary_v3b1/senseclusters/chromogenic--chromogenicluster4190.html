chromogenic

adjective
producing color
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkroʊməˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊməˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
producing color or capable of developing color, especially as a result of a chemical reaction

Examples

Chromogenic bacteria
Certain bacteria are chromogenic and produce pigments as they grow.
The chromogenic reaction in the test tube indicated the presence of the enzyme.
Modern color film relies on a chromogenic process to develop photographs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: color-producing - able to create or develop color
  • antonym: colorless - having no color
