quietism

noun
religious mysticism focused on passive contemplation
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkwaɪ.əˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪ.ət.ɪzm / (BrE)
a form of religious mysticism that emphasizes withdrawal from worldly activity and passive contemplation of God

Examples

Quietism in religion
She found solace in quietism, seeking God through passive meditation and stillness.
Some critics argue that quietism discourages social action in favor of spiritual withdrawal.
The rise of quietism in 17th-century Europe alarmed church authorities.
