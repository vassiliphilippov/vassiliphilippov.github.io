circulation

noun
the spread or transmission of something
4.0 3.17/m
Pronunciation: / Àås…ùÀê.kj…ôÀàle…™. É…ôn / (AmE), / Àås…úÀê.kj äÀàle…™. É…ôn / (BrE)
the spreading or transmission of something, such as information, news, or money, to a wider group or area

Examples

Circulation of news
The circulation of fake news on social media has become a major concern.
The new currency notes were put into circulation last month.
Wider circulation of these reports could help inform the public debate.

Synonyms

  • dissemination
    6.6 3.51/m
    dissemination spreads information widely and often deliberately, while circulation refers to the general movement of news or money among people.
  • spreading
    2.3 0.02/m
    spreading moves ideas or facts across areas in a general way, while circulation often implies regular movement through a system.
  • diffusion
    5.8 2.30/m
    diffusion describes a slow, natural spread through a space or group, while circulation focuses on broader movement or exchange.
  • distribution
    3.2 9.45/m
    distribution gives out items to people or places in a planned way, while circulation stresses continuous movement or reach.
  • transmission
    3.6 5.79/m
    transmission sends a message from one person or place to another directly, while circulation suggests wider spread among many.
  • propagation
    6.5 0.47/m
    propagation promotes ideas or beliefs so others adopt them, while circulation names the general movement or flow among people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissemination - the action of spreading something widely
  • synonym: distribution - the act of giving or delivering something to people
