vestigia

noun
evolutionary remnants
9.9
Pronunciation: / vɛˈstɪdʒiə / (AmE), / vɛˈstɪdʒiə / (BrE)
small anatomical structures or features in organisms that are remnants of evolutionary ancestors.

Examples

Fish vestigia in bones
Scientists pointed to tiny vestigia in the fish's skeleton as evidence of terrestrial ancestry.
The presence of vestigia in modern whales suggests their ancestors once walked on land.
Researchers study vestigia to understand how certain species have evolved over millions of years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rudiment - a basic or undeveloped part, especially in biology
  • synonym: vestige - a trace or remnant of something that once existed
