Archeozoic

noun
ancient geological eon; Earth's crust formed
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrkioʊˈzoʊɪk /; / ˌɑːr.ki.oʊˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪəʊˈzəʊɪk /; / ˌɑː.ki.əʊˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
The geological eon from about 3,800 million to 2,500 million years ago, during which the Earth's crust formed and the earliest unicellular life appeared.

Examples

The Archeozoic eon
Complex life had not yet evolved during the Archeozoic era.
Scientists study rocks from the Archeozoic to understand Earth's earliest history.
The Archeozoic is characterized by the formation of the planet's first solid crust.
Fossils from the Archaeozoic reveal the earliest forms of life on Earth.
Geologists study rocks formed during the Archaeozoic to learn about the planet's early history.
The Archaeozoic era saw the creation of Earth's first stable crust.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Archaean - the main alternative term for this early geological eon
  • synonym: Precambrian - the time in Earth's history before the Cambrian period, including the Archaeozoic and Proterozoic eons
  • degree higher: Proterozoic - the geological eon after the Archaeozoic, from 2,500 to 541 million years ago
