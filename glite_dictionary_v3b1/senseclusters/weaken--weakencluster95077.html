weaken

verb
make less strong or effective
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːkən / (AmE), / ˈwiːkən / (BrE)
to make or become less strong, powerful, or effective

Examples

To weaken the body
Continued stress can weaken your immune system over time.
The medicine will weaken the effects of the virus in your body.
They built barriers to weaken the impact of the incoming waves.
Her illness has seriously weakened her immune system.
If you don’t reinforce the wall, it may weaken over time.
The intense heat began to weaken the metal structure.
Her voice began to weaken as she grew more tired.
Over time, the storm will weaken and move inland.
The team's defense started to weaken after the break.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diminish - to decrease in strength, size, or importance
  • synonym: sabotage - to deliberately damage or destroy something to prevent it from working properly
  • synonym: sap - to gradually weaken or destroy
  • antonym: strengthen - to make something stronger or more effective
