catalyzer

noun
agent speeding reaction
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkætəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈkætəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a substance or agent that increases the speed of a chemical reaction without being consumed in the process

Examples

Chemical catalyzer
A catalyzer is often added to speed up the reaction in industrial processes.
The scientist explained how the catalyzer made the experiment more efficient.
Without a catalyzer, the chemical reaction would take much longer to complete.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalyst - a substance that speeds up a chemical reaction
  • antonym: inhibitor - a substance that slows down or prevents a chemical reaction
