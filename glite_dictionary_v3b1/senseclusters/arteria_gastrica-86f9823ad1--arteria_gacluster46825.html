arteria gastrica

noun
artery supplying the stomach
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈtɪəriə ɡæˈstrɪkə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ɡaˈstrɪkə / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the walls of the stomach

Examples

Arteria gastrica blood supply
Damage to the arteria gastrica can lead to gastric ischemia.
During the surgery, the arteria gastrica was carefully preserved to maintain blood flow to the stomach.
The left arteria gastrica is a branch of the celiac artery.
