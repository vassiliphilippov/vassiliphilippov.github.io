immanence

noun
state of being inherent or within
5.4 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɪm.ə.nəns/ (AmE), /ˈɪm.ə.nəns/ (BrE)
The state or quality of being inherent or existing within something; especially, the idea that something (such as a divine presence or a quality) remains within or pervades the material world or a particular domain.

Examples

The immanence of spirit
Debates about immanence often arise in discussions contrasting it with transcendence.
Many religious traditions discuss the immanence of the divine in the physical world.
The philosopher emphasized the immanence of consciousness in human experience.
