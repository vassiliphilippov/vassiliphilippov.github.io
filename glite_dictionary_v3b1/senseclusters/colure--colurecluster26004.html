colure

noun
celestial great circle
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈlʊr / (AmE), / kəˈljʊə / (BrE)
an imaginary great circle on the celestial sphere that passes through the celestial poles and either the equinoxes or the solstices

Examples

The colure circle
Astronomers use the colure to help define important points on the celestial sphere.
The solstitial colure passes through the celestial poles and the solstices.
Ancient star maps often marked the position of each colure for reference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: celestial equator - the imaginary circle around the sky directly above Earth's equator
  • synonym: meridian - an imaginary line passing from the north to the south pole on the celestial sphere
