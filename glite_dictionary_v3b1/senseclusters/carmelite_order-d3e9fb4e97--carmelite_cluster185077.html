Carmelite order

noun
Roman Catholic religious order
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrməˌlaɪt ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːməlʌɪt ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a Roman Catholic religious order founded in the 12th century, known for its contemplative tradition and devotion to prayer

Examples

The Carmelite order community
Many members of the Carmelite order have contributed to Catholic spirituality and mysticism.
Saint Teresa of Ávila was a prominent reformer of the Carmelite order in the 16th century.
The Carmelite order places a strong emphasis on contemplative prayer and solitude.
