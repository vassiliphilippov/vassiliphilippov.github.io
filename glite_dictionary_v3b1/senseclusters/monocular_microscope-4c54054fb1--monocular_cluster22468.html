monocular microscope

noun
microscope for use with one eye
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑːkjələr ˈmaɪkrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / məˈnɒkjʊlə ˈmaɪkrəskəʊp / (BrE)
A microscope with a single objective and a single eyepiece, designed for use with one eye.

Examples

A monocular microscope lens
A monocular microscope can be more affordable for beginners in microscopy.
Older laboratories frequently use a monocular microscope rather than a binocular model.
The student adjusted the monocular microscope to observe the details of the onion cell.

Related Terms

  • antonym: binocular microscope - microscope with two eyepieces for both eyes
