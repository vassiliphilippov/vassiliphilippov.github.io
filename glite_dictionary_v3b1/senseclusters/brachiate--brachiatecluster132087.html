brachiate

verb
swing from hold to hold with the arms
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrækiˌeɪt / (BrE)
(of certain animals, especially primates) to move by swinging from one hold to another using the arms.

Examples

To brachiate through trees
Gibbons are well known to brachiate through the forest canopy.
Scientists observed how easily the monkeys brachiated from branch to branch.
Some athletes can brachiate across monkey bars with surprising speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: swing - move back and forth or from side to side while suspended or on an axis
