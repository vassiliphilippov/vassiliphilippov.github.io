isotopy

noun
continuous deformation
Pronunciation: / aɪˈsɑːtəpi / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒtəpi / (BrE)
a continuous transformation in topology that changes one shape or embedding into another without cutting or gluing

Examples

Topological isotopy
In topology, an isotopy can show that two knots are actually the same up to deformation.
The mathematician explained how an isotopy could transform one curve into another without breaking it.
Understanding isotopy is essential for studying the properties of geometric objects in higher dimensions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeomorphism - a continuous, bijective mapping with a continuous inverse between topological spaces
  • synonym: homotopy - a continuous transformation between two functions or shapes in topology
