well-to-do

adjective
having plenty of money; moderately rich
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌwɛl tə ˈdu / (AmE), / ˌwɛl tə ˈduː / (BrE)
Having plenty of money and a comfortable standard of living; moderately rich.

Examples

A well-to-do family
He grew up in a well-to-do household and attended private schools.
Many of the guests at the gala were well-to-do business owners and professionals.
The neighborhood is mostly well-to-do families who live in large homes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: poor - lacking sufficient money or possessions
  • synonym: wealthy - having a lot of money and possessions
