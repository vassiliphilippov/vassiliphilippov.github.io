baking

noun
precomputing lighting into textures
6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.kɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbeɪ.kɪŋ/ (BrE)
(in computer graphics) the process of precomputing lighting, shadows, or other shading information into texture maps (lightmaps) so that scenes render faster in real time.

Examples

Baking textures
Texturing artists rely on baking to store complex lighting information in compact texture files.
Before export, the team performed a final baking pass to embed shadows into the game's lightmaps.
Real-time engines often use GPU-accelerated baking workflows to reduce artists' iteration time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lightmapping - the process of baking lighting into textures (lightmaps) for use in real-time rendering
  • synonym: precomputation - the act of calculating data in advance so it can be used quickly at runtime
