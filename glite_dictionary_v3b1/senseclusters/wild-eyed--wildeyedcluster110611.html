wild-eyed

adjective
appearing extremely agitated or excited
Pronunciation: / waɪld aɪd / (AmE), / waɪld aɪd / (BrE)
looking extremely agitated, excited, or crazy, often with a staring or wide-eyed expression

Examples

Wild-eyed and desperate
After days without sleep, he staggered into the room, looking wild-eyed and desperate.
The wild-eyed horse reared nervously at the sound of thunder.
The protesters grew wild-eyed as the tension increased during the rally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or agitation
  • synonym: frenzied - wildly excited or uncontrolled
