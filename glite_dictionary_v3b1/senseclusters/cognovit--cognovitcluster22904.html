cognovit

noun
confession of judgment
Pronunciation: / kɑɡˈnoʊvɪt /; / kəgˈnoʊvɪt / (AmE), / kɒɡˈnəʊvɪt /; / kɒgˈnəʊvɪt / (BrE)
a written statement by a defendant in a lawsuit admitting the plaintiff's claim and allowing judgment to be entered without a trial

Examples

A cognovit statement
The defendant signed a cognovit to avoid a lengthy court battle.
By submitting a cognovit, she allowed the court to enter judgment immediately.
The lawyer explained the consequences of agreeing to a cognovit in the settlement.
The defendant signed a cognovit to avoid a lengthy trial.
By submitting a cognovit, she accepted the court's judgment without further dispute.
A lawyer may advise a client to issue a cognovit if the claim is clearly valid.
