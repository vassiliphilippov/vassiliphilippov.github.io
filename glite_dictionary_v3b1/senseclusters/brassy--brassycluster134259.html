brassy

adjective
bold and shameless; brazen
Pronunciation: / ˈbræsi / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːsi / (BrE)
(of a person, behavior, or thing) bold, flashy, or lacking in subtlety; shameless or showy in a way that is not considered polite or tasteful

Examples

Brassy behavior
His brassy remarks during the presentation shocked the entire boardroom.
She made a brassy entrance, ignoring everyone who stared at her unusual outfit.
The comedian is known for her brassy style and unapologetic jokes.
His brassy jewelry caught everyone's eye, but not in a good way.
The room was decorated in a brassy style that some found overwhelming.
The singer wore a brassy dress covered in sequins to the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barefaced - shameless; without disguise or concealment
  • synonym: brazen - shamelessly bold or without respect for convention
  • synonym: flashy - showy in a way that attracts attention
  • synonym: gaudy - excessively bright or showy, lacking in good taste
  • antonym: modest - having a humble or reserved manner; not brash
  • antonym: subtle - not obvious or showy; understated
