catalog buying

noun
buying goods via mail-order catalog
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæt.ə.lɔg ˈbaɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkæt.əlɒɡ ˈbaɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of purchasing goods through mail-order catalogs, usually to be delivered directly to the buyer's address

Examples

Catalog buying by mail
Many companies offered catalog buying as an alternative to visiting physical stores.
She used to do most of her shopping by catalog buying before online stores became popular.
The convenience of catalog buying allowed rural families to access a variety of goods.
