distal

adjective
anatomy: farther from the body's center
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪstl̩ /; / ˈdɪs.təl / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəl /; / ˈdɪs.təl / (BrE)
(anatomy) situated away from the center or point of attachment of the body, especially of a limb, bone, or tooth

Examples

The distal end
In dentistry, the distal surfaces of teeth face away from the center of the dental arch.
The fingers are distal to the wrist.
The tibia is distal to the femur in the human leg.
In anatomy, the thumb is on the distal side of the radius bone.
Nerve damage often affects the distal parts of the limbs first, such as the hands and feet.
The distal end of the femur articulates with the knee joint.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proximal - situated nearer to the center or point of attachment
