Ascaris

noun
genus of parasitic roundworms
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæskərɪs / (AmE), / ˈæskərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, parasitic roundworms with a three-lipped mouth, especially known for infecting the intestines of humans and animals.

Examples

Ascaris roundworm infection
Ascaris infections can cause abdominal discomfort and nutritional deficiencies, particularly in children.
Researchers study Ascaris to better understand how parasitic worms affect human health.
The veterinarian diagnosed the puppy with Ascaris after analyzing a stool sample.
