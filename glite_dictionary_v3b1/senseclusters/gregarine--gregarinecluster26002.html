gregarine

noun
a protozoan parasite of invertebrates
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈɡɛrɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɛərɪn / (BrE)
a protozoan parasite, typically worm-shaped, that lives in the intestines of insects and other invertebrates

Examples

A gregarine parasite
A single gregarine can produce numerous spores during its parasitic phase.
Researchers studied the life cycle of the gregarine found in freshwater shrimp.
The gregarine invaded the locust’s gut, disrupting its digestive processes.
