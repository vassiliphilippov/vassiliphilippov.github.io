iron deficiency anemia

noun
anemia caused by too little iron
3.4 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.ərn dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ /; / /ˈaɪrn dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ /; / /ˈaɪ.ərn dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.sɪ ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.ən dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.sɪ ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ /; / /ˈaɪ.ən dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ (BrE)
a type of anemia caused by insufficient iron in the body, typically due to poor diet or chronic blood loss, resulting in reduced red blood cell production

Examples

Iron deficiency anemia symptoms
Chronic blood loss can eventually lead to iron deficiency anemia if not properly treated.
The doctor diagnosed her with iron deficiency anemia after noticing she was frequently tired and pale.
Vegetarians are sometimes at risk of iron deficiency anemia if they do not consume enough iron-rich foods.
A diet lacking in red meat and green vegetables can sometimes lead to iron deficiency anaemia.
Doctors diagnosed her with iron deficiency anaemia after she complained of constant fatigue and weakness.
Pregnant women are at higher risk of developing iron deficiency anaemia and may need supplements.
