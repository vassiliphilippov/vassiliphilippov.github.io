Roman calendar

noun
ancient Roman lunar calendar
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.mən ˈkæl.ən.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈkæl.ən.də / (BrE)
the lunar calendar system used in ancient Rome before the adoption of the Julian calendar in 46 BC.

Examples

The Roman calendar system
Historians debate how accurately the Roman calendar reflected the lunar cycle.
Important Roman festivals were scheduled according to the Roman calendar.
The Roman calendar was eventually replaced by the Julian calendar in the first century BC.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Julian calendar - the solar calendar introduced in Rome in 46 BC, replacing the Roman calendar
