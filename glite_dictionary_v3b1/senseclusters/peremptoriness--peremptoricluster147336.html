peremptoriness

noun
commanding quality
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɛmptərɪnəs / (AmE), / pəˈrɛmptərɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being commanding, decisive, and not allowing refusal or contradiction

Examples

The peremptoriness of tone
The peremptoriness in her tone made it clear that no objections would be accepted.
His peremptoriness often discouraged others from voicing their opinions during meetings.
The manager's peremptoriness ensured that decisions were made quickly and without debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assertiveness - the quality of being confident and direct
  • antonym: timidity - the quality of being shy or lacking confidence
