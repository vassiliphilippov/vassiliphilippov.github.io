hypolimnetic

adjective
of deep lake layer
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊlɪmˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊlɪmˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the hypolimnion, the cold, deep layer of water in a stratified lake

Examples

Hypolimnetic oxygen levels
The hypolimnetic oxygen levels are crucial for the survival of certain fish species.
Researchers measured the temperature changes in the hypolimnetic region of the lake.
Nutrient concentrations are often higher in hypolimnetic waters during the summer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epilimnetic - relating to the upper layer of water in a stratified lake
