drylot

noun
livestock holding area
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪlɑt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪlɒt / (BrE)
an outdoor enclosure where livestock are kept and fed without access to pasture

Examples

A drylot enclosure
During the rainy season, the cows were kept in the drylot to prevent damage to the fields.
Farmers use a drylot to manage livestock when pasture is unavailable.
The new drylot was built near the barn for easier feeding and cleaning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feedlot - an area where livestock are fed and fattened for market
  • antonym: pasture - land covered with grass for grazing animals
