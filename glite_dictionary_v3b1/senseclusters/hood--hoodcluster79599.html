hood

noun
leather covering for a hawk's head
Pronunciation: / hʊd / (AmE), / hʊd / (BrE)
a covering for the head, often attached to a coat or cloak, or used to cover a bird's head

Examples

A jacket hood
A richly decorated leather hood protected the bird’s eyes during the noisy journey.
The falconer gently removed the hood from the hawk before releasing it to hunt.
Without its hood, the hawk became restless and alert to its surroundings.
Many winter coats come with a detachable hood for extra warmth.
She pulled her hood up to protect herself from the rain.
The medieval monk wore a brown robe with a large hood covering his head.

Related Terms

  • synonym: helmet - a hard or padded protective hat
