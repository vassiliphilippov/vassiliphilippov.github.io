piano sonata

noun
a sonata for solo piano
Pronunciation: / piˈænoʊ səˈnɑtə / (AmE), / pɪˈænəʊ səˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A sonata composed specifically for solo piano, typically consisting of several movements with contrasting tempos and characters.

Examples

A piano sonata piece
Beethoven's most famous piano sonata is often considered to be the 'Moonlight Sonata'.
She spent months practicing the difficult passages in the new piano sonata.
The concert featured a rarely performed piano sonata by a contemporary composer.
