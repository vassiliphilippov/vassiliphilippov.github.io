spruik

noun
public promoter
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / spruÀêk / (AmE), / spruÀêk / (BrE)
a person who publicly promotes or advertises goods, events, or ideas, often in a loud or enthusiastic manner

Examples

A market spruik
The market hired a spruik to attract more customers to the food stalls.
A lively spruik stood at the entrance, calling out special offers to passersby.
Every festival needs a good spruik to create excitement and draw a crowd.

Synonyms

  • pitchman
    6.8 0.31/m
    a pitchman gives lively, persuasive sales talks in public, while a spruik loudly promotes goods or events to attract customers.
  • plugger
    7.8 0.01/m
    a plugger actively supports or promotes something with steady effort and enthusiasm, while a spruik focuses on loud, public selling.
  • evangelist
    5.7 0.92/m
    an evangelist strongly advocates products or ideas in business or tech settings, while a spruik loudly promotes goods at events or on the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barker - a person who stands outside and calls out to attract customers
