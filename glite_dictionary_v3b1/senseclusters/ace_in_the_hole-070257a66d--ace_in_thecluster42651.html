ace in the hole

noun
hidden advantage or resource
Pronunciation: / eɪs ɪn ðə hoʊl / (AmE), / eɪs ɪn ðə həʊl / (BrE)
a secret advantage or resource kept in reserve until it is needed

Examples

An ace in the hole advantage
Her ability to speak four languages was her ace in the hole when applying for international jobs.
If negotiations fail, we have an ace in the hole that could turn things in our favor.
The company's research team proved to be the ace in the hole during fierce competition.
