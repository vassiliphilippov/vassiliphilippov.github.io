wood cudweed

noun
a weedy plant with woolly leaves and white flowers
4.8
Pronunciation: / wʊd ˈkʌdˌwid / (AmE), / wʊd ˈkʌdˌwiːd / (BrE)
A weedy perennial plant of northern temperate regions, with woolly leaves and dirty white flowers arranged in a leafy spike.

Examples

A wood cudweed plant
During their hike, they identified a patch of wood cudweed growing beside the trail.
Gardeners often mistake wood cudweed for other woolly-leaved weeds in early summer.
The wood cudweed thrives in damp, shaded areas of the forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cudweed - a group of plants with woolly foliage in the genus Gnaphalium and related genera
