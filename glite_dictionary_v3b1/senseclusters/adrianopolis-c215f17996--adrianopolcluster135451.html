Adrianopolis

proper noun
ancient city in Turkey, renamed by Hadrian
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪdriəˈnɑːpəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌeɪdrɪəˈnɒpəlɪs / (BrE)
An ancient city in northwestern Turkey, originally a Thracian town, rebuilt and renamed by the Roman Emperor Hadrian, now known as Edirne.

Examples

Adrianopolis ancient city
Hadrian rebuilt Thracian Uskudama and renamed it Adrianopolis in his own honor.
Many architectural remnants from Roman times remain in what was once Adrianopolis.
The city of Adrianopolis played a key role in several historical battles.
