embourgeoisement

noun
becoming middle-class
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑ̃ˌbuʒwɑːzˈmɑ̃ / (AmE), / ɑ̃ˌbuːʒwɑːzˈmɑ̃ /; / ɑ̃ˌbuːʒwɑːzˈmɒ̃ / (BrE)
the process of becoming or turning middle-class, often involving changes in lifestyle, values, or the character of a place

Examples

The embourgeoisement process
The embourgeoisement of the old district led to trendy cafes and higher property values.
Many longtime residents opposed the embourgeoisement that changed the character of their neighborhood.
Rapid embourgeoisement often results in the displacement of lower-income families.
The rapid embourgeoisement of the neighborhood led to new cafes and boutiques opening everywhere.
Some critics argue that embourgeoisement can erode traditional working-class culture.
After years of economic growth, the country experienced widespread embourgeoisement among its population.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decline - a process in which an area becomes poorer or less desirable
  • synonym: gentrification - the process of making a neighborhood more middle-class, often displacing poorer residents
  • antonym: proletarianization - the process of becoming part of the working class
  • synonym: urban renewal - the redevelopment of areas within a city, often leading to changes in social character
