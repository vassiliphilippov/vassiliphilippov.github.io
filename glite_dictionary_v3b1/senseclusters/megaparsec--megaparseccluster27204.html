megaparsec

noun
astronomical distance unit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛɡəˈpɑːrsɛk / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈpɑːsɛk / (BrE)
a unit of distance in astronomy equal to one million parsecs, used to measure vast intergalactic distances

Examples

One megaparsec distance
The nearest galaxy cluster is about 20 megaparsecs away from the Milky Way.
Astronomers use the megaparsec to describe distances between large-scale cosmic structures.
Light from a galaxy 100 megaparsecs distant takes hundreds of millions of years to reach us.
