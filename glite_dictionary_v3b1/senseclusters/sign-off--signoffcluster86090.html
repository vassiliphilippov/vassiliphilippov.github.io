sign-off

noun
end broadcast message
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪn ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈsaɪn ɒf / (BrE)
a message, piece of music, or announcement broadcast at the end of a radio or television program or service

Examples

TV sign-off message
The television station played its traditional sign-off before going off the air for the night.
Listeners recognized the familiar tune used as the radio show's sign-off every evening.
After the sign-off, the screen faded to black until the next morning's broadcast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: closing - the final part of a broadcast or event
  • antonym: introduction - the beginning part of a broadcast or event
