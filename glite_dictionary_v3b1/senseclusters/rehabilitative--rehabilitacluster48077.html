rehabilitative

adjective
helping restore health or normal life
2.5 6.4
Pronunciation: / riˌhæbɪˈleɪtɪv / (AmE), / riːˌhæbɪˈleɪtətɪv /; / riˌhæbɪˈleɪtətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the process or intention of helping someone return to health, normal life, or good condition after illness, injury, addiction, or imprisonment

Examples

A rehabilitative approach
Many experts support a rehabilitative rather than punitive approach to drug addiction.
She underwent rehabilitative therapy after her knee surgery.
The prison introduced several rehabilitative programs to help inmates reintegrate into society.
A rehabilitative approach can help offenders reintegrate into society.
Physiotherapists often design rehabilitative programs tailored to individual needs.
The patient participated in rehabilitative exercises after the knee surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: curative - able to cure disease or restore health
  • synonym: restorative - having the ability to restore health, strength, or well-being
  • synonym: therapeutic - relating to the treatment of illness or injury
