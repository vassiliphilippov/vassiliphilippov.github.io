hydatid disease

noun
disease from tapeworm cysts (Echinococcus)
8.5 6.3
Pronunciation: /haɪ.ˈdæt.ɪd dɪ.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /haɪ.ˈdæt.ɪd dɪ.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
A disease caused by infestation with the larval stage of tapeworms of the genus Echinococcus, forming cysts in body organs.

Examples

Hydatid disease cysts
In many rural areas, hydatid disease remains a significant health concern due to the prevalence of sheep farming.
Surgical removal of the cysts is one treatment for hydatid disease.
The patient was diagnosed with hydatid disease after cysts were found in her liver.

Related Terms

  • synonym: echinococcosis - infection caused by Echinococcus tapeworms
  • degree lower: taeniasis - infection with tapeworms of the genus Taenia
