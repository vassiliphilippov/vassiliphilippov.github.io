microaerophilic

adjective
needs little oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˌɛroʊˈfɪlɪk /; / ˌmaɪkroʊˌɛrəˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˌeərəʊˈfɪlɪk /; / ˌmaɪkrəʊˌeərəˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
requiring or relating to an environment with a low amount of oxygen (used especially for certain bacteria or organisms)

Examples

Microaerophilic bacteria
Certain bacteria are microaerophilic and can only survive in environments with low oxygen levels.
The scientist studied microaerophilic organisms found in deep soil layers.
Some pathogens are microaerophilic, making them difficult to culture in normal laboratory conditions.
Certain bacteria are microaerophilic and can only survive in environments with limited oxygen.
The laboratory used a microaerophilic chamber to culture sensitive microorganisms.
Helicobacter pylori is a well-known microaerophilic bacterium found in the human stomach.

  • degree lower: anaerobic - not requiring oxygen to live or grow
