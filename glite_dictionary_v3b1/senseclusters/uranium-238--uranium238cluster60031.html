the most common isotope of uranium, not directly fissionable but capable of producing fissionable plutonium-239 when irradiated with neutrons

Examples

Natural uranium-238
Natural uranium consists mostly of uranium-238, with only a small fraction of uranium-235.
Nuclear reactors often use uranium-238 as a fertile material to breed more fissile fuel.
When uranium-238 absorbs a neutron, it can be transformed into plutonium-239.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: plutonium-239 - a fissionable isotope produced from uranium-238 when irradiated with neutrons
  • degree lower: uranium-235 - an isotope of uranium that is fissionable and used in nuclear reactors and weapons
