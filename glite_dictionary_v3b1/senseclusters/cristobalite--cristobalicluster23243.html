cristobalite

noun
a white silica mineral found in volcanic rocks
6.9
Pronunciation: / krɪˈstoʊbəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / krɪˈstəʊbəˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a white mineral form of silica, occurring naturally in volcanic rocks

Examples

White cristobalite mineral
Scientists analyzed the formation of cristobalite in the cooling lava samples.
The presence of cristobalite can indicate a rock's exposure to high temperatures.
The volcanic ash contained a high percentage of cristobalite crystals.
