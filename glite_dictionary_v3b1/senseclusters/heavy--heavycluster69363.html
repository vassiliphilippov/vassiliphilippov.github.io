heavy

adjective
weighed down; full of something
1.0 20/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉ›vi / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ›vi / (BrE)
full of or bearing a great weight; carrying or containing a large amount of something

Examples

Heavy with snow
After the rain, the branches were heavy with water droplets.
By autumn, the apple trees were heavy with ripe fruit.
The table was heavy with plates of delicious food.

Synonyms

  • weighty
    4.1 0.43/m
    weighty is formal and used for serious or important things, while heavy is more common in everyday speech.
  • laden
    4.8 5.03/m
    laden appears as heavily loaded, often followed by with to show a large load or burden, while heavy covers general weight.
  • hefty
    3.7 1.18/m
    hefty means large and strong in size and weight, often used for objects or costs, while heavy is more neutral.
  • massive
    3.1 16/m
    massive is very large, solid, and often much bigger than expected, while heavy simply means having great weight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burdened - carrying a burden; loaded
  • antonym: light - not heavy; lacking great weight
