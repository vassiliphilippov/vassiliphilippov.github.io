ply

noun
a layer in combined materials
5.1 0.92/m
Pronunciation: / plaÉª / (AmE), / plaÉª / (BrE)
a layer or strand of material, such as cloth, paper, wood, yarn, or thread

Examples

A ply of wood
The roof was repaired with three plies of waterproof material to prevent leaks.
The thread is a two-ply yarn, making it thicker and more durable than single-ply.
This plywood is made of several plies glued together for extra strength.
She bought some thick four-ply yarn for her knitting project.
The sweater is made from fine two-ply wool, making it both warm and lightweight.
This rope consists of several strong plies twisted together for extra durability.

Synonyms

  • layer
    2.0 41/m
    a layer is a flat sheet or thickness that covers surfaces, while a ply is a single layer or strand used in cloth, wood, or paper.
  • strand
    3.1 6.61/m
    a strand is a thin single fiber or thread, while a ply is often a strand or layer twisted or stacked to make stronger yarn or wood.
