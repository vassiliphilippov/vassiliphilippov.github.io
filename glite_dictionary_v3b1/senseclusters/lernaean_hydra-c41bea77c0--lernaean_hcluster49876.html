Lernaean Hydra

proper noun
many-headed serpent in Greek myth
Pronunciation: / lɜrˈniː.ən ˈhaɪ.drə / (AmE), / lɜːˈniː.ən ˈhaɪ.drə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a serpent-like monster with nine heads; when one head was cut off, two more would grow in its place. The Hydra was ultimately slain by Hercules as one of his twelve labors.

Examples

The Lernaean Hydra monster
In the myth, Hercules fought the Lernaean Hydra as one of his twelve labors.
Some writers use the Lernaean Hydra as a metaphor for persistent challenges.
The Lernaean Hydra symbolizes a problem that becomes worse each time you try to solve it.
