disulfiram

noun
drug for alcoholism treatment
Pronunciation: / daɪˈsʌl.fɪˌræm / (AmE), / dɪˈsʌl.fɪˌræm / (BrE)
a drug used to support the treatment of chronic alcoholism by producing an acute sensitivity to alcohol, often known by the trade name Antabuse

Examples

Disulfiram drug treatment
Developing side effects is a strong sign that disulfiram is interacting with alcohol in the body.
Patients taking disulfiram must avoid all forms of alcohol, even in sauces or medicines.
The doctor prescribed disulfiram to help him stay sober after years of heavy drinking.
