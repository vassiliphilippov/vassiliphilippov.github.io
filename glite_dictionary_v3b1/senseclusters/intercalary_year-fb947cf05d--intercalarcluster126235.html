intercalary year

noun
year with an added day in February

a year in which an extra day is added to February to keep the calendar year synchronized with the astronomical year

Examples

An intercalary year
An intercalary year helps ensure that the seasons match the calendar dates.
The Gregorian calendar includes an intercalary year every four years to account for the extra fraction of a day.
Without the intercalary year, our calendar would gradually drift away from the solar year.
