ectothermic

adjective
animal with variable body temperature
9.4 7.3
Pronunciation: /ek.tə.ˈθɜːr.mɪk/ /; / /ek.toʊ.ˈθɜːr.mɪk/ (AmE), /ek.tə.ˈθɜː.mɪk/ (BrE)
(of an animal) having a body temperature that varies with the temperature of the surroundings, rather than being internally regulated; cold-blooded.

Examples

Ectothermic animals
Because fish are ectothermic, the temperature of their environment is crucial for their survival.
Frogs, being ectothermic, become sluggish when the air temperature drops.
Most reptiles are ectothermic animals and depend on sunlight to regulate their body temperature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cold-blooded - (of animals) having a body temperature that varies with the environment
  • antonym: endothermic - (of animals, especially birds and mammals) maintaining a constant body temperature independent of the environment
  • antonym: warm-blooded - (of animals) maintaining a constant, internally regulated body temperature
