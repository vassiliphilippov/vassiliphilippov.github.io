truthiness

noun
apparent truth
Pronunciation: / ˈtruːθi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtruːθi.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of seeming or feeling true, even if not actually based on fact or reality

Examples

Truthiness of a story
The comedian joked about the truthiness of news stories that sound believable but are not true.
Many people were convinced by the truthiness of the rumor, even though it was false.
Social media can spread truthiness quickly, making it hard to separate fact from fiction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plausibility - the quality of seeming reasonable or probable
  • antonym: veracity - the quality of being true or accurate
