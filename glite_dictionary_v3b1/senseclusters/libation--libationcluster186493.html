libation

noun
act of pouring a drink as an offering
7.4
Pronunciation: / laɪˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / laɪˈbeɪʃ(ə)n /; / laɪˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of pouring a liquid, especially wine, as an offering to a god or in a religious ceremony

Examples

Religious libation ceremony
During the ancient ritual, the priest made a libation of wine to honor the gods.
In Greek mythology, heroes often performed a libation before embarking on their journeys.
The ceremony began with a libation poured onto the ground as an offering to the ancestors.
Ancient Greeks often offered a libation to the gods during their meals.
As part of the ritual, a small libation of wine was poured onto the earth.
The priests performed a libation at the altar before the ceremony began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: offering - something presented in religious worship as a gift to a deity
