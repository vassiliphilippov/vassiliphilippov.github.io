disorientation

noun
a state of confusion about location or direction
5.1 5.4
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ɔːr.ɪ.ən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪs.ɔːr.iː.ən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪs.ɔːr.ɪ.ən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪs.ɔːr.iː.ən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a state of confusion about where you are, who you are, or what is real

Examples

Mental disorientation
After waking from anesthesia, she experienced disorientation and needed help remembering where she was.
The map helped reduce his disorientation in the unfamiliar city.
The sudden loss of power in the tunnel led to momentary disorientation among the passengers.
During the experiment, some participants reported vivid disorientation and altered perceptions of reality.
Prolonged use of the drug can lead to episodes of disorientation characterized by wild delusions.
The patient suffered from disorientation after ingesting the hallucinogenic mushrooms.

  • synonym: hallucination - an experience involving the apparent perception of something not present
