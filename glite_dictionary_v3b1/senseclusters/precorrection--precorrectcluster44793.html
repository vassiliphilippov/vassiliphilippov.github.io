precorrection

noun
advance technical adjustment
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
a technical adjustment made in advance to data, images, or equipment to compensate for expected distortions or errors

Examples

Automatic precorrection of data
The software automatically applies precorrection to compensate for known sensor errors before processing the data.
Engineers performed a precorrection on the equipment to ensure accurate measurements during the experiment.
By using precorrection, the camera reduces image distortion before saving the final photo.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calibration - the process of adjusting equipment to ensure accuracy
  • synonym: correction - an adjustment to fix an error or distortion
