TURP

noun
prostate surgery to treat enlargement
Pronunciation: / tɜrp / (AmE), / tɜːp / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the removal of part of the prostate gland through the urethra, typically to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia

Examples

TURP surgery
TURP is commonly performed to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia in older men.
After undergoing TURP, the patient experienced significant improvement in bladder function.
The doctor recommended TURP to relieve his urinary symptoms.
