take up

verb
absorb or soak up
3.9 2.7
Pronunciation: / teɪk ʌp / (AmE), / teɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to absorb or soak up something, either physically (like a liquid or gas) or metaphorically (like information)

Examples

Take up water
Roots take up water from the soil to nourish the plant.
The cloth quickly took up the spilled wine.
This sponge takes up twice its weight in liquid.
Activated charcoal can take up toxic gases from the air.
Roots take up nutrients and moisture from the soil.
The material takes up water quickly, making it ideal for cleaning spills.
Blotting paper is designed to take up ink spills efficiently.
Charcoal can take up odors from the air.
The new towels quickly take up water after a swim.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - to soak up or take in a substance
  • synonym: adsorb - to accumulate a substance on a surface
  • synonym: draw in - to take in, especially air or a liquid
  • synonym: soak up - to take in a liquid by absorption
