definite

adjective
precise and clearly defined
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛf.ɪ.nət /; / ˈdɛf.ə.nət / (AmE), / ˈdɛf.ɪ.nət / (BrE)
clearly stated or decided; precise and not vague or doubtful

Examples

A definite answer
The manager gave a definite answer to the proposal during the meeting.
There has been a definite improvement in her performance since last month.
We need definite instructions before we can move forward with the project.
He gave a definite answer to the question, leaving no room for doubt.
It is definite that the meeting will take place on Friday.
We need a definite plan before we can proceed further.

Related Terms

  • synonym: certain - known for sure; established beyond doubt
  • synonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion or doubt
  • antonym: indefinite - not clearly expressed or defined; vague
  • synonym: precise - marked by exactness and accuracy of expression or detail
  • synonym: specific - clearly defined or identified
  • antonym: uncertain - not able to be relied on; not known or definite
