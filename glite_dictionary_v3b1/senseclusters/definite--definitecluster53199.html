Synonyms

  • unequivocal
    6.7 1/m
    unequivocal is completely clear and leaves no doubt, while definite is clearly stated or decided and not vague.
  • definitive
    5.6 4/m
    definitive is final and leaves little room for change, while definite means clearly decided but not necessarily final.
  • categoric
    6.4 0.01/m
    categoric is stated without any qualification and sounds absolute, while definite means clearly decided but not always unconditional.
  • explicit
    4.8 7/m
    explicit is said in full and with clear detail, while definite means something is simply clear or decided.
  • clear-cut
    4.2 1/m
    clear-cut is easily seen and obvious at once, while definite means clear or decided but can be less blunt.
  • precise
    3.6 13/m
    precise is exact and correct in every detail, while definite means clearly stated or decided but not always exact.
  • specific
    3.9 70/m
    specific points to one exact person or thing or detail, while definite means clear or decided in a general sense.
