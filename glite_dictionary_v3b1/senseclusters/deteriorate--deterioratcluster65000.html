Examples

To deteriorate quickly
His health began to deteriorate rapidly after the accident.
If we do not repair the roof soon, it will deteriorate further.
Relations between the two countries continue to deteriorate amid ongoing disputes.
Her health continued to deteriorate despite the treatments.
If you don't repair the leak soon, the damage will quickly deteriorate.
Over time, the abandoned building began to deteriorate due to weather and neglect.

Synonyms

  • get worse
    2.5 14/m
    get worse is a common, informal phrase used in everyday speech for becoming more serious, while deteriorate sounds more formal and written.
  • degenerate
    6.3 2.70/m
    degenerate suggests a fall into poorer quality or character over time, while deteriorate is a general loss of condition.
  • decay
    4.5 4.22/m
    decay is a slow breakdown often of material or living things, while deteriorate covers many kinds of becoming worse.
  • devolve
    7.4 1.62/m
    devolve often means to change into a less ordered or worse state, while deteriorate simply means to become worse.
  • erode
    4.2 7.93/m
    erode describes a gradual wearing away, often by forces over time, while deteriorate means to become worse generally.
