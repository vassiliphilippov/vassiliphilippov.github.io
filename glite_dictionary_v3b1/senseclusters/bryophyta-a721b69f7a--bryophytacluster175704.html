Bryophyta

noun
division of nonflowering plants including mosses and liverworts
7.6 9.9
Pronunciation: /braɪ.ə.ˈfaɪ.tə/ (AmE), /braɪ.ə.ˈfaɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a division of nonflowering plants that have rhizoids instead of true roots, lack well-developed vascular tissue, and show alternation of generations, including mosses, liverworts, and hornworts

Examples

Bryophyta moss plants
Bryophyta are distinguished from higher plants by the absence of true roots and vascular tissues.
Scientists classify mosses, liverworts, and hornworts collectively as Bryophyta.
The study of Bryophyta provides important insights into the evolution of land plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Tracheophyta - division of plants with vascular tissues (ferns, conifers, flowering plants)
  • synonym: moss - small flowerless green plant, member of Bryophyta
