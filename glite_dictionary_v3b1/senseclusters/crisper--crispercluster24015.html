crisper

adjective
more firm or fresh
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪspər / (AmE), / ˈkrɪspə / (BrE)
having a firmer, fresher, or more pleasantly sharp texture or quality than something else, especially in food or weather.

Examples

Crisper apples
The apples tasted even crisper after being stored in the fridge overnight.
She prefers her salad greens crisper rather than wilted.
The air felt crisper this morning compared to yesterday.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: crisp - firm, fresh, and pleasantly sharp in texture or quality
  • antonym: soft - not firm or crisp in texture
