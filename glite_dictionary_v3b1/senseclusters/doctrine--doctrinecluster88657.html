doctrine

noun
authoritative belief or set of beliefs
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːk.trɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɒk.trɪn / (BrE)
a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a group, especially a religious or political one

Examples

Religious doctrine
Students were required to study the basic doctrines of the philosophy.
The church formulated a new doctrine to address contemporary issues.
The company's hiring doctrine emphasizes diversity and inclusion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
