petty jury

noun
jury deciding facts in a trial

Pronunciation: /ˈpet.i ˈdʒʊr.i/ /; / /ˈpet.i ˈdʒuːr.i/ (AmE), /ˈpet.i ˈdʒʊə.ri/ /; / /ˈpet.i ˈdʒɔː.ri/ (BrE)
A jury of twelve people who decide the facts and verdict in civil or criminal legal proceedings.

Examples

A petty jury decides
A petty jury is responsible for determining the facts in a criminal trial.

The petty jury deliberated for hours before reaching a verdict.
