AEC

noun
US Atomic Energy Commission, 1946–1974, atomic research agency
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ.iːˈsi / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.iːˈsi / (BrE)
the United States Atomic Energy Commission, a federal agency from 1946 to 1974 responsible for research and development of atomic energy for peacetime uses

Examples

AEC agency
After the AEC was dissolved in 1974, its functions were divided between other government agencies.
Many of the early nuclear power plants in the United States were regulated by the AEC.
The AEC played a pivotal role in shaping America’s nuclear policy during the Cold War era.
