New York aster

noun
North American autumn-blooming perennial herb
Pronunciation: / nuː ˈjɔrk ˈæstər / (AmE), / njuː ˈjɔːk ˈæstə / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb (Symphyotrichum novi-belgii) with small purple, pink, or white flowers that bloom in autumn, widely cultivated and naturalized in Europe.

Examples

The New York aster flower
After transplanting, the New York aster blossomed with delicate purple flowers each fall.
Many gardeners choose the New York aster for its vibrant autumn color and hardiness.
The New York aster thrives in sunny borders and is popular in European parks.
