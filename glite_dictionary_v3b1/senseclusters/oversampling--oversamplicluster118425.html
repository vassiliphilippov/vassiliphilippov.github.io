oversampling

noun
sampling subgroup more
6.8
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈsæm.plɪŋ/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər.sæm.plɪŋ/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈsæm.plɪŋ/ /; / /ˈəʊ.və.sæm.plɪŋ/ (BrE)
the practice of selecting a larger proportion of a specific subgroup in a study sample than in the general population to obtain more reliable data about that group

Examples

Oversampling minority groups
The researchers used oversampling to ensure enough data from minority groups in the survey.
By applying oversampling, the study achieved more accurate results for the targeted population.
Statisticians often recommend oversampling when a subgroup is too small for reliable analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sampling - the process of selecting a subset from a population for study
  • antonym: undersampling - selecting a smaller proportion of a subgroup than exists in the population
