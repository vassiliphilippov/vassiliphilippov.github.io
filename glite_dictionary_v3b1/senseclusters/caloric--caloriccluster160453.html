caloric

noun
obsolete heat substance
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɔrɪk / (AmE), / kəˈlɒrɪk / (BrE)
an old scientific term for the supposed material substance that carried heat

Examples

The caloric in physics
In the 18th century, scientists believed that caloric was a real substance responsible for heat.
The theory of caloric was eventually replaced by the modern understanding of thermodynamics.
Textbooks from the early 1800s often described heat in terms of caloric flow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phlogiston - an obsolete scientific theory of a fire-like element released during combustion
