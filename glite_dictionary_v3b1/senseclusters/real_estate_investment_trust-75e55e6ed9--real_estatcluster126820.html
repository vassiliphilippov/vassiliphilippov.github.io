Real Estate Investment Trust

noun
company investing in income-producing real estate
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈriəl ɛˌsteɪt ɪnˈvɛstmənt trʌst / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl ɪsˌteɪt ɪnˈvɛstmənt trʌst / (BrE)
A company that owns, operates, or finances income-producing real estate, allowing investors to buy shares and receive income from properties.

Examples

Real Estate Investment Trust dividends
Dividends from a Real Estate Investment Trust can provide a steady income stream.
Many investors prefer to buy shares in a Real Estate Investment Trust to diversify their portfolios.
The company converted its property portfolio into a Real Estate Investment Trust to attract more investors.
