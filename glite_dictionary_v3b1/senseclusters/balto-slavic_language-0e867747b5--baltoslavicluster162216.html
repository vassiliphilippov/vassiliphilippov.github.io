Balto-Slavic language

noun
Indo-European language family (Slavic & Baltic)
Pronunciation: / ˌbɔltoʊ ˈslævɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌbɔːltəʊ ˈslævɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A family of Indo-European languages that includes the Slavic and Baltic branches, such as Russian, Polish, and Lithuanian.

Examples

Balto-Slavic language family
Linguists study the similarities between different Balto-Slavic language branches.
Russian and Lithuanian both belong to the Balto-Slavic language family.
The Balto-Slavic language group split into separate Baltic and Slavic languages over time.
