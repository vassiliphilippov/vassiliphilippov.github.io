ridership

noun
number of passengers
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪdərʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈraɪdəʃɪp / (BrE)
the number or group of people who use a public transportation system

Examples

Bus ridership
Bus ridership increased significantly after the new routes were introduced.

During the holiday season, train ridership often reaches its peak.
The city increased bus frequency to boost ridership during rush hour.
After the new subway line opened, ridership grew by nearly twenty percent.
Low ridership led to the cancellation of several late-night train services.
