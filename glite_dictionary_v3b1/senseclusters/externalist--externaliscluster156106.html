externalist

adjective
focused on external factors
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɜrnəlɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəlɪst / (BrE)
relating to the belief that mental content or meaning depends on factors outside the individual, such as environment or social context

Examples

Externalist theory of mind
The philosopher presented an externalist theory of knowledge that emphasized the role of the environment.
Many debates in philosophy of mind contrast externalist and internalist perspectives.
Her externalist approach to meaning highlights the importance of social interaction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: internalist - relating to the belief that mental content depends only on internal factors
