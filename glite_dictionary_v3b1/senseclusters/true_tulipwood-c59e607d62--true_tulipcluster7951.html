true tulipwood

noun
wood from the tulip tree, used in furniture
Pronunciation: / tru ˈtulɪpˌwʊd / (AmE), / truː ˈtjuːlɪpwʊd / (BrE)
The light, easily worked wood of the tulip tree (Liriodendron tulipifera), used especially for furniture and veneers.

Examples

true tulipwood furniture
Cabinetmakers often choose true tulipwood for its workability and attractive grain.
She prefers to use true tulipwood rather than other hardwoods for custom furniture.
The dresser is made from true tulipwood, giving it a lightweight and lustrous finish.
