refortify

verb
to strengthen again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfɔːrtɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɔːtɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something strong or restore its quality again, especially after it has been weakened (such as confidence or nutrients in food)

Examples

To refortify confidence
After losing the first round, the team needed to refortify their confidence before the next match.
The city leaders decided to refortify the old walls after the recent attack.
She took a short break to refortify her determination and continue studying for the exam.
After processing, manufacturers often refortify juices with vitamin C to restore lost nutrients.
The company decided to refortify the bread with extra calcium for health benefits.
Some winemakers refortify their products by adding more alcohol after fermentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fortify - to make something stronger, especially by adding nutrients or substances
  • synonym: reinforce - to make something stronger or more effective
  • antonym: weaken - to make something less strong or effective
