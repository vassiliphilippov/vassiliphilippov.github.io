firepot

noun
container for burning fuel
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.ər.pɑːt/ /; / /ˈfaɪr.pɑːt/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.ə.pɒt/ (BrE)
a container, usually made of metal or earthenware, used to hold burning fuel for heating, cooking, metalworking, or sometimes as a weapon

Examples

A firepot container
The blacksmith heated the metal in a firepot before shaping it.
They cooked their meal over a firepot while camping in the mountains.
A small firepot kept the room warm during the cold winter night.
The soldiers prepared a firepot to launch over the fortress walls during the night attack.
Archaeologists discovered a broken firepot among the ruins of the ancient city.
A single firepot could ignite large sections of a wooden palisade in medieval warfare.
