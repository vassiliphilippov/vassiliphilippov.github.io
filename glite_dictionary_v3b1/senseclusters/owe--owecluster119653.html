owe

verb
be indebted for ideas or influence
2.2 3.0
Pronunciation: / oʊ / (AmE), / əʊ / (BrE)
to have something, such as success, knowledge, or an idea, because of another person or thing; to be indebted to in an abstract sense

Examples

Owe success to hard work
Modern physics owes a great deal to Einstein's theories.
She owes much of her success to her mentor's guidance and support.
The company owes its reputation to years of dedicated service.
