dragonfish

noun
deep-sea fish
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡənˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdræɡənˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a type of fish with a long body and fierce appearance, found in deep or tropical waters

Examples

Deep-sea dragonfish
The biologist studied the unique features of the dragonfish collected from the ocean depths.
A dragonfish can produce light to attract prey in the darkness of deep waters.
Children were fascinated by the fierce-looking dragonfish at the marine exhibit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anglerfish - a deep-sea fish known for its bioluminescent lure
  • synonym: viperfish - a deep-sea fish with long teeth and a slender body
