DEAE cellulose

noun
cellulose used as a chromatography medium
Pronunciation: / ˌdiː.iː.eɪ ˈsɛl.jəˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˌdiː.iː.eɪ ˈsɛl.jʊˌləʊs / (BrE)
a form of cellulose modified with diethylaminoethyl groups, used as an ion-exchange medium in chromatography

Examples

DEAE cellulose resin
He replaced the standard resin with DEAE cellulose to improve the chromatography results.
Researchers often prefer DEAE cellulose when purifying nucleic acids.
The protein sample was loaded onto the DEAE cellulose column for separation.
