victim

noun
person harmed by accident, crime, or misfortune
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪktəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪm / (BrE)
a person who suffers harm, injury, or loss, for example because of an accident, crime, or being tricked

Examples

A victim of crime
He became a victim of identity theft after his wallet was stolen.
Many victims of the flood received help from local charities.
The earthquake left hundreds of victims in need of urgent medical care.
She felt foolish after realizing she was just another victim of the phishing email.
The con artist's latest victim lost thousands of dollars in the scheme.
The scam targeted elderly people and made each victim believe they had won a prize.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dupe - a person who is easily deceived or tricked
  • antonym: survivor - a person who continues to live after hardship or danger
  • antonym: swindler - a person who cheats or defrauds others
