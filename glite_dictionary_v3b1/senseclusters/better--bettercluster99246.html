better

adjective
in improved health or condition
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛtər /; / ˈbɛt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɛtə / (BrE)
of a higher quality, more satisfactory, or more advisable than something else; improved or preferable

Examples

Feel better
After resting for a few days, she felt much better.
He asked if I was better after the surgery.
My cold is getting better, so I’ll be back at work tomorrow.
She got a better grade on her final exam than on her midterm.
Some people believe electric cars are better for the environment.
This is a better solution than any we've tried so far.
It would be better to wait until morning before making a decision.
It's always better to ask for help if you're unsure.
You know better than to leave your keys in the car.
