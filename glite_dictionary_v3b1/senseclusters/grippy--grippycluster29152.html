grippy

adjective
prevents slipping
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɪpi / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪpi / (BrE)
having a surface or texture that prevents slipping or provides good grip or traction

Examples

Grippy shoes
The new running shoes have a grippy sole that works well on wet pavement.
I prefer using grippy gloves when I ride my bike in the rain.
The yoga mat is very grippy, so it stays in place during difficult poses.
The new tires are much more grippy on wet roads than the old ones.
She bought a pair of grippy gloves for rock climbing.
This yoga mat is especially grippy, so you won't slip during your workout.

Related Terms

  • antonym: slippery - difficult to hold or stand on because it is smooth or wet
  • synonym: sticky - having a surface that adheres to things easily
