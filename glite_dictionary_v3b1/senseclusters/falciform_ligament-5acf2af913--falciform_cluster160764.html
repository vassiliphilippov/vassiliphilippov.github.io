falciform ligament

noun
ligament connecting liver to diaphragm and abdomen
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfæl.sɪ.fɑːrm ˈlɪg.ə.mənt/ /; / /ˈfæl.sɪ.fɔːrm ˈlɪg.ə.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈfæl.sɪ.fɔːm ˈlɪg.ə.mənt/ /; / /ˈfɒl.sɪ.fɔːm ˈlɪg.ə.mənt/ (BrE)
anatomy: a thin, sickle-shaped ligament that attaches part of the liver to the diaphragm and the anterior abdominal wall.

Examples

The falciform ligament
In human anatomy, the falciform ligament separates the left and right lobes of the liver.
