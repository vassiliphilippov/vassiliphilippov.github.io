objective lens

noun
lens closest to the object in microscopy or telescopy
7.8 3.2
Pronunciation: /əb.ˈdʒek.tɪv lenz/ (AmE), /ɒb.ˈdʒek.tɪv lenz/ /; / /əb.ˈdʒek.tɪv lenz/ (BrE)
the lens or system of lenses in a microscope or telescope that is closest to the object being viewed and forms the primary image

Examples

The objective lens system
Careful cleaning of the objective lens is essential for clear and accurate images.
The objective lens of the microscope provides the initial magnification of the specimen.
The quality of the objective lens directly affects the resolving power of the telescope.
