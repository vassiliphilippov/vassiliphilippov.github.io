chessboard

noun
board for playing chess
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛsˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛsˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A square board divided into 64 alternating light and dark squares, used for playing chess.

Examples

A chessboard game
During the tournament, every table was set with a clean chessboard and a set of pieces.
He carefully placed each piece in its starting position on the chessboard.
The wooden chessboard had beautifully carved squares and edges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: checkerboard - a board with a checked pattern; used for draughts and chess
