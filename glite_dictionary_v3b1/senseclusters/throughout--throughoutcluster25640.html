throughout

preposition
in every part; during all
1.0 134/m
Pronunciation: / Œ∏ruÀêÀàa ät / (AmE), / Œ∏ruÀêÀàa ät / (BrE)
in every part of a place or during the whole of a period of time

Examples

Rain throughout the city
The tradition is observed throughout the country every spring.
She felt nervous throughout the entire interview process.
The museum remained open throughout the renovation period.

Synonyms

  • over
    1.0 178/m
    over can mean during a whole time or across an area, while throughout stresses every part of a place or the entire period.
  • all the while
    2.9 3.36/m
    all the while stresses the whole time of an event, while throughout can mean in every part of a place or during the whole period.
  • all over
    1.0 68/m
    all over means in every place across an area, while throughout also covers during the whole time as well as every part of a place.
  • around
    1.0 18/m
    around is used for general times or places and often sounds informal and approximate, while throughout means every part or the whole period.
