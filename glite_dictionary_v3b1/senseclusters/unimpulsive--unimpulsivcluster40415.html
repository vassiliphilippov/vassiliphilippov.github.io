unimpulsive

adjective
not sudden or abrupt
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
not produced by a short, sudden force or impulse; gradual or continuous rather than sudden, especially in physics or engineering.

Examples

Unimpulsive movement
The engineer designed an unimpulsive system to ensure smooth and continuous movement.
In this experiment, the force applied was unimpulsive, resulting in a steady acceleration.
The spacecraft's unimpulsive maneuver allowed for precise adjustments without sudden changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gradual - happening slowly over time
  • antonym: impulsive - produced by a sudden force or impulse
