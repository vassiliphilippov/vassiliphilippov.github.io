capybara

noun
largest South American rodent
Pronunciation: / ˌkæp.iˈbɑ.rə / (AmE), / ˌkæp.ɪˈbɑː.rə / (BrE)
A large, pig-sized, tailless South American rodent with partly webbed feet that lives near water; the largest living rodent.

Examples

A capybara near water
A group of capybaras lounged together on the riverbank, barely disturbed by passing boats.
Despite its size, the capybara is gentle and social, living peacefully with other animals.
The capybara can often be seen basking in the sun near rivers in South America.
