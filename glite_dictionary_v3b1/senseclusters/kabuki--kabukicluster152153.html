kabuki

adjective
theatrical or staged
8.8
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈbuː.kiː/ /; / /kə.ˈbuː.kɪ/ (AmE), /kə.ˈbuː.kiː/ /; / /kə.ˈbuː.kɪ/ (BrE)
showy or theatrical in a way that suggests performance rather than genuine feeling or intention, especially in politics or public life

Examples

Kabuki style speech
The press conference was pure kabuki, with officials pretending to address the issue but offering no real solutions.
Many voters saw the debate as kabuki rather than a genuine discussion of policies.
Critics described the negotiations as kabuki, saying the outcome was decided in advance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genuine - real and sincere, not performed or fake
  • synonym: theatrical - exaggerated and dramatic, like a performance
