proper

adjective
suitable or appropriate
2.2 42/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀêp…ôr / (AmE), / Ààpr…íp…ô / (BrE)
suitable, appropriate, or correct for a particular purpose, situation, or according to social or moral standards

Examples

Proper behavior
He waited for the proper moment to ask his question.
It is important to wear proper safety equipment when working with chemicals.
Make sure you address the letter to the proper department to avoid delays.
It is important to wash your hands before eating to maintain proper hygiene.
They received proper training before starting their new jobs.
Wearing a suit and tie is considered proper attire for the ceremony.

Synonyms

  • appropriate
    3.5 109/m
    appropriate is chosen for a situation or event, while proper also carries social or moral expectations about correct behaviour.
  • suitable
    1.8 20/m
    suitable fits a person, purpose, or condition, while proper adds ideas of social rules and correct conduct in that situation.
  • correct
    1.0 18/m
    correct means accurate or free from error, while proper focuses more on what fits a situation and on social standards.
  • right
    1.0 601/m
    right often means morally or factually correct and a strong choice, while proper stresses what suits a situation or social rule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appropriate - suitable or fitting for a particular purpose
  • antonym: improper - not suitable or appropriate; not correct
  • antonym: inappropriate - not suitable or fitting for a particular situation or purpose
  • synonym: suitable - appropriate for a particular person, purpose, or situation
