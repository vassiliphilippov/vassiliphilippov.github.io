seismicity

noun
earthquake activity
Pronunciation: / saɪzˈmɪsəti / (AmE), / saɪzˈmɪsəti / (BrE)
the frequency, type, and size of earthquakes occurring in a specific area over a period of time

Examples

Seismicity in an area
Scientists monitor seismicity to assess earthquake risks in the region.
The seismicity of the area increased after the recent volcanic eruption.
Maps showing regional seismicity help engineers design safer buildings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earthquake - a sudden shaking of the ground caused by movement of the Earth's crust
  • synonym: volcanism - the processes and phenomena associated with volcanic activity
