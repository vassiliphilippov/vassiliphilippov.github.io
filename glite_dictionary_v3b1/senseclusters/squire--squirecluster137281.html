squire

verb
act as a squire; escort or attend someone
Pronunciation: / ˈskwaɪər / (AmE), / ˈskwaɪə / (BrE)
to attend to someone as a squire; to accompany or escort, especially in a courteous or attentive manner

Examples

To squire a guest
During the ceremony, young assistants squired the guests to their seats.
He offered to squire her to the dance, making sure she arrived safely.
She was squired around the city by her attentive host.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accompany - to go somewhere with someone as a companion
  • synonym: escort - to accompany someone as a protector or guide
