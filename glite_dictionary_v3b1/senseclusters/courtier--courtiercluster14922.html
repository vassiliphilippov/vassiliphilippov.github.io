courtier

noun
attendant at a royal court
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrtiər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtiə / (BrE)
a person who attends the court of a sovereign or other important person, often as an adviser or companion

Examples

A courtier at court
A clever courtier could quickly rise in influence through wit and charm.
At Versailles, every courtier was eager to gain the queen's favor.
The king surrounded himself with trusted courtiers who offered him advice on every matter.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: nobleman - a man of noble rank; aristocrat
  • degree lower: servant - a person who performs duties for others, typically domestic tasks
