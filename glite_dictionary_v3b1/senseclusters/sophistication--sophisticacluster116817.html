sophistication

noun
misleading by deceptive reasoning
Pronunciation: / səˌfɪstəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌfɪstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of misleading or falsifying by means of sophistry or deceptive reasoning.

Examples

Debate sophistication
Academic debates can sometimes descend into sophistication that obscures the real issues.
He was skilled in the sophistication of arguments to win any side.
The lawyer's sophistication of the facts confused the jury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone
  • antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being truthful
