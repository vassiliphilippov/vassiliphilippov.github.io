electroscope

noun
instrument for detecting electric charge
2.2 7.6
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlek.trə.skoʊ/ /; / /ə.ˈlek.trə.skoʊ/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlek.trə.skəʊ/ /; / /ə.ˈlek.trə.skəʊ/ (BrE)
an instrument used for detecting the presence and magnitude of electric charge, often by the divergence of gold leaves due to electrical repulsion

Examples

A electroscope instrument
A simple electroscope can show whether an object is charged by the movement of its gold leaves.
The scientist used an electroscope to demonstrate static electricity to the students.
When the rod touched the electroscope, the leaves immediately spread apart.
