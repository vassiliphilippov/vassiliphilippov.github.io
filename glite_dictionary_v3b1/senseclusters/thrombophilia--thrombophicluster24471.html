thrombophilia

noun
tendency to form clots
Pronunciation: / ˌθrɑːmboʊˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˌθrɒmbəʊˈfɪliə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the blood has an increased tendency to form clots

Examples

Thrombophilia blood condition
People with thrombophilia may need special care during surgery.
A family history of thrombophilia can increase your risk of blood clots.
Doctors often test for thrombophilia in patients with unexplained clotting problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hemophilia - a medical condition where the blood does not clot easily
  • synonym: hypercoagulability - an increased tendency of the blood to clot
