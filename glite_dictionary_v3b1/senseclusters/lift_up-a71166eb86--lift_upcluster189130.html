Synonyms

  • lift
    1.0 30/m
    lift is to raise something using hands or a machine, while lift up is the everyday phrasal verb for this action.
  • raise up
    2.1 0.3/m
    raise up means to move something to a higher level, while lift up is more neutral and used in everyday speech.
  • elevate
    3.9 6/m
    elevate means to raise something to a higher level in formal or technical speech, while lift up is more common in everyday talk.
  • hoist
    4.7 4/m
    hoist means to raise something with ropes or machines, while lift up is a general verb for raising by hand or tool.
  • pick up
    1.0 40/m
    pick up means to lift something from a surface with your hands, while lift up can describe any way of raising an object.
  • take up
    2.0 3/m
    take up means to scoop or pick something up, while lift up is a broader verb for raising by many methods or people.
  • push up
    2.1 0.5/m
    push up means to move something higher by pushing from below, while lift up says nothing about the method used to raise it.
  • boost
    2.5 1.0/m
    boost means to help or push something higher from below, often in support or speed, while lift up only describes the physical raising.
  • heave up
    4.5 0.1/m
    heave up means to lift something with strong effort, often by many people, while lift up covers any raising, with less force implied.
  • heft up
    6.1 0.01/m
    heft up means to lift or test the weight of something with effort, while lift up simply names the act of moving something higher.
