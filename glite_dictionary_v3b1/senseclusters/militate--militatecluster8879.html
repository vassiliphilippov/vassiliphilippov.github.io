militate

verb
to have force or influence; affect strongly
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪteɪt / (BrE)
(usually followed by 'against' or 'in favor of') to have a strong effect or influence in preventing or supporting something.

Examples

To militate against change
Her experience and skills militate in her favor during the interview process.
Strict regulations often militate against quick decision-making in large organizations.
The lack of funds will militate against the success of the project.
