amino group

noun
chemical group –NH₂

Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmiː.noʊ gruːp/ /; / /ə.ˈmaɪ.noʊ gruːp/ (AmE), /ə.ˈmiː.nəʊ gruːp/ /; / /ə.ˈmaɪ.nəʊ gruːp/ (BrE)
A functional group in organic chemistry with the formula –NH₂, consisting of a nitrogen atom attached to two hydrogen atoms.

Examples

An amino group in chemistry
Many drugs are modified by adding an amino group to their structure.
The amino group is essential for the formation of amino acids.
