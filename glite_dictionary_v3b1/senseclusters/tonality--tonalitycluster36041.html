tonality

noun
quality of sound
Pronunciation: / toʊˈnæləti / (AmE), / təʊˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the character or quality of musical or spoken sounds, especially in terms of mood, feeling, or pitch

Examples

Warm tonality
The tonality of the speech made the audience feel calm and reassured.
Classical composers often experiment with tonality to create different emotional effects.
She admired the warm tonality of the singer's voice throughout the performance.
The tonality of the music shifted from bright and cheerful to dark and somber.
She admired the warm tonality of the singer's voice during the concert.
Different languages can have unique tonality that affects how words are pronounced.

Related Terms

  • antonym: atonality - music that lacks a key or tonal center
  • synonym: tone - the general character or attitude of a sound or piece of music
