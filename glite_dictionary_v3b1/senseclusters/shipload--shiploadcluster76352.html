shipload

noun
amount a ship can carry
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪpˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈʃɪp.ləʊd /; / ˈʃɪpləʊd / (BrE)
an amount of cargo or people that fills a ship

Examples

A shipload of grain
A single shipload of grain was enough to feed the entire town for a week.
After the storm, they found a shipload of timber washed up on the shore.
The company exported a shipload of electronics to Europe last month.
The port received a shipload of electronics from overseas this morning.
They exported a shipload of wheat to Europe last season.
A single shipload of passengers arrived on the island for the festival.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boatload - an amount of cargo that can be carried by a single boat
  • synonym: truckload - an amount that fills a truck
