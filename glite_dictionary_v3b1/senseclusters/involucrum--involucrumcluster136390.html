involucrum

noun
ring of bracts
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnvəˈluːkrəm / (AmE), / ˌɪnvɒˈljuːkrəm / (BrE)
a whorl or cluster of bracts that surrounds a single flower or a group of flowers in some plants

Examples

Flower involucrum
The involucrum of the sunflower is made up of several green bracts.
Botanists examined the involucrum to identify the species of the plant.
Each flower in the cluster was surrounded by a protective involucrum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bract - a modified leaf associated with a flower or inflorescence
