proletariat

noun
working class; wage laborers
5.7 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊ.lɪˈtɛr.i.ət /; / ˌproʊ.ləˈtɛr.i.ət / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.lɪˈteə.ri.ət /; / ˌprəʊ.lɪˈtɛə.ri.ət / (BrE)
the social class of people who work for wages and do not own property or capital, especially industrial workers (often used in Marxist theory)

Examples

The proletariat class
Many factory owners exploited the proletariat during the Industrial Revolution.
The novel depicts the daily struggles faced by the urban proletariat.
The rise of the proletariat shaped much of nineteenth-century European history.
As factory jobs grew, the proletariat became a larger part of society.
Marx believed that the proletariat would eventually rise against the bourgeoisie.
The proletariat played a central role in the revolution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bourgeoisie - the middle/upper class, especially as the capitalist class in Marxist theory
  • synonym: working class - the social group consisting of people who are employed for wages, especially in manual or industrial work
