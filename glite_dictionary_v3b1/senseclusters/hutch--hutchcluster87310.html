hutch

noun
small crude dwelling shelter
Pronunciation: / hʌtʃ / (AmE), / hʌtʃ / (BrE)
a small, crude shelter used as a dwelling, often temporary and makeshift

Examples

Wooden hutch shelter
Children pretended their playhouse was a hutch hidden deep in the forest.
He spent several nights in a hutch by the river after losing his home.
The refugees built a hutch from scraps of wood and metal.

Related Terms
