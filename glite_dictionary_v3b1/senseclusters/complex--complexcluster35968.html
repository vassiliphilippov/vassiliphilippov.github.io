complex

noun
chemical compound with central atom and ligands
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːm.pleks/ /; / /kəm.ˈpleks/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.pleks/ /; / /kəm.ˈpleks/ (BrE)
(Chemistry) A chemical compound in which a central atom, usually a metal, is bonded to a surrounding group of molecules or ions.

Examples

A metal complex
Many transition metals form colored complexes with organic compounds.
The scientist synthesized a nickel complex with four ammonia ligands.
The stability of a coordination complex depends on several factors.
