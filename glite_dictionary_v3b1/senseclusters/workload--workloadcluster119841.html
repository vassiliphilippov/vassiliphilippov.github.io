workload

noun
amount of tasks for a computer/system
3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈwɜːrk.loʊd/ (AmE), /ˈwɜːk.ləʊd/ (BrE)
(in computing) the total volume and mix of processing tasks or requests assigned to a computer system, server, or component, considered for performance measurement, capacity planning, or scheduling.

Examples

Server workload
The cloud service monitors its current workload to adjust CPU and memory allocation automatically.
During the stress test, engineers simulated a heavy workload of mixed database and web requests.
Developers changed the scheduler to prioritize latency-sensitive tasks when the workload spikes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resource utilization - the degree to which computing resources (CPU, memory, I/O) are used by running tasks
  • synonym: system load - the amount of computational work or processes a computer or server is handling
