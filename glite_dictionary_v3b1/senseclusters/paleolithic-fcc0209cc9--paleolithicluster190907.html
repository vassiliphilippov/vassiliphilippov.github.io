Paleolithic

noun
early Stone Age period
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌpaliəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
The early period of the Stone Age, lasting from about 2.5 million years ago to around 8,500 BC, characterized by the development of the first stone tools.

Examples

The Paleolithic period
During the Paleolithic, humans survived as hunter-gatherers.
Researchers discovered cave paintings dating back to the Paleolithic period.
The Paleolithic era marked the beginning of human tool use.
Archaeologists discovered Palaeolithic tools in the cave, revealing details about early human life.
Art from the Palaeolithic era, such as cave paintings, provides insight into prehistoric cultures.
The Palaeolithic period is marked by the use of simple stone implements and hunter-gatherer societies.
