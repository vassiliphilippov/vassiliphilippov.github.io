grand piano

noun
large, horizontal concert piano
Pronunciation: / ɡrænd piˈænoʊ / (AmE), / ɡrænd piˈænəʊ / (BrE)
a large, wing-shaped piano with a horizontal frame and strings, typically supported by three legs and known for its rich sound

Examples

A grand piano sound
Many professional pianists prefer to practice on a grand piano for its superior sound quality.
She inherited a beautiful grand piano from her grandmother.
The concert featured a Steinway grand piano at center stage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: upright piano - a piano with a vertical frame and strings
