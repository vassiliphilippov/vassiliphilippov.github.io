ischemic stroke

noun
stroke caused by blocked blood flow to the brain
8.7 4.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskimɪk stroʊk /; / ɪˈskiəmɪk ˈstroʊk / (AmE), / ɪˈskɛmɪk strəʊk /; / ɪˈsiːəmɪk ˈstrəʊk / (BrE)
a type of stroke caused by a blockage or interruption in the blood supply to the brain, usually due to a clot blocking a blood vessel, resulting in reduced blood flow and potential brain damage.

Examples

An ischemic stroke event
Doctors explained that controlling high blood pressure can help reduce the risk of an ischemic stroke.
Immediate treatment is crucial to minimize brain damage during an ischemic stroke.
The patient was diagnosed with an ischemic stroke after experiencing sudden weakness on one side of his body.
Quick treatment is crucial in cases of ischaemic stroke to restore blood flow and prevent brain damage.
The patient was diagnosed with an ischaemic stroke after doctors found a blood clot blocking an artery in the brain.
Unlike a hemorrhagic stroke, an ischaemic stroke is typically caused by a blockage rather than bleeding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hemorrhagic stroke - a type of stroke caused by bleeding in the brain
