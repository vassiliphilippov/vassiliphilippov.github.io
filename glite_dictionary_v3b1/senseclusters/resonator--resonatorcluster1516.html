resonator

noun
circuit that maximizes electric oscillations
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛzəˌneɪtər /; / ˈrɛzəˌneɪtəɹ / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəneɪtə / (BrE)
a device or system (such as an electrical circuit or hollow chamber) that naturally vibrates or resonates at certain frequencies, often used to amplify or filter sound, electrical, or electromagnetic waves

Examples

An electrical resonator
At certain frequencies, the resonator amplifies electrical oscillations.
Engineers adjusted the resonator circuit to achieve maximum signal strength.
The radio uses a resonator to select the desired frequency.
In microwave technology, a cavity resonator is essential for selecting certain frequencies.
Many musical instruments include a resonator to enrich their tonal quality.
The guitar body acts as a resonator, amplifying the sound of the vibrating strings.
He tuned the resonator to match the frequency of the incoming sound waves.
Microwave ovens often use a cavity resonator to contain the energy.
The violin's sound is amplified by its wooden resonator.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dampener - device that reduces or stops resonance
  • synonym: oscillator - electrical circuit that produces a repeating signal
