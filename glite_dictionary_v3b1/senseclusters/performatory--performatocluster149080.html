performatory

adjective
causing action by speech
9.9
Pronunciation: / pərˈfɔːrməˌtɔːri / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːmətəri / (BrE)
having the power to produce an action or legal effect simply by being spoken or declared

Examples

Performatory words in court
The judge's performatory statement made the verdict official in the eyes of the law.
When the president made a performatory declaration, it immediately changed the status of the territory.
Her words were not just symbolic; they were performatory and had real consequences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: performative - relating to an utterance that performs an action by being spoken
