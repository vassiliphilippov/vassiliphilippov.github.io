quicksand

noun
dangerous, inescapable situation
9.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkwɪk.sænd/ (AmE), /ˈkwɪk.sænd/ (BrE)
A dangerous or difficult situation from which it is hard to escape.

Examples

Quicksand of trouble
Getting involved in their dispute was like stepping into quicksand; the more I tried to help, the worse it became.
He found himself in political quicksand after making that controversial statement.
The company is trapped in financial quicksand and can't find a way out.
