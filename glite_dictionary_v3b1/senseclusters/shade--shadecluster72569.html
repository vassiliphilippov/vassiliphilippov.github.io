shade

verb
protect from light, heat, or view
2.5 1.0
Pronunciation: /ʃeɪd/ (AmE), /ʃeɪd/ (BrE)
to protect or block something from light, heat, or view by covering or casting a shadow

Examples

To shade your eyes
He used his hand to shade his face from the camera.
The tall trees shade the patio in the afternoon, keeping it cool.
You should shade your eyes when you look toward the sun.
Large clouds shaded the field from the afternoon sun.
She put up an umbrella to shade her face while reading outside.
The tree shades the bench, making it a cool place to sit on hot days.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expose - to leave unprotected or uncovered
  • synonym: shadow - to cast a shadow on something
  • synonym: shield - to protect from harm or danger
