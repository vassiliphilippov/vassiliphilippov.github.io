to make something into a mongrel, typically by crossbreeding or mixing breeds or types, often implying loss of purity

Examples

To mongrelize a breed
Critics argue that careless crossbreeding can mongrelize distinct species.
Some breeders refuse to mongrelize their purebred dogs.
The garden variety was mongrelized by mixing imported plants with local ones.
Some animal rights activists argue that to mongrelise a rare species endangers its unique traits.
The breeder refused to mongrelise the pedigree dogs by allowing indiscriminate mating.
They worried that crossbreeding might mongrelise the traditional cattle stock.

Synonyms

  • crossbreed
    4.6 0.18/m
    crossbreed means to produce offspring from different breeds to combine traits, while mongrelize suggests mixing breeds with loss of purity.
  • hybridize
    7.0 0.56/m
    hybridize means to cross different species or varieties to make hybrids, while mongrelize focuses on mixing that lowers purity.
  • cross
    3.9 1.37/m
    cross means to breed two different varieties or species, while mongrelize often carries a negative sense of purity loss.
  • cross-fertilize
    7.2 0.00/m
    cross-fertilize means to mix genetic material between individuals, while mongrelize stresses making hybrids and reducing original purity.
