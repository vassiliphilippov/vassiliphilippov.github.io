montmorillonite

noun
swelling clay mineral
Pronunciation: / mɑntˈmɔrɪlɪˌnaɪt / (AmE), / mɒntˈmɒrɪlɪˌnaɪt / (BrE)
a soft, swelling clay mineral used in industry and science, especially for its ability to absorb water

Examples

Montmorillonite clay mineral
The geologist identified montmorillonite as the main component of the clay sample.
Because of its swelling properties, montmorillonite is often used in drilling muds.
Researchers studied how montmorillonite absorbs water and expands in different environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bentonite - a type of absorbent clay formed from volcanic ash
  • antonym: kaolinite - a soft white clay mineral used in ceramics and paper
