perniciously

adverb
in a harmfully insidious way
9.9
Pronunciation: / pərˈnɪʃəsli / (AmE), / pəˈnɪʃəsli / (BrE)
in a way that causes harm or damage, especially gradually or subtly

Examples

Changes perniciously affect
Small but persistent policy changes altered the institution perniciously over decades.
The disease advanced perniciously, making early detection difficult for doctors.
The misinformation spread perniciously through social media, influencing many people without their notice.
Corruption has perniciously affected the effectiveness of the institution.
Misinformation can perniciously influence public opinion over time.
The rumor spread perniciously through the office, undermining morale.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benignly - in a gentle or harmless way
  • synonym: harmfully - in a harmful way
  • synonym: insidiously - in a gradual, subtle, but harmful manner
