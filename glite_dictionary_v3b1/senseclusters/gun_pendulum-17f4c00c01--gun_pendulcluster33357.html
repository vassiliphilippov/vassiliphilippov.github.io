gun pendulum

noun
ballistics device for measuring gun recoil
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡʌn ˈpɛn.dʒə.ləm / (AmE), / ɡʌn ˈpɛn.djʊ.ləm / (BrE)
a device consisting of a suspended gun used to measure the velocity of a projectile by analyzing the recoil when the gun is fired.

Examples

A gun pendulum device
After firing, the recoil motion of the gun pendulum was carefully measured to determine velocity.
Scientists used a gun pendulum to accurately calculate the speed of the bullet fired.
The gun pendulum was instrumental in 19th-century experiments on firearm ballistics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ballistic pendulum - a device for measuring projectile velocity using the movement of a suspended target
