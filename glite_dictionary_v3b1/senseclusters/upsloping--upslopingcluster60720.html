upsloping

adjective
rising in pitch
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsloʊpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌsləʊpɪŋ / (BrE)
rising in pitch or tone toward the end, especially in speech or intonation

Examples

Upsloping intonation
The teacher noticed an upsloping intonation at the end of his question.
In some languages, an upsloping tone can indicate uncertainty or a question.
Her voice became upsloping as she asked for clarification during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: falling - decreasing in pitch or going downward
  • synonym: rising - increasing in pitch or going upward
