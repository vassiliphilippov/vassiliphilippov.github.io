Pythagoreans

proper noun
followers of Pythagoras
9.9
Pronunciation: / paɪˌθæɡəˈriːənz / (AmE), / paɪˌθæɡəˈriːənz / (BrE)
members of an ancient Greek philosophical and religious group founded by Pythagoras

Examples

The Pythagoreans group
The Pythagoreans believed that numbers were the foundation of the universe.
Many mathematical discoveries are attributed to the Pythagoreans and their studies.
The Pythagoreans combined philosophy, mathematics, and religious beliefs in their teachings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pythagorean - a follower of Pythagoras or relating to his teachings
  • antonym: Stoics - members of an ancient Greek school of philosophy emphasizing self-control
