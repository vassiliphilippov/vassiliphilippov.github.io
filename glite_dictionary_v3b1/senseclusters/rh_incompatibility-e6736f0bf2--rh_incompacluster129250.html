Rh incompatibility

noun
blood-type incompatibility (Rh factor)
5.5 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.eɪtʃ ɪn.kəm.pə.ˈtɪb.əl.ɪ.ti/ /; / /ˈɑːr.eɪtʃ ɪn.kən.pə.ˈtɪb.əl.ɪ.ti/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.eɪtʃ ɪn.kəm.pə.ˈtɪb.əl.ɪ.ti/ (BrE)
A condition in which a difference between the Rh blood types of two individuals, such as mother and fetus or blood donor and recipient, can cause harmful immune reactions.

Examples

Rh incompatibility in pregnancy
Blood transfusions carry risks, especially if Rh incompatibility is not properly tested for.
Doctors closely monitored her pregnancy for signs of Rh incompatibility between her and the baby.
Severe anemia in newborns can result from untreated Rh incompatibility.
