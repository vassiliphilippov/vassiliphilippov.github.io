caddisfly

noun
fishing fly lure
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkædɪsˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkædɪsˌflaɪ / (BrE)
an artificial fishing fly designed to imitate an adult caddisfly, used as a lure in fly fishing

Examples

A caddisfly lure
The angler tied a new caddisfly to his line before casting into the stream.
Trout often rise to take a well-presented caddisfly during the evening hatch.
She learned to tie a realistic caddisfly for her next fly fishing trip.

  • synonym: nymph - an artificial fishing fly imitating the immature stage of aquatic insects
