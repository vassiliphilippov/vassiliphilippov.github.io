omertà

noun
criminal code of silence
Pronunciation: / oʊmɛrˈtɑ /; / oʊˌmɛrˈtɑ / (AmE), / əʊmɛəˈtɑː /; / əʊˌmɛəˈtɑː / (BrE)
a code of silence that prevents people from speaking out about wrongdoing, especially in criminal organizations or groups

Examples

Omertà code
The members of the gang followed omertà and refused to cooperate with the police.
Breaking omertà can have serious consequences in criminal organizations.
Because of omertà, investigators struggled to find anyone willing to testify.
The community's strong sense of omertà made it difficult for police to gather evidence.
Breaking the omertà was seen as a betrayal by the group.
A culture of omertà at the company kept employees from reporting safety violations to regulators.

  • synonym: secrecy - the action of keeping something secret or hidden
  • synonym: silence - the state of not speaking or making noise
