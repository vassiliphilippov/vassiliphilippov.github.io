ground

noun
position or advantage in conflict or competition
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
A position or advantage to be won or defended in a conflict, competition, or debate.

Examples

Lose ground
After a slow start, the team regained lost ground with a strong second half.
Negotiators refused to give up any ground during the talks.
The company managed to gain ground on its competitors after releasing its new product.
