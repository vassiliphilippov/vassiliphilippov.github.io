duffer

noun
incompetent or clumsy person
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌfər / (AmE), / ˈdʌfə / (BrE)
A person who is incompetent or clumsy, especially in a sport or other activity.

Examples

A duffer in tennis
Although he tried his best, Mark was a complete duffer on the tennis court.
I must admit I’m a bit of a duffer when it comes to fixing things around the house.
The chess club welcomed everyone, from seasoned champions to total duffers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill
  • synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
