larboard

adjective
left side of a ship (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːrbɔːrd / (AmE), / ˈlɑːbəd / (BrE)
(archaic) Relating to or located on the left side of a ship or aircraft when facing forward; port side.

Examples

Larboard windows
During the storm, waves crashed against the larboard windows of the galley.
Old nautical charts label the larboard side for clarity in navigation instructions.
The captain ordered the crew to turn the ship to larboard to avoid the reef.

Related Terms

  • synonym: port - the left side of a ship or aircraft when facing forward
  • antonym: starboard - the right side of a ship or aircraft when facing forward
