Valentinian

noun
follower of Valentinianism
Pronunciation: / ˌvælənˈtɪniən / (AmE), / ˌvalənˈtɪniən / (BrE)
a follower or adherent of Valentinianism, a Gnostic Christian movement founded by Valentinus in the 2nd century

Examples

Valentinian beliefs
A Valentinian believed that spiritual knowledge was the key to salvation.
The writings of a Valentinian often explored complex theological ideas.
Early church leaders debated the teachings of the Valentinian community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gnostic - a follower of Gnosticism, an early Christian movement emphasizing esoteric knowledge
