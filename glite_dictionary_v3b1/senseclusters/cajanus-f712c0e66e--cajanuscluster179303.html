Cajanus

noun
genus of tropical shrubby plants
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈdʒeɪnəs / (AmE), / kəˈdʒeɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of erect, densely branched shrubby perennial plants native to the Old World tropics, which includes the pigeon pea; widely naturalized in other warm regions.

Examples

Cajanus species
Cajanus species, such as pigeon pea, are cultivated for their protein-rich seeds.
Botanists studied the distribution of Cajanus in newly colonized tropical regions.
The farmer planted rows of Cajanus to help enrich the soil with nitrogen.
