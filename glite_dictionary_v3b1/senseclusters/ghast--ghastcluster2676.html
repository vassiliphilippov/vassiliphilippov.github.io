ghast

verb
to frighten
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡæst / (AmE), / ɡɑːst / (BrE)
to frighten or terrify someone; to fill with horror.

Examples

To ghast the children
The ghost story was told to ghast the children on Halloween night.
Legends of the haunted forest would ghast even the bravest villagers.
The sudden scream in the dark managed to ghast everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfort - to make someone feel less afraid or anxious
  • synonym: scare - to cause someone to feel frightened
