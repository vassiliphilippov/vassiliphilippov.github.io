foreshortening

noun
making shorter
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔrˈʃɔrtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈʃɔːtənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making something shorter in length, duration, or extent

Examples

Foreshortening of time
The foreshortening of the meeting allowed everyone to leave earlier than expected.
Budget constraints led to the foreshortening of the research project by several months.
The foreshortening of the vacation disappointed the children who wanted to stay longer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lengthening - the act of making something longer
  • synonym: shortening - the act of making something shorter
