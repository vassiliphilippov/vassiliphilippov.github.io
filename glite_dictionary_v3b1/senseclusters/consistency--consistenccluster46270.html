consistency

noun
agreement of data across replicas in distributed systems

(computing) The property of a distributed data system that describes whether and when copies of the same data across multiple machines are the same; includes models such as strong consistency (immediate agreement) and eventual consistency (updates propagate and replicas converge over time).

Examples

Data consistency
Many NoSQL databases rely on eventual consistency to balance availability and performance.
Under strong consistency, a read returns the most recent write from any node in the cluster.
Designers sometimes accept temporary consistency delays to keep the system responsive during high traffic.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: eventual consistency - a consistency model in which updates are not immediately visible on all replicas but will converge over time
  • degree higher: strong consistency - a model where all reads return the most recent write and replicas appear identical immediately
