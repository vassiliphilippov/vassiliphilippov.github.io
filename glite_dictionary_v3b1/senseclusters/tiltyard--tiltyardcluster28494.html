tiltyard

noun
enclosed area for jousting tournaments
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪl.jɑrd / (AmE), / ˈtɪl.jɑːd / (BrE)
(historical) An enclosed area where jousting or tilting tournaments were held, especially in medieval and Renaissance times.

Examples

The tiltyard tournament
During the festival, crowds cheered as armor-clad riders clashed in the tiltyard.
The knights gathered in the tiltyard to compete in the annual tournament.
The old palace grounds still have the remains of a medieval tiltyard.
