bivalve

adjective
having a two-part shell (of mollusks)
5.6 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.vælv/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.vælv/ (BrE)
(of a mollusk) having a shell consisting of two hinged parts or valves, as in clams, oysters, and mussels.

Examples

Bivalve mollusk

Scientists found several bivalve fossils in the ancient lake bed.
The oyster is a classic example of a bivalve animal.
