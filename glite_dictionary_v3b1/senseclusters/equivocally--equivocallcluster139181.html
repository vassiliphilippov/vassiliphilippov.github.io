equivocally

adverb
in an ambiguous manner
6.0 6.8
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈkwɪ.və.kə.liː/ /; / /ə.ˈkwɪ.və.kə.liː/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈkwɪ.və.kə.liː/ /; / /ə.ˈkwɪ.və.kə.liː/ (BrE)
In an ambiguous or unclear manner; using language that can be interpreted in more than one way.

Examples

To answer equivocally
She responded equivocally to the allegations, neither confirming nor denying them.
The instructions were equivocally written, leading to several different interpretations.
The spokesperson answered the question equivocally, leaving everyone confused about the official position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambiguously - in a way that is open to more than one interpretation
  • antonym: unequivocally - in a clear and unambiguous manner
