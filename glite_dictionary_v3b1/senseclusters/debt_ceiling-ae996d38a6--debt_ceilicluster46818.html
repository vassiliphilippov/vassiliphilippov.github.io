debt ceiling

noun
legal limit on government borrowing
4.0
Pronunciation: / dɛt ˈsilɪŋ / (AmE), / dɛt ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the maximum amount of money that a government is allowed to borrow by law

Examples

The debt ceiling limit
Debates about the debt ceiling often cause financial uncertainty in the markets.
Many economists warn that failing to increase the debt ceiling could lead to a default on government obligations.
The United States Congress voted to raise the debt ceiling to avoid a government shutdown.
