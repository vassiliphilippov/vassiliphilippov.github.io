external respiration

noun
the exchange of gases between air and the lungs
7.3 3.7
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈstɜːr.nəl res.pər.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɛk.ˈstɜːr.nəl res.pɪ.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈstɜː.nəl res.pɪ.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɛk.ˈstɜː.nəl res.pɪ.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which oxygen is taken into the body from inhaled air and carbon dioxide is expelled through exhalation, typically occurring in the lungs
