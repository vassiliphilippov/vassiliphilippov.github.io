dichromatopsia

noun
color vision deficiency with two color receptors
2.4
Pronunciation: / daɪˌkroʊməˈtɑpsiə / (AmE), / dɪˌkrəʊməˈtɒpsiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person has only two functioning types of color receptors in the eyes, resulting in limited color vision.

Examples

Dichromatopsia color vision
Genetic variations are often responsible for dichromatopsia in humans.
Individuals with dichromatopsia usually cannot distinguish between red and green hues.
Some animal species naturally possess dichromatopsia rather than full trichromatic vision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: color blindness - inability or decreased ability to see color or perceive color differences
