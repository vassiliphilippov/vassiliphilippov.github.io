up to my neck

adjective
deeply involved or immersed (in trouble, work, etc.)
2.2
Pronunciation: / ʌp tuː maɪ nɛk / (AmE), / ʌp tə maɪ nɛk / (BrE)
deeply involved in a difficult or overwhelming situation, especially to the point of being almost overwhelmed

Examples

Be up to my neck in work
I'm up to my neck in paperwork and don't have time for anything else.
She found herself up to her neck in problems after taking the new job.
They're up to their necks in debt after buying the new car.
