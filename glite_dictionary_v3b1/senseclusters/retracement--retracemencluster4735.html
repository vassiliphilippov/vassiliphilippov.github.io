retracement

noun
going back over a path
7.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtreɪsmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈtreɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of going back over a route or path, especially to return to a previous point.

Examples

Retracement of the path
The hikers made a careful retracement of their steps after getting lost in the woods.
A retracement of the original route helped the team find the missing equipment.
After the error was discovered, a full retracement of the process was necessary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - the act of moving forward
  • synonym: return - the act of going back to a place
