low-down

noun
slang; confidential information, real facts
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌdaʊn / (BrE)
slang the true facts or relevant information about something, especially facts that are not widely known.

Examples

The low-down on something
He gave me the low-down on the company's financial situation before the meeting.
If you want the low-down on this year's festival, talk to Jane—she knows everything.
What's the low-down on the new neighbor? I've heard some interesting rumors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dope - slang for inside information
  • synonym: scoop - exclusive news or information, often first reported
