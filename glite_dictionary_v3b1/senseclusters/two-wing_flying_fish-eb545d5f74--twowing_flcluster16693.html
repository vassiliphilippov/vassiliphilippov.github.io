two-wing flying fish

noun
flying fish with only enlarged pectoral fins
6.7
Pronunciation: / tuː wɪŋ ˈflaɪɪŋ fɪʃ / (AmE), / tuː wɪŋ ˈflaɪɪŋ fɪʃ / (BrE)
A flying fish species with only its pectoral fins enlarged, allowing it to glide over the surface of water.

Examples

A two-wing flying fish gliding
Researchers observed a group of two-wing flying fish escaping predators by skimming the water's surface.
The two-wing flying fish can leap impressive distances above the ocean waves.
Unlike the four-wing variety, the two-wing flying fish relies solely on its pectoral fins to glide.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: four-wing flying fish - flying fish with both pectoral and pelvic fins enlarged
