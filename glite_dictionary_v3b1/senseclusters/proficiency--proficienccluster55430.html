proficiency

noun
high degree of skill or competence
Pronunciation: / prəˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / prəˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
a high level of skill or competence in a subject or activity, usually gained through practice and experience

Examples

Proficiency in French
Her proficiency in French impressed the interviewer.
Regular practice led to a noticeable improvement in his proficiency on the piano.
The company values technical proficiency in its employees.
Her proficiency in several languages impressed the interviewers.
The course is designed to improve students' proficiency in written communication.
You must demonstrate technological proficiency to qualify for the position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently
  • synonym: expertise - expert skill or knowledge in a particular field
  • antonym: incompetence - lack of ability, skill, or knowledge
  • antonym: ineptitude - lack of skill or ability
  • synonym: mastery - comprehensive knowledge or skill in a subject or accomplishment
