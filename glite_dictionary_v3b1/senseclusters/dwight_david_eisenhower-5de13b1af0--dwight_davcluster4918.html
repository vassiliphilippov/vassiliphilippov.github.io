Dwight David Eisenhower

proper noun
U.S. President and WWII general
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdwaɪt ˈdeɪvɪd ˈaɪzənˌhaʊər / (AmE), / ˈdwaɪt ˈdeɪvɪd ˈaɪzənhɑː / (BrE)
(1890–1961) The 34th President of the United States, a general who oversaw the Allied invasion of Normandy and the defeat of Nazi Germany during World War II.

Examples

Dwight David Eisenhower as president
Dwight David Eisenhower played a crucial role in planning the D-Day invasion during World War II.

Many historians credit Dwight David Eisenhower with promoting peace and stability during the early years of the Cold War.
