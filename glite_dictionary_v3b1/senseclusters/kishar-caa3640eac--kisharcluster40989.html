A goddess in Babylonian mythology, consort of Anshar, whose name in Sumerian signifies ‘the totality of the lower world’.

Kishar goddess
According to ancient texts, Kishar is considered the mother of several important gods.
In Babylonian mythology, Kishar represents the earth and is paired with Anshar, who represents the sky.
Scholars continue to debate the role of Kishar in the creation myths of Mesopotamia.
