scribe

noun
person who writes documents by hand
6.2 5.0
Pronunciation: /skraɪb/ (AmE), /skraɪb/ (BrE)
a person whose job is to write or record information, such as a copyist, journalist, or medical assistant

Examples

Ancient scribe
In ancient Egypt, a scribe was highly respected for his literacy skills.
She hired a scribe to document the proceedings of the meeting.
The scribe carefully copied the manuscript onto fresh parchment.
As a seasoned scribe, she covered political beats for over a decade.
Several award-winning scribes attended the launch party for the new magazine.
The scribe hurriedly took notes at the press conference, hoping to get a scoop.
During the busy clinic shift, the attending relied on a scribe to enter notes into the EHR.
Medical schools now train scribes to document patient histories and procedures accurately for physicians.
The hospital hired three experienced scribes to reduce doctors' administrative time and improve charting quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clinical scribe - a scribe who specifically assists clinicians by documenting patient histories, examinations, and procedures
  • synonym: copyist - a person who makes copies of written material
  • synonym: journalist - a person who writes for newspapers, magazines, or news websites
  • synonym: medical scribe - a person who documents clinical visits and medical information in health records
  • synonym: reporter - a person who reports news for a newspaper or broadcast
