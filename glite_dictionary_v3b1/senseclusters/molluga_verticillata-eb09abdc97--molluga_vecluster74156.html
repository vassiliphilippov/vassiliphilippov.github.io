Molluga verticillata

noun
a widespread North American mat-forming weed
Pronunciation: / məˈluːɡə vərˌtɪsɪˈleɪtə / (AmE), / məˈluːɡə vəˌtɪsɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
an annual, prostrate, mat-forming weed with whorled leaves and small greenish-white flowers, widespread throughout North America

Examples

Molluga verticillata weed plant
Molluga verticillata is commonly found growing in disturbed soils and lawns across North America.
Gardeners often regard Molluga verticillata as a persistent and troublesome weed.
The small, greenish-white flowers of Molluga verticillata make it easy to identify among ground-covering plants.
