brownish-black

adjective
black tinged with brown
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbraʊnɪʃ ˈblæk / (AmE), / ˈbraʊnɪʃ ˈblak / (BrE)
Of a black color tinged with brown; having a brownish tone to black.

Examples

Brownish-black color
She wore a brownish-black coat that looked almost charcoal in the dim light.
The artist mixed several paints to achieve the perfect brownish-black tone for the portrait's background.
The soil in that area is a deep brownish-black from decaying leaves.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: black - the darkest color, the absence of light
  • degree lower: brown - of the color produced by mixing red, yellow, and black
  • degree lower: brownish - having a somewhat brown color
  • degree higher: jet-black - intensely black; pure black
