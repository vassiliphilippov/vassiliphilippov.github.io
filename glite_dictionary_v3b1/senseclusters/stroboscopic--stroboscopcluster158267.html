stroboscopic

adjective
in rapid bursts
8.8
Pronunciation: /stroʊ.bə.ˈskɑː.pɪk/ (AmE), /strəʊ.bə.ˈskɒp.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or using rapid flashes of light (like a strobe), or appearing in short, quick bursts rather than continuously

Examples

Stroboscopic light effects
The film used stroboscopic lighting effects to create a sense of disorientation.
During the thunderstorm, the sky was illuminated in a stroboscopic pattern by frequent lightning.
The dancer's movements appeared stroboscopic under the flashing stage lights.
The stroboscopic lights at the concert created a dramatic visual effect.
A stroboscopic lamp can make fast-moving objects appear to stand still.
Scientists used stroboscopic techniques to study the motion of the machinery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuous - happening without interruption or pause
  • synonym: intermittent - happening at irregular intervals; not continuous
  • synonym: strobe - a device that produces regular flashes of light
