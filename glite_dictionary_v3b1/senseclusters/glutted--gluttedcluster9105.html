Having an excessive supply of something, especially more than is needed or can be sold or consumed.

Examples

A glutted market
A glutted labor market means more people are competing for fewer jobs.
After a poor holiday season, retailers found themselves with a glutted inventory they struggled to sell.
The oil industry has suffered from a glutted global market for several years.

Synonyms

  • overstocked
    3.9 0.06/m
    overstocked stores hold more products than can be sold, while glutted describes a market with too much supply.
  • overabundant
    5.8 0.05/m
    overabundant describes things produced in very large amounts that are not needed, while glutted focuses on supply too large to sell.
  • overfull
    5.5 0.09/m
    overfull is filled past capacity and often feels literal, while glutted refers to markets with more goods than buyers.

Related Terms
