glutted

adjective
filled to excess; oversupplied
8.4 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈglʌt.ɪd/ (AmE), /ˈglʌt.ɪd/ (BrE)
Having an excessive supply of something, especially more than is needed or can be sold or consumed.

Examples

A glutted market
A glutted labor market means more people are competing for fewer jobs.
After a poor holiday season, retailers found themselves with a glutted inventory they struggled to sell.
The oil industry has suffered from a glutted global market for several years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oversupplied - having more supply than is needed
  • antonym: scarce - in short supply or not enough to meet demand
