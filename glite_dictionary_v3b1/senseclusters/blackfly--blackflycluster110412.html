blackfly

noun
biting fly with aquatic larvae
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌflaɪ / (BrE)
A small, dark-colored biting fly with aquatic larvae, known for sucking the blood of birds and mammals, including humans.

Examples

Biting blackfly
Birds sometimes suffer from blackfly bites, just like humans do.
In early summer, hikers often complain about being bitten by the blackfly near rivers and streams.
The blackfly population increased after several weeks of heavy rain flooded the area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gnat - a small biting fly
  • synonym: mosquito - a small insect that feeds on blood and can carry disease
