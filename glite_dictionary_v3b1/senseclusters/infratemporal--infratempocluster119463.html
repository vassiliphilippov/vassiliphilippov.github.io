infratemporal

adjective
beneath temporal bone
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnfrəˈtɛmpərəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnfrəˈtɛmpərəl / (BrE)
located beneath the temporal bone, especially in reference to the skull or anatomical structures

Examples

Infratemporal area of the skull
The surgeon examined the infratemporal fossa during the procedure.
Damage to the infratemporal region can affect several important nerves and blood vessels.
A tumor in the infratemporal area may cause difficulty in opening the mouth.
