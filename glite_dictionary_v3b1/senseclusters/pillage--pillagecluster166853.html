pillage

to steal goods using force, especially during war or civil disorder

Examples

To pillage a village
After the storm, some people tried to pillage the abandoned shops.
During the conflict, soldiers were accused of pillaging the local villages and taking their food and supplies.
The invading army began to pillage the town, taking anything of value they could find.

Synonyms

  • plunder
    6.0 1.54/m
    plunder means taking goods by force, often by armies in war, while pillage focuses on violent theft during disorder.
  • loot
    4.2 2.32/m
    loot is stealing goods from a place during riots or disasters and is more informal, while pillage is violent wartime theft.
  • despoil
    9.3 0.44/m
    despoil means to strip and damage a place, often causing loss, while pillage focuses on stealing goods by force in war.
  • spoil
    7.6 0.16/m
    spoil means to ruin or take items and is used for damage in many contexts, while pillage is violent theft in war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loot - to steal things from houses or stores during a war or disaster
  • synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force and in a time of disorder
  • antonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or injury
