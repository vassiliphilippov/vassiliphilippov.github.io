depreciating

adjective
losing value; decreasing in worth
5.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpriʃiˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈpriːʃieɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Decreasing in value; tending to lose monetary or other worth over time.

Examples

A depreciating asset
A depreciating asset can negatively affect a company's financial statements.
Property values in some areas are depreciating rapidly due to economic downturn.
The depreciating currency made imports more expensive for local buyers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appreciating - increasing in value
