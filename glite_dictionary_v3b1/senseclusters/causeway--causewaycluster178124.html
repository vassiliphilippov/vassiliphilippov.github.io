causeway

verb
to build a raised road
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔzˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːzweɪ / (BrE)
to build a raised road or path, especially across wet ground or water, or to connect places with such a road.

Examples

To causeway a marsh
The engineers causewayed the tidal flats to provide year-round access to the island laboratory.
Villagers causewayed the marsh to reach the other side safely during the rainy season.
A new road was causewayed over the wetlands to connect the two towns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: construct - to build or put together
  • antonym: demolish - to destroy or tear down a structure
