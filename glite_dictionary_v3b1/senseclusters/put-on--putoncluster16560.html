put-on

adjective
feigned or pretended, not genuine
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊt.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈpʊt.ɒn / (BrE)
(of an action or quality) feigned or adopted in order to deceive; not genuine.

Examples

Put-on smile
His put-on enthusiasm faded as soon as the boss left the room.
She greeted us with a put-on smile that didn’t fool anyone.
The comedian’s put-on accent made everyone laugh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fake - not real; made to look like something real
  • antonym: genuine - authentic; real and not false or pretended
  • synonym: pretended - not genuine; fake or feigned
