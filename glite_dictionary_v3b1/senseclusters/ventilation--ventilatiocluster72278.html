ventilation

noun
supplying fresh air; removing stale air
3.3 3.0
Pronunciation: /ven.tɪ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ven.tɪ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act, process, or system of providing fresh air and removing stale air from a space (such as in a building)

Examples

Room ventilation
During renovations, they improved ventilation to keep the workspace safe and comfortable.
Proper ventilation in a room is necessary to maintain good indoor air quality.
The hospital upgraded its ventilation system to reduce the spread of airborne diseases.
Poor ventilation can lead to a buildup of harmful gases and unpleasant odors.
She adjusted the ventilation to let in more fresh air during the meeting.
The building's ventilation was upgraded to improve air quality throughout the offices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aeration - the process of introducing air into a material, especially soil or liquid
  • degree higher: air conditioning - system for controlling air temperature and humidity
