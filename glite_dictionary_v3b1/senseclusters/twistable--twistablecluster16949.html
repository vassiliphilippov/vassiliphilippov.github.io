twistable

adjective
able to be distorted
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtwɪstəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪstəbəl / (BrE)
able to be twisted, distorted, or misrepresented, especially in meaning or interpretation

Examples

Twistable statement
His statement was so twistable that the media interpreted it in several different ways.
The contract's language is twistable, which could lead to legal disputes.
Politicians often avoid twistable comments to prevent misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambiguous - having more than one possible meaning
  • antonym: clear - easy to understand and not able to be misinterpreted
