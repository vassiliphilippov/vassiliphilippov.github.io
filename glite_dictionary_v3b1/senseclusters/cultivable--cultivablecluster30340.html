cultivable

adjective
suitable for farming
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌltɪvəbl / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪvəbl / (BrE)
(of land) suitable for farming or capable of being cultivated productively.

Examples

Cultivable land
Much of the region's cultivable land is used for growing wheat and barley.
Only a small percentage of the desert is cultivable after irrigation.
The government invested in turning previously barren soil into cultivable farmland.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arable - suitable for growing crops
  • antonym: uncultivable - not capable of being cultivated or farmed
