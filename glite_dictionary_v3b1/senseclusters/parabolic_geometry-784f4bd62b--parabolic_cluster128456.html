parabolic geometry

noun
geometry based on Euclidean axioms
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈbɑlɪk dʒiˈɑmətri / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈbɒlɪk dʒiˈɒmətri / (BrE)
A branch of geometry that follows Euclid's axioms, distinguishing it from hyperbolic and elliptic geometries.

Examples

Parabolic geometry branch
Researchers compared properties in parabolic geometry with those in other non-Euclidean frameworks.
The principles of parabolic geometry are foundational for classical mathematics.
Unlike hyperbolic or elliptic systems, parabolic geometry strictly follows Euclid's five postulates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Euclidean geometry - geometry based on Euclid's postulates
  • antonym: elliptic geometry - non-Euclidean geometry with constant positive curvature
  • antonym: hyperbolic geometry - non-Euclidean geometry with constant negative curvature
