electronic signal

noun
a signal generated electronically
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
a signal produced or transmitted by electronic equipment, often representing information such as audio, video, or data

Examples

Electronic signal transmission
A computer processes information by manipulating electronic signals within its circuits.
Engineers use oscilloscopes to analyze the shape of an electronic signal.
The television converts the broadcast into an electronic signal that produces images and sound.
