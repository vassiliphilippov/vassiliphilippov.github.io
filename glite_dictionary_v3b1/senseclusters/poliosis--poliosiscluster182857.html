poliosis

noun
loss of color from hair
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊliˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpəʊliˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A condition characterized by the loss of color or pigment from areas of the hair, causing white or gray patches.

Examples

Poliosis hair patches
Some genetic disorders can cause poliosis in children at an early age.
The patient exhibited poliosis on her scalp, with a distinct streak of white hair.
The presence of poliosis is sometimes associated with autoimmune conditions.
