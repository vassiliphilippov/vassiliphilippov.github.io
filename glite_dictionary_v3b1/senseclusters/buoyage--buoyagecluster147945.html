buoyage

noun
system of buoys
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbɔɪ.ɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈbuː.ɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈbɔɪ.ɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈbjuː.ɪdʒ/ (BrE)
the system or arrangement of buoys used to mark safe water, channels, or hazards for navigation

Examples

The buoyage system
The harbor's buoyage was updated to help ships avoid new underwater hazards.
International standards regulate the colors and shapes used in buoyage systems.
Sailors rely on proper buoyage to safely navigate unfamiliar waters.
The buoyage along the river ensures that boats can travel safely through shallow areas.
International standards for buoyage help sailors recognize safe passages in unfamiliar waters.
After the storm, the harbor authorities checked the buoyage to make sure all markers were in place.
