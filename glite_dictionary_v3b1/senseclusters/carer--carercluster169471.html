carer

noun
person who provides care
3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkeə.rər/ /; / /ˈke.rər/ (AmE), /ˈkeə.rə/ (BrE)
a person who looks after someone who is ill, elderly, or unable to care for themselves, especially as a job or responsibility

Examples

A carer for the elderly
The carer visits the elderly man every morning to help with his daily routine.
Many families rely on a carer to support relatives with disabilities.
After her surgery, she needed a carer to assist her at home.
The carer visits her client every morning to help with breakfast.
After his accident, he relied on a carer to assist with daily activities.
Many families hire a carer to support elderly relatives living at home.

  • synonym: nurse - a person trained to care for the sick or infirm
  • antonym: patient - a person receiving medical care or assistance
