spin down

verb
cause a device to stop or slow spinning
6.6
Pronunciation: /spɪn daʊn/ (AmE), /spɪn daʊn/ (BrE)
(transitive, computing/electronics) to cause a motor or spinning device (for example a hard drive or fan) to reduce its rotational speed or stop, typically to save power or prepare for shutdown.

Examples

To spin down a hard drive
The system can spin down the hard drives after ten minutes of inactivity to save power.
Before transporting the servers, technicians spun down all drives to prevent damage during the move.
To reduce battery use, the phone's firmware will spin down unused sensors when idle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: power down - to turn off electrical power to a device or put it into a low-power state
  • antonym: spin up - to increase the rotational speed of a device (e.g., a disk) so it reaches operating speed
