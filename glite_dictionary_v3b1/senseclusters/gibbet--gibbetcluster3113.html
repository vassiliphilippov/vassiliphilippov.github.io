gibbet

verb
to hang or display a body on a gibbet
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪbət / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪbɪt / (BrE)
to hang or expose (a body) on a gibbet as a form of execution or public display

Examples

To gibbet a criminal
After the execution, the body was gibbeted at the crossroads for several days.
In medieval times, criminals were sometimes gibbeting in public to deter crime.
The authorities decided to gibbet the traitor as a warning to others.
