permittivity

noun
dielectric constant
8.8
Pronunciation: /pər.mɪ.ˈtɪ.vɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /pə.mɪ.ˈtɪ.vɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the property of a material that affects how it responds to an electric field, including how much the field is reduced and how electrical energy is stored

Examples

Material permittivity value
The permittivity of the material determines how it responds to an electric field.
Scientists compare the permittivity of different substances to design better capacitors.
Water has a high permittivity, which affects how it interacts with electromagnetic waves.
The permittivity of a material determines how it responds to an electric field.
Scientists measured the permittivity of the new polymer to assess its suitability for capacitors.
Vacuum permittivity is a fundamental constant used in many physics equations.
The permittivity of glass is higher than that of air, affecting how capacitors work.
Scientists measured the permittivity of the new material to determine its suitability for electronic devices.
Changes in temperature can influence the permittivity of certain substances.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conductivity - the ability of a material to conduct electric current
  • synonym: dielectric constant - the ratio of a material's permittivity to that of a vacuum
  • antonym: resistivity - the resistance of a material to the flow of electric current
