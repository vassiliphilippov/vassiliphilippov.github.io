surface-active agent

noun
agent reducing liquid surface tension
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːfɪs ˈæktɪv ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfɪs ˈæktɪv ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a chemical substance that reduces the surface tension of a liquid in which it is dissolved, making the liquid spread or mix more easily

Examples

A surface-active agent in detergents
A surface-active agent is often added to detergents to help dissolve oils and grease.
Painters sometimes use a surface-active agent to help paints spread evenly across surfaces.
The laboratory tested the effectiveness of the new surface-active agent in reducing water surface tension.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surfactant - a substance that reduces surface tension in liquids
