comedy ballet

noun
ballet with comic or humorous elements
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmədi bæˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmədi bæˈleɪ / (BrE)
A ballet that focuses on a dramatic narrative but incorporates comic elements or features of comedy.

Examples

Comedy ballet performance
Comedy ballet emerged in the 18th century as choreographers sought to blend humor and dance.
Many classical comedy ballets use exaggerated movements to create a light-hearted mood.
The new comedy ballet delighted audiences with its witty storyline and playful choreography.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tragedy ballet - a ballet that centers on serious or tragic themes
