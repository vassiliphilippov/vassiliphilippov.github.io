ENIAC

proper noun
early digital computer
Pronunciation: / ˈiːniˌæk / (AmE), / ˈiːniˌæk / (BrE)
the first general-purpose electronic digital computer, built in the United States in the 1940s

Examples

The ENIAC computer
The ENIAC was completed in 1945 and could perform thousands of calculations per second.
Historians often cite ENIAC as a milestone in the development of modern computers.
Many students learn about ENIAC when studying the history of technology.
