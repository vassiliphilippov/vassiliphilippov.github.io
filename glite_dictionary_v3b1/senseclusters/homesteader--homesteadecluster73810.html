homesteader

noun
person settling to acquire land ownership
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊmˌstɛdər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌstedə /; / ˈhəʊmˌstɛdə / (BrE)
a person who settles on and works land, especially to gain ownership under a government program

Examples

Homesteaders on land
Her ancestors were homesteaders who built a life in Nebraska.
Many homesteaders braved harsh conditions to claim their land on the frontier.
The museum tells the story of early homesteaders in the American West.
In the late 1800s, many homesteaders moved west to claim land and start new lives.
A modern homesteader might raise chickens and grow vegetables to support their family.
The museum displays tools once used by homesteaders on the American frontier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pioneer - a person who is among the first to settle a new area
  • synonym: settler - a person who moves to a new area and settles there
