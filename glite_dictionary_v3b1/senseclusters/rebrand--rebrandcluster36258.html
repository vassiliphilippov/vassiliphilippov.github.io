rebrand

verb
to change brand identity
3.9
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈbrænd/ /; / /rɪ.ˈbrænd/ (AmE), /riː.ˈbrænd/ /; / /rɪ.ˈbrænd/ (BrE)
to change the name, image, or design of a company, product, or organization to create a new identity

Examples

To rebrand a product
The company decided to rebrand to attract younger customers.

Many businesses rebrand their products to stay relevant in a changing market.
After the merger, the company decided to rebrand to reflect its new direction.
The restaurant rebranded itself with a modern logo and updated menu.
Many businesses rebrand to appeal to a different customer base or market.
