celestial body

noun
natural object in space (star, planet, etc.)
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛstʃəl ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛstʃəl ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
a natural object in space, such as a star, planet, moon, or asteroid, that can be seen from Earth

Examples

A celestial body in space
Astronomers study every type of celestial body, from stars to comets.
Scientists observed a new celestial body in the night sky.
The Sun is the closest celestial body to Earth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: astronomical object - any physical entity in the observable universe beyond Earth's atmosphere
