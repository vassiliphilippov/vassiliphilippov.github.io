heroic verse

noun
verse form for heroic or elevated themes
5.4 7.7
Pronunciation: / hɪˈroʊ.ɪk vɜrs / (AmE), / hɪˈrəʊ.ɪk vɜːs / (BrE)
a verse form traditionally used for the treatment of heroic or lofty themes, especially dactylic hexameter in classical poetry or iambic pentameter in English poetry

Examples

Heroic verse form
Many classical poets composed in heroic verse to emphasize their stories of myth and legend.
Milton's 'Paradise Lost' is written in heroic verse to suit its grand subject matter.
The use of heroic verse in epics gives them a stately and elevated tone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blank verse - unrhymed iambic pentameter
