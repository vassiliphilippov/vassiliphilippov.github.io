Pecos

proper noun
a river in southwestern United States
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɛkɒs / (BrE)
A river in the southwestern United States, a tributary of the Rio Grande, flowing southeast from New Mexico through western Texas.

Examples

The Pecos River
Many towns along the Pecos have relied on its resources for centuries.
The Pecos River provides vital water to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico.
The Pecos eventually merges with the Rio Grande near the Texas-Mexico border.
