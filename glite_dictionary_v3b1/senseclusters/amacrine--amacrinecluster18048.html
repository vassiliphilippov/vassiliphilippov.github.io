amacrine

noun
retinal interneuron
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæməˌkraɪn / (AmE), / ˈæməˌkraɪn / (BrE)
a type of interneuron in the retina that lacks a long axon and modulates signals between bipolar and ganglion cells

Examples

Amacrine cell function
Researchers discovered a new function for amacrine cells in processing visual information.
The activity of an amacrine cell can influence how we detect motion in low light.
Damage to amacrine cells may affect the way the retina transmits signals to the brain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bipolar cell - a type of neuron in the retina that transmits signals from photoreceptors to ganglion cells
  • synonym: ganglion cell - a type of neuron in the retina that receives visual information from bipolar and amacrine cells
