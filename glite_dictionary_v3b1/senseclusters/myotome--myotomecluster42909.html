myotome

noun
muscle group segment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪəˌtoʊm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪətəʊm / (BrE)
a group of muscles served by a single spinal nerve root, or the part of an embryo that develops into muscles

Examples

A myotome group
The neurologist tested each myotome to assess muscle strength in the patient's limbs.
During embryonic development, every myotome gives rise to specific muscle groups.
Damage to a spinal nerve can affect the myotome it supplies, leading to muscle weakness.
The neurologist examined the patient's myotome responses to assess nerve function.
Damage to a specific myotome can cause weakness in certain muscle groups.
Medical students learn to identify each myotome during their anatomy courses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dermatome - an area of skin supplied by a single spinal nerve
  • synonym: sclerotome - the part of an embryo that forms vertebrae and ribs
