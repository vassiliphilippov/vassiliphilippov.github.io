transmissible

adjective
able to be transmitted, especially by infection
4.1 4.3
Pronunciation: /trænz.ˈmɪs.ə.bəl/ /; / /trænz.ˈmɪs.ɪ.bəl/ /; / /træns.ˈmɪs.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /trænz.ˈmɪs.ə.bəl/ /; / /trænz.ˈmɪs.ɪ.bəl/ /; / /træns.ˈmɪs.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) capable of being transmitted from one person or organism to another, especially by infection.

Examples

A transmissible disease
Measles is a highly transmissible viral disease, especially among children.
Some illnesses are transmissible through direct contact, while others require a vector.
The researchers are studying how transmissible the new variant is.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contagious - (of a disease) spread by close contact between people
  • synonym: infectious - (of a disease) able to be passed from one person, animal, or organism to another
  • antonym: noncommunicable - (of a disease) not capable of being passed from person to person
