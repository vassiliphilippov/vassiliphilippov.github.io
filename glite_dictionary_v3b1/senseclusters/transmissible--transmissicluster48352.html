(of a disease or condition) capable of being transmitted from one person or organism to another, especially by infection.

Examples

A transmissible disease
Measles is a highly transmissible viral disease, especially among children.
Some illnesses are transmissible through direct contact, while others require a vector.
The researchers are studying how transmissible the new variant is.

Synonyms

  • communicable
    4.2 0.46/m
    communicable is used for diseases spread by infection in medicine, while transmissible mainly means capable of being passed between organisms.
  • transmittable
    3.9 0.09/m
    transmittable means able to be passed from person to person, while transmissible is the more common medical adjective for that ability.
  • infectious
    3.9 5.66/m
    infectious describes diseases caused by germs that spread easily, while transmissible focuses on anything capable of being passed between organisms.
  • contagious
    3.1 4.46/m
    contagious means something that spreads easily through touch or close contact, while transmissible is a neutral term for being passed.
  • catching
    3.5 0.00/m
    catching is an informal British adjective for diseases that pass between people, while transmissible is the general medical word for that ability.

  • antonym: noncommunicable - (of a disease) not capable of being passed from person to person
