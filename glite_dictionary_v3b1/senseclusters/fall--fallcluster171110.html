fall

noun
a lapse into sin; loss of innocence
Pronunciation: / fɔl / (AmE), / fɔːl / (BrE)
a lapse into sin or immorality; a loss of innocence or purity, often in a moral or spiritual context

Examples

The fall of man
Many religious texts discuss the fall of humanity and the need for salvation.
She viewed her youthful mistake as a fall from innocence.
The story describes the fall of a once-honorable man into corruption.

Related Terms

  • antonym: redemption - the action of being saved from sin, error, or evil
  • synonym: sin - an act considered to be a transgression against divine law
