wild celery

noun
a type of aquatic plant
8.5
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈsɛləri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈsɛləri / (BrE)
a submerged aquatic plant with long, narrow leaves, found in Europe, Asia, and Australia; often referring to various species of the genus Vallisneria

Examples

Lake wild celery
Ducks often feed on wild celery found in shallow lakes.
Scientists study the growth patterns of wild celery to monitor aquatic ecosystem health.
The pond was covered in wild celery, providing shelter for small fish.
