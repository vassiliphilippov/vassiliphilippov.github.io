causal agency

noun
entity causing an effect or result
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔzəl ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈkɔːzl ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
an entity that brings about an effect or is responsible for causing events or results

Examples

The causal agency effect
In his research, he argued that the virus was the main causal agency behind the outbreak.
Many philosophers debate whether free will or determinism is the true causal agency in human behavior.
We must identify the causal agency responsible for the sudden shift in climate patterns.

  • synonym: agent - a person or thing that takes an active role or produces a specified effect
