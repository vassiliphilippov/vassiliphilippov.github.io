resensitization

noun
becoming sensitive again
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌsɛnsɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming sensitive again to a stimulus or experience after having lost sensitivity

Examples

Resensitization to sounds
The therapy aimed at the resensitization of patients to certain sounds after hearing loss.
Repeated exposure to the allergen led to the resensitization of the immune system.
Researchers studied the resensitization of nerve cells following prolonged medication use.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desensitization - the process of becoming less sensitive to a stimulus
