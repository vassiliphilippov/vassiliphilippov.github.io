Plesiosaurus

noun
extinct genus of marine reptiles
9.9
Pronunciation: /pliː.si.ə.ˈsɔːr.əs/ (AmE), /pliː.si.ə.ˈsɔːr.əs/ (BrE)
an extinct genus of marine reptiles of the suborder Plesiosauria, known for their long necks and paddle-like limbs, that lived during the Jurassic period

Examples

The Plesiosaurus fossil
Scientists believe that Plesiosaurus used its flippers to navigate through ancient seas.
The distinctive long neck of Plesiosaurus makes it one of the most recognizable prehistoric reptiles.
The fossils of Plesiosaurus were first discovered in the early nineteenth century.
