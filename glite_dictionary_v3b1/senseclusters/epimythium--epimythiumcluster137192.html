epimythium

noun
moral at the end of a story
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈmɪθiəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈmɪθɪəm / (BrE)
A short statement expressing the moral or lesson at the end of a fable or story.

Examples

An epimythium at story end
Aesop's tales often include an epimythium summarizing their message.
After the narrative, the author added an epimythium to clarify the intended lesson.
The fable ends with an epimythium that teaches the value of honesty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moral - a lesson about right and wrong conduct, often at the end of a story
