Bahama Islands

proper noun
island country in the Atlantic Ocean
2.1
Pronunciation: / bəˈhɑmə ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / bəˈhɑːmə ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
A group of islands in the Atlantic Ocean, constituting an independent country to the east of Florida and Cuba, known for tourism and as a popular winter resort.

Examples

The Bahama Islands beaches
Many tourists visit the Bahama Islands each year to enjoy their beautiful beaches and warm climate.
The Bahama Islands gained independence from Britain in 1973.
Wildlife conservation is a key issue in the Bahama Islands due to their unique ecosystems.
