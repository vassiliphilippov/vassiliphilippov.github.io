bone-forming cell

noun
cell that creates bone tissue
Pronunciation: / boʊn ˈfɔrmɪŋ sɛl / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈfɔːmɪŋ sɛl / (BrE)
a cell that produces new bone tissue during growth or healing

Examples

Bone-forming cell activity
An injury will trigger the activity of bone-forming cells to repair the damaged area.
During childhood, the number of bone-forming cells is higher to support rapid bone growth.
Vitamin D plays a crucial role in the development and function of bone-forming cells.
