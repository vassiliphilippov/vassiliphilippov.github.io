determine

verb
find out or learn with certainty
Pronunciation: / dəˈtɜrmɪn /; / dɪˈtɜrmɪn /; / dəˈtɝː.mɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪn /; / dɪˈtɜː.mɪn / (BrE)
to find out, discover, or establish something, especially by investigation, calculation, or study

Examples

To determine the cause
She called to determine the exact time of his arrival.
The investigation will determine whether fraud has occurred.
We must determine if the results are accurate before proceeding.
Astronomers use calculations to determine the exact position of a planet.
The committee will determine the date for the next meeting by the end of the week.
The regulations determine which products may be sold in the country.
Can you determine the total cost by adding all these figures together?
It took the committee several weeks to determine the cause of the failure.
The experiment was conducted to determine whether the new drug was effective.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascertain - find out or make certain
  • synonym: decide - to make a choice or come to a resolution
  • synonym: discover - find out, especially information previously unknown
  • synonym: fix - to set or establish something firmly
  • antonym: guess - to estimate without sufficient information
  • synonym: specify - to state something clearly and exactly
