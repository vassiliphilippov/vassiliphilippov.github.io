milium

noun
small white skin lump (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪəm / (BrE)
A small, white, raised lump on the skin caused by a clogged sebaceous gland.

Examples

A milium lump
After a few weeks, the milium disappeared on its own without treatment.
Infants often develop a milium on their nose or cheeks shortly after birth.
The dermatologist identified the small bump on her cheek as a milium.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whitehead - a similar small white bump on the skin, commonly caused by blocked pores
