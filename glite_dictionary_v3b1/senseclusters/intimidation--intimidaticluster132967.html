intimidation

noun
the act of making someone afraid or discouraged
2.8
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɪməˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɪmɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making someone afraid or discouraging them, especially by using threats or pressure

Examples

Workplace intimidation
Attempts at intimidation will not stop us from expressing our views.
Her supervisor used subtle intimidation to keep her from reporting the problem.
Some students dropped out of the contest because of the intimidation they faced from others.
Many people spoke out against the intimidation used during the elections.
She reported the intimidation she experienced at school to the authorities.
The manager's intimidation of his staff led to a toxic work environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coercion - the act of making someone do something by force or threats
  • antonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support or confidence
  • synonym: threat - a statement of intention to inflict harm or punishment
