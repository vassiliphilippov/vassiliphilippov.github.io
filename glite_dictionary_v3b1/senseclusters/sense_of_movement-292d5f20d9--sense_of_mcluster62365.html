sense of movement

noun
awareness of body or limb movement
6.0 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / s…õns …ôv ÀàmuÀêvm…ônt / (AmE), / s…õns …ôv ÀàmuÀêvm…ônt / (BrE)
the ability to perceive the movement of one's limbs or body, usually through internal sensory mechanisms

Examples

Sense of movement in limbs
After her injury, her sense of movement in the right arm was significantly reduced.
Physical therapists assess a patient's sense of movement to determine nerve function and coordination.
The drug is known to affect the sense of movement, making walking more difficult.

Synonyms

  • kinesthesia
    9.8 0.09/m
    kinesthesia is the direct feeling of muscle and joint motion during movement, while sense of movement names the general ability to detect motion.
  • proprioception
    9.9 0.07/m
    proprioception is the body's internal sense of limb position and movement, while sense of movement focuses on perceiving movement itself.
  • muscle sense
    6.3 0.00/m
    muscle sense refers to signals from muscles about limb position and movement, while sense of movement names the overall ability to feel motion.
  • feeling of movement
    6.0 0.00/m
    feeling of movement describes a person's simple awareness of motion and muscle tension, while sense of movement is a technical term for that ability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proprioception - the sense that detects position and movement of the body
