Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution law

noun
law describing molecular energy distribution in gases
Pronunciation: / ˈmæksˌwɛl ˈbɔlt.smən dɪˌstrɪb.juˈʃən lɔ / (AmE), / ˈmæks.wɛl ˈbɒlts.mən dɪ.strɪˌbjuː.ʃən lɔː / (BrE)
a law in physics that describes how the energies of molecules in a gas are distributed when the gas is in thermal equilibrium

Examples

The Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution law
In introductory physics, students often encounter the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution law when studying thermodynamics.
The Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution law predicts how particles in a gas move at different speeds.
Understanding the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution law is crucial for explaining the behavior of ideal gases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Boltzmann distribution - the distribution of energies among particles in a system at thermal equilibrium
