aversive conditioning

noun
behavioral learning using unpleasant stimuli
9.9 6.2
Pronunciation: / əˈvɜrsɪv kənˌdɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈvɜːsɪv kənˈdɪʃ(ə)nɪŋ / (BrE)
a learning process in which a behavior is modified or suppressed through the use of unpleasant or aversive stimuli

Examples

Aversive conditioning therapy
Researchers studied the effects of aversive conditioning on phobic responses in laboratory animals.
The therapist used aversive conditioning to help the patient break their smoking habit.
Through aversive conditioning, certain behaviors can be reduced by associating them with unpleasant experiences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classical conditioning - a learning process that pairs a neutral stimulus with a response-producing stimulus
  • synonym: operant conditioning - a learning process involving reward and punishment to shape behavior
