break

verb
find a flaw in (argument, proof, etc.)
1.0
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
to solve, decipher, or find the solution to something difficult or secret, such as a code or problem

Examples

To break a code
A skilled lawyer knows how to break a witness's testimony with careful questioning.
It took hours, but the mathematician finally broke the complex proof.
The detective was able to break the suspect's alibi during the interrogation.
After weeks of trying, the scientists finally broke the encryption on the files.
It took a team of experts to break the complex cipher used in the message.
The detective was able to break the mysterious code after hours of analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confirm - to prove that an argument or statement is true
  • synonym: crack - to find the solution to or decipher (often used with codes or mysteries)
  • synonym: disprove - to show an argument or statement is false
  • synonym: solve - to find the answer to a problem or puzzle
