come

verb
to happen or occur
1.0 959/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm / (AmE), / kÊŒm / (BrE)
to happen, occur, or become known (especially after some time or as a result)

Examples

A chance comes
A big opportunity comes only once in a while, so make the most of it.
The news finally came late in the afternoon, bringing relief to the whole family.
Winter comes earlier in the northern regions compared to the south.
No harm will come from telling the truth.
Nothing good will come of all this arguing.
What comes of their investment remains to be seen.
No official word has come from the authorities yet.
Reports have come in about flooding in the region.
The results of the election will come in tomorrow morning.

Synonyms

  • happen
    1.0 745/m
    happen is something that takes place, often by chance, while come means to happen or become known over time.
  • occur
    2.1 103/m
    occur takes place or exists in a situation, often in formal speech, while come is used more in everyday language.
  • transpire
    6.9 3.45/m
    transpire often means that something becomes known or happens later, while come is more general and common in speech.
  • emerge
    4.0 76/m
    emerge becomes visible or known after time, often slowly, while come can mean something happens or becomes known more broadly.
  • arise
    3.3 38/m
    arise begins to exist, often for problems or opportunities, while come covers general happening or becoming known in many cases.
  • appear
    1.0 86/m
    appear comes into sight or starts to exist visibly, while come also includes things happening or being learned over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrive - to reach a place or destination; also used for information/news being received
  • antonym: depart - to leave, go away from a place; opposite of arrive (in some contexts)
  • synonym: ensue - to happen or occur afterward as a result
  • synonym: happen - to take place; to occur
  • synonym: occur - to happen or take place
  • synonym: result - to happen as a consequence of something
