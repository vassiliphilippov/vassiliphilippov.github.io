FEC

noun
U.S. election agency
5.6
Pronunciation: / ɛf i si / (AmE), / ɛf iː siː / (BrE)
the Federal Election Commission, a U.S. government agency that regulates campaign finance laws for federal elections

Examples

The FEC regulates elections
The FEC enforces rules about how political campaigns can raise and spend money.
Candidates must report their donations and expenses to the FEC regularly.
The FEC investigated several complaints about illegal campaign contributions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Federal Election Commission - the full name of the U.S. agency abbreviated as FEC
  • synonym: SEC - U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, another federal regulatory agency
