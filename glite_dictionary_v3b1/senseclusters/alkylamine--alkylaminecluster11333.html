alkylamine

noun
organic amine compound
Pronunciation: / ˌælkaɪl əˈmiːn / (AmE), / ˌalkɪl əˈmiːn / (BrE)
an organic compound in which an alkyl group is bonded to an amine group

Examples

An alkylamine compound
The chemist added an alkylamine to the reaction to observe its effect on the product.
Many pharmaceuticals contain an alkylamine group as part of their structure.
Researchers are studying how different alkylamines interact with biological systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alkyl group - a group of carbon and hydrogen atoms arranged in a chain, derived from an alkane
  • synonym: amine - an organic compound derived from ammonia by replacement of one or more hydrogen atoms by organic groups
