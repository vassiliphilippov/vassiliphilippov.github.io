methodize

verb
to organize systematically
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛθəˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛθədaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or organize something in a systematic or orderly way

Examples

To methodize your work
She tried to methodize her notes before the exam to make studying easier.
The librarian methodized the collection so that books could be found quickly.
It is helpful to methodize your tasks when working on a large project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disorganize - to make something untidy or without order
  • synonym: organize - to arrange or structure things in an orderly way
  • synonym: systematize - to arrange according to a system or plan
