Anglo-French

noun
the French language used in medieval England
Pronunciation: / ˌæŋɡloʊ ˈfrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊ ˈfrɛntʃ / (BrE)
the French language as spoken and used in medieval England, especially by the ruling classes and in law and administration

Examples

Anglo-French language
Legal documents in medieval England were often written in Anglo-French.
Many Norman aristocrats spoke Anglo-French as their first language in England.
The influence of Anglo-French can still be seen in English legal terms today.
