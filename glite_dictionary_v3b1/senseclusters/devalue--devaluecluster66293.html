devalue

verb
to lose in value
5.3 1.51/m
Pronunciation: / diÀàv√¶l.ju / (AmE), / diÀàv√¶l.juÀê / (BrE)
(of a currency or asset) to lose in value; to become less valuable, especially in terms of exchange rates or worth.

Examples

To devalue a currency
Experts warned that the policy could cause the local currency to devalue against the dollar.
If the company continues to perform poorly, its stock may devalue significantly.
Many currencies tend to devalue during periods of economic instability.

Synonyms

  • depreciate
    5.9 0.96/m
    depreciate describes a gradual loss of value over time, while devalue usually refers to a currency falling in market or by policy.
  • devaluate
    7.5 0.00/m
    devaluate often means a planned drop in a currency's value by government or market forces, while devalue covers losing value generally.
  • undervalue
    4.9 0.63/m
    undervalue is often used when people judge something as worth less than it is, while devalue means lose market value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appreciate - to increase in value over time
  • synonym: depreciate - to decrease in value over time
