lansoprazole

noun
acid-reducing drug for stomach issues
6.9
Pronunciation: / lænˈsɑːprəˌzoʊl / (AmE), / lænˈsɒprəzəʊl / (BrE)
a drug used to reduce stomach acid production, often prescribed for conditions like ulcers or gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)

Examples

lansoprazole medicine
After starting lansoprazole, he noticed a significant improvement in his heartburn.
Many patients take lansoprazole daily to prevent stomach ulcers caused by certain medications.
The doctor prescribed lansoprazole to help manage her acid reflux symptoms.
