anthracene

noun
aromatic hydrocarbon
Pronunciation: / ˈænθrəˌsin / (AmE), / ˈænθrəsiːn / (BrE)
a solid, crystalline aromatic hydrocarbon used in scientific and industrial applications, such as making dyes, detecting radiation, and organic electronics

Examples

Anthracene crystals
The laboratory used anthracene crystals to detect faint flashes of radiation.
Organic light-emitting diodes sometimes contain anthracene as a key material.
Researchers studied the fluorescence properties of anthracene under ultraviolet light.
The laboratory ordered pure anthracene for use in their organic chemistry experiments.
Many synthetic dyes are produced using anthracene as a starting material.
Exposure to anthracene should be minimized due to its potential health risks.
