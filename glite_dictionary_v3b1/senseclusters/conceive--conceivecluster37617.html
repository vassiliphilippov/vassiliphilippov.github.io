conceive

verb
form or create an idea in the mind
5.2 7.68/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàsiv / (AmE), / k…ônÀàsiÀêv / (BrE)
to form or develop an idea, plan, or intention in the mind

Examples

To conceive a plan
A solution to the problem was finally conceived after hours of discussion.
She conceived the idea while traveling abroad last year.
The architect conceived an innovative design for the new museum.

Synonyms

    8.4 0.00/m
    ideate is a formal word for making new ideas in design or business, while conceive is general for forming plans.
  • conceptualize
    6.9 4.29/m
    conceptualize means forming a clear plan or model in the mind, while conceive is making an idea or plan.
  • think of
    2.1 32/m
    think of means to invent or come up with an idea casually, while conceive is more formal and often implies planning.
  • think up
    3.2 2.47/m
    think up means to invent or create something, often quickly or as an excuse, while conceive suggests forming and developing an idea.
  • gestate
    7.5 0.14/m
    gestate means to develop an idea slowly over time in your mind, while conceive can mean forming an idea at any stage.
  • envisage
    6.8 2.66/m
    envisage means to picture something as a possible future, while conceive means to form or plan an idea more generally.
  • envision
    4.1 15/m
    envision means to picture a possible future situation in your mind, while conceive focuses on forming or developing an idea or plan.
  • imagine
    1.3 133/m
    imagine means to form a mental image of something not real, while conceive means to form or develop an idea or plan.
