municipalization

noun
becoming municipal
8.0
Pronunciation: / mjuːˌnɪsəpələˈzeɪʃən /; / mjuˌnɪsəpələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / mjuːˌnɪsɪpəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transferring control or ownership of a service, asset, or area to a city or local government

Examples

The municipalization of services
The municipalization of water services improved access for residents in the growing town.
Debates about municipalization often focus on whether local control leads to better management.
After years of private operation, the city voted in favor of municipalization of the bus system.
The municipalization of the electricity grid allowed the city to set its own energy policies.
Debates over municipalization often focus on whether local control improves public services.
After municipalization, the water system became more responsive to residents' needs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nationalization - the process of bringing industries or assets under national government control
  • antonym: privatization - the process of transferring ownership from the public sector to private individuals or companies
