kerning

noun
letter spacing adjustment
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkɜːr.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkɜː.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
the adjustment of space between individual characters in printed or digital text to improve appearance or readability

Examples

Text kerning
Graphic designers pay close attention to kerning to make sure their text looks professional.
Poor kerning can make words difficult to read and reduce the impact of a design.
Adjusting the kerning between letters can help create a more balanced and attractive logo.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leading - the vertical spacing between lines of text
  • synonym: tracking - the uniform adjustment of spacing across a range of characters
