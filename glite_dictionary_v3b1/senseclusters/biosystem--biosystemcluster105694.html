biosystem

noun
engineered biological system
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪoʊˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈbaɪəʊˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
a human-made or engineered biological system designed to perform or simulate biological functions, often used in research or technology

Examples

Engineered biosystem
Researchers developed a biosystem to study how bacteria respond to antibiotics in controlled conditions.
The new biosystem can simulate human organ functions for drug testing purposes.
Students learned how to assemble a simple biosystem using microfluidic devices in their laboratory class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioreactor - a device or vessel used to grow organisms under controlled conditions
  • antonym: ecosystem - a community of living organisms interacting with their environment
