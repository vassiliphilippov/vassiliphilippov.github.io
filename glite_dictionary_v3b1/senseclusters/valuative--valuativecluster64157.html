expressing or relating to the act of evaluating or assigning value to something

Examples

A valuative statement
In philosophy, a valuative statement expresses approval or disapproval rather than just facts.
The teacher's comments were more valuative than descriptive, focusing on the quality of the work.
Words like 'excellent' and 'bad' are considered valuative adjectives in grammar.

Synonyms

  • valuational
    8.1 0.02/m
    valuational relates to assessing or judging worth in contexts like prices or importance, while valuative refers to the act of assigning value.
  • evaluative
    7.1 1.83/m
    evaluative means making judgments about quality or significance, often in a systematic review, while valuative focuses on assigning value to something.
  • appraisive
    8.4 0.01/m
    appraisive expresses a judgment often tied to monetary or formal appraisal, whereas valuative describes the general act of assigning value.

Related Terms
