idealistic

adjective
relating to the doctrine of idealism in philosophy
Pronunciation: / aɪˌdiəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / aɪˌdɪəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the philosophical doctrine that reality is fundamentally based on ideas or the mind rather than material objects.

Examples

Idealistic philosophy
His idealistic beliefs led him to view the world as fundamentally shaped by ideas, not by material forces.
Many philosophers in the 18th century defended idealistic theories that prioritized mind over matter.
The idealistic tradition in philosophy argues that reality depends on the mental rather than the physical.

Related Terms

  • antonym: materialistic - relating to the doctrine that physical matter is the only reality
