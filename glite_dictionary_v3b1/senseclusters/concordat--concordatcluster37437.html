concordat

noun
formal agreement, often between governments
7.3 8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːŋ.kɔːr.dæt/ /; / /ˈkɑːn.kɔːr.dæt/ (AmE), /ˈkɒŋ.kɔː.dæt/ /; / /ˈkɒn.kɔː.dæt/ (BrE)
a formal agreement, especially one between the Vatican and a government, or between two states, to perform a specific action or establish mutual relations

Examples

A concordat agreement

The concordat was signed by the two nations after months of negotiation.
Under the terms of the concordat, the church gained certain privileges.
