posterior

noun
Bayesian posterior distribution
9.6
Pronunciation: /pɔː.ˈstɪr.ɪ.ər/ /; / /poʊ.ˈstɪr.ɪ.ər/ /; / /pɑː.ˈstɪr.ɪ.ər/ (AmE), /pɒ.ˈstɪə.rɪ.ə/ (BrE)
(in statistics, especially Bayesian inference) the probability distribution for a parameter or hypothesis after updating a prior distribution with observed data.

Examples

Posterior probability
After analyzing the data, the researchers reported the posterior distribution for the model parameter.
Bayesian methods update the posterior by combining the prior with the likelihood of observed data.
The textbook shows how to compute a sample from the posterior using Markov chain Monte Carlo.

Related Terms

  • synonym: posterior distribution - the full phrase for the probability distribution of a parameter after updating with data (same concept).
  • antonym: prior - the probability distribution representing belief about a parameter before observing the data.
