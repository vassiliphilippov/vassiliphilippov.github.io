lube

verb
apply a lubricant
6.4 0.42/m
Pronunciation: / luÀêb / (AmE), / luÀêb / (BrE)
to apply a lubricant to something, such as a machine or vehicle part, to reduce friction or wear

Examples

To lube a chain
After every oil change, it's a good idea to lube moving engine parts.
He took the time to lube the chain before the bike race.
Make sure you lube the door hinges to stop them squeaking.

Synonyms

  • lubricate
    4.4 1.66/m
    lubricate is a formal word for applying oil or grease in technical contexts, while lube is the informal everyday verb used in casual speech.
  • grease
    2.8 5.50/m
    grease means putting thick lubricant on moving parts to make them run smoothly, while lube means applying any lubricant more generally.
  • oil
    4.1 2.77/m
    oil means putting oil on something for treatment or ritual use, while lube specifically means applying a lubricant to reduce friction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lubricate - to apply oil or grease so as to make smooth
