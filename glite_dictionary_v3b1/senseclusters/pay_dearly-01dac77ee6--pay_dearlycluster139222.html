pay dearly

verb
suffer serious consequences
Pronunciation: / peɪ ˈdɪrli / (AmE), / peɪ ˈdɪəli / (BrE)
to experience serious or significant suffering, loss, or consequences as a result of an action or decision

Examples

To pay dearly for mistakes
Families pay dearly when disaster strikes due to poor planning.
If you continue to ignore medical advice, you may pay dearly for it later.
The company paid dearly for its environmental violations in court.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gain - obtain something desirable, the opposite of paying dearly
  • synonym: suffer - experience pain or distress
