hypovolemia

noun
decreased blood volume condition

Pronunciation: / Àåha…™po äv…ôÀàlimi…ô /; / ha…™.po ä.v…ôÀàliÀê.mi.…ô / (AmE), / Àåh å…™p…ô äv…ôÀàliÀêm…™…ô /; / ha…™.p…ô ä.v…ôÀàliÀê.mi.…ô / (BrE)
A condition in which there is a decreased volume of circulating blood in the body.

Examples

Severe hypovolemia treatment
Rapid fluid replacement is necessary to treat severe hypovolemia.
Symptoms such as dizziness and low blood pressure can indicate hypovolemia.
The patient was diagnosed with hypovolemia after losing a significant amount of blood.
Doctors quickly treated his hypovolaemia with intravenous fluids.
If left untreated, hypovolaemia can lead to shock and organ failure.
The patient developed hypovolaemia after severe blood loss from the accident.
