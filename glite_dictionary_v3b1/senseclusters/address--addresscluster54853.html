address

verb
to direct remarks or questions to someone
4.2 3.2
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈdres/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdres/ (BrE)
to speak to someone directly or to direct a question, remark, or statement towards someone, especially in a formal way

Examples

To address a person
At the press conference, the reporter addressed the minister with a tough question.
If you have any concerns, please address them to the committee during the meeting.
The interviewer addressed several questions directly to the guest.
Please address the judge as 'Your Honor' in the courtroom.
She always addresses her professor as 'Doctor Smith'.
The president addressed the queen as 'Your Majesty' during the ceremony.
Before you begin, please address the chairperson with your question.
She stood up to address the audience during the ceremony.
The president will address the nation tonight in a televised speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ask - to put a question to someone
  • synonym: call - to refer to someone by a name or title
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately not speak to or pay attention to someone
  • synonym: speak to - to talk to someone
