biosecurity

noun
protection from biological threats
5.1
Pronunciation: /baɪ.oʊ.sɪ.ˈkjʊr.ə.ti/ /; / /baɪ.oʊ.sɪ.ˈkjʊr.ɪ.ti/ (AmE), /baɪ.əʊ.sɪ.ˈkjʊə.rɪ.ti/ /; / /baɪ.əʊ.sɪ.ˈkjʊə.rə.ti/ (BrE)
measures and practices to protect people, animals, plants, or other living things from harmful biological agents, diseases, or threats

Examples

Strict biosecurity measures
Strict biosecurity measures are essential to prevent the spread of infectious diseases on farms.
The laboratory upgraded its biosecurity protocols after discovering a new virus.
International cooperation is needed to improve biosecurity and stop the misuse of dangerous pathogens.
The government increased biosecurity measures at airports to prevent the spread of invasive species.
Farmers must follow strict biosecurity protocols to protect their livestock from disease.
International organizations work together to improve biosecurity and prevent global outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biosafety - practices to prevent accidental release of harmful biological agents
  • antonym: bioterrorism - the use of biological agents to cause harm or fear
  • synonym: quarantine - isolation to prevent the spread of disease
