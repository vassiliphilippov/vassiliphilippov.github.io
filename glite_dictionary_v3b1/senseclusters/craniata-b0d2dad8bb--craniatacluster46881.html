Craniata

noun
taxonomic group of animals with skulls
Pronunciation: / kreɪniˈeɪtə / (AmE), / krəˈnaɪətə / (BrE)
A major taxonomic group of animals comprising all vertebrates and their close relatives, characterized by the presence of a skull.

Examples

Craniata animals
All mammals belong to the group Craniata, distinguished by the presence of a skull.
Scientists debate which early fossil species can be classified within Craniata.
The evolutionary history of Craniata includes diverse forms from fishes to mammals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Vertebrata - animals with backbones; a subgroup of Craniata
