leek

noun
long white-bulbed vegetable; mild-tasting
Pronunciation: / lik / (AmE), / liːk / (BrE)
a vegetable with a long white stem and flat dark green leaves, related to the onion and used in cooking for its mild flavor

Examples

Fresh leeks in soup
After harvesting, fresh leeks can be stored in the refrigerator for several weeks.
The recipe calls for one large leek, thoroughly cleaned and sliced.
We added chopped leek to the soup for extra flavor.
A traditional Welsh dish often features leek as a main ingredient.
She added chopped leek to the soup for extra flavor.
The farmer harvested a row of fresh leeks from the garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: garlic - a plant with a strong odor and flavor, related to the leek
  • synonym: onion - a round vegetable with a strong smell and flavor, closely related to the leek
