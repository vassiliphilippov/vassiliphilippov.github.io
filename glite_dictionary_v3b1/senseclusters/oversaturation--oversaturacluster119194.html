oversaturation

noun
excessive saturation
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a state where there is more of something (such as a substance, quality, or media) than can be handled or is effective

Examples

Oversaturation of media
The oversaturation of advertisements made it hard to enjoy the website.
Too much fertilizer can lead to oversaturation of nutrients in the soil.
The artist avoided oversaturation of colors to keep the painting looking natural.
The oversaturation of advertisements on social media made users start ignoring them completely.
Critics argue that oversaturation of superhero movies has led to audience fatigue.
When a market reaches oversaturation, new products struggle to attract attention.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: saturation - the state of being fully soaked or filled
  • antonym: underexposure - insufficient exposure to something, such as media or advertising
  • antonym: undersaturation - the state of containing less than the normal amount of a substance
