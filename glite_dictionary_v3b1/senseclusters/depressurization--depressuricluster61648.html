depressurization

noun
loss of air pressure
Pronunciation: / diˌprɛʃəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌprɛʃəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of reducing or losing air pressure in a sealed space or environment

Examples

Cabin depressurization
A sudden depressurization in an airplane can be dangerous for passengers.
The crew trained for emergency depressurization scenarios in the spacecraft simulator.
Engineers checked the cabin for any signs of depressurization after the alarm sounded.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pressurization - the process of increasing or maintaining air pressure in a space
