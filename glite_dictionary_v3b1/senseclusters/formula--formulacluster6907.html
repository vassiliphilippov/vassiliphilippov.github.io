formula

noun
mathematical rule or method
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrmjələ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmjələ /; / ˈfɔːmjʊlə / (BrE)
a mathematical, scientific, or chemical rule or relationship expressed in symbols or numbers

Examples

Math formula
She memorized the formula to solve quadratic equations for the exam.
The professor introduced a new formula to simplify complex calculations.
You can calculate the area of a circle using the standard formula of πr².
Every compound has a unique formula that tells you what elements are present.
She memorized the formula for carbon dioxide, which is CO₂.
The formula for water is H₂O, indicating it contains two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.
Einstein's most famous formula is E = mc², which expresses the equivalence of mass and energy.
The Pythagorean theorem provides a formula for calculating the length of the hypotenuse.
The area of a circle can be found using the simple formula A = πr².
