unfrock

verb
to remove from office
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfrɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈfrɒk / (BrE)
to officially remove someone from a position of authority or professional status, especially as a disciplinary action.

Examples

To unfrock a leader
After the scandal, the board unfrocked the CEO, stripping him of all his titles and privileges.
The corrupt official was unfrocked following a lengthy investigation into his actions.
The organization decided to unfrock the member who violated its code of conduct.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appoint - to assign someone to a position or office
  • synonym: depose - to remove someone from a position of power
  • synonym: dismiss - to remove someone from their job or position
