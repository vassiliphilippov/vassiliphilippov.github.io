prehearing

noun
meeting before hearing
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈhɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈhɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
a meeting or session held before a formal hearing to discuss procedures or issues

Examples

A prehearing meeting
The lawyer attended a prehearing to review the evidence with the judge.
A prehearing can help clarify the main issues before the formal proceedings begin.
During the prehearing, both parties agreed on the schedule for presenting their arguments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conference - a formal meeting for discussion
  • degree higher: hearing - a formal meeting in a court or before an official body
