myenteric plexus

noun
nerve network in the gut wall
8.3
Pronunciation: / maɪˌɛnˈtɛrɪk ˈplɛksəs / (AmE), / maɪˌɛnˈtɛrɪk ˈplɛksəs / (BrE)
a network of nerve fibers and ganglia located between the layers of the muscular wall of the gastrointestinal tract, controlling gut motility

Examples

Myenteric plexus network
Damage to the myenteric plexus can lead to severe digestive problems.
Researchers studied the structure of the myenteric plexus in patients with gastrointestinal disorders.
The myenteric plexus helps regulate the contractions that move food through the intestines.
