seeding

noun
spread of cells
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which cells, organisms, or particles spread to new locations and begin to grow, especially in the context of disease such as cancer.

Examples

Cancer cell seeding
The surgeon took precautions to prevent seeding of cancer cells during the operation.
Researchers study how seeding occurs to develop better treatments for metastatic diseases.
Early detection can help stop the seeding of harmful cells to other organs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissemination - the act of spreading something widely
  • synonym: metastasis - the spread of cancer cells from one part of the body to another
