equatorial

noun
a telescope or mounting with two axes for tracking objects
6.1 8.3
Pronunciation: /iː.kwə.ˈtɔː.r.i.əl/ /; / /e.kwə.ˈtɔː.r.i.əl/ (AmE), /e.kwə.ˈtɔː.r.i.əl/ (BrE)
an astronomical telescope or its mounting with two axes, one parallel to the Earth's axis and one perpendicular, allowing the telescope to track celestial objects as the Earth rotates

Examples

Equatorial telescope mount
The observatory recently installed a new equatorial to improve its tracking accuracy.
The old equatorial in the dome requires careful calibration before use.
Using an equatorial, astronomers can easily follow stars as they move across the night sky.
