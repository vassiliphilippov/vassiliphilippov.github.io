romanization

noun
adoption of Roman culture
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌroʊmənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrəʊmənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which people or societies adopt Roman culture, language, laws, and institutions, especially during the expansion of the Roman Empire

Examples

Romanization of Britain
The romanization of Britain introduced new laws, roads, and architecture to the region.
Historians study the romanization of conquered territories to understand cultural changes in ancient Europe.
The romanization of local customs often led to a blend of Roman and native traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acculturation - the process of cultural change and adaptation
  • synonym: assimilation - the process of becoming similar to another culture
