friar preacher

noun
Dominican friar (Catholic order)
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfraɪər ˈpriːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈfraɪə ˈpriːtʃə / (BrE)
a Roman Catholic friar who is a member of the Dominican order, distinguished by wearing a black mantle

Examples

A friar preacher in black
As a friar preacher, he spent much of his life traveling and teaching.
In medieval Europe, people often sought advice from a respected friar preacher.
The friar preacher delivered a stirring sermon on charity and humility.
