Fabian Society

proper noun
British socialist organization advocating gradual reform
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪ.bi.ən səˈsaɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.bɪ.ən səˈsaɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
A British socialist organization founded in 1884, advocating gradual and democratic reforms toward socialism.

Examples

The Fabian Society members
Many leading Labour politicians have been members of the Fabian Society.

The Fabian Society played a key role in the early development of British socialism.
