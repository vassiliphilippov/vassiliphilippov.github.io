slack

adjective
(of water) moving slowly or barely moving
2.7
Pronunciation: / slæk / (AmE), / slæk / (BrE)
not tight or firm; loose or not held tightly (especially of ropes, cables, sails, or similar objects)

Examples

Slack rope
At slack tide, the river seemed calm and almost motionless.
Fishing is often best during slack water, when the current slows before changing direction.
The boat waited for slack tide to safely pass through the narrow channel.
After the wind died down, the sails became slack and hung loosely from the mast.
He noticed his grip was slack, so he held the handle more firmly.
The clothesline was slack and needed tightening before use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: still - (of water) not flowing or moving
  • antonym: taut - pulled tight; not slack
