closely held corporation

noun
company with shares held by few owners
6.8 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààklo äsli h…õld Àåk…îÀêrp…ôÀàre…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / Ààkl…ô äsli h…õld Àåk…írp…ôÀàre…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
A corporation whose shares are owned by a small number of shareholders, with limited public trading and no intention to sell major holdings.

Examples

A closely held corporation
As a closely held corporation, its shares rarely change hands on the market.
Regulations for a closely held corporation may differ from those for publicly traded companies.
The Smith family still controls the closely held corporation they founded a century ago.

Synonyms

  • privately held corporation
    5.4 0.02/m
    privately held corporation is owned by a small group and its shares are not publicly traded, while closely held corporation stresses tight ownership.
  • private corporation
    3.7 0.50/m
    private corporation is a company whose shares are not on stock exchanges, while closely held corporation usually has few shareholders who keep large ownership.
  • close corporation
    7.3 0.00/m
    close corporation is a legal form with rules on share transfer, while closely held corporation describes a company owned by few shareholders.
  • closed corporation
    7.0 0.00/m
    closed corporation is often seen as a company not open to outside shareholders, while closely held corporation emphasizes a small group's long-term ownership.
