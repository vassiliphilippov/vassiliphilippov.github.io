acrosome

noun
structure at tip of sperm cell
Pronunciation: / ˈækrəˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈakrəsəʊm / (BrE)
a cap-like structure at the front of a sperm cell that releases enzymes to help the sperm penetrate the egg during fertilization

Examples

The acrosome cap
Damage to the acrosome can reduce the ability of sperm to fertilize an egg.
Scientists study the acrosome reaction to better understand the process of fertilization.
The acrosome contains enzymes essential for the sperm to penetrate the outer layers of the egg.
