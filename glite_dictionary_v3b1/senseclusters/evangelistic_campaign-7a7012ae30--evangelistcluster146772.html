evangelistic campaign

noun
organized effort to spread religious beliefs
Pronunciation: / ɪˌvæn.dʒəˈlɪstɪk kəmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ɪˌvan.dʒəˈlɪstɪk kəmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
an organized effort or series of activities aimed at spreading religious beliefs, especially through preaching or outreach

Examples

Church evangelistic campaign
She volunteered as a speaker during the evangelistic campaign held in the city square.
The evangelistic campaign used social media to reach a wider audience with their message.
The church organized an evangelistic campaign that lasted for two weeks across local towns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: revival - a meeting or series of meetings for the purpose of reawakening religious faith
