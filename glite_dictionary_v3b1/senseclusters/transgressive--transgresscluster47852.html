transgressive

adjective
geological sea advance
Pronunciation: / trænzˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / trænzˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
relating to a geological event where rising sea levels cause marine sediments to be deposited over older land or nearshore deposits

Examples

Transgressive sea
The transgressive layers in the rock record indicate a period of rising sea levels.
Scientists studied the transgressive sequence to understand ancient climate changes.
A transgressive event can result in marine fossils being found far inland.

Related Terms

  • antonym: regressive - relating to a geological event where sea level falls and land is exposed
