prohibitive

adjective
(of price/cost) excessively high, discouraging purchase
Pronunciation: / prəˈhɪb.ə.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / prəˈhɪb.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a price or cost) so high that it prevents people from buying or doing something.

Examples

A prohibitive price
Many medicines remain prohibitive in price for people without insurance.
Rising rents in the city have become prohibitive for many young professionals.
The cost of repairs was so prohibitive that we decided to buy a new car instead.

Related Terms

  • antonym: affordable - inexpensive; reasonably priced
  • synonym: exorbitant - (of a price or amount) unreasonably high
