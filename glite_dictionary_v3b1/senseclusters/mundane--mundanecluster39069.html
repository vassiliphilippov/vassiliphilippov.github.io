mundane

adjective
of ordinary or worldly matters
4.3 3.5
Pronunciation: /mən.ˈdeɪn/ /; / /mʌn.ˈdeɪn/ (AmE), /mʌn.ˈdeɪn/ /; / /mən.ˈdeɪn/ (BrE)
ordinary, everyday, or not special; sometimes dull or not interesting; relating to the normal world rather than something spiritual or extraordinary

Examples

A mundane task
Even the most mundane tasks can be satisfying if you do them with care.
Philosophers sometimes ignore mundane concerns in search of deeper meaning.
She was tired of her mundane daily routine and longed for adventure.
Despite its mundane appearance, the building had a fascinating history.
He found beauty in the most mundane daily tasks, like making coffee.
She dreaded returning to her mundane office routine after the excitement of her trip.
Artists found inspiration in the mundane world, seeing beauty in everyday existence.
Her interests are far from mundane; she seeks knowledge that goes beyond earthly concerns.
The ancient philosopher wrote extensively about both the spiritual and the mundane aspects of life.
