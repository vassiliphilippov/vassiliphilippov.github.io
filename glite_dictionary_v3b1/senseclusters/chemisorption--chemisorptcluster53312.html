chemisorption

noun
adsorption by chemical bonding
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛmɪˈsɔrpʃən /; / ˌkɛmɪˈsɔːrpʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɛmɪˈsɔːpʃ(ə)n /; / ˌkɛmɪˈsɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the process in which a substance attaches to the surface of another by forming chemical bonds, usually in a way that cannot be easily reversed

Examples

Chemisorption process
Because chemisorption involves stronger bonding, it is often irreversible compared to physisorption.
Researchers studied the chemisorption of sulfur dioxide on metal oxide surfaces.
The chemisorption of hydrogen on platinum is essential in many catalytic reactions.
Due to chemisorption, the process is often irreversible and involves strong chemical bonds.
The chemisorption of hydrogen on platinum is an important step in catalysis.
Understanding chemisorption is crucial for designing better catalysts in industrial reactions.
