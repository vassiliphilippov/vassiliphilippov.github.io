brownout

noun
partial service degradation to preserve availability
7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbraʊ.naʊt/ (AmE), /ˈbraʊ.naʊt/ (BrE)
(in computing and systems engineering) a deliberate, partial reduction or degradation of a digital service or system to reduce load and maintain availability, often by disabling non‑essential features or lowering quality.

Examples

System brownout
During the peak event, the platform triggered a brownout of nonessential features to stay online.
To avoid a full outage, engineers implemented a rolling brownout that reduced image quality for some users.
Our incident playbook recommends initiating a brownout when latency spikes threaten overall system availability.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: blackout - a complete loss of power or service (total outage), which is more severe than a partial degradation
  • synonym: graceful degradation - a design approach where a system continues operating with reduced functionality when parts fail or are disabled
