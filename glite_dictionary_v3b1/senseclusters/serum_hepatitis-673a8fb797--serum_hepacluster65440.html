serum hepatitis

noun
acute viral hepatitis transmitted via blood or body fluids
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪrəm hɛˈpaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪərəm hɛˈpatɪs / (BrE)
an acute form of viral hepatitis (often hepatitis B) caused by a DNA virus, transmitted through blood or body fluids and characterized by its persistence in blood serum

Examples

Serum hepatitis infection
Before improved screening, serum hepatitis was a major risk associated with blood donations.
Medical professionals take precautions to prevent the spread of serum hepatitis in hospitals.
The patient was diagnosed with serum hepatitis after receiving a contaminated blood transfusion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hepatitis B - a form of hepatitis caused by the hepatitis B virus
  • antonym: infectious hepatitis - another form of hepatitis, usually hepatitis A
