heteroscedasticity

noun
unequal statistical variance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬əroʊˌskɛːˈdæstɪsɪti / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˌskiːˈdæstɪsɪti / (BrE)
a situation in statistics where the variability of a variable is not constant across values of another variable

Examples

Heteroscedasticity in statistics
The presence of heteroscedasticity can affect the reliability of regression analysis results.
Statisticians often test for heteroscedasticity before interpreting their data models.
If heteroscedasticity is detected, adjustments may be needed to ensure valid conclusions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homoscedasticity - a situation where the variability of a variable is constant across values of another variable
  • synonym: variance - a measure of how much values in a data set differ from the mean
