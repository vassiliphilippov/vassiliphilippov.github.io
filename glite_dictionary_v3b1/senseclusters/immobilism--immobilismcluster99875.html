immobilism

noun
resistance to change
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmoʊbɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊbɪˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
a policy or tendency of remaining unchanged and resisting progress or reform

Examples

Political immobilism
The party's immobilism made it difficult to introduce any new policies.
Critics accused the administration of immobilism in the face of urgent economic problems.
Long-term immobilism can prevent a society from adapting to new challenges.
The government's immobilism frustrated citizens who demanded urgent reforms.
Critics accused the organization of immobilism in the face of obvious problems.
Periods of political immobilism can slow down a country's development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservatism - a political or social philosophy promoting traditional values and resistance to change
  • synonym: inertia - a tendency to do nothing or to remain unchanged
  • antonym: progressivism - a political or social philosophy advocating progress and reform
