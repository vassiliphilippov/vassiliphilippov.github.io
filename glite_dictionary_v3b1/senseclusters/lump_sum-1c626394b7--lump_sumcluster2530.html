lump sum

noun
a single, complete payment of money
7.2 3.1
Pronunciation: /lʌmp sʌm/ (AmE), /lʌmp sʌm/ (BrE)
an amount of money paid in a single complete payment rather than in several smaller amounts or installments

Examples

A lump sum payment
Instead of a monthly pension, he opted for a lump sum payout.
She received her inheritance as a lump sum rather than regular payments.
The contractor was paid a lump sum for completing the entire job.

Related Terms

  • antonym: installment - a sum of money due as one of several equal payments for something, spread over an agreed period of time
