grouser

noun
track traction bar
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊzər / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊzə / (BrE)
a metal bar or projection attached to the track of a vehicle to increase traction on soft or uneven ground

Examples

Grouser bar on tracks
The bulldozer's grouser helped it move through the muddy field.
Without a proper grouser, the excavator struggled to gain traction on the loose soil.
Mechanics inspected each grouser to ensure the tank could cross rough terrain safely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cleat - a projecting piece on a surface to provide grip
  • synonym: tread - the part of a tire or track that touches the ground
