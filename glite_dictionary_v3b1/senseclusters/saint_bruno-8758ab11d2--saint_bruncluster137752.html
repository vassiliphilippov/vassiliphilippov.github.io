Saint Bruno

proper noun
founder of the Carthusian order; Catholic saint
9.8
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈbruːnoʊ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈbruːnəʊ / (BrE)
A German-born French cleric (1032–1101) who founded the Carthusian order in 1084, recognized as a saint in the Roman Catholic Church.

Examples

Saint Bruno founded order
Saint Bruno founded the Carthusian order of monks in the eleventh century.
Many Catholics honor Saint Bruno on his feast day, October 6th.
The life of Saint Bruno is often discussed in lectures on monasticism.
