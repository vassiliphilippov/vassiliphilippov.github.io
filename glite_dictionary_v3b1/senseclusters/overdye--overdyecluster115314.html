overdye

noun
dye over existing color
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌdaɪ /; / ˈəʊvədaɪ / (BrE)
the process or result of dyeing fabric or material again to change, deepen, or correct its color

Examples

A deep overdye

Applying a blue overdye can transform faded jeans into a trendy new look.
She chose a red overdye to correct the uneven color on her curtains.
The artist used an overdye technique to give the fabric a unique, layered appearance.
Vintage rugs often feature an overdye that enhances their original patterns with new colors.
After the initial color faded, the shirt received an overdye to refresh its look.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dye - a substance used to change the color of something
  • synonym: redye - to dye something again, especially to change its color
