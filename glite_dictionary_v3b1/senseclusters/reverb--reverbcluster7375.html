reverb

noun
sound echo effect
6.1 0.41/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀêv…úrb / (AmE), / ÀàriÀêv…úÀêb / (BrE)
an effect in which sound continues to be heard as it reflects off surfaces after the original sound has stopped

Examples

Room reverb
The singer's voice had a warm reverb that filled the entire concert hall.
Adding reverb to the guitar track made the song sound more atmospheric.
He prefers to record vocals without any reverb for a cleaner sound.

Synonyms

  • reverberation
    6.7 0.35/m
    reverberation is the repeated sound that lingers after a note ends, while reverb is the effect engineers use to imitate that space.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry - without added sound effects such as reverb or echo
  • synonym: echo - a sound that is reflected off a surface and heard again
  • synonym: reverberation - the persistence of sound after the original sound is produced
