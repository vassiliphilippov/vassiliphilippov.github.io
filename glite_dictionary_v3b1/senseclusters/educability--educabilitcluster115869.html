educability

noun
capacity to learn
Pronunciation: / ˌɛdʒuːəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɛdjʊəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability or capacity to be educated or to learn new skills or knowledge

Examples

The educability of children
The educability of young children is often higher than that of adults.
Researchers are interested in the educability of students with different backgrounds.
Some animals show surprising educability when trained by humans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or education
  • synonym: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
  • synonym: learnability - the quality of being easy or possible to learn
