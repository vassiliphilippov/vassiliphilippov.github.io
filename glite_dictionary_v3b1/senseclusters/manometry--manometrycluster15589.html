manometry

noun
pressure measurement
7.6
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑːmətri / (AmE), / məˈnɒmətri / (BrE)
the measurement of pressure within a liquid, gas, or body cavity, often for medical purposes

Examples

Manometry test
The doctor recommended manometry to evaluate the patient's swallowing difficulties.
During the procedure, manometry was used to measure the pressure inside the esophagus.
Advances in manometry have improved the diagnosis of digestive disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barometry - the measurement of atmospheric pressure
  • synonym: sphygmomanometry - the measurement of blood pressure
