homoiousian

adjective
theological doctrine
9.9
Pronunciation: /hɑː.mɔɪ.ˈuː.zi.ən/ (AmE), /hɒ.mɔɪ.ˈuː.zi.ən/ (BrE)
relating to the belief that the Son is of a similar but not identical substance to the Father in Christian theology

Examples

Homoiousian doctrine
The council debated the homoiousian position, which argued for a similar but not identical divine nature.
Some early theologians were labeled homoiousian because of their nuanced views on the Trinity.
The homoiousian doctrine was influential during the theological controversies of the fourth century.
