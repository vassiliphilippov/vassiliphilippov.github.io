scavenge

verb
remove unwanted substances
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈskævɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈskævɪndʒ / (BrE)
to remove unwanted or harmful substances from a place, material, or system, especially as part of a cleaning or purifying process

Examples

Chemicals scavenge toxins
Certain chemicals can scavenge free radicals in the body, reducing cell damage.
Engineers developed a system to scavenge sulfur dioxide from industrial emissions.
The filter is designed to scavenge impurities from the water supply.
