ironism

noun
skeptical irony stance
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.ər.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈaɪr.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.ən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈaɪ.ə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a philosophical or literary attitude characterized by deliberate, sustained irony and skepticism toward fixed beliefs or systems

Examples

Philosophical ironism
The philosopher's ironism led her to question every established tradition in her field.
Literary critics often debate whether ironism undermines or enriches the meaning of a text.
His essays are marked by a persistent ironism that challenges readers to reconsider their assumptions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dogmatism - the tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true
  • synonym: irony - the use of words to express something different from their literal meaning
  • synonym: skepticism - an attitude of doubting accepted beliefs or claims
