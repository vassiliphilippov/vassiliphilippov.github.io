mannerist

noun
person with affected style
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmæn.ər.ɪst/ (AmE), /ˈmæn.ər.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who displays affected, artificial, or exaggerated manners or gestures

Examples

A mannerist performer
The actor was criticized as a mannerist for his overly dramatic performances.
Some people found her a mannerist because of her exaggerated hand gestures.
He was labeled a mannerist due to his artificial way of speaking in public.

Related Terms

  • antonym: naturalist - a person who behaves in a natural and unaffected way
  • synonym: poseur - a person who pretends to be something they are not, often for effect
