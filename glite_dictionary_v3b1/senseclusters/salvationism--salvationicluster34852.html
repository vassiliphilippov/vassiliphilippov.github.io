salvationism

noun
belief in salvation
Pronunciation: / sælˈveɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / sælˈveɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in or promotion of salvation, especially as a central principle in religion

Examples

Religious salvationism
The church's teachings are deeply influenced by salvationism and the promise of eternal life.
Critics argue that salvationism can sometimes overshadow other aspects of religious practice.
Her interest in salvationism led her to study different world religions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: redemption - the act of being saved from sin or evil
  • antonym: secularism - the belief that religion should not play a role in government or public life
