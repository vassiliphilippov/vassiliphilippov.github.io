free

verb
grant exemption from a rule or duty
4.6
Pronunciation: / friː / (AmE), / friː / (BrE)
to release someone from a duty, rule, requirement, obligation, blame, or responsibility; to grant an exemption or absolve

Examples

To free from duty
Employees may be freed from mandatory training if they already have certification.
He was freed from jury duty due to a conflict of interest.
The school freed her from taking the final exam because of her illness.
He hopes to free himself of debt by the end of the year.
The agreement will free her from all contractual obligations.
The new law frees small businesses from unnecessary regulations.
Her apology did not free her of her responsibilities.
The court's decision will free the company from further liability.
The evidence helped free him from all suspicion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolve - to set someone free from blame or responsibility
  • synonym: exempt - to officially release (someone) from an obligation
  • synonym: exonerate - to clear someone from blame or responsibility
  • synonym: release - to set free from confinement or obligation
