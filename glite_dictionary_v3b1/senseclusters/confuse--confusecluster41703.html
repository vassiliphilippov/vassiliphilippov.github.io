confuse

verb
to mistake one thing for another
1.9 8.65/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàfjuz / (AmE), / k…ônÀàfjuÀêz / (BrE)
to think wrongly that one person or thing is another; to mistake one thing for another

Examples

To confuse names
Don't confuse kindness with weakness.
I always confuse her name with her sister's.
Some people confuse Austria with Australia.

Synonyms

  • mistake
    2.6 4.58/m
    mistake means wrongly identify someone or something, while confuse often means think two things are the same or make things unclear.
  • misidentify
    6.5 0.62/m
    misidentify means to identify someone or something incorrectly, while confuse also suggests mixing people or things in your mind.
  • misrecognize
    8.1 0.05/m
    misrecognize means to identify someone wrongly from memory, while confuse is broader and can mean mix up different things.
  • confound
    7.3 2.75/m
    confound means to mix up or baffle, often in formal writing, while confuse is a more general everyday mixing or wrong identification.
  • mix up
    2.7 2.06/m
    mix up means to put things or people in the wrong order or mix them, while confuse focuses on thinking one is the other.
