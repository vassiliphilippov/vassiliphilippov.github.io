incubus

noun
male demon in folklore; attacks sleepers
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋkjəˌbəs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkjʊbəs / (BrE)
(in folklore) a male demon believed to lie upon sleeping persons, especially women, and engage in sexual activity with them.

Examples

A demon incubus
According to legend, an incubus visits women in their sleep, causing disturbing nightmares.
During the Middle Ages, people believed that an incubus was responsible for unexplained pregnancies.
The old book details rituals to ward off an incubus from a sleeping household.

Related Terms

  • antonym: succubus - a female demon believed to have sexual intercourse with sleeping men
