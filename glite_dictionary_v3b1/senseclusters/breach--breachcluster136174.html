breach

noun
a break in relations
5.2 1.45/m
Pronunciation: / briÀêt É / (AmE), / briÀêt É / (BrE)
a break in friendly relations between people or groups, often resulting in tension or conflict

Examples

A breach between friends
After years of trust, a breach developed between the partners, making collaboration nearly impossible.
Diplomats worked tirelessly to heal the breach between the neighboring countries.
The family tried to mend the breach that had divided them for decades.
