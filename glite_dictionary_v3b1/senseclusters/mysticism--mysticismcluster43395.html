mysticism

noun
belief in direct spiritual communion
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪstɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
a belief system or religious practice centered on direct, personal communion with an ultimate reality or the divine, often through contemplation or spiritual experiences

Examples

Religious mysticism
She devoted her life to studying mysticism and the pursuit of spiritual enlightenment.
Some critics argue that mysticism offers answers that logic and science cannot provide.
The poetry of Rumi often explores themes of mysticism and union with the divine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rationalism - belief in reason as the chief source of knowledge
  • synonym: spiritualism - belief in communication with spiritual entities
