cleave

verb
split or cut with a sharp instrument
6.1
Pronunciation: / kliːv / (AmE), / kliːv / (BrE)
to split or cut something, especially with a sharp tool or by force

Examples

To cleave wood
She cleaved the ice with the blade to create a fishing hole.
The chef skillfully cleaves the chicken along the backbone before roasting.
Using a heavy axe, he cleaved the log in two with a single blow.
Lightning can sometimes cleave tree trunks cleanly down the middle.
Rivers gradually cleave deep valleys through mountains over millions of years.
The sculptor used a chisel to cleave the marble in two.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut - to divide something with a sharp instrument
  • antonym: join - to put or bring two or more things together
  • synonym: sever - to cut something into two or more pieces
  • synonym: split - to divide or break something into parts
