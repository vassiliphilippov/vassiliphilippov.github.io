cleave

verb
split or cut with a sharp instrument
8.0 0.67/m
Pronunciation: / kliÀêv / (AmE), / kliÀêv / (BrE)
to split or cut something, especially with a sharp tool or by force

Examples

To cleave wood
She cleaved the ice with the blade to create a fishing hole.
The chef skillfully cleaves the chicken along the backbone before roasting.
Using a heavy axe, he cleaved the log in two with a single blow.
Lightning can sometimes cleave tree trunks cleanly down the middle.
Rivers gradually cleave deep valleys through mountains over millions of years.
The sculptor used a chisel to cleave the marble in two.

Synonyms

  • split
    1.3 25/m
    split is a common word for breaking something into parts, while cleave suggests cutting with a tool or strong force.
  • rive
    9.8 0.02/m
    rive is a forceful, old-fashioned verb for tearing something apart with a sharp tool, while cleave is a general term for cutting or splitting.
  • sever
    5.6 4.96/m
    sever means cutting something completely away from a whole, while cleave focuses on splitting or cutting, not always removing part.

Related Terms
