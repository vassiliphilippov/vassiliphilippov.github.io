gullibility

noun
tendency to be easily deceived
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɡʌləˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɡʌlɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the tendency to believe things too readily and therefore to be easily deceived or tricked

Examples

High gullibility
His gullibility made him the perfect target for the scam artist.
Stories like that test the gullibility of even the most skeptical listeners.
The magician relied on the gullibility of the audience to perform his tricks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credulity - willingness to believe or trust too readily
  • antonym: suspicion - tendency to distrust or doubt others
