near-blind

adjective
having very poor vision; almost blind
Pronunciation: / nɪr ˈblaɪnd / (AmE), / nɪə ˈblaɪnd / (BrE)
Having greatly reduced vision; almost blind.

Examples

A near-blind person
After the accident, he was near-blind and required special assistance to navigate the house.
Despite being near-blind, he continues to pursue his passion for painting.
She has been near-blind since childhood due to a rare eye condition.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: blind - unable to see; lacking sight
  • degree lower: partially sighted - having limited but not completely lost vision
