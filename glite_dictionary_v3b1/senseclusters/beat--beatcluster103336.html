beat

noun
a stroke or blow; rhythmic unit
3.2 1.0
Pronunciation: /biːt/ (AmE), /biːt/ (BrE)
a regular rhythm, pulse, or pattern of sound or movement, especially in music, poetry, or the body

Examples

A strong beat
She gave the door three sharp beats with her fist to get attention.
The clock chimed with each beat of the hour.
The drummer kept a steady beat throughout the song.
After running up the stairs, she could feel her own beat quicken in her chest.
She listened carefully to the steady beat of his heart through the stethoscope.
The monitor displayed every irregular beat during the patient's examination.
In iambic pentameter, every second syllable receives the beat.
She practiced reciting the poem, placing emphasis on each beat.
The beat in each line helps establish the poem's rhythm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frequency - the rate at which a repetitive event occurs
  • synonym: oscillation - a regular movement back and forth
  • synonym: pulse - the regular throbbing of arteries as blood is propelled through them, especially as felt at the wrist or neck
  • synonym: rhythm - a strong, regular, repeated pattern of movement or sound
  • synonym: stress - emphasis placed on a syllable in speech or verse
  • synonym: stroke - a single movement of hitting or striking something
  • synonym: tap - a light, usually repeated, blow or stroke
  • synonym: thump - a dull sound made by a heavy blow
  • antonym: unstressed - a syllable or beat without strong emphasis in poetry
