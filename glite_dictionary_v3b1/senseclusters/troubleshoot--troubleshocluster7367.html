troubleshoot

verb
identify and solve problems
2.1 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈtrʌb.əl.ʃuːt/ (AmE), /ˈtrʌb.əl.ʃuːt/ (BrE)
to find and fix problems, especially with technical or mechanical things

Examples

To troubleshoot a device
He spent the entire afternoon troubleshooting the printer until it finally worked.

If you can't connect to the internet, try troubleshooting your network settings first.
She often troubleshoots complex software problems for her colleagues.
The technician was called in to troubleshoot the server issue right away.
