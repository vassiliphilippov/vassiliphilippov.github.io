consume

verb
to absorb or occupy fully
5.3 15/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàsuÀêm / (AmE), / k…ônÀàsjuÀêm / (BrE)
(of an activity, feeling, or influence) to completely fill or occupy someone's attention, time, or energy; to engross fully.

Examples

Consumed by worry
Grief consumed him after the loss of his friend.
He was so consumed by his passion for art that he forgot about everything else.
The project consumed all her free time for several months.

Synonyms

  • engross
    5.8 1.80/m
    engross holds someone's full attention for a long time, while consume suggests filling someone's time, energy, or mind completely.
  • absorb
    3.1 10/m
    absorb draws all your attention in like a gripping book, while consume means to use up time, energy, or thought fully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engross - to absorb all the attention or interest of someone
  • synonym: preoccupy - to dominate or fill the mind to the exclusion of other thoughts
