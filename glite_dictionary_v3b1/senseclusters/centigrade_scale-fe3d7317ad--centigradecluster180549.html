centigrade scale

noun
temperature scale with 0–100 for water
7.6 3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.tɪˌɡreɪd skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɛntɪɡreɪd skeɪl / (BrE)
a temperature scale in which the freezing point of water is 0 degrees and the boiling point is 100 degrees, now more commonly known as the Celsius scale

Examples

The centigrade scale
Many scientific experiments record temperatures using the centigrade scale for consistency.
The centigrade scale is widely used in most countries outside the United States.
Water boils at 100 degrees on the centigrade scale under standard atmospheric pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Celsius scale - the temperature scale with 0 as the freezing and 100 as the boiling point of water, synonymous with centigrade scale
  • antonym: Fahrenheit scale - a temperature scale where water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F
