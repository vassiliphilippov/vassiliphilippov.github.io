misdiagnose

verb
to diagnose incorrectly
6.1
Pronunciation: /mɪs.daɪ.əɡ.ˈnoʊz/ /; / /mɪs.dɪ.əɡ.ˈnoʊz/ (AmE), /mɪs.daɪ.əɡ.ˈnəʊz/ /; / /mɪs.dɪ.əɡ.ˈnəʊz/ (BrE)
to identify an illness or problem incorrectly, especially in a medical context

Examples

To misdiagnose a disease
It is dangerous when doctors misdiagnose a serious condition as something minor.
The rare disease was misdiagnosed for years before the correct treatment began.
Sometimes symptoms are so similar that even experts can misdiagnose the problem.
The doctor misdiagnosed her condition, leading to unnecessary treatment.
Rare diseases are often misdiagnosed because their symptoms resemble more common illnesses.
He was misdiagnosed with asthma when he actually had a heart problem.
If doctors misdiagnose a condition, patients may not receive the right treatment.
The rare disease was misdiagnosed as a common cold for several weeks.
It is important not to misdiagnose symptoms that could indicate a serious illness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diagnose - to identify an illness or problem correctly
  • synonym: misinterpret - to understand or explain something incorrectly
