rumen

noun
first stomach compartment of a ruminant
9.9 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàruÀê.m…ôn / (AmE), / ÀàruÀê.m…ôn / (BrE)
the first compartment of the stomach in ruminant animals, where food is collected and later regurgitated for further chewing as cud

Examples

The rumen of cow
After swallowing its food, the cow will regurgitate it from the rumen and chew it as cud.
Cattle rely on the rumen to break down tough plant fibers into nutrients they can digest.
Veterinarians studied the microbial activity in the sheep's rumen to improve digestion efficiency.

Synonyms

  • first stomach
    8.3 0.00/m
    first stomach is a plain, everyday name farmers or students use for that part, while rumen is the technical name used in science.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: omasum - the third compartment of a ruminant's stomach, which absorbs water and nutrients
  • degree higher: reticulum - the second compartment of a ruminant's stomach, involved in food regurgitation
