Rushmore

proper noun
mountain with presidential sculptures in South Dakota, USA
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌʃˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃmɔː / (BrE)
A mountain in the Black Hills of South Dakota, famous for the massive sculptures of the faces of four U.S. presidents: Washington, Jefferson, Lincoln, and Roosevelt.

Examples

Rushmore mountain
Many tourists visit Rushmore every year to see the giant presidential carvings.
The construction of Rushmore began in 1927 and took over a decade to complete.
The film included a dramatic scene set against the backdrop of Rushmore.
