hollowness

noun
lack of sincerity or truthfulness
7.1 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉ‘loÊŠnÉ™s /; / \u02c8h\u0251lo\u028an\u0259s / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ’lÉ™nÉ™s /; / \u02c8h\u0252l\u0259n\u0259s / (BrE)
a lack of sincerity, meaning, or emotional fulfillment; emptiness inside a person or in words

Examples

Hollowness of words
After years of working there, she grew tired of the hollowness of corporate promises.
He saw the hollowness behind her friendly smile and empty compliments.
The politician's speech was full of hollowness and failed to convince the audience.
After achieving every goal, she could not shake the deep hollowness inside her.
He spoke about his travel plans, but a quiet hollowness softened his voice.
Therapy helped her name the growing hollowness and begin to rebuild meaning.

Synonyms

  • emptiness
    5.3 4.10/m
    emptiness feels like a hollow space inside a person or words, while hollowness means lack of feeling or truth.
  • vacuity
    9.2 0.08/m
    vacuity shows an empty state of ideas or content, while hollowness also covers a lack of feeling or sincerity.
  • vacuousness
    9.2 0.02/m
    vacuousness describes speech or thought that lacks sense or depth, while hollowness can mean empty words or empty feelings.
  • meaninglessness
    7.6 0.69/m
    meaninglessness says something has no purpose or value, while hollowness often also suggests inner emptiness or false warmth.
  • insincerity
    5.6 0.33/m
    insincerity shows false or staged feelings toward others, while hollowness points to inner emptiness or lack of true meaning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emptiness - a feeling or state of having nothing inside, often used for emotional void or lack of meaning
  • antonym: fulfillment - a sense of satisfaction, purpose, or meaningful achievement; opposite of inner emptiness
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and truthful
  • synonym: insincerity - lack of sincerity or genuineness
