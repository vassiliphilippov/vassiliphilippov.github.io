suboscine

noun
passerine bird group
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈɑːsaɪn / (AmE), / sʌbˈɒsaɪn / (BrE)
a bird belonging to a group of passerines with less complex vocal organs than oscines

Examples

A suboscine species
The tyrant flycatcher is a well-known suboscine native to the Americas.
Scientists study the vocalizations of suboscines to understand bird evolution.
Unlike oscines, suboscines have simpler syrinx structures and less varied songs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oscine - a songbird with complex vocal organs
  • synonym: passerine - a bird of the order Passeriformes, including perching birds
