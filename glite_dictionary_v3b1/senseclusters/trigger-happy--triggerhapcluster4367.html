trigger-happy

adjective
eager or ready to use violence or act rashly
5.0 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtrɪɡ.ər ˈhæp.iː/ /; / /ˈtrɪɡ.ər ˈhæp.ɪ/ (AmE), /ˈtrɪɡ.ə ˈhæp.iː/ /; / /ˈtrɪɡ.ə ˈhæp.ɪ/ (BrE)
eager or ready to use violence or force, especially with firearms; acting aggressively or without thinking

Examples

Trigger-happy soldier
His trigger-happy attitude in meetings made teamwork challenging for everyone involved.
Some politicians fear trigger-happy police responses could escalate the situation unnecessarily.
The soldier was accused of being trigger-happy during patrols in the tense border region.
He grew concerned about his trigger-happy neighbor after hearing frequent gunshots.
Some critics say the police department has a trigger-happy reputation.
The general warned his troops not to become trigger-happy during the tense standoff.

  • synonym: reckless - without care for consequences or danger
