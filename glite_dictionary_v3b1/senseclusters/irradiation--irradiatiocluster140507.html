irradiation

noun
treatment of disease with radiation
Pronunciation: / ˌɪˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən /; / ɪˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən /; / ɪˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of exposing something or someone to radiation, especially for medical treatment or experiments

Examples

Medical irradiation treatment
Advancements in irradiation techniques have reduced side effects compared to older radiation therapies.
After her diagnosis, the patient received irradiation as part of her cancer treatment plan.
Doctors discussed the benefits and risks of irradiation with the patient's family before starting the treatment.
After irradiation therapy, the patient must be monitored for side effects.
Prolonged irradiation of cells can cause significant genetic damage.
The food industry uses irradiation to preserve and sterilize products.

  • synonym: radiotherapy - the treatment of cancer and other diseases using radiation
