foolishly

adverb
in a foolish manner
2.8 2.07/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfuÀêl…™ Éli / (AmE), / ÀàfuÀêl…™ Éli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of good sense or judgment.

Examples

To foolishly trust
He foolishly trusted the stranger despite the obvious warning signs.
She foolishly spent all her savings on unnecessary gadgets.
They foolishly ignored the weather forecast and went hiking in the storm.

Synonyms

  • unwisely
    4.1 0.40/m
    unwisely is used for choices that lack careful thought or sense, while foolishly shows silly or thoughtless behavior.
  • injudiciously
    8.4 0.03/m
    injudiciously is a formal word for acting without good judgment or care, while foolishly sounds more casual and silly.
  • imprudently
    7.7 0.05/m
    imprudently means acting without prudence and ignoring consequences, while foolishly suggests silly or unwise choices in general.
  • incautiously
    7.0 0.05/m
    incautiously describes acting with careless lack of caution or heed, while foolishly highlights poor sense or silly judgment.
  • rashly
    5.6 0.32/m
    rashly means acting quickly and recklessly without thought, while foolishly can mean unwise choices but not always hasty.
