retarded blowback

noun
firearms operating system with delayed bolt movement
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɑrdɪd ˈbloʊˌbæk / (AmE), / rɪˈtɑːdɪd ˈbləʊˌbæk / (BrE)
a firearms operating system in which the bolt must overcome initial resistance before moving, though it is not fully locked

Examples

Retarded blowback system
Designers chose retarded blowback to handle higher-pressure cartridges safely.
Some submachine guns are famous for their reliable retarded blowback systems.
The firearm uses a retarded blowback mechanism to control the rate of fire.
