unbundling

noun
separating products
5.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbʌndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈbʌndlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of separating parts of a product or service that were previously sold together, so they can be offered individually

Examples

Unbundling services
The streaming industry has seen a trend toward unbundling as customers want to choose specific channels.
Regulations required the unbundling of telecommunications services to increase competition.
After unbundling its software suite, the company allowed users to purchase only the programs they needed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bundling - the practice of selling several products or services together as a single combined unit
  • synonym: separation - the act of dividing or splitting something into parts
