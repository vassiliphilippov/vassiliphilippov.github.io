Krampus

proper noun
Christmas folklore figure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkræmpəs / (AmE), / ˈkrampʊs / (BrE)
a mythical creature in Central European folklore who punishes misbehaving children during the Christmas season

Examples

The Krampus parade
In Austria, people dress as Krampus during holiday parades.
The legend says that Krampus visits naughty children on the night of December 5th.
Many families tell stories about Krampus to encourage good behavior before Christmas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Belsnickel - a Christmas figure in German folklore who visits children before Christmas
  • antonym: Santa Claus - a legendary figure who brings gifts to well-behaved children at Christmas
