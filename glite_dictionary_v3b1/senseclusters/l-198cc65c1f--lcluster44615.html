L

noun
cgs unit of illumination (lambert)
9.9
Pronunciation: /el/ (AmE), /el/ (BrE)
a cgs unit of illumination equal to the brightness of a perfectly diffusing surface that emits or reflects one lumen per square centimeter; also called a lambert

Examples

One L of brightness
A value of one L is equivalent to one lambert in photometry.
Older textbooks often refer to L as a standard unit of luminance.
The brightness of the screen was measured at 2 L to ensure accurate calibration.
