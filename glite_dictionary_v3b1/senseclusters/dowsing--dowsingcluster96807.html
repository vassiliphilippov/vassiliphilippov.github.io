dowsing

noun
searching for water or minerals with rods
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of searching for underground water, minerals, or other hidden substances using a divining rod or similar tool

Examples

Dowsing for water
A demonstration of dowsing attracted a crowd at the local science fair.
Many people in rural areas have used dowsing to locate wells for generations.
Skeptics question the effectiveness of dowsing for finding underground resources.
