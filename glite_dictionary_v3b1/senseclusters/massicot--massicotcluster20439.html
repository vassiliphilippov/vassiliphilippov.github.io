massicot

noun
yellow mineral form of lead monoxide; pigment
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmæsɪˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈmæsɪkɒt / (BrE)
A yellow mineral form of lead monoxide (PbO), used as a pigment and in making glass and ceramics.

Examples

Yellow massicot pigment
Because massicot contains lead, handling it requires proper safety precautions.
The artist preferred to use massicot as a pigment in her yellow paints.
Traces of massicot were found in the ancient Roman glass workshop.
