radioactive iodine uptake test

noun
test measuring thyroid iodine absorption
5.1 4.7
Pronunciation: /reɪ.diː.oʊ.ˈæk.tɪv ˈaɪ.ə.daɪn ˈʌp.teɪk test/ (AmE), /reɪ.diː.əʊ.ˈæk.tɪv ˈaɪ.ə.daɪn ˈʌp.teɪk test/ (BrE)
a medical test that measures how much radioactive iodine is absorbed by the thyroid gland to help assess its function

Examples

The radioactive iodine uptake test result
After taking the radioactive iodine uptake test, he waited for his specialist to explain the results.
Doctors often use the radioactive iodine uptake test to diagnose the cause of hyperthyroidism.
The radioactive iodine uptake test showed that her thyroid gland was overactive.
