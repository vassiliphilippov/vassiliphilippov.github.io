Cottonian

noun
manuscript from Cotton library
Pronunciation: / kəˈtoʊniən / (AmE), / kɒˈtəʊniən / (BrE)
a manuscript or book that is part of the Cottonian library, the collection formed by Sir Robert Cotton

Examples

A Cottonian book
The British Library houses several important Cottonian manuscripts dating back to the Middle Ages.
Scholars traveled from around the world to study a rare Cottonian in the collection.
The archivist carefully removed a Cottonian from its protective cover for examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collection - a group of objects or works gathered together, often for study or display
  • synonym: manuscript - a handwritten or typed document, especially one of historical value
