substructural

adjective
restricting logical rules
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbstrʌkˈtʃʊrəl / (AmE), / ˌsʌbstrʌkˈtʃʊərəl / (BrE)
relating to logical systems or theories that restrict or omit certain standard structural rules in proofs, such as weakening, contraction, or exchange.

Examples

Substructural logic rules
The professor explained how substructural logics differ from classical logic in their treatment of inference rules.
Research in substructural proof theory has led to new insights in computer science and linguistics.
Some substructural systems do not allow the duplication of assumptions in proofs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: structural - relating to systems that include all standard structural rules in logic
