Sabin vaccine

noun
oral vaccine against polio
4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈseɪ.bɪn ˈvæk.siːn/ /; / /ˈseɪ.bɪn væk.ˈsiːn/ (AmE), /ˈseɪ.bɪn ˈvæk.siːn/ (BrE)
An oral vaccine containing live but weakened poliovirus, used to provide immunity to poliomyelitis (polio).

Examples

The Sabin vaccine oral
Many countries used the Sabin vaccine during mass immunization campaigns in the 20th century.
The Sabin vaccine played a major role in reducing polio cases worldwide.
Unlike the injectable Salk vaccine, the Sabin vaccine is administered orally.
