gigue

noun
lively Baroque dance in triple time
8.4
Pronunciation: / ʒiɡ / (AmE), / ʒiːɡ / (BrE)
A lively dance originating in the Baroque period, typically performed in triple time, or a piece of music written for such a dance.

Examples

A gigue dance
Many Baroque composers included a gigue in their works as a lively finale.
She danced a gigue with infectious enthusiasm at the festival.
The last movement of the suite was a fast gigue that brought the performance to a joyful close.
