upsloping

adjective
rising upward
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsloʊpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌsləʊpɪŋ / (BrE)
rising or slanting upward from a lower to a higher point, or increasing steadily (such as a path, line, or trend)

Examples

Upsloping path
The upsloping curve on the chart indicated a steady increase in profits over the quarter.
We hiked along an upsloping path that became steeper as we neared the summit.
An upsloping trend in housing prices has continued for several consecutive months.
The upsloping path made the walk to the summit more difficult.
We built our house on an upsloping lot to get a better view of the valley.
The upsloping driveway can be slippery in winter weather.

  • antonym: descending - moving or going downward
