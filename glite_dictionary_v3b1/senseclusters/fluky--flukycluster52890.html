fluky

adjective
happening by chance or luck
Pronunciation: / ˈfluːki / (AmE), / ˈfluːki / (BrE)
Happening by good or bad luck rather than through planning or intention; due to chance.

Examples

A fluky goal
A fluky goal bounced off the defender and into the net, surprising everyone.
She admitted that her high test score was a bit fluky since several guessed answers turned out to be correct.
The team's victory was seen as fluky because their opponents missed several easy shots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chancy - depending on chance; uncertain
  • degree higher: fortunate - happening because of good luck
  • antonym: unlucky - affected by bad luck
