rhema

noun
personal divine message
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪmə / (AmE), / ˈriːmə / (BrE)
in Christian theology, a specific message or revelation believed to be personally received from God

Examples

Personal rhema
Many believers pray for a rhema to help guide them through difficult times.
He shared the rhema he felt God had spoken to him during his morning meditation.
The pastor explained the difference between a rhema and the written scriptures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: logos - in Christian theology, the written or universal word of God
  • synonym: revelation - a divine or supernatural disclosure to humans
