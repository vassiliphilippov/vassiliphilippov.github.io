adopt

verb
to take up and use (idea, method, practice)
2.6 2.7
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈdɑːpt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdɒpt/ (BrE)
to start to use or follow a new idea, method, policy, or practice

Examples

To adopt a policy
After much debate, the committee decided to adopt the new safety regulations.
Many people quickly adopt new technologies to make their lives easier.
She was inspired by the philosophy and chose to adopt it as her own.
After much deliberation, the company decided to adopt a new management strategy.
Many immigrants adopt the customs of their new country over time.
She was inspired to adopt a vegetarian lifestyle after reading several books on the subject.
After much debate, the board decided to adopt a new policy on remote work.
Many scientists were quick to adopt the new theory after the evidence was published.
Some countries adopt strict environmental regulations to combat pollution.
