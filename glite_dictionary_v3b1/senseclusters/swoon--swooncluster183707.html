swoon

verb
to faint from emotion or weakness
9.9
Pronunciation: / swun / (AmE), / swuːn / (BrE)
to faint, especially from extreme emotion or physical weakness

Examples

To swoon from heat
As the heat rose in the crowded hall, several people started swooning and needed assistance.
She was so overcome with joy that she began to swoon in his arms.
The sight of blood made him swoon unexpectedly during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: faint - to lose consciousness for a short time because of a temporarily insufficient supply of oxygen to the brain
