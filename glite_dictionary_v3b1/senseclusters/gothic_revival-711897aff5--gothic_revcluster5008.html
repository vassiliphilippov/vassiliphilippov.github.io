Gothic revival

noun
architectural style inspired by medieval Gothic
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːθɪk rɪˈvaɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk rɪˈvaɪvəl / (BrE)
an architectural style inspired by medieval Gothic architecture, popular in the 18th and 19th centuries, especially in Britain and the United States

Examples

Gothic revival architecture style
He researched the impact of Gothic revival architects on urban development.
Many churches built in the 19th century show clear influences of the Gothic revival style.
The Gothic revival transformed city skylines with its pointed arches and ornate details.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Neoclassical - architectural style based on classical Greek and Roman forms
