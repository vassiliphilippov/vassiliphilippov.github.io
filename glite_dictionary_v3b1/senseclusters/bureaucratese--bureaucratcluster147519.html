bureaucratese

noun
official jargon
9.9
Pronunciation: / bjʊˌrɑːkrəˈtiz / (AmE), / bjʊəˌrɒkrəˈtiːz / (BrE)
language that is overly formal, complicated, or filled with jargon, especially in official or government documents

Examples

Official bureaucratese language
The report was so full of bureaucratese that most people could not understand it.
She rewrote the policy to remove the bureaucratese and make it clearer for everyone.
Critics complained that the mayor's speech was just more bureaucratese with no real answers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jargon - specialized language used by a particular group
  • antonym: plain English - clear and simple language that is easy to understand
