oriole

noun
American songbird with bright plumage
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːriˌoʊl /; / ˈɔriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪəʊl / (BrE)
a brightly colored songbird, usually with black and orange or yellow feathers, found in the Americas and tropical regions

Examples

A bright oriole bird
A bright oriole perched on the branch, its orange feathers gleaming in the sunlight.

The Baltimore oriole is known for its striking coloration and distinctive song.
In spring, orioles migrate north to their breeding grounds.
The male oriole is easily recognized by its striking orange and black feathers.
We hung fruit from the tree to attract an oriole to our garden.
