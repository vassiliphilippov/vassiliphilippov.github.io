irrigator

noun
medical flushing device
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪˌɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a medical device used to wash or flush wounds, body cavities, or surgical sites with fluid

Examples

Wound irrigator
The nurse used a sterile irrigator to clean the patient's wound before bandaging it.
A irrigator is often required to flush out debris from surgical sites during procedures.
Doctors recommend using an irrigator to gently rinse body cavities after certain operations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catheter - a tube inserted into the body to remove or deliver fluids
  • synonym: syringe - a device used to inject or withdraw fluids
