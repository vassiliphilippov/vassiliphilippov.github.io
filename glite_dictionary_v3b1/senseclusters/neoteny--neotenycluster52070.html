neoteny

noun
retention of juvenile traits in adults
9.9 9.1
Pronunciation: /niː.ˈɑː.tə.niː/ /; / /niː.ˈoʊ.tə.niː/ (AmE), /niː.ˈɒ.tə.niː/ /; / /niː.ˈəʊ.tə.niː/ (BrE)
the retention of juvenile features in the adult form of an animal, especially as a result of delayed development

Examples

Neoteny in animals
Axolotls are a classic example of neoteny, retaining their juvenile gills into adulthood.
Some scientists argue that human evolution involves neoteny, as adults keep childlike skull features.
The process of neoteny allows certain species to adapt quickly to environmental changes.
