preoptic

noun
brain region before optic chiasm
Pronunciation: / priˈɑptɪk / (AmE), / priˈɒptɪk / (BrE)
the region of the brain located in front of the optic chiasm, involved in regulating body temperature, sleep, and reproductive behavior

Examples

The preoptic controls temperature
The preoptic plays a crucial role in maintaining the body's internal temperature.
Damage to the preoptic can disrupt normal sleep patterns and reproductive behaviors.
Scientists studied the preoptic to better understand how the brain regulates basic physiological functions.
