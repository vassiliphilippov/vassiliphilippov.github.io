perjury

noun
the crime of lying under oath
8.2 3.54/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úrd í…ôri / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀêd í…ôri / (BrE)
The crime of deliberately making false statements while under oath.

Examples

Committing perjury
Committing perjury can result in a lengthy prison sentence.
Laws against perjury are essential to ensure the integrity of the legal system.
The witness was charged with perjury after lying on the stand.

Synonyms

  • lying under oath
    8.9 0.43/m
    lying under oath is the plain phrase for saying false things in court, while perjury is the formal legal name for that crime.
  • bearing false witness
    8.8 0.19/m
    bearing false witness sounds formal or religious and refers to testifying falsely in legal or sacred settings, whereas perjury names the criminal act.

Related Terms

  • synonym: falsehood - an untrue statement; the act of lying
  • antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being honest and not lying
