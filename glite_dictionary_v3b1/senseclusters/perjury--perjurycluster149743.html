perjury

noun
the crime of lying under oath
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
The crime of deliberately making false statements while under oath.

Examples

Committing perjury
Committing perjury can result in a lengthy prison sentence.
Laws against perjury are essential to ensure the integrity of the legal system.
The witness was charged with perjury after lying on the stand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: falsehood - an untrue statement; the act of lying
  • antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being honest and not lying
