ritard

noun
gradual slowing in music
9.0
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈtɑːrd/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈtɑːd/ (BrE)
a musical direction to slow down the tempo gradually

Examples

Ritard marking
The orchestra followed the ritard perfectly, creating a dramatic ending to the piece.
As the choir approached the final note, the conductor indicated a ritard for added emphasis.
The pianist marked a ritard in the score to highlight the transition to the coda.
The conductor signaled a ritard before the final chord of the symphony.
Musicians must watch closely for the ritard marked in the sheet music.
A well-placed ritard can add emotional depth to a performance.

  • synonym: ritardando - a gradual decrease in tempo in music
