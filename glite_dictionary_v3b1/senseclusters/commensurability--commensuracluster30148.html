commensurability

noun
comparability by standard
Pronunciation: / kəˌmɛnsərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / kəˌmɛnsjʊrəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being measurable or comparable by the same standard or scale

Examples

Commensurability of standards
The commensurability of the two currencies made international trade much easier.
Scientists questioned the commensurability of the results from different experiments.
Without commensurability, it is difficult to compare the effectiveness of the two methods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comparability - the quality of being able to be compared
  • antonym: incommensurability - the quality of not being measurable by the same standard
