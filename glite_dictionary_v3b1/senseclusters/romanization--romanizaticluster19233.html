romanization

noun
writing in Latin alphabet
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌroʊmənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrəʊmənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of writing a language or converting text using the Roman (Latin) alphabet instead of its original script

Examples

Romanization of Chinese
The romanization of Chinese makes it easier for foreigners to learn the language.
Many street signs in Korea include both Hangul and its romanization for tourists.
Different systems of romanization can lead to various spellings of the same Japanese word.
The romanization of Chinese names helps English speakers pronounce them more easily.
Many language learners rely on romanization before mastering a new script.
The guide included both the original script and its romanization for each phrase.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transcription - the act of representing spoken sounds with written symbols
  • synonym: transliteration - the process of representing words from one script in the letters of another
