dun

verb
repeatedly demand payment
Pronunciation: / dʌn / (AmE), / dʌn / (BrE)
to persistently demand payment of a debt, especially in a repeated or annoying way

Examples

To dun a debtor
After months of being dunned, he finally settled his outstanding bill.
Creditors are known to dun customers with calls and letters.
The company had to dun the client several times before receiving the overdue amount.
The company kept dunning him for the unpaid balance on his account.
After several months, the landlord began dunning the tenant for overdue rent.
She was tired of being dunned by collection agencies every week.
