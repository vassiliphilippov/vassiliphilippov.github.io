backstrap

noun
loin cut of meat
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.stræp/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.stræp/ (BrE)
the long, tender loin muscle along the back of a large animal, especially a deer, used for steaks or roasts

Examples

Grilled backstrap steak
The chef grilled the backstrap to perfection and served it with roasted vegetables.
Hunters often prize the backstrap as the most flavorful part of the deer.
She marinated the venison backstrap overnight before cooking it for dinner.
