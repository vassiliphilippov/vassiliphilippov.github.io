alarm

noun
sudden fear or anxiety from danger
2.3 9.01/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàl…ërm / (AmE), / …ôÀàl…ëÀêm / (BrE)
a sudden feeling of fear or anxiety caused by the realization of danger or threat

Examples

Feel sudden alarm
His face showed visible alarm as the car skidded on the icy road.
She could not hide her alarm when she heard the unexpected news.
There was a sense of alarm in the city after the earthquake.

Synonyms

  • scare
    2.0 6.22/m
    scare is a short, sudden fear from a quick shock, while alarm is fear that comes from real danger being noticed.
  • consternation
    7.7 1.74/m
    consternation is a shocked, confused worry at something unexpected, while alarm is sudden fear after you notice a real threat.
  • dismay
    3.4 5.24/m
    dismay is a sinking, disappointed worry often from bad news, while alarm is sudden fear when you see immediate danger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - state of being peaceful and untroubled
  • degree higher: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety
