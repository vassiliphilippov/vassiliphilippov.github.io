somewhat

adverb
to a moderate degree or extent
2.0 67/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÊŒmËŒwÊŒt / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒmwÉ’t / (BrE)
to a small or moderate degree; slightly; rather

Examples

A somewhat surprised look
She looked somewhat surprised when she heard the news.
The instructions were somewhat unclear, so I had to ask for help.
The weather was somewhat better today than yesterday, but it was still chilly.
His mood seemed somewhat improved after the walk.
She looked somewhat confused by the instructions.
The film was somewhat disappointing, but the performances were excellent.

Synonyms

  • fairly
    1.0 57/m
    fairly means moderately or to a good degree, while somewhat covers both small and moderate degrees in neutral tone.
  • moderately
    2.8 6.68/m
    moderately means to a medium degree, while somewhat can mean either a small or a modest degree in many contexts.
  • reasonably
    2.2 11/m
    reasonably means fairly and sensible in many situations, while somewhat only shows a small or moderate amount without judgement.
  • pretty
    1.0 297/m
    pretty is an informal way to say fairly or quite, while somewhat is neutral and fits both written and spoken language.
  • slightly
    1.0 87/m
    slightly means a very small degree, while somewhat can mean either a little or a bit more than slight in tone.
  • a bit
    1.0 144/m
    a bit is an informal phrase for a small amount, while somewhat is more neutral and fits formal and informal contexts.
  • a little
    1.0 431/m
    a little means a small amount or degree, while somewhat covers both small and modest degrees and sounds more formal.
  • mildly
    2.7 8.18/m
    mildly means in a gentle or not strong way, while somewhat is a general degree word that can be stronger or weaker.
  • marginally
    4.1 3.71/m
    marginally means by a very small or barely noticeable amount, while somewhat can mean a small or slightly larger degree.
  • passably
    4.6 0.14/m
    passably means just good enough or acceptable, while somewhat only describes amount and does not judge quality or adequacy.
