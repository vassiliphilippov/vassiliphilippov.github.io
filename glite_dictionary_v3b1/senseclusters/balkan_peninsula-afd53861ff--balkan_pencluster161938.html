Balkan Peninsula

proper noun
large peninsula in southeastern Europe
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːlkən pəˈnɪnsələ / (AmE), / ˈbælkən pəˈnɪnsjʊlə / (BrE)
a large peninsula in southeastern Europe that includes countries such as Albania, Bulgaria, Greece, and parts of several others, and is named after the Balkan Mountains.

Examples

The Balkan Peninsula region
Several major rivers, including the Danube, flow through the Balkan Peninsula.
The Balkan Peninsula has a rich and complex history involving many different cultures.
Tourists are drawn to the scenic coastlines and mountainous landscapes of the Balkan Peninsula.
