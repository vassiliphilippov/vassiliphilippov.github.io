fake

verb
make a copy to deceive
Pronunciation: / feɪk / (AmE), / feɪk / (BrE)
to make or alter something in order to deceive; to counterfeit, forge, or tamper with

Examples

To fake a signature
He tried to fake his boss's signature on the contract.
She was accused of faking her identification documents to get a job.
The criminals faked hundreds of passports before being caught.
Hackers are often able to fake online identities to trick people.
She managed to fake the document, making it look completely authentic.
The company was accused of trying to fake its sales numbers to impress investors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authenticate - to prove or show that something is genuine
  • synonym: counterfeit - to make an exact imitation of something, especially money, with the intention to deceive
  • synonym: falsify - to change information in order to deceive
  • synonym: forge - to produce a fraudulent copy or imitation, especially of an official document or signature
