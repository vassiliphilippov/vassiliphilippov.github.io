pegmatite

noun
coarse-grained igneous rock
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛɡ.məˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛɡ.mə.taɪt / (BrE)
an igneous rock with extremely coarse grains, typically granite, formed from the crystallization of magma rich in rare elements

Examples

Rare pegmatite deposits
Rare minerals are often mined from pegmatite deposits.
The geologist identified the large crystals as part of a pegmatite formation.
The museum's display included a specimen of pegmatite with visible quartz and feldspar.
