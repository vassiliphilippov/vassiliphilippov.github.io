mortgager

noun
person who gives a mortgage
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrtˌɡeɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈmɔːɡɪdʒə / (BrE)
a person who borrows money by giving a mortgage on their property as security

Examples

The mortgager pays
As the mortgager, he was responsible for making timely payments to the bank.
If the mortgager failed to repay the loan, the house could be repossessed.
The contract required the mortgager to keep the property insured at all times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mortgagee - the person or institution who lends money by accepting a mortgage
