hillbilly

noun
unsophisticated rural person (disparaging)
9.6 0.97/m

(informal, often offensive) A disparaging term for an unsophisticated person from a rural, usually mountainous, region, especially in the United States.

Examples

A hillbilly stereotype
Despite the stereotype, not everyone from the Appalachians likes being called a hillbilly.
She resented outsiders using the word hillbilly to mock her family roots.
The movie portrayed him as a clueless hillbilly who had never visited a big town.

Synonyms

  • hick
    9.6 1.18/m
    hick is a rude, short word for a rural person seen as uneducated or uncultured, while hillbilly adds a mountain-region, U.S. slur.
  • bumpkin
    8.9 0.48/m
    bumpkin is a somewhat comic word for a country person who is awkward or simple, while hillbilly is more offensive and regional.
  • hayseed
    9.8 0.31/m
    hayseed is a rustic, slightly old-fashioned name for a country person seen as naive, while hillbilly is a harsher American mountain-region slur.

Related Terms

  • antonym: city slicker - a sophisticated person from a city
  • synonym: redneck - an uneducated or unsophisticated white person from the southern US
