frozenness

noun
state of being fixed
8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfroʊ.zən.nəs/ /; / /ˈfroʊz.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈfrəʊ.zən.nəs/ /; / /ˈfrəʊz.nəs/ (BrE)
the state of being deliberately kept fixed or unchanged, especially by policy or regulation.

Examples

Frozenness of policy
The frozenness of the market made it difficult for new businesses to enter.
Many criticized the frozenness of government policies during the economic crisis.
The frozenness in negotiations led to a long period without any progress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexibility - the quality of being able to change or adapt
  • synonym: stability - the quality of being steady and not changing
