European Community

noun
historic European political and economic organization
3.7
Pronunciation: / jʊˌroʊpiən kəˈmjunəti / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊpiːən kəˈmjuːnɪti / (BrE)
an international organization of European countries established after World War II to promote economic integration and cooperation, later forming the core of the European Union

Examples

The European Community countries
Britain joined the European Community in 1973, marking a significant shift in its foreign policy.
The European Community was a major driver of economic integration in Western Europe during the late twentieth century.
The policies set by the European Community greatly influenced trade relations among member states.
