watt

noun
unit of power (1 joule per second)
Pronunciation: / wɑt / (AmE), / wɒt / (BrE)
the standard unit of power, equal to one joule per second or the power generated by a current of one ampere flowing through a resistance of one ohm

Examples

One watt power
A microwave oven typically uses about 1,000 watt during operation.
The new light bulb consumes only ten watt of power.
The solar panel can generate up to 300 watt on a sunny day.
