cool down

verb
make or become cooler
1.1 1.41/m
Pronunciation: / kul da än / (AmE), / kuÀêl da än / (BrE)
to become less hot or to make something less hot; to reduce temperature

Examples

To cool down soup
After the engine overheated, we had to wait for it to cool down.
Let the soup cool down before serving it to the guests.
Opening the window helped the room cool down quickly.
After exercise, walk around to help your body cool down gradually.
The engine takes a while to cool down after a long drive.
You should let the soup cool down before eating it.

Synonyms

  • cool
    1.0 19/m
    cool is a general verb for making something less warm, while cool down focuses on lowering temperature after heating.
  • cool off
    2.7 1.18/m
    cool off often means calming down emotionally or slowly losing heat, while cool down refers specifically to reducing temperature.
  • chill
    1.0 12/m
    chill often means making food or drink very cold by refrigeration, while cool down means simply lowering temperature more generally.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cool - to make cool or become cool
