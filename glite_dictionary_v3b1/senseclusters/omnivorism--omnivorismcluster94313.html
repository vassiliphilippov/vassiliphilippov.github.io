omnivorism

noun
eating plants and animals
7.0
Pronunciation: / ɑmˈnɪvərɪzəm / (AmE), / ɒmˈnɪvərɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or condition of eating both animal and plant foods

Examples

Dietary omnivorism
The flexibility of omnivorism has helped humans adapt to many different environments.
Some scientists study omnivorism to understand how diet affects health and evolution.
Unlike strict vegetarians, people who practice omnivorism eat both meat and vegetables.

Related Terms

  • antonym: carnivorism - the practice of eating only animal foods
  • antonym: herbivorism - the practice of eating only plant foods
