teem

verb
move or be present in large numbers
6.0 2.96/m
Pronunciation: / tim / (AmE), / tiÀêm / (BrE)
to be full of or swarming with large numbers of people, animals, or things

Examples

Streets teemed with people
After the storm, the streets teemed with people eager to see the damage.
At dusk, the market square teems with vendors and shoppers.
During the summer, the fields teem with wildflowers and butterflies.
Her garden teems with colorful butterflies every spring.
On Black Friday, the mall teemed with eager shoppers hunting for deals.
The river used to teem with fish in the summer months.

Synonyms

    swarm means to move together in large numbers and often enter a place, while teem means to be full of them.
