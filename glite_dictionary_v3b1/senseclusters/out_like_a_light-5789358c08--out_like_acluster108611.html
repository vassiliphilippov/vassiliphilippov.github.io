out like a light

adjective
fall asleep instantly; become unconscious suddenly
8.6
Pronunciation: / aʊt laɪk ə laɪt / (AmE), / aʊt laɪk ə laɪt / (BrE)
To fall asleep very quickly or suddenly become unconscious.

Examples

Out like a light quickly
After running the marathon, he was out like a light the moment he hit the bed.
As soon as the plane took off, the kid beside me was out like a light.
She lay down for just a minute and was out like a light before she knew it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall asleep - to go from being awake to being asleep
  • antonym: wide awake - fully awake and alert
