weak

adjective
regularly inflected (grammar)
2.8 4.9
Pronunciation: /wiːk/ (AmE), /wiːk/ (BrE)
(Of a verb) following the standard pattern of inflection, especially forming the past tense and past participle by adding '-ed'.

Examples

Weak verb
In German, most verbs are classified as weak because they form their past tense regularly.
Linguists often distinguish between weak and strong verbs when analyzing language structure.
The verb 'talk' is considered weak because its past tense is formed by adding '-ed'.
