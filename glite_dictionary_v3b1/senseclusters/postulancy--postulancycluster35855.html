postulancy

noun
trial membership period
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːstʃələnsɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊlənsi / (BrE)
the period when someone is a candidate for membership in a group or organization, especially before joining a religious order

Examples

The postulancy period
Her postulancy at the organization lasted six months before she became a full member.
During his postulancy, he participated in all group activities to demonstrate his commitment.
The club requires a period of postulancy before granting official membership.
After completing her postulancy, she was accepted as a novice in the convent.
The postulancy is a time for candidates to learn about the religious community.
He reflected on his experiences during his postulancy before taking the next step.

Related Terms

  • synonym: initiation - the process of being formally accepted as a member
  • degree higher: novitiate - the period or state of being a novice in a religious order
  • synonym: probation - a trial period to test suitability for a role
