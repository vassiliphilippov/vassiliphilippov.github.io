schoolish

adjective
like a school of thought
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈskuːlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskuːlɪʃ / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a particular school of thought, artistic movement, or intellectual tradition, often in a conventional or unoriginal way

Examples

Schoolish style
Her schoolish approach to painting made her work seem less innovative than her peers'.
The essay was criticized for its schoolish repetition of established theories.
He avoided schoolish methods, preferring to develop his own unique style.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doctrinaire - rigidly following a theory or doctrine
  • antonym: original - new and not derived from something else
