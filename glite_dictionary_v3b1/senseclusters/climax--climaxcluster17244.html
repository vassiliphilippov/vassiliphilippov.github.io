climax

noun
arrangement of clauses by increasing forcefulness
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪ.mæks / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.maks / (BrE)
the arrangement of phrases or clauses in increasing order of importance or emphasis in speech or writing

Examples

Rhetorical climax
The essay's structure followed a climax, each argument more compelling than the last.
The speech reached its climax with a powerful declaration of unity.
Writers often use climax to build suspense and impact in their works.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticlimax - an abrupt decline from a dignified or important idea to a trivial or ludicrous one
