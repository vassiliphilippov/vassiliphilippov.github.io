troponin

noun
heart muscle protein
6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtroʊ.pə.nɪn/ (AmE), /ˈtrəʊ.pə.nɪn/ (BrE)
a protein found in muscle tissue, especially the heart, that is released into the blood when the heart muscle is damaged and is used as a marker for heart damage

Examples

Troponin blood test
Doctors ordered a blood test to check her troponin levels after she complained of chest pain.
A significant rise in troponin can indicate damage to the heart muscle.
The emergency room staff monitored his troponin results to help diagnose a possible heart attack.
The doctor ordered a blood test to check the patient's troponin levels after the heart attack.
Elevated troponin can indicate damage to the heart muscle.
Researchers study troponin to better understand how muscles contract and relax.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biomarker - a substance used as an indicator of a biological state or condition
  • synonym: creatine kinase - an enzyme measured in blood tests to detect muscle damage
  • synonym: myoglobin - a protein in muscle cells that stores oxygen
