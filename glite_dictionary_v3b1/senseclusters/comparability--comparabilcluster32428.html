comparability

noun
quality of being able to be compared
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmpərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be compared; the extent to which things are similar enough to be compared

Examples

Financial comparability standards
Strict standards improve the comparability of financial statements across companies.
The comparability of the test scores between the two schools is being questioned.
When assessing research findings, comparability of data collection methods is essential.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comparison - the act of comparing two or more things
  • antonym: incomparability - state of not being comparable
