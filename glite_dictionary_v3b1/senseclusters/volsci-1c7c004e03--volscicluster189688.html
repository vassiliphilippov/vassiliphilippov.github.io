Volsci

proper noun
ancient Italic people
Pronunciation: / ˈvɒlskaɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɒlskaɪ / (BrE)
an ancient Italic people who lived in central Italy and were rivals of early Rome

Examples

Ancient Volsci tribe
The Volsci frequently clashed with the early Romans over territory in central Italy.
Archaeologists discovered pottery that once belonged to the Volsci in the ancient ruins.
Legends describe the Volsci as fierce warriors who resisted Roman expansion.
The Volsci were known for their frequent conflicts with the early Romans.
Archaeologists have discovered artifacts from the Volsci in central Italy.
Legends describe how the Volsci resisted Roman expansion for many years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Etruscans - an ancient civilization of Italy before Roman dominance
  • synonym: Latins - an ancient Italic people of central Italy, neighbors of the Volsci
  • antonym: Romans - the people of ancient Rome, who often fought the Volsci
