intemperate

adjective
given to excess, especially with alcohol
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛmpərət / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛmpərət / (BrE)
showing a lack of self-control or being excessive, especially in actions, feelings, or indulgence (such as with alcohol)

Examples

Intemperate behavior
Critics accused the politician of intemperate behavior during the campaign.
His intemperate habits led to frequent absences from work.
The novel depicts an intemperate man unable to resist the lure of drink.
He displayed intemperate anger during the heated discussion.
Her intemperate spending habits soon led to financial trouble.
The coach was criticized for his intemperate remarks after the match.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excessive - exceeding normal or proper limits
  • antonym: temperate - showing moderation or self-restraint
