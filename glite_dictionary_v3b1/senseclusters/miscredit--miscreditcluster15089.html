miscredit

verb
to credit incorrectly
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
to record a payment or credit in the wrong account or ledger entry, especially in accounting

Examples

To miscredit a payment
The accountant accidentally miscredited the payment to the wrong client.
If you miscredit a transaction, it can cause confusion in the financial records.
The bank quickly fixed the error after they miscredited the funds to another account.

Related Terms

  • antonym: credit - to add money to an account
  • synonym: misallocate - to assign or distribute something to the wrong place or purpose
