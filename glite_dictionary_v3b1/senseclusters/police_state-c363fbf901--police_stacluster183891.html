police state

noun
oppressive government ruled by police control
7.9 7.5
Pronunciation: / pəˈlis steɪt / (AmE), / pəˈliːs steɪt / (BrE)
a country or government where the authorities use strict control, surveillance, and police power to limit people's freedoms

Examples

A police state government
Critics warn that the government’s new surveillance laws risk turning the country into a police state.

Writers often use dystopian fiction to explore life in a police state.
Citizens living in a police state may find their privacy and freedoms severely limited.
Many activists warned that the new laws could turn the country into a police state.
Under a police state, dissent is often met with harsh punishment and censorship.
