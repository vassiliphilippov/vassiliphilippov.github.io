Abrahamic

adjective
relating to Abraham
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪbrəˈhæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪbrəˈhæmɪk / (BrE)
relating to Abraham or to the religions (Judaism, Christianity, Islam) that trace their origins to him

Examples

Abrahamic religions
Judaism, Christianity, and Islam are often called Abrahamic religions because they trace their roots to Abraham.
The Abrahamic traditions share many common stories and values.
He studied the influence of Abrahamic beliefs on modern society.
Judaism, Christianity, and Islam are often grouped as Abrahamic religions due to their shared origins.
The Abrahamic faiths all recognize Abraham as a significant figure in their traditions.
Many ethical teachings are common across the Abrahamic religions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biblical - relating to the Bible or its contents
  • synonym: monotheistic - believing in a single god
