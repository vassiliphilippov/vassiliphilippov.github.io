Vaccaria pyramidata

noun
annual plant with pale rose flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / vəˈkɛəriə ˌpɪr.əˈmɛɪ.də / (AmE), / vakˈkeəriə ˌpɪr.əˈmeɪ.də / (BrE)
A European annual plant with pale rose-colored flowers, grown ornamentally or found as a weed in grain fields; also introduced in North America and sometimes classified as a soapwort.

Examples

Vaccaria pyramidata plant
Vaccaria pyramidata can often be found growing wild along roadsides and in grain fields.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Vaccaria pyramidata for its delicate rose-colored blooms.
This region has reported an increase in Vaccaria pyramidata since the introduction of new crops.
