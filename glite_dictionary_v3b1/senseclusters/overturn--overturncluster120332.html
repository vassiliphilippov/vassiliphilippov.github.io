overturn

verb
remove from power, especially rulers or governments
3.3 5.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈtɜːrn/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈtɜːn/ (BrE)
to cause someone in power, especially a government or ruler, to be removed from authority, often by force

Examples

To overturn a government
History is full of attempts to overturn monarchies and install new regimes.
The rebels hoped to overturn the corrupt government through a popular uprising.
The revolutionaries failed in their bid to overturn the emperor's rule.
