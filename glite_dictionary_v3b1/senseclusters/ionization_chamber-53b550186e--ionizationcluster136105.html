ionization chamber

noun
device for measuring ionizing radiation
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən ˈtʃeɪmbər / (AmE), / ˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a device used to measure the amount of ionizing radiation by detecting electrically charged particles produced within a gas-filled chamber

Examples

An ionization chamber device
A properly calibrated ionization chamber is essential for accurate dosimetry in radiation therapy.
The scientist placed the radioactive sample inside the ionization chamber to measure its emission rate.
Unlike Geiger counters, an ionization chamber is more suitable for measuring high levels of radiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Geiger counter - an instrument for detecting and measuring ionizing radiation
