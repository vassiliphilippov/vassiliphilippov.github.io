bone cell

noun
a cell found in bone tissue
4.6
Pronunciation: / boʊn sɛl / (AmE), / bəʊn sɛl / (BrE)
a cell that is a structural or functional component of bone tissue, such as an osteocyte, osteoblast, or osteoclast

Examples

Strong bone cells
A healthy diet can promote the growth of strong bone cells in children.
Microscopic analysis revealed several damaged bone cells near the fracture site.
Osteoblasts are a type of bone cell responsible for forming new bone tissue.
