clean

verb
remove dirt or unwanted substances
Pronunciation: / klin / (AmE), / kliːn / (BrE)
to remove dirt, stains, or unwanted substances from something; to make something free from dirt or impurities

Examples

To clean the kitchen
He spent the afternoon trying to clean the old stains from the carpet.
Remember to clean your hands before preparing food.
The janitor cleaned the classroom every evening after the students left.
Farmers clean the grain to prepare it for milling.
It is important to clean the rice thoroughly by removing all the husks.
The factory cleans large quantities of wheat every day before packaging.
He forgot to clean behind his ears after showering.
It’s important to clean your feet thoroughly after a long day.
You should always clean your hands before eating lunch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shell - to remove the shell or outer covering from nuts or peas
  • synonym: wash - to clean with water and usually soap
