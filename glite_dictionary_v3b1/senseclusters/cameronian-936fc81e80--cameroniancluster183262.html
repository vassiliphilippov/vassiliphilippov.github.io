Cameronian

noun
Scottish religious sect member
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæməˈroʊniən / (AmE), / ˌkæməˈrəʊniən / (BrE)
a member of a Scottish Presbyterian sect founded by followers of Richard Cameron in the late 17th century

Examples

A strict Cameronian
The Cameronian movement was known for its strict religious beliefs and opposition to state interference.
As a proud Cameronian, he attended secret meetings to practice his faith.
The history of the Cameronian sect is closely tied to Scotland's struggle for religious freedom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Presbyterian - a member of a Protestant Christian church governed by elders
