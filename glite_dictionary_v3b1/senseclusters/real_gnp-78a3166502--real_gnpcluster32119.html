real GNP

noun
GNP adjusted for inflation
6.6 5.1
Pronunciation: /riːl ˈdʒiː.en.piː/ /; / /riː.əl ˈdʒiː.en.piː/ (AmE), /riːl ˈdʒiː.en.piː/ /; / /riː.əl ˈdʒiː.en.piː/ (BrE)
Gross National Product (GNP) adjusted for the effects of inflation, representing the value of goods and services produced by a country's residents in constant prices.

Examples

Increase in real GNP
A sharp increase in real GNP indicates that a country's economy is expanding in real terms.
Economists often use real GNP to compare economic growth over time, since it accounts for inflation.
When inflation is high, real GNP provides a more accurate picture of economic progress than nominal figures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nominal GNP - Gross National Product not adjusted for inflation
