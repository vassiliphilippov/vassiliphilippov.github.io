prosaic

adjective
dull; lacking excitement or imagination
Pronunciation: / proʊˈzeɪɪk /; / proʊˈzeɪ.ɪk / (AmE), / prəʊˈzeɪɪk /; / prəʊˈzeɪ.ɪk /; / prəˈzeɪɪk / (BrE)
lacking imagination or excitement; dull, ordinary, or uninteresting

Examples

A prosaic routine
After the adventure, returning to her prosaic routine felt disappointing.
His explanation was surprisingly prosaic given the mysterious circumstances.
The movie's prosaic storyline failed to capture the audience's attention.
Although the proposal was well-researched, its presentation was rather prosaic and uninspiring to the committee.
She longed for excitement in her prosaic daily routine, which rarely changed from week to week.
The chef's menu was disappointingly prosaic, with no adventurous flavors or unusual pairings.
Despite the beautiful setting, his comments were disappointingly prosaic.
Her explanation was prosaic and did little to capture the audience's attention.
The prosaic routine of office life soon became monotonous for him.
