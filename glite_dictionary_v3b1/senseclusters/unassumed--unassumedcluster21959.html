unassumed

adjective
not adopted or pretended
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnəˈsuːmd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈsjuːmd / (BrE)
not taken on, adopted, or pretended; genuine or not affected.

Examples

An unassumed attitude
Her modesty was genuine and unassumed.
He greeted everyone with an unassumed friendliness that made people feel comfortable.
The artist's unassumed style set her apart from her more flamboyant peers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genuine - real and sincere, not pretended
