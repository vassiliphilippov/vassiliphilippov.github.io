undemonstrable

adjective
impossible to prove
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɪˈmɑnstrəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈmɒnstrəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to prove or show to be true by evidence or demonstration

Examples

An undemonstrable theory
The theory remained undemonstrable despite years of research and debate.
Some philosophical ideas are considered undemonstrable because they cannot be tested or observed.
His accusation was undemonstrable and lacked any supporting evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demonstrable - able to be shown or proven clearly
  • synonym: unprovable - not able to be proven true or false
