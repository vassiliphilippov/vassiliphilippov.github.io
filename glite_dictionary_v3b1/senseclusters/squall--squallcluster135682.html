squall

verb
cry out in a high-pitched, whiny way
4.3 5.8
Pronunciation: /skwɔːl/ (AmE), /skwɔːl/ (BrE)
to cry out or make a loud, high-pitched, or harsh noise (used for people or animals)

Examples

Babies squall loudly
He could hear the kittens squalling for food in the alley.
Seagulls squall overhead as the fishermen unload their catch.
The baby began to squall loudly when she dropped her toy.
The baby was squalling loudly in the middle of the night.
The cat squalled when its tail was accidentally stepped on.
The infant began to squall as soon as the doctor entered the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cry - to make a loud sound, especially to express pain or emotion
  • synonym: scream - make a loud, high-pitched cry or sound
  • synonym: wail - make a long, loud, high-pitched cry
  • antonym: whisper - to speak very quietly, using the breath rather than the voice
  • synonym: yell - to shout loudly, especially because of anger, pain, or excitement
