performativity

noun
creation of identity by action
9.8
Pronunciation: / pərˌfɔrməˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / pəˌfɔːməˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the idea that actions or words can create or bring about realities or effects, especially in social or identity contexts

Examples

Performativity of gender
The theory of performativity suggests that gender is not something we are, but something we do.
Many sociologists debate the role of performativity in shaping cultural norms and expectations.
Her research explores how language and performativity influence the construction of social identity.
The performativity of language means that saying something can actually make it happen.
Scholars debate the performativity of gender roles in society.
The artist explored the performativity of everyday actions in her latest exhibition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constative - a statement that describes or states facts rather than performing an action
  • antonym: essentialism - the belief that things have a fixed, inherent nature
  • synonym: performance - the act of carrying out or accomplishing an action
