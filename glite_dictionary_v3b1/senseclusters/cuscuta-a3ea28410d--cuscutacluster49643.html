Cuscuta

noun
genus of leafless parasitic plants (dodder)
9.9
Pronunciation: / kʌsˈkuːtə / (AmE), / kʌsˈkjuːtə / (BrE)
A genus of twining, leafless parasitic herbs lacking chlorophyll, commonly known as dodder.

Examples

Cuscuta parasitic plant
Because Cuscuta lacks chlorophyll, it must rely entirely on other plants for survival.
Botanists often study Cuscuta to understand plant parasitism in ecosystems.
The stem of Cuscuta twined tightly around its host plant, draining it of nutrients.
