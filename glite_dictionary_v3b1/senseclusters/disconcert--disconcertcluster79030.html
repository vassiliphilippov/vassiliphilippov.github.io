disconcert

verb
make uneasy or unsettled
8.1 5.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.skən.ˈsɜːrt/ (AmE), /dɪ.skən.ˈsɜːt/ (BrE)
to make someone feel uneasy, unsettled, or embarrassed

Examples

To disconcert a person
Her direct question seemed to disconcert him during the meeting.
It can disconcert people when their routines are suddenly changed.
The loud noise from outside began to disconcert the performers on stage.
His strange behavior began to disconcert his colleagues.
Loud noises can disconcert even the calmest individuals.
The sudden question seemed to disconcert her during the interview.
