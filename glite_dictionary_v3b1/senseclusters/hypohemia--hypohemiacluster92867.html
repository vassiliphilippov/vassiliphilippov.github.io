hypohemia

noun
low blood volume
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈhiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˈhiːmiə / (BrE)
a condition in which there is a lower than normal volume of blood in the body

Examples

Severe hypohemia case
Severe dehydration can lead to hypohemia in patients.
Doctors monitored the patient's blood levels to check for signs of hypohemia after surgery.
Rapid blood loss from an accident may result in hypohemia if not treated quickly.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: anemia - a condition marked by a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin
  • synonym: hypovolemia - a decreased volume of circulating blood in the body
