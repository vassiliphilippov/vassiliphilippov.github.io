high-ceilinged

adjective
having a higher than normal ceiling
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈsiːlɪŋd / (AmE), / haɪ ˈsiːlɪŋd / (BrE)
(of a room or building) having a ceiling that is higher than is typical or standard.

Examples

A high-ceilinged room
She preferred high-ceilinged rooms because they felt airy and spacious.
The high-ceilinged hall was filled with sunlight streaming through tall windows.
The restaurant was located in a high-ceilinged warehouse that had been stylishly renovated.
