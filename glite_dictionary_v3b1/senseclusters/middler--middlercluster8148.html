middler

noun
student in middle year
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdlər / (AmE), / ˈmɪdlə / (BrE)
a student in the middle year of a multi-year academic program, such as the second year in a three-year course

Examples

College middler
As a middler, she felt more confident navigating the campus than she did as a freshman.
The club elected a middler as its new vice president this semester.
Many middlers start thinking about internships and future career plans.

  • antonym: senior - a student in the final year of a program
