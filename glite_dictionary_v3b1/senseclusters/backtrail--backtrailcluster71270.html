backtrail

verb
to retrace route
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌtreɪl / (AmE), / ˈbækˌtreɪl / (BrE)
to retrace a route or path back toward the starting point, especially when trying to find one's way.

Examples

To backtrail the route
After realizing they were lost, the hikers decided to backtrail to the last marked signpost.
We had to backtrail for nearly an hour before we found the correct path.
If you miss a turn, you might need to backtrail until you recognize a familiar landmark.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward in a direction
  • synonym: backtrack - to go back along the same route one has just taken
