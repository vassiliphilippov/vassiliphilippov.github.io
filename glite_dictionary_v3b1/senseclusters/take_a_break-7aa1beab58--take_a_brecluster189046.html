take a break

verb
pause for rest or relaxation
1.0 1.3
Pronunciation: /teɪk ə breɪk/ (AmE), /teɪk ə breɪk/ (BrE)
to stop doing something for a short time in order to rest or relax before continuing

Examples

To take a break from work
After working for three hours without stopping, he decided to take a break and get some fresh air.
She suggested they take a break from studying to grab some coffee.
Whenever the discussion becomes too heated, it's wise to take a break and cool down.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rest - to cease work or movement to relax or recover strength
  • antonym: resume - to begin again or continue after a pause or interruption
