plural

noun
grammatical term; more-than-one form
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈplʊrəl / (AmE), / ˈplʊərəl / (BrE)
The form of a word, especially a noun or pronoun, that refers to more than one person or thing.

Examples

The plural of 'child'
In English, most nouns form the plural by adding '-s' or '-es'.
The plural of 'child' is 'children'.
You should use the plural when talking about more than one object.

Related Terms

  • antonym: singular - the grammatical form denoting one person or thing
