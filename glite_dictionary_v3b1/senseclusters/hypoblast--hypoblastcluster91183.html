hypoblast

noun
embryonic germ layer forming digestive and respiratory linings
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ.poʊˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪ.pəʊˌblast / (BrE)
the inner germ layer in an embryo that develops into the lining of the digestive and respiratory tracts

Examples

The hypoblast layer
Abnormal differentiation of the hypoblast can result in congenital defects.
During development, the hypoblast contributes to the formation of the gut lining.
The hypoblast is one of the first structures observed in early embryogenesis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endoderm - the innermost germ layer of an embryo, synonym for hypoblast
