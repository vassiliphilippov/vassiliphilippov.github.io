repopulation

noun
filling area again
4.5
Pronunciation: /riː.pɑːp.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rə.pɑːp.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.pɒp.jʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rə.pɒp.jʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of filling an area, system, or habitat with people, animals, or organisms again after they have left or disappeared

Examples

Repopulation of villages
After the war, the government encouraged the repopulation of abandoned villages.
The repopulation of the island was made possible by conservation efforts to reintroduce native species.
Scientists studied the repopulation of the lake by fish after pollution levels dropped.
After the wildfire, the repopulation of the forest by native animals took several years.
Scientists are studying the repopulation of damaged tissues using stem cells.
Efforts to support the repopulation of endangered species have shown promising results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonization - the act of establishing a population in a new area
  • antonym: depopulation - the reduction in the number of people or animals in an area
  • synonym: resettlement - the process of settling people in a new place
