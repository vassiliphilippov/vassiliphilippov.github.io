wobble

verb
move unsteadily; shake or sway
5.0 3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːbəl /; / ˈwɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒbəl / (BrE)
to move or shake unsteadily from side to side or in an irregular way

Examples

To wobble on the beam
She tried to balance on the beam, but her legs continued to wobble.
The child's first steps caused his knees to wobble slightly.
The table began to wobble when I leaned on it.
After spinning too fast, the top would wobble and eventually tip over.
He tried to ride the bike, but the wheels started to wobble dangerously.
The glass began to wobble on the edge of the table before falling.
He tried to speak, but his voice wobbled with emotion.
Her hands wobble when she is nervous.
The vase began to wobble on the edge of the table during the earthquake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rock - to move gently backward and forward or from side to side
  • synonym: shake - to move with quick, short movements up and down or from side to side
  • antonym: stabilize - make or become stable
  • antonym: steady - to make or become firm, stable, or fixed
  • synonym: teeter - to move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically from anxiety or excitement
