crock

verb
release color when rubbed (fabric)
6.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /krɑːk/ (AmE), /krɒk/ (BrE)
(of dyed fabric) to release color when rubbed, especially as a sign of poor dyeing.

Examples

Fabric crocks color
Be careful with these pants—they will crock if they get damp.
The fabric may crock if it hasn't been properly colorfasted.
This cheap blue shirt tends to crock and stain other clothes in the wash.
