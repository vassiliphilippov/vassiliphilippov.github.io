shoetree

noun
device to keep a shoe's shape
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuˌtri / (AmE), / ˈʃuːˌtriː / (BrE)
a device, often made of wood or metal, that is placed inside a shoe to preserve its shape when not being worn

Examples

A shoetree inside
After polishing his dress shoes, he inserted shoetrees before storing them in the closet.
He placed a shoetree inside each leather boot to prevent them from creasing.
The cobbler recommended using a shoetree to maintain the shoes' original form.
