impulsivity

noun
acting without thinking
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmpəlˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌɪmpʌlˈsɪvəti / (BrE)
the tendency to act quickly without careful thought, planning, or consideration of consequences

Examples

High impulsivity behavior
Children often show more impulsivity than adults, especially when excited or upset.
High impulsivity can make it difficult to resist temptations or delay gratification.
The psychologist measured the participant's impulsivity using a series of decision-making tasks.
His impulsivity often got him into trouble at work.

Learning to control impulsivity can help improve decision-making skills.
High impulsivity can make it difficult for people to manage their emotions and actions.

Children with greater impulsivity may struggle to wait their turn in class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: caution - careful thought before acting
  • synonym: impulsiveness - the quality of acting on impulse
  • degree higher: recklessness - lack of concern for the consequences of actions
  • antonym: self-control - the ability to control one's actions and emotions
  • degree lower: spontaneity - the quality of being spontaneous or unplanned
