mission

noun
group of representatives sent for a purpose
3.1 2.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈmɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
an important task or duty, or the group of people sent to do it, often by a government, military, or organization

Examples

A mission to Mars
A diplomatic mission arrived in the capital for peace talks.
An economic mission will visit Japan next week to discuss trade.
The UN sent a mission to the region to investigate human rights abuses.
Completing this rescue mission will require teamwork and courage.
He considered it his personal mission to help the community.
The government sent her on a top-secret mission overseas.
Our unit completed its reconnaissance mission successfully and returned to base.
She was assigned a top-secret mission that required absolute discretion.
The pilots prepared thoroughly for their dangerous mission over enemy territory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assignment - a task given to someone as part of their job or studies
  • synonym: delegation - a group of people chosen to represent others
  • synonym: duty - something that one is required to do as part of a job or position
  • synonym: operation - a planned activity involving many people, especially in the military
  • degree lower: routine - a regular or usual task (less important than mission)
