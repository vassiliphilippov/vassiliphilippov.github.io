torpedo their chances

verb
ruin someone's chances
7.5
Pronunciation: / tɔrˈpidoʊ ðɛr ˈtʃænsɪz / (AmE), / tɔːˈpiːdəʊ ðeə ˈtʃɑːnsɪz / (BrE)
to deliberately or accidentally ruin someone's chances of succeeding or achieving something

Examples

To torpedo their chances badly
A single missed deadline might torpedo their chances for promotion this year.
Leaking the sensitive information could seriously torpedo their chances in the competition.
One careless mistake could torpedo their chances of landing the contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boost their chances - to improve someone's chances of succeeding
  • synonym: jeopardize - to put at risk or in danger
  • synonym: sabotage - to deliberately destroy, damage, or obstruct something
