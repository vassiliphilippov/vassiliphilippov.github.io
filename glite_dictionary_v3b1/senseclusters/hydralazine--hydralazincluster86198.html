hydralazine

noun
medicine for lowering blood pressure
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdræləˌzin / (AmE), / haɪˈdraləziːn / (BrE)
a medication used to lower high blood pressure by relaxing blood vessels, often prescribed for hypertension and heart failure

Examples

Hydralazine medication
Hydralazine is sometimes given with other medications to treat congestive heart failure.
Because of its vessel-dilating effects, hydralazine can cause headaches as a side effect.
The doctor prescribed hydralazine to help manage her high blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antihypertensive - a drug used to treat high blood pressure
  • synonym: vasodilator - a substance that widens blood vessels
