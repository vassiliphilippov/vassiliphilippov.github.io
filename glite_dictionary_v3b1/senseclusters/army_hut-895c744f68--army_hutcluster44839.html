army hut

noun
temporary military shelter
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrmi hʌt / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi hʌt / (BrE)
a small, temporary shelter used by soldiers, typically during training or wartime

Examples

An army hut shelter
During the war, many soldiers were housed in army huts near the front line.
Each recruit was assigned a bunk in a crowded army hut for the duration of his training.
The old army hut was repurposed as a community hall after peace returned.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barracks - buildings used to house soldiers
