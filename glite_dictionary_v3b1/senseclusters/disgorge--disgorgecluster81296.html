disgorge

verb
pour or discharge forcefully
8.6 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / d…™sÀà…°…îrd í / (AmE), / d…™sÀà…°…îÀêd í / (BrE)
(of a substance) to pour out or allow to pour out in large amounts, often suddenly or forcefully; to discharge contents forcefully or in a stream.

Examples

The pipe disgorged water
During the flood, the river disgorged tons of debris onto the nearby fields.
The overturned truck disgorged its cargo across the busy highway.
The volcano began to disgorge thick black smoke into the sky.
