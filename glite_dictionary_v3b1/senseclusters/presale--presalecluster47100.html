presale

adjective
before public sale
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌseɪl / (BrE)
available or occurring before the official public sale, often offered to a select group in advance

Examples

Presale tickets
Fans who joined the club received presale access to concert tickets before the general public.
The company offered a special presale discount to loyal customers ahead of the product launch.
We secured our seats during the presale period, so we did not have to wait in line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advance - happening before something else, especially before a public event
  • antonym: post-sale - occurring after the sale has taken place
