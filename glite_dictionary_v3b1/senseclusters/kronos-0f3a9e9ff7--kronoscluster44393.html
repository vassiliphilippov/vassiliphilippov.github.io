Kronos

proper noun
mythic time entity
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkroʊ.nəs/ /; / /ˈkrəʊ.nəʊs/ (AmE), /ˈkrəʊ.nəʊs/ /; / /ˈkrɒ.nɒs/ (BrE)
a figure from Greek mythology, the leader of the Titans and father of Zeus, often associated with time; also used in modern fiction as a powerful being with control over time

Examples

Kronos controls time
In the novel, Kronos manipulates time to change the fate of the universe.
The film's villain, Kronos, threatens to erase entire centuries from history.
Many video games feature Kronos as a godlike figure with control over time.
In Greek mythology, Kronos was known for overthrowing his own father Uranus.
According to legend, Kronos swallowed his children to prevent them from taking his throne.
The ancient Greeks believed that Kronos ruled during a golden age of peace.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Chronos - the personification of time in Greek mythology
  • synonym: Cronus - alternate spelling of Kronos, leader of the Titans in Greek mythology
  • antonym: Zeus - the king of the gods in Greek mythology, son of Kronos
