carrier

noun
radio wave used to transmit signals
7.3 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.rɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈkæ.rɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a radio wave that is modulated to convey information in radio, television, or other wireless transmissions

Examples

Radio carrier
Engineers adjusted the amplitude of the carrier to improve signal clarity.
The radio station broadcasts on an FM carrier frequency.
Without a carrier wave, the audio signal could not be transmitted over the air.
