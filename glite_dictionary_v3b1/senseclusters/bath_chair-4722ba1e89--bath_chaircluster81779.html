bath chair

noun
historical wheelchair, usually pushed by attendant
Pronunciation: / bæθ tʃɛr / (AmE), / bɑːθ tʃɛː / (BrE)
a type of wheelchair, often with three or four wheels and a canopy, formerly used for transporting invalids, especially at spas or seaside resorts, and usually pushed by an attendant

Examples

A bath chair with canopy
In Victorian England, a bath chair was a common sight at health resorts.
She hired a bath chair to take her grandfather around the gardens.
The patient was wheeled along the promenade in a bath chair.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wheelchair - a chair mounted on wheels for people who cannot walk
