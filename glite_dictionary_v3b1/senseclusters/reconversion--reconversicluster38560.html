reconversion

noun
return to civilian production
7.0 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêk…ônÀàv…úÀêr í…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêk…ônÀàv…úÀê É…ôn / (BrE)
the process of changing something back to its previous form, state, or use (for example, converting factories from military to civilian production after a war)

Examples

Industrial reconversion
The country faced many challenges during the reconversion of its wartime industries.
After World War II, reconversion created new jobs in the civilian sector.
Government policies supported the reconversion of factories to produce consumer goods.
After the war, the factory underwent reconversion to produce consumer goods instead of weapons.
The reconversion of the old church into a community center was welcomed by local residents.
Environmental groups supported the reconversion of farmland back into natural wetlands.
