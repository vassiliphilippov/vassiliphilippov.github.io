vena metatarsus

noun
vein serving metatarsal region of foot
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈviː.nə me.tə.ˈtɑːr.səs/ (AmE), /ˈviː.nə me.tə.ˈtɑː.səs/ (BrE)
Either of the veins, with dorsal and plantar branches, serving the metatarsal region of the foot.

Examples

The vena metatarsus vein
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully located the vena metatarsus to prevent excessive bleeding.
Injury to the vena metatarsus can lead to swelling and impaired circulation in the foot.
The vena metatarsus plays a key role in draining blood from the metatarsal area of the foot.
