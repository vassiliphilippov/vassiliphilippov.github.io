sluff

verb
to shed a layer
Pronunciation: / slʌf / (AmE), / slʌf / (BrE)
to shed or cast off a layer or outer covering, especially dead skin, or (in animals) skin, hair, horn, or feathers, often as part of a natural process of renewal or growth.

Examples

To sluff old skin
The snake sluffed its old skin in the spring.
After the sunburn, his skin began to sluff off in thin layers.
Some reptiles regularly sluff their outer skin as they grow.
As the wound began to heal, dead tissue started to slough off gradually.
During molting, birds slough their feathers to make way for fresh growth.
Every spring, the snake will slough its old skin in one continuous piece.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retain - to keep or hold onto something
  • synonym: shed - to lose or let fall a covering or layer naturally
