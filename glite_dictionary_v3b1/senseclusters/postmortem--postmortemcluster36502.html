postmortem

noun
discussion after an event to analyze it
7.1 0.58/m
Pronunciation: / po ästÀàm…îrt…ôm / (AmE), / p…ô ästÀàm…îÀêt…ôm / (BrE)
a discussion or analysis of an event after it has happened, especially to examine what went right or wrong

Examples

A postmortem meeting
After the project ended, the team held a postmortem to identify what worked and what didn't.
The coach led a detailed postmortem to analyze the reasons behind the team's defeat.
We always conduct a postmortem after major incidents to improve our response in the future.

Synonyms

  • post-mortem
    6.5 0.08/m
    post-mortem is the hyphenated written form often used in reports or articles, while postmortem means the same after-event analysis.
  • post mortem
    6.0 0.14/m
    post mortem is the spaced variant seen in some texts or notes, while postmortem is the common one-word term for the same review.
  • post-mortem examination
    5.0 0.00/m
    post-mortem examination is a formal, detailed review often used in official reports, while postmortem is the general after-event discussion.
