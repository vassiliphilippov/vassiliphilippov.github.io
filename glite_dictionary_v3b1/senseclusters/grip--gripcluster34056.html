grip

verb
seize or hold firmly, esp. in struggle
1.9 1.2
Pronunciation: /grɪp/ (AmE), /grɪp/ (BrE)
to seize or hold something or someone firmly, especially when struggling or in competition; to take and hold something tightly or firmly

Examples

To grip a rope
Rescue workers gripped the trapped man's hands and pulled him to safety.
She had to grip the ladder to keep from falling as the wind blew.
The wrestler managed to grip his opponent's arm tightly during the match.
Nervous, he gripped the microphone before starting his speech.
She gripped his hand tightly as they crossed the busy street.
The climber gripped the rock face, refusing to look down.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clutch - to grasp or hold tightly
  • synonym: grab - to seize suddenly or roughly
  • synonym: grasp - to take hold of something firmly
  • antonym: release - to let go of something that was held
