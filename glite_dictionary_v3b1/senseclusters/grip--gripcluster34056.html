grip

verb
seize or hold firmly, esp. in struggle
1.3 11/m
Pronunciation: / É¡rÉªp / (AmE), / É¡rÉªp / (BrE)
to seize or hold something or someone firmly, especially when struggling or in competition; to take and hold something tightly or firmly

Examples

To grip a rope
Rescue workers gripped the trapped man's hands and pulled him to safety.
She had to grip the ladder to keep from falling as the wind blew.
The wrestler managed to grip his opponent's arm tightly during the match.
Nervous, he gripped the microphone before starting his speech.
She gripped his hand tightly as they crossed the busy street.
The climber gripped the rock face, refusing to look down.

Synonyms

  • grasp
    1.1 7.71/m
    grasp means to take and hold something firmly with the hands, while grip often implies holding tightly during struggle.
  • take hold of
    3.1 2.33/m
    take hold of means to deliberately grasp something with your hands, while grip suggests a tight, forceful hold.
  • grapple
    5.1 0.88/m
    grapple means to seize and struggle with someone physically, while grip means simply to hold firmly or tightly.
  • clutch
    3.4 15/m
    clutch means to hold something tightly, often from fear or strong feeling, while grip is more neutral and physical.
  • hold tight
    2.2 1.49/m
    hold tight tells someone to keep a firm grip for safety or support, while grip names the general action of holding.
  • hold on
    2.0 6.19/m
    hold on asks someone to grasp firmly to avoid letting go, while grip is the act of holding tightly in general.
  • hold onto
    3.0 7.93/m
    hold onto can mean keep possession or grasp physically, while grip focuses on the physical act of holding tightly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clutch - to grasp or hold tightly
  • synonym: grab - to seize suddenly or roughly
  • synonym: grasp - to take hold of something firmly
  • antonym: release - to let go of something that was held
