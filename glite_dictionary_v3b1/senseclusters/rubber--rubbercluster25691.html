rubber

noun
series of games or matches between competitors
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌb.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈrʌb.ə / (BrE)
a series of games or matches played between the same competitors, especially in cards, in which the winner is the first to achieve a set number of victories

Examples

A card rubber
After winning two rounds, they needed one more game to win the rubber.
The final rubber of the bridge tournament lasted nearly an hour.
Whoever takes this rubber will claim the championship title.
