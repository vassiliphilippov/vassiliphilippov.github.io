sheared

adjective
fur or wool cut to uniform length
Pronunciation: / ʃɪrd / (AmE), / ʃɪəd / (BrE)
(of fur, wool, or similar material) cut or trimmed to a uniform length, especially for use in clothing or textiles.

Examples

Sheared wool coat
A sheared lambskin coat is both warm and stylish for winter.
She prefers sheared wool carpets because they feel smoother underfoot.
The jacket is made from sheared mink, giving it a soft, even texture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unshorn - not sheared; with natural, untrimmed wool or hair
