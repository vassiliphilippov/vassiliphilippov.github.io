thinness

noun
lack of substance or strength
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪnnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking substance, depth, or strength.

Examples

Thinness of the story
He was disappointed by the thinness of the plot in the latest novel.
Some critics pointed out the thinness of the evidence supporting his theory.
The thinness of her argument became apparent during the debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strength - the quality of being strong, firm, or substantial
  • synonym: weakness - lack of strength or firmness
