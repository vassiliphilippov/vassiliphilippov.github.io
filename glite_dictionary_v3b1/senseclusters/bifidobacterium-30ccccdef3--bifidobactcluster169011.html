Bifidobacterium

noun
probiotic bacterium
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪfɪdoʊbækˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / ˌbɪfɪdəʊbækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
a type of beneficial bacterium commonly found in the intestines and used as a probiotic in foods and supplements

Examples

Bifidobacterium in yogurt
Many yogurts contain live Bifidobacterium to help support digestive health.
Scientists are studying how Bifidobacterium can improve the balance of bacteria in the gut.
Some infant formulas are enriched with Bifidobacterium to promote healthy digestion in babies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Lactobacillus - a genus of beneficial bacteria often used as probiotics
