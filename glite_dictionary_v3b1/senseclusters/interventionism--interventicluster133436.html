interventionism

noun
belief in divine action
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that a deity or supernatural force actively intervenes in human affairs and events

Examples

Divine interventionism
Some religious traditions strongly support interventionism as an explanation for miracles.
The debate centered on whether interventionism is compatible with scientific understanding of the world.
Her belief in interventionism gave her hope during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deism - the belief that God does not intervene in the universe
  • synonym: providence - the protective care of God or a higher power
