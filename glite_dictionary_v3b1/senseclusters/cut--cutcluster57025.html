cut

verb
turn or change direction sharply
2.9 2.4
Pronunciation: /kʌt/ (AmE), /kʌt/ (BrE)
to move quickly or directly through or across something, often by taking a shortcut or changing direction abruptly

Examples

To cut across the park
At the last moment, the cab cut to the right and missed the exit.
She cut left at the intersection, startling the drivers behind her.
The cyclist cut across the street to avoid the traffic jam.
She cut across the empty lot instead of walking around the block.
The children cut through the alley on their way home from school.
We decided to cut through the park to save time.
A jet fighter cut across the sky, its engines roaring.
She cut through the crowd to reach the exit quickly.
The speedboat cut through the still lake, leaving a trail of ripples behind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shortcut - a shorter, quicker way to get somewhere
  • synonym: slice - to move smoothly and quickly through something
  • synonym: swerve - to change direction suddenly, especially to avoid something
  • synonym: veer - to change direction suddenly or unexpectedly
