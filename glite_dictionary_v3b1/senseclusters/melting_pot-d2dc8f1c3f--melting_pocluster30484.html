melting pot

noun
vessel for high-temperature chemical reactions
1.1 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmel.tɪŋ pɑːt/ (AmE), /ˈmel.tɪŋ pɒt/ (BrE)
A vessel made of material that does not melt easily, used for high-temperature chemical reactions and for melting or fusing substances.

Examples

A melting pot for metals
After hours in the melting pot, the mixture had fused into a single alloy.
Ceramic melting pots are commonly used in laboratories because they can withstand extreme temperatures.
The chemist carefully placed the metal samples in the melting pot before starting the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crucible - a container used for melting or subjecting substances to high temperatures
