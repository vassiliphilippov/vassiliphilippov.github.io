minor tranquilizer

noun
antianxiety drug; mild tranquilizer
1.6 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.nər ˈtræŋ.kwə.laɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.nə ˈtræŋ.kwɪ.laɪ.zə/ (BrE)
A drug that is used to relieve anxiety and reduce tension and irritability, without causing major sedation or mental dullness.

Examples

A minor tranquilizer drug
She was advised to avoid alcohol while taking a minor tranquilizer for her nerves.
Some patients respond well to minor tranquilizers during periods of acute stress.
The doctor prescribed a minor tranquilizer to help manage his anxiety symptoms.
After experiencing severe stress, the doctor prescribed a minor tranquillizer to help her relax.
He was advised not to operate heavy machinery while taking a minor tranquillizer.
Many people take minor tranquillizers to manage symptoms of anxiety disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anxiolytic - a drug that reduces anxiety
  • antonym: major tranquilizer - a drug used to treat psychosis and severe mental disorders
  • degree higher: major tranquillizer - a more potent tranquillizer used mainly to manage psychosis
