archon

noun
Byzantine official
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrkɑn / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɒn / (BrE)
a high-ranking official or public leader in Byzantine and medieval Greek societies, or an honorary lay title in the Eastern Orthodox Church

Examples

Byzantine archon
In ancient Athens, an archon was responsible for overseeing important civic duties.
He was honored as an archon by the church for his dedication to the community.
The title of archon signified great respect and authority in Byzantine society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magistrate - a civil officer or judge who administers the law
  • synonym: official - a person holding public office or having official duties
