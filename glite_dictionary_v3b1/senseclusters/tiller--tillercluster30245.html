tiller

verb
(of plants) produce new basal shoots
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪlə / (BrE)
(of a plant) to produce new shoots, especially from the base, forming clumps or stools.

Examples

Grass tillers quickly
After fertilization, the grass began to tiller and spread across the field.
Certain varieties of barley tiller more, resulting in denser crops.
Wheat plants often tiller vigorously when growing conditions are favorable.
