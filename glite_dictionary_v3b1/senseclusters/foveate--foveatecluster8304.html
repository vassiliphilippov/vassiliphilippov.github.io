foveate

adjective
having a central pit
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊviˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊviˌeɪt / (BrE)
having a fovea or foveae; possessing a small pit or central depression, especially in anatomical or morphological contexts

Examples

Foveate retina
The retina of many birds is highly foveate, allowing for excellent visual acuity.
Some insects have foveate eyes that help them detect movement with great precision.
The fossil showed a foveate structure in the skull, suggesting specialized sensory abilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convex - having a surface that curves outward
  • synonym: depressed - having a sunken or lowered surface
