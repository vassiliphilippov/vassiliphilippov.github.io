louse fly

noun
parasitic bloodsucking fly of birds and mammals
7.4
Pronunciation: / laʊs flaɪ / (AmE), / laʊs flaɪ / (BrE)
a bloodsucking dipterous fly parasitic on birds and mammals, often found in feathers or fur

Examples

The louse fly parasite
Farmers sometimes encounter the louse fly infesting sheep and cattle.
She studied the louse fly as part of her research into parasitic insects.
The louse fly is often found in the feathers of pigeons and other birds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hippoboscid - any fly of the family Hippoboscidae, to which louse flies belong
