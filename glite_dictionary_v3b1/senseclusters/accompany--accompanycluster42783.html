Synonyms

  • escort
    4.0 11/m
    escort means going with someone to protect or guide, often in a formal way, while accompany is more neutral for going together.
  • take someone to
    1.7 94/m
    take someone to usually means leading or bringing a person to a place, while accompany focuses on going with someone as company.
  • see
    3.9 27/m
    see often means going partway to a place as a polite act, while accompany covers going along as a companion on the trip.
