interactionism

noun
theory of social development
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈækʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntərˈækʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that ideas, behavior, or meaning develop through social interaction with others

Examples

Social interactionism
Interactionism emphasizes the importance of communication in shaping our understanding of the world.
Many sociologists use interactionism to explain how people develop their sense of identity.
According to interactionism, social roles are learned through everyday interactions with others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behaviorism - the theory that behavior is learned through conditioning
  • synonym: social constructivism - the theory that knowledge is constructed through social processes
