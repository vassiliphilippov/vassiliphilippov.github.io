retaining wall

noun
wall built to hold back earth or water
1.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈteɪnɪŋ wɔl / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪnɪŋ wɔːl / (BrE)
a wall constructed to hold back or support earth or water on one side of it

Examples

A retaining wall holds soil
Heavy rain can cause a retaining wall to fail if it is not properly drained.
The engineer designed a retaining wall to stop the hillside from collapsing onto the road.
They built a stone retaining wall to create level ground for the garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wall - a continuous vertical brick or stone structure that encloses or divides an area of land
