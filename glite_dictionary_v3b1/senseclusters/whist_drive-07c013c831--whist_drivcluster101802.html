whist drive

noun
social event playing progressive whist
9.1
Pronunciation: / wɪst draɪv / (AmE), / wɪst draɪv / (BrE)
A social event in which people play the card game whist in a progressive tournament style, often for charity or entertainment.

Examples

A whist drive event
Dozens of people attended the annual whist drive organized by the church.
She learned to play whist at her grandmother’s weekly whist drive.
The village hall hosted a whist drive to raise funds for the local school.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whist - A classic trick-taking card game that was popular in the 18th and 19th centuries
