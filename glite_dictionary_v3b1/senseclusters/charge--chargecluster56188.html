charge

verb
entrust for safekeeping
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to give someone a responsibility, duty, or task; to instruct or command with authority

Examples

To charge someone with a task
The teacher charged a student with taking care of the class hamster over the holidays.
They charged the documents to the archivist for safekeeping.
You can charge your valuables to the front desk during your stay.
Before leaving, he charged the group to uphold the company’s reputation.
She charged her assistant to handle the arrangements with utmost care.
The judge charged the jury to consider only the evidence presented.
As head chef, I am charged with maintaining kitchen safety standards.
She was charged to oversee the entire project.
The manager charged him with organizing the team meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assign - to give someone a task or responsibility
  • synonym: command - to give an authoritative order
  • synonym: consign - to deliver into the possession of another for care or safekeeping
  • synonym: delegate - to give responsibility or work to someone else
  • synonym: entrust - to assign a responsibility to someone
  • synonym: instruct - to give someone information or teach them how to do something
  • synonym: order - to give an instruction with authority
