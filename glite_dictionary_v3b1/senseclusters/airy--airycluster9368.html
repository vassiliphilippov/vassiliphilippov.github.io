airy

adjective
impractical or speculative (idea/plan)

(of ideas, plans, etc.) not practical or realizable; based on speculation rather than reality.

Examples

Airy ideas
He dismissed her airy proposal as hopelessly unrealistic.
The novel is full of airy visions of an ideal future.
Too many airy promises were made during the campaign.

Synonyms

  • impractical
    5.3 3.61/m
    impractical describes plans that are not likely to work in real life, while airy describes ideas based on speculation.
  • unrealistic
    4.0 8.78/m
    unrealistic means not matching what can be achieved in practice, while airy suggests ideas built on fancy or speculation.
  • airy-fairy
    8.8 0.02/m
    airy-fairy feels childish and overly fanciful in speech, while airy is a more formal term for impractical ideas.
  • visionary
    7.4 3.33/m
    visionary describes grand imaginative plans often praised, while airy describes ideas that are speculative and not practical.
