tinderbox

noun
container for tinder to start a fire
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪndɚˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈtɪndəbɒks / (BrE)
A small container used to hold tinder and other materials for starting a fire, typically used before the invention of matches.

Examples

Old tinderbox for fire
Before matches were invented, every household had a tinderbox.
She found a rusty tinderbox among her grandfather’s possessions.
The old explorer carried a tinderbox to light his campfires.
