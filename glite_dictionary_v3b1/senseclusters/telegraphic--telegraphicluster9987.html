telegraphic

adjective
relating to telegraph or telegraphy
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛləˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to telegrams or using a very short, concise style like a telegram

Examples

A telegraphic style
Her answers were short and telegraphic, resembling the style of a telegram.
In the 19th century, telegraphic communication revolutionized long-distance correspondence.
The message was sent in a telegraphic code to ensure secrecy.
His messages to the team are often telegraphic, containing only the essential facts.
She spoke in a telegraphic manner, wasting no words during the meeting.
The judge's summary was so telegraphic that it omitted several crucial details.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concise - expressing much in few words
  • synonym: telegraph - a system for transmitting messages over long distances using coded signals
  • antonym: verbose - using more words than needed
