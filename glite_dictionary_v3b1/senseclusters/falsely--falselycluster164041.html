falsely

adverb
in an untrue or incorrect way
2.3 2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔlsli /; / ˈfɔl.sli / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlsli /; / ˈfɔːls.li / (BrE)
in a way that is not true, correct, or genuine; in an untruthful or insincere manner

Examples

To be falsely accused
He was falsely accused of stealing the money.
The company falsely advertised their product as 100% natural.
The witness falsely claimed to have seen the suspect at the scene.
His statement falsely suggested that he had nothing to do with the incident.
She was accused of falsely claiming the money belonged to her.
They were falsely optimistic about the project's chances of success.
