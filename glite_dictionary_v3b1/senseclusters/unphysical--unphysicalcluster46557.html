unphysical

adjective
not physically possible
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
not consistent with the laws or principles of physics; impossible according to physical reality

Examples

Unphysical results
The scientist dismissed the theory because it predicted unphysical results.
If the numbers are negative, the answer is unphysical and cannot represent a real measurement.
The simulation produced an unphysical scenario that would never occur in nature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: physical - consistent with the laws of physics
