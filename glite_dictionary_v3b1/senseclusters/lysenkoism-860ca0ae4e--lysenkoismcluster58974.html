Lysenkoism

noun
rejection of genetics
Pronunciation: / laɪˈsɛŋkoʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / laɪˈsɛŋkəʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
a set of agricultural theories and practices promoted by Trofim Lysenko in the Soviet Union, rejecting modern genetics in favor of the inheritance of acquired characteristics

Examples

Lysenkoism in farming
During the mid-20th century, Soviet agriculture was heavily influenced by Lysenkoism.
Many scientists opposed Lysenkoism because it denied the principles of modern genetics.
The failure of Lysenkoism led to setbacks in biological research in the Soviet Union.
During the Soviet era, Lysenkoism was widely adopted despite criticism from geneticists.
Many scientists opposed Lysenkoism because it ignored established genetic principles.
The failure of Lysenkoism led to significant setbacks in agricultural productivity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Lamarckism - the theory that acquired traits can be inherited
  • antonym: genetics - the scientific study of heredity and genes
  • synonym: pseudoscience - a system of beliefs mistakenly regarded as scientific
