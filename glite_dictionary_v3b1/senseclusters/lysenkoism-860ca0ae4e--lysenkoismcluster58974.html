Lysenkoism

noun
rejection of genetics
9.9
Pronunciation: /lɪ.ˈsen.koʊ.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈlɪ.zən.koʊ.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /lɪ.ˈsen.kəʊ.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈlɪ.zən.kəʊ.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a set of agricultural theories and practices promoted by Trofim Lysenko in the Soviet Union, rejecting modern genetics in favor of the inheritance of acquired characteristics

Examples

Lysenkoism in farming
During the mid-20th century, Soviet agriculture was heavily influenced by Lysenkoism.
Many scientists opposed Lysenkoism because it denied the principles of modern genetics.
The failure of Lysenkoism led to setbacks in biological research in the Soviet Union.
