coronary sinus

noun
vein collecting blood from the heart muscle
9.0 7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrənˌɛri ˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənəri ˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
a short vein in the heart that collects blood from the heart muscle and delivers it to the right atrium

Examples

The coronary sinus vein
Obstruction of the coronary sinus can lead to problems with blood flow in the heart.
The doctor examined the coronary sinus during the cardiac procedure.
Venous blood from the myocardium returns to the right atrium through the coronary sinus.
