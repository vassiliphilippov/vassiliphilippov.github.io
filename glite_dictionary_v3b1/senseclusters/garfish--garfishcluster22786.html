garfish

noun
long, predatory North American fish
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑrˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large, long-bodied predatory fish found in North America, covered with hard scales and having elongated jaws filled with needle-like teeth.

Examples

The garfish scales
Fishermen often admire the armored scales of the garfish.
The garfish glided silently just below the surface of the river.
The garfish uses its needlelike teeth to catch smaller prey.
