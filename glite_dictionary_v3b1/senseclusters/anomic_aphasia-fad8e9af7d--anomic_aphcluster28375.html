anomic aphasia

noun
difficulty in naming objects (medical)
Pronunciation: / əˈnoʊmɪk əˈfeɪʒə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊmɪk əˈfeɪzɪə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by difficulty in naming objects or retrieving words, while comprehension remains largely intact.

Examples

Anomic aphasia difficulty naming
After the stroke, John was diagnosed with anomic aphasia and struggled to recall names of common objects.
People with anomic aphasia can often describe an object's function, but cannot remember its name.
The neurologist explained that anomic aphasia affects word retrieval more than speech comprehension.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphasia - impairment of language affecting production or comprehension of speech
