licensee

noun
person granted a license
Pronunciation: / ˌlaɪsənˈi / (AmE), / ˌlaɪsənˈiː / (BrE)
a person or organization to whom a license is officially granted, especially the legal right to perform an act or use something

Examples

The licensee agreement
A breach of contract can result in the licensee losing all granted rights.
Each licensee must comply with the terms set by the licensing authority.
The company became a licensee of the patented technology last year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: licensor - the person or organization granting a license
