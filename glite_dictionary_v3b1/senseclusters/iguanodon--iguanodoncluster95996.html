iguanodon

noun
large herbivorous dinosaur of the Cretaceous period
Pronunciation: / ɪˈɡwɑːnəˌdɑn / (AmE), / ɪˈɡwɑːnədɒn / (BrE)
a large, herbivorous dinosaur of the early Cretaceous period, characterized by a long heavy tail and thumb spikes, formerly found in Europe and northern Africa

Examples

A iguanodon dinosaur
As a child, I was fascinated by the model of an iguanodon at the natural history museum.
Scientists believe that the iguanodon used its thumb spikes for self-defense against predators.
The fossilized remains of an iguanodon were discovered in southern England.
