larceny

noun
theft; unlawful taking of property
7.2 1.14/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ëÀêrs…ôni / (AmE), / Ààl…ëÀês…ôni / (BrE)
the unlawful taking of someone else's property with the intent to permanently deprive them of it; theft

Examples

Charge of larceny
He was charged with larceny after stealing several items from the store.
Petty larceny is considered a misdemeanor in many jurisdictions.
The court found her guilty of grand larceny due to the high value of the stolen goods.

Synonyms

  • theft
    1.1 17/m
    theft is taking someone else's property to keep it, while larceny is the legal name for that crime.
  • stealing
    1.0 0.14/m
    stealing is the everyday word for taking someone's property without permission, while larceny is the formal legal term used in court.
  • thievery
    5.6 0.75/m
    thievery is a general word for stealing from people or shops, while larceny is the precise legal crime term.
  • thieving
    3.7 0.69/m
    thieving is the act or habit of stealing from others, while larceny is the defined legal offense used in criminal cases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burglary - breaking into a building to commit a crime, usually theft
  • synonym: robbery - the act of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat
  • synonym: theft - the act of stealing something
