noop

verb
to do nothing (computing)
9.9
Pronunciation: /nuːp/ /; / /nʊp/ (AmE), /nuːp/ /; / /nʊp/ (BrE)
to execute or insert an operation that intentionally does nothing, especially in computing for timing, alignment, or testing purposes

Examples

To noop a process
The programmer decided to noop in the loop to maintain consistent timing between operations.
Sometimes, developers noop a function to temporarily disable its effect during testing.
To align the instructions, the compiler will noop at certain points in the code.

Related Terms

  • antonym: execute - to carry out an instruction or operation
  • synonym: no-op - an operation that has no effect
