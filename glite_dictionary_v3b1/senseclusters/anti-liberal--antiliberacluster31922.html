anti-liberal

noun
opponent of liberalism
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈlɪbərəl / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈlɪbərəl / (BrE)
a person who opposes liberal beliefs, policies, or political groups

Examples

An anti-liberal politician
The candidate was described as an anti-liberal because of his opposition to progressive reforms.
Some media outlets portrayed the protestors as anti-liberals challenging the new government policies.
Her anti-liberal views often sparked heated debates with her classmates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - a person who favors traditional values and is cautious about change
  • antonym: liberal - a person who supports liberal beliefs or policies
