nonspeech

noun
nonverbal signals
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑnˌspiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌspiːtʃ / (BrE)
behavior or signals, such as sounds, gestures, or facial expressions, that are not spoken words but can convey meaning

Examples

Child's nonspeech signals
The therapist observed the child's nonspeech to better understand their emotional state.
Animals often rely on nonspeech cues, such as body language and sounds, to communicate.
Researchers recorded infants' nonspeech to study early forms of communication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nonverbal communication - communication without spoken words, using gestures, facial expressions, or body language
  • antonym: speech - spoken words used to communicate
