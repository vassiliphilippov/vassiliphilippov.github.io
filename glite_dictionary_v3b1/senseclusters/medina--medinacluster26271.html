medina

noun
old city quarter in northern Africa
Pronunciation: / məˈdiːnə / (AmE), / məˈdiːnə / (BrE)
The old, usually walled quarter of a city in northern Africa, typically characterized by narrow streets and traditional architecture.

Examples

The medina quarter
Each North African city has its own unique medina that reflects its history and culture.
The narrow alleys of the medina are filled with shops and market stalls.
Tourists love exploring the vibrant medina in Marrakech with its ancient walls and bustling atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kasbah - the citadel or fortress in many North African cities
