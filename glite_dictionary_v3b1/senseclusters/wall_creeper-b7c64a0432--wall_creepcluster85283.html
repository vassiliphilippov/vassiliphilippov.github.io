wall creeper

noun
crimson-and-grey Eurasian songbird
Pronunciation: / wɔl ˈkripər / (AmE), / wɔːl ˈkriːpə / (BrE)
a small crimson-and-grey songbird (Tichodroma muraria) that lives on town walls and mountain cliffs in southern Eurasia and northern Africa

Examples

The wall creeper bird
Birdwatchers often travel to southern Europe to catch a glimpse of the elusive wall creeper.
The wall creeper is known for its distinctive crimson wings and its ability to cling to vertical surfaces.
We spotted a wall creeper flitting among the rocky cliffs during our hike.
