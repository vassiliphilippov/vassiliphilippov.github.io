photocatalyst

noun
light-activated catalyst
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˈkætəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈkætəlɪst / (BrE)
a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction when exposed to light, without being consumed itself

Examples

A photocatalyst substance
A photocatalyst can help remove harmful chemicals from the air when exposed to sunlight.
Researchers developed a new photocatalyst to make water purification more efficient.
The effectiveness of the photocatalyst depends on the intensity of the light source.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalyst - a substance that speeds up a chemical reaction without being consumed
