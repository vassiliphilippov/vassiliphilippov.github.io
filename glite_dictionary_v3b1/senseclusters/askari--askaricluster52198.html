askari

noun
East African soldier
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈskɑːri / (AmE), / ɑːˈskɑːri / (BrE)
a soldier or police officer in East Africa, especially one serving under colonial rule or European command

Examples

Colonial askari
During the colonial period, many askari served in the British and German armies in East Africa.
The museum displayed uniforms once worn by askari in the early twentieth century.
Local askari were often responsible for maintaining order in rural communities.
The askari guarded the entrance to the outpost during the night.
Many askari were recruited from local communities by colonial authorities.
A group of askari marched through the village in their uniforms.
