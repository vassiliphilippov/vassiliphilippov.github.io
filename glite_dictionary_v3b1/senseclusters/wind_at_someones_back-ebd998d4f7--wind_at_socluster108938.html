wind at someone's back

noun
having support or favorable conditions
Pronunciation: / wɪnd æt ˈsʌmwʌnz bæk / (AmE), / wɪnd æt ˈsʌmwənz bæk / (BrE)
enjoying support or favorable conditions that help to achieve success or make progress easier

Examples

The wind at her back

They launched the product with the wind at their back, thanks to the booming market.
With the wind at his back, the candidate won the election easily.
