right of way

noun
priority to proceed (traffic law)
8.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /raɪt əv weɪ/ /; / /raɪt ə weɪ/ (AmE), /raɪt əv weɪ/ /; / /raɪt ə weɪ/ (BrE)
the legal right to proceed before other vehicles or vessels in a particular situation, such as at intersections or on waterways.

Examples

Right of way at intersections
At the intersection, the driver on the right has the right of way.
Failure to yield the right of way can result in a traffic ticket.
Sailboats usually have right of way over powerboats in open water.
