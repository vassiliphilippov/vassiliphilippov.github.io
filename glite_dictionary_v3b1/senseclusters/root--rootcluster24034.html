root

noun
plant organ that anchors and absorbs nutrients
1.0 35/m
Pronunciation: / rut / (AmE), / ruÀêt / (BrE)
The part of a plant, typically underground, that anchors it in the soil and absorbs water and nutrients.

Examples

A plant root
Carrots store nutrients in their thick, edible root.
If you damage the root during transplanting, the plant may not survive.
The tree's root system helps prevent soil erosion after heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shoot - the part of a plant that grows above the ground
  • antonym: stem - the main above-ground structural part of a plant
