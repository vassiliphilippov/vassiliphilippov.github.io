syncarp

noun
compound fruit from multiple ovaries
8.7 9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪŋ.kɑːrp/ /; / /ˈsɪn.kɑːrp/ (AmE), /ˈsɪŋ.kɑːp/ /; / /ˈsɪn.kɑːp/ (BrE)
A fruit formed from many individual small fruits or drupes that develop from separate ovaries within a common receptacle, as in the blackberry, raspberry, or pineapple.

Examples

A syncarp fruit
Botanists classify the raspberry as a syncarp because it develops from multiple ovaries.
The blackberry is a syncarp consisting of numerous small drupes clustered together.
The structure of a pineapple exemplifies a unique form of syncarp seen in some tropical fruits.
