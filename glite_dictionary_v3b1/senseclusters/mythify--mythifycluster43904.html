mythify

verb
to make legendary
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪθɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪθɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something or someone seem like a myth or legendary figure, often by exaggerating their qualities or achievements

Examples

To mythify a hero
The documentary tried to mythify the life of the famous explorer, making him seem larger than life.
Some fans tend to mythify celebrities, ignoring their flaws and focusing only on their achievements.
Historians warn against efforts to mythify political leaders, as it can distort the truth.
The documentary tried to mythify the life of the famous explorer, making his achievements seem almost magical.
Some authors mythify historical events to make their stories more exciting and dramatic.
It is easy to mythify the past and forget the real challenges people faced.
The documentary tried to mythify the life of the famous artist, making him seem larger than life.

Writers often mythify historical figures to make their stories more exciting and memorable.

  • synonym: romanticize - to make something seem more attractive or ideal than it really is
