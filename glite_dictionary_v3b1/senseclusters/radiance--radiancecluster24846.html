radiance

noun
amount of electromagnetic radiation at a surface point
1.4 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.diː.əns/ /; / /ˈreɪ.dɪ.əns/ (AmE), /ˈreɪ.dɪ.əns/ /; / /ˈreɪ.diː.əns/ (BrE)
the amount of electromagnetic radiation leaving or arriving at a point on a surface, typically measured per unit area and per unit solid angle

Examples

Thermal radiance
An increase in thermal radiance indicates a hotter surface temperature.
Astronomers measure the radiance of distant stars to determine their energy output.
The radiance of the sun at Earth's surface is affected by atmospheric conditions.
