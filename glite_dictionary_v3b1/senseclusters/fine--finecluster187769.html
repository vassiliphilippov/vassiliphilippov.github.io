fine

adjective
of high or specified purity (esp. metals)
Pronunciation: / faɪn / (AmE), / faɪn / (BrE)
(of metal or materials) free from impurities; having a high or specified degree of purity.

Examples

Fine gold
The jeweler only purchases fine gold that meets strict purity standards.
They assured me the necklace was made of fine platinum, not an alloy.
This coin is struck in 99.9% fine silver, making it highly valuable to collectors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pure - free from contamination or impurities
