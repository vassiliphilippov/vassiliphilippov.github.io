collation

noun
rules for sorting and comparing text (computing)
7.3
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(computing) a set of rules that determines how text is compared and put in order, usually language- or locale-specific; these rules govern character order, case sensitivity, and how accents are treated.

Examples

Database collation
The database uses a Finnish collation to sort characters properly.
Choose the right collation when importing data so accented letters appear in order.
Different languages require different collations, which affects search and sort results in applications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sort order - the ordering used to arrange text or items, often determined by collation rules
  • synonym: sorting - the process of arranging items or text into a sequence according to rules such as collation
