scrap

verb
dispose of something as useless or old
1.0
Pronunciation: / skræp / (AmE), / skræp / (BrE)
to throw away, discard, or break up something that is no longer useful

Examples

To scrap an idea
After several failed attempts, the engineers finally scrapped the whole project.
The government plans to scrap outdated regulations.
We decided to scrap the old washing machine and buy a new one.
The airline plans to scrap several older planes due to high maintenance costs.
The company decided to scrap the outdated machinery and invest in new equipment.
We had no choice but to scrap the old car after it broke down again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discard - to get rid of something that is no longer useful or wanted
  • synonym: junk - to throw away something as worthless
  • antonym: keep - to retain possession of something
  • synonym: salvage - to save useful parts from something that is damaged or going to be thrown away
