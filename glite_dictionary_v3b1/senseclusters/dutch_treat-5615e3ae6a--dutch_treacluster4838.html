Dutch treat

noun
event where each person pays their own way
9.3 4.8
Pronunciation: /dʌtʃ triːt/ /; / /dʌtʃ tʃriːt/ (AmE), /dʌtʃ triːt/ /; / /dʌtʃ tʃriːt/ (BrE)
An occasion, such as a meal or outing, where each person pays for their own expenses rather than one person paying for the group.

Examples

A Dutch treat meal
Let's make it a Dutch treat this time, since it's easier to split the costs.
The company picnic was a Dutch treat, with each employee responsible for their own food and drinks.
We went out for dinner on a Dutch treat basis, so everyone paid their own bill.

Related Terms
