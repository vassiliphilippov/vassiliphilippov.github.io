lumbar vertebra

noun
vertebrae in lower back
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌm.bɑr vərˈtiː.brə / (AmE), / ˈlʌm.bɑː vɜːˈtiː.brə / (BrE)
any of the five vertebrae in the lower part of the human spine, between the thoracic vertebrae and the sacrum

Examples

The lumbar vertebra fracture
After the accident, the X-ray revealed a fracture in the third lumbar vertebra.
Lower back pain often originates in the lumbar vertebrae due to poor posture or injury.
Surgeons had to insert screws into his lumbar vertebrae to stabilize his spine.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: sacral vertebra - any of the vertebrae fused to form the sacrum, below the lumbar region
  • degree higher: thoracic vertebra - any of the twelve vertebrae in the middle section of the spine
