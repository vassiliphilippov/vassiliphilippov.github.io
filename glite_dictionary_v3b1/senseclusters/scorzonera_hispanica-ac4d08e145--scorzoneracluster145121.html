Scorzonera hispanica

noun
European herb with edible black roots
9.9
Pronunciation: / skɔrˌzoʊˈnɛrə hɪˈspænɪkə / (AmE), / skɔːˌzəʊˈnɪərə hɪˈspannɪkə / (BrE)
A perennial European herb with narrow leaves, yellow flower heads, and long, black, edible roots shaped like carrots; commonly called black salsify.

Examples

Scorzonera hispanica black roots
Scorzonera hispanica is often cultivated in European gardens for its nutritious black roots.
Chefs sometimes use Scorzonera hispanica as a substitute for asparagus or parsnip in gourmet dishes.
The roots of Scorzonera hispanica must be peeled and boiled before eating.
