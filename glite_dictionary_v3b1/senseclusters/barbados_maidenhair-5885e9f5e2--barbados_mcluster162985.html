Barbados maidenhair

noun
a species of tropical maidenhair fern
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɑːrˈbeɪdoʊz ˈmeɪd(ə)nhɛr / (AmE), / bɑːˈbeɪdəʊz ˈmeɪd(ə)nhɛː / (BrE)
a species of maidenhair fern (Adiantum tenerum), native to tropical regions, noted for its delicate, fan-shaped fronds; named after a country house in Barbados where it was first identified.

Examples

The Barbados maidenhair fern
Because of its unique appearance, the Barbados maidenhair is popular among tropical fern enthusiasts.
Botanists discovered several specimens of Barbados maidenhair growing in a shady garden on the island.
The Barbados maidenhair is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its graceful foliage.
