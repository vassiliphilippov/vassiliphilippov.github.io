bill of review

noun
procedure to review a court decree
Pronunciation: / bɪl əv rɪˈvjuː / (AmE), / bɪl əv rɪˈvjuː / (BrE)
a legal proceeding in which a party seeks an explanation, alteration, or reversal of a decree by the same court that issued it

Examples

A bill of review filed
A bill of review must present new evidence or legal errors in the original decree.
Lawyers discussed whether a bill of review could overturn the previous verdict.
The losing party filed a bill of review to challenge the judge's decision.
