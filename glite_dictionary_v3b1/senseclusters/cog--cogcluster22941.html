cog

verb
to cheat or rig
Pronunciation: / kɑɡ / (AmE), / kɒɡ / (BrE)
to cheat or rig something, especially by dishonest manipulation.

Examples

To cog the results
They were cogging the ballot counts to make sure their candidate won the election.
The referee caught the player cogging the score during the match.
Some companies have been accused of cogging the results to appear more successful.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • antonym: play fair - to act honestly and follow the rules
  • synonym: rig - to arrange or influence something dishonestly for a desired result
