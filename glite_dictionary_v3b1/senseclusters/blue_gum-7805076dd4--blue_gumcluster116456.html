blue gum

noun
a species of eucalyptus tree
8.0 5.7
Pronunciation: / bluː ɡʌm / (AmE), / bluː ɡʌm / (BrE)
a tall, fast-growing eucalyptus tree (Eucalyptus globulus) with bluish young leaves and leaves containing a medicinal oil, grown for timber and shelter

Examples

The blue gum tree
The blue gum is widely planted in Australia and other warm climates for its high-quality timber.
The essential oil extracted from blue gum leaves is used in a variety of medicinal products.
Young blue gum leaves are often bluish, giving the tree its common name.
