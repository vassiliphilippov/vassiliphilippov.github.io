law

noun
legal rule or statute
Pronunciation: / lɔ / (AmE), / lɔː / (BrE)
a rule or system of rules made and enforced by a government or authority to control people's behavior

Examples

Break a law
Breaking a law can result in a fine or imprisonment.
He studied the law concerning intellectual property before filing his patent.
The government passed a new law to protect endangered species.
Everyone is expected to respect the law if society is to function smoothly.
She decided to study law at university.
The law prohibits smoking in all public buildings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bylaw - a rule made by a company or society to control its members
  • degree higher: constitution - a system of fundamental principles or established precedents according to which a state is governed
  • degree lower: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something, which may form a basis for law
  • synonym: regulation - a rule or directive made and maintained by an authority
  • synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
