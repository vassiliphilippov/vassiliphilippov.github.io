paleopathology

noun
study of ancient diseases via fossils
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪlioʊpæˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpaliəʊpæˈθɒlədʒi /; / ˌpæliəʊpæˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of diseases in ancient times, especially as revealed by fossil, skeletal, or mummified remains.

Examples

Paleopathology study ancient diseases
Paleopathology has provided insights into how tuberculosis affected ancient populations.
He specializes in paleopathology, analyzing skeletal remains for signs of historical illnesses.
The field of paleopathology combines archaeology and medical research to trace disease origins.
Recent advances in palaeopathology have identified new diseases from ancient remains.
She specializes in palaeopathology, focusing on infections visible in mummified bodies.
Thanks to palaeopathology, we can understand the prevalence of arthritis in prehistoric humans.
