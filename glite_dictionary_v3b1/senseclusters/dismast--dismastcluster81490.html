dismast

verb
to remove ship's mast
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈmæst / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɑːst / (BrE)
to remove or lose the mast or masts of a ship, usually as a result of damage or accident

Examples

To dismast a ship
The violent storm threatened to dismast the old sailing vessel.
After being dismasted in rough seas, the crew worked quickly to stabilize the ship.
A sudden gust of wind can easily dismast a poorly maintained boat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demast - to remove the mast from a ship
  • antonym: rig - to equip a ship with masts and sails
