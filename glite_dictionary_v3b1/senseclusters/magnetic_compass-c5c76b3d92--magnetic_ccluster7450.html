magnetic compass

noun
navigation device with magnetic needle
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˈkʌmpəs / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˈkʌmpəs / (BrE)
A compass that uses a magnetized needle or indicator to show direction relative to the Earth's magnetic north.

Examples

A magnetic compass needle
Accurate use of a magnetic compass requires understanding the difference between true north and magnetic north.
Some explorers still prefer using a magnetic compass over digital devices for navigation.
The captain checked the magnetic compass to determine the ship's heading.
