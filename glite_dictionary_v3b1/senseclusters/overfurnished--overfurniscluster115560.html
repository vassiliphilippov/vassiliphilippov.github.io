overfurnished

adjective
too much furniture
6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.fɜːr.nɪʃt/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.fɜː.nɪʃt/ (BrE)
containing more furniture than is needed or suitable for the space

Examples

An overfurnished room
The hotel suite was so overfurnished that there was barely any space to walk.
An overfurnished office can make it difficult to concentrate and move around.
They decided to remove a few chairs because the dining room looked overfurnished.
The living room felt cramped and uncomfortable because it was overfurnished with large sofas and tables.
Her essay was overfurnished with unnecessary details, making it difficult to follow the main argument.
They realized the office was overfurnished and decided to remove some desks to create more space.
