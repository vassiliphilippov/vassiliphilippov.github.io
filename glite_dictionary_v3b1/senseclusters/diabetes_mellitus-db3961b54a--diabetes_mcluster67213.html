diabetes mellitus

noun
chronic disease of high blood sugar due to insulin deficiency
5.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tiːz mə.ˈlaɪ.təs/ /; / /daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tɪs mə.ˈlaɪ.təs/ (AmE), /daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tiːz mə.ˈlaɪ.təs/ /; / /daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tɪs mə.ˈlaɪ.təs/ (BrE)
A chronic medical condition in which the body is unable to properly regulate blood sugar levels due to a deficiency or ineffectiveness of insulin, typically characterized by excessive urination (polyuria), increased thirst, and high blood glucose.

Examples

Diabetes mellitus symptoms
Advances in treatment have improved the quality of life for those living with diabetes mellitus.
Many people with diabetes mellitus require regular monitoring of their blood sugar levels.
The risk of cardiovascular disease increases significantly in patients diagnosed with diabetes mellitus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diabetes insipidus - rare form of diabetes not related to blood sugar
  • degree lower: type 1 diabetes - form of diabetes mellitus characterized by lack of insulin production
  • degree higher: type 2 diabetes - form of diabetes mellitus characterized by insulin resistance
