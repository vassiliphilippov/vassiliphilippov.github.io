informed

adjective
having much knowledge or education
2.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɔrmd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːmd / (BrE)
having or showing a lot of knowledge about a particular subject or situation, often as a result of careful study or reliable information

Examples

Well informed consumer
As a well informed consumer, she always researches products before buying.
It is important to be informed before voting in any election.
The committee made an informed decision based on the latest research.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • synonym: knowledgeable - well informed; having much knowledge
