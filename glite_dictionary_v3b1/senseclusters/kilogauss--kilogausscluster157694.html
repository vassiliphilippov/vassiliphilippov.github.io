kilogauss

noun
magnetic unit
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪloʊˌɡaʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɪləˌɡaʊs / (BrE)
a unit of magnetic flux density equal to one thousand gauss

Examples

One kilogauss unit
The laboratory magnet reached a strength of 5 kilogauss during the experiment.
A kilogauss is often used to describe the intensity of strong magnetic fields.
The engineer measured the magnetic field at exactly one kilogauss.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: tesla - the SI unit of magnetic flux density, equal to 10,000 gauss
