capture

noun
forcible taking of property
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.tʃɚ /; / ˈkæptʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tʃə /; / ˈkæptʃə / (BrE)
the act of catching, seizing, or taking control of someone or something, often by force

Examples

The capture of a city

The army's capture of the rebel leader's estate was seen as a decisive victory.
Her capture by enemy soldiers was reported in the morning news.
The capture of the fugitive ended a week-long manhunt.
The general ordered the capture of the city.

  • antonym: release - the act of letting something go that was previously captured
  • synonym: seizure - the act of taking something by force
