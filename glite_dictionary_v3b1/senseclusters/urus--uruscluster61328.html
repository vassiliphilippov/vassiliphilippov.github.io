urus

noun
extinct European wild ox, ancestor of cattle
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈjʊrəs / (AmE), / ˈjʊərəs / (BrE)
a large, recently extinct European wild ox, also known as the aurochs, regarded as one of the ancestors of domestic cattle

Examples

The urus remains
Scientists have studied urus remains to understand the domestication of cattle.
The urus once roamed the forests of Europe in great herds.
The extinction of the urus marked the end of Europe's wild giant cattle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aurochs - another name for the extinct European wild ox, ancestor of domestic cattle
