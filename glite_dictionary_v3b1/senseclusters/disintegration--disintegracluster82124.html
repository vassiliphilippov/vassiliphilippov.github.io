disintegration

noun
total destruction; complete collapse
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˌteɡrəˈʃən /; / ˌdɪsɪnˌtɪɡrəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˌtɪɡrəˈʃən /; / ˌdɪsɪnˌtɪɡrəˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of breaking apart, decomposing, or being destroyed into smaller parts or fragments

Examples

Material disintegration
Environmental factors can accelerate the disintegration of natural materials over time.
Historians study the gradual disintegration of ancient civilizations after invasions.
The atomic bomb led to the disintegration of the entire city.
Exposure to harsh chemicals caused the disintegration of the fabric.
His sudden resignation led to the disintegration of the leadership team.
The rapid disintegration of the ancient empire shocked historians.
Exposure to water accelerated the disintegration of the old manuscript.
Social unrest led to the disintegration of the once-united community.
The slow disintegration of the ancient wall was evident after years of neglect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking or being broken into fragments
  • antonym: integration - the process of combining or adding parts to make a whole
  • synonym: shattering - breaking into many pieces
