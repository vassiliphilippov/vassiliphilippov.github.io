financial bloodbath

noun
severe financial loss or destruction
Pronunciation: / fəˈnænʃəl ˈblʌdˌbæθ / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəl ˈblʌdˌbɑːθ / (BrE)
a situation involving severe financial loss or destruction, especially in markets or investments

Examples

A financial bloodbath
After the company's earnings report was released, the stock suffered a financial bloodbath in the market.
Analysts warned that a sudden interest rate hike could trigger a financial bloodbath on Wall Street.
Many investors lost their life savings during the financial bloodbath of the recession.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crash - a sudden and severe fall in prices or value, especially in financial markets
  • synonym: meltdown - a rapid and disastrous decline in value or performance, especially in the financial sector
