on its merits

adverb
judged by actual qualities alone
3.2 5.0
Pronunciation: /ɑːn ɪts ˈmer.ɪts/ /; / /ɔːn ɪts ˈmer.ɪts/ (AmE), /ɒn ɪts ˈmer.ɪts/ (BrE)
considered or judged according to its own qualities and facts, rather than influenced by external factors, reputation, or previous assumptions.

Examples

Decide on its merits
The committee decided to assess each case on its merits rather than applying a blanket policy.
The grant applications will be reviewed strictly on its merits without favoritism.
We believe that every student’s performance should be judged on its merits.
