lacquer tree

noun
tree yielding sap for making lacquer
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlækər triː / (AmE), / ˈlakə triː / (BrE)
A small tree native to Asia that produces a toxic sap used to make lacquer.

Examples

The lacquer tree sap
Artisans have relied on the lacquer tree for centuries to produce durable and glossy finishes.
Due to its toxic exudate, the lacquer tree must be handled with care during cultivation.
The sap of the lacquer tree is harvested and processed to create traditional Japanese lacquerware.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Toxicodendron vernicifluum - the botanical name for the lacquer tree
