preemptive right

noun
right to buy new company shares first
6.4 7.2
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈempt.ɪv raɪt/ (AmE), /priː.ˈempt.ɪv raɪt/ (BrE)
The right of existing shareholders to have the first opportunity to purchase new shares issued by a company, in order to maintain their proportion of ownership and avoid dilution.

Examples

Exercise preemptive right
As a shareholder, he exercised his preemptive right to buy additional shares before they were offered to the public.
The company's charter guarantees a preemptive right to all common stockholders.
Without a preemptive right, minority investors might see their ownership diluted in subsequent funding rounds.
