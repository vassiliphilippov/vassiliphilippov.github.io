finder's fee

noun
payment for facilitating a business deal
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪndərz fiː / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndəz fiː / (BrE)
a payment made to someone for facilitating a business deal or introducing parties in a transaction, especially for securing financial backing

Examples

A finder's fee payment
He was entitled to a finder's fee for helping secure the investment deal.
The consultant received a finder's fee after introducing two companies that later merged.
Their agency charged a finder's fee whenever they connected clients with new business partners.
