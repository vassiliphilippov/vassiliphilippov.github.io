Edward V

proper noun
King of England (1470–1483), one of the 'Princes in the Tower'
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈed.wərd ðə fɪfθ/ /; / /ˈed.wərd viː/ (AmE), /ˈed.wəd ðə fɪfθ/ /; / /ˈed.wəd viː/ (BrE)
(1470–1483) King of England who ascended the throne at age 13, but was confined to the Tower of London and disappeared, presumed murdered, along with his brother.

Examples

Edward V king of England
As a child king, Edward V never got the chance to rule England independently.
Many historians debate the fate of Edward V, who vanished with his brother from the Tower of London.
Shakespeare's play 'Richard III' references the tragic story of Edward V.
