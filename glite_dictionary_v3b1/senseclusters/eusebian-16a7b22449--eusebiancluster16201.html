Eusebian

adjective
of Gospel cross-references
9.9
Pronunciation: / juːˈsiːbiən / (AmE), / juːˈsiːbiən / (BrE)
relating to the system of Gospel cross-references, known as the Eusebian canons, used to compare parallel passages in the Gospels.

Examples

Eusebian canon tables
The ancient manuscript included beautifully decorated Eusebian canon tables at the beginning.
Scholars use Eusebian references to compare similar stories across the four Gospels.
The Eusebian system made it easier for readers to find parallel passages in the Bible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canonical - relating to or following a recognized set of rules or standards, especially in religious texts
