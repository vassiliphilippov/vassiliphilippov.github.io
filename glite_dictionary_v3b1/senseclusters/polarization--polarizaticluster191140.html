polarization

noun
the condition of having polarity
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊlərəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌpoʊlərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the condition or process in which something acquires or has polarity, especially in physics or chemistry.

Examples

Electric polarization
Certain materials allow easy polarization under an electric field.
The polarization of water molecules gives it unique properties.
The experiment measured the degree of polarization in the sample.
Electrical polarisation occurs when there is a separation of positive and negative charges in a material.
Researchers studied the polarisation of light waves passing through the crystal.
The polarisation of the molecule affects its interactions with other substances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polarity - the property of having distinct and opposite charges or characteristics
  • synonym: polarization - US spelling of the same concept; the condition of having or imparting polarity
