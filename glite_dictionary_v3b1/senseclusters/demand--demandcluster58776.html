demand

verb
claim as due or as a right
1.0 2.3
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈmænd/ /; / /də.ˈmænd/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈmɑːnd/ /; / /də.ˈmɑːnd/ (BrE)
to ask for or claim something forcefully, especially as a right or requirement

Examples

To demand an explanation
She demanded an explanation for the unexpected charges.
The city council demands compliance with building codes from all developers.
The employees demanded higher wages during the negotiation.
During the meeting, several employees demanded an explanation for the policy change.
She demanded to know the truth behind the sudden cancellation.
The manager demanded an immediate response from his team.
Police demanded that the suspect surrender his weapon.
She demanded to speak with the manager immediately after the incident.
The workers demanded better pay and safer working conditions.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: ask - to say something in order to obtain an answer or information
  • synonym: claim - to assert ownership or right to something
  • degree higher: command - to order someone to do something with authority
  • degree higher: insist - to demand something forcefully, not accepting refusal
  • degree lower: request - to politely or formally ask for something
