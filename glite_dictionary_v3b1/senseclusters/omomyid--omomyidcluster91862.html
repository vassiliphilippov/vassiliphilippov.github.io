omomyid

noun
extinct group of early primates
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmoʊ.maɪd/ /; / /oʊ.ˈmoʊ.maɪd/ (AmE), /əʊ.ˈmɒ.maɪd/ /; / /ə.ˈməʊ.maɪd/ (BrE)
a member of an extinct group of small, nocturnal primates that lived in North America and Europe about 30 to 50 million years ago, believed to be related to tarsiers and possibly ancestral to anthropoids

Examples

Fossil omomyid primates
Fossils of omomyid primates have been found in both Europe and North America.
Some scientists debate whether omomyids were ancestral to modern tarsiers or anthropoids.
The omomyid lineage provides important clues about early primate evolution.
