blood-twig

noun
European shrub, turns red in autumn
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌd ˌtwɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈblʌd ˌtwɪɡ / (BrE)
A European deciduous shrub (Cornus sanguinea), which turns red in autumn and produces dull white flowers, commonly known as dogwood.

Examples

The blood-twig shrub
In Europe, blood-twig is often used as an ornamental shrub in parks and gardens.
She planted a blood-twig hedge for its attractive autumn colors and white spring blossoms.
The garden featured a display of blood-twig shrubs, their leaves blazing red in the fall.
