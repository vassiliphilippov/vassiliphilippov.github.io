chinampa

noun
Aztec farming island
9.9
Pronunciation: / tʃɪˈnɑːmpə / (AmE), / tʃɪˈnæmpə / (BrE)
an artificial island built in shallow lake beds for farming, especially used by the Aztecs

Examples

A chinampa farm
The Aztecs developed chinampas to grow crops on the shallow lakes around their city.
A single chinampa could produce several harvests each year due to its fertile soil.
Modern farmers in Mexico still use the chinampa method to cultivate vegetables.
