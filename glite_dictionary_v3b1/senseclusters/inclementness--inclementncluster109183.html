inclementness

noun
state of being severe (weather)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈklɛməntnɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈklɛmən(t)nɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being inclement, especially in reference to weather; severity or harshness of weather conditions.

Examples

The inclementness of weather
Farmers are particularly vulnerable to the inclementness of the local climate.
The inclementness of the winter forced the school to close for several days.
We sheltered indoors to escape the inclementness of the coastal storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mildness - the quality of being moderate or gentle (weather)
  • synonym: severity - the fact or condition of being severe
