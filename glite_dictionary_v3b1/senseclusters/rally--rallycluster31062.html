rally

verb
to tease or mock repeatedly
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
to tease or mock someone, often in a good-natured or persistent way

Examples

To rally a friend
His friends rallied him about his new haircut all afternoon.
She was rallied mercilessly by her colleagues on her first day at the new job.
The siblings would often rally each other over small mistakes, but it was all in fun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mock - to tease or laugh at in a scornful or contemptuous manner
  • synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke in a playful or annoying way
