inflame

verb
cause inflammation in a body part
3.7
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfleɪm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪm / (BrE)
to cause a body part to become red, swollen, and painful (to become inflamed, usually because of infection or injury)

Examples

To inflame a wound
Allergic reactions may inflame the lining of the nose, causing discomfort.
Poor posture over time can inflame the muscles in your back.
Repetitive motion can inflame the tendons in your wrist.
After catching a cold, his throat would often inflame and feel sore.
Her eyes began to inflame after she spent hours swimming in the pool.
If you scratch the rash, it may inflame further and become more painful.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irritate - cause slight inflammation or discomfort in body tissue
