curst

adjective
deserving a curse; intensifier (archaic)
6.9
Pronunciation: / kɝst / (AmE), / kɜːst / (BrE)
(archaic) Deserving a curse; sometimes used for emphasis or as an intensifier.

Examples

A curst fate
He considered the misfortune to be the result of a curst fate.
In many old folktales, the curst forest is home to vengeful spirits and lost souls.
With a curst look, she warned him never to return to the village.

  • synonym: cursed - deserving or invoking a curse; afflicted or damned
