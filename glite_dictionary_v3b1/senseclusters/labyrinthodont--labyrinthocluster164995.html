labyrinthodont

noun
extinct primitive amphibian
Pronunciation: / ˌlæbəˈrɪnθəˌdɑnt / (AmE), / ˌlæbəˈrɪnθədɒnt / (BrE)
any of a group of extinct, primitive amphibians of the superorder Labyrinthodontia, characterized by complex infolding of the tooth enamel.

Examples

A labyrinthodont amphibian
A distinctive feature of every labyrinthodont is the intricate pattern within its teeth.
Scientists believe labyrinthodonts were among the earliest vertebrates to transition from water to land.
The fossilized remains of a labyrinthodont were discovered in the sedimentary layers of the valley.
