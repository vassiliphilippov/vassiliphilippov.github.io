Nabalus serpentarius

noun
a perennial herb of the U.S. with pinkish flowers and thick leaves
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnæb.ə.ləs sɜːr.pən.ˈter.ɪ.əs/ (AmE), /ˈnæb.ə.ləs sɜː.pən.ˈteə.rɪ.əs/ (BrE)
A perennial herb native to the southern and eastern United States, with drooping clusters of pinkish flowers and thick basal leaves resembling a lion's foot; sometimes classified in the genus Prenanthes.

Examples

Nabalus serpentarius plant
Nabalus serpentarius is commonly found in open woodlands throughout the southeastern United States.
Many botanists still refer to Nabalus serpentarius by its older genus name, Prenanthes.
The thick basal leaves of Nabalus serpentarius are said to resemble a lion’s foot.
