ammonoid

noun
extinct coiled fossil mollusk
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈæməˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈamənɔɪd / (BrE)
an extinct group of marine mollusks with coiled, chambered shells, commonly found as fossils

Examples

Fossil ammonoids
Many geologists use the presence of ammonoids to help date the age of certain rock layers.
Scientists are fascinated by the complex suture patterns in ammonoid shells.
The museum's fossil exhibit features a remarkably well-preserved ammonoid shell dating back millions of years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ammonite - an extinct cephalopod mollusk with a spiral shell, a subgroup of ammonoids
