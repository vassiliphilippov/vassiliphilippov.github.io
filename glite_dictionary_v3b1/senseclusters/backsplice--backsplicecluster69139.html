backsplice

verb
to form circular RNA
Pronunciation: / ˈbæksplaɪs / (AmE), / ˈbæksplaɪs / (BrE)
to join segments of an RNA molecule in reverse order, resulting in the formation of a circular RNA.

Examples

To backsplice RNA segments
Scientists can backsplice exons to create stable circular RNA molecules in the laboratory.
The process of backsplicing is essential for understanding certain gene regulation mechanisms.
Researchers observed increased backsplicing activity in specific cell types during the experiment.
