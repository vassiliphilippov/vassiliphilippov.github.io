white-hot

adjective
extremely intense or passionate
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌhɑt /; / ˈwaɪt hɑt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌhɒt /; / ˈwaɪt hɒt / (BrE)
extremely intense, passionate, or energetic; showing strong feelings or attracting a lot of attention

Examples

White-hot passion
Her white-hot ambition drove her to achieve every goal she set.
The protestors displayed white-hot anger at the proposed law.
The writer's white-hot creativity produced three novels in one year.
The athlete flourished under the white-hot spotlight of national attention.
The blacksmith removed the white-hot iron from the forge, ready to shape it.
The debate reached a white-hot level of intensity during the final hour.
