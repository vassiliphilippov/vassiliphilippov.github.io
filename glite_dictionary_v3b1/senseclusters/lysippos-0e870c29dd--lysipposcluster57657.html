Lysippos

proper noun
Greek sculptor, 4th century BC
Pronunciation: / laɪˈsɪp.ɒs / (AmE), / lɪˈsɪp.ɒs / (BrE)
(c. 4th century BC) A prominent Greek sculptor known for his influence on classical art and as Alexander the Great's court sculptor.

Examples

Lysippos Greek sculptor
Lysippos was known for creating lifelike statues that influenced generations of artists.
Historians regard Lysippos as one of the foremost artists of the ancient world.
The bronze works of Lysippos are often cited as some of the greatest achievements in Greek sculpture.
