black duck

noun
a North American species of duck
Pronunciation: / blæk dʌk / (AmE), / blak dʌk / (BrE)
a dusky-brown duck native to northeastern United States and Canada, especially the American black duck (Anas rubripes)

Examples

The black duck bird
Many birdwatchers visit the region hoping to see the rare black duck.
The black duck can be difficult to distinguish from female mallards.
We spotted a black duck swimming near the edge of the marsh.
