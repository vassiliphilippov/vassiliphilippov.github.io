impassibility

noun
incapacity for suffering
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpæsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpæsɪˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being incapable of suffering or feeling pain, especially as attributed to a deity or divine being

Examples

Divine impassibility
The theologian wrote about the impassibility of God in classical doctrine.
Some philosophers debate whether true impassibility is possible for any being.
The concept of impassibility is central to certain religious traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invulnerability - the state of being immune to harm or damage
  • antonym: sensitivity - the quality of being easily affected or hurt
