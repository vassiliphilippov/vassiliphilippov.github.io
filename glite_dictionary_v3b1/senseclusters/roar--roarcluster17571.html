roar

verb
make a loud, deep, continuous noise
1.0 8.82/m
Pronunciation: / r…îr / (AmE), / r…îÀê / (BrE)
to make a loud, deep, and continuous sound, like an animal, crowd, machine, or nature

Examples

The wind roared
Last night, the wind roared through the empty streets, keeping everyone awake.
The engines roared as the airplane prepared for takeoff.
The river roared past the rocks after the heavy rainstorm.
Crowds would roar in protest outside the government building each night.
She couldn't help but roar with laughter at the comedian's joke.
The child began to roar when he scraped his knee.
Crowds listened as the engines roared to life at the racetrack.
The lion began to roar as it sensed danger near the camp.
Thunder roared overhead, shaking the windows with its force.

Synonyms

  • bellow
    4.7 4.54/m
    bellow is a loud, angry shout from a person or animal, while roar is a deep, powerful continuous sound.
  • rumble
    2.5 6.46/m
    rumble is a low, long vibrating noise from machines or earth, while roar is a loud, forceful and continuous sound.
  • thunder
    3.2 2.34/m
    thunder is a sudden, very loud noise often from storms or crowds, while roar is a steady, deep continuous sound.
  • boom
    3.4 3.28/m
    boom is a single loud, sharp sound like an explosion, while roar is a longer, deeper, continuous noise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bellow - to make a deep loud roar, typically of an animal
  • degree lower: growl - to make a low guttural sound, usually in threat or anger
  • synonym: howl - to make a long, loud, wailing sound
  • synonym: wail - to make a long, high-pitched cry of pain or sadness
