buckler mustard

noun
a plant with yellow, mustard-like flowers and shield-shaped pods
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌk.lɚ ˈmʌs.tɚd / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.lə ˈmʌs.təd / (BrE)
a plant native to southeastern Europe, with yellow flowers resembling those of mustard and seed pods shaped like bucklers

Examples

buckler mustard plant
Researchers studied the buckler mustard for its resilience in dry soils.
The garden featured several unusual plants, including a bright cluster of buckler mustard.
The seed pods of buckler mustard resemble tiny shields, inspiring its common name.
