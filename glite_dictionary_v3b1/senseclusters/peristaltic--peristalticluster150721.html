peristaltic

adjective
moving fluids rhythmically
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪˈstæltɪk /; / ˌpɛrɪˈstɔltɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈstæltɪk / (BrE)
relating to wave-like contractions that move substances through a tube, as in the digestive system or in devices that imitate this action

Examples

The peristaltic pump
The peristaltic action of the pump ensures a steady flow of liquid through the tubing.
A peristaltic device is often used in laboratories to transfer sensitive solutions without contamination.
The engineer recommended a peristaltic system for handling corrosive chemicals safely.
The doctor explained how peristaltic action moves food through the intestines.
A blockage can disrupt the normal peristaltic movement in the digestive system.
Certain medications may affect peristaltic contractions in the esophagus.

  • synonym: pumping - moving fluids using mechanical force
