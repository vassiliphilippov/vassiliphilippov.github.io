pahautea

noun
New Zealand evergreen tree
Pronunciation: / pɑːˈhaʊtiə / (AmE), / pɑːˈhaʊtiə / (BrE)
An evergreen tree (Libocedrus bidwillii) native to New Zealand, resembling the kawaka tree.

Examples

The pahautea tree
Botanists studied the differences between pahautea and kawaka trees in their native habitats.
The pahautea is commonly found in the mountainous regions of New Zealand's North and South Islands.
The distinctive bark of the pahautea makes it easy to identify among other native evergreens.
