cut it close

verb
leave very little time before a deadline or event
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌt ɪt kloʊs / (AmE), / ˈkʌt ɪt kləʊs / (BrE)
to leave very little time before a deadline or before something happens, often risking being late or missing it

Examples

To cut it close before deadline
If you keep cutting it close, you'll eventually miss an important deadline.
She always cuts it close, arriving just in time for meetings.
We really cut it close getting to the airport, with only ten minutes to spare.
