whistleblowing

adjective
reporting misconduct
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪsəlˌbloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəlˌbləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
relating to the reporting of illegal or unethical conduct within an organization, especially by employees.

Examples

Whistleblowing policy
The company established a whistleblowing hotline to encourage employees to report unethical behavior.
A new whistleblowing policy was introduced to protect those who expose misconduct.
Many organizations now have whistleblowing procedures to ensure transparency and accountability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cover-up - an attempt to prevent the public from discovering information about wrongdoing
  • synonym: reporting - the act of informing authorities about wrongdoing
