MD

noun
Doctor of Medicine degree or title
Pronunciation: / ˌɛm ˈdi /; / ˌɛmˈdi / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˈdiː /; / ˌɛmˈdiː / (BrE)
a person who is qualified and licensed to practice medicine; a medical doctor (or the degree itself)

Examples

A licensed MD
After years of study, she finally earned her MD and started practicing medicine.
Dr. Roberts, an MD, specializes in internal medicine at the city hospital.
To apply for the position, you must be an MD with at least five years of experience.
After years of study, she finally became an MD and started her own clinic.
The hospital has several MDs on call at all times.
You should see an MD if your symptoms persist for more than a few days.
