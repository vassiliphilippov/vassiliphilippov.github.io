paratuberculosis

noun
chronic cattle disease
Pronunciation: / ˌpærətjuːbərˈkjuːləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpærətjuːbɜːˈkjuːləsɪs / (BrE)
a chronic infectious disease of cattle and other ruminants caused by a specific bacterium, leading to weight loss and diarrhea

Examples

Chronic paratuberculosis disease
The veterinarian diagnosed the herd with paratuberculosis after several tests.
Farmers are concerned about the spread of paratuberculosis among their cattle.
Effective control of paratuberculosis requires strict hygiene and regular testing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Johne's disease - another name for paratuberculosis in cattle
  • degree higher: tuberculosis - a serious infectious disease affecting the lungs and other organs
