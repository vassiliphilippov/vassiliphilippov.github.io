headhunt

verb
to recruit executives
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌhʌnt / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌhʌnt / (BrE)
to actively search for and recruit highly qualified people, especially for senior or specialized positions

Examples

To headhunt a manager
The company decided to headhunt a new marketing director from a rival firm.
Many top executives are headhunted by international corporations looking for experienced leaders.
She was surprised when a recruiter tried to headhunt her for a position abroad.
The tech company decided to headhunt talented engineers from its competitors.
She was headhunted by a major firm after her impressive work on the project.
Many executives are headhunted for top positions in the industry.
The tech company is headhunting top software developers from its competitors.
She was headhunted by a major firm because of her impressive track record.
Many executives are headhunted for leadership roles in international companies.
