nonthinking

noun
meditative state
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
a state or condition of not engaging in conscious thought or mental activity, often characterized by calm awareness

Examples

Practice nonthinking
During the retreat he practiced nonthinking, achieving a calm awareness without words or judgment.
Some meditation techniques encourage nonthinking to help reduce stress and anxiety.
She found that moments of nonthinking brought her a sense of peace and clarity.
After the exam she enjoyed a brief period of nonthinking before planning her next steps.
Meditation sometimes aims for a state of nonthinking to achieve relaxation.
He drifted into nonthinking as he stared out the window on the long train ride.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contemplation - the act of thinking deeply about something
  • synonym: meditation - the practice of focusing the mind for relaxation or spiritual purposes
  • synonym: mindfulness - a mental state achieved by focusing awareness on the present moment
  • synonym: mindlessness - the quality of lacking active thought or attention
  • antonym: rumination - the act of thinking deeply or repeatedly about something
