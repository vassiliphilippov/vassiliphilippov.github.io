rage

verb
feel or express intense anger
Pronunciation: / reɪdʒ / (AmE), / reɪdʒ / (BrE)
to feel or express intense anger or violent emotion in an uncontrolled way

Examples

To rage with anger
All night, the storm raged outside, matching her turmoil inside.
He raged at the unfair decision until he was red in the face.
Sometimes, he rages over the smallest annoyance, frightening everyone around him.
He began to rage when he heard the disappointing news, throwing objects across the room.
The crowd raged against the new policy, overturning barriers and shouting loudly.
When provoked, she could rage for hours without calming down.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not feeling or showing anger or agitation
  • synonym: fume - to feel, show, or express great anger
