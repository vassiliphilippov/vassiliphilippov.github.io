militarism

noun
policy of maintaining strong military power
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪtəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəˌrɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or policy that a country should maintain a strong military capability and be ready to use it to defend or promote national interests

Examples

Militarism policy
After decades of militarism, the nation shifted its focus toward diplomacy and peaceful coexistence.
Some critics argue that excessive government spending on defense is a symptom of militarism.
The rise of militarism in the early 20th century contributed to escalating tensions between European countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: militarist - a person who supports militarism
  • antonym: pacifism - belief in avoiding war and violence
