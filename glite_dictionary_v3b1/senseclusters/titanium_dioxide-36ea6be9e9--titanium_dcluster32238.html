titanium dioxide

noun
white pigment used in paints and sunscreens
5.9 3.8
Pronunciation: /taɪ.ˈteɪ.niː.əm daɪ.ˈɑːk.saɪd/ /; / /tɪ.ˈteɪ.niː.əm daɪ.ˈɑːk.saɪd/ (AmE), /taɪ.ˈteɪ.niː.əm daɪ.ˈɒk.saɪd/ /; / /tɪ.ˈteɪ.niː.əm daɪ.ˈɒk.saɪd/ (BrE)
a white, powdery compound (TiO2) used mainly as a pigment in paints, coatings, and sunscreens due to its high opacity and durability.

Examples

Titanium dioxide pigment
Many sunscreens rely on titanium dioxide to block harmful ultraviolet rays.
The artist chose paints containing titanium dioxide for their lasting brightness.
Toothpaste often includes titanium dioxide to give it a brilliant white color.
