pepper

verb
bombard with repeated attacks or questions
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
to attack, bombard, or scatter many small things over or at someone or something

Examples

To pepper with questions
During the hailstorm, the windows were peppered with small stones and ice.
Fans peppered the field with confetti after the team's victory.
The reporters peppered the senator with difficult questions after the press conference.
The teacher peppered her lecture with interesting facts to keep the students engaged.
Confetti was peppered across the floor after the celebration ended.
He likes to pepper his emails with jokes to make them more enjoyable.

  • synonym: pelt - to attack repeatedly with things thrown or with blows
