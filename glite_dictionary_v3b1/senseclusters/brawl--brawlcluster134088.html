brawl

verb
to fight or quarrel noisily
5.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /brɔːl/ (AmE), /brɔːl/ (BrE)
to fight or quarrel in a noisy, disorderly, or violent way, especially in a public place

Examples

To brawl loudly
Several fans began to brawl in the stands after the controversial call.
The two groups brawled outside the nightclub, attracting the attention of the police.
They were thrown out of the bar for brawling and disturbing the peace.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: melee - a confused fight, often involving many people
