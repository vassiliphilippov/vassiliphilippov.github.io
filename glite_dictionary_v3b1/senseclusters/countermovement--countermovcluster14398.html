countermovement

noun
opposing movement
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəmˌmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a movement or organized effort that opposes or reacts against another movement or trend

Examples

A political countermovement
The government reforms led to a strong countermovement among conservative groups.
A countermovement quickly formed to challenge the new social trend.
Historians studied the rise of the countermovement in response to the civil rights era.

Related Terms

  • antonym: movement - a group of people working together to advance their shared ideas
  • synonym: opposition - resistance or dissent expressed in action or argument
