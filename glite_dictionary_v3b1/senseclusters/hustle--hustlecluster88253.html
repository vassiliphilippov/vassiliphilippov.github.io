hustle

noun
a dishonest scheme or swindle
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌsəl / (BrE)
a dishonest scheme or swindle, especially one involving cheating at gambling or persuading someone to buy worthless property

Examples

A gambling hustle
Beware of casino hustles where players collude to cheat newcomers.
He fell for a hustle that promised high returns on a worthless investment.
The antique watch turned out to be a hustle by a street vendor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: con - a trick or swindle to deceive someone
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair
  • synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud
