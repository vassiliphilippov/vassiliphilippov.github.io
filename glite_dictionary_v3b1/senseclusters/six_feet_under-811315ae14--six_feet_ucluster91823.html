six feet under

adjective
euphemism for dead or buried
7.7 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / s…™ks fit Àà ånd…ö / (AmE), / s…™ks fiÀêt Àà ånd…ô / (BrE)
A humorous or euphemistic expression meaning dead and buried, or completely covered or submerged, especially in reference to burial.

Examples

Six feet under water
After the heavy rain, half the village was six feet under water.
He's been six feet under for more than a decade now, but people still talk about him.
She knew one day her secrets would be six feet under with her.

Synonyms

  • buried
    2.7 0.00/m
