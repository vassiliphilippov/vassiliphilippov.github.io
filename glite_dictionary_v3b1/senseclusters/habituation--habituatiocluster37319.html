habituation

noun
becoming dependent on habit-forming substances
3.1 7.1
Pronunciation: /hə.bɪ.tʃuː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /hə.bɪ.tjuː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /hə.bɪ.tʃʊ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /hə.bɪ.tʃuː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /hə.bɪ.tjuː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /hə.bɪ.tʃʊ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of becoming abnormally tolerant to and dependent on a habit-forming substance, such as alcohol or drugs

Examples

Habituation to alcohol
After years of alcohol abuse, his habituation made it nearly impossible for him to quit.
Long-term habituation to narcotics can have severe health consequences.
Medical professionals monitor patients for signs of habituation when prescribing strong painkillers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: addiction - the condition of being unable to stop taking a harmful substance
  • synonym: dependence - the state of relying on or being controlled by something, especially a drug
  • antonym: sobriety - the state of not being affected by alcohol or drugs
