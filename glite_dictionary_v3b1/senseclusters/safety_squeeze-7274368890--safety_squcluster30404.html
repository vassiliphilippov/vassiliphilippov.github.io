safety squeeze

noun
baseball play where runner waits for bunt before running home
8.6 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈseɪf.tiː skwiːz/ /; / /ˈseɪf.tɪ skwiːz/ (AmE), /ˈseɪf.tiː skwiːz/ /; / /ˈseɪf.tɪ skwiːz/ (BrE)
(in baseball) a play in which the runner on third base waits until the batter has made a successful bunt before running home.

Examples

A safety squeeze play
During the ninth inning, their coach signaled for a safety squeeze.
The safety squeeze is less risky than a suicide squeeze but still requires perfect timing.
The team executed a safety squeeze to bring in the winning run.
