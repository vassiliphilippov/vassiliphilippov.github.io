cobia

noun
large tropical marine food and game fish
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.bi.ə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.bi.ə / (BrE)
a large tropical marine fish (Rachycentron canadum) with a long dark stripe on each side, valued as a food and game fish and found in coastal and open waters worldwide

Examples

Large cobia fish
Fishermen in Florida often catch cobia during their annual migration near the coast.
Grilled cobia is considered a delicacy in many seaside restaurants.
The cobia is prized for both its size and fighting ability among sport anglers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remora - a type of fish related to the cobia, known for attaching itself to other marine animals
