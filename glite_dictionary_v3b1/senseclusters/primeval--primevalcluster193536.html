primeval

adjective
from the earliest ages; ancient
6.5
Pronunciation: / praɪˈmiːvəl / (AmE), / praɪˈmiːvəl /; / prɪˈmiːvəl / (BrE)
(of something) belonging to the earliest ages; ancient or original.

Examples

The primeval forest
Scientists studied rocks that dated back to the primeval days of the Earth.
The primeval forest stretched for miles, dense with trees unchanged for thousands of years.
There was a primeval quality to the landscape, as if untouched by human hands.
Legends spoke of a primaeval world of darkness and chaos.
Scientists studied the primaeval oceans to learn about the origins of life.
The explorers trekked through a primaeval forest untouched by modern civilization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ancient - belonging to the distant past
  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
  • synonym: primordial - existing at or from the very beginning
