bowshot

noun
archery shot or distance
7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈboʊ.ʃɑːt/ (AmE), /ˈbəʊ.ʃɒt/ (BrE)
the act of shooting an arrow with a bow, or the distance an arrow travels when shot from a bow

Examples

A bowshot distance
The castle was just a bowshot away from the river, close enough for a skilled archer.
With a single bowshot, the hunter brought down the bird from the tree.
In old tales, heroes could send a bowshot across the wide valley.
The village was only a bowshot from the edge of the forest.
He could see the enemy camp just a bowshot away across the field.
The treasure was buried within a bowshot of the old oak tree.
