toot

noun
a brief blast of a horn
5.5
Pronunciation: / tuːt / (AmE), / tuːt / (BrE)
a short, sharp sound made by a horn, whistle, or similar instrument

Examples

A car toot
I heard a loud toot from the car behind me as the traffic light turned green.
She gave a cheerful toot on her whistle to signal the end of the game.
The train announced its arrival with a long toot from its horn.
From across the lake, we could hear the toot of a distant train.
She smiled as her friend drove by, giving a cheerful toot on the car horn.
The bus driver gave a toot of the horn to alert the pedestrians.

  • synonym: honk - a loud, sharp sound made by a car horn
