lauroyl

noun
chemical group from lauric acid
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔːrɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈlɔːrɔɪl / (BrE)
a chemical group derived from lauric acid, commonly found in surfactants and detergents

Examples

Lauroyl group
The shampoo contains a lauroyl group to improve its cleaning power.
Scientists studied the effects of lauroyl derivatives in new surfactant formulas.
Adding lauroyl compounds can enhance the foaming ability of detergents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acetyl - a chemical group derived from acetic acid
  • synonym: lauric acid - a saturated fatty acid from which lauroyl is derived
