scald

verb
criticize harshly
8.4
Pronunciation: / skɔld / (AmE), / skɔːld / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something severely and harshly

Examples

To scald the team
After the failed project, the manager scalded the team during the meeting.
Critics were quick to scald the new policy for its shortcomings.
The editor did not hesitate to scald the article for its lack of accuracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blister - to criticize harshly and openly
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • synonym: scorch - to criticize severely, often publicly
