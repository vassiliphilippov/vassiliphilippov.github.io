medius

noun
hip muscle
8.8 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàmiÀêdi…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàmiÀêdi…ôs / (BrE)
a muscle in the human body, most commonly referring to the gluteus medius in the hip

Examples

Gluteus medius muscle
The medius is important for stabilizing the pelvis when you walk or run.
Injury to the gluteus medius can cause hip pain and difficulty moving the leg.
Physical therapists often recommend exercises to strengthen the medius muscle.

Synonyms

  • gluteus medius
    7.1 0.08/m
    gluteus medius is the specific middle hip muscle between the larger and smaller buttock muscles, while medius is a general medical name.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: gluteus maximus - the largest muscle in the buttocks
  • synonym: gluteus medius - a muscle on the outer surface of the pelvis
  • degree lower: gluteus minimus - the smallest of the gluteal muscles
