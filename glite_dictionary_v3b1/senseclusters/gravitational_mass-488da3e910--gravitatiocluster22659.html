gravitational mass

noun
mass determining gravitational attraction
1.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /græv.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən.əl mæs/ (AmE), /græv.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən.əl mæs/ /; / /græv.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən.əl mɑːs/ (BrE)
the property of a body that determines the strength of its gravitational attraction to other bodies, measured by the force it exerts or experiences due to gravity

Examples

Gravitational mass property
Einstein's equivalence principle states that inertial mass and gravitational mass are equivalent in most circumstances.
Scientists measure the gravitational mass of celestial bodies to understand their influence on the motion of other objects.
The gravitational mass of the Earth determines the force it exerts on satellites orbiting it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inertial mass - the property of a body that determines its resistance to acceleration
