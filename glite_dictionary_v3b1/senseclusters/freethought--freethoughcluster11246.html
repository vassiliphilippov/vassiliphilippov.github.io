freethought

noun
rational independent thinking
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfriː.θɑːt/ /; / /ˈfriː.θɔːt/ (AmE), /ˈfriː.θɔːt/ (BrE)
the practice of forming opinions based on reason and evidence rather than accepting authority, tradition, or dogma (especially in religion or politics)

Examples

Freethought movement
The rise of freethought in the nineteenth century challenged traditional religious beliefs.
Many scientists support freethought as a way to encourage open inquiry and debate.
She admired the principles of freethought and questioned ideas that lacked evidence.
The movement for freethought encouraged people to question traditional beliefs and seek their own answers.
Many philosophers have promoted freethought as a way to advance knowledge and understanding.
Her commitment to freethought sometimes put her at odds with established authorities.
The movement of freethought challenged traditional religious beliefs in the nineteenth century.
She admired his commitment to freethought and open-minded discussion.
Many scientific advances have been driven by a spirit of freethought and inquiry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dogmatism - the tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true, without consideration of evidence
  • synonym: rationalism - the belief that reason is the main source of knowledge
