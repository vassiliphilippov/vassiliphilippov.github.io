subclinically

adverb
without clear symptoms
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈklɪnɪkli / (AmE), / sʌbˈklɪnɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that does not show obvious or detectable symptoms of disease.

Examples

To be subclinically present
The virus can remain subclinically present in the body for years before any signs appear.
Some patients carry the bacteria subclinically and never develop the disease.
The condition progressed subclinically, making early detection difficult for doctors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asymptomatically - in a way that shows no symptoms
  • antonym: clinically - in a way that shows clear symptoms of disease
