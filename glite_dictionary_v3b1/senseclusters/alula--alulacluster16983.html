alula

noun
projection of feathers on a bird's wing
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæljʊlə / (AmE), / ˈæl.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
A small projection on the front edge of a bird's wing, consisting of a tuft of short, stiff feathers attached to the first digit, that aids in flight control.

Examples

Bird alula
Birdwatchers often notice the alula when studying a bird's wing structure up close.
Damage to the alula can seriously affect a bird's ability to maneuver in flight.
The bird spread its alula as it prepared to land gently on the branch.
