prompt

verb
incite or cause to act
6.5
Pronunciation: / prɑmpt / (AmE), / prɒmpt / (BrE)
to cause or encourage someone to do something or to respond

Examples

To prompt action
Her curiosity prompted her to ask more questions about the project.
The unexpected noise prompted everyone to look out the window.
What prompted you to change your job?
Her curiosity prompted her to ask a lot of questions.
Rising prices prompted many people to shop elsewhere.
The teacher's question prompted a lively discussion among the students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deter - to discourage someone from taking action
  • antonym: discourage - to cause someone not to want to do something
  • synonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • synonym: motivate - to provide someone with a reason for doing something
