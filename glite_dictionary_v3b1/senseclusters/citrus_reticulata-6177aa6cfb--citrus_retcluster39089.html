Citrus reticulata

noun
species of mandarin orange tree
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtrəs rɪˌtɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs rɪˌtɪkjʊˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of shrub or small tree native to southeastern Asia, producing flattened, globose fruit known as mandarin oranges, which have very sweet, aromatic pulp and a thin, easily removable yellow-orange to deep orange rind.

Examples

Citrus reticulata fruit
Many commercial orchards grow Citrus reticulata for its sweet, easy-to-peel fruit.
Researchers studied the fruit of Citrus reticulata for its high vitamin C content.
The fragrant blossoms of Citrus reticulata attract a variety of pollinators in the spring.
