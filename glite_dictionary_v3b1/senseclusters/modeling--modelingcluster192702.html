modeling

noun
sculpture made by molding
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdlɪŋ /; / ˈmɑdəlɪŋ /; / ˈmɑːd(ə)lɪŋ /; / ˈmɑːdəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəlɪŋ /; / ˈmɒd(ə)lɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of shaping a material like clay or wax into a sculpture, or the preliminary sculpture made as a guide for a finished work

Examples

Modeling clay figures
His love for modeling led him to create lifelike animal figures from soft clay.
Many masterpieces in museums are the product of careful modeling in wax or bronze.
The artist's modeling in clay captured the subtle curves of the human body.
Her modeling of the animal helped guide the sculptors during the finishing process.
Many sculptors consider modeling a crucial first step in developing complex sculptures.
The artist created a modeling in clay before casting the final statue in bronze.
Her skills in modelling allowed her to experiment with different poses before completing the final piece.
In classical sculpture, modelling in clay was a crucial step prior to casting in bronze.
The artist spent hours on the modelling of the figure before beginning the marble carving.
