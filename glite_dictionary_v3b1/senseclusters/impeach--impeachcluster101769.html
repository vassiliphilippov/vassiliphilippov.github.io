impeach

verb
formally accuse or charge
6.5 6.4
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpiːtʃ/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpiːtʃ/ (BrE)
to formally accuse or charge a public official with wrongdoing or misconduct, especially in a legal or government context

Examples

To impeach a president
Congress began impeaching the official after receiving new evidence.
Some historians argue that few leaders have actually been impeached throughout history.
The committee voted to impeach the governor for corruption.
He was the third president in history to be impeached by Congress.
Several lawmakers debated whether to impeach the judge after the scandal came to light.
The House of Representatives voted to impeach the governor for abusing his powers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accuse - to charge someone with an offense or crime
  • antonym: acquit - to formally declare not guilty of criminal charges
  • antonym: convict - to find guilty of a criminal offense
  • antonym: exonerate - to officially declare someone not guilty
