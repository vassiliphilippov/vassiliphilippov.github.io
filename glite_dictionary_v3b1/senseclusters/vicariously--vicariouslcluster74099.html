vicariously

adverb
Experiencing through another person
6.7 6.5
Pronunciation: / vaɪˈkɛriəsli / (AmE), / vɪˈkɛərɪəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is experienced through the feelings or actions of another person, rather than directly.

Examples

To feel vicariously proud
He felt vicariously proud of his friend's achievement.
Many readers experience adventure vicariously by immersing themselves in novels.
She lived vicariously through her daughter's travels around the world.
