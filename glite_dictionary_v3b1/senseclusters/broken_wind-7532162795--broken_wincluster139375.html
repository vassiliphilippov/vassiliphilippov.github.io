broken wind

noun
chronic equine lung disease causing breathing difficulty
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊkən wɪnd / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən wɪnd / (BrE)
(of a horse) a chronic condition resembling emphysema that causes difficulty in breathing out and visible heaving of the flanks.

Examples

A broken wind horse
He sold the horse because it showed signs of broken wind, making it unfit for hard work.
Racehorses with broken wind are often retired due to their declining performance.
The veterinarian diagnosed the old mare with broken wind after observing her labored breathing.
