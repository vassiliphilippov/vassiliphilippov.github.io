transfusion

noun
pouring liquid from one vessel to another
Pronunciation: / trænsˈfjuːʒən / (AmE), / trænsˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the act of pouring a liquid from one vessel or container into another

Examples

Liquid transfusion
Ancient alchemists believed that the transfusion of liquids could change their properties.
Before storage, the transfusion of chemicals into clean bottles was essential to maintain purity.
The careful transfusion of wine from one cask to another preserved its delicate flavor.
