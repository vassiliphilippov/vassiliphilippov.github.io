southwester

noun
strong wind from the southwest
Pronunciation: / ˌsaʊθˈwɛstər / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈwɛstə / (BrE)
A strong wind blowing from the southwest.

Examples

A southwester wind
After days of calm, a sudden southwester brought cool air across the fields.
Sailors kept a wary eye on the horizon as a southwester began to pick up.
The southwester battered the coastline with heavy rain and strong gusts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: northwester - a strong wind from the northwest
  • degree lower: southeaster - a strong wind from the southeast
  • synonym: southwesterly - wind coming from the southwest (variant form)
