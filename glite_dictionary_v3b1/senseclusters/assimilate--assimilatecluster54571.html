assimilate

verb
to absorb into a solution (science)
1.0
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪməˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈsɪmɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of a gas, light, or heat) to take into a solution or a substance, so as to become part of it.

Examples

To assimilate heat
Certain materials assimilate heat rapidly, making them effective for thermal management.
In this process, plants assimilate carbon dioxide from the air into organic compounds.
The solution was able to assimilate more gas as the temperature increased.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - to take in or soak up (energy, liquid, or other substance)
