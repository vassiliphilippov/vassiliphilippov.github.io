overground

adjective
mainstream or public
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
relating to the mainstream, public, or widely accepted part of culture, music, or society, as opposed to the underground.

Examples

Overground music scene
After years in the underground scene, the band became overground and started playing at major festivals.
Her artwork moved from galleries to the overground art world, gaining widespread recognition.
Many trends that start as subcultures eventually become overground and influence popular culture.

  • antonym: underground - relating to alternative or non-mainstream culture or activities
