wear away

verb
erode or diminish by friction or use
Pronunciation: / wɛr əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɛə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually reduce, damage, or remove something by continuous use, rubbing, or exposure

Examples

To wear away fabric
Constant washing will wear away the fabric's softness.
The paint on the old fence wore away over time.
Years of footsteps have worn away the stone steps.
Over time, the constant flow of water began to wear away the stone surface.
The inscription on the old coin has worn away and is now difficult to read.
You must act quickly before rust wears away the metal completely.
After years of wind and rain, the stone steps began to wear away.
Repeated criticism can wear away a person's confidence over time.
The paint on the old bench has worn away from years of use.

Related Terms

  • antonym: build up - to increase or accumulate over time (opposite of wear away)
  • synonym: decay - to break down or decline gradually
  • synonym: deteriorate - to become progressively worse; to diminish in quality or value
  • synonym: diminish - to make or become less; reduce
  • synonym: erode - to wear away gradually, usually by natural forces
