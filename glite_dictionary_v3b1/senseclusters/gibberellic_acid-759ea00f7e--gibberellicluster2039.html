gibberellic acid

noun
a plant growth hormone
6.5
Pronunciation: / dʒɪbəˈrɛlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / dʒɪbəˈrɛlɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a plant hormone used to stimulate growth and development in plants; a crystalline organic acid in the gibberellin group

Examples

Gibberellic acid hormone
Gibberellic acid is commonly used to promote the growth of grapes and other fruit crops.
Researchers tested the effects of gibberellic acid on the germination rate of wheat seeds.
The seedlings responded rapidly to treatment with gibberellic acid.

Related Terms
