play the wag

verb
pretend to be absent to avoid duties
Pronunciation: / pleɪ ðə wæɡ / (AmE), / pleɪ ðə wæɡ / (BrE)
to pretend to be ill or otherwise absent in order to avoid work or duties, especially school

Examples

To play the wag at school
It's tempting to play the wag when the weather is beautiful outside.
She was caught trying to play the wag instead of going to her morning lecture.
Whenever there was a test, Tom would play the wag and not show up to class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attend - to be present at a place or event
  • synonym: play hooky - to stay away from school without permission
  • synonym: skive - to avoid work or duty by staying away or pretending to be ill
