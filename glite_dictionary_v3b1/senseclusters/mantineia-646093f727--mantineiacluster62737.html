Mantineia

proper noun
ancient Greek town, site of famous battles
9.9
Pronunciation: / mænˈtɪniə / (AmE), / manˈtɪnɪə / (BrE)
an ancient town in Arcadia, Greece, noted as the site of three significant battles among Greek city-states in 418 BC, 362 BC, and 207 BC.

Examples

Mantineia ancient town
Archaeologists have uncovered ancient ruins at Mantineia in the Greek region of Arcadia.
Many historians study the events at Mantineia to understand the dynamics of Greek city-state warfare.
The Battle of Mantineia in 418 BC was a pivotal conflict during the Peloponnesian War.
