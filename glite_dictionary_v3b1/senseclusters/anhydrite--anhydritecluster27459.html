anhydrite

noun
industrial mineral
Pronunciation: / ænˈhaɪdraɪt /; / ænˈhaɪˌdraɪt / (AmE), / ænˈhaɪdraɪt /; / ænˈhɪdraɪt / (BrE)
a white or gray mineral made of anhydrous calcium sulfate, used in industry (such as plasters, cements, and as a drying agent)

Examples

White anhydrite mineral
The geologist identified the white crystals as anhydrite during the field survey.
Manufacturers often use anhydrite in the production of certain types of cement.
Because it contains no water, anhydrite is valuable as a drying agent in industry.
The geologist identified the white layer as anhydrite during the field survey.
Large deposits of anhydrite are often found in areas where ancient seas have evaporated.
Unlike gypsum, anhydrite does not contain water in its crystal structure.
