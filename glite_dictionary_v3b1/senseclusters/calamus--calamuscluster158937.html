calamus

noun
genus of tropical Asian rattan palms
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.ə.məs / (AmE), / ˈkal.ə.məs / (BrE)
A tropical Asian palm of the genus Calamus, whose light, tough stems are used as a source of rattan canes.

Examples

Calamus palm stem
Rattan, which comes from the calamus palm, is prized for its strength and flexibility.
Several species of calamus are cultivated for their long, slender canes.
The stems of the calamus are harvested to make furniture and baskets.
