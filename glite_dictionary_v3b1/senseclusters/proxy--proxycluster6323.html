proxy

noun
person authorized to act for another
4.3 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈprɑːk.siː/ /; / /ˈprɑːk.sɪ/ (AmE), /ˈprɒk.siː/ /; / /ˈprɒk.sɪ/ (BrE)
a person who is authorized to act on behalf of another person, especially in matters such as voting or attending meetings

Examples

A proxy voter
A proxy must present proper identification in order to represent someone else.
She acted as his proxy during the negotiations with the board.
You may appoint a proxy to vote at the meeting if you cannot attend yourself.
