reassimilation

noun
phonological sound change
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˌsɪməˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːəˌsɪmɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in phonology by which a speech sound becomes more like a neighboring sound again, often restoring a previous similarity

Examples

Phonetic reassimilation
Linguists studied the reassimilation of certain consonants in the evolution of the language.
The reassimilation process helped restore the original pronunciation in some dialects.
Historical changes in speech often involve both assimilation and reassimilation over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assimilation - the process by which a speech sound becomes similar to a neighboring sound
