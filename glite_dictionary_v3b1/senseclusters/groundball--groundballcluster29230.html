groundball

noun
baseball hit that rolls on the ground
3.6 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°ra ändÀåb…îl / (AmE), / Àà…°ra ändÀåb…îÀêl / (BrE)
(Baseball) A batted ball that travels along or close to the ground.

Examples

A groundball hit
A hard-hit groundball got past the third baseman and into left field.
He specializes in pitching groundballs to avoid home runs.
The shortstop fielded the groundball smoothly and threw to first base for the out.

Synonyms

  • ground ball
    3.4 0.42/m
    ground ball is written as two words and names a batted ball that rolls or bounces on the ground, while groundball is the one-word form.
  • grounder
    5.6 0.57/m
    grounder is a hit that travels along the ground and often focuses on the batter's action, while groundball names the batted ball.
