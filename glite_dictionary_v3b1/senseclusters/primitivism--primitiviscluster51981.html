primitivism

noun
art style imitating primitive or child art
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪˌvɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪ.vɪ.zəm / (BrE)
a style or movement in art characterized by the imitation or emulation of the art of primitive peoples or children, often emphasizing simplicity and directness of expression

Examples

Artistic primitivism
Her exhibit explores primitivism through sculptures reminiscent of ancient tribal art.
Many modern artists were influenced by primitivism and incorporated bold lines into their work.
The painter adopted primitivism to evoke a sense of innocence in his landscapes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: naïve art - art created by a person without formal training
  • antonym: realism - art movement focused on accurate, detailed depiction of reality
