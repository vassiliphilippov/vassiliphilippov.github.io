rescue

verb
take forcibly from legal custody
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛskju /; / ˈrɛs.kju / (AmE), / ˈrɛskjuː /; / ˈres.kjuː / (BrE)
to save someone or something from danger, harm, or an unpleasant situation

Examples

To rescue a child

The firefighters managed to rescue the family from the burning building.
Volunteers worked tirelessly to rescue animals affected by the flood.

  • synonym: liberate - to set someone free from a situation, especially imprisonment or oppression
  • synonym: save - to keep someone or something safe from danger or harm
