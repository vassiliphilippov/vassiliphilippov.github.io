top fermenting yeast

noun
yeast used for brewing ales
Pronunciation: / tɑp fərˈmɛntɪŋ jist / (AmE), / tɒp fəˈmɛntɪŋ jiːst / (BrE)
a type of brewer's yeast that rises to the surface during fermentation and is used in the production of ales

Examples

Top fermenting yeast in ales
Because top fermenting yeast works best at warmer temperatures, ales are brewed with it rather than lagers.
The brewery uses a special top fermenting yeast to create its signature pale ale.
Unlike bottom fermenting strains, top fermenting yeast floats to the top of the fermentation vessel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bottom fermenting yeast - yeast that settles at the bottom during fermentation, used for lagers
