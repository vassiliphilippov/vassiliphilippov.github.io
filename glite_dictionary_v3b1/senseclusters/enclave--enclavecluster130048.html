enclave

noun
territory surrounded by another, culturally distinct
Pronunciation: / ˈɛn.kleɪv /; / ˈɛnˌkleɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒn.kleɪv /; / ˈɒŋˌkleɪv / (BrE)
a distinct area or group within a larger territory or community, often surrounded by and different from the area around it

Examples

Ethnic enclave
Political tensions increased after the government tried to annex the ethnic enclave.
The city is home to a vibrant enclave of artists with their own distinct traditions.
The small mountain village formed a cultural enclave within the larger region.
The city is home to a small enclave of Italian immigrants who maintain their traditions.
Tourists love to visit the cultural enclave known for its unique cuisine and festivals.
Within the large metropolis, a quiet enclave of artists has developed its own identity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: community - a group of people living in the same place or having a particular characteristic in common
  • antonym: exclave - a portion of a state or territory geographically separated from the main part by surrounding foreign territory
