enclave

noun
territory surrounded by another, culturally distinct
5.9 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈen.kleɪv/ /; / /ˈeŋ.kleɪv/ (AmE), /ˈen.kleɪv/ /; / /ˈeŋ.kleɪv/ (BrE)
a distinct area or group within a larger territory or community, often surrounded by and different from the area around it

Examples

Political tensions increased after the government tried to annex the ethnic enclave.
