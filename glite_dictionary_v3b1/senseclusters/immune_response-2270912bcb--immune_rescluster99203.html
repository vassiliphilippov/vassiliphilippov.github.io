immune response

noun
bodily defense reaction to invaders
2.8 3.6
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈmjuːn rɪ.ˈspɑːns/ /; / /ɪ.ˈmjuːn riː.ˈspɑːns/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈmjuːn rɪ.ˈspɒns/ (BrE)
the reaction of the body’s immune system to an invading substance such as a virus, bacteria, or transplanted organ, resulting in the production of antibodies or other defensive actions

Examples

The immune response system
An excessive immune response can sometimes lead to allergic reactions or autoimmune diseases.
The body’s immune response is essential for fighting off infections.
Vaccines work by stimulating a controlled immune response to a harmless version of the virus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system that binds to specific antigens
  • synonym: antigen - a substance that induces an immune response, often foreign to the body
