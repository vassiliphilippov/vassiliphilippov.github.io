derision

noun
mockery; scornful ridicule
9.6
Pronunciation: / dəˈɹɪʒən /; / dəˈrɪʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪʒ.ən /; / dɪˈrɪʒən / (BrE)
the act of mocking or treating someone or something with contempt or ridicule

Examples

To meet derision
His suggestion was met with derision from his colleagues.
She could not hide her derision when she heard the absurd claim.
The politician's awkward mistake became the subject of public derision.
She responded to their criticisms with calm dignity, ignoring their derision.
The announcement of his idea was met with derision from his colleagues.
The comedian used derision to highlight the absurdities in everyday life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mockery - teasing or contemptuous language or behavior directed at a person or thing
  • antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something
