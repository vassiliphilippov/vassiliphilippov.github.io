genetic defect

noun
inherited disease or disorder
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛtɪk dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk dɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
A disease or disorder caused by abnormalities in an individual's genes, inherited from one or both parents.

Examples

A genetic defect disease
Prenatal screening can detect certain genetic defects before a child is born.
Some rare diseases are caused by a single genetic defect present from birth.
The doctor explained that a genetic defect was responsible for his illness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congenital disorder - a condition present from birth, often genetic
  • synonym: hereditary disorder - a disease passed down from parents to offspring
