crypticness

noun
quality of blending in
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪptɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪptɪknəs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to see or detect because of blending into the surroundings, as with camouflage

Examples

Crypticness of animal skin
The crypticness of the lizard's skin made it almost invisible on the rocks.
Scientists studied the crypticness of certain insects to understand how they avoid predators.
The bird's crypticness allows it to nest safely without being easily spotted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: camouflage - the act or result of hiding by blending in with the environment
  • antonym: visibility - the state of being able to be seen
