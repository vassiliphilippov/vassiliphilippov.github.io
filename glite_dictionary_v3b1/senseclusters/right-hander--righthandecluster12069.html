right-hander

noun
person who primarily uses right hand
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪtˌhændər /; / ˌraɪtˈhændər / (AmE), / ˈraɪtˌhændə /; / ˌraɪtˈhændə / (BrE)
a person who uses their right hand more skillfully or more often than their left

Examples

A right-hander writes
Despite being a right-hander, she learned to write with her left hand after the injury.
Most right-handers find it easier to use scissors designed for their dominant hand.
The teacher noticed that nearly all the students in the class were right-handers.
As a natural right-hander, she struggled to use the scissors in her left hand.
Most people are right-handers and feel more comfortable writing with their right hand.
The coach asked the right-hander to demonstrate the proper grip for throwing.
