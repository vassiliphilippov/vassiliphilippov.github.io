lower

noun
bottom berth in a train or ship
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə / (BrE)
The lower of two berths, especially in a train or ship compartment, typically referring to the bottom sleeping place.

Examples

A lower bunk
He prefers the lower because it's easier to access than the top bunk.
I reserved the lower for the overnight journey to ensure a more comfortable sleep.
The lower was already made up with clean sheets when she boarded the train.
