convect

verb
move fluid or gas by convection
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛkt / (AmE), / kənˈvɛkt / (BrE)
move or circulate (a fluid or gas, especially air) by convection, typically transferring heat in the process.

Examples

To convect warm air
As the sun heats the ground, warm air will rise and convect to higher altitudes.
Modern climate control systems efficiently convect air throughout the entire building.
The oven is designed to convect hot air evenly around the food for consistent cooking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conduct - transfer heat or electricity through direct contact
  • antonym: radiate - emit (energy, especially heat or light) in the form of rays or waves
