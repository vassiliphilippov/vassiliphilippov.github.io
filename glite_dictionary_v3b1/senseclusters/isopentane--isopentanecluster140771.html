isopentane

noun
branched alkane hydrocarbon
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsoʊˈpɛnteɪn / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈpɛnteɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable liquid hydrocarbon (C5H12), also called 2-methylbutane, used as a solvent and in refrigeration

Examples

Isopentane solvent use
The laboratory used isopentane to rapidly freeze tissue samples for analysis.
Because of its low boiling point, isopentane is often used as a refrigerant in scientific experiments.
Chemists distinguish isopentane from other C5H12 isomers by its branched structure.
The laboratory stored isopentane in a tightly sealed container.
Because of its low boiling point, isopentane is often used in refrigeration systems.
Always handle isopentane with care due to its flammability.
