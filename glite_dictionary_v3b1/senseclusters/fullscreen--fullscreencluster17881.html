fullscreen

adjective
entire screen display
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊlˌskriːn / (AmE), / ˈfʊlˌskriːn / (BrE)
occupying or using the entire display area of a screen or monitor

Examples

Fullscreen mode
To get a better view, switch the presentation to fullscreen mode.
The game automatically launches in fullscreen when you start it.
He prefers watching movies in fullscreen for a more immersive experience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: windowed - displayed in a window rather than using the whole screen
