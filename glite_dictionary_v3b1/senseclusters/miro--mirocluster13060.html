miro

noun
New Zealand conifer tree and its wood
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪərəʊ / (BrE)
A large evergreen conifer native to New Zealand (Prumnopitys ferruginea), valued for its dark wood used in interior carpentry and lumber.

Examples

The miro tree
In New Zealand forests, the miro is an important food source for native birds.
The furniture was crafted from native miro wood, giving the room a distinctly rich appearance.
They chose miro panels for the interior because of the wood's warmth and color.
