animadvert

verb
to express blame or critical comment
Pronunciation: / ˌænəmædˈvɜrt /; / ˌænəməˈdvɜrt / (AmE), / ˌænɪmədˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to criticize or speak unfavorably about someone or something, especially in a formal or public way

Examples

To animadvert on faults
Critics did not hesitate to animadvert against the film's confusing plot.
She would often animadvert on her students' lack of discipline in class.
The novelist dared to animadvert upon the political climate of his country.
During the debate, several speakers animadverted on the government's handling of the crisis.
She was known to animadvert harshly against those who broke the rules.
The professor did not hesitate to animadvert on the shortcomings of the proposal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: criticize - to express disapproval or find fault with someone or something
  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration
