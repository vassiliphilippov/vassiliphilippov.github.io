reaccustom

verb
to get used to again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈkʌstəm / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈkʌstəm / (BrE)
to become used to something again, or to help someone become used to something again, after a period of change or absence

Examples

To reaccustom yourself
After living abroad for years, he had to reaccustom himself to the local customs.
It took her a few weeks to reaccustom to the cold winters of her hometown.
The team needed time to reaccustom to their old training schedule after the break.
After living abroad for years, it took time to reaccustom herself to her hometown's climate.
The dog needed to reaccustom to its old routine after returning from the kennel.
Teachers helped students reaccustom to in-person classes after months of online learning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accustom - to make someone familiar with something through repeated experience
  • synonym: readapt - to adjust again to a new or changed environment
  • antonym: unaccustom - to make someone or something not used to something
