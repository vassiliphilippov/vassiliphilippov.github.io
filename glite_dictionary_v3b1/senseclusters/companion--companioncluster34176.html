companion

verb
to accompany as a companion
6.4
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpænjən / (AmE), / kəmˈpanjən /; / kəmˈpænjən / (BrE)
to accompany or be with someone, especially to offer company or support

Examples

To companion a friend
During his illness, friends and neighbors companion him so that he never felt lonely.
He companioned the writer during her travels through Europe.
She companioned her grandmother on the long journey back to their homeland.
She is companioning her friend through chemotherapy, attending appointments and offering quiet support.
Volunteers spent hours companioning elderly residents at the care home.
He found comfort in companioning his neighbor during a difficult time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to leave someone completely and not help them
  • synonym: accompany - to go somewhere with someone as a companion or escort
  • synonym: support - to give help or encouragement to someone
