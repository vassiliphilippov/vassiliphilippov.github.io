T

noun
abbreviation for thyroxine hormone
8.9
Pronunciation: / ti / (AmE), / ti / (BrE)
An abbreviation for thyroxine, a hormone produced by the thyroid gland that regulates metabolism by controlling the rate of oxidation in cells.

Examples

T hormone level
Doctors may reference T levels when evaluating thyroid function.
Low T can cause symptoms like fatigue and weight gain.
Research studies often measure T to assess metabolic health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thyroxine - the main hormone produced by the thyroid gland, important for metabolism regulation
