solenodon

noun
Caribbean insect-eating mammal
9.9
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛnədɒn / (AmE), / səˈliːnədɒn / (BrE)
a small, nocturnal, insect-eating mammal native to the Caribbean, known for its elongated snout and venomous bite

The solenodon is rarely seen because it is active only at night in the forests of Hispaniola.
Scientists study the solenodon to learn more about the evolution of venom in mammals.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the endangered solenodon from habitat loss.
