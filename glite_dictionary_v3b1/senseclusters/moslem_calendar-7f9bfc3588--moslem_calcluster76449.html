Moslem calendar

noun
lunar calendar used by Muslims
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑzləm ˈkælənˌdɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɒzləm ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
the lunar calendar used by Muslims, dating from 622 AD (the year of the Hegira), where the start of the year moves through the solar year over a 32-year cycle

Examples

The Moslem calendar
According to the Moslem calendar, Ramadan can fall in any season over a 32-year period.
Muslims around the world use the Moslem calendar to determine the timing of major religious observances.
The Moslem calendar is based on lunar months, making its holidays shift each year relative to the Gregorian calendar.
