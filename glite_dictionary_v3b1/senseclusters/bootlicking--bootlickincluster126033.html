bootlicking

adjective
excessive flattery to gain favor
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌlɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtlɪkɪŋ /; / ˈbuːtˌlɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
behaving in an excessively flattering or obsequious way toward people in power to gain favor

Examples

Bootlicking employee
His bootlicking attitude toward the boss irritated his coworkers.
She disliked the bootlicking behavior that permeated the office culture.
The senator's bootlicking speeches failed to win him genuine respect.
His bootlicking attitude was obvious whenever he spoke to people in power.
She was promoted not for her skills but for her bootlicking behavior towards the boss.
The manager's constant bootlicking annoyed everyone in the office.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fawning - seeking favor by excessive praise
  • antonym: independent - not influenced by flattery toward those in authority
  • synonym: obsequious - showing too great a willingness to serve or obey
  • synonym: sycophantic - behaving in a way that flatters those in power for personal gain
