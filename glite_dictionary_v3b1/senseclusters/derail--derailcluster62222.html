to cause a plan, process, or event to fail or be interrupted; to make something go off its intended course

Examples

To derail a plan
The protest threatened to derail the peace talks between the two countries.
A technical error could derail the entire project if not fixed quickly.
The train was derailed by debris on the tracks after the storm.
A sudden power outage can easily derail an important business meeting.
The scandal threatened to derail the politician's campaign for re-election.
Unexpected expenses may derail your plans to buy a new car this year.
