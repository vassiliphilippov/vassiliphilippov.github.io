pin

noun
small pointed fastener
1.0 10/m
Pronunciation: / pÉªn / (AmE), / pÉªn / (BrE)
a small, slender object (usually metal or plastic) used for fastening, attaching, marking, or as a pivot (such as in sewing, jewelry, locks, machinery, or marking points)

Examples

A sharp pin
Please be careful not to prick yourself with that sharp pin.
She used a pin to attach the note to her shirt.
The tailor inserted a pin into the fabric to hold it in place.
Each player's score was tracked using a pin on the board.
She used a colored pin to highlight important locations on the display.
The coach placed a pin on the map to show where the next tournament would be held.
He secured the oar in the pin before setting off across the lake.
The wooden pin on the boat had to be replaced after years of use.
Without a sturdy pin, the oar would slip out of position while rowing.
