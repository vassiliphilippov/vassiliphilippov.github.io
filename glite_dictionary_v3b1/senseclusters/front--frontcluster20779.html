front

to lead a group
4.8 0.89/m
Pronunciation: / frÊŒnt / (AmE), / frÊŒnt / (BrE)
to lead, represent, or be the main person in charge of a group, organization, or activity

Examples

To front a band
She fronted the band during their world tour and gained many new fans.
He was chosen to front the new marketing campaign for the company.
The actor fronts several charity events each year to raise awareness.

Synonyms

    3.9 2.60/m
    head up is a common phrase for officially leading an organization, while front means to lead and represent publicly.
  • head
    2.9 28/m

  • lead
    1.0 137/m
    lead means guide or be first in charge in many settings, while front stresses being the public representative or face.
