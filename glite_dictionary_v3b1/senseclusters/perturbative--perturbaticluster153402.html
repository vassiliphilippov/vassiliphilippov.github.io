perturbative

adjective
using small changes
Pronunciation: / pərˈtɜrbətɪv / (AmE), / pəˈtɜːbətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or using small changes to find approximate solutions in complex systems, especially in science or mathematics

Examples

Perturbative method
The perturbative method is often used in physics to solve difficult equations.
Scientists applied a perturbative analysis to estimate the effects of the new variable.
In quantum mechanics, perturbative techniques can simplify complex calculations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approximate - close to the actual but not completely accurate
  • antonym: exact - completely correct and without any error
