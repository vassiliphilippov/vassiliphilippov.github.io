Kuroshio

noun
warm ocean current off Japan
Pronunciation: / kuˈroʊʃioʊ / (AmE), / kʊəˈrɒʃiəʊ / (BrE)
A warm ocean current flowing northeastward off the coast of Japan into the northern Pacific Ocean.

Examples

The Kuroshio current
Marine biologists study the Kuroshio to understand patterns of ocean biodiversity in the Pacific.
The Kuroshio significantly influences the climate and fisheries of the Japanese coast.
The powerful Kuroshio current carries warm water northward, affecting weather patterns as far as Alaska.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gulf Stream - a warm Atlantic Ocean current similar to the Kuroshio
