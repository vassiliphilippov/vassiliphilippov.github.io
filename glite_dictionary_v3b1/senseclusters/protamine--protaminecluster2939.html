protamine

noun
simple protein, antidoes for heparin
9.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpro ät…ôÀåmin / (AmE), / Ààpr…ô ät…ômiÀên / (BrE)
a simple protein found especially in fish sperm that is rich in arginine and is used in medicine to counteract the anticoagulant effect of heparin

Examples

Protamine neutralizes heparin
Protamine is administered to neutralize the effects of heparin during surgery.
Researchers isolated protamine from salmon sperm for biochemical analysis.
The doctor ordered a dose of protamine after noticing prolonged bleeding.
