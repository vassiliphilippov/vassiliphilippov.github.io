fall behind

verb
fail to keep pace; lag behind
1.5 2.4
Pronunciation: / fɔl bəˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / fɔːl bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to not keep up with others or with a schedule; to make less progress than expected

Examples

To fall behind in class
If you fall behind in your studies, it's hard to catch up later.
Many small businesses fell behind during the economic downturn.
She was running fast at first, but soon began to fall behind the others.
If you don't study regularly, you might fall behind in your coursework.
She was ill for two weeks and started to fall behind with her assignments.
The company can't afford to fall behind its competitors in technology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lag behind - to remain at a lower level than others in development or progress
