steady

adjective
marked by firm determination
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛdi / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi / (BrE)
firm, stable, or not easily moved, shaken, or upset (physically or emotionally)

Examples

Steady hands
Despite the criticism, she remained steady in her commitment to the project.
His steady determination inspired everyone around him to keep working hard.
The team showed steady resolve in the face of tough opposition.
Please keep the boat steady as I climb aboard.
She held the camera steady while taking the photograph.
The patient stood steady on his feet after the surgery.
A surgeon needs steady hands and nerves during delicate procedures.
Despite the chaos around him, his steady demeanor reassured the team.
She remained steady throughout the crisis and helped everyone stay calm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other emotions
  • antonym: nervous - easily agitated or alarmed
  • synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful and unwavering
  • synonym: stable - not likely to give way; firmly fixed
  • antonym: unstable - prone to change or fail; not steady
  • antonym: unsteady - not firm or secure in position; likely to move or shake
