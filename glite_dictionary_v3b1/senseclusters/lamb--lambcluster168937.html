lamb

noun
an easily deceived or naive person
3.4 4.6
Pronunciation: /læm/ (AmE), /læm/ (BrE)
a person who is gentle, innocent, or easily deceived

Examples

He is a lamb
He invested in the scheme like a lamb, trusting the promises without question.
She walked into the negotiation as a lamb, unaware of the tricks they would use.
The inexperienced trader was a lamb among wolves on Wall Street.
Everyone loved her because she was such a lamb and never caused any trouble.
He's a real lamb, always trying to help his classmates and never raising his voice.
The teacher described the new student as a lamb due to her gentle nature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dupe - a person who is easily tricked or deceived
  • synonym: innocent - a person not guilty or free from moral wrong
  • antonym: swindler - a person who cheats or deceives others
  • synonym: victim - a person harmed as a result of deception
  • antonym: wolf - a predatory or aggressive person (in contrast to 'lamb')
