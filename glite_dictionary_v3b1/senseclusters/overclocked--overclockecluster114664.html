overclocked

adjective
working at high intensity
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈklɑːkt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈklɒkt / (BrE)
operating at an unusually high speed, intensity, or workload, often beyond normal limits, especially in a figurative sense.

Examples

Overclocked team
After weeks of tight deadlines, the entire team felt overclocked and in need of a break.
Her overclocked schedule left little time for rest or relaxation.
The students were overclocked with assignments during the exam period.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: exhausted - extremely tired or worn out
  • synonym: overworked - working too hard or too much
  • antonym: relaxed - free from stress or tension
