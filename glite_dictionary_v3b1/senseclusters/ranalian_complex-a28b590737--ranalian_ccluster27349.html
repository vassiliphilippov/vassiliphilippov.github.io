ranalian complex

noun
primitive group of flowering plants (botany)
9.9
Pronunciation: /rə.ˈneɪ.liː.ən ˈkɑːm.pleks/ /; / /rə.ˈneɪ.liː.ən kəm.ˈpleks/ (AmE), /rə.ˈneɪ.liː.ən ˈkɒm.pleks/ /; / /rə.ˈneɪ.liː.ən kəm.ˈpleks/ (BrE)
a group of primitive extant flowering plant families, characterized by well-developed perianths and apocarpous ovaries, formerly classified as a superorder including families such as Magnoliaceae and Ranunculaceae.

Examples

The ranalian complex families
Magnoliaceae, a family within the ranalian complex, includes several ancient tree species.
Several botanists have debated the taxonomic boundaries of the ranalian complex.
The ranalian complex is considered crucial in understanding the early evolution of flowering plants.
