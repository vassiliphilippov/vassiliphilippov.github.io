high-stepped

adjective
having or showing a high step or gait
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈstɛpt / (AmE), / haɪ ˈstɛpt / (BrE)
moving or walking with a high step or steps, often in a noticeably elevated or showy manner.

Examples

A high-stepped walk
He entered the room with a high-stepped stride, attracting everyone's attention.
The parade featured a high-stepped horse leading the procession.
The trainer admired the high-stepped movements of the prize foal.
