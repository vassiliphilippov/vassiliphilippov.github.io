Copernican system

noun
Copernicus' sun-centered astronomical model
6.9 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / k…ôÀàp…úrn…™k…ôn Ààs…™st…ôm / (AmE), / k…ôÀàp…úÀên…™k…ôn Ààs…™st…ôm / (BrE)
the astronomical model proposed by Copernicus in which the Earth and other planets revolve around the sun

Examples

The Copernican system
Before the Copernican system, most believed the sun revolved around the Earth.
Galileo's observations helped provide evidence for the Copernican system over the geocentric model.
The acceptance of the Copernican system marked a turning point in scientific thought.

Synonyms

  • heliocentrism
    8.4 0.07/m
    heliocentrism is the general scientific idea that the planets orbit the sun, while the Copernican system is Copernicus's original model of this.
  • Copernicanism
    9.3 0.08/m
    Copernicanism says the sun, not the earth, is the center of the system, while the Copernican system is Copernicus's specific model.
