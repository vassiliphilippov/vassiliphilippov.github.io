Copernican system

noun
Copernicus' sun-centered astronomical model
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈpɜrnɪkən ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / kəˈpɜːnɪkən ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the astronomical model proposed by Copernicus in which the Earth and other planets revolve around the sun

Examples

The Copernican system
Before the Copernican system, most believed the sun revolved around the Earth.
Galileo's observations helped provide evidence for the Copernican system over the geocentric model.
The acceptance of the Copernican system marked a turning point in scientific thought.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Ptolemaic system - the geocentric model with Earth at the center of the universe
  • synonym: heliocentric model - the model of the solar system in which the sun is at the center
