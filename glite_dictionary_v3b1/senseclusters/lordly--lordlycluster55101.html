lordly

adjective
befitting a lord; grand or dignified
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔrdli / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdli / (BrE)
of, relating to, or befitting a lord; grand, dignified, or haughty in manner or appearance.

Examples

Lordly mansion
From their lordly manor, the family oversaw the entire countryside.
She swept through the hall with a lordly bearing, commanding attention.
The heir enjoyed a lordly inheritance that set him apart from others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
  • synonym: majestic - having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
