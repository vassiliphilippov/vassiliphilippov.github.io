Giotto

proper noun
influential pre-Renaissance Florentine painter
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒɑː.t.oʊ/ /; / /ˈdʒɒ.t.oʊ/ (AmE), /ˈdʒɒ.t.ə/ /; / /ˈdʒɒ.t.əʊ/ (BrE)
(c. 1267–1337) An influential Florentine painter who abandoned the stiff Byzantine style and pioneered a more naturalistic approach; regarded as the greatest Italian painter before the Renaissance.

Examples

Giotto painter
Art historians agree that Giotto revolutionized Western painting with his innovative techniques.
Many artists during the Renaissance were inspired by the work of Giotto.
The frescoes painted by Giotto in the Scrovegni Chapel are admired for their emotional realism.
