agonize

verb
suffer intense pain or anguish
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to experience intense mental or physical pain or anguish; to suffer agony

Examples

To agonize over a decision
He agonized through the long night, unable to sleep because of his worries.
Many parents agonize about whether they are raising their children properly.
She would agonize for days over making even the smallest decision.
He continued to agonise over the decision long after everyone else had moved on.
She agonised for hours before finally accepting the offer.
The patient was left to agonise in pain due to the delayed treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy
  • synonym: suffer - to experience pain or distress
  • synonym: worry - to feel anxious or troubled
  • synonym: writhe - to twist or squirm in pain or discomfort
