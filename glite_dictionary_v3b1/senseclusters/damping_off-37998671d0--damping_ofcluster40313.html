damping off

noun
fungal plant disease affecting seedlings
Pronunciation: / ˈdæmpɪŋ ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈdæmpɪŋ ˌɒf / (BrE)
A disease of seedlings caused by fungi or molds, resulting in their decay and collapse, especially in conditions of excessive moisture.

Examples

Damping off disease
Gardeners can reduce the risk of damping off by avoiding overwatering and ensuring good ventilation.
Soil that stays wet for too long often encourages damping off in vulnerable seedlings.
The greenhouse was struck by damping off, killing most of the young tomato plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: root rot - plant disease causing decay of roots, often by fungi
