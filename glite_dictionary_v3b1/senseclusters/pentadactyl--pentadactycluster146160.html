pentadactyl

adjective
five-fingered or toed
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛntəˈdæktɪl / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəˈdæktɪl / (BrE)
having five fingers or toes on each hand or foot

Examples

Pentadactyl limb structure
The human hand is a classic example of a pentadactyl structure.
Most mammals, including dogs and cats, have pentadactyl limbs.
Fossil evidence shows that early amphibians were also pentadactyl.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polydactyl - having more than the usual number of fingers or toes
  • antonym: tetradactyl - having four fingers or toes on each hand or foot
