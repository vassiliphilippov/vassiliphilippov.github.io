nonsignificantly

adverb
without statistical meaning
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌsɪɡˈnɪfɪkəntli / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌsɪɡˈnɪfɪkəntli / (BrE)
in a way that is not significant, important, or meaningful (often used in statistics to mean not showing a real difference or effect)

Examples

Improved nonsignificantly in trial
The treatment group improved nonsignificantly compared to the control group in the clinical trial.
After the policy change, sales figures changed nonsignificantly from the previous quarter.
The difference between the two methods was nonsignificantly small according to the statistical analysis.
The medication changed her symptoms only nonsignificantly, so the doctor recommended a different treatment.
Profits increased nonsignificantly last quarter, barely making a difference to the company's finances.
The results differed nonsignificantly between the two groups, suggesting no major impact from the intervention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insignificantly - in a way that is not important or noticeable
  • antonym: significantly - in a way that is important or shows a meaningful difference
