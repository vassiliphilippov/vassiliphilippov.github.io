gag

verb
to silence by tying something over mouth
Pronunciation: / ɡæɡ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ / (BrE)
to tie a cloth or other device around someone's mouth to prevent them from speaking or making noise

Examples

To gag a prisoner
She was gagged and left in the basement, unable to call for help.
The burglars gagged the homeowner before searching the house.
The kidnappers gagged their victim to stop him from shouting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: muzzle - to prevent someone from speaking, especially by physical means
