mortise joint

noun
ankle joint formed by tibia, fibula, and talus
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrtɪs dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪs dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a gliding joint formed between the distal ends of the tibia and fibula and the proximal end of the talus bone in the human ankle

Examples

Ankle mortise joint
A clear X-ray of the mortise joint helps diagnose fractures or dislocations in the ankle.
Arthritis in the mortise joint can cause significant pain and limited mobility.
The surgeon examined the mortise joint to assess the extent of the ankle injury.
