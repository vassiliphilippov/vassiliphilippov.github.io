glut

noun
an excessive supply; oversupply
7.8 5.5
Pronunciation: /glʌt/ (AmE), /glʌt/ (BrE)
An excessive supply of something, especially leading to a decrease in its value or price.

Examples

A glut of products
A glut of oil in global markets has pushed fuel prices to record lows.
The housing bubble burst after a glut of new apartments became available.
There was a glut of strawberries on the market, causing prices to plummet.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dearth - a lack or scarcity of something
  • antonym: shortage - a situation in which there is not enough of something
  • synonym: surplus - an amount that is more than what is needed
