glut

noun
an excessive supply; oversupply
6.8 1.33/m
Pronunciation: / É¡lÊŒt / (AmE), / É¡lÊŒt / (BrE)
An excessive supply of something, especially leading to a decrease in its value or price.

Examples

A glut of products
A glut of oil in global markets has pushed fuel prices to record lows.
The housing bubble burst after a glut of new apartments became available.
There was a glut of strawberries on the market, causing prices to plummet.

Synonyms

  • oversupply
    4.5 0.48/m
    oversupply is an excess of goods on the market that pushes prices down, while glut stresses the resulting price fall.
  • oversaturation
    6.5 0.01/m
    oversaturation is when a market is filled so customers stop buying, while glut focuses on excess that lowers prices.
  • surfeit
    8.8 0.63/m
    surfeit describes having too much, often causing discomfort or waste, while glut highlights an oversupply that cuts value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dearth - a lack or scarcity of something
  • antonym: shortage - a situation in which there is not enough of something
  • synonym: surplus - an amount that is more than what is needed
