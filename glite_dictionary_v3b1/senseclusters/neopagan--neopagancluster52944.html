neopagan

adjective
modern pagan beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊˈpeɪɡən / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈpeɪɡən / (BrE)
relating to modern forms of paganism, especially new religious movements inspired by or based on ancient pagan beliefs and practices

Examples

Neopagan traditions
The festival featured several neopagan ceremonies celebrating the changing of the seasons.
Many neopagan groups draw inspiration from ancient myths and nature worship.
She wrote her thesis on the influence of neopagan traditions in contemporary art.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstream - belonging to the dominant or widely accepted group or tradition
  • synonym: pagan - relating to religious beliefs other than the main world religions, especially pre-Christian religions
