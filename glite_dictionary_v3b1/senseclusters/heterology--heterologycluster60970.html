heterology

noun
lack of correspondence in similar body parts (biology)
4.3 8.9
Pronunciation: /he.tə.ˈrɑː.lə.dʒiː/ /; / /hə.tə.ˈrɑː.lə.dʒiː/ (AmE), /he.tə.ˈrɒl.ə.dʒiː/ /; / /hə.tə.ˈrɒl.ə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
(biology) The lack of correspondence between apparently similar body parts or structures in different organisms.

Examples

The heterology of limbs
Scientists studied the heterology between the limb structures of whales and crocodiles.
The wings of insects and birds are an example of heterology, as they have different evolutionary origins.
Understanding heterology can provide insight into convergent evolution across different species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homology - correspondence of body parts in different species due to shared ancestry
