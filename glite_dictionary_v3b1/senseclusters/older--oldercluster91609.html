older

adjective
more experienced through long practice
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈəʊldə / (BrE)
having lived for more years or having more experience; not young

Examples

An older person
After years of working in the courtroom, the older lawyers were respected for their wisdom.
She listened to the older members of the team, knowing their advice came from experience.
The older technicians quickly solved the problem that baffled their younger colleagues.
As he grew older, his interests changed and matured.
Her older brother just graduated from college this year.
The older residents of the village gather every morning in the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aged - having lived a long life; of advanced age
  • synonym: elderly - old or advanced in age, especially about people
  • antonym: novice - someone with little or no experience
  • synonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular field
  • antonym: younger - having lived fewer years; less advanced in age
