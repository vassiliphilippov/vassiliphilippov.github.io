magister

noun
university teacher
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈɡɪstər / (AmE), / məˈɡɪstə / (BrE)
a person who teaches or is an expert, especially at a university or in a scholarly field (often historical or formal)

Examples

A magister teaches

The title of magister was given to those who completed advanced studies in philosophy.
During the ceremony, the magister addressed the graduating class in Latin.
