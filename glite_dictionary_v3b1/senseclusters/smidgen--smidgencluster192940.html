smidgen

noun
a tiny amount
Pronunciation: / ˈsmɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈsmɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
A very small amount; a tiny or scarcely detectable quantity.

Examples

A smidgen of salt
Add just a smidgen of salt to enhance the flavor.
She only gave me a smidgen of her time before she hurried off.
There wasn't even a smidgen of evidence to support his theory.
Could I have just a smidgeon of sugar in my tea, please?
She showed not even a smidgeon of interest in the conversation.
The soup only needs a smidgeon of salt to enhance the flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dash - a small quantity, especially of seasoning
  • synonym: smidgen - an alternative spelling meaning a tiny amount
  • synonym: trace - a very slight amount
