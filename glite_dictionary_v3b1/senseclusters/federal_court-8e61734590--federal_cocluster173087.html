federal court

noun
court established by a federal government
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛdərəl kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl kɔːt / (BrE)
A court established by the authority of a federal government, responsible for adjudicating cases under federal law or disputes involving federal matters.

Examples

A federal court judge
A federal court judge ruled in favor of the plaintiff.
Many high-profile cases involving constitutional rights are heard in federal court.
The case was appealed to the federal court after the state court made its decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: state court - a court established by a state's authority, handling cases under state law
