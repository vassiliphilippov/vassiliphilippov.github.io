essentially

adverb
in essence; fundamentally
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsɛnʃəli / (AmE), / ɪˈsɛnʃəli / (BrE)
used to emphasize the basic, fundamental, or intrinsic nature of something; in essence or most important respects.

Examples

Is essentially true
She is essentially a kind person, though she sometimes appears stern.
The company has essentially changed its strategy to adapt to new markets.
The two proposals are essentially the same despite their differences in detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basically - in the most essential respects; fundamentally
  • synonym: fundamentally - in central or primary respects; basically
