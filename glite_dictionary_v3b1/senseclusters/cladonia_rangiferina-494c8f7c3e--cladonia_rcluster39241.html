Cladonia rangiferina

noun
a greyish Arctic lichen, reindeer food source
Pronunciation: / kləˌdoʊni.ə ræŋˌgɪfəˈriːnə / (AmE), / klaˈdəʊni.ə raŋˌɡɪf.əˈriːnə / (BrE)
an erect, greyish branching lichen found in Arctic and some northern temperate regions, serving as a main food source for reindeer and caribou

Examples

Cladonia rangiferina lichen
Cladonia rangiferina is a vital food source for reindeer during the harsh Arctic winter.
In some northern cultures, people have also consumed Cladonia rangiferina when other foods were scarce.
Scientists collected samples of Cladonia rangiferina to study its resilience in changing climates.
