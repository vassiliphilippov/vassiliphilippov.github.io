pitter-patter

adverb
with a light, rhythmic, rapid beat
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪt̬ɚˌpæt̬ɚ /; / ˈpɪtərˌpætər / (AmE), / ˈpɪtəˌpætə / (BrE)
in a light, quick, and repeated way, like the sound of raindrops or small footsteps

Examples

Heart beats pitter-patter
He could feel his heart go pitter-patter as he waited for the results.
She tiptoed pitter-patter down the hallway to avoid waking anyone.
The rain fell pitter-patter on the rooftops all night long.
I woke up to the soothing pitter-patter of our cat's paws moving through the living room.
Rain began to pitter-patter on the windowpane late in the evening.
The toddler's feet made a gentle pitter-patter as she ran across the wooden floor.
