dotard

noun
elderly person with impaired intellect
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊ.tɑrd / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.tɑːd / (BrE)
an old person, especially one whose mental faculties have declined due to age

Examples

An old dotard
He dismissed the king as a dotard unfit to rule the nation.
Some described him unkindly as a dotard, but others remembered his brilliant youth.
The old dotard wandered the halls, muttering to himself and forgetting names.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elder - an older person, especially one of respect
  • antonym: sage - an old, wise person
