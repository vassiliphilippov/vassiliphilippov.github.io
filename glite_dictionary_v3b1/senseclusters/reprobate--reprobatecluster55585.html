reprobate

verb
formally express strong disapproval
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛprəˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛprəʊˌbeɪt / (BrE)
(formal) to express strong disapproval of; to condemn or censure.

Examples

To reprobate dishonesty
Many citizens reprobate such dishonesty in political leaders.
She was reprobated for her decision by her peers.
The board publicly reprobated the author's inflammatory comments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approve - to have a positive opinion of; to consent to
  • synonym: condemn - to express complete disapproval of, usually in public
