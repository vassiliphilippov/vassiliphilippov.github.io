shake

noun
musical ornament: rapid alternation of notes
8.1
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪk / (AmE), / ʃeɪk / (BrE)
A musical ornament in which a note alternates rapidly with the note a semitone above it; also known as a trill.

Examples

A shake in music
A well-performed shake can add excitement and ornamentation to a melody.
During the Baroque period, composers often indicated a shake in their music to embellish certain phrases.
The violinist executed a brilliant shake on the high note, impressing the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trill - a rapid alternation between two adjacent notes, especially in music
