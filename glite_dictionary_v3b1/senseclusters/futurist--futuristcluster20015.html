futurist

noun
theologian interpreting Revelation as future prophecy
4.5 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfjuː.tʃə.rɪst/ /; / /ˈfjuː.tʃ.rɪst/ (AmE), /ˈfjuː.tʃə.rɪst/ /; / /ˈfjuː.tʃ.rɪst/ (BrE)
a theologian who believes that the prophecies in the Book of Revelation are predictions of future events

Examples

Futurist theology view
Many futurists believe that the events described in Revelation have yet to occur.
She studied under a futurist who specialized in apocalyptic prophecy.
The debate between the preterist and futurist interpretations has shaped much of Christian eschatology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: historicist - a theologian who interprets prophecies in Revelation as unfolding across history
  • antonym: preterist - a theologian who interprets prophecies in Revelation as having already happened
