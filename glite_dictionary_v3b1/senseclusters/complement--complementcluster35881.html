complement

noun
complete number or quantity
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmpləmənt /; / ˈkɑːmpləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪmənt /; / ˈkɒmplɪmɛnt / (BrE)
the full number or amount needed to make something complete, such as a group, team, or set

Examples

Full complement of staff
Please make sure we have a full complement of supplies before starting the project.
The orchestra performed with its complete complement of musicians.
The ship set sail with a complement of 50 crew members.
The committee reached its complement of ten members after the election.
The ship sailed with a complement of 200 crew.
We are currently short of our full complement of staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quota - a fixed share or quantity
