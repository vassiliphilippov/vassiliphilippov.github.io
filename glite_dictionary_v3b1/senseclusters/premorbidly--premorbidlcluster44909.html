premorbidly

adverb
before illness onset
9.9
Pronunciation: / priːˈmɔːrbɪdli / (AmE), / priːˈmɔːbɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the state or condition before the onset of a disease or illness

Examples

To live premorbidly independent
The patient was premorbidly independent and lived alone before the stroke.
Doctors assessed how premorbidly active she was to plan her rehabilitation.
He was premorbidly healthy, with no history of chronic illness before the diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postmorbidly - in a way that relates to the state after the onset of a disease
  • synonym: pre-disease - relating to the period before a disease develops
