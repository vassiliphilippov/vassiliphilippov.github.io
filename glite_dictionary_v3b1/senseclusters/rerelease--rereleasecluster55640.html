rerelease

noun
new issue of work
Pronunciation: / ˌriːrɪˈliːs / (AmE), / ˌriːrɪˈliːs / (BrE)
a new issuance of a work, such as a film, album, or book, that has been released before, often with changes or additions

Examples

A rerelease of a movie
The rerelease of the classic movie attracted a new generation of fans to theaters.
Collectors were excited about the rerelease of the rare vinyl album with bonus tracks.
After its initial success, the publisher announced a rerelease of the bestselling novel with a new cover.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original release - the first time a work is made available to the public
  • synonym: reissue - a new release of something that was previously issued
