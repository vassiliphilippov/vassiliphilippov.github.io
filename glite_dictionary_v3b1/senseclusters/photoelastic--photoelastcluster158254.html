photoelastic

adjective
changes optically under stress
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
describing a material that changes its optical properties, such as color or brightness, when under mechanical stress

Examples

Photoelastic material
Engineers use photoelastic materials to visualize stress patterns in transparent models.
A photoelastic disk reveals colorful stress lines when placed under polarized light.
The photoelastic effect helps researchers study how forces are distributed in structures.
