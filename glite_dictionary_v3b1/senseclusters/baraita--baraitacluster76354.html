baraita

noun
Talmudic teaching
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɑːˈraɪtə / (AmE), / bɑːˈraɪtə / (BrE)
a Jewish teaching or tradition from the Tannaitic period not included in the Mishnah but cited in the Talmud

Examples

A baraita teaching
The scholar quoted a baraita to clarify the ancient law.
Many baraita are referenced throughout the Talmud to explain different traditions.
A baraita can provide valuable historical context for understanding Jewish customs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Mishnah - the first major written collection of Jewish oral traditions
  • synonym: Talmud - the central text of Rabbinic Judaism, including the Mishnah and Gemara
