floor joist

noun
beam supporting a floor
Pronunciation: / flɔr dʒɔɪst / (AmE), / flɔː dʒɔɪst / (BrE)
A horizontal structural beam that supports a floor.

Examples

A floor joist beam
A rotten floor joist can cause the entire floor to sag.
The carpenter installed a new floor joist to strengthen the old house.
They used steel instead of wood for the floor joist in the basement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: joist - a length of timber or steel supporting part of the structure of a building
