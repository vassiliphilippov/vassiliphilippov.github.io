plague spot

noun
dark skin spot characteristic of the plague
Pronunciation: / pleɪɡ spɑt / (AmE), / pleɪɡ spɒt / (BrE)
a dark spot on the skin that is a characteristic sign of the plague, especially bubonic plague

Examples

A plague spot on skin
Doctors in the Middle Ages described the plague spot in great detail in their records.
The appearance of a plague spot was often a sign of imminent death during outbreaks.
The patient developed a painful plague spot on his arm, prompting immediate quarantine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bubo - a swollen lymph node, another symptom of the plague
