box

noun
partitioned area for a few people
2.9 27/m
Pronunciation: / bÉ‘ks / (AmE), / bÉ’ks / (BrE)
a separate, enclosed, or marked area for people, such as in a theater, stadium, workplace, carriage, or on a sports field

Examples

VIP box at the theater
A guard watched the entrance from his security box near the gate.
The judges reviewed the match from their box at the football stadium.
The royal family sat in the private box overlooking the stage.
After adjusting his gloves, the batter stepped back into the box to continue his at-bat.
The coach gave instructions from the third-base box during the game.
The umpire warned the player not to step out of the box before the pitch.
The armed guard sat in the box beside the coachman as they drove through the dark countryside.
The rain made riding in the exposed box particularly uncomfortable for the coachman.
Traditionally, the driver would keep his whip and coat in the box while traveling.
