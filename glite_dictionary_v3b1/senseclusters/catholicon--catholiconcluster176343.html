catholicon

noun
hypothetical universal remedy
Pronunciation: / kəˈθɑːlɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / kəˈθɒlɪkɒn / (BrE)
A hypothetical universal remedy, especially one for all diseases, once sought by alchemists.

Examples

The catholicon remedy
Ancient alchemists spent years searching for the catholicon to cure every disease.
No scientific evidence has ever supported the existence of a true catholicon.
Some early physicians claimed to have discovered their own catholicon, though these remedies never worked as promised.

Related Terms

  • synonym: panacea - a solution or remedy for all problems or diseases
