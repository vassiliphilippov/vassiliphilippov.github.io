sunfish

noun
North American freshwater fish
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small carnivorous freshwater fish of North America, often with a laterally compressed body and a metallic sheen, including species such as crappies, black bass, bluegills, and pumpkinseed.

Examples

Freshwater sunfish
Sunfish typically feed on insects, small fish, and crustaceans in freshwater habitats.
The lake is known for its abundance of sunfish that attract anglers from all over.
We caught several colorful sunfish near the pier yesterday afternoon.

  • synonym: pumpkinseed - a brightly colored species of North American sunfish
