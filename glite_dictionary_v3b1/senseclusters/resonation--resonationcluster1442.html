resonation

noun
emotional impact
7.5
Pronunciation: /re.zə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /re.zə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the quality of causing an emotional or meaningful response in people; the state of being relevant or significant to someone

Examples

Resonation with people
The speaker's words had a strong resonation with the audience, moving many to tears.
Her story found resonation among people who had faced similar challenges in their lives.
The novel's themes lacked resonation for younger readers, who struggled to relate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irrelevance - the quality of not being important or meaningful
  • synonym: resonance - the ability to evoke or suggest images, memories, and emotions
  • synonym: significance - the quality of being important or meaningful
