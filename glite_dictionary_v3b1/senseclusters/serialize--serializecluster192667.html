serialize

verb
arrange in a series or sequence
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or order in a series or sequence

Examples

To serialize a list
He was asked to serialize the files before archiving them.
Please serialize the results so we can review them step by step.
The program can serialize the data to ensure the correct order is maintained.
The archivist needed to serialise the documents by date to make retrieval easier.
To ensure clarity, the teacher asked the students to serialise their ideas before presenting.
You must serialise the data before importing it into the new database.
