prep

verb
to prepare
3.6
Pronunciation: / prɛp / (AmE), / prɛp / (BrE)
to prepare or get ready for something, or to get something ready (informal)

Examples

To prep for exams
She is prepping for her final exams this week.
We spent the afternoon prepping the house for the party.
He always preps his meals on Sunday to save time during the week.
She always preps her notes before an important meeting to feel confident.
The chef prepped all the vegetables before starting to cook the meal.
We need to prep the classroom for tomorrow's presentation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or prepare something
  • synonym: prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
  • synonym: ready - to make something ready for use or action
