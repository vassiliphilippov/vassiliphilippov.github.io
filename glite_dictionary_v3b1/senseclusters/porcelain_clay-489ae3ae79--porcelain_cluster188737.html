porcelain clay

noun
fine white clay for porcelain and ceramics
4.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈpɔːr.sə.lən kleɪ/ /; / /ˈpɔːr.slɪn kleɪ/ (AmE), /ˈpɔː.səl.ɪn kleɪ/ /; / /ˈpɔː.slɪn kleɪ/ (BrE)
A fine, usually white clay formed by the weathering of aluminous minerals, used in making porcelain and ceramics, and as a filler or absorbent.

Examples

Fine porcelain clay
Paper manufacturers sometimes use porcelain clay as a filler to improve texture and brightness.
The artist carefully shaped the porcelain clay before firing it in the kiln.
Traditional Chinese vases are often made from porcelain clay prized for its whiteness and strength.
