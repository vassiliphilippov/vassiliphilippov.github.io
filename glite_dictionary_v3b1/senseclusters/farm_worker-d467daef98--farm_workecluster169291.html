farm worker

noun
person employed to do farm work
1.1 2/m
Pronunciation: / f…ërm Ààw…úrk…ôr / (AmE), / f…ëÀêm Ààw…úÀêk…ô / (BrE)
a person who is employed to work on a farm, especially performing physical labor

Examples

The farm worker harvests crops
During the harvest season, the farm worker starts work at sunrise every day.
Many farm workers travel from region to region looking for employment.
The farm worker operated heavy machinery as part of his daily duties.

Synonyms

  • farmhand
    3.0 0.6/m
    a farmhand is the common everyday word for someone who does physical work on a farm, while a farm worker is more neutral.
  • field hand
    4.0 0.3/m
    a field hand works mainly in the fields doing outdoor manual tasks, while a farm worker can work across the whole farm.
  • hired hand
    3.6 0.5/m
    a hired hand is any person paid to do manual work, often temporarily, while a farm worker is specifically employed on a farm.
  • agricultural laborer
    3.2 0.1/m
    an agricultural laborer is a formal term for people who do manual farm tasks in reports, while a farm worker is the everyday name.
