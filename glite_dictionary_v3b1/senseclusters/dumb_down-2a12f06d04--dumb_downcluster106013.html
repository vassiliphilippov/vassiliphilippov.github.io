dumb down

verb
make simpler, often excessively so
Pronunciation: / dʌm daʊn / (AmE), / dʌm daʊn / (BrE)
to make something simpler and easier to understand, often to the point of reducing its original complexity or intellectual level

Examples

To dumb down language
I wish the publisher wouldn't always dumb down these reports; we can handle technical details.
Rather than dumbing down the curriculum, the school chose to support students with extra resources.
The documentary was criticized for dumbing down complex scientific concepts for a general audience.

Related Terms
