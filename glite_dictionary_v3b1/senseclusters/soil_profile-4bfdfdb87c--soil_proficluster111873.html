soil profile

noun
vertical section of soil layers
Pronunciation: / sɔɪl ˈproʊ.faɪl / (AmE), / sɔɪl ˈprəʊ.faɪl / (BrE)
a vertical section of soil extending from the ground surface down to the underlying parent rock, showing the distinct layers or horizons of soil.

Examples

A soil profile section
A typical soil profile contains layers such as topsoil, subsoil, and bedrock.
The scientist dug a pit to examine the soil profile for different horizons.
Understanding the soil profile helps farmers improve their crop yields.
