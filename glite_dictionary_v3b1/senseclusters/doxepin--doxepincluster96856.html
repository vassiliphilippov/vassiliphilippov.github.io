doxepin

noun
a tricyclic antidepressant drug
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑːk.sə.pɪn/ /; / /ˈdɑːks.ə.pɪn/ (AmE), /ˈdɒk.sə.pɪn/ /; / /ˈdɒks.ə.pɪn/ (BrE)
a tricyclic antidepressant medication used to treat depression, anxiety, and certain types of insomnia, often known by the trade names Adapin and Sinequan

Examples

doxepin medication
He found that doxepin was effective in relieving his symptoms of depression.
Patients taking doxepin should be monitored for possible side effects such as dry mouth and drowsiness.
The doctor prescribed doxepin to help manage her chronic anxiety and sleep disturbances.
