defrock

verb
remove a priest's official status
6.2
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfrɑk / (AmE), / dɪˈfrɒk / (BrE)
to officially remove a priest or minister from his or her position and privileges in the church

Examples

To defrock a priest
After the investigation, the church decided to defrock the priest for misconduct.
The bishop was defrocked after violating church doctrine.
They are considering defrocking any official found guilty of abuse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: unfrock - to deprive a priest or minister of ecclesiastical status
