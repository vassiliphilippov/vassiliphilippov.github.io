oblique

adjective
indirect, not straightforward
Pronunciation: / əˈbliːk, oʊˈbliːk / (AmE), / əˈbliːk, əʊˈbliːk / (BrE)
not expressed or done in a direct way; indirect or misleading, especially to avoid the truth or to deceive

Examples

Oblique comment
His oblique remark left everyone unsure about his real opinion.
Instead of addressing the issue directly, she used oblique criticism.
The politician gave oblique answers to all the tough questions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct - done or said in a straightforward way
