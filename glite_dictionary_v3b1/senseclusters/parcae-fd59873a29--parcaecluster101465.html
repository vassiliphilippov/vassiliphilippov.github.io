Parcae

noun
Roman goddesses of fate or destiny
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑːr.kiː/ /; / /ˈpɑːr.keɪ/ (AmE), /ˈpɑː.kiː/ /; / /ˈpɑː.keɪ/ (BrE)
In Roman mythology, any of the three goddesses of fate or destiny, often identified with the Greek Moirai and similar to the Norse Norns.

Examples

The Parcae goddesses
In ancient Roman art, the Parcae are often depicted as solemn women holding tools of fate.
The Parcae were believed to spin, measure, and cut the thread of human life.
Writers sometimes invoke the Parcae as symbols of destiny beyond human control.
