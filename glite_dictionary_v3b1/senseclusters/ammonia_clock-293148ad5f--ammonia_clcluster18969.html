ammonia clock

noun
atomic clock using ammonia's nitrogen vibration
Pronunciation: / əˈmoʊniə klɑk / (AmE), / əˈməʊniə klɒk / (BrE)
an atomic clock that operates by measuring the vibrational frequency of the nitrogen atom in an ammonia molecule

Examples

An ammonia clock device
Although less accurate than newer models, an ammonia clock played a crucial role in the development of time standards.
Scientists first used the ammonia clock in the 1940s to demonstrate the principle of atomic time measurement.
The ammonia clock was a precursor to modern atomic clocks used for precise timekeeping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atomic clock - a clock that uses the frequency of atomic transitions as its timekeeping element
