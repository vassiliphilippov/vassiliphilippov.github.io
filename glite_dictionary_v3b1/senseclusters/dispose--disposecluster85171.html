dispose

verb
to transfer or get rid of something
1.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspoʊz / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to get rid of something, especially by throwing away, selling, or giving it to someone else

Examples

To dispose of waste
After deciding to downsize, they disposed of many household items through an online auction.
She disposed of her late uncle's book collection by donating it to a library.
The company disposed of its non-core assets to focus on its main business.
After reviewing the old documents, she decided to dispose of most of them.
It is illegal to dispose batteries in regular household trash in some areas.
Many people do not know how to properly dispose of hazardous waste.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retain - to keep possession of something
