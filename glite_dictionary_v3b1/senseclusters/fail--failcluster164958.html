fail

verb
to deteriorate in health or strength
3.2 6.05/m
Pronunciation: / feÉªl / (AmE), / feÉªl / (BrE)
(of strength, health, or quality) to become weaker or deteriorate gradually.

Examples

Fail with age
As he aged, his eyesight began to fail and reading became more difficult.
Her strength seemed to fail after the long illness.
You could see that the old building was starting to fail structurally over the years.
