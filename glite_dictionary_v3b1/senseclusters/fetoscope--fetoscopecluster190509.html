fetoscope

noun
device for hearing fetal heartbeat
8.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfiÀêt…ôÀåsko äp / (AmE), / ÀàfiÀêt…ôsk…ô äp / (BrE)
A stethoscope designed to be placed on a pregnant woman's abdomen to listen to the fetal heartbeat.

Examples

Prenatal fetoscope check
Nurses are trained to operate a fetoscope as part of prenatal care.
The doctor used a fetoscope to check the baby's heartbeat during the examination.
Unlike electronic monitors, a fetoscope relies on acoustic detection of fetal heart sounds.
Compared to electronic monitors, a foetoscope is simple and requires no batteries or electricity.
Nurses are trained to operate a foetoscope in prenatal care settings.
The doctor used a foetoscope to check the baby's heartbeat during the examination.
