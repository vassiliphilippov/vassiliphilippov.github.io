Caryota urens

noun
species of fishtail palm
Pronunciation: / kɛrˈjoʊtə ˈjʊrɛnz / (AmE), / kəˈrɪətə ˈjʊərɛnz / (BrE)
A species of fishtail palm native to India and Southeast Asia, valued for its sap (used to make jaggery) and trunk pith (used to make sago).

Examples

Caryota urens palm tree
In some regions, Caryota urens is planted for shade and traditional food resources.
Local communities harvest Caryota urens for both its edible pith and its ornamental value.
The sap of Caryota urens is collected to produce a rich, brown sugar called jaggery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fishtail palm - common name for Caryota species
  • synonym: jaggery - unrefined sugar made from palm sap
  • synonym: sago - starch extracted from tropical palm stems
