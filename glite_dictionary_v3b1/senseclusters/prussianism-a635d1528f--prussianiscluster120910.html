Prussianism

noun
Prussian political system
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈprʌ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈprʌ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the political principles or system associated with Prussia, especially emphasizing strong central authority, strict bureaucracy, and conservative policies

Examples

Prussianism in government
The new laws were criticized as a return to Prussianism and excessive government control.
Historians debate whether Prussianism helped modernize the country or stifled local freedoms.
Some leaders admired the efficiency of Prussianism but rejected its rigid bureaucracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarianism - a system favoring strict obedience to authority
  • antonym: liberalism - a political philosophy emphasizing individual freedoms and limited government
