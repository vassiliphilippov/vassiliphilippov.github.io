scarification

noun
making surface cuts
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌskærəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌskærɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making cuts or scratches on a surface, often to promote growth or for decorative purposes

Examples

Seed scarification method
The gardener used scarification to help the seeds sprout more quickly.
Traditional scarification is practiced in some cultures as a form of body art.
Lawn care experts recommend scarification to improve soil aeration and grass growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abrasion - the process of scraping or wearing something away
  • synonym: incision - a cut made into something, especially surgically
