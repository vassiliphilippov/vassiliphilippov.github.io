simony

noun
the buying or selling of church offices
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪ.mə.ni / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.ə.ni / (BrE)
the buying or selling of ecclesiastical offices or church privileges, regarded as a sin

Examples

Simony in church
During the Middle Ages, simony was a widespread issue within the Catholic Church.
Laws were enacted to combat simony and ensure that church offices were not bought or sold.
The bishop was accused of simony when he sold church positions for personal gain.
