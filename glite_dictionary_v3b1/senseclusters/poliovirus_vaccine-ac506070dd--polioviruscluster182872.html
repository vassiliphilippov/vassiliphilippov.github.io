poliovirus vaccine

noun
vaccine for polio immunization
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊlioʊˌvaɪrəs vækˈsin / (AmE), / ˌpəʊliəʊˌvaɪərəs ˌvækˈsiːn / (BrE)
a vaccine made from poliovirus that is used to provide immunity against poliomyelitis (polio)

Examples

The poliovirus vaccine
After receiving the poliovirus vaccine, the risk of contracting polio is greatly reduced.
Children are routinely given the poliovirus vaccine as part of their immunization schedule.
The poliovirus vaccine was a major breakthrough in the fight against polio.
