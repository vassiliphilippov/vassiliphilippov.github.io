anagnost

noun
minor clerical reader in Eastern Orthodox Church
Pronunciation: / əˈnæɡnɑst / (AmE), / əˈnæɡnɒst / (BrE)
A cleric in the minor orders of the Eastern Orthodox Church who is responsible for reading the lessons aloud during the liturgy.

Examples

Anagnost reads scripture
As an anagnost, he had the privilege of reading scripture during the liturgy.
In the Orthodox tradition, the role of anagnost is sometimes given to young men as their first minor order.
The anagnost approached the lectern to begin the reading for the service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lector - a reader in the Western Christian Church, especially the Roman Catholic Church
