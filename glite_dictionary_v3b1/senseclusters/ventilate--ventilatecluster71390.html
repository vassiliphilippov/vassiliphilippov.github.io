ventilate

verb
provide with means for air or gas circulation
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈven.tə.leɪt/ /; / /ˈven.tɪ.leɪt/ (AmE), /ˈven.tɪ.leɪt/ /; / /ˈven.tə.leɪt/ (BrE)
to let fresh air into a room, building, or container, or to allow air to circulate

Examples

To ventilate a room
It's important to ventilate bathrooms regularly to prevent moisture buildup and mold.
The shelter was poorly ventilated, making it stuffy and uncomfortable for the animals.
We need to ventilate the basement by installing a window or vent.
Hospitals are designed to ventilate every ward frequently to ensure good air quality.
Please ventilate the room by opening the windows wide for a few minutes.
We need to ventilate the kitchen after frying fish last night.
Grain bins must be regularly ventilated to keep the harvest fresh.
It is important to ventilate the pantry to prevent mold growth.
The cellar was poorly ventilated, leading to faster spoilage of stored vegetables.
