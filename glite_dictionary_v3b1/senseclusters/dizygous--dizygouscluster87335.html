dizygous

adjective
derived from two fertilized eggs
Pronunciation: / daɪˈzaɪɡəs / (AmE), / daɪˈzɪɡəs / (BrE)
(of twins or embryos) derived from two separate eggs fertilized by two different sperm; fraternal.

Examples

Dizygous twins
Fraternal twins are also known as dizygous twins in medical literature.
In a dizygous pregnancy, each twin develops from a separate fertilized egg.
The probability of having dizygous twins is influenced by both genetics and the mother's age.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fraternal - (of twins) not identical; developed from two different eggs
  • antonym: monozygous - (of twins or embryos) derived from a single fertilized egg; identical
