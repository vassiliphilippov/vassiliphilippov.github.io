German chamomile

noun
annual herb used in medicine, similar to chamomile
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɜrmən kəˈmoʊmiːl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən kəˈməʊmiːl / (BrE)
an annual Eurasian herb (Matricaria chamomilla) with a fragrance and medicinal uses similar to chamomile, though with a more bitter taste and considered less potent

Examples

German chamomile plant
Despite its similarities to the common chamomile, German chamomile is said to have a more bitter flavor.
Many herbalists recommend German chamomile as a soothing tea for digestive discomfort.
The essential oil extracted from German chamomile is valued for its calming properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Roman chamomile - herbaceous plant (Chamaemelum nobile) used as a medicinal herb and for tea
  • synonym: chamomile - plant with daisy-like flowers used medicinally and for tea
