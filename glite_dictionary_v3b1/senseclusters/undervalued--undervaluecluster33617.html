undervalued

adjective
not properly valued
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈvæljuːd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈvæljuːd / (BrE)
not considered to be as important or valuable as it really is

Examples

Undervalued talent
Many artists feel their work is undervalued by the public.
The company is undervalued on the stock market compared to its actual performance.
Teachers are often undervalued despite the importance of their role in society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appreciated - recognized as valuable or important
  • antonym: overvalued - considered to be more valuable than it really is
  • synonym: unappreciated - not given the recognition or thanks deserved
