caddice fly

noun
aquatic moth-like insect; hairy wings, aquatic larvae
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkædɪs flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkædɪs flaɪ / (BrE)
A small, moth-like insect with two pairs of hairy membranous wings; its larvae are aquatic and often build protective cases of debris.

Examples

The caddice fly larvae
Fishermen often use artificial caddice flies as bait because trout are attracted to them.
The caddice fly is known for its larvae that create protective cases from small stones and plants.
We spotted several caddice flies hovering near the stream at dusk.
