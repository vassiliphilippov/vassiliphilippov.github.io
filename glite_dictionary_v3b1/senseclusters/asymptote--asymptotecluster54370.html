asymptote

noun
line a curve approaches at infinity
Pronunciation: / ˈæs.ɪmˌtoʊt / (AmE), / ˈæs.ɪm.təʊt / (BrE)
A straight line that a curve approaches but never touches or crosses, even at infinity.

Examples

The asymptote line
As x approaches infinity, the curve gets closer and closer to its asymptote.
Mathematicians use the term asymptote to describe how a curve behaves near certain lines.
The graph of the function has a horizontal asymptote at y = 3.
