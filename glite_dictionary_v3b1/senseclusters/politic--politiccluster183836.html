politic

adjective
smoothly agreeable and courteous
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːlətɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪtɪk / (BrE)
showing good judgment, tact, or prudence, especially to avoid offense or gain an advantage

Examples

A politic reply
He offered a politic reply to the sensitive question, avoiding controversy.
It would not be politic to challenge your boss in front of the whole team.
She remained politic throughout the negotiations, winning respect from all parties.
Choosing to stay silent was the most politic action he could take in that situation.
His politic response helped ease tensions during the heated discussion.
It was politic of her to avoid criticism by addressing the main concerns directly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diplomatic - tactful and able to deal with people in a sensitive way
  • synonym: prudent - acting with or showing care and thought for the future
  • antonym: tactless - lacking sensitivity in dealing with others
