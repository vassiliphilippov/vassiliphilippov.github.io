permeance

noun
magnetic field measure
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈpɜːr.miː.əns/ /; / /ˈpɜːr.mɪ.əns/ (AmE), /ˈpɜː.miː.əns/ /; / /ˈpɜː.mɪ.əns/ (BrE)
the measure of how easily a material allows the passage of a magnetic field through it

Examples

Magnetic permeance
Iron has a high permeance compared to air, making it useful in electromagnets.
The engineer calculated the permeance of the core to improve the device's efficiency.
Materials with low permeance are less effective at conducting magnetic fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: permeability - the ability of a material to allow something to pass through it, especially a magnetic field
  • antonym: reluctance - the resistance of a material to the passage of a magnetic field
