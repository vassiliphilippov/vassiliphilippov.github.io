ineradicably

adverb
impossibly to remove
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪˈrædɪkəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈrædɪkəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be removed, eliminated, or destroyed

Examples

Ineradicably woven into culture
The tradition is ineradicably woven into the culture of the village.
Some childhood experiences remain ineradicably in our memories throughout life.
The stain was ineradicably set into the old carpet despite many cleaning attempts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: permanently - in a way that lasts forever or for a very long time
  • antonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time
