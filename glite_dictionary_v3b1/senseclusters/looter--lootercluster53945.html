looter

noun
person who steals goods during unrest
5.3 2.31/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀêt…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàluÀêt…ô / (BrE)
a person who steals goods, especially during a riot, war, or natural disaster

Examples

Looters steal goods
During the chaos of war, looters ransacked homes and businesses.
The museum was heavily guarded to protect it from looters during the conflict.
The police arrested several looters who were breaking into abandoned shops after the hurricane.

Synonyms

  • plunderer
    7.2 0.09/m
    a plunderer steals goods by force during war or civil disorder, while a looter often takes goods in riots or disasters.
  • pillager
    8.1 0.09/m
    a pillager attacks and takes property in war, while a looter often steals opportunistically during riots or disasters.
  • marauder
    8.8 0.60/m
    a marauder roams and attacks places to steal valuables, while a looter usually steals during riots, wars, or disasters.
  • raider
    5.1 1.79/m
    a raider makes a quick armed attack to take goods, while a looter usually steals amid riots or disasters.
  • despoiler
    9.9 0.05/m
    a despoiler strips and ruins places, taking valuables in war, while a looter commonly steals during riots or disasters.
  • sacker
    9.5 0.05/m
    a sacker loots and destroys whole towns after attack, while a looter usually takes goods in brief riots or disasters.
