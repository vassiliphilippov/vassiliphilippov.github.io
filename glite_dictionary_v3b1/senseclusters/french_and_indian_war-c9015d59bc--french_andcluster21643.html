French and Indian War

noun
1754–1763 North American war between Britain and France
5.9
Pronunciation: /frentʃ ənd ˈɪn.diː.ən wɔːr/ /; / /frentʃ ænd ˈɪn.diː.ən wɔːr/ /; / /frentʃ ənd ˈɪn.diː.ən wɑːr/ (AmE), /frentʃ ənd ˈɪn.dɪ.ən wɔː/ /; / /frentʃ ænd ˈɪn.dɪ.ən wɔː/ (BrE)
a war fought in North America from 1754 to 1763 between France and Britain, both aided by different American Indian tribes, resulting in British dominance in Canada and eastern North America

Examples

The French and Indian War
British victory in the French and Indian War led to new taxes that angered American colonists.
Many historians see the French and Indian War as a precursor to the American Revolution.
The French and Indian War dramatically shifted the balance of power in colonial North America.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Seven Years' War - a global conflict from 1756 to 1763, of which the French and Indian War was the North American theater
