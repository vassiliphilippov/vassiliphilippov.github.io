cerebral edema

noun
swelling of the brain due to fluid accumulation
Pronunciation: / səˈribɹəl ɪˈdimə / (AmE), / səˈriːbrəl ɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
Swelling of the brain caused by the accumulation of excess fluid in the brain's tissue.

Examples

Cerebral edema swelling
Doctors administered medication to reduce cerebral edema and prevent further brain damage.
Severe cerebral edema can lead to increased intracranial pressure and neurological deficits.
The patient was diagnosed with cerebral edema following a traumatic head injury.
