equipollent

adjective
equal in effect
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌiːkwəˈpɑːlənt / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈpɒlənt / (BrE)
having equal power, force, or effect, especially in mathematics or logic

Examples

Equipollent statements
In logic, two statements are equipollent if each implies the other.
The two forces acting on the object are equipollent and cancel each other out.
Mathematicians proved that the two sets are equipollent because they have the same cardinality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equivalent - having the same value, function, or meaning
  • antonym: unequal - not equal in amount, value, or effect
