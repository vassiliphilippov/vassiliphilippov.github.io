nonstop

adjective
without intermediate stops
1.3 2.57/m
Pronunciation: / nÉ‘nËˆstÉ‘p /; / ËˆnÉ‘nËŒstÉ‘p / (AmE), / nÉ’nËˆstÉ’p /; / ËˆnÉ’nËŒstÉ’p / (BrE)
happening or continuing without any stops, interruptions, or pauses

Examples

A nonstop race
The athlete completed the marathon in a nonstop run without resting.
Their train journey was nonstop, saving them hours of travel time.
We booked a nonstop flight from New York to Los Angeles.
She works at a nonstop pace to finish all her assignments on time.
The nonstop rain made it difficult to go outside all week.
The hospital provides nonstop care for its patients, day and night.

Synonyms

    4.2 2.40/m
    uninterrupted goes on without any break at all, while nonstop means continuing without stops or pauses in general.
  • continuous
    3.7 24/m
    continuous describes something that keeps going smoothly without breaks, while nonstop means occurring without any stops or interruptions.
  • unbroken
    3.9 1.29/m
    unbroken means joined or whole without gaps in time or space, while nonstop focuses on not stopping or pausing.
  • unceasing
    6.0 0.03/m
    unceasing means never stopping and often feels endless, while nonstop simply says something continues without pauses or breaks.
  • incessant
    6.3 2.74/m
    incessant describes something annoyingly or strongly continuous, while nonstop is neutral and just says it continues without pauses.
  • constant
    2.5 14/m
    constant means regular or happening again and again over time, while nonstop means going on without any stops or interruptions.
  • sustained
    3.9 2.48/m
    sustained means kept up steadily, often with effort, while nonstop means it continues without any break or pause.
  • around-the-clock
    3.2 0.29/m
    around-the-clock runs all day and night without stopping, while nonstop means continuing without pauses and does not always mean twenty-four hours.
  • round-the-clock
    3.9 1.17/m
    round-the-clock describes services that run every hour of the day, while nonstop means something continues without any stop or pause.
  • day-and-night
    3.9 4.88/m
    day-and-night suggests something available or happening at all times, while nonstop focuses on an action that does not stop or pause.
