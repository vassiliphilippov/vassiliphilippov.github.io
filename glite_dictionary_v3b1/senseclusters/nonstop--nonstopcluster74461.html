nonstop

adjective
without intermediate stops
3.3
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈstɑp /; / ˈnɑnˌstɑp / (AmE), / nɒnˈstɒp /; / ˈnɒnˌstɒp / (BrE)
happening or continuing without any stops, interruptions, or pauses

Examples

A nonstop race
The athlete completed the marathon in a nonstop run without resting.
Their train journey was nonstop, saving them hours of travel time.
We booked a nonstop flight from New York to Los Angeles.
She works at a nonstop pace to finish all her assignments on time.
The nonstop rain made it difficult to go outside all week.
The hospital provides nonstop care for its patients, day and night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: connecting - (of flights or trains) requiring a change or stop before reaching the final destination
  • synonym: continuous - occurring without interruption
  • synonym: direct - (of a train, bus, or flight) going to a destination without breaking the journey
  • antonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous
