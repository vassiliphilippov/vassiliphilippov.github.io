deal

noun
large amount, often 'a deal of'
1.4 62/m
Pronunciation: / diÀêl / (AmE), / diÀêl / (BrE)
a large quantity or amount of something; often used in the phrase 'a deal of'

Examples

A great deal of time
She spent a great deal of her time traveling for work.
There's been a good deal of debate about the new policy.
We had to endure a fair deal of difficulty before reaching our goal.

Synonyms

  • a great deal
    1.8 22/m
    a great deal is a common phrase for a very large amount or a good bargain, while deal is a neutral large quantity.
  • lot
    1.0 811/m
    lot is an informal word used with 'of' to mean many things, while deal is a more formal large-amount phrase.
  • plenty
    1.0 81/m
    plenty means enough or more than enough for use, while deal means a large amount without implying sufficiency.

Related Terms
