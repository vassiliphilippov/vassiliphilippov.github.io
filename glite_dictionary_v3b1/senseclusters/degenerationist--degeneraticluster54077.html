degenerationist

noun
believer in decline theory
Pronunciation: / dɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / dɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or supports the theory that societies or species decline or deteriorate over time

Examples

A degenerationist thinker
The degenerationist argued that modern culture was a sign of society's decline.
In the nineteenth century, some scientists were known as degenerationists because of their theories about human evolution.
Critics of the degenerationist view believe that societies can also improve over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pessimist - a person who tends to see the worst aspect of things
  • antonym: progressivist - a person who believes in or supports social progress and improvement
