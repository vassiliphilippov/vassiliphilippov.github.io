cork oak

noun
oak tree with cork-producing bark
7.7 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / k…îrk o äk / (AmE), / k…îÀêk …ô äk / (BrE)
a medium-sized evergreen oak tree of southern Europe and northern Africa with thick, corky bark that is harvested to produce commercial cork

Examples

The cork oak tree
Portugal is home to vast forests of cork oak, which support a thriving cork industry.
The bark of the cork oak is harvested without harming the tree, making it a renewable resource.
The unique ecosystem created by the cork oak forests supports many endangered species.

Synonyms
