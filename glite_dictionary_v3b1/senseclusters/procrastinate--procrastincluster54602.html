procrastinate

verb
delay doing something unnecessarily
Pronunciation: / proʊˈkræs.təˌneɪt /; / proʊˈkræstəˌneɪt / (AmE), / prəˈkræs.tɪ.neɪt /; / prəˈkræstɪneɪt / (BrE)
to delay or postpone doing something that should be done, often unnecessarily or out of habit

Examples

To procrastinate on tasks
If you procrastinate on writing your paper, you might be up all night before the deadline.
Many students procrastinate by cleaning their rooms instead of studying.
She tends to procrastinate when faced with difficult decisions.
I tend to procrastinate when faced with difficult decisions.
If you procrastinate too much, important tasks may never get done.
She procrastinated all week and finished her assignment at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move something forward to an earlier time
  • synonym: delay - to put off something to a later time
  • antonym: hasten - to do something quickly; to hurry
