because

interjection
curt reply
Pronunciation: / bɪˈkəz / (AmE), / bɪˈkɒz / (BrE)
used as a brief or abrupt response to indicate a reason without further explanation.

Examples

Just because
When asked why he didn't attend the meeting, he just said because and left the room.
She refused to explain her decision, replying only with a sharp because.
"Why can't I go?" "Because," his mother answered, ending the discussion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whatever - used to indicate indifference or refusal to elaborate
