anomalistic year

noun
Earth's revolution from perihelion to perihelion
Pronunciation: / əˌnɑːməˈlɪstɪk jɪr / (AmE), / əˌnɒməˈlɪstɪk jɪə / (BrE)
the period taken by the Earth to complete one revolution from perihelion to perihelion, approximately 365 days, 6 hours, 13 minutes, and 53.1 seconds

Examples

An anomalistic year period
An anomalistic year is slightly longer than a tropical year due to the Earth's shifting orbit.
Astronomers calculate the anomalistic year to account for changes in Earth's distance from the Sun.
The difference between a sidereal year and an anomalistic year is small but significant in celestial mechanics.
