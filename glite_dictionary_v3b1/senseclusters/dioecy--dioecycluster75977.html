dioecy

noun
separate sexes
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdaɪ.oʊ.siː/ /; / /ˈdaɪ.ə.siː/ (AmE), /ˈdaɪ.ə.siː/ /; / /ˈdaɪ.əʊ.siː/ (BrE)
the condition in which a species has separate male and female individuals, each producing only one type of reproductive organ

Examples

Plant dioecy condition
Many flowering plants display dioecy, with male and female flowers on separate plants.
The study focused on the evolution of dioecy in certain species of fish.
In dioecy, only female individuals can produce seeds or offspring.
In dioecy, male and female flowers grow on separate plants, requiring both for reproduction.
The presence of dioecy in a species can affect how it is cultivated in gardens.
Some trees, like holly, display dioecy, so both male and female plants are needed for berries.

  • synonym: unisexual - having only male or only female reproductive organs
  • synonym: unisexuality - the state of having only one sex or type of reproductive organ
