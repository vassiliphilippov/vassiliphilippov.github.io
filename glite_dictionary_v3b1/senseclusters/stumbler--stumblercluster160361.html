stumbler

noun
someone prone to making mistakes
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌmblər / (AmE), / ˈstʌmblə / (BrE)
a person who frequently makes mistakes or errors, especially through lack of skill or competence

Examples

A stumbler at work
In the world of chess, a single stumbler can change the outcome of a tournament.
She feared being seen as a stumbler during her first public presentation.
The new employee turned out to be a stumbler who struggled with even basic tasks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bungler - a person who does things clumsily or ineptly
  • antonym: expert - a person with a high level of skill or competence
