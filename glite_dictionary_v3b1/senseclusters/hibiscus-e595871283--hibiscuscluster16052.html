Hibiscus

noun
genus of flowering plants with large blossoms
Pronunciation: / hɪˈbɪskəs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical and subtropical plants, including herbs, shrubs, and trees, widely grown for their large, colorful flowers

Examples

A Hibiscus flower
In tropical regions, Hibiscus can bloom year-round, attracting butterflies and hummingbirds.
Many gardeners choose Hibiscus for its showy and vibrant flowers.
The Hibiscus genus includes both small bushes and tall, tree-like plants.
