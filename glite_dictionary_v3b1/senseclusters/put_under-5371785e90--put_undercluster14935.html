put under

verb
administer anesthesia; make unconscious
6.0 0.45/m

to administer an anesthetic drug to someone so they become unconscious, especially before surgery or a medical procedure

Examples

To put under anesthesia
He had never been put under with general anesthesia before his knee operation.
I was very nervous before being put under for my wisdom tooth extraction.
The doctors will put under the patient before starting the procedure.

Synonyms

  • anesthetize
    7.1 0.85/m
    anesthetize means to give an anesthetic that causes unconsciousness in medical procedures, while put under is a common phrasal alternative.
  • put out
    7.3 0.23/m
    put out is a casual phrasal verb often used in speech for giving anesthetic, while put under sounds more formal and clinical.
  • put to sleep
    6.3 0.59/m
    put to sleep can mean euthanize an animal or make someone unconscious by anesthesia, while put under refers only to medical anesthesia.
