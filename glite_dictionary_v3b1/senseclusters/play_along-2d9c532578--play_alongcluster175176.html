play along

verb
cooperate or pretend to cooperate
1.7 3.5
Pronunciation: /pleɪ ə.ˈlɔːŋ/ (AmE), /pleɪ ə.ˈlɒŋ/ (BrE)
to cooperate with someone or pretend to do so, often in order to avoid trouble or deception

Examples

To play along with a joke
He didn't believe the story, but he played along to see what would happen.
She decided to play along with their plan until she could find a way out.
Sometimes it's easier to just play along rather than start an argument.
