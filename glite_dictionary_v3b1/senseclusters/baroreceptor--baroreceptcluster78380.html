baroreceptor

noun
pressure-sensitive sensory nerve ending
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛroʊrɪˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˌbɑːrərɪˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a sensory nerve ending in the body that responds to changes in blood pressure

Examples

Baroreceptors in arteries
A sudden drop in blood pressure stimulates the baroreceptors to trigger a reflex response.
Damage to baroreceptors can result in impaired blood pressure regulation.
The carotid sinus contains a specialized baroreceptor that helps regulate blood pressure.
