ectoblast

noun
outer germ layer in embryos
Pronunciation: / ˈɛk.toʊˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈɛk.təʊˌblɑːst / (BrE)
the outermost layer of cells in the early embryo, which gives rise to the skin and nervous system.

Examples

The ectoblast layer
Damage to the ectoblast can result in significant developmental disorders affecting skin and nerves.
Researchers studied how the ectoblast differentiates during early embryonic development.
The ectoblast eventually forms the nervous system and the epidermis in vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mesoblast - the middle germ layer in an embryo
