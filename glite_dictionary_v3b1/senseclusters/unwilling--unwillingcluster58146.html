Synonyms

  • reluctant
    3.5 19/m
    reluctant is hesitant and not eager to act, while unwilling is simply not wanting to do something.
  • loath
    6.9 1.37/m
    loath is formal and a strong way to say you do not want something, while unwilling is more general.
  • disinclined
    6.4 0.46/m
    disinclined shows a mild preference against acting and sounds polite, while unwilling means a clear lack of desire.
  • grudging
    6.2 0.64/m
    grudging describes actions done unwillingly or with bitterness, while unwilling describes the person's lack of desire to act.
  • indisposed
    6.8 0.01/m
    indisposed often politely says someone cannot or will not do something, whereas unwilling says they do not want to do it.
