unwilling

adjective
not wanting or refusing to do something
1.0
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
not wanting to do something or doing it with reluctance; done against one's will

Examples

Unwilling to help
He seemed unwilling to listen to our advice, despite our best efforts.
Many people are unwilling to change their routines, even when it would benefit them.
She was unwilling to participate in the meeting because she felt unprepared.
Despite being an unwilling participant, he completed the task.
His unwilling smile showed that he was not truly happy.
She gave an unwilling apology, clearly forced by circumstance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eager - keenly wanting to do something; enthusiastic
  • synonym: reluctant - hesitant or unwilling to do something
  • antonym: willing - ready or prepared to do something
