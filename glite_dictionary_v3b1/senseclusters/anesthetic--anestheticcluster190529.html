anesthetic

noun
pain-numbing drug used in medicine
Pronunciation: / ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk /; / ænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌænɪsˈθɛtɪk /; / ˌanɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
A drug or substance that causes temporary loss of bodily sensation, especially used to prevent pain during medical procedures.

Examples

Local anesthetic
Patients are usually given a local anesthetic during minor surgical procedures.
She was allergic to one type of anesthetic used in hospitals.
The dentist applied an anesthetic before extracting the tooth.
An injection of local anaesthetic will numb the area for the procedure.
He was under general anaesthetic during the operation and felt nothing.
The dentist gave me an anaesthetic before removing my wisdom tooth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analgesic - a drug that relieves pain without causing loss of consciousness
  • synonym: anesthetic - American spelling of 'anaesthetic'
  • antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity in the body
