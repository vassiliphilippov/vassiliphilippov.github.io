in series

adjective
arranged one after another, sequentially
5.8 0.38/m
Pronunciation: / …™n Ààs…™riÀêz / (AmE), / …™n Ààs…™…ôriÀêz / (BrE)
(of electrical devices, operations, etc.) arranged or happening one after another in a sequence, usually so that each depends on the completion of the previous.

Examples

In series connection
Each stage of the experiment was carried out in series rather than simultaneously.
The batteries must be connected in series for the circuit to work properly.
The computers were configured in series to handle tasks one after another.

Synonyms

  • serial
    3.7 9.13/m
    serial describes things happening one after another in a set, while in series emphasizes electrical or linked arrangement with dependent parts.
  • sequential
    4.9 6.13/m
    sequential describes items arranged in a specific order, while in series often implies electrical or linked parts that depend on each other.
  • sequentially
    4.5 1.21/m
    sequentially means doing things one after another in time, while in series names a physical or electrical arrangement of dependent parts.
