in series

adjective
arranged one after another, sequentially
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈsɪriːz / (AmE), / ɪn ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
(of electrical devices, operations, etc.) arranged or happening one after another in a sequence, usually so that each depends on the completion of the previous.

Examples

In series connection
Each stage of the experiment was carried out in series rather than simultaneously.
The batteries must be connected in series for the circuit to work properly.
The computers were configured in series to handle tasks one after another.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in parallel - arranged or happening at the same time, often alongside each other
