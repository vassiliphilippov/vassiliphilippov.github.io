outpass

verb
to permit temporary leave
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtpæs / (AmE), / ˈaʊtpɑːs / (BrE)
to officially allow someone to leave a place temporarily, usually by issuing a permit or pass

Examples

To outpass a patient
The principal agreed to outpass the students for the afternoon so they could attend the competition.
You must request the warden to outpass you if you need to leave the hostel overnight.
The officer refused to outpass anyone during the lockdown period.
