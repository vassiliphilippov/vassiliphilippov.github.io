reimagination

noun
new interpretation
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪˌmædʒɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˌmædʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of creating a new or different version of something that already exists

Examples

The reimagination of a story
The director's reimagination of the classic play brought new life to the story.
This novel is a clever reimagination of traditional fairy tales for a modern audience.
The museum's exhibit is a reimagination of ancient art using digital technology.
The artist's reimagination of the city skyline brought a fresh perspective to the exhibition.
A successful reimagination of the product can attract a new generation of customers.
The play is a bold reimagination of a well-known fairy tale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adaptation - a version of something that has been changed for a new use or situation
  • synonym: innovation - the introduction of something new or different
  • antonym: original - the first or earliest form of something, not changed or adapted
  • synonym: reinterpretation - a new or different explanation or presentation of something
  • antonym: tradition - a long-established custom or belief
