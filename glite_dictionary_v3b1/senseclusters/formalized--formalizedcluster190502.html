formalized

adjective
characterized by adherence to established forms
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrməˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlaɪzd / (BrE)
characterized by strict or systematic adherence to established forms or conventions, especially in art or religion

Examples

Formalized dance style
Classical ballet is a highly formalized form of dance with precise movements and traditions.
Her artwork became increasingly formalized, drawing on strict aesthetic principles.
The museum's display of formalized rituals explores the roots of religious ceremonies.
Many traditional art forms are formalized, requiring artists to follow established methods.
The ritual was highly formalized, with each step precisely choreographed.
Their arguments often became formalized debates instead of open discussions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conventional - following traditional forms or standards
  • antonym: informal - not following established or official forms or conventions
