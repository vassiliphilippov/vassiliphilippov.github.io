draw

verb
(of chimney, etc.) allow draft/air to flow
7.7 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / dr…î / (AmE), / dr…îÀê / (BrE)
(of a chimney, stove, etc.) to allow air to flow through it, creating a draft that carries smoke or gases away.

Examples

The chimney draws well
If the stove doesn't draw well, the fire won't burn efficiently.
The old cottage's chimney does not draw properly, filling the room with smoke.
This model of fireplace is designed to draw even in windy conditions.
