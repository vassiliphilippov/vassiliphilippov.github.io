kaddish

noun
Jewish prayer of praise
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkadɪʃ / (BrE)
a Jewish prayer recited in synagogue services, especially in memory of the dead

Examples

The kaddish prayer
The family gathered at the synagogue to recite the kaddish for their loved one.
During the service, the congregation stood and chanted the kaddish together.
He learned to say the kaddish as part of his religious education.
After the funeral, the family gathered to recite the kaddish together.
He attended synagogue every day to say kaddish for his father.
The community supported her as she said kaddish during the mourning period.
