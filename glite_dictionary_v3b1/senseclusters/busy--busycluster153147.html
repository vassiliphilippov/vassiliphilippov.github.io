busy

verb
occupy oneself with an activity
1.0 1.32/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆbÉªzi / (AmE), / ËˆbÉªzi / (BrE)
to occupy oneself with an activity or task, often to avoid idleness

Examples

To busy oneself
During the long summer, he busied himself with learning new recipes.
Many retirees busy themselves with volunteer work after leaving their jobs.
She likes to busy herself with gardening in her free time.

Synonyms

  • occupy oneself
    4.1 0.16/m
    occupy oneself means doing an activity to stay active and avoid idleness, while busy describes being occupied with tasks.
  • occupy
    3.5 6.27/m
    occupy means to fill someone's time or mind with an activity, while busy means to be engaged in tasks.
  • otherwise engaged
    4.6 0.07/m
    otherwise engaged describes being tied to another commitment and unavailable, while busy simply means taking part in any activity.
  • have one's hands full
    3.9 0.45/m
    have one's hands full means to deal with many tasks and responsibilities, while busy can mean being occupied in general.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engage - to participate or become involved in
  • synonym: occupy - to fill or take up (a space, time, or attention)
