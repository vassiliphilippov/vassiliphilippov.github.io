Brith

noun
Jewish ritual circumcision ceremony
Pronunciation: / brɪθ / (AmE), / brɪθ / (BrE)
The Jewish religious ceremony of circumcision performed on a male child, usually on the eighth day after birth.

Examples

The Brith ceremony
A rabbi is usually present to perform the Brith ceremony.
The Brith marks the child's induction into the Jewish faith.
The family gathered for the baby's Brith at the synagogue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: baptism - Christian religious rite of initiation, cited for comparison as a ritual ceremony
  • synonym: circumcision - the surgical removal of the foreskin of the penis; ritual in several religions
