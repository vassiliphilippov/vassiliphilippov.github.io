bodger

noun
traditional woodworker
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːdʒər / (AmE), / ˈbɒdʒə / (BrE)
a traditional woodworker who makes chair legs and other parts from green wood, especially using a pole lathe

Examples

Traditional bodger crafts
The museum displayed tools once used by a skilled bodger in the English countryside.
A bodger would set up a temporary workshop in the woods to craft chair legs from fresh timber.
Many traditional chairs were made with parts turned by a bodger using a pole lathe.
