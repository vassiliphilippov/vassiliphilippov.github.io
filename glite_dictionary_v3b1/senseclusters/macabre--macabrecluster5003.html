macabre

adjective
disturbing, gruesome, related to death
5.8
Pronunciation: / məˈkɑb / (AmE), / məˈkɑːbə / (BrE)
Disturbing and horrifying because it is concerned with or depicts death and injury.

Examples

A macabre story
His fascination with macabre stories made him an expert on Gothic literature.
The artist's latest exhibit features macabre sculptures inspired by Victorian mourning customs.
The haunted house was decorated with macabre props and eerie lighting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: ghastly - causing great horror or fear
  • synonym: gruesome - causing horror or repugnance; grisly
