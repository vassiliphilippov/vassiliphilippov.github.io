mendicant

noun
person who begs for a living
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛn.dɪ.kənt / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.dɪ.kənt / (BrE)
a person, often poor, who lives by begging for money or food

Examples

A mendicant on streets
After losing his job, he became a mendicant wandering the city streets.
In medieval times, some religious orders required their members to live as mendicants.
The old mendicant sat at the church steps, hoping for charity from passersby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beggar - a person who begs, typically for money or food
  • synonym: pauper - a very poor person
