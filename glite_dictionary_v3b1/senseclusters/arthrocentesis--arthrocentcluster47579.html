arthrocentesis

noun
removal of fluid from a joint
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrθroʊsɛnˈtiːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːθrəʊsɛnˈtiːsɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which fluid is removed from a joint using a needle for diagnosis or treatment

Examples

Arthrocentesis procedure
Arthrocentesis is often performed under local anesthesia to minimize discomfort.
Fluid collected during arthrocentesis can help diagnose different types of arthritis.
The doctor recommended arthrocentesis to relieve the swelling in the patient's knee joint.
