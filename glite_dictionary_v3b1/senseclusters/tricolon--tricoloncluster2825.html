tricolon

noun
three-part phrase
9.2
Pronunciation: / traɪˈkoʊlɒn / (AmE), / traɪˈkəʊlɒn / (BrE)
a rhetorical device consisting of a series of three parallel words, phrases, or clauses

Examples

A tricolon phrase
The famous phrase 'life, liberty, and happiness' is a classic tricolon.
Writers often use a tricolon to create rhythm and emphasis in their speeches.
The politician's speech ended with a powerful tricolon that inspired the audience.
