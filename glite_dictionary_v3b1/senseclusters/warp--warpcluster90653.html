warp

noun
moral or mental distortion
Pronunciation: / wɔrp / (AmE), / wɔːp / (BrE)
a mental or moral distortion; a deviation from what is normal or expected in thinking or character

Examples

A moral warp
She explained his cruelty as a warp in his moral judgment.
The novel explores the psychological warp that can develop from years of loneliness.
The traumatic events in his childhood left a lasting warp in his personality.
His opinions show a strange warp that makes him distrustful of everyone.
The novel's plot reveals a psychological warp in the main character.
There was a noticeable warp in her moral judgment after the incident.
