actuary

noun
professional in insurance risk analysis
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.tʃuˌɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈaktjʊəri / (BrE)
a person whose job is to collect and analyze numerical data, especially statistics, in order to calculate insurance risks and premiums

Examples

An actuary calculates risks
After ten years as an actuary, she had become an expert in evaluating insurance claims.
Becoming an actuary requires strong mathematical skills and extensive training.
The company hired an actuary to determine the financial risks involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: statistician - an expert in statistics
