bright line

noun
clear, definite rule or distinction
Pronunciation: / braɪt laɪn / (AmE), / braɪt laɪn / (BrE)
a clear and definite distinction or rule, especially one used to make decisions in law or ethics

Examples

A bright line rule
In some ethical debates, a bright line is hard to establish.
The judge applied a bright line rule to decide the dispute.
The legislation draws a bright line between legal and illegal conduct.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gray area - an unclear or undefined situation
