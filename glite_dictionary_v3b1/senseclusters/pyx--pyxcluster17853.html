pyx

noun
box for coins awaiting assay
8.9
Pronunciation: / pɪks / (AmE), / pɪks / (BrE)
A box or chest in which coins from the mint are stored while awaiting assay.

Examples

Coin pyx
A rare pyx from the seventeenth century was displayed at the numismatic exhibition.
The newly minted coins were sealed in a pyx before undergoing official testing.
Traditionally, the pyx was opened during a formal ceremony to check the quality of the coins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assay - an analysis to determine composition or quality, especially of precious metals
