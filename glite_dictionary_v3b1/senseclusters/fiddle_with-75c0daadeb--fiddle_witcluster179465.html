fiddle with

verb
nervously handle or play with something
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪdəl wɪð / (AmE), / ˈfɪdəl wɪð / (BrE)
to touch or handle something in a nervous, restless, or unconscious way, often without a specific purpose

Examples

To fiddle with a pen
He couldn't stop fiddling with his pen during the meeting.
Nervous, he kept fiddling with the buttons on his coat.
She fiddled with her necklace absentmindedly as she listened to the lecture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: toy with - to handle or move something aimlessly or absentmindedly
  • synonym: twiddle - to move something repeatedly between one's fingers, often nervously
