baiocco

noun
old Italian coin
Pronunciation: / baɪˈɑːkoʊ / (AmE), / baɪˈɒkəʊ / (BrE)
a small copper coin that was formerly used as currency in the Papal States of Italy

Examples

Old baiocco coin
The merchant asked for two baiocchi in exchange for a loaf of bread.
Collectors value the baiocco for its historical significance in Italian currency.
He found an old baiocco while exploring the ruins of a Roman villa.
