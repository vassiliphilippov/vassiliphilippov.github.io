rainy day

noun
future time of financial need
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪni deɪ / (AmE), / ˈreɪni deɪ / (BrE)
a day when it rains; also, a time when you might need extra money or resources, especially in case of future difficulty

Examples

A rainy day fund
It's wise to prepare for a rainy day, just in case something unexpected happens.
She always puts a little money aside for a rainy day.
They broke into their savings when a rainy day finally arrived.
She always puts some money aside for a rainy day just in case.
We planned an indoor movie marathon because it was a rainy day.
When the company lost major clients, they had to use their rainy day fund.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • synonym: emergency - a serious, unexpected, and often dangerous situation requiring immediate action
  • synonym: emergency fund - money set aside for unexpected situations or expenses
