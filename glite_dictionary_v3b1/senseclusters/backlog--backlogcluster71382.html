backlog

verb
to accumulate uncompleted tasks
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbæk.lɔɡ /; / ˈbækˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈbæk.lɒɡ /; / ˈbækˌlɒg / (BrE)
to accumulate or queue work, tasks, or orders that have not yet been completed

Examples

Orders backlog quickly
Due to staff shortages, orders began to backlog quickly in the warehouse.
If you don't answer your emails daily, they will backlog and become overwhelming.
The repair requests continued to backlog during the busy season.
Due to the high volume of orders, the company backlogged shipments until more staff became available.
The IT department backlogs requests that cannot be resolved immediately.
After the holiday, they backlogged dozens of customer emails for later response.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accumulate - to gather or collect a quantity over time
  • synonym: postpone - to delay something to a later time
  • antonym: process - to deal with or handle tasks or requests
