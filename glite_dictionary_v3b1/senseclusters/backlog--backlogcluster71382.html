backlog

verb
to accumulate uncompleted tasks
3.0 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbæ.klɑːg/ /; / /ˈbæ.klɔːg/ (AmE), /ˈbæ.klɒg/ (BrE)
to accumulate or queue work, tasks, or orders that have not yet been completed

Examples

Orders backlog quickly

If you don't answer your emails daily, they will backlog and become overwhelming.
The repair requests continued to backlog during the busy season.

The IT department backlogs requests that cannot be resolved immediately.
After the holiday, they backlogged dozens of customer emails for later response.
