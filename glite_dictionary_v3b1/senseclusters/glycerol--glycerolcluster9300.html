glycerol

noun
a sweet, syrupy alcohol used in products
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɪsəˌrɔl / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsərɒl / (BrE)
a sweet, colorless, syrupy liquid used in making soaps, food, and pharmaceuticals, chemically known as a trihydroxy alcohol

Examples

Glycerol syrup
Glycerol is commonly found in many food products as a sweetener and preservative.
Scientists often use glycerol in laboratory experiments as a solvent.
The soap contains glycerol to help moisturize the skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glycerin - another name for glycerol, especially in commercial or non-chemical contexts
