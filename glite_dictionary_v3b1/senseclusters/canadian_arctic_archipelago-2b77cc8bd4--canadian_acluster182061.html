Canadian Arctic Archipelago

noun
group of Arctic islands north of Canada
Pronunciation: / kəˈneɪdiən ˈɑrktɪk ˌɑrkɪˈpɛləˌgoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən ˈɑːktɪk ˌɑːkɪˈpeləɡəʊ / (BrE)
all the islands located north of mainland Canada and within the Arctic Circle, forming a major archipelago in the Arctic region.

Examples

The Canadian Arctic Archipelago
Climate change has had a significant impact on the wildlife inhabiting the Canadian Arctic Archipelago.
Icebreakers are often needed to navigate the waters around the Canadian Arctic Archipelago during the summer months.
The Canadian Arctic Archipelago is home to some of the most remote and unspoiled landscapes on Earth.
