vie

verb
compete to achieve or win
6.2 3.59/m
Pronunciation: / vaÉª / (AmE), / vaÉª / (BrE)
to compete eagerly with someone in order to achieve or win something

Examples

To vie for victory
Several teams will vie for the championship title this year.
The candidates are vying for attention in the crowded marketplace.
Writers vied with one another for the prestigious award.

Synonyms

  • compete
    2.6 61/m
    compete is a common, neutral verb for trying to win against others, while vie implies eager, direct rivalry.
  • contest
    2.8 2.99/m
    contest is used when people formally compete or challenge a result, while vie suggests eager personal rivalry.
  • contend
    3.7 6.81/m
    contend often means struggle or assert in formal contexts, while vie stresses eager competition to win.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compete - to strive to gain or win something by defeating or establishing superiority over others
  • antonym: cooperate - to work jointly towards the same end
