open syllable

noun
syllable ending with a vowel
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ˈsɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈsɪləbəl / (BrE)
A syllable that ends with a vowel or diphthong, typically resulting in a long vowel sound.

Examples

An open syllable ends vowel
A common feature of an open syllable is that the vowel sound is long.
In the word 'piano', the first syllable 'pi-' is an open syllable.
Teachers often highlight the difference between a open syllable and a closed syllable when teaching reading.

Related Terms

  • antonym: closed syllable - a syllable ending with a consonant
