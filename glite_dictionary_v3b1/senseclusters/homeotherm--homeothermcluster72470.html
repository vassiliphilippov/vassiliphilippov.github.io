homeotherm

noun
animal with constant body temperature
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊ.mi.oʊˌθɝm / (AmE), / ˈhəʊ.mi.əʊˌθɜːm / (BrE)
an animal whose body temperature remains relatively constant and is independent of the surrounding environment

Examples

A homeotherm animal
A homeotherm maintains its internal body temperature regardless of cold or hot environments.
Birds and mammals are classic examples of homeotherm species.
Unlike a lizard, a homeotherm can remain active even in freezing weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym: poikilotherm - an animal whose body temperature varies with the environment
