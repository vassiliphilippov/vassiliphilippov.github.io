hypophysial dwarf

noun
person with growth hormone deficiency dwarfism
Pronunciation: / haɪˌpɒfɪziəl ˈdwɔrf / (AmE), / haɪˌpɒfɪziəl ˈdwɔːf / (BrE)
a person of abnormally short stature caused by a deficiency of growth hormones produced by the pituitary gland, rather than by genetic factors such as achondroplasia

Examples

A hypophysial dwarf child
The diagnosis confirmed that the child was a hypophysial dwarf rather than having a genetic form of dwarfism.
Treatment with growth hormone may benefit a hypophysial dwarf if started early enough.
Unlike his brother, who had achondroplasia, he was considered a hypophysial dwarf due to pituitary dysfunction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: achondroplastic dwarf - a person whose short stature is due to achondroplasia, a genetic disorder
  • synonym: pituitary dwarf - a person whose short stature is due to underfunction of the pituitary gland
