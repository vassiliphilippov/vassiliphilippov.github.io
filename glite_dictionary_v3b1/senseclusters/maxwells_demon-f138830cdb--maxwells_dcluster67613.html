Maxwell's demon

noun
experimental device implementing the thought experiment
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmæks.wəlz ˈdiː.mən/ (AmE), /ˈmæks.wəlz ˈdiː.mən/ (BrE)
(in experimental physics) a physical device or system that uses measurement and feedback to extract work or reduce entropy from a thermal bath, thereby implementing the idea of Maxwell's thought experiment.

Examples

A Maxwell's demon device
Researchers built a nanoscale setup that acts as a Maxwell's demon, converting information into work.
The lab demonstrated a feedback-controlled engine functioning like a Maxwell's demon in the experiment.
Their paper explains how the device qualifies as an experimental Maxwell's demon under precise measurement protocols.
