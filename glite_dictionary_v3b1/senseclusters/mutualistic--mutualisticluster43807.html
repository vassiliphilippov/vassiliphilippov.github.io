mutualistic

adjective
benefiting both sides
6.0
Pronunciation: /mjuː.tʃu.ˈæl.ɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /mjuː.tʃu.ˈæl.ɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
describing a relationship in which both parties benefit from each other

Examples

Mutualistic relationship
The clownfish and sea anemone share a mutualistic relationship in the ocean.
Scientists study mutualistic interactions to understand how species help each other survive.
A mutualistic partnership can increase the chances of survival for both organisms involved.
The two species have a mutualistic relationship, each helping the other survive in the wild.
A mutualistic agreement between the organizations led to shared resources and greater success.
In nature, bees and flowers often form mutualistic partnerships that benefit both.

Related Terms

  • antonym: competitive - involving rivalry or opposition
  • synonym: cooperative - working together for a common benefit
  • antonym: parasitic - describing a relationship where one organism benefits and the other is harmed
  • synonym: symbiotic - involving close cooperation between different organisms, often benefiting both
