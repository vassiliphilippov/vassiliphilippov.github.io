plink

verb
to make a light ringing sound
Pronunciation: / plɪŋk / (AmE), / plɪŋk / (BrE)
to make a short, high-pitched, metallic or ringing sound, especially by striking objects or playing simple notes on an instrument

Examples

Coins plink in a jar
The coins plinked as they dropped into the glass jar.
She heard a pebble plink against the window late at night.
Tiny bells plink softly in the breeze outside the shop.
The small stone plinked against the window, catching her attention.
He plinks the strings of the guitar to tune it before playing.
Raindrops plinked on the metal roof throughout the night.
She sat by the window plinking a few cheerful notes on her old guitar.
He absentmindedly plinks the piano keys while waiting for his lesson to begin.
Raindrops plinking on the metal roof created a gentle, soothing rhythm.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: clang - to make a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • synonym: clink - to make a light, sharp, ringing sound
  • synonym: ping - to make a short, sharp, high-pitched sound
  • synonym: tinkle - to make a series of light, ringing sounds
