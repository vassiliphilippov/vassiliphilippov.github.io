silent

adjective
implied; not said aloud
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪlənt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənt / (BrE)
implied or understood without being directly stated; communicated without words

Examples

Silent approval
Her parents gave their silent approval to the plan by not objecting.
Their silent consent was clear from their actions, even though nothing was discussed.
There was a silent agreement among the team members to work extra hours if needed.
His silent apology was clear from the look in his eyes.
She offered a silent prayer for his safety before the ceremony began.
There was a silent understanding between the two friends, needing no words.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tacit - implied or understood without being stated; unspoken
  • synonym: unspoken - not spoken; expressed without speech
  • antonym: voiced - spoken out loud; expressed verbally
