rush

noun
sudden strong flow
2.7 1.6
Pronunciation: /rʌʃ/ (AmE), /rʌʃ/ (BrE)
a sudden or intense movement, flow, or period of activity, often due to urgency or excitement

Examples

A rush of water
A sudden rush of water swept through the valley after the dam broke.
She felt a rush of adrenaline as she stepped onto the stage.
There was a rush of cold air when he opened the door.
In his morning rush, he spilled coffee all over the desk.
The students made a rush for the exits when the fire alarm sounded.
There was a mad rush to get tickets when the box office opened.
Let's avoid the lunchtime rush and eat later when it’s quieter.
She felt a rush of excitement as the concert began.
The store was crowded during the morning rush as people hurried to finish their shopping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haste - excessive speed or urgency of movement
  • antonym: leisure - free time and relaxed pace
  • antonym: lull - a temporary period of quiet or lack of activity
  • synonym: surge - a sudden powerful forward or upward movement
