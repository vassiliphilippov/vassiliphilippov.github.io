nightfish

noun
nocturnal fish
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a type of fish that is active or most easily seen at night, often found in freshwater habitats

Examples

The nightfish swims
The nightfish hides under rocks during the day and comes out to feed after sunset.
Researchers observed the nightfish using special cameras that work in low light.
Anglers often try to catch nightfish because they are more active after dark.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dayfish - a fish that is active during the day
  • synonym: nocturnal - active during the night
