Dardic language

noun
group of Indic languages in South-Central Asia
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑrdɪk ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːdɪk ˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
Any of a group of Indic languages spoken in Kashmir, eastern Afghanistan, and northern Pakistan.

Examples

Dardic language group
Kashmiri is a well-known Dardic language spoken in the region of Kashmir.
Researchers have studied the phonology of several Dardic languages in Pakistan.
The Dardic language group includes Shina, Khowar, and Kalasha.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Indic language - any language belonging to the Indo-Aryan branch of the Indo-European language family
