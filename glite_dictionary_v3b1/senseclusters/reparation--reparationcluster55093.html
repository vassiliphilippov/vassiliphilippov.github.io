reparation

noun
war compensation paid by defeated country
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(usually plural) compensation paid by a defeated country to another for damage or injury during a war.

Examples

War reparations payment
Failure to fulfill reparations obligations led to economic instability in the defeated country.
Germany struggled to pay the reparations imposed after the Treaty of Versailles.
The victors demanded substantial reparations for damages caused during the conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indemnity - money paid as compensation, especially by a country after war
