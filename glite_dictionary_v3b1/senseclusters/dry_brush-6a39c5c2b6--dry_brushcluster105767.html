dry brush

verb
to paint with little paint
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪ ˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
to apply paint with a brush that has very little paint or moisture, creating a textured, scratchy effect on the surface

Examples

To dry brush paint
The artist decided to dry brush the background to give it a rough, textured appearance.
If you dry brush the edges of the canvas, you can create a soft, faded effect.
She learned to dry brush details onto her model to highlight the raised surfaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dab - to apply paint or color with light, quick touches
  • antonym: wash - to apply a thin, diluted layer of paint
