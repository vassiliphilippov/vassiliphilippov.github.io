Mycenaean culture

noun
late Bronze Age Greek civilization (1400-1100 BC)
9.9
Pronunciation: /maɪ.sɪ.ˈniː.ən ˈkʌl.tʃər/ /; / /maɪ.sə.ˈniː.ən ˈkʌl.tʃər/ (AmE), /maɪ.sɪ.ˈniː.ən ˈkʌl.tʃə/ /; / /maɪ.sə.ˈniː.ən ˈkʌl.tʃə/ (BrE)
the Bronze Age civilization centered in Mycenae in Greece that flourished from about 1400 to 1100 BC, known for its palatial states, monumental architecture, and early Greek writing system (Linear B).

Examples

Mycenaean culture palaces
Archaeologists have uncovered remarkable palaces built by the Mycenaean culture during the late Bronze Age.
Many myths of ancient Greece are believed to have origins in the Mycenaean culture.
The Mycenaean culture collapsed around 1100 BC, marking the end of the Bronze Age in Greece.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Aegean civilization - general term for Bronze Age cultures of the Aegean area
  • degree higher: Ancient Greek civilization - later civilization in Greece emerging after the Mycenaean period
  • degree lower: Minoan civilization - early Bronze Age culture from Crete, preceding Mycenaean culture
