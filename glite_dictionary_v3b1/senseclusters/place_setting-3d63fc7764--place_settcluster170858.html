place setting

noun
tableware for one person at a meal
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪs ˌsɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪs ˌsɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
A set of tableware and utensils arranged for the use of one person at a meal.

Examples

A place setting on table
Each guest had a place setting with matching silverware and china.
The waitress replaced the dirty place setting before serving the new customer.
They ordered special napkins to coordinate with every place setting for the wedding reception.
