Bergsonism

noun
the philosophy of Henri Bergson
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜːrɡsəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡsənɪzəm / (BrE)
The philosophical doctrine and theories of Henri Bergson, especially emphasizing the concept of duration and the existence of the élan vital as a fundamental life force.

Examples

Bergsonism philosophy
Many intellectuals debated the merits of Bergsonism during the rise of modernist philosophy.
Some critics argue that Bergsonism offers a unique perspective on the nature of consciousness.
The influence of Bergsonism on twentieth-century art and literature is widely documented.
