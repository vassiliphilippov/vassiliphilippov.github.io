aftergrowth

noun
new growth after harvest
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈæf.tər.groʊθ/ (AmE), /ˈɑːf.tə.grəʊθ/ (BrE)
new growth of plants or tissue that appears after the main period of growth or harvest

Examples

Field aftergrowth
After the wheat was harvested, a thick aftergrowth covered the field within weeks.
Gardeners often remove the aftergrowth to prepare the soil for the next planting season.
The forest floor was green with aftergrowth following the spring rains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: regrowth - the process of growing again after being cut or damaged
  • synonym: stubble - short stalks left in a field after crops have been harvested
