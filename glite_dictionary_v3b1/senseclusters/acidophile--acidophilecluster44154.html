acidophile

noun
organism thriving in acid
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪdəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / əˈsɪdəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
an organism that thrives in acidic environments

Examples

Acidophiles in acid
Scientists study acidophiles to understand how life persists in acidic habitats.
Some bacteria are classified as acidophiles because they grow best in low pH conditions.
The hot springs are inhabited by various acidophiles adapted to extreme environments.

  • synonym: acidophilic - having an affinity for acidic environments
