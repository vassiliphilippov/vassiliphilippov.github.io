Synonyms

  • exorbitant
    7.9 1/m
    exorbitant is much larger than normal for prices and sounds formal, while steep means unreasonably high in everyday speech.
  • extortionate
    7.9 0.1/m
    extortionate is used for unfairly high demands often in complaint language, while steep describes prices that are simply unreasonably high.
  • outrageous
    4.2 5/m
    outrageous feels shocking or scandalous about a price or act, while steep focuses on being unreasonably high.
  • high-priced
    3.5 1/m
    high-priced describes items that cost a lot without judgement, while steep implies the price is unreasonably high.
