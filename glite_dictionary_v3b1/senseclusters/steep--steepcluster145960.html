steep

adjective
unreasonably high (esp. prices or costs)
6.2 3.5
Pronunciation: /stiːp/ (AmE), /stiːp/ (BrE)
(of prices, charges, or demands) unreasonably high; excessive.

Examples

Steep prices
Even though I loved the shoes, I couldn't afford the steep cost.
Many people think concert ticket prices have become too steep in recent years.
The restaurant had to close because their steep prices drove away most customers.
