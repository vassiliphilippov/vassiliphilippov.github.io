steep

adjective
unreasonably high (esp. prices or costs)
4.2 2.62/m
Pronunciation: / stip / (AmE), / stiÀêp / (BrE)
(of prices, charges, or demands) unreasonably high; excessive.

Examples

Steep prices
Even though I loved the shoes, I couldn't afford the steep cost.
Many people think concert ticket prices have become too steep in recent years.
The restaurant had to close because their steep prices drove away most customers.

Synonyms

  • exorbitant
    7.9 1.99/m
    exorbitant is much larger than normal for prices and sounds formal, while steep means unreasonably high in everyday speech.
  • extortionate
    7.9 0.14/m
    extortionate is used for unfairly high demands often in complaint language, while steep describes prices that are simply unreasonably high.
  • outrageous
    4.2 4.46/m
    outrageous feels shocking or scandalous about a price or act, while steep focuses on being unreasonably high.
  • high-priced
    3.5 1.79/m
    high-priced describes items that cost a lot without judgement, while steep implies the price is unreasonably high.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exorbitant - (of a price or demand) much too high
  • antonym: reasonable - (of prices or demands) fair, not excessive
