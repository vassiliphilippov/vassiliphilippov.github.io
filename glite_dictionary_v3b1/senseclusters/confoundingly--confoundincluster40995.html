confoundingly

adverb
in a confusing way
8.4
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈfaʊn.dɪŋ.liː/ (AmE), /kən.ˈfaʊn.dɪŋ.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that is confusing or very difficult to understand

Examples

A confoundingly difficult problem

Her explanation was confoundingly vague, leaving everyone more confused than before.
The rules of the game are confoundingly complex for beginners.
The instructions were confoundingly complex, leaving many people unsure how to proceed.
She solved the puzzle confoundingly fast, surprising everyone in the room.
The results of the experiment were confoundingly different from what the scientists expected.
