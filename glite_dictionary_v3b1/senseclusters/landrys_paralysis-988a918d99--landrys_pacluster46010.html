Landry's paralysis

noun
medical: acute limb paralysis disorder
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈlæn.driːz pə.ˈræl.ə.sɪs/ /; / /ˈlæn.driːz pə.ˈræl.ɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /ˈlæn.driːz pə.ˈræl.ɪ.sɪs/ /; / /ˈlæn.driːz pə.ˈræl.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
A rare disorder involving inflammation of peripheral nerves, leading to pain, muscle weakness, and sometimes paralysis of the limbs; the cause is unknown.

Examples

Landry's paralysis disorder
Landry's paralysis is now considered an early classification of what is known as Guillain-Barré syndrome.
Doctors observed progressive limb weakness in patients diagnosed with Landry's paralysis.
The history of Landry's paralysis traces back to the 19th century and its discovery in France.

Related Terms
