common burdock

noun
a widespread burred plant of Europe and North Africa
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈbɜrdɑk / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈbɜːdɒk / (BrE)
a large, coarse biennial plant (Arctium minus) native to Europe and North Africa, known for its broad leaves and burred seed heads, commonly found in varied soils except very acidic ones.

Examples

The common burdock plant
Gardeners often remove common burdock because its burs can be a nuisance.
Herbalists have long used roots from the common burdock for traditional remedies.
The seeds of the common burdock easily cling to animal fur and clothing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burdock - the general term for plants in the Arctium genus
