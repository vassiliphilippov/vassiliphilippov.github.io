relitigate

verb
to argue again
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈlɪtɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈlɪtɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to argue about or try to settle an issue again that has already been decided, especially in discussion or debate

Examples

To relitigate an argument
The committee refused to relitigate the decision that had already been made last year.
She did not want to relitigate old arguments every time they met.
Some members tried to relitigate the issue, but most agreed it was settled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reopen - to start discussing or considering something again
  • antonym: settle - to resolve or bring to an end an argument or issue
