treat

verb
behave towards someone/something
1.0 68/m
Pronunciation: / trit / (AmE), / triÀêt / (BrE)
to behave towards or deal with someone or something in a particular way

Examples

To treat people kindly
He wasn't sure how to treat the new information when he heard it.
It's important to treat everyone with respect, regardless of their background.
Please treat this matter with sensitivity and care.
He treated the criticism as constructive feedback and worked on improving his skills.
She treated his comment as a joke, laughing it off immediately.
The law treats all citizens equally, regardless of background.
