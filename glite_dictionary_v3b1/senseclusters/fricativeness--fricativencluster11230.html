fricativeness

noun
quality of producing friction in speech sounds
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪk.ə.tɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɪk.ə.tɪvnəs / (BrE)
(phonetics) The quality of a speech sound being produced by forcing air through a narrow constriction, creating friction and often a hissing or buzzing sound, as in English /f/ and /s/.

Examples

The fricativeness of sounds
In some dialects, the fricativeness of 'th' is particularly strong.
Linguists study the fricativeness of certain speech sounds to understand language patterns.
The fricativeness of the consonant can be heard in the hissing sound it produces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fricative - a consonant produced by forcing air through a narrow opening
