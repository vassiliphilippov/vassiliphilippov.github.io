Socinian

noun
adherent of Socinus's teachings; anti-Trinitarian Christian
Pronunciation: / soʊˈsɪniən / (AmE), / səˈsɪnɪən / (BrE)
A follower of the teachings of Socinus, especially one who rejects the divinity of Christ, the Trinity, and original sin; historically influential in the development of Unitarian theology.

Examples

Socinian beliefs
As a committed Socinian, he firmly denied the doctrine of the Trinity.
Many of the early controversies in Protestant Europe involved debates with Socinian thinkers.
The Socinians played a significant role in shaping modern Unitarian beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Unitarian - a member of a Christian movement rejecting the Trinity; have some shared theology
