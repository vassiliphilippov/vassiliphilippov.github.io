revaccinate

verb
to vaccinate again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈvæksɪneɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈvæksɪneɪt / (BrE)
to give a vaccine to someone again after a previous vaccination, usually to maintain or boost immunity

Examples

To revaccinate children
Doctors may revaccinate children if their immunity to measles has decreased.
It is important to revaccinate pets regularly to keep them protected from certain diseases.
Some countries require travelers to revaccinate against yellow fever before entering.
Doctors may revaccinate patients if their immunity has decreased.
It is important to revaccinate children according to the recommended schedule.
Some travelers need to be revaccinated before visiting certain countries.
