wiggly

adjective
twisting or squirming in motion
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪɡli / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡli / (BrE)
moving or shaped with many bends, curves, or twists; twisting or squirming like a worm or snake

Examples

A wiggly fish
After being caught, the wiggly fish slipped out of his hands.
The little boy drew a wiggly line across the page.
The path through the forest was narrow and wiggly, making the hike more challenging.
She followed the wiggly path through the forest.
The children loved drawing wiggly lines on their paper.
The worm left a wiggly trail in the dirt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: curvy - having many curves
  • synonym: sinuous - having many curves and turns; lithe and supple
  • antonym: straight - extending or moving in one direction without curves or bends
