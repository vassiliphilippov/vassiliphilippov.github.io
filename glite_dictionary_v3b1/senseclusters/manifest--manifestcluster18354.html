manifest

verb
show or reveal clearly
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪˌfɛst /; / ˈmæn.ɪ.fɛst / (AmE), / ˈmanɪfɛst /; / ˈman.ɪ.fɛst / (BrE)
to show, display, or become visible or evident (a quality, feeling, or fact)

Examples

To manifest feelings
Compassion manifests as genuine concern for others in daily interactions.
His anxiety began to manifest itself in frequent headaches and insomnia.
The artist's vision manifested in vibrant colors and bold shapes throughout the gallery.
Frustration can manifest as irritability or impatience in some people.
His symptoms did not manifest until several days after the infection.
Strange phenomena would sometimes manifest during the full moon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appear - become visible, come into sight or existence
  • antonym: conceal - to hide or keep something secret
  • synonym: demonstrate - to show evidence for or prove something clearly
  • antonym: disappear - cease to be visible or present
  • synonym: display - to show or exhibit something clearly
