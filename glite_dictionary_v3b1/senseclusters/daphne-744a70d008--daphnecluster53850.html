Daphne

proper noun
mythological nymph; turned into a laurel tree
Pronunciation: / ˈdæfni / (AmE), / ˈdæfni / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a nymph who was transformed into a laurel tree to escape the pursuit of Apollo.

Examples

Myth of Daphne
According to the legend, Daphne was transformed into a laurel tree to escape Apollo's advances.
Many poets have used Daphne's tale as a symbol of chastity and pursuit in their writings.
The story of Daphne has inspired countless works of art, especially in sculpture and painting.
