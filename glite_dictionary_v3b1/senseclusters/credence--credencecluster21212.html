credence

noun
belief in the truth of something
6.6 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkriː.dəns/ (AmE), /ˈkriː.dəns/ (BrE)
belief or acceptance that something is true or valid; the state of being regarded as credible

Examples

Give credence to claims
She gave little credence to the rumors about her colleague.
The theory gained credence after several experiments confirmed its predictions.
Within the community, his words held great credence due to his reputation for honesty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: belief - acceptance that something exists or is true
  • antonym: disbelief - inability or refusal to accept that something is true
