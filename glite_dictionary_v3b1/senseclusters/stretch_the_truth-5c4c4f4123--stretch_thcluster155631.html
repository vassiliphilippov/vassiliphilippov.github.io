stretch the truth

verb
to exaggerate or misrepresent facts
Pronunciation: / strɛtʃ ðə truθ / (AmE), / strɛtʃ ðə truːθ / (BrE)
to exaggerate or distort the facts, often in order to make something seem more impressive or plausible than it really is

Examples

To stretch the truth in stories
He admitted to stretching the truth about his experience during the interview.
Sometimes politicians stretch the truth to make their achievements sound more impressive.
Try not to stretch the truth when telling your side of the story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to represent as greater than is actually the case
  • degree higher: lie - to say something untrue with intent to deceive
