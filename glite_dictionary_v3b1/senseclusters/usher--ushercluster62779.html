usher

noun
court or legislative official; attendant
Pronunciation: / ˈʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈʌʃə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to show people to their seats and help maintain order at events such as in a theater, church, courtroom, or public gathering

Examples

Theater usher
A uniformed usher stood by the entrance of the legislative chamber.
Before the session began, the usher ensured that all visitors were seated properly.
The usher guided the witnesses to their seats in the courtroom.
At the wedding, a usher greeted each guest and handed out programs.
She worked as an usher at the local cinema during the summer holidays.
The usher guided us to our seats before the performance began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attendant - a person who assists or serves others in a place
  • synonym: guide - a person who leads or directs other people on a journey
