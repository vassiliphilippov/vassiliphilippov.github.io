game

verb
exploit or manipulate a system
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ɡeɪm / (BrE)
to try to gain an advantage by deliberately exploiting or manipulating the rules, procedures, or weaknesses of a system.

Examples

To game the system
They tried to game the tax code to minimize their liabilities before filing.
The company gamed the bidding process and won unfairly, according to investigators.
Some regulators are trying to stop politicians from gaming the election rules to gain advantage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exploit - to use something or someone selfishly or unfairly for one's advantage
  • synonym: manipulate - to control or influence cleverly, unfairly, or unscrupulously
  • antonym: play fair - to act honestly and follow the rules rather than seeking unfair advantage
