journal

noun
book for recording financial transactions
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɝː.nəl /; / c8d925dd0n59l / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.nəl /; / c8d925cd0n59l / (BrE)
a book or record where financial transactions or events are written in order, or a log of events (including in computers)

Examples

Accounting journal record
Careful maintenance of a journal is essential for accurate financial statements.
Each sale was recorded in the journal on the day it occurred.
The accountant entered the expenses in the journal before updating the ledger.
After the crash, engineers examined the journal to reconstruct the sequence of system events.
The filesystem's journal ensured all recent writes were replayed correctly during recovery after power loss.
For compliance, the company kept an immutable journal of access attempts and configuration changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: audit trail - a chronological record of system activity kept to support auditing and accountability
  • synonym: ledger - a principal accounting book for final entry of transactions
  • synonym: log - a record of events or messages produced by a computer system or application
