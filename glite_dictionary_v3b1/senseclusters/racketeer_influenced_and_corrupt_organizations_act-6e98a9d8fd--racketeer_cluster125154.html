Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act

noun
U.S. law targeting organized crime (RICO Act)
Pronunciation: / ˌræ.kəˈtɪr ɪnˈfluːənst ənd kəˈrʌpt ˌɔːr.ɡən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃənz ækt / (AmE), / ˌrak.ɪˈtɪə ɪnˈfluːənst ənd kəˈrʌpt ˌɔː.ɡən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃənz ækt / (BrE)
a U.S. federal law enacted in 1970 to combat organized crime by providing extended penalties and forfeiture for acts performed as part of a criminal organization
