convene

verb
to assemble formally
5.7 2.50/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàvin / (AmE), / k…ônÀàviÀên / (BrE)
to come together or bring people together for a formal meeting or gathering

Examples

To convene a meeting
A special committee was convened to investigate the incident.
Experts from around the world will convene for the international climate summit next month.
The board will convene at 9 a.m. to discuss the budget proposal.
An emergency meeting was convened to address the crisis.
Every year, experts from around the world convene for this international conference.
The committee will convene at 10 a.m. to discuss the proposal.

Synonyms

  • convoke
    8.1 0.11/m
    convoke formally calls people to a meeting, while convene means bringing people together for a formal meeting.
  • assemble
    3.3 11/m
    assemble brings people together in one place for a purpose, while convene refers specifically to calling formal meetings.
  • gather
    1.0 65/m
    gather is used for collecting people, things, or information informally, while convene focuses on formal meetings and official gatherings.
  • meet
    1.0 18/m
    meet is a common, neutral word for coming together for many reasons, while convene implies calling a formal meeting.
  • come together
    2.7 13/m
    come together often means people unite or cooperate casually, while convene means people are formally called to meet.
  • bring everyone together
    3.4 0.15/m
    bring everyone together aims to unite people emotionally or socially, while convene focuses on formally calling people to a meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adjourn - to suspend a meeting to a later time or indefinitely
  • synonym: assemble - to come or bring together as a group
