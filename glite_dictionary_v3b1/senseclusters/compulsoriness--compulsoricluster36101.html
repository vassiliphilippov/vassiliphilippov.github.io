compulsoriness

noun
state of being required
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpʌlsərinəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpʌlsərinəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being required, obligatory, or enforced by rule or law

Examples

Compulsoriness of rules
The compulsoriness of school attendance ensures that all children receive an education.
Some employees questioned the compulsoriness of the new safety training program.
Debates continue about the compulsoriness of wearing uniforms in public schools.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obligation - something that must be done because of a law or rule
  • antonym: voluntariness - the quality of being done by choice, not by force
