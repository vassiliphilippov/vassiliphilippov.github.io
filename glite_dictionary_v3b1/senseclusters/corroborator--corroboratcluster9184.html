corroborator

noun
person who confirms
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɑbəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / kəˈrɒbəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that confirms or supports a statement, theory, or finding with evidence

Examples

A corroborator of evidence
The detective relied on a corroborator to strengthen the case against the suspect.
Without a corroborator, the story would have been difficult to believe.
In court, a corroborator provided evidence that matched the victim's account.

Related Terms

  • antonym: denier - a person who refuses to accept or confirm something
  • synonym: witness - a person who sees an event and can provide evidence
