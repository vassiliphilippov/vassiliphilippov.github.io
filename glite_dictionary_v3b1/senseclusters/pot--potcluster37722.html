pot

noun
deep round cooking vessel
Pronunciation: / pɑt / (AmE), / pɒt / (BrE)
a round container, usually made of metal, clay, or plastic, used for cooking, holding plants, or as a toilet in the past

Examples

Cooking pot
He bought a new stainless steel pot for making pasta.
She lifted the heavy pot from the stove and set it on the table.
The soup simmered gently in the pot all afternoon.
Remember to water the pot regularly to help the plant grow.
She placed her new fern in a ceramic pot on the windowsill.
The balcony was decorated with colorful pots filled with flowers.
Before the advent of indoor plumbing, most households had a pot in each bedroom.
In the middle of the night, she reached under her bed for the pot.
The antique pot was decorated with faded blue flowers.
