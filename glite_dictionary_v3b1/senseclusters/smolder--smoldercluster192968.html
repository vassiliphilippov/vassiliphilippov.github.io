smolder

verb
show suppressed strong feelings
8.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsmoʊl.dər/ /; / /ˈsməʊl.dər/ (AmE), /ˈsməʊl.də/ (BrE)
to show or feel a strong emotion, especially anger or resentment, that is not expressed openly but is hardly contained

Examples

Anger smolders inside
During the meeting, anger began to smolder in his eyes, though he said nothing.
Resentment smoldered within her after the unfair treatment she had received.
She tried to hide her irritation, but it continued to smolder beneath the surface.
Even after the argument ended, her frustration kept smouldering inside her.
His eyes smouldered with resentment as he listened to their criticism.
She tried to hide her anger, but it continued to smoulder beneath the surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explode - to express emotion, especially anger, suddenly and openly
  • synonym: fume - to feel or express anger in a restrained or suppressed manner
  • synonym: seethe - to be filled with intense but unexpressed emotion
  • synonym: suppress - to restrain or hold back emotion or expression
