Moche

proper noun
ancient Peruvian culture
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊtʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈməʊtʃeɪ / (BrE)
an ancient civilization of northern coastal Peru (c. 100–800 CE), its people, or their archaeological remains and artifacts

Examples

The Moche civilization
The Moche built impressive adobe pyramids along the northern coast of Peru.
Many Moche artifacts, such as pottery and jewelry, have been discovered by archaeologists.
Researchers study Moche tombs to learn about the society's customs and beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Inca - a later major civilization of the Andes in Peru
  • synonym: Nazca - an ancient civilization of southern Peru known for the Nazca Lines
