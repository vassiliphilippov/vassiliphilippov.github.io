mark up

verb
increase the price of something
3.5 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / m…ërk  åp / (AmE), / m…ëÀêk  åp / (BrE)
to increase the price of something, especially so it can be sold at a profit

Examples

To mark up prices
Some retailers mark up products by as much as 50%.
The store decided to mark up all electronics before the holiday shopping season.
They often mark up imported goods to cover shipping and customs fees.

Synonyms

  • hike up the prices
    4.8 0.01/m
    hike up the prices means raising prices quickly and a lot, while mark up means adding to a price to make profit.
  • inflate
    4.4 2.52/m
    inflate means increasing prices or value generally, often for broad economic reasons, while mark up means adding to price for profit.

  • antonym: discount - reduce the price of something
