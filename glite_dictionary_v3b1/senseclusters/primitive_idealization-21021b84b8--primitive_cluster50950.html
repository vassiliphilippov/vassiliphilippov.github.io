primitive idealization

noun
archaic, unrealistic form of idealization
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪm.ə.t̬ɪv aɪˌdi.ə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪv aɪˌdɪə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an unrealistic and archaic form of idealization in which another person is perceived as entirely perfect or flawless, often used as a psychological defense mechanism

Examples

Primitive idealization in psychology
Borderline personality disorders often involve defenses such as splitting and primitive idealization.
In therapy, she began to recognize her tendency toward primitive idealization of authority figures.
The patient's primitive idealization made it difficult for him to acknowledge any flaws in his parents.
