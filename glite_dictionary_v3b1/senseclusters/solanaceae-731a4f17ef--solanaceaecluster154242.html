Solanaceae

noun
family of flowering plants including potato and tomato
Pronunciation: / soʊˌleɪnəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / səˌleɪnəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a large and economically important family of flowering plants, including many herbs, shrubs, and trees, some of which are strongly scented, narcotic, or poisonous; includes potatoes, tomatoes, peppers, tobacco, and petunias

Examples

The Solanaceae family
Caution is needed when handling some Solanaceae species, as they can be toxic if ingested.
Many important food crops, such as tomatoes and potatoes, belong to the Solanaceae family.
Tobacco and petunia are both well-known members of the Solanaceae.
