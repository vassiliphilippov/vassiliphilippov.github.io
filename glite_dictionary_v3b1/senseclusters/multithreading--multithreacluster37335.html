multithreading

noun
multiple storylines
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈθrɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈθrɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
a narrative technique in which several separate but related storylines are presented and developed at the same time

Examples

Multithreading in stories
The novel's use of multithreading allows readers to follow several characters' journeys at once.
Directors often employ multithreading to build suspense by switching between different plotlines.
In modern television, multithreading keeps audiences engaged by weaving together multiple stories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: linear narrative - a storytelling method that follows a single storyline in chronological order
  • synonym: parallel narrative - a storytelling method with two or more storylines running at the same time
