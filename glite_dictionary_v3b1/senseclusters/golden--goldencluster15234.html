golden

adjective
supremely fortunate or favored
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldən /; / ˈɡoʊl.dən / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən /; / ˈɡəʊl.dən / (BrE)
very good, valuable, fortunate, or special; excellent or promising (figurative use)

Examples

Golden opportunity
During her golden years in the company, every project she touched turned into a triumph.
He was considered the golden child of the family, destined for success.
The athlete enjoyed a golden period in his career, winning every major tournament.
Moving to the city was a golden chance he could not pass up.
She knew it was a golden opportunity to present her ideas to the director.
The team is in a golden position to win the championship this year.
Historians often describe the Renaissance as a golden age for art and learning.
Many people look back on the 1950s as a golden era of economic growth and optimism.
The company enjoyed a golden period of innovation during the late 1990s.
