circle around

verb
move in a circular path around
2.3 1.92/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ùÀêk…ôl …ôÀàra änd / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀêk…ôl …ôÀàra änd / (BrE)
to move in a circular course around something or someone

Examples

To circle around a building
Security guards circled around the building all night to keep it safe.
The birds began to circle around the fishing boat, hoping for scraps.
The children circled around the bonfire, singing and laughing.

Synonyms

  • circle round
    3.5 0.02/m
    circle round is a common British form using 'round' to mean circular motion, while circle around names the same movement.
  • circle
    1.8 14/m
    circle often means move all the way around or fly above something, while circle around stresses moving around someone directly.
  • revolve around
    3.6 0.60/m
    revolve around suggests orbiting a central point and is often used for planets or ideas, while circle around describes simple circular movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orbit - move in an orbit around something, especially a planet or star
  • synonym: revolve around - move in a circular orbit or path around
