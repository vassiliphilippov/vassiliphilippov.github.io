clearing house

noun
financial center for interbank exchanges
6.5 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈklɪr.ɪŋ haʊs/ /; / /ˈklɪr.ɪŋ haʊz/ (AmE), /ˈklɪə.rɪŋ haʊs/ /; / /ˈklɪə.rɪŋ haʊz/ (BrE)
a central institution where banks or other organizations exchange financial instruments such as checks, and settle accounts with each other

Examples

Bank clearing house
Each participating bank holds funds in the clearing house to cover its daily transactions.
Many countries have established a national clearing house for interbank settlements.
The central clearing house ensures that payments between banks are processed efficiently every day.
