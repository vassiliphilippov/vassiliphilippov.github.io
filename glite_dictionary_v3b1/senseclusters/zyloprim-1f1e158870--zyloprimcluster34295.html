Zyloprim

noun
brand name drug for gout (allopurinol)
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈzaɪ.ləˌprɪm / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ləˌprɪm / (BrE)
A trademarked brand name for allopurinol, a drug used to lower uric acid levels in the blood, typically prescribed to treat gout and related conditions.

Examples

Zyloprim medicine
If you are allergic to Zyloprim, inform your healthcare provider before starting treatment.
My doctor prescribed Zyloprim to help control my uric acid levels.
Patients with chronic gout may benefit from taking Zyloprim regularly to prevent painful flare-ups.
