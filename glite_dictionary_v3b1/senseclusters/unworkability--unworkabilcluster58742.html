unworkability

noun
impracticality
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˌwɜrkəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌwɜːkəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible or extremely difficult to make work or succeed

Examples

Unworkability of a plan
The unworkability of the new policy was obvious after several failed attempts to implement it.
Critics pointed out the unworkability of the proposed solution before it was even tested.
After reviewing the plan, the team discussed its unworkability and suggested alternative approaches.

Related Terms

  • antonym: feasibility - the quality of being possible or workable
  • synonym: impracticality - the quality of not being practical or workable
