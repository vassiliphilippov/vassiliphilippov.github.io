expel

verb
force to leave (officially)
2.9 4.1
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈspel/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈspel/ (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place, organization, or country, often officially or against their will

Examples

To expel a student
After being found guilty, he was expelled from the country.
The student was expelled for violating school rules.
They threatened to expel anyone who broke the agreement.
The invading army tried to expel the enemy forces from the region.
The new policies were meant to expel unwanted wildlife from the urban park.
They hoped loud noises would expel the birds from the warehouse.
After an internal investigation, he was expelled from the board for misconduct.
Several members were expelled from the organization due to unethical behavior.
The school decided to expel the principal after the scandal broke.
