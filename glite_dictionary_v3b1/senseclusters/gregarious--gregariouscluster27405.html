gregarious

adjective
tending to form groups (of animals)
6.1 5.6
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈɡɛriəs / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
(of animals) tending to form groups with others of the same species; living or moving in flocks or herds.

Examples

Gregarious animals gather
Flamingos are gregarious birds that gather in large colonies on salt flats.
Some primates are gregarious by nature, spending most of their lives in tight-knit groups.
Unlike tigers, wolves are highly gregarious and depend on pack cooperation to hunt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: social - seeking or enjoying the company of others; sociable
  • antonym: solitary - living or spending a lot of time alone; not gregarious
