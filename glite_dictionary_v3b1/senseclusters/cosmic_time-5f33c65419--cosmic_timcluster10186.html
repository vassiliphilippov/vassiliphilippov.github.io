cosmic time

noun
time since the universe's beginning
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑz.mɪk taɪm / (AmE), / ˈkɒz.mɪk taɪm / (BrE)
time as measured from the origin of the universe, used to describe the history and development of the cosmos.

Examples

Cosmic time scale
According to modern cosmology, cosmic time began with the Big Bang.
Changes in the universe's structure are often charted over billions of years of cosmic time.
Physicists measure the age of the universe using cosmic time as a reference scale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: universal time - time reckoned from the Big Bang to the present (scientific term)
