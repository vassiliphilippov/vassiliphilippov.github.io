reaction propulsion

noun
propulsion by expelling mass (as in rockets or jets)
Pronunciation: / riˈækʃən prəˈpʌlʃən / (AmE), / riˈakʃən prəˈpʌlʃən / (BrE)
propulsion generated by expelling mass at high speed in one direction, causing the vehicle to move in the opposite direction by the principle of equal and opposite momentum.

Examples

Reaction propulsion system
Most modern rockets use reaction propulsion to launch spacecraft into orbit.
Research in reaction propulsion has led to advances in both aviation and space travel.
The principle of reaction propulsion is explained by Newton's third law of motion.
