CO

noun
conscientious objector (military service)
8.2
Pronunciation: / siː oʊ / (AmE), / siː əʊ / (BrE)
a person who refuses to serve in the armed forces or bear arms on grounds of conscience or moral principle; conscientious objector

Examples

CO status
During the war, he registered as a CO because of his religious beliefs.
Many COs were assigned to alternative non-combat roles during the conflict.
The government established a process to review the claims of COs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conscientious objector - a person who refuses military service on grounds of conscience
