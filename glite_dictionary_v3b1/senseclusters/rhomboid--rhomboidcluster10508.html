rhomboid

noun
intramembrane serine protease
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈrɑːm.bɔɪd/ (AmE), /ˈrɒm.bɔɪd/ (BrE)
(in molecular biology/biochemistry) any of a family of intramembrane serine proteases that cleave membrane proteins and regulate signaling or protein maturation.

Examples

Rhomboid protease
Researchers identified a rhomboid protease that regulates signaling by cleaving membrane-bound substrates.
The study showed how a bacterial rhomboid enzyme modifies cell-surface proteins during host infection.
Pharmacologists are exploring inhibitors of the rhomboid protease to control disease-related signaling pathways.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intramembrane serine protease - an enzyme that cleaves substrate proteins within or near cellular membranes using a serine-based catalytic mechanism
  • synonym: rhomboid protease - a protease of the rhomboid family; an intramembrane serine protease
