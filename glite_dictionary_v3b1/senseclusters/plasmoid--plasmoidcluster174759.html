plasmoid

noun
plasma structure
Pronunciation: / ˈplæzmɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈplæzmɔɪd / (BrE)
a coherent, self-contained structure of plasma and magnetic fields, often found in space or laboratory experiments

Examples

Laboratory plasmoid
During the experiment, a plasmoid was created and studied under controlled conditions.
Astronomers detected a massive plasmoid ejected from the sun during a solar flare.
The formation of a plasmoid can influence the behavior of magnetic fields in space.
During the experiment, a plasmoid was created in the laboratory chamber.
Astronomers detected a massive plasmoid ejected from the sun during the solar storm.
The spacecraft's instruments recorded the passage of a plasmoid through Earth's magnetosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magnetosphere - the region around a planet dominated by its magnetic field
  • synonym: plasma - an ionized gas consisting of free electrons and ions
