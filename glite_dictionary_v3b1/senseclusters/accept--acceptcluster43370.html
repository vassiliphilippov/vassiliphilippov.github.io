accept

verb
make use of something offered
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛpt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛpt / (BrE)
to receive or agree to take something that is offered, such as a gift, offer, invitation, or membership

Examples

To accept a gift
After much thought, they chose to accept the risk involved in the venture.
He always tries to accept opportunities to learn new skills.
She decided to accept the challenge and lead the new project.
He accepted the gift with a big smile.
I am pleased to accept your invitation to the ceremony.
She graciously accepted the award on behalf of her team.
After months of waiting, she was finally accepted into the medical school.
Many international students hope to be accepted by prestigious universities.
The club will only accept new members after a thorough review process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: admit - to allow someone to enter a place or join a group
  • synonym: approve - to agree with or accept something formally
  • antonym: decline - to politely refuse an offer or invitation
  • synonym: receive - to be given or presented with something
  • antonym: refuse - to not accept or agree to something offered
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or consider someone for a group or position
  • synonym: take - to acquire or use something for a purpose
