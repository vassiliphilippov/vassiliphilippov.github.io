get in

verb
(of trains, etc.) arrive at a station
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɪn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn / (BrE)
(of trains, buses, or other vehicles) to arrive or reach a station or destination.

Examples

The train gets in late
The train is due to get in at 8 a.m. sharp.
We waited on the platform for the express to get in.
What time does your bus get in to the station?

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrive - to reach a place at the end of a journey or trip
  • antonym: depart - to leave a place, especially at the start of a journey
