flight feather

noun
large wing or tail feather of a bird
3.6
Pronunciation: / flaɪt ˈfɛðər / (AmE), / flaɪt ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
any of the large, stiff feathers on a bird's wing or tail that are essential for flight

Examples

Long flight feathers
A peacock's vibrant tail includes many long flight feathers.
During molting, birds may lose several flight feathers at once.
The broken flight feather prevented the hawk from soaring gracefully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contour feather - the outermost feather that gives shape to a bird's body and wings
