open up

verb
make something become open
3.1 2.1
Pronunciation: /ˈəʊ.pən ʌp/ /; / /ˈoʊ.pən ʌp/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.pən ʌp/ (BrE)
to make something open or become open, especially by removing a barrier or after being closed

Examples

To open up a door
She used her key to open up the front door.
Spring rain can open up flower buds overnight.
The doctor will open up the chest cavity during surgery.
After years of construction, the new tunnel opened up to the public last week.
As soon as the store opened up, customers rushed inside looking for bargains.
The curtain opened up to reveal a beautiful stage set.

Related Terms

  • antonym: close up - to make or become closed
  • synonym: open - to move (something) so as to leave a space allowing access or view
  • synonym: unseal - to remove a seal so as to open
