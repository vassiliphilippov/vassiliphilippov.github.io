arbitrage

noun
simultaneous buying and selling for profit
9.0 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrbɪˌtrɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtrɑːʒ / (BrE)
the simultaneous purchase and sale of the same asset in different markets to profit from tiny differences in the asset's price

Examples

Currency arbitrage
Currency arbitrage is popular among investors who track fluctuations in exchange rates.
He specialized in arbitrage and frequently made quick, low-risk trades across global markets.
The company profited from arbitrage by exploiting price differences in stocks traded on two exchanges.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: gambling - staking money on uncertain outcomes, typically riskier than arbitrage
  • degree lower: risk-free - involving no risk of financial loss
  • synonym: speculation - investment involving risk with hopes of significant profit
