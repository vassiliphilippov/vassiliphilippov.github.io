pressure

verb
attempt to persuade or force by influence
6.0 3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
to try to persuade or force someone to do something

Examples

To pressure a friend
She felt pressured by her boss to work overtime every weekend.
The company is pressuring employees to meet unrealistic targets this quarter.
They tried to pressure him into signing the agreement, but he refused.
He felt constantly pressured to meet unrealistic sales targets.
Her boss tried to pressure her into working overtime by threatening to reduce her hours.
The company was pressured by activists to improve its environmental policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coerce - to force someone to do something by use of pressure, threats, or intimidation
  • synonym: persuade - to induce someone to do something through reasoning or argument
