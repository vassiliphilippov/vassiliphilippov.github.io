arbuscular

adjective
tree-like form
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈbʌskjələr / (AmE), / ɑːˈbʌskjʊlə / (BrE)
having a branching, tree-like form or relating to fungi that form tree-like structures (arbuscules) inside plant roots

Examples

The arbuscular fungi play a crucial role in helping plants absorb nutrients from the soil.
Researchers study arbuscular associations to improve crop growth and soil health.
Many agricultural systems benefit from the presence of arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi in the soil.
The scientist studied the arbuscular structures formed by fungi in the plant roots.
Arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi play a key role in nutrient exchange between soil and arbuscular root cells.
Under the microscope, the arbuscular network appeared as tiny, branched trees inside the root cells.
