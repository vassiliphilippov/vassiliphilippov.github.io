European brown bat

noun
a small, common European bat species
Pronunciation: / jʊˌroʊpiən ˈbraʊn ˌbæt / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊpiən ˈbraʊn ˌbat / (BrE)
a common species of brown bat native to Europe, known for its small size and wide distribution across the continent

Examples

The European brown bat
Population studies have shown that European brown bats adapt well to urban environments.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of the European brown bat across multiple countries.
The European brown bat is often seen hunting insects at dusk in rural areas.
