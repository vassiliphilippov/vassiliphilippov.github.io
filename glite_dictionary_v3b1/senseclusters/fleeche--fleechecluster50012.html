fleeche

verb
to cheat by trickery
9.9
to cheat or swindle someone, especially by deception or trickery (archaic)

Examples

To fleeche a customer
Unscrupulous traders would fleeche customers by selling fake goods in the old marketplace.
Travelers were warned not to let anyone fleeche them out of their valuables.
In ancient tales, villains often fleeched the poor with clever tricks and lies.

  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and fair
