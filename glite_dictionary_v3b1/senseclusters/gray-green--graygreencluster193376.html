gray-green

adjective
green tinged with gray
3.3
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ ˈɡrin /; / ɡreɪˈɡrin / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈɡriːn /; / ɡreɪˈɡriːn / (BrE)
Of a green color tinged with gray; having a subdued shade of green mixed with gray.

Examples

A gray-green color
She chose a gray-green skirt that matched the autumn scenery perfectly.
The gray-green leaves of the sage plant added texture to the garden.
The walls were painted a subtle gray-green that brought a calm feeling to the room.
As the storm approached, the sky turned a strange grey-green color.
She wore a beautiful scarf in soft grey-green tones that matched her eyes.
The old statue had a grey-green patina from years of exposure to the weather.
