contractibility

noun
ability to shrink space
Pronunciation: / kənˌtræktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kənˌtræktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the property of a topological space that can be continuously shrunk to a single point without breaking or tearing

Examples

Topological contractibility
The contractibility of the space was essential for proving the theorem in algebraic topology.
Mathematicians often study the contractibility of complex shapes to understand their fundamental properties.
If a space lacks contractibility, it may have holes or other interesting features.

Related Terms

  • antonym: noncontractible - not able to be continuously shrunk to a point in topology
