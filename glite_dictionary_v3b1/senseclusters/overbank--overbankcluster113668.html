overbank

verb
to tilt too far
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbæŋk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbæŋk / (BrE)
to tilt an aircraft or vehicle too far when turning, beyond the safe or intended angle

Examples

To overbank a plane
The pilot had to be careful not to overbank during the sharp turn.
If you overbank the car on a curve, you could lose control.
New pilots are trained to avoid overbanking when making tight maneuvers.
