Munchausen's syndrome

noun
psychiatric disorder involving feigned illness
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌnˌtʃaʊzənz ˌsɪnˈdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈmʌnˌtʃaʊzənz ˌsɪnˈdrəʊm / (BrE)
a psychiatric disorder in which a person repeatedly feigns or induces illness or injury in themselves in order to receive medical attention, without obvious external incentives

Examples

Munchausen's syndrome patient
Doctors struggled to diagnose her rare ailments before realizing she had Munchausen's syndrome.
Medical professionals are trained to recognize the signs of Munchausen's syndrome.
The patient was eventually referred to a psychiatrist for suspected Munchausen's syndrome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: factitious disorder - a broader psychiatric category for intentionally produced or feigned physical or psychological symptoms
