shale

noun
fine-grained sedimentary rock
7.9
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪl / (AmE), / ʃeɪl / (BrE)
a fine-grained sedimentary rock formed from compressed layers of clay, splitting easily into thin sheets

Examples

Layers of shale
Natural gas is often extracted from shale formations deep underground.
The geologist discovered a thick layer of shale beneath the surface.
The museum displayed samples of ancient shale containing fossilized plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slate - a fine-grained, foliated metamorphic rock derived from shale
