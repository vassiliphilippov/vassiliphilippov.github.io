squib

verb
write a short commentary or article
Pronunciation: / skwɪb / (AmE), / skwɪb / (BrE)
to write a short, often witty or critical, article or commentary, especially in a newspaper or magazine

Examples

To squib a column
Every week, she squibs clever pieces for the university magazine.
He enjoys squibbing brief reviews of new restaurants for the food section.
The columnist squibbed a satirical article about the mayor's recent proposal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comment - to express an opinion or statement about something
  • synonym: review - to write a critique or evaluation of a work
