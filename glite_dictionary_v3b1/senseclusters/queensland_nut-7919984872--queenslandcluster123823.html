Queensland nut

noun
Australian tree producing edible nuts
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkwiːnzlænd nʌt / (AmE), / ˈkwiːnzlənd nʌt / (BrE)
A bushy Australian tree (Macadamia integrifolia) with pink to purple flowers, cultivated for its edible nuts.

Examples

The Queensland nut tree
Farmers in Australia export large amounts of the Queensland nut worldwide.
The Queensland nut is prized not only for its nuts but also for its attractive pink flowers.
The Queensland nut thrives in warm, subtropical climates.
