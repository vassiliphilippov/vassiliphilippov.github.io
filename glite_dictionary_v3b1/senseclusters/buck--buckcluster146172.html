buck

verb
move quickly and violently
Pronunciation: / bʌk / (AmE), / bʌk / (BrE)
to move in a sudden, quick, and violent way, often as if trying to throw someone or something off

Examples

The boat bucks hard
Suddenly, the boat bucked violently as it hit the large wave.
The old car bucked down the rough road, jolting everyone inside.
The wild horse began to buck and throw off the rider.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jerk - to move or make something move with a sudden short, sharp movement
  • synonym: lurch - to make a sudden, unsteady movement forward or to one side
