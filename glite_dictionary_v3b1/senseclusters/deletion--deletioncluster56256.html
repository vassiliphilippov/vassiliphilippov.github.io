deletion

noun
removal of written or printed content

the act of removing or omitting something from written or printed text, especially during editing

Examples

Deletion of words
After the deletion of the offensive word, the letter was approved.
Please indicate any deletion of information in the revised document.
The editor made a deletion from the final draft of the manuscript.
His document contained so many deletions that the original meaning was lost.
Some deletions were necessary to shorten the introduction for publication.

Synonyms

  • excision
    7.9 0.04/m
    excision is a formal, surgical removal of part of a text made by editors, while deletion names the general act of omitting material.
  • erasure
    5.9 0.44/m
    erasure often means rubbing out or hiding a record physically, while deletion is the neutral act of removing text in editing.
  • cut
    4.0 68/m
    cut is an informal edit or reduction used in media or budgets, while deletion is the specific act of omitting text.
  • elision
    8.9 0.13/m
    elision is the deliberate omission often in speech or poetic lines to ease flow, while deletion is removing text during editing.
