Galton

proper noun
English scientist; founder of eugenics; pioneer in statistics
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæl.tən / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.tən / (BrE)
Francis Galton (1822–1911), English scientist known for pioneering work in heredity, statistics, anthropology, and for founding eugenics; he introduced the use of fingerprints for identification.

Examples

Galton fingerprint method
Galton was instrumental in developing the first techniques for fingerprint identification.
Historians often debate the ethical implications of Galton's research on heredity and eugenics.
Many modern statistical methods can be traced back to the work of Galton.
