communitarianism

noun
community-focused philosophy
9.1
Pronunciation: / kəˌmjunɪˈtɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪˈteəriəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
a philosophy or belief system that emphasizes the importance of the community, social institutions, and civic responsibility over individual interests

Examples

Communitarianism philosophy
The new law was inspired by communitarianism, aiming to strengthen neighborhood ties.
Some critics argue that communitarianism can limit personal freedoms in favor of group goals.
Her political views shifted toward communitarianism after volunteering in her local community.
The mayor promoted communitarianism by encouraging residents to participate in local decision-making.
Many policies inspired by communitarianism focus on strengthening neighborhood ties and social support networks.
Debates about communitarianism often contrast it with more individualistic approaches to government.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individualism - a belief in the importance of individual rights and independence
