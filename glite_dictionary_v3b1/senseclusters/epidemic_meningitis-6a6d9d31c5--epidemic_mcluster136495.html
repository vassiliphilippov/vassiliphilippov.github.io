epidemic meningitis

noun
bacterial meningitis occurring in outbreaks
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk məˌnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk mɛˌnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A severe form of bacterial meningitis that occurs in outbreaks affecting many people at once and is often fatal if untreated.

Examples

Severe epidemic meningitis
In the early 20th century, epidemic meningitis caused widespread panic in several cities.
The outbreak of epidemic meningitis led health officials to launch a mass vaccination campaign.
Without prompt treatment, epidemic meningitis can result in serious complications or death.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meningitis - inflammation of the membranes covering the brain and spinal cord
