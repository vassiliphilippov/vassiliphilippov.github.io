unquenched

adjective
not hardened by cooling
8.8
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈkwentʃt/ /; / /ən.ˈkwentʃt/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈkwentʃt/ /; / /ən.ˈkwentʃt/ (BrE)
not subjected to a quenching process, especially of metal or glass, and therefore not hardened or tempered by rapid cooling

Examples

Unquenched steel
The sword remained unquenched, making it softer and more flexible than a hardened blade.
If the glass is left unquenched, it may be more prone to breaking under stress.
The blacksmith explained that unquenched steel is easier to shape but less durable.

  • synonym: tempered - made stronger or harder by heating and cooling
