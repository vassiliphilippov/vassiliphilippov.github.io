ventral horn

noun
anterior gray matter column of the spinal cord
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛntrəl hɔrn / (AmE), / ˈvɛntrəl hɔːn / (BrE)
the anterior column of gray matter in the spinal cord, containing motor neurons whose axons exit the spinal cord via the ventral roots

Examples

The ventral horn
Damage to the ventral horn of the spinal cord can result in muscle weakness or paralysis.
Motor neurons located in the ventral horn transmit signals to skeletal muscles.
The disease primarily affects cells in the ventral horn, leading to progressive loss of motor function.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dorsal horn - the posterior column of gray matter in the spinal cord, associated with sensory neurons
