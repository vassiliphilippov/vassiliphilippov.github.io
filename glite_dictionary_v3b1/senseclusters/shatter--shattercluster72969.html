shatter

verb
break into many pieces
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæt̬.ɚ /; / ˈʃæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃæt.ə / (BrE)
to break or make something break suddenly into many small pieces

Examples

To shatter glass
A sudden explosion shattered all the windows in the building.
Her dreams were shattered when she heard the disappointing news.
The children accidentally shattered the vase while playing in the living room.
Careless handling can easily shatter a fragile glass vase.
If you drop that plate, it will shatter on the floor.
The explosion caused the windows to shatter instantly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break - to separate into pieces as a result of a blow, shock, or strain
  • synonym: smash - to break into pieces violently or noisily
