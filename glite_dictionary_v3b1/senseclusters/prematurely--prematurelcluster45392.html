prematurely

adverb
too soon; before the right time
4.1 3.61/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåpriÀê.m…ôÀàt É är.li / (AmE), / ÀåpriÀê.m…ôÀàt É ä…ô.li / (BrE)
in a way that happens too soon or before the appropriate or expected time

Examples

Left prematurely early
He left the meeting prematurely, missing the final decision.
Her excitement caused her to announce the news prematurely.
If you judge prematurely, you might misunderstand the situation.
Flowers that bloom prematurely can be damaged by late frosts.
She ended the meeting prematurely because of a sudden emergency.
The baby was delivered prematurely and required special care in the hospital.

Synonyms

  • too early
    1.0 8.73/m
    too early happens at a time before the expected moment, while prematurely suggests it happens before the proper time and causes problems.
  • early
    1.0 230/m
    early means before the usual or expected time and can be neutral, while prematurely means too soon and often causes problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: belatedly - later than expected or usual
  • synonym: early - before the expected or usual time
  • antonym: late - after the expected time
