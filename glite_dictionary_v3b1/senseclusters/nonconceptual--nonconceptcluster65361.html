nonconceptual

adjective
without concepts
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈsɛptʃuəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈsɛptʃuəl / (BrE)
not involving, based on, or related to concepts or conceptual thinking

Examples

Nonconceptual knowledge
The philosopher discussed the possibility of nonconceptual forms of knowledge.
Some theories suggest that infants have nonconceptual experiences before they develop language.
Art can sometimes evoke a nonconceptual response that is hard to describe in words.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceptual - involving or based on concepts
