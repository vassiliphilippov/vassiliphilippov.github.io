dimethyl

noun
two methyl groups
7.7
Pronunciation: / daɪˈmɛθəl / (AmE), / daɪˈmiːθɪl / (BrE)
a chemical group or substituent consisting of two methyl groups attached to an atom or molecule

Examples

Dimethyl group
The scientist added a dimethyl group to the compound to study its effects.
Certain drugs contain a dimethyl structure that changes how they interact with the body.
Removing the dimethyl group altered the chemical's properties significantly.
