minyan

noun
required quorum for Jewish communal worship
9.9 8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪn.jən/ /; / /mɪ.ˈnaɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈmɪn.jən/ /; / /mɪ.ˈnaɪ.ən/ (BrE)
In Judaism, the minimum number of people required (traditionally ten males over thirteen) for certain communal prayers and religious services.

Examples

A minyan of ten
A daily minyan is held at the synagogue every morning.
He traveled to find a minyan so that he could say the Kaddish prayer for his father.
We need one more person to complete the minyan before we can start the service.
