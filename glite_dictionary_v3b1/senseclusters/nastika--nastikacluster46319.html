nastika

noun
person or school rejecting Vedic authority in Hinduism
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːstɪkə / (AmE), / ˈnɑːstɪkə / (BrE)
A follower of a philosophical school or tradition in Indian philosophy, especially in Hinduism, that does not accept the authority of the Vedas as supreme revealed scriptures.

Examples

nastika philosophy
Some scholars regard early Buddhism as fundamentally nastika because of its rejection of the Vedas.
The Charvaka is perhaps the most well-known nastika school in Indian philosophy.
The distinction between nastika and astika schools remains important in classical Indian philosophical debates.

Related Terms
