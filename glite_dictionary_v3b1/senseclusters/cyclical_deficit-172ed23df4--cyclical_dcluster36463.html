cyclical deficit

noun
deficit due to business cycle changes
7.7 0.00/m

A government budget deficit that arises as a result of fluctuations in economic activity, typically increasing during periods of economic downturn and decreasing in times of growth.

Examples

The cyclical deficit rises
Because of the recession, the government ran a larger cyclical deficit than in previous years.
Economic slowdowns typically lead to a rise in the cyclical deficit as tax revenues decrease.
Policymakers distinguish between the cyclical deficit and the structural deficit when planning fiscal policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: structural deficit - a deficit that exists regardless of the economic cycle
