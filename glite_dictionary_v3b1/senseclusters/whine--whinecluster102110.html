Synonyms

    8.1 0.1/m
    whinge is chiefly British and means persistent petty complaining, while whine is general English for a high-pitched, petulant complaint.
  • grizzle
    8.2 0.01/m
    grizzle is a childish, repeated sulk or small complaint, while whine covers any high-pitched, petulant complaining.
  • bleat
    7.7 0.2/m
    bleat is a weak, animal-like cry used for complaining, while whine is a human high-pitched, irritating complaint.
