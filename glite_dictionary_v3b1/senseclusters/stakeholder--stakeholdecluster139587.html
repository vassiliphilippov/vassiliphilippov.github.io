stakeholder

noun
interested party
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪkˌhoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪkˌhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person or group that has an interest or concern in an organization, project, or decision, and may be affected by its outcomes

Examples

Company stakeholder
The company invited every stakeholder to the annual meeting to discuss future plans.
Government policies often affect a wide range of stakeholders in the community.
As a stakeholder, she voiced her concerns about the project's environmental impact.
The company invited all stakeholders to a meeting to discuss the new project.
Government policies often consider the needs of various stakeholders in society.
As a stakeholder, she had the right to voice her concerns about the changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bystander - a person who is present but not involved
  • synonym: participant - a person who takes part in something
  • synonym: shareholder - a person who owns shares in a company
