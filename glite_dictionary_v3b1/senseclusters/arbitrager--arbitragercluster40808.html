arbitrager

noun
person who profits from market price differences
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrbɪˌtreɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪˌtrɑːdʒə / (BrE)
a person who engages in arbitrage, buying and selling financial instruments in different markets to profit from price differences

Examples

A skilled arbitrager
A skilled arbitrager can generate profit without taking significant market risks.
As an arbitrager, she monitored currency fluctuations throughout the day.
The experienced arbitrager quickly sensed the price gap between the two markets.
