expectorator

noun
medicine that clears mucus from airways
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspɛktəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktəreɪtə / (BrE)
a medicine that promotes the discharge of mucus from the respiratory tract

Examples

Cough syrup expectorator
Many over-the-counter syrups contain an expectorator to aid in clearing chest congestion.
The doctor prescribed an expectorator to help relieve her persistent cough.
Using an expectorator can make it easier for patients to expel phlegm from their lungs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expectorant - a medicine that causes the removal of mucus from the lungs and air passages
