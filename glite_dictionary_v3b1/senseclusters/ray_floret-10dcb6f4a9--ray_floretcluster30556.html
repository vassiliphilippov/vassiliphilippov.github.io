ray floret

noun
strap-shaped peripheral flower of composites
9.9
Pronunciation: / reɪ ˈfloʊr.ɛt / (AmE), / reɪ ˈflɒr.ɛt / (BrE)
a small, strap-shaped flower typically found in the outer rings of composite flower heads, such as daisies or sunflowers

Examples

The ray floret flower
Botanists often distinguish between the ray floret and the disk floret when describing composite flowers.
In daisies, each white ray floret surrounds the cluster of smaller disk florets.
The bright yellow ray floret caught the sunlight along the edge of the sunflower.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disk floret - a small tubular flower in the central disc of a composite flower
