sandboxing

noun
regulatory trial program
Pronunciation: / ˈsændˌbɑksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsændˌbɒksɪŋ / (BrE)
a regulatory approach that allows companies to test new products or services under controlled, temporary exemptions from certain rules

Examples

Regulatory sandboxing for startups
The government introduced sandboxing to encourage innovation in the financial technology sector.
Several startups benefited from sandboxing by testing their services without facing full regulatory requirements.
Through sandboxing, companies can identify potential risks before launching products to the wider market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pilot program - a small-scale test of a new idea or product
  • antonym: regulation - a rule or directive made by an authority
