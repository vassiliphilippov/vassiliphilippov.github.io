storm off

verb
leave angrily and abruptly
4.5 0.61/m
Pronunciation: / st…îrm Àà…îf / (AmE), / st…îÀêm Àà…íf / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly and angrily, often as a reaction to something upsetting or offensive

Examples

To storm off angrily
After the heated discussion, Jake decided to storm off without saying another word.
She didn't want to storm off in anger, but the comment hurt her deeply.
Whenever things don't go his way, he tends to storm off instead of talking it out.

Synonyms

  • leave with a huff
    7.8 0.00/m
    leave with a huff shows clear annoyance by sighing or stomping away, while storm off is a loud, dramatic angry exit.
  • walk off
    3.5 2.73/m
    walk off means leaving suddenly to show annoyance or disapproval, while storm off shows stronger anger and a more dramatic exit.
  • walk out
    3.4 3.90/m
    walk out often means leaving an event suddenly to protest or show anger, while storm off is a personal, more emotional, dramatic exit.
  • walk out of
    3.1 4.63/m
    walk out of means leaving a room or meeting in protest, while storm off describes leaving angrily after a personal upset or insult.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calmly leave - to leave in a quiet, composed manner
  • synonym: march off - to leave somewhere purposefully, often in an angry or determined way
  • synonym: walk out - to leave a place or situation suddenly, especially because you are angry or dissatisfied
