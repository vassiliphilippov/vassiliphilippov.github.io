interoception

noun
perception of internal bodily sensations
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəroʊˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntərəʊˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the perception of sensations arising from within the body, such as hunger, heartbeat, or internal pain

Examples

Interoception of body signals
Meditation often heightens one's interoception, making internal sensations more noticeable.
Some people with anxiety disorders may have increased interoception and be overly aware of their heartbeats.
Understanding interoception helps doctors treat conditions related to body awareness and internal cues.

  • synonym: proprioception - perception of body position and movement
