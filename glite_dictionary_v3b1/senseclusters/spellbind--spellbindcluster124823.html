spellbind

verb
hold the attention of; fascinate
6.3 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈspel.baɪnd/ (AmE), /ˈspel.baɪnd/ (BrE)
to hold someone's complete attention as if by magic; to fascinate or captivate intensely

Examples

To spellbind an audience
His powerful speech spellbound everyone in the hall, leaving them silent and attentive.
She knew how to spellbind her readers with vivid descriptions and thrilling plots.
The magician's performance managed to spellbind the entire audience for over an hour.
As the magician revealed his final trick, he proceeded to spellbind the entire crowd.
Her performance on stage seemed to spellbind everyone in the theater.
The storyteller managed to spellbind the audience with his vivid tales.
Her performance was so powerful that it seemed to spellbind everyone in the theater.
The children were spellbound by the storyteller’s vivid and thrilling tales.
The magician managed to spellbind the entire crowd with her incredible illusions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bore - to make someone feel weary and uninterested
  • synonym: captivate - to attract and hold the interest and attention of; charm
  • synonym: enchant - to delight or charm completely
  • synonym: fascinate - to attract and hold the attention of someone
  • synonym: mesmerize - to hold someone's attention as if by hypnosis
