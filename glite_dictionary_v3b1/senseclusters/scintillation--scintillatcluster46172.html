scintillation

noun
twinkling of stars due to atmospheric effects
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the twinkling or sparkling of stars, caused when variations in the Earth's atmosphere produce uneven refraction of their light

Examples

Atmospheric scintillation
Astronomers must account for scintillation when making precise observations.
Atmospheric turbulence increases the scintillation that causes stars to twinkle.
The scintillation of the stars made the night sky seem alive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: twinkle - shine with a gleam that changes constantly
