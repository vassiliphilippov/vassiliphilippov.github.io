simplism

noun
oversimplification of complex issues
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪm.plɪz(ə)m /; / ˈsɪm.plɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.plɪz(ə)m /; / ˈsɪm.plɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the practice of making something seem simpler than it really is, often by ignoring important details or complexities

Examples

A simplism argument
His argument was criticized for its simplism and lack of nuance.
The report falls into simplism by ignoring the complexity of the problem.
To suggest that poverty has a single cause is pure simplism.
Critics accused the report of simplism for its overly broad recommendations.
Political debates often suffer from simplism when complex topics are reduced to slogans.
The article's simplism failed to address the real challenges of the issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oversimplification - the act of simplifying something so much that a distorted impression is given
