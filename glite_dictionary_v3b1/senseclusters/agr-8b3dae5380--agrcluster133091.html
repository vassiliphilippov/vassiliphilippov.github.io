abbreviation for business or finance terms, such as annual growth rate (percentage change per year) or adjusted gross revenue (total revenue after adjustments)

Examples

AGR of 5 percent
The company's AGR reached 5 percent last year, showing steady progress.
Analysts use AGR to compare the performance of different industries over time.
A higher AGR can indicate rapid expansion in a developing market.
The company reported its AGR for the fiscal year to comply with regulatory requirements.
Tax authorities requested documentation to verify the AGR figures submitted by the business.
After accounting for deductions, the firm's AGR was significantly lower than its gross revenue.

Related Terms
