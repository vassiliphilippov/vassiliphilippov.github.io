typesetting

noun
arranging text for print
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪpˌsɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪpˌsɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or skill of arranging text and images on a page for printing or digital publication, including layout, fonts, and spacing

Examples

Modern typesetting software
Modern typesetting software allows for precise control over page layout.
The book's beautiful typesetting made it easy and pleasant to read.
He learned about typesetting while working at a small printing press.
She took a course in typesetting to improve her publishing skills.
Modern typesetting is mostly done using computer software rather than by hand.
Accurate typesetting is essential for producing professional-looking books and magazines.
The typesetting of the magazine gave it a modern and professional look.
Advances in digital typesetting have made book production much faster and easier.
Good typesetting can make a long article more readable and attractive to the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: handwriting - the act or style of writing by hand
  • synonym: layout - the arrangement of text and images on a page
  • synonym: printing - the process of producing text and images on paper
