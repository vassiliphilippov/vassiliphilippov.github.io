suboscine

adjective
of suboscine birds
9.9
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈɑːsaɪn / (AmE), / sʌbˈɒsaɪn / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of suboscines, a group of passerine birds distinguished by their simple vocal organs and innate songs.

Examples

Suboscine birds
The suboscine species in the rainforest have unique nesting habits compared to other birds.
Researchers observed that suboscine birds develop their songs without learning from adults.
A suboscine anatomy is adapted for producing simpler vocalizations than those of oscines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oscine - relating to songbirds with complex vocal organs
