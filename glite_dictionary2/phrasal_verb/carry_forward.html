1. ⏭️ move to future ("carry forward unused days")

Pronunciation: / ˈkæri ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to move or transfer something (such as an amount, item, or result) to a later time or period
continue or move to a later time or situation / move an item to a later period

Examples

Any unused vacation days will be carried forward to the next calendar year.
If the expense can't be claimed this year, you may be able to carry it forward and use it later.
The accountant needs to carry forward last quarter's losses to this year's records.
Any unused vacation days will be carried forward to the next year.
We decided to carry forward the remaining tasks to next week.
You should carry forward your experience from this project to future assignments.
