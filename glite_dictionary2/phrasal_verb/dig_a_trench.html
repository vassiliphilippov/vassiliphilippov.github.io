1. ⛏️ to make a ditch ("to dig a trench for pipes")

VERB
Pronunciation: / dɪɡ ə trɛntʃ / (AmE), / dɪɡ ə trɛntʃ / (BrE)
to make a long, narrow excavation in the ground, especially for drainage, irrigation, or as part of construction work
make a long, narrow hole in the ground

Examples

Before planting the hedge, you should dig a trench deep enough for the roots.
In preparation for the water pipes, we have to dig a trench down the side of the road.
The workers were asked to dig a trench around the foundation of the new building.

Related Terms

  • synonym – excavate: to remove earth carefully and systematically from an area
  • antonym – fill in a trench: to put soil or material back into a trench
