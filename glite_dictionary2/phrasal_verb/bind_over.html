1. ⚖️ order to obey ("court binds over a defendant")

Pronunciation: / baɪnd ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / baɪnd ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of a court) to order a defendant to be held in custody or to comply with certain conditions pending further legal proceedings.
order custody pending trial (legal)

Examples

If found guilty, the court may bind over the defendant for sentencing.
She was bound over to keep the peace for twelve months.
The judge decided to bind over the suspect until the next hearing.
