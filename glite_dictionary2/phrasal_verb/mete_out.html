1. ⚖️ to give punishment ("to mete out punishment")

Pronunciation: / mit aʊt / (AmE), / miːt aʊt / (BrE)
to give or administer something, especially justice, punishment, or portions, usually in a measured or controlled way
to administer or distribute (usually punishment or justice)

Examples

It is not his responsibility to mete out justice; that is for the courts.
Teachers sometimes have to mete out punishment when rules are broken.
The judge decided to mete out a harsh sentence to those found guilty of fraud.
