1. ⛵ float freely ("be adrift at sea")

Pronunciation: / bi əˈdrɪft / (AmE), / bi əˈdrɪft / (BrE)
to be carried along by air or water currents without being steered or anchored
carried along by currents

Examples

After the storm, the damaged yacht was adrift for days before being rescued.
The inflatable raft may be adrift somewhere beyond the bay by now.
We worried that our canoe would be adrift all night if we didn’t anchor it.
