1. ✅ to accept ("to rule in an option")

VERB
Pronunciation: / ruːl ɪn / (AmE), / ruːl ɪn / (BrE)
to decide to include or accept something as a possibility, especially after considering the options or following procedures
accept or include as a possibility / to decide to include; judge in favor

Examples

The judge will rule in favor of the plaintiff if the evidence is convincing.

The committee decided to rule in several of the proposals after much discussion.
When reviewing the applications, we should rule in anyone who meets the minimum requirements.
