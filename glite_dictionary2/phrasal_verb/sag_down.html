1. ⬇️ to droop ("sag down under weight")

VERB
Pronunciation: / sæɡ daʊn / (AmE), / sæɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to sink or droop downward, or to cause something to do so, due to weight, loss of support, or relaxation
sink or droop downward

Examples

After hours of carrying the heavy backpack, his shoulders began to sag down.
She tried to keep the curtains taut, but they slowly sagged down and looked untidy.
The old mattress would sag down in the middle whenever anyone lay on it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – droop: to bend or hang downward limply
  • antonym – perk up: to become more upright or lively (opposite of sag down)
