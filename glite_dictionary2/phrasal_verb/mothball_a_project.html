1. ⏸️ to pause work ("to mothball a project temporarily")

VERB
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑθˌbɔl ə ˈprɑdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈmɒθˌbɔːl ə ˈprɒdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to temporarily suspend work on a project, often with the intention to resume at a later time
temporarily suspend a project

Examples

After several delays, the team had no choice but to mothball the project temporarily.
Due to budget cuts, the company decided to mothball the project until funding improved.
Sometimes it's more practical to mothball a project than to let it fail outright.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abandon: to give up completely on a project or activity
  • synonym – shelve: to decide not to proceed with a plan or project for a time
