NOUN
Pronunciation: / poʊˈstɪriɚ veɪn əv ði lɛft vɛnˈtrɪkəl / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəriə veɪn əv ði lɛft vɛnˈtrɪkl̩ / (BrE)
a vein that originates near the apex of the heart and drains into the coronary sinus, located on the posterior surface of the left ventricle
a vein behind the left heart ventricle

Examples

An obstruction in the posterior vein of the left ventricle can impair cardiac function.
During surgery, the doctor carefully avoided injuring the posterior vein of the left ventricle.
The posterior vein of the left ventricle helps return deoxygenated blood from the heart muscle back to the right atrium.
