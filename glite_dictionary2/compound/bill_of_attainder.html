1. ⚖️ law without trial ("bill of attainder against a person")

Pronunciation: / bɪl əv əˈteɪndər / (AmE), / bɪl əv əˈteɪndə / (BrE)
(in law) a legislative act that pronounces a person guilty of a crime, especially treason or felony, and punishes them without a trial.
legislative pronouncement of guilt without trial

Examples

Legal scholars often debate the definition and scope of a bill of attainder in constitutional law.
Several historical monarchs used a bill of attainder to punish their political enemies without due process.
The United States Constitution explicitly forbids Congress from passing any bill of attainder.
