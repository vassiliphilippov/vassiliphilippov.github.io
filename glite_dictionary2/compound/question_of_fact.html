1. ⚖️ factual issue ("question of fact in court")

Pronunciation: / ˈkwɛs.tʃən əv fækt / (AmE), / ˈkwɛs.tʃən əv fækt / (BrE)
an issue concerning a factual dispute to be determined by a jury or judge, rather than a question of law
legal issue over factual dispute

Examples

In civil trials, the jury resolves any question of fact that arises from the evidence presented.
The judge instructed the jury to decide the question of fact presented in the case.
Whether the defendant was present at the scene is a question of fact for the jury to determine.
