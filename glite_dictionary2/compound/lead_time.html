lead time

1. ⏳ waiting period ("short lead time")

Pronunciation: / lid taɪm / (AmE), / liːd taɪm / (BrE)
the period of time between starting and finishing a process, especially in business, manufacturing, or project management
the time needed to complete a process / time between starting and finishing a process

Examples

Because of the short lead time, the company was able to deliver orders quickly.
Manufacturers strive to minimize lead time to improve efficiency and customer satisfaction.
Planning ahead helps reduce the lead time for major projects.
Accurate forecasting helps reduce the lead time on new product launches.
Due to high demand, the lead time for this item may be longer than usual.
The supplier requires a lead time of three weeks before shipment.
