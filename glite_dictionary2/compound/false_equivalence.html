false equivalence

1⚖️unfair comparisonmake a false equivalence

1. ⚖️ unfair comparison ("make a false equivalence")

Pronunciation: / fɔls ɪˈkwɪvələns / (AmE), / fɔːls ɪˈkwɪvələns / (BrE)
an assertion or implication that two things are equivalent when they are actually significantly different, leading to a misleading or inaccurate comparison
misleading claim of equivalence between differing things

Examples

By equating constructive debate with personal attacks, he made a clear false equivalence in his response.
Some critics have pointed out that comparing minor mistakes to deliberate crimes is a false equivalence.
The politician's argument was dismissed as a false equivalence that confused the real differences between the policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym – false analogy: a fallacy where a misleading comparison is made based on irrelevant similarities
