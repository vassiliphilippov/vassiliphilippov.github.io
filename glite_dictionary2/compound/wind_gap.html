1. ⛰️ dry mountain pass ("mountain wind gap")

Pronunciation: / wɪnd ɡæp / (AmE), / wɪnd ɡap / (BrE)
A pass or notch in a mountain ridge through which a stream formerly flowed but is now dry.
dry pass in a mountain ridge

Examples

Geologists studied the wind gap to understand the ancient river that once ran through it.
Instead of flowing water, only dry grass lined the wind gap in the mountains.
The hikers crossed the wind gap before descending into the valley below.
