Radiation consisting of a stream of atomic or subatomic particles, which may be positively charged (such as alpha particles), negatively charged (such as beta particles), or uncharged (such as neutrons).
radiation of atomic or subatomic particles

Examples

Exposure to corpuscular radiation can damage living tissue at the cellular level.
Nuclear reactors are a significant source of corpuscular radiation in controlled environments.
Scientists use sensitive detectors to measure corpuscular radiation emitted by certain radioactive materials.
