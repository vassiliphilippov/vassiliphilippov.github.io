NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌɛnərˈdʒɛtɪk ˈmɑːnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɛnəˈdʒɛtɪk ˈmɒnɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical theory that energy is the fundamental substance of all phenomena and that all observable changes are transformations of energy
philosophical view: all is energy

Examples

Discussions of energetic monism often contrast it with materialistic or dualistic theories of reality.
The philosopher's work was grounded in energetic monism, viewing energy as the essence of all material existence.
Wilhelm Ostwald was a leading proponent of energetic monism in the late nineteenth century.
