NOUN
Pronunciation: / hit ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / hiːt ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A device that converts heat energy into mechanical work, typically by moving a working substance between hot and cold reservoirs.
device converting heat into work

Examples

A steam locomotive is an example of a heat engine that converts thermal energy into motion.
Modern power plants use a heat engine to generate electricity from burning fuel.
The efficiency of a heat engine is limited by the temperatures of its heat reservoirs.
