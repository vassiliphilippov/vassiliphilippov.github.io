1. ⏳ time to halve ("half-life of a substance")

Pronunciation: / ˈhæfˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌlaɪf / (BrE); / ˈhæf ˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf ˌlaɪf / (BrE)
the time it takes for something (especially a radioactive substance) to decrease to half its original amount
time for amount to decrease by half / time for something to halve, esp. radioactive decay

Examples

Scientists measured the half-life of the new isotope accurately.
Some medications have a short half-life, requiring more frequent dosing.
