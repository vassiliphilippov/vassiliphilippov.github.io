1. ➗ average by multiplying ("geometric mean of numbers")

Pronunciation: / dʒiˌɑːmɛtrɪk ˈmiːn / (AmE), / dʒɪˌɒmɪtrɪk ˈmiːn / (BrE)
the mean of a set of n positive numbers, calculated as the n-th root of their product
average found by taking root of a product

Examples

In statistics, the geometric mean is less affected by extreme values than the arithmetic mean.
The geometric mean is often used to calculate average growth rates over time.
To compare performance, analysts computed the geometric mean of the investment returns.
