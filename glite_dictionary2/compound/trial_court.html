1. ⚖️ first-level court ("trial court decision")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪ.əl kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əl kɔːt / (BrE)
A court of law where a case is first heard and the facts are determined, as opposed to an appellate court.
court where a case is first tried

Examples

After losing in trial court, the company decided to appeal the decision.
The trial court found the defendant guilty based on the evidence presented.
The case will be heard in the trial court before any appeals are considered.
