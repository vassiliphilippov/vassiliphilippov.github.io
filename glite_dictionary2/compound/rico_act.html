1. ⚖️ US anti-mafia law ("charged under the RICO Act")

Pronunciation: / ˈriːkoʊ ækt / (AmE), / ˈriːkəʊ ækt / (BrE)
A U.S. federal law aimed at combating organized crime by imposing strong sanctions and forfeiture provisions on those engaged in racketeering.
U.S. law against organized crime

Examples

Several executives were indicted for racketeering in violation of the RICO Act.
The RICO Act allows authorities to seize assets gained from organized crime activities.
The prosecutor charged the mob leaders under the RICO Act to dismantle their criminal enterprise.
