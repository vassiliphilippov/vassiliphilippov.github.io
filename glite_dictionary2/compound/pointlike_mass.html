NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪnt.laɪk mæs / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnt.laɪk mæs / (BrE)
an idealized object with mass but no spatial extent, used especially in physics as a simplification in calculations
idealized mass with no size (physics)

Examples

In classical mechanics, a pointlike mass is often used to simplify the analysis of motion.
The gravitational field of a pointlike mass is described by Newton's law of universal gravitation.
When modeling planets as pointlike masses, their actual size is ignored for most calculations.
