Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnəməli ɪnˈveɪsɪv kəˈrɔːnəri ˈbaɪpæs ˈsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməli ɪnˈveɪsɪv kəˈrɒnəri ˈbaɪpɑːs ˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
A coronary bypass procedure performed on a beating heart using small instruments and cameras inserted through small incisions, rather than open-chest surgery.
heart bypass using small incisions

Examples

Advances in technology have made minimally invasive coronary bypass surgery a viable option for many people with coronary artery disease.
After discussing the risks, her doctor recommended minimally invasive coronary bypass surgery over the traditional procedure.
Patients who are eligible for minimally invasive coronary bypass surgery often experience a quicker recovery time.
