1. ⚖️ charity law principle ("rule of cy pres in court")

Pronunciation: / ruːl əv siː ˈpreɪ / (AmE), / ruːl əv siː ˈpreɪ / (BrE)
a legal principle stating that if literal compliance with a charitable gift or will is impracticable or impossible, the donor's or testator's intention should be carried out as closely as possible
legal doctrine for interpreting impracticable charitable gifts or wills

Examples

Legal scholars debated whether the rule of cy pres was appropriate in this particular case.
Thanks to the rule of cy pres, the donor's intention was honored even though the specific project could not be completed.
The court applied the rule of cy pres to redirect the trust funds when the original charity ceased to exist.
