1. ⚖️ following orders excuse ("use the Nuremberg defense in court")

Pronunciation: / ˈnʊrəmˌbɜrɡ dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəmˌbɜːɡ dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
A legal defense asserting that a person was only following orders, especially in the context of actions carried out during war, and therefore should not be held personally responsible for those actions.
legal defense of acting under orders

Examples

During the trial, the defendant attempted to invoke the Nuremberg defense, claiming he was merely obeying orders.
He questioned whether the Nuremberg defense could ever truly excuse participation in atrocities.
Many legal scholars debate the moral implications of the Nuremberg defense in cases involving war crimes.
