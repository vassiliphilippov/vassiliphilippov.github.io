NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪ.t̬ɚ.ən ˈkaʊn.səl / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.tər.ən ˈkaʊn.səl / (BrE)
any of five major general councils of the Western Catholic Church held in the Lateran Palace in Rome between the 12th and 16th centuries.
ecclesiastical councils held at the Lateran Palace

Examples

Historians often refer to decisions made at the Lateran Council when discussing medieval church doctrine.
Several reforms were enacted by the Lateran Council to address corruption among clergy.
The Fourth Lateran Council was a landmark event in the history of the Catholic Church.
