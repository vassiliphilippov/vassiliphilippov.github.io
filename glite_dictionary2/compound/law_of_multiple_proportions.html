1. ⚗️ chemical ratio rule ("law of multiple proportions in chemistry")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv ˈmʌltəpəl prəˈpɔrʃənz / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈmʌltɪpl prəˈpɔːʃənz / (BrE)
(in chemistry) the law stating that when two elements form more than one compound, the masses of one element that combine with a fixed mass of the other are in ratios of small whole numbers.
chemical law on ratios of elements in compounds

Examples

According to the law of multiple proportions, the ratio of oxygen in two iron oxides is a small whole number.
John Dalton proposed the law of multiple proportions in the early nineteenth century.
The law of multiple proportions supports the atomic theory by explaining the relationship between elements in compounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym – law of definite proportions: law stating that a chemical compound always contains exactly the same proportion of elements by mass
