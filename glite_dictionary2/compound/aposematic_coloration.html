1. ⚠️ warning colors ("aposematic coloration warns predators")

Pronunciation: / ˌæp.oʊ.səˈmæt.ɪk ˌkʌl.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌap.əʊ.sɪˈmat.ɪk ˌkʌl.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the conspicuous coloring or markings of an animal that serve to warn predators that it is poisonous, unpalatable, or dangerous
warning coloration in animals

Examples

A skunk's distinctive black and white stripes are a form of aposematic coloration.
Many butterflies display aposematic coloration to signal that they are unpalatable to birds.
The poison dart frog's aposematic coloration vividly warns predators of its toxicity.
