1. ⚖️ court advice ("submit an amicus curiae brief")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / əˈmikəs ˈkjʊriˌaɪ briːf / (AmE), / əˈmiːkəs ˈkjʊəriˌaɪ briːf / (BrE)
a written statement submitted to a court by a person or organization not directly involved in a case but interested in influencing its outcome
legal brief submitted by an interested non-party

Examples

Many experts submitted amicus curiae briefs to inform the court on the scientific aspects of the case.
The Supreme Court received an amicus curiae brief from several civil rights organizations.
The judge considered the arguments presented in the amicus curiae brief before making her decision.
