1. ⚖️ pleasure over pain ("the pleasure-pain principle in psychology")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈplɛʒɚ peɪn ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈplɛʒə peɪn ˈprɪnsəpl / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) The principle that behavior is governed by the pursuit of pleasure and the avoidance of pain, especially in the id and early childhood development, where immediate gratification is sought without regard for reality.
psychoanalytic principle of seeking pleasure and avoiding pain

Examples

According to Freud, the pleasure-pain principle dominates the earliest stages of human development.
In psychoanalysis, the pleasure-pain principle explains why children often cannot distinguish between fantasy and reality.
The pleasure-pain principle suggests that we are naturally driven to seek pleasure and avoid discomfort.
