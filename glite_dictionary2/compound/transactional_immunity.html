1. ⚖️ legal protection ("transactional immunity for testimony")

Pronunciation: / trænˌzækʃənəl ɪˈmjunɪti / (AmE), / trænˌzækʃənəl ɪˈmjuːnɪti / (BrE)
A legal protection that prevents prosecution of a witness for offenses related to the transactions about which they have testified.
legal immunity preventing prosecution for testified acts

Examples

His lawyer negotiated transactional immunity before agreeing to let him testify about the alleged bribery scheme.
If you receive transactional immunity, you cannot be prosecuted for any crimes related to your testimony.
The court granted transactional immunity to the witness in exchange for their full cooperation.
