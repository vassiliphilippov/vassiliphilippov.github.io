NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪdʒən ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒən ˌbɛri / (BrE)
A tall, coarse perennial American herb with small white flowers and blackish-red berries; young stems are edible, but berries and root are poisonous.
American herb with toxic berries and edible stems

Examples

Children are warned not to eat the pigeon berry because of its poisonous properties.
Early settlers sometimes ate the young stems of pigeon berry as a vegetable.
The garden was bordered by a thick stand of pigeon berry, its berries glistening in the sun.
