1. ⚖️ legal question ("matter of law decided by judge")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈmætər əv lɔ / (AmE), / ˈmætə əv lɔː / (BrE)
an issue or question in a legal case that is decided by a judge rather than by a jury, often involving the interpretation or application of the law
legal issue decided by a judge

Examples

Appeals are often based on a matter of law, not just the facts of the case.
The judge ruled on the matter of law before allowing the trial to proceed.
Whether the contract was valid is a matter of law for the court to determine.
