1. ⚓ balance point ("centre of buoyancy in a ship")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntɚ əv bjuːˈjænsi / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə əv bjuːˈjænsi / (BrE)
the point in a floating object at which the upward force of buoyancy is considered to act, equal to the center of mass of the submerged part
center of mass of the submerged part

Examples

Engineers calculated the centre of buoyancy to ensure the submarine would surface properly.
Flooding the ballast tanks changed the centre of buoyancy, improving the vessel’s balance.
The ship remained stable because its centre of buoyancy was correctly aligned with its center of gravity.
