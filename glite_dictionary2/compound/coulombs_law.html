1. ⚡ electric force rule ("Coulomb's Law of electric force")

Pronunciation: / ˈkuːlɑmz lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkuːlɒmz lɔː / (BrE)
the principle in physics stating that the force between two electric charges is proportional to the product of their charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them
inverse-square law for electric charges

Examples

Coulomb's Law explains why two like charges repel each other with a force that decreases as distance increases.
Physics students often use Coulomb's Law to calculate the strength of electric forces between charged particles.
Understanding Coulomb's Law is essential for analyzing phenomena in electrostatics and magnetism.
