1. ❄️ lowest temperature ("absolute zero temperature")

Pronunciation: / ˌæbsəˌlut ˈzɪɹoʊ / (AmE), / ˌabsəˌluːt ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
the lowest possible temperature, at which the motion of particles is minimal; 0 Kelvin, −273.15°C, or −459.67°F
lowest possible temperature (0 Kelvin)

Examples

At absolute zero, molecular motion nearly ceases, making it a key concept in thermodynamics.
No physical system can reach absolute zero through any finite process.
Scientists have approached temperatures close to absolute zero in laboratory experiments.
