1. ✊ protest by sitting ("sit-in protest")

Pronunciation: / ˈsɪt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪt ɪn / (BrE)
A form of protest in which people occupy a place, such as a building or public area, and refuse to leave until their demands are considered or met.
form of protest by occupation

Examples

During the civil rights era, sit-ins played a key role in challenging segregation laws.
The students staged a sit-in in the university library to protest the new policy.
Workers organized a sit-in at the factory, demanding better wages and working conditions.

  • synonym – boycott: refusal to buy or use goods and services as a protest
  • synonym – demonstration: a public meeting or march protesting against something or expressing views on a political issue
