1. ⛪ non-Calvinist Baptist ("an Arminian Baptist church")

Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈmɪniən ˈbæptɪst / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪnɪən ˈbæptɪst / (BrE)
a member of a group of Baptist congregations that follow the teachings of Jacobus Arminius, especially in rejecting the doctrine of strict predestination upheld by Calvinists
member of a Baptist group with Arminian beliefs

Examples

He joined an Arminian Baptist church because of its focus on individual choice.
Many Arminian Baptists oppose strict Calvinist doctrine such as predestination.
The Arminian Baptist tradition emphasizes free will in the matter of salvation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Calvinist Baptist: a Baptist who adheres to Calvinist doctrine, especially predestination
