Pronunciation: / ˌprɛʃəˌraɪzd ˈwɔtɚ riˈæktɚ / (AmE), / ˌprɛʃəˌraɪzd ˈwɔːtə riˈæktə / (BrE)
A type of nuclear reactor that uses water under high pressure as both a coolant and moderator, producing steam to drive a turbine.
nuclear reactor using pressurized water as coolant/moderator

Examples

A pressurized water reactor keeps water under high pressure to prevent it from boiling inside the core.
Engineers conducted safety checks on the pressurized water reactor before restarting it.
The pressurized water reactor is the most common type of nuclear power plant in the world.
