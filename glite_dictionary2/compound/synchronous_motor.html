1. ⚡ electric motor ("synchronous motor speed control")

Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkrəˌnəs ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrənəs ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
an electric motor whose speed of rotation is exactly proportional to the frequency of the alternating current power supply
electric motor with rotation synchronized to AC frequency

Examples

Synchronous motors are commonly used in industrial machinery that require precise speed control.
The efficiency of a synchronous motor makes it ideal for large-scale ventilation systems.
When the frequency of the power supply fluctuated, the synchronous motor maintained a constant speed.
