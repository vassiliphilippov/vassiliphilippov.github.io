1. ⚖️ court order ("issue a writ of prohibition")

Pronunciation: / rɪt əv proʊˌhɪbɪˈʃən / (AmE), / rɪt ɒv prəʊˌhɪbɪˈʃən / (BrE)
a judicial order issued by a higher court to prevent a lower court from proceeding with a case that exceeds its jurisdiction
judicial order preventing lower court from acting

Examples

A writ of prohibition may be issued when a lower court acts outside its authority.
The Supreme Court granted a writ of prohibition to halt the proceedings in the lower tribunal.
The defense lawyer filed a writ of prohibition to challenge the lower court's jurisdiction.
