Pronunciation: / ˈpɛti ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / ˈpɛti ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
A jury of twelve people who decide the facts and verdict in civil or criminal legal proceedings.
jury deciding facts in a trial

Examples

Both the prosecution and the defense questioned prospective members of the petty jury.
