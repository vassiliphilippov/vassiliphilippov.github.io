1. ⚖️ universal moral rules ("natural law theory")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈnætʃrəl lɔ / (AmE), / ˈnatʃrəl lɔː / (BrE)
a system of rules or principles regarded as inherent in human nature and universally binding on human society, forming a basis for moral and legal standards
principles of right and wrong inherent in human nature

Examples

Judges sometimes refer to natural law principles when interpreting the constitution.
Many philosophers have debated the relationship between natural law and human-made laws.
The concept of natural law suggests that some rights are universal and not dependent on legislation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – positive law: law established by the authority of a state or society
