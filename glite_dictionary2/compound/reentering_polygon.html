1. ⭐ inward angle polygon ("star-shaped reentering polygon")

Pronunciation: / riˈɛntɚɪŋ ˈpɑlɪˌɡɑn / (AmE), / riˈɛntərɪŋ ˈpɒlɪˌɡɒn / (BrE)
a polygon that has one or more reentrant angles, where the interior angle is greater than 180 degrees
polygon with at least one angle over 180°

Examples

A star-shaped figure is a classic example of a reentering polygon.
The architect designed the courtyard in the shape of a reentering polygon for aesthetic effect.
Unlike convex shapes, a reentering polygon can have inward-pointing angles.
