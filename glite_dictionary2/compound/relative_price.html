1. ⚖️ price ratio ("relative price of oil")

Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlətɪv praɪs / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv ˈpraɪs / (BrE)
the price of one good or service expressed in terms of another, typically represented as the ratio of two prices
price of one item in terms of another

Examples

An increase in the relative price of gasoline may lead people to drive less.
Economists often analyze relative price changes to understand consumer behavior.
The relative price of coffee compared to tea increased after the harvest failure.

  • antonym – absolute price: the price of a good in monetary terms, not compared to another
