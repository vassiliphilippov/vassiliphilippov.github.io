1. ✈️ wing weight ratio ("span loading of an aircraft")

Pronunciation: / spæn ˈloʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / spæn ˈləʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
The ratio of the weight of an aircraft to the length of its wingspan, used as a measure in aeronautical engineering.
ratio of aircraft weight to wingspan

Examples

Aircraft with high span loading typically require stronger wings to support the additional weight.
Designers calculated the span loading to ensure optimal performance for the new glider.
Reducing span loading can improve lift and fuel efficiency in lightweight airplanes.
