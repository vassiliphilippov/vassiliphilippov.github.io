NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛl.jə.lɚ ˌrɛspɪˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.jʊ.lə ˌrɛspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the set of metabolic processes in living cells and tissues by which energy is obtained from organic molecules and released as ATP, producing carbon dioxide as a byproduct
energy-releasing process in cells

Examples

During cellular respiration, glucose is broken down to release usable energy for the cell.
Plants and animals both rely on cellular respiration to meet their energy requirements.
Scientists study cellular respiration to better understand how cells convert nutrients into energy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aerobic respiration: metabolic process using oxygen to produce energy in cells
  • antonym – anaerobic respiration: energy production in cells without using oxygen
