1. ⚖️ shared fault ("comparative negligence rule applies")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpærətɪv ˈnɛɡlɪdʒəns / (AmE), / kəmˈparətɪv ˈnɛɡlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
A legal principle in which responsibility for an accident is divided between the parties involved, reducing the damages paid to the plaintiff according to the degree of their own negligence.
legal principle allocating negligence and damages

Examples

In states that follow comparative negligence, a plaintiff's damages are reduced by their percentage of fault.
The court applied comparative negligence because both driver and pedestrian shared blame for the accident.
Under comparative negligence rules, each party must pay for their proportion of the damages.

Related Terms

  • antonym – contributory negligence: a legal doctrine where any fault on the part of the plaintiff blocks recovery
