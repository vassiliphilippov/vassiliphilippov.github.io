1. ⚗️ reference electrode ("hydrogen electrode in solution")

Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrədʒən ɪˈloʊˌdroʊd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrədʒən ɪˈləʊdrəʊd / (BrE)
an electrode in which hydrogen gas is adsorbed onto platinum, used as a standard reference in electrochemistry to measure electrode potentials
electrode using hydrogen gas and platinum as reference

Examples

Accurate pH readings can be obtained with the help of a hydrogen electrode.
