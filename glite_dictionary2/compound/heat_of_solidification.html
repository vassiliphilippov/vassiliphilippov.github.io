1. ❄️ freezing heat loss ("heat of solidification in metal casting")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / hit əv ˌsɑlɪdəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / hiːt əv ˌsɒlɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The amount of heat released by a unit mass of a substance as it solidifies at its freezing point.
heat released when liquid solidifies

Examples

Engineers accounted for the heat of solidification when designing the metal casting process.
The heat of solidification must be considered in the thermal management of phase-changing materials.
When water freezes to form ice, it releases its heat of solidification into the environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – heat of fusion: heat required to convert solid to liquid at its melting point
  • synonym – latent heat: energy absorbed or released during a phase change without temperature change
