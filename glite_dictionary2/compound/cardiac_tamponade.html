NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdiˌæk tæmˈpoʊnɑd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiæk tæmˈpəʊnɑːd / (BrE)
A medical condition in which fluid builds up in the pericardial sac around the heart, compressing it and impairing its normal function.
compression of the heart by fluid buildup

Examples

Emergency surgery was required to treat the cardiac tamponade that developed after the accident.
Patients with cardiac tamponade often present with low blood pressure and difficulty breathing.
The doctor quickly diagnosed cardiac tamponade by observing fluid around the heart on the ultrasound.
