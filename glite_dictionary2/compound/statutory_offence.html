1. ⚖️ law-defined crime ("serious statutory offence")

Pronunciation: / stəˈtʃuː.tɔːr.i əˈfɛns / (AmE), / stəˈtjuː.tər.i əˈfɛns / (BrE)
A crime that is specifically defined and prohibited by a statute rather than by common law.
crime created by statute, not common law

Examples

A statutory offence may carry penalties set out in the legislation itself.
Driving without insurance is considered a statutory offence in many countries.
Unlike theft, which can be a common law crime, copyright infringement is a statutory offence.
