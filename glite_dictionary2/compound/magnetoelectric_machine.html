NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌmæɡ.nə.toʊ.iːˈlɛk.trɪk məˈʃin / (AmE), / ˌmaɡ.nɪ.təʊ.ɪˈlɛk.trɪk məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
a small electrical generator with a secondary winding that produces high voltage, enabling a spark to jump between spark plug poles in a gasoline engine
generator producing high-voltage sparks in engines

Examples

Early automobiles often relied on a magnetoelectric machine to generate sparks for combustion.
Modern engines rarely use a magnetoelectric machine, having been replaced by electronic ignition systems.
The mechanic inspected the magnetoelectric machine to diagnose the engine's ignition problem.
