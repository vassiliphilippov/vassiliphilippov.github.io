1. ⚖️ jury approval ("return a true bill")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / tru bɪl / (AmE), / truː bɪl / (BrE)
A legal document indicating that a grand jury has found sufficient evidence for a criminal charge to proceed to trial.
indictment approved by a grand jury

Examples

If the grand jury does not find enough evidence, it will not return a true bill.
Once a true bill is issued, the accused must stand trial for the alleged crime.
The grand jury returned a true bill against the defendant after reviewing the evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – no bill: a decision by a grand jury not to indict
