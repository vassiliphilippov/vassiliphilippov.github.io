1. ⚡ electric heat ("dielectric heating of plastic")

Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈlɛktrɪk ˈhiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈlɛktrɪk ˈhiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
Heating of an insulating material by exposure to a high-frequency electric field, causing the material's molecules to oscillate and generate heat.
heating insulators using high-frequency electric fields

Examples

Because dielectric heating directly warms the material, it is more efficient than traditional surface heating methods.
Industrial microwave ovens use dielectric heating to rapidly process large quantities of food.
Some types of plastic welding rely on dielectric heating to bond materials without direct contact.
