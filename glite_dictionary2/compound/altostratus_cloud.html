1. ☁️ mid-level cloud ("thick altostratus cloud layer")

Pronunciation: / ˌæltoʊˈstreɪtəs klaʊd / (AmE), / ˌaltəˈstrɑːtəs klaʊd / (BrE)
A gray or bluish cloud sheet of uniform appearance, typically found at mid-level altitudes (about 2–7 km or 2–4 miles), often covering most of the sky and sometimes producing light precipitation.
mid-level gray sheet-like cloud

Examples

A thick layer of altostratus cloud covered the sky, making the afternoon look unusually dull.
Pilots kept an eye on the approaching altostratus cloud, which signaled a change in the weather.
The soft sunlight filtered through the altostratus cloud, giving the landscape a bluish hue.
