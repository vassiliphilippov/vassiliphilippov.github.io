1. ⚖️ unwritten legal rules ("common law system")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑm.ən lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən lɔː / (BrE); / ˈkɑmən lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən lɔː / (BrE)
a system or body of law based on judicial decisions and customs, rather than written laws, especially as developed in England and used in many English-speaking countries
body of law based on judicial precedents / legal system based on precedents, not statutes

Examples

Many countries that were once British colonies still follow the principles of common law.
The U.S. legal system is influenced by both statutes and common law precedents.
Under common law, court decisions have significant authority in shaping future legal outcomes.
Many countries with a British heritage have legal systems based on common law.
The principles of common law have evolved over centuries of court rulings.
Under common law, decisions by higher courts are binding on lower courts.
