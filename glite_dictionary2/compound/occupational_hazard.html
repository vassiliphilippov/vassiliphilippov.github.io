1. ⚠️ job risk ("occupational hazard for workers")

Pronunciation: / ˌɑːkjəˈpeɪʃənəl ˈhæzərd / (AmE), / ˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃənəl ˈhæzəd / (BrE); / ˌɑːkjəˈpeɪʃənəl ˈhæzərd / (AmE), / ˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃənəl ˈhazəd / (BrE)
a risk or danger that is a natural or unavoidable part of a particular job or occupation
job-related risk or danger / risk inherent to a job or occupation

Examples

Exposure to chemicals is an occupational hazard for many laboratory workers.
Many construction workers accept that accidents are an occupational hazard in their line of work.
Repetitive motion injuries are a common occupational hazard in office environments.
Dealing with angry customers is an occupational hazard in the retail industry.
For nurses, exposure to illness is a common occupational hazard.
He accepted the back pain as just another occupational hazard of his profession.
