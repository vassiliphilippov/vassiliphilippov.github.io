1. ⚖️ incomplete violation ("partial breach of contract")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrʃəl ˈbriːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːʃəl ˈbriːtʃ / (BrE)
A breach of contract that does not completely destroy the contract's value but may entitle the injured party to claim damages.
incomplete breach of contract

Examples

A partial breach does not necessarily void the entire contract, but it may result in compensation.
The contractor committed a partial breach by delivering the materials two days late.
The court ruled that only a partial breach had occurred, so the agreement remained in force.
