1. ⚡ particle detector ("spark chamber detects particles")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / spɑrk ˈtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / spɑːk ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
an instrument used in particle physics to detect ionizing radiation from elementary particles, where the passage of a particle triggers visible sparks between electrodes
device for detecting particle radiation

Examples

A spark chamber can reveal the tracks left by cosmic rays passing through its plates.
Early experiments in particle physics often relied on the spark chamber for detecting charged particles.
The researchers used a spark chamber to visualize the paths of subatomic particles.
