1. ⚖️ comparison test ("run a control experiment")

Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl ɪkˈspɛrəmənt / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl ɪkˈspɛrɪmənt / (BrE)
an experiment conducted to account for factors that could affect the results of another experiment, serving as a standard of comparison
experiment for comparison in scientific tests

Examples

In a proper control experiment, all variables except the one being tested are held constant.
The researchers used a control experiment to determine if the treatment was responsible for the observed effect.
Without a control experiment, it is difficult to accurately interpret the findings of the study.
