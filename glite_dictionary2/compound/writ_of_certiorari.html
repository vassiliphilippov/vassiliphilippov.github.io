1. ⚖️ court review order ("file a writ of certiorari")

Pronunciation: / ˌrɪt əv ˌsɜːr.ti.oʊˈrɛr.i / (AmE), / ˌrɪt əv ˌsɜː.ti.əˈrɑːr.aɪ / (BrE)
a legal order issued by a higher court directing a lower court to deliver the record in a specified case for review
legal order to review a lower court's case

Examples

Attorneys filed for a writ of certiorari in hopes of overturning the verdict.
Only a small percentage of cases receive a writ of certiorari each year.
The Supreme Court issued a writ of certiorari to review the lower court's decision.
