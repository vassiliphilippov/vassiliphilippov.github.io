1. ⚖️ physics law ("conservation of momentum in collisions")

Pronunciation: / kɑnsɚˌveɪʃən əv moʊˈmɛntəm / (AmE), / kɒnsəˌveɪʃən əv məʊˈmɛntəm / (BrE)
The scientific principle stating that the total linear momentum of a closed system remains constant, regardless of internal processes.
principle of constant total momentum in a system

Examples

In collisions, the conservation of momentum allows physicists to predict the resulting motion of objects.
Students conducted experiments to demonstrate the conservation of momentum using carts on a track.
The conservation of momentum explains why a rocket moves forward when its fuel is expelled backward.
