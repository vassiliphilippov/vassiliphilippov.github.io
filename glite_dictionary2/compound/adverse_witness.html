1. ⚖️ unfriendly court witness ("call an adverse witness")

Pronunciation: / ˈædˌvɝs ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈædvɜːs ˈwɪtnɪs / (BrE)
A witness called by one party in a court case whose relationship or testimony may be unfavorable to that party, often allowing special questioning procedures.
legal term for a possibly hostile witness

Examples

Because she was considered an adverse witness, the lawyer was permitted to ask leading questions.
If a witness is declared an adverse witness, cross-examination rules may apply immediately.
The attorney filed a motion to treat the expert as an adverse witness during the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hostile witness: a witness whose testimony is antagonistic to the calling party
