1. ✏️ grammar mistake ("fix a dangling modifier")

Pronunciation: / ˈdæŋɡlɪŋ ˈmɑːdɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈdaŋɡlɪŋ ˈmɒdɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a word or phrase placed so that it seems to modify the wrong word in a sentence, often leading to ambiguity or error
misplaced word or phrase in a sentence

Examples

The sentence 'After eating lunch, the park was peaceful' contains a dangling modifier.
When a dangling modifier appears, it's often unclear who or what is being modified.
Writers are advised to avoid using a dangling modifier because it can confuse readers.
