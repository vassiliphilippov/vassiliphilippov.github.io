1. ⏸️ delay punishment ("grant a stay of execution")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / steɪ əv ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən / (AmE), / steɪ əv ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən / (BrE)
an official order that temporarily stops the enforcement of a court judgment, especially the carrying out of a death sentence or other penalty
temporary suspension of a court judgment

Examples

Activists celebrated when the prisoner was granted a stay of execution just hours before the sentence was to be carried out.
The court refused to grant a stay of execution, and the ruling was enforced immediately.
The judge issued a stay of execution to give the defense more time to file an appeal.
