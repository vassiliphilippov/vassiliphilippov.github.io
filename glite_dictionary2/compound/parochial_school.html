1. ⛪ church-run school ("parochial school education")

Pronunciation: / pəˈroʊkiəl skuːl / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊkiəl skuːl / (BrE)
A private school operated by a church parish, especially one providing primary or secondary education with a religious affiliation.
private religious school run by a parish

Examples

Many parents choose a parochial school for the emphasis on both academics and faith.
She attended a parochial school throughout her elementary years.
The local parochial school requires students to participate in daily religious instruction.
