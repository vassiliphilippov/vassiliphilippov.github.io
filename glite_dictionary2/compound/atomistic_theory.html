1. ⚛️ matter is atoms ("atomistic theory of matter")

Pronunciation: / ˌæt̬.əˈmɪs.tɪk ˈθɪr.i / (AmE), / ˌat.əˈmɪs.tɪk ˈθɪə.ri / (BrE)
(Chemistry, philosophy) A theory that proposes all matter consists of tiny, discrete, indivisible, and indestructible particles called atoms.
theory that matter is made of tiny indivisible particles

Examples

Dalton revived the atomistic theory in the early 19th century to describe chemical reactions.
Many scientific advancements were influenced by the acceptance of the atomistic theory.
The ancient Greeks developed the atomistic theory to explain the nature of matter.
