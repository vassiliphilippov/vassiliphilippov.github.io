1. ⚓ privateer license ("letters of marque for private ships")

Pronunciation: / ˈlɛtɚz əv mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəz əv mɑːk / (BrE)
an official license granted by a government to a private person, allowing them to capture vessels and goods of an enemy nation
government license to seize enemy ships

Examples

During wartime, governments sometimes issued letters of marque to private ship owners.
The pirate captain claimed his letters of marque protected his actions from prosecution.
Without letters of marque, attacks on enemy vessels would have been considered piracy.
