1. ⚾ baseball pitch area ("the strike zone in baseball")

Pronunciation: / ˈstraɪk ˌzoʊn / (AmE), / ˈstraɪk ˌzəʊn / (BrE)
(in baseball) the area over home plate between a batter's knees and shoulders through which a pitch must pass to be called a strike.
baseball: area pitch must pass for strike

Examples

Every batter tries to anticipate what part of the strike zone the next pitch will target.
Pitchers who consistently throw in the strike zone are often more successful.
The umpire's interpretation of the strike zone can change the outcome of the game.
