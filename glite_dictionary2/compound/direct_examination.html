1. ⚖️ witness questioning ("direct examination of witness")

Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt ˌɛɡ.zəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / dəˈrɛkt ˌɛɡ.zəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
In law, the questioning of a witness by the party that has called them to testify, before cross-examination by the opposing side.
legal questioning of one's own witness

Examples

During the direct examination, the attorney asked the witness about the events leading up to the accident.
Her answers during direct examination were clear and detailed, helping her lawyer's argument.
The jury listened attentively as the direct examination established the timeline of the case.
