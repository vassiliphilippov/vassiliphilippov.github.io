NOUN
Pronunciation: / hʌŋ ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / hʌŋ ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
A jury in a trial that cannot reach a unanimous or sufficient majority verdict, leading to a mistrial.
a jury unable to agree on a verdict

Examples

Despite strong evidence, the case resulted in a hung jury and had to be retried.
The trial ended in a hung jury after several days of deliberation.
When there is a hung jury, the judge may declare a mistrial.
