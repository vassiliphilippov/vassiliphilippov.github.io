1. ⚠️ severe allergic reaction ("severe anaphylactic shock")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌænəfɪˈlæktɪk ʃɑk / (AmE), / ˌanəfɪˈlaktɪk ʃɒk / (BrE)
A severe and rapid allergic reaction that can cause difficulty breathing, drop in blood pressure, and can be life-threatening if not treated quickly.
severe, rapid allergic reaction

Examples

Anaphylactic shock can be fatal if epinephrine is not administered quickly.
He went into anaphylactic shock after being stung by a bee and needed immediate medical attention.
People with severe allergies should carry an epinephrine injector in case of anaphylactic shock.
