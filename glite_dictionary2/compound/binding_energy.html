1. ⚡ energy to separate parts ("nuclear binding energy")

Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪndɪŋ ˌɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndɪŋ ˌɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the energy required to separate the components of a molecule, atom, or nucleus into individual parts; numerically equal to the mass defect in nuclear contexts.
energy needed to separate atomic or nuclear components

Examples

Nuclear binding energy determines the stability of atomic nuclei.
Scientists calculated the binding energy needed to split the helium nucleus.
The binding energy of electrons in a molecule affects its chemical reactivity.
