NOUN
Pronunciation: / lə ʃæˈtɛl.i.eɪ ˈprɪn.sə.pəl / (AmE), / lə ʃæˈtɛl.i.eɪ ˈprɪn.sɪ.pl̩ / (BrE)
The principle that if a change is imposed on a system at equilibrium, the system will adjust in a way that tends to counteract the change and restore equilibrium.
principle describing system response to equilibrium change

Examples

According to the Le Chatelier principle, increasing the pressure will shift the reaction towards fewer gas molecules.
Students often use the Le Chatelier principle to predict the outcome of changes in chemical equilibrium.
The Le Chatelier principle explains why adding heat to an exothermic reaction decreases the yield of products.
