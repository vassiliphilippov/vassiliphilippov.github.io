NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.kəˌlaɪ ˈpɔɪ.zən.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪ ˈpɔɪ.zən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
Poisoning caused by swallowing or being exposed to an alkali compound, such as lye or ammonia.
poisoning by alkaline substance

Examples

Accidental alkali poisoning can occur if children ingest household cleaners containing lye.
Emergency treatment for alkali poisoning often requires immediate hospitalization.
Symptoms of alkali poisoning include burning pain in the mouth and throat and difficulty swallowing.
