NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪv ɪˌlɛktroʊloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv ɪˌlɛktrəʊləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which an animal detects objects and its environment by generating an electric field and sensing changes in that field with electroreceptor organs.
animal sensing by generating electric fields

Examples

During active electrolocation, the animal emits a weak electric field and senses objects that distort it.
Many species of fish use active electrolocation to navigate murky waters where vision is limited.
Researchers study active electrolocation to better understand how electric fish perceive their surroundings.
