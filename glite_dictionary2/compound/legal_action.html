1. ⚖️ court process ("take legal action in court")

Pronunciation: / ˈliɡəl ˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˈækʃən / (BrE)
A formal process taken in a court of law to resolve a dispute between parties, usually where one side seeks remedy for a wrong or the protection of a right.
formal court process to resolve disputes

Examples

After months of negotiation, they finally resorted to legal action.
She threatened to take legal action if the issue was not resolved.
The company filed a legal action against the supplier for breach of contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lawsuit: a case brought before a court by one party against another
  • antonym – settlement: an official agreement intended to resolve a dispute or conflict
