NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌroʊ.məˈnɛsk ˌɑːr.kɪˌtɛk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌrəʊ.məˈnɛsk ˌɑː.kɪˈtɛk.tʃə / (BrE)
a style of architecture developed in Europe after 1000 AD, characterized by round arches, vaulted ceilings, thick walls, piers instead of columns, and elaborate ornamentation.
European architectural style with round arches and vaults

Examples

Distinctive features of Romanesque architecture include sturdy piers, thick walls, and large towers.
Many medieval cathedrals in Europe are fine examples of Romanesque architecture.
The transition from Romanesque architecture to Gothic can be seen in several 12th-century churches.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – Gothic architecture: a style of architecture that followed Romanesque, characterized by pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses
