1. ⚖️ law rule breach ("commit a regulatory offence")

Pronunciation: / ˈrɛgjəˌleɪtɔri əˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈrɛgjʊlət(ə)ri əˈfɛns / (BrE)
An offence defined by statute rather than by common law, often involving the breach of regulations rather than inherently wrongful acts.
statutory offence; breach of regulation (law)

Examples

Many environmental violations are classified as regulatory offences rather than criminal offences.
The company paid a fine for a regulatory offence related to workplace safety laws.
Unlike other crimes, a regulatory offence may not require proof of intent.

Related Terms

  • antonym – common law offence: an offence established by common law rather than statute
  • synonym – statutory offence: an offence created by statute law
