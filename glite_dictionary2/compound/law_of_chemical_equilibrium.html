NOUN
Pronunciation: / lɔ ˈʌv ˌkɛmɪkəl ˌikwəˈlɪbriəm / (AmE), / lɔː ˈʌv ˌkɛmɪkəl ˌiːkwɪˈlɪbriəm / (BrE)
the scientific principle stating that, in a reversible chemical reaction at equilibrium, the ratio of the rates of the forward and reverse reactions is constant for that reaction
principle about reaction rates at chemical equilibrium

Examples

According to the law of chemical equilibrium, the rates of the forward and reverse reactions are balanced at equilibrium.
Every chemistry student must understand how the law of chemical equilibrium governs reversible reactions.
The law of chemical equilibrium explains why the concentrations of reactants and products remain constant in a closed system.
