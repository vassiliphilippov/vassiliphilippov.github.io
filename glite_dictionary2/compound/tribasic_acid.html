tribasic acid

1⚗️three hydrogens chemistrytribasic acid donates protons

1. ⚗️ three hydrogens ("tribasic acid donates protons")

Pronunciation: / traɪˈbeɪsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / traɪˈbeɪsɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an acid that contains three replaceable hydrogen atoms per molecule, each capable of being replaced by a base or metal
acid with three replaceable hydrogens

Examples

Citric acid acts as a tribasic acid because it contains three ionizable protons.
Each molecule of a tribasic acid can donate three hydrogen ions in reaction with a base.
Phosphoric acid is a classic example of a tribasic acid in inorganic chemistry.
