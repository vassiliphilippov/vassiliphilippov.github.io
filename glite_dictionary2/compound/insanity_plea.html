1. ⚖️ mental illness defense ("enter an insanity plea")

Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsænəti pli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsænəti pliː / (BrE)
a legal plea in which a defendant claims they are not responsible for their actions due to mental illness at the time of the crime
legal defense based on mental illness

Examples

His lawyer advised him to consider an insanity plea given his long history of mental illness.
The defendant entered an insanity plea, arguing he was not in control of his actions during the crime.
The jury had to decide whether the insanity plea was convincing enough to acquit her.

Related Terms

  • antonym – guilty plea: a formal statement in court by the defendant admitting guilt
  • synonym – not guilty by reason of insanity: a verdict that a person is not responsible for a crime because of mental illness
