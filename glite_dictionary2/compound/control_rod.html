1. ☢️ nuclear safety bar ("control rod in reactor")

Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl rɑd / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl rɒd / (BrE)
a rod made of steel or aluminum that is inserted or withdrawn from a nuclear reactor to control the rate of the nuclear reaction by absorbing neutrons
rod for controlling nuclear reactions

Examples

A malfunctioning control rod can lead to dangerous fluctuations in reactor power.
Inserting a control rod deeper into the core slows the nuclear reaction.
The technician checked the position of the control rod before starting the reactor.

  • synonym – moderator: material used to slow down neutrons in a reactor
