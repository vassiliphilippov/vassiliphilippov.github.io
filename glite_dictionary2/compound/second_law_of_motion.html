Pronunciation: / ˈsɛk.ənd lɔ ʌv moʊˈʃən / (AmE), / ˈsek.ənd lɔː əv məʊˈʃən / (BrE)
Newton's law stating that the rate of change of momentum of a body is proportional to the force applied and occurs in the direction of the force.
Newton's law on force and momentum

Examples
