1. ☢️ low-fallout nuke ("test a clean bomb")

Pronunciation: / klin bɑm / (AmE), / kliːn bɒm / (BrE)
an atomic bomb designed to produce very little radioactive fallout or contamination
atomic bomb with minimal radioactive fallout

Examples

After the test, scientists reported that the clean bomb produced far less fallout than earlier nuclear devices.
Some military experts argue that a clean bomb could be used to limit collateral damage while still achieving strategic goals.
The development of the clean bomb was seen as a response to concerns about long-term radioactive contamination.
