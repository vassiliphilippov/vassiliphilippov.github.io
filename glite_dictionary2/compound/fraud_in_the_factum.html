1. ✍️ document trick ("fraud in the factum signing documents")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / frɔd ɪn ðə ˈfæktəm / (AmE), / frɔːd ɪn ðə ˈfæktəm / (BrE)
A legal term referring to fraud that occurs when a person is deceived about the nature of the document they are signing, resulting in a disparity between what was intended to be executed and what was actually executed.
legal fraud by misrepresenting a document's nature

Examples

A case of fraud in the factum occurs when someone tricks you into signing a document you do not understand.
Courts distinguish fraud in the factum from cases where someone is merely misled about the details of an agreement.
The signature was obtained through fraud in the factum, so the contract is void.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fraud in the inducement: fraud involving misleading someone about terms or benefits, not the nature of the document
