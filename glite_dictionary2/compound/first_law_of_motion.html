1. ⚖️ inertia principle ("first law of motion in physics")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝst lɔ əv moʊʃn̩ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst lɔː əv ˈməʊʃn̩ / (BrE)
the principle in physics stating that a body remains at rest or continues to move at a constant velocity unless acted upon by an external force; also known as Newton's first law of motion or the law of inertia.
Newton's law stating objects' motion remains constant unless acted on

Examples

According to the first law of motion, an object will stay in motion unless a force acts upon it.
The first law of motion explains why passengers lurch forward when a car suddenly stops.
Understanding the first law of motion is fundamental to studying classical mechanics.
