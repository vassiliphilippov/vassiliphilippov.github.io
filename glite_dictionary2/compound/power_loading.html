1. ✈️ weight to power ratio ("power loading of an aircraft")

Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊ.ɚ ˌloʊ.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə ˌləʊ.dɪŋ / (BrE)
The ratio of an aircraft's weight to the power output of its engine, used to assess performance.
ratio of aircraft weight to engine power

Examples

A heavier plane requires a higher engine output to maintain the same power loading as a lighter one.
Designers calculated the power loading of the aircraft to optimize fuel efficiency and climb rate.
Modern fighter jets often have a low power loading to ensure high acceleration and maneuverability.

  • synonym – thrust-to-weight ratio: the ratio of thrust produced by an aircraft to its weight
