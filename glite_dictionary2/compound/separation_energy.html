Pronunciation: / ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the amount of energy required to remove a particle from an atom, molecule, or nucleus; equivalent to the mass defect.
energy needed to remove particle from atom/nucleus

Examples

A low separation energy often suggests that a nucleus is close to radioactive decay.
Scientists calculated the separation energy to better understand nuclear stability.
The separation energy of a neutron from this isotope is particularly high.
