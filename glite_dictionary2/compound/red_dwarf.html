NOUN
Pronunciation: / rɛd ˈdwɔrf / (AmE), / rɛd ˈdwɔːf / (BrE)
A small, relatively cool, and long-lived star of low mass, typically much less bright than the Sun.
small, cool, low-mass star

Examples

A red dwarf can live for trillions of years, far longer than more massive stars.
Astronomers believe that a red dwarf could have planets in its habitable zone.
The Sun is much larger and brighter than a typical red dwarf.
