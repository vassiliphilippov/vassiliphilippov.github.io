NOUN
Pronunciation: / sɪmˌpæθətɪk ˈnɜrvəs ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / sɪmˌpæθətɪk ˈnɜːvəs ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the part of the autonomic nervous system that originates in the thoracic regions of the spinal cord and generally prepares the body for action by increasing heart rate, reducing digestive activity, and contracting blood vessels
part of the autonomic nervous system that prepares the body for action

Examples

Activation of the sympathetic nervous system increases heart rate and redirects blood to major muscle groups.
During intense exercise, your sympathetic nervous system works to prepare your body for increased activity.
The sympathetic nervous system is responsible for the body's fight-or-flight response in stressful situations.
