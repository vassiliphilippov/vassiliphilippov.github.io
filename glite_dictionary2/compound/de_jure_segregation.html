1. ⚖️ legal separation ("de jure segregation in schools")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / deɪ ˈdʒʊri ˌsɛɡ.rɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / deɪ ˈdʒʊə.ri ˌsɛɡ.rɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
Segregation that exists or is maintained by legal requirements or government action, rather than by societal custom.
segregation by law

Examples

During the civil rights era, Southern states had de jure segregation in schools and public facilities.
The Supreme Court declared de jure segregation unconstitutional in the landmark Brown v. Board of Education decision.
Unlike de jure segregation, de facto segregation arises from societal patterns rather than explicit laws.

Related Terms

  • antonym – de facto segregation: racial or other segregation that occurs in practice but not by legal requirement
