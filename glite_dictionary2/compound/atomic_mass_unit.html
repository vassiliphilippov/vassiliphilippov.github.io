1. ⚖️ mass measure ("one atomic mass unit of carbon")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / əˈtɑmɪk ˈmæs ˌjunɪt / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈmæs ˌjuːnɪt / (BrE)
A unit of mass used to express the masses of atoms, molecules, and subatomic particles, equal to one twelfth the mass of a carbon-12 atom.
unit of mass for atoms or molecules

Examples

A water molecule weighs about eighteen atomic mass units.
Scientists use the atomic mass unit to measure very small particles.
The mass of a proton is approximately one atomic mass unit.
