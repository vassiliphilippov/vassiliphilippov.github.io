1. ⚖️ legal right to lawyer ("the right to an attorney in court")

Pronunciation: / raɪt tu ən əˈtɝːni / (AmE), / raɪt tə ən əˈtɜːni / (BrE)
The legal right of a person accused of a crime to have a lawyer represent them, including state-provided counsel if they cannot afford one, as guaranteed by the Sixth Amendment to the US Constitution.
legal right to have a lawyer provided in court

Examples

Because she could not afford legal representation, the court informed her of her right to an attorney.
Every suspect has the right to an attorney before police questioning in the United States.
The judge reminded the defendant of his right to an attorney during the arraignment.
