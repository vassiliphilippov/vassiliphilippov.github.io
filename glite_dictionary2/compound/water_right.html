1. ⚖️ legal water use ("water right for irrigation")

Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər raɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə raɪt / (BrE)
a legal right to use or access water from a particular source, such as a river, lake, or groundwater
legal right to use water

Examples

Farmers in the region have water rights that date back over a century.
The dispute was settled by granting water rights to the landowner.
Without a water right, it is illegal to divert water from the river for irrigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – riparian right: a legal right to use water for those whose land borders a river or stream
