1. ⚡ energy unit ("one electron volt energy")

Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛk.trɑn voʊlt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɒn vəʊlt / (BrE)
a unit of energy equal to the work done by an electron moving through an electric potential difference of one volt, commonly used in physics.
unit of energy (physics)

Examples

In particle physics experiments, energies are often measured in electron volts rather than joules.
One electron volt is a tiny quantity compared to everyday energy units, but it is useful at atomic scales.
The energy of a single photon of visible light is typically around 2 to 3 electron volts.
