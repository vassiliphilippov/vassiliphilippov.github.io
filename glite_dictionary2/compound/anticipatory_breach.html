1. ⚖️ early contract breaking ("anticipatory breach of contract")

Pronunciation: / ænˌtɪsɪˌpeɪtəri ˈbriːtʃ / (AmE), / ænˌtɪsɪˌpeɪtəri ˈbriːtʃ / (BrE)
In law, a breach of contract that occurs when one party indicates, before the time required for performance, that they will not fulfill their obligations.
legal: failure to perform contract before due

Examples

Legal action was taken because the contractor committed an anticipatory breach before work even began.
The company accused the supplier of an anticipatory breach when they announced they could not deliver the goods on time.
With evidence of an anticipatory breach, the other party may immediately seek damages or cancel the agreement.
