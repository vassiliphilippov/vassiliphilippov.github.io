NOUN
Pronunciation: / bæd tʃɛk / (AmE), / bæd tʃɛk / (BrE)
A cheque that is refused by a bank because the account it is drawn on does not have enough funds to cover it.
a cheque with insufficient funds

Examples

Banks charge a penalty if you deposit a bad cheque.
He was arrested for writing a bad cheque at the grocery store.
Passing a bad cheque can be considered a criminal offense in many countries.
