1. ⚖️ legal deception ("constructive fraud in contracts")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌktɪv frɔd / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪv frɔːd / (BrE)
an act or omission that, while not actually intended to deceive, breaches a legal or equitable duty, trust, or confidence, resulting in misleading another person
deception by breach of duty or trust (legal)

Examples

Even if there was no intent to deceive, a breach of fiduciary duty can amount to constructive fraud.
The company was found liable for constructive fraud due to its failure to disclose important information to investors.
The court ruled that the seller's actions constituted constructive fraud, awarding damages to the buyer.
