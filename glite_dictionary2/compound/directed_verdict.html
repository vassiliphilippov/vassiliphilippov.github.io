1. ⚖️ judge's decision ("judge orders directed verdict")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛktɪd ˈvɜrdɪkt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktɪd ˈvɜːdɪkt / (BrE)
a verdict in a jury trial that is ordered by the judge without being considered by the jury, typically because there is insufficient evidence for the case to go to the jury
verdict ordered by a judge, not the jury

Examples

A directed verdict is rare in criminal trials because questions of guilt are usually left for the jury.
After the prosecution's case fell apart, the defense requested a directed verdict.
The judge granted a directed verdict in favor of the defendant due to lack of evidence.
