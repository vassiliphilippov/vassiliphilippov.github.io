1. ✊ work protest ("sit-down strike at factory")

Pronunciation: / ˈsɪt daʊn straɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪt daʊn straɪk / (BrE)
A form of protest in which workers remain at their place of work and refuse to leave or continue working until their demands are met or a settlement is reached.
strike where workers stay in workplace

Examples

During the 1930s, many factories experienced sit-down strikes by organized labor.
Management was forced to negotiate after the sit-down strike brought production to a halt.
The workers launched a sit-down strike to protest unsafe working conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – strike: refusal to work as a form of protest, usually by a group of workers
