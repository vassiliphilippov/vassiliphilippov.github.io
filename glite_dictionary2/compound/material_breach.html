1. ⚖️ serious contract violation ("material breach of contract")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / məˈtɪriəl briːtʃ / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəl briːtʃ / (BrE)
A serious violation of a contract that significantly harms its purpose and gives legal grounds for ending the contract or seeking damages.
serious violation of a contract

Examples

A material breach allows the non-breaching party to terminate the agreement and seek compensation.
Missing a minor deadline is usually not considered a material breach unless it severely affects the deal.
The court found that the company’s failure to deliver on time was a material breach of the contract.
