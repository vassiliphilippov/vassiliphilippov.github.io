algebraic number

1➗math root mathan algebraic number is a root

1. ➗ math root ("an algebraic number is a root")

Pronunciation: / ˌæl.dʒəˈbreɪ.ɪk ˈnʌm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˌæl.dʒəˈbreɪ.ɪk ˈnʌm.bə / (BrE)
a complex number that is a root of a non-zero polynomial equation with rational coefficients
complex number that solves a rational-coefficient polynomial

Examples

Every integer is an algebraic number because it is a root of a simple polynomial equation.
The square root of 2 is an algebraic number, as it satisfies the equation x² - 2 = 0.
Unlike π, which is transcendental, the cube root of 8 is an algebraic number.

Related Terms

  • antonym – transcendental number: a number that is not algebraic, i.e., not a root of any polynomial with rational coefficients
