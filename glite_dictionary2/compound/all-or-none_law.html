1. ⚡ nerve fires fully ("all-or-none law in nerve response")

Pronunciation: / ˌɔl ɔr ˈnʌn lɔ / (AmE), / ˌɔːl ɔː ˈnʌn lɔː / (BrE)
a principle in neurophysiology stating that the response of a nerve or muscle to a stimulus is always the same size, regardless of the strength of the stimulus, provided it exceeds the threshold
nerve or muscle response principle

Examples

According to the all-or-none law, a neuron either fires completely or not at all.
Biology students must understand the all-or-none law when learning about how nerves and muscles respond to stimuli.
The all-or-none law explains why increasing the intensity of a stimulus does not increase the strength of a nerve impulse.
