1. ➗ not exact divisor ("aliquant part of a number")

Pronunciation: / ˈælɪˌkwɑnt pɑrt / (AmE), / ˈælɪkwənt pɑːt / (BrE)
A number that divides another without leaving an integer quotient; an integer that is not an exact divisor of a specified quantity.
integer not an exact divisor

Examples

In the fraction 3/8, the numerator 3 is an aliquant part of the denominator 8.
Seven is an aliquant part of twenty, as it does not divide it evenly.
Understanding the difference between an aliquot and an aliquant part is useful in number theory.

Related Terms
