1. ⚙️ power wheel ("a tread-wheel for lifting")

Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛdˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛdˌwiːl / (BrE)
A large wheel turned by people or animals walking inside or upon it, used to power machinery such as mills.
wheel powered by walking for machinery

Examples

In medieval times, a tread-wheel was often used to raise heavy loads in construction.
The workers powered the mill by walking inside a massive tread-wheel.
Unlike modern engines, the tread-wheel relied entirely on human or animal strength.
