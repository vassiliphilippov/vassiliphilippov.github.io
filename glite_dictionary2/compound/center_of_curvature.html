Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnɾɚ əv kɚˈveɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə əv kəˈvɜːtʃə / (BrE)
The center of the circle whose arc best approximates a curve at a particular point.
point center of circle best fitting a curve at a point

Examples

At each point on a curve, there is a unique center of curvature.
The center of curvature lies on the concave side of the curve.
The center of curvature of a mirror determines how it focuses light.
