1. ⬆️ middle form ("comparative degree of adjectives")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpær.ə.t̬ɪv dɪˈɡri / (AmE), / kəmˈpær.ə.tɪv dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
the form of an adjective or adverb used to compare two people or things, expressing a higher degree of the quality than the positive form but less than the superlative.
grammar term: higher comparison of adjectives or adverbs

Examples

In English, the comparative degree of 'happy' is 'happier.'
Students often confuse the comparative degree with the superlative when learning English.
The teacher explained how to form the comparative degree using 'more' for longer adjectives.
