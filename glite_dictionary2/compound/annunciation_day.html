NOUN
Pronunciation: / əˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən deɪ / (AmE), / əˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən deɪ / (BrE)
A Christian festival commemorating the announcement by the angel Gabriel to the Virgin Mary that she would conceive Jesus; observed on March 25, and historically one of the quarter days in England, Wales, and Ireland.
Christian festival marking Gabriel's message to Mary

Examples

In medieval England, Annunciation Day marked one of the traditional quarter days for settling rents and accounts.
Many churches hold special services on Annunciation Day to commemorate the angel Gabriel's message to Mary.
She read a poem each year in honor of Annunciation Day and its meaning in the Christian calendar.
