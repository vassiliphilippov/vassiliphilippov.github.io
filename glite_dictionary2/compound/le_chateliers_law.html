1. ⚖️ equilibrium change ("Le Chatelier's law in chemical reactions")

Pronunciation: / lə ʃæˈtɛlɪ.eɪz lɔ / (AmE), / lə ʃaˈtɛlɪeɪz lɔː / (BrE)
The principle stating that if a system at equilibrium experiences a change in concentration, temperature, pressure, or volume, the system will adjust to partially counteract the change and restore a new equilibrium.
principle in chemistry about equilibrium changes

Examples

According to Le Chatelier's law, increasing the temperature will shift the equilibrium to favor the endothermic reaction.
In industrial chemistry, Le Chatelier's law helps engineers maximize product yield by adjusting reaction conditions.
Students often use Le Chatelier's law to predict the effects of adding more reactants to a system.
