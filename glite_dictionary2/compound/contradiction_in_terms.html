NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑntrəˈdɪkʃən ɪn tɝmz / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkʃən ɪn tɜːmz / (BrE)
a statement or expression that contains elements which are logically inconsistent or mutually exclusive, making the statement necessarily false or absurd
logically inconsistent statement or expression

Examples

He argued that the idea of 'a true fake' is simply a contradiction in terms.
Saying someone is 'a married bachelor' is a classic contradiction in terms.
The phrase 'deafening silence' is not a contradiction in terms, but rather an oxymoron.
