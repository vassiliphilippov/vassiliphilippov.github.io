Pronunciation: / ˈfiːldɪŋ ˈævərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfiːldɪŋ ˈævərɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a statistic that measures a fielder's performance, calculated by dividing the sum of assists and putouts by the total number of chances.
baseball measure of a fielder's performance

Examples

His fielding average improved significantly during the second half of the season.
The coach compared the fielding averages of all outfielders to decide the starting lineup.
With a perfect fielding average, the shortstop did not commit a single error all year.
