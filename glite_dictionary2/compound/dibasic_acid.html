1. ⚗️ two-hydrogen acid ("a dibasic acid molecule")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / daɪˈbeɪsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / daɪˈbeɪsɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a chemical acid that contains two replaceable hydrogen atoms per molecule, enabling it to form two salts with bases
acid with two replaceable hydrogens

Examples

Phosphoric acid is another common example of a dibasic acid in chemistry.
Sulfuric acid is a dibasic acid because it can donate two protons per molecule.
When titrating a dibasic acid, two equivalence points are observed.
