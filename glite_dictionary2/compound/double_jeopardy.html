1. ⚖️ second trial ban ("double jeopardy protection")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl ˈdʒɛpɚdi / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈdʒɛpədi / (BrE)
The prosecution of a person twice for the same criminal offense, prohibited by law in many legal systems.
prosecution for the same offense twice

Examples

Concerns about double jeopardy were raised when the prosecutor filed new charges for the same incident.
The defendant was protected from double jeopardy after being acquitted of the crime.
Under the U.S. Constitution, double jeopardy prevents the state from prosecuting someone twice for the same offense.
