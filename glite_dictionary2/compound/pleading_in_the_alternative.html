1. ⚖️ multiple legal claims ("pleading in the alternative by a lawyer")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈplidɪŋ ɪn ði ˈɔltərˌnətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpliːdɪŋ ɪn ði ˈɔːltəˌnətɪv / (BrE)
a legal pleading in which a party presents multiple, distinct sets of facts or claims, leaving it unclear which one will ultimately be relied upon
legal pleading with multiple alternative claims or facts

Examples

In complex cases, lawyers often use pleading in the alternative to cover all possible legal grounds.
The defendant's pleading in the alternative allowed them to argue either an alibi or self-defense depending on the evidence.
The judge noted that the pleading in the alternative did not make the plaintiff’s actual position clear.
