1. ⚖️ legal reason ("probable cause for arrest")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːbəbəl kɔz / (AmE), / ˈprɒbəbəl kɔːz / (BrE)
(law) Reasonable grounds for believing that a crime has been committed or that evidence of a crime can be found, sufficient to justify an arrest, search, or seizure.
legal grounds for arrest/search

Examples

Her lawyer argued that there was no probable cause for the arrest, so the charges should be dropped.
The judge determined that probable cause existed, allowing the trial to proceed.
The police needed probable cause before they could obtain a search warrant for the property.
