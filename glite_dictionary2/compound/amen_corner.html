1. ⛪ church group ("the amen corner singers")

Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ.mɛn ˈkɔːr.nər / (AmE), / ˈɑː.mɛn ˈkɔː.nə / (BrE)
An area in a church reserved for people, often a group, who lead or are especially vocal in the responsive 'amens' during a service.
area in church for leading 'amens'

Examples

Some congregants prefer to join the amen corner where the spirit is lively and the amens are loud.
The choir sat just beside the amen corner, filling the church with their energetic responses.
Traditionally, the amen corner was reserved for church elders who led the responses during worship.
