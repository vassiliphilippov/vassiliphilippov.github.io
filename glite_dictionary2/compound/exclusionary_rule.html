1. ⚖️ law evidence ban ("exclusionary rule blocks evidence")

Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskluːʒəˌnɛri ruːl / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːʒənəri ruːl / (BrE)
A legal rule that prohibits the use of evidence obtained through illegal police conduct from being admitted in a criminal trial.
legal rule excluding illegally obtained evidence

Examples

Law students often debate whether the exclusionary rule helps or hinders the pursuit of justice.
Thanks to the exclusionary rule, evidence obtained through unconstitutional searches was thrown out of the case.
The judge applied the exclusionary rule to prevent the jury from seeing evidence seized without a warrant.
