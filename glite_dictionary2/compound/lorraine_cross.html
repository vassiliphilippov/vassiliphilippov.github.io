NOUN
Pronunciation: / ləˈreɪn krɑs / (AmE), / lɒˈreɪn krɒs / (BrE)
a cross with two horizontal bars, the upper shorter than the lower, commonly associated with the region of Lorraine and used as a symbol of resistance or honor
a cross with two horizontal bars; symbol of Lorraine

The Lorraine cross became a symbol of the Free French Forces during World War II.
