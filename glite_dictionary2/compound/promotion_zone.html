1. ♟️ chess upgrade area ("promotion zone in chess")

Pronunciation: / prəˈmoʊʃən zoʊn / (AmE), / prəˈməʊʃən zəʊn / (BrE)
In chess and some other board games, the area of the board where a pawn or certain piece is eligible to be promoted to another piece.
chess area allowing piece promotion

Examples

If a pawn reaches the promotion zone, it can be exchanged for a queen, rook, bishop, or knight.
In Shogi, the rules for entering the promotion zone differ from those in Western chess.
Players must strategize to move their pawns safely into the promotion zone before promoting them.
