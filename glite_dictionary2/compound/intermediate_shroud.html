NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˈmid.i.ɪt ˈʃraʊd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiət ˈʃraʊd / (BrE)
A wire or rope support fitted to the mast of a sailing vessel with multiple spreaders, located between the upper and lower shrouds to provide additional stability.
mast support on rigs with multiple spreaders

Examples

The broken intermediate shroud was quickly replaced by the experienced crew.
The yacht's intermediate shroud needed tightening after a long day of sailing.
To withstand high winds, the rigging included an extra intermediate shroud on each side of the mast.
