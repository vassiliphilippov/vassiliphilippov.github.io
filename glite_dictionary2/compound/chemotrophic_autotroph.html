NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌkiːmoʊˈtroʊfɪk ˈɔːtəˌtroʊf / (AmE), / ˌkɛməˈtrəʊfɪk ˈɔːtəˌtrɒf / (BrE)
An organism that obtains its energy from chemical reactions and uses carbon dioxide as its main source of carbon.
organism using chemicals for energy and CO₂ for carbon

Examples

In deep-sea vents, chemotrophic autotrophs form the base of the ecosystem.
Some bacteria are classified as chemotrophic autotrophs because they utilize inorganic chemicals for energy.
Unlike plants, chemotrophic autotrophs do not rely on sunlight for growth.
