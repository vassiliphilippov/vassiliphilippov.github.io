1. ⚓ boat anchor ("mushroom anchor for mooring boats")

Pronunciation: / ˈmʌʃruːm ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃrəm ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
An anchor with a bowl-shaped head, used especially for semipermanent moorings, that digs into the seabed regardless of how it lands.
bowl-shaped anchor for moorings

Examples

A mushroom anchor is ideal for mooring buoys because it holds well in soft mud.
The marina recommended we use a mushroom anchor for the floating dock.
They replaced their old anchor with a larger mushroom anchor to secure the pontoon.
