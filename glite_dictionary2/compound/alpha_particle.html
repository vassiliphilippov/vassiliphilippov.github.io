1. ☢️ helium nucleus ("radioactive alpha particle")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈælfə ˌpɑrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈælfə ˌpɑːtɪkl / (BrE)
a positively charged particle consisting of two protons and two neutrons, identical to the nucleus of a helium atom, emitted during certain types of radioactive decay
helium nucleus emitted during radioactive decay

Examples

Because an alpha particle is relatively heavy, it can be stopped by a sheet of paper.
Rutherford's famous experiment involved firing an alpha particle beam at a thin sheet of gold.
The uranium sample emitted an alpha particle during its radioactive decay process.
