magic number

1⚛️stable nucleus physicsmagic number of protons

1. ⚛️ stable nucleus ("magic number of protons")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈmædʒɪk ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈmædʒɪk ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a number of nucleons (protons or neutrons) at which an atomic nucleus is especially stable: 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, or 126
number marking extra stability in atomic nuclei

Examples

Nuclei with a magic number of protons or neutrons are exceptionally stable against radioactive decay.
Scientists discovered that lead-208 is doubly magic because both its protons and neutrons match a magic number.
The concept of the magic number has helped explain why some elements have unusually high natural abundances.
