1. ⚡ unbound electron ("free electron in metal")

Pronunciation: / ˌfri ɪˈlɛk.trɑn / (AmE), / ˌfriː ɪˈlɛk.trɒn / (BrE); / ˌfri ɪˈlɛktrən / (AmE), / ˌfriː ɪˈlɛktrɒn / (BrE)
an electron that is not attached to an atom, ion, or molecule and can move freely, especially in a conductor or under an electric field
electron not bound to an atom / electron not bound to atoms, free to move

Examples

In metals, a free electron can carry electric current efficiently.
The movement of each free electron contributes to the material’s overall conductivity.
When exposed to intense energy, one atom may release a free electron.
Copper's excellent conductivity is due to the presence of many free electrons in its structure.
In physics, the term free electron often refers to those in metals that are responsible for conductivity.
When an external electric field is applied, free electrons flow and create an electric current.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bound electron: an electron that is attached to an atom, ion, or molecule and cannot move freely
  • synonym – valence electron: an electron involved in chemical bonding, often less tightly bound to atoms
