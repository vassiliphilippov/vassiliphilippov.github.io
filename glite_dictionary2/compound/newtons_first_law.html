1. ⚖️ motion stays same ("Newton's first law of motion")

Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtənz ˈfɜrst lɔ / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtənz ˈfɜːst lɔː / (BrE)
The scientific principle stating that an object will remain at rest or continue moving at a constant velocity unless acted upon by an external force.
law of inertia in physics

Examples

Newton's first law explains why passengers lurch forward when a car stops suddenly.
Many high school students learn about Newton's first law in their introductory physics courses.
The tendency of a ball to keep rolling until friction slows it down is described by Newton's first law.
