1. ⚖️ court-ordered action ("court orders specific performance")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / spəˈsɪfɪk pərˈfɔrməns / (AmE), / spəˈsɪfɪk pəˈfɔːməns / (BrE)
the legal requirement for a party to carry out the precise terms of a contract, as ordered by a court
fulfillment of contract as ordered by a court

Examples

In cases involving rare goods, plaintiffs often seek specific performance instead of monetary compensation.
The court ordered specific performance when the builder refused to complete the house.
The judge ruled that only specific performance could provide a fair remedy in this contract dispute.
