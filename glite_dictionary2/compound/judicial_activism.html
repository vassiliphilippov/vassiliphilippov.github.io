1. ⚖️ judges change law ("judicial activism in court decisions")

Pronunciation: / dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˈæktɪvɪzəm / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˈæktɪvɪzəm / (BrE); / dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˈæktɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˈæktɪvɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or philosophy of judges making decisions that are influenced by social, political, or personal considerations, rather than strictly following the law
allowing current needs to influence judicial rulings / courts influencing policy through rulings

Examples

Accusations of judicial activism often arise during controversial court cases involving constitutional rights.
Many legal scholars debate whether judicial activism undermines or strengthens democracy.
The Supreme Court was criticized for its judicial activism in overturning longstanding precedents.
Many scholars debate whether judicial activism enhances or undermines democracy.
Public reaction to judicial activism can vary depending on the outcome of the case.
