NOUN
Pronunciation: / skɪn ˈsɛnsɪz / (AmE), / skɪn ˈsɛnsɪz / (BrE)
the faculties by which external objects or forces are perceived through contact with the body, especially via the skin (such as touch, pressure, temperature, and pain)
faculties for perceiving stimuli through the skin

Examples

Children develop awareness of their skin senses as they explore different textures with their hands.
Damage to nerve endings can impair the skin senses and reduce sensitivity to pain.
The skin senses allow us to detect warmth, cold, and pressure through physical contact.
