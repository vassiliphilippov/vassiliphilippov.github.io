NOUN
Pronunciation: / bɪˈtuːmɪnəs koʊl / (AmE), / bɪˈtjuːmɪnəs kəʊl / (BrE)
A type of coal that contains high levels of tarry hydrocarbons, burning readily with a smoky, yellow flame.
type of coal rich in hydrocarbons

Examples

Many industrial furnaces rely on bituminous coal due to its high heat content.
The power plant switched from bituminous coal to cleaner forms of energy to reduce pollution.
When burned, bituminous coal produces a noticeable, smoky yellow flame.
