A form of diabetes insipidus in which the kidneys fail to respond to normal levels of vasopressin, resulting in excessive urination and thirst.
kidney-related diabetes insipidus

Examples

Children with nephrogenic diabetes insipidus may present symptoms such as excessive thirst and frequent urination from an early age.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with nephrogenic diabetes insipidus after confirming the kidneys were unresponsive to vasopressin.
Unlike other types of diabetes insipidus, nephrogenic diabetes insipidus does not improve with hormone therapy alone.
