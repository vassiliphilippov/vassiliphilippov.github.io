In scholastic philosophy, any of the four cardinal virtues (prudence, justice, fortitude, and temperance) considered to arise from human nature, rather than from divine grace.
a cardinal virtue arising from human nature

Examples

In medieval philosophy, prudence was regarded as a natural virtue accessible to all rational beings.
Justice, as a natural virtue, is held to be achievable through human reason alone.
The distinction between natural virtue and theological virtue is a key point in Catholic theology.
