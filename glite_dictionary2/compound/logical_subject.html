In grammar and logic, the subject of a sentence that expresses the actual agent of an action or is the entity about which something is predicated, regardless of its grammatical placement.
the real agent or topic in a sentence, not always the grammatical subject

Examples

In some passive sentences, the logical subject is not explicitly stated.
Philosophers often distinguish between the grammatical and the logical subject of a proposition.
The logical subject in this sentence is 'your father', even though 'it' is the grammatical subject.
