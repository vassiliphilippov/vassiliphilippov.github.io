1. ⚖️ molecule mass ("molecular weight of water")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / məˈlɛkjələr weɪt / (AmE), / məˈlɛkjʊlə weɪt / (BrE)
The sum of the relative atomic masses of the atoms in a molecule, used as a measure of its mass.
mass of a molecule in atomic mass units

Examples

Knowing the molecular weight of a compound is essential for many chemical calculations.
Researchers compared the molecular weight of different proteins to analyze their sizes.
The molecular weight of water is approximately 18 grams per mole.
