1. ➕ math group ("commutative group in algebra")

Pronunciation: / kəˈmjuːtəˌtɪv ɡruːp / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːtətɪv ɡruːp / (BrE)
A group in which the group operation satisfies the commutative law, meaning the order of operation does not affect the result.
mathematics: group with commutative operation

Examples

Every commutative group is also called an abelian group.
In a commutative group, a + b is always equal to b + a.
The set of integers with addition forms a classic example of a commutative group.
