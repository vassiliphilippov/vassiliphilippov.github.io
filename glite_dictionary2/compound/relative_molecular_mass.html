(Chemistry) The sum of the relative atomic masses of the atoms in a molecule, compared to one-twelfth the mass of carbon-12.
sum of atomic masses in a molecule

Examples

Knowing the relative molecular mass is essential for determining the amount of substance in chemical reactions.
Scientists calculate the relative molecular mass by adding together the atomic masses of each atom in the molecule.
The relative molecular mass of water is approximately 18.
