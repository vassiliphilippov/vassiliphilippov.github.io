NOUN

The group of Christian churches that developed from the Byzantine tradition, adhering to Eastern rites and theology, and not accepting the authority of the Pope.
group of Christian churches of Byzantine tradition

Examples

He joined the Eastern Orthodox Church after learning more about its traditions.
The Eastern Orthodox Church celebrates Easter on a different date from the Western churches.
The Eastern Orthodox Church has played a major role in the history of Eastern Europe.
