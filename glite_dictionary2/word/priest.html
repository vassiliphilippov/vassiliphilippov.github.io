1. ⛪ religious leader ("Catholic priest")

Pronunciation: / priːst / (AmE), / priːst / (BrE); / prist / (AmE), / priːst / (BrE)
a person who is authorized to perform religious ceremonies and duties, especially in a church or other religious tradition
ordained Christian minister / religious official (non-Christian)

Examples

In many traditions, a priest wears special robes when conducting religious rites.
She spoke to a priest about arranging her wedding ceremony at the church.
The priest led the congregation in prayer during the Sunday service.
A Shinto priest performed the ritual purification before the festival.
The Hindu priest led the temple ceremony at dawn.
The village priest played a central role in organizing traditional rites.

Related Terms

  • antonym – laity: Non-ordained members of a religious community
  • synonym – minister: A member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches
  • synonym – priestess: female priest in some religions
