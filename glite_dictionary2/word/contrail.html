1. ✈️ plane cloud ("contrail behind a plane")

Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːnˌtreɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒnˌtreɪl / (BrE)
A line-shaped cloud formed by an aircraft at high altitude, produced by condensation of water vapor in the engine exhaust or changes in air pressure.
line-shaped cloud left by an aircraft

Examples

On cold days, contrails can linger for hours and gradually spread out.
Some people mistakenly believe that contrails are evidence of secret government activities.
The airplane left a long, white contrail stretching across the clear blue sky.
