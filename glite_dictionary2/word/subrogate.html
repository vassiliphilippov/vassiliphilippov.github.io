1. ⚖️ to transfer rights ("to subrogate an insurance claim")

VERB
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbrəˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to substitute one person or entity for another in a legal claim or right, especially when an insurance company assumes the rights of the insured to pursue recovery from a third party
substitute one party for another in legal rights

Examples

After paying the claim, the lender was subrogated to the homeowner's rights against the contractor.
Once the debt is paid, a surety can subrogate and seek reimbursement from the principal debtor.
The insurance company may subrogate against the driver who caused the accident.
