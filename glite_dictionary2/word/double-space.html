1. ↕️ to add extra line ("to double-space a document")

VERB
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl speɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl speɪs / (BrE)
to type or format text so that there is a full blank line between each line of text
to type text with a blank line between each line

Examples

Be sure to double-space all references in your bibliography.
She accidentally double-spaced the entire document instead of just the introduction.
The professor asked us to double-space our essays for easier reading.
