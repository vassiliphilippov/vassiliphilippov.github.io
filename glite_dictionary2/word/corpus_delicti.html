1. ⚖️ crime evidence ("corpus delicti in a trial")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːrpəs dɪˈlɪktaɪ / (AmE), / ˌkɔːpəs dɪˈlɪkti / (BrE)
the facts and evidence proving that a crime has been committed, regarded as essential before someone can be convicted; not limited to a physical body in homicide cases
legal evidence proving a crime

Examples

Many people wrongly assume that corpus delicti refers only to a murder victim's body.
The prosecutor was unable to establish the corpus delicti, so the charges were dropped.
Without the corpus delicti, it is impossible to convict someone of the alleged offense.
