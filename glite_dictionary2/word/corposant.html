1. ⚡ glowing light ("corposant on a mast")

Pronunciation: / ˈkɔr.poʊˌsænt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pəʊˌsænt / (BrE)
an electrical discharge, often seen as a glowing light on pointed objects such as ship masts, caused by ionization of the atmosphere; also known as St. Elmo's fire
atmospheric electrical discharge (St. Elmo's fire)

Examples

As the storm approached, a blue corposant glowed eerily on the ship’s mast.
Sailors once believed a corposant was a sign of the saint’s protection during storms.
The phenomenon of corposant is most likely to appear during intense electrical weather at sea.
