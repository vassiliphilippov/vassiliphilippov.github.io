1. ☠️ toxic mine gas ("dangerous afterdamp in mine")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈæftərˌdæmp / (AmE), / ˈɑːftədæmp / (BrE)
A toxic mixture of gases, mainly carbon dioxide, carbon monoxide, and nitrogen, that remains in a mine after a firedamp explosion.
mine gas mixture after explosion

Examples

Rescue workers wore breathing apparatus to avoid inhaling afterdamp.
Tests showed that dangerous levels of afterdamp remained in the shaft.
The miners were at risk from afterdamp following the explosion.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – blackdamp: a suffocating mixture of gases, mainly nitrogen and carbon dioxide, in mines
  • synonym – firedamp: a mixture of gases (mainly methane) found in coal mines
