1. ⚡ Greek god ("Cronus overthrows Uranus")

Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.nəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the leader and youngest of the first generation of Titans, who ruled the universe until he was overthrown by his son Zeus; identified with the Roman god Saturn.
Titan and supreme god in Greek mythology

Examples

In Roman mythology, Cronus is commonly identified with the god Saturn.
In ancient tales, Cronus was said to have swallowed his own children to prevent being overthrown.
The statue in the museum depicts Cronus holding a sickle, his traditional symbol.
