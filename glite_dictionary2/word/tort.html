1. ⚖️ legal wrong ("civil tort case")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / tɔrt / (AmE), / tɔːt / (BrE)
A wrongful act or an infringement of a right (other than under contract) leading to civil legal liability.
legal wrong leading to civil liability

Examples

Many civil cases arise when one party claims that a tort has been committed by another.
She brought a lawsuit alleging a tort after suffering injuries in the accident.
The company was found liable for the tort committed against its former employee.

Related Terms

  • synonym – civil wrong: a non-criminal wrongful act that results in injury or harm
  • antonym – crime: an action or omission that constitutes an offense punishable by law
