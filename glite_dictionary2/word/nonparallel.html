1. ↗️ not parallel ("nonparallel lines")

ADJ
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈpærəˌlɛl / (AmE), / nɒnˈpærəlɛl / (BrE)
(of lines, paths, or surfaces) not parallel; tending to meet or diverge.
not parallel; converging or diverging

Examples

The artist drew nonparallel lines to create a sense of movement in the composition.
The train tracks are intentionally designed to be nonparallel where they approach the junction.
Two nonparallel roads eventually cross each other in the center of town.

Related Terms

  • antonym – parallel: (of lines, surfaces, or objects) side by side and having the same distance continuously between them

2. ⏳ not simultaneous ("nonparallel processing")

ADJ
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈpɛrəˌlɛl / (AmE), / nɒnˈparəlɛl / (BrE)
relating to the sequential processing of operations rather than their simultaneous execution, especially in computing.
not involving simultaneous processing

Examples

Many legacy systems were designed for nonparallel processing, resulting in slower performance.
Switching from a nonparallel to a parallel model significantly improved processing speeds.
The program used a nonparallel approach, handling each request one after the other.

Related Terms

  • antonym – parallel: relating to the simultaneous performance of multiple operations
