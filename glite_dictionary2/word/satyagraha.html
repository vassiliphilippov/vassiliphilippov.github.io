1. ✊ nonviolent protest ("Gandhi's Satyagraha movement")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑːtjəˈɡrɑːhə / (AmE), / ˌsætjəˈɡrɑːhə / (BrE)
A policy of nonviolent resistance, especially as initiated by Mahatma Gandhi in India against British rule.
Gandhian nonviolent resistance movement

Examples

Mahatma Gandhi used Satyagraha as a powerful tool in the fight for India's independence.
Martin Luther King Jr. was inspired by the principles of Satyagraha in the American civil rights movement.
The strategy of Satyagraha emphasized nonviolent protest and moral persuasion over confrontation.
