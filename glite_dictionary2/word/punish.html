1. ⚖️ to give penalty ("to punish a child")

Pronunciation: / ˈpʌnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpʌnɪʃ / (BrE)
to make someone suffer because they have done something wrong or broken a rule
to impose a penalty for wrongdoing

Examples

If you break the rules, the school will punish you with detention.
Parents sometimes punish their children by taking away privileges.
The judge decided not to punish him too harshly for the mistake.
