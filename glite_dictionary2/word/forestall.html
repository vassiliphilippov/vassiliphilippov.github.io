1. ⛔ to prevent early ("to forestall a problem")

Pronunciation: / fɔrˈstɔl / (AmE), / fɔːˈstɔːl / (BrE)
to act early in order to prevent or stop something from happening
prevent in advance / prevent or deal with in advance

Examples

By warning her colleagues early, she was able to forestall any major objections to the proposal.
Proper maintenance can forestall many common car problems before they become serious.
The government acted quickly to forestall a banking crisis.
He took quick action to forestall any potential objections from the committee.
The government introduced new regulations to forestall a financial crisis.
They tried to forestall criticism by explaining their decision early.

Related Terms

  • antonym – allow: to permit or make possible
  • synonym – anticipate: to regard as probable or expect and prepare for
  • synonym – preclude: to make impossible, especially by taking action in advance
  • synonym – prevent: to stop something from happening
