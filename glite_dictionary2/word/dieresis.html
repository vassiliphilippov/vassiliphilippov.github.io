1. ¨ two-dot mark ("dieresis mark over vowels")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɛrəsɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈɪərɪsɪs / (BrE)
A diacritical mark (¨) placed over a vowel, especially to indicate that it should be pronounced separately from an adjacent vowel rather than forming a diphthong.
diacritical mark (¨) over vowels

Examples

A dieresis distinguishes the pronunciation of vowels in words like 'coöperate'.
In older editions, the publisher used a dieresis to clarify the pronunciation of certain words.
The word 'naïve' is often written with a dieresis over the 'i'.
