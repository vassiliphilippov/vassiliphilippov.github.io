1. ⚖️ legal priority ("federal preemption rule")

Pronunciation: / priˈɛmpʃən / (AmE), / priˈɛmpʃən / (BrE)
the legal principle that federal law takes precedence over state law when both address the same subject
legal principle: federal law overrides state law

Examples

The court cited the principle of preemption when overturning the state statute.
The doctrine of preemption prevented the state from enforcing its own regulations in that area.
Under preemption, state laws that conflict with federal rules may be rendered invalid.

2. ⏩ prior right ("preemption right purchase")

Pronunciation: / priˈɛm(p)ʃən / (AmE), / priˈɛm(p)ʃən / (BrE); / priˈɛmpʃən / (AmE), / priːˈɛmpʃən / (BrE)
the act or right of taking, acquiring, or purchasing something before others, especially as a prior right or intervention
government's right to seize or acquire property / prior appropriation; act of taking priority / right to purchase before others

Examples

Critics argue that the preemption of public space by private events reduces community access.
Rapid preemption of fresh water sources can lead to conflicts between neighboring countries.
The company's preemption of the market left little opportunity for competitors.
She exercised her preemption to buy the land before it was put on the open market.
The contract granted the company preemption on all mineral rights in the area.
Under the agreement, we have preemption for any resale of their shares.
Laws regarding preemption can override property rights in certain circumstances.
The government exercised its preemption to acquire land for the new highway.
Through preemption, a state may seize assets deemed necessary for public use.
