1. ✝️ to make Christian ("to Christianize a country")

Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstʃəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃənaɪz / (BrE)
to convert a person, group, or place to Christianity, or to make something Christian in character or influence
convert to Christianity / make or adapt to Christianity

Examples

Missionaries aimed to Christianize remote villages by building churches and schools.
Some rulers sought to Christianize pagan festivals and rituals in their domains.
There were efforts to Christianize the indigenous population during the colonial period.
During the 16th century, missionaries sought to Christianize the indigenous peoples of South America.
Many schools in the region were established to Christianize local children through religious education.
The old festival was eventually Christianized and became known as All Saints' Day.
