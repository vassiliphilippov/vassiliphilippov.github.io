1. ⛪ Catholic pope ("Pope Paul VI")

Pronunciation: / pɔl sɪksθ / (AmE), / pɔːl sɪksθ / (BrE)
(1897–1978) The pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 1963 to 1978, known for implementing reforms initiated by the Second Vatican Council, and for easing restrictions on fasting and interfaith marriages.
Pope of the Roman Catholic Church, 1963–1978

Examples

As pope, Paul VI sought to build bridges between different religious traditions.
In 1965, Paul VI became the first pope to visit the United States.
Many of the changes to the Mass were put into place by Paul VI after the Second Vatican Council.
