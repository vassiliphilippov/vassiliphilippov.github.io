1. ✝️ Christian movement ("Anabaptism in the Reformation")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈbæptɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌanəˈbaptɪzəm / (BrE)
A Protestant Christian movement of the 16th century that advocated baptism only for believing adults, the primacy of the Bible, and the separation of church and state.
Protestant Christian movement of the 16th century

Examples

Many early adherents to Anabaptism faced persecution for their beliefs about baptism.
Modern Mennonite communities trace their roots back to Anabaptism.
The spread of Anabaptism in central Europe led to significant religious and political changes.
