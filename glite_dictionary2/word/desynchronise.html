VERB
Pronunciation: / diːˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈsɪŋkrənaɪz / (BrE)
to cause to become unsynchronized; to make things happen at different or unrelated times
to make unsynchronized

Examples

Jet lag can desynchronise a person's internal body clock from local time.
Software errors may desynchronise the audio and video tracks in a recording.
The two clocks began to desynchronise after a power outage.
