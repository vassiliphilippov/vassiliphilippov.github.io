(1792–1878) The pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 1846 to 1878, known for proclaiming the dogma of the Immaculate Conception and for being the longest-reigning elected pope.
pope of the Roman Catholic Church, 1846–1878

Pius IX was the longest-reigning elected pope in the history of the Catholic Church.
During the reign of Pius IX, the doctrine of papal infallibility was defined at the First Vatican Council.
