1. ⚖️ force unit ("500 lbf of force")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌɛl bi ˈɛf / (AmE), / ˌɛl bi ˈɛf / (BrE)
an abbreviation for pound-force, a unit of force equal to the force exerted by gravity on a mass of one pound (standard gravity: 32 feet/s² or 9.80665 m/s²)
unit of force: pound-force

Examples

A bolt in this assembly is rated to withstand 500 lbf before failure.
Specifications often list maximum loads in lbf for safety reasons.
The engine must produce at least 1000 lbf of thrust to lift the vehicle.
