1. ✨ living light ("bioluminescence in fireflies")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊ.luː.mɪˈnɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊ.ljuː.mɪˈnɛs.əns / (BrE)
The production and emission of light by living organisms, such as fireflies and certain types of fish, through chemical processes within their bodies.
light emission by living organisms

Examples

Fireflies use bioluminescence to attract mates and warn predators.
Many deep-sea creatures rely on bioluminescence to find food and communicate in the darkness.
The glowing waves were caused by the bioluminescence of tiny marine organisms.
