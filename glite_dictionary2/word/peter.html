1. ⛪ Jesus's apostle ("Saint Peter")

Pronunciation: / ˈpiːtər / (AmE), / ˈpiːtə / (BrE)
One of the twelve Apostles of Jesus Christ, regarded as the leader of the Apostles and, in Catholic tradition, as the first Pope.
disciple of Jesus and leader of the Apostles

Examples

According to tradition, Peter was crucified upside down in Rome.
Many consider Peter to be the most prominent of the Apostles.
The pope is said to be the successor of Peter in the Catholic Church.
