The branch of Christianity consisting of churches that separated from the Roman Catholic Church during the Reformation, characterized by distinct theological doctrines and practices.
major branch of Christianity

Examples

Many countries in northern Europe saw Protestantism become the dominant form of Christianity after the Reformation.
Martin Luther is often credited as a key figure in the rise of Protestantism.
The history of Protestantism includes a wide range of beliefs and practices distinct from Catholicism.
