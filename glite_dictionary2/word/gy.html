1. ☢️ radiation unit ("1 Gy of radiation")

Pronunciation: / dʒiː waɪ / (AmE), / dʒiː waɪ / (BrE)
the SI unit of absorbed dose of ionizing radiation, equal to the absorption of one joule of radiation energy by one kilogram of matter
SI unit of absorbed radiation dose

Examples

A cumulative dose of more than 50 Gy can increase the risk of side effects.
In radiation therapy, doses are measured in Gy to ensure safety and effectiveness.
The patient's CT scan showed an exposure of 2 Gy to the affected tissue.
