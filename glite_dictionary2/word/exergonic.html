1. ⚡ releasing energy ("exergonic chemical reaction")

Pronunciation: / ˌɛksɚˈɡɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛksəˈɡɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a biochemical process) accompanied by the release of energy.
releasing energy (biochemistry)

Examples

Cellular respiration is an exergonic reaction because it releases energy that the cell can use.
Photosynthesis is not exergonic; instead, it requires an input of energy.
The spontaneous movement of ions across a membrane is often an exergonic process.

Related Terms

  • antonym – endergonic: (of a process) requiring an input of energy
