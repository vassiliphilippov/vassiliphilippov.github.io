1. ⚗️ acid and base ("amphoteric metal oxide")

ADJ
Pronunciation: / ˌæmfəˈtɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˌæmfəˈtɛrɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) having the properties of both an acid and a base and able to react as either.
able to act as acid or base

Examples

Some metal oxides exhibit amphoteric behavior depending on the substances they react with.
The amphoteric nature of amino acids allows them to buffer changes in pH in living systems.
Water is considered amphoteric because it can act as both an acid and a base in chemical reactions.
