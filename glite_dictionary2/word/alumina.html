1. ⚪ aluminum oxide ("alumina ceramic material")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / əˈluːmɪnə / (AmE), / əˈluːmɪnə / (BrE)
a white or nearly colorless crystalline substance, aluminum oxide (Al2O3), which occurs naturally as corundum and is used in the manufacture of ceramics, glass, and aluminum
aluminum oxide used in ceramics and industry

Examples

Industrial ceramics often contain alumina due to its exceptional hardness and thermal stability.
Many laboratory crucibles are made from alumina because of its resistance to heat and chemicals.
The extraction of aluminum from bauxite involves the removal of alumina.
