1. ❓ illogical statement ("a non sequitur joke")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / nɑn ˈsɛkwɪtər / (AmE), / nɒn ˈsɛkwɪtə / (BrE); / nɑn ˈsɛkwɪtɚ / (AmE), / nɒn ˈsɛkwɪtə / (BrE)
a statement or conclusion that does not logically follow from what was said before
illogical or irrelevant reply / illogical or unrelated conclusion

Examples

His argument ended with a non sequitur that confused everyone in the meeting.
The comedian's jokes relied on random non sequiturs that made the audience laugh.
To claim that increased screen time causes bad weather is a clear non sequitur.
His comment about the weather was a non sequitur in the middle of our serious discussion.
She replied with a non sequitur that left everyone confused during the meeting.
The comedian's jokes often rely on the use of absurd non sequiturs for humor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fallacy: an error in reasoning; a mistaken belief, especially based on an unsound argument
  • synonym – non sequacious: not logically following from what preceded
