1. ✝️ Christian leader ("Bishop Ulfilas translated Bible")

PROPN
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪʃəp ʊlˈfaɪləs / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəp ʊlˈfiːləs / (BrE)
(c. 311–383) A Christian bishop of Cappadocian descent who became bishop of the Visigoths, translated the Bible into Gothic, and is traditionally credited with inventing the Gothic alphabet.
early bishop who created the Gothic alphabet

Examples

Bishop Ulfilas was instrumental in spreading Christianity among the Gothic tribes.
Many early translations of the Bible into Gothic are credited to Bishop Ulfilas.
The Gothic alphabet is widely attributed to Bishop Ulfilas.
