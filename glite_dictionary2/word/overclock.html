overclock

1. ⚡ to speed up ("to overclock a computer")

VERB
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈklɑk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈklɒk / (BrE)
To operate a computer processor or other electronic component at a speed higher than its manufacturer's recommended maximum.
run hardware above recommended speed

Examples

Enthusiasts often overclock their CPUs to achieve better performance for gaming or intensive tasks.
He spent the afternoon overclocking his computer to see if he could get higher benchmark scores.
I accidentally overclocked my graphics card and it started overheating.
