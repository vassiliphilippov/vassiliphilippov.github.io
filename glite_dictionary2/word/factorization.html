1. ➗ breaking into factors ("factorization of numbers")

Pronunciation: / ˌfæk.tɚ.ɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfæk.tər.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(Mathematics) the process of breaking down an expression into a product of simpler factors that, when multiplied together, yield the original expression.
decomposition of an expression into factors

Examples

Prime factorization is often used in cryptography.
The factorization of 12 is 2 × 2 × 3.
Understanding factorization is crucial for solving quadratic equations.
