1. ✂️ censorship ("Bowdlerism in classic books")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈbaʊd.ləˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈbaʊd.lə.rɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the practice of removing or altering material considered indecent or offensive, especially by prudish censorship
practiced censorship by expurgation

Examples

Critics argue that Bowdlerism diminishes the power and intent of literary works.
Many classic novels were subjected to Bowdlerism during the Victorian era, altering or removing controversial passages.
The film’s televised version suffered significant Bowdlerism, cutting several scenes deemed inappropriate for children.
