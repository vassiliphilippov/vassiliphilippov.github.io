1. ⚗️ old magic science ("medieval alchemy experiments")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.kə.mi / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.mi / (BrE)
a medieval philosophical and proto-scientific tradition concerned with the transmutation of materials, especially the attempt to transform base metals into gold, and the search for the elixir of life; regarded as a forerunner of modern chemistry
medieval precursor to chemistry, transformation of materials

Examples

Medieval scholars devoted much of their lives to the study of alchemy in hopes of discovering the philosopher's stone.
Modern science sees alchemy as a blend of mysticism and early chemistry.
The legend of turning lead into gold has its roots in alchemy.
