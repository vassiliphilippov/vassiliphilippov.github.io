1. ⛪ Christian thinker ("Augustine of Hippo")

Pronunciation: / əˈɡʌstɪn / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡʌstɪn / (BrE)
(354–430) An early Christian theologian and philosopher, regarded as one of the most important Church Fathers, who became bishop of Hippo Regius and emphasized the need for divine grace.
early Christian theologian and bishop

Examples

Augustine wrote extensively on theology and philosophy, influencing the direction of Western Christianity.
Many scholars regard Augustine as one of the most significant thinkers in Christian history.
The teachings of Augustine on original sin have been debated for centuries.
