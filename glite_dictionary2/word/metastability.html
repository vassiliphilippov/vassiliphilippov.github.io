1. ⚖️ unstable stability ("metastability of a phase")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəstəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəstəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of a physical system to remain in a non-equilibrium but stable state until sufficiently disturbed, at which point it transitions to a more stable state.
condition of being stable until disturbed

Examples

One challenge in material science is understanding the metastability of certain phases under different conditions.
Quantum systems can display metastability when they remain in a local energy minimum until perturbed.
The supercooled liquid demonstrated remarkable metastability for several minutes before rapidly crystallizing.

Related Terms

  • antonym – instability: the quality of being likely to change or fall apart
  • synonym – stability: the state of being stable and not likely to change
