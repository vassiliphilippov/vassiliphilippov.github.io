The property of certain materials, such as bismuth or copper, that causes them to become weakly magnetized in a direction opposite to an applied magnetic field and thus slightly repelled by a magnet.
property of being weakly repelled by magnets

Examples

Copper exhibits diamagnetism, meaning it is weakly repelled by a magnetic field.
The phenomenon of diamagnetism was first observed in bismuth by Michael Faraday.
Unlike iron, materials that show diamagnetism do not retain magnetic properties after the external field is removed.
