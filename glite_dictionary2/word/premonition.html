1. ⚠️ feeling of warning ("premonition of danger")

Pronunciation: / ˌpriːməˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːməˈnɪʃən / (BrE); / ˌprɛməˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːməˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a strong feeling or sense that something (usually bad) is going to happen
a feeling that something (bad) will happen / sense that something bad will happen

Examples

Despite her cheerful expression, a premonition of trouble lingered in her mind.
Moments before the accident, he felt a strange premonition that something was wrong.
She had a sudden premonition of danger, which made her pause at the door.
He tried to ignore the premonition that something was about to go wrong.
Just before entering the building, I felt a strange premonition of danger.
She had a premonition that the trip would not go well.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foreboding: a strong feeling that something bad will happen
  • synonym – omen: an event regarded as a sign of future good or evil
  • synonym – presentiment: an intuitive feeling about the future, especially a foreboding one
