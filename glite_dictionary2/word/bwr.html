1. ⚛️ nuclear reactor ("BWR nuclear reactor")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / biː ˈdʌbljuː ˈɑr / (AmE), / biː ˈdʌbljuː ˈɑː / (BrE)
a type of nuclear reactor that uses water as both coolant and moderator, where the water boils inside the reactor core to produce steam that drives a turbine
boiling water nuclear reactor

Examples

Engineers conducted a thorough inspection of the BWR after detecting abnormal vibrations.
The new power plant features a modern BWR designed for maximum safety and efficiency.
Unlike a PWR, a BWR allows water to boil directly in the reactor core.
