1. ⚖️ court lawyer ("barrister in court")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈbærɪstər / (AmE), / ˈbærɪstə / (BrE)
a lawyer in Britain or Canada who is qualified to represent clients in the higher courts of law
lawyer qualified to act in higher courts

Examples

She qualified as a barrister after several years of study and training.
The barrister presented a strong argument before the High Court.
Unlike a solicitor, a barrister can speak on your behalf in the Court of Appeal.

Related Terms

  • antonym – solicitor: a type of lawyer in Britain who advises clients and prepares cases but does not act as advocate in higher courts
