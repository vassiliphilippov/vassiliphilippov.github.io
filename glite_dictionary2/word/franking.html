1. ✉️ postage marking ("franking mark on mail")

Pronunciation: / ˈfræŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfræŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
a device or marking, such as a postage stamp, printed or stamped impressions, coding, labels, or authorized manuscript writing, affixed to mail to show that it qualifies for postal service or has had postage paid
authorized marking on mail to qualify for postal service

Examples

Early collectors often sought envelopes with rare franking for their stamp collections.
Some countries used colorful franking labels during commemorative events.
The letter had a distinctive franking that showed it had been sent from the embassy.
