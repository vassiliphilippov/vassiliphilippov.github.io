1. ⚖️ case dismissed ("non prosequitur entered by judge")

Pronunciation: / nɑn ˌproʊˈsɛkwɪtər / (AmE), / nɒn ˌprəʊˈsɛkwɪtʊə / (BrE)
(Law) A judgment entered in favor of the defendant when the plaintiff fails to continue the action, such as by not appearing in court.
legal judgment for defendant when plaintiff fails to proceed

Examples

After several delays, the judge entered a non prosequitur, ending the lawsuit.
Due to the plaintiff’s lack of action, the case concluded with a non prosequitur.
The court issued a non prosequitur when the plaintiff failed to appear for the hearing.
