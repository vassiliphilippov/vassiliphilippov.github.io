1. ✝️ one divine nature ("Monophysitism in church history")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑːfɪˌsaɪtɪzəm / (AmE), / məˈnɒfɪˌsaɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine, regarded as heretical by mainstream Christianity, which asserts that Jesus Christ has only one, divine nature rather than two (divine and human).
belief in Christ's single, divine nature

Examples

Many Eastern Christian communities were influenced by Monophysitism for centuries.
Scholars debate the impact of Monophysitism on early Christian theological divisions.
The Council of Chalcedon condemned Monophysitism as a heresy in 451.

Related Terms
