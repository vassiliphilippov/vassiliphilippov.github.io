1. ✨ to make attractive ("to glamorize a job")

VERB
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlæməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡlaməraɪz / (BrE); / ˈɡlæm.əˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡlæm.ə.raɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone seem more attractive, exciting, or appealing than it really is, often by exaggerating or romanticizing
make appear more attractive or exciting / make glamorous or attractive

Examples

She tried to glamorize the job to make it more appealing to applicants.
The movie glamorizes the lives of criminals, presenting them as exciting and stylish.
The photoshoot glamorized a simple kitchen, turning it into a dreamy space.
He didn't want to glamorize war, preferring to show its tragic consequences.
Many movies tend to glamorize the lives of criminals, ignoring the harsh realities.
The article glamorized the fast-paced lifestyle but failed to mention its drawbacks.
