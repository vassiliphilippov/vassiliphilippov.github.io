1. ⛪ religious table ("church altar")

Pronunciation: / ˈɔltɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːltə / (BrE)
a raised structure or table used in religious ceremonies, such as for making offerings, sacrifices, or in Christian communion services
raised structure for religious offerings / table in a Christian church for communion

Examples

Offerings of fruit and flowers were placed on the altar during the festival.
The ancient temple featured a stone altar where sacrifices were made.
The bride and groom stood before the altar to exchange their vows.
Fresh flowers are arranged on the altar every Sunday.
Many wedding ceremonies take place in front of the church altar.
The priest placed the chalice on the altar before the service began.
