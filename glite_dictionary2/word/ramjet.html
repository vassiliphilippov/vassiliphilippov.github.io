1. ✈️ jet engine type ("ramjet engine design")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈræmˌdʒɛt / (AmE), / ˈramˌdʒɛt / (BrE)
A type of jet engine that uses the engine's forward motion to compress incoming air, with no moving parts, and must be launched at high speed to operate.
jet engine using forward motion for air compression

Examples

Engineers are exploring new materials to improve the efficiency of ramjets for future aircraft.
The missile is powered by a ramjet that allows it to reach supersonic speeds.
Unlike a turbojet, a ramjet has no moving parts and relies on its speed for air compression.
