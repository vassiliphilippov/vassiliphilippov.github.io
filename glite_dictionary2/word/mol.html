1. ⚗️ amount unit ("one mol of water")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / moʊl / (AmE), / məʊl / (BrE)
The SI unit for the amount of substance, equal to the quantity containing as many elementary entities as there are atoms in 12 grams of carbon-12.
unit of amount of substance (SI)

Examples

Amounts of reactants are often measured in mol rather than grams for better accuracy.
In chemistry class, we learned that one mol of water contains approximately 6.022 x 10²³ molecules.
The reaction requires exactly two mol of hydrogen to proceed efficiently.
