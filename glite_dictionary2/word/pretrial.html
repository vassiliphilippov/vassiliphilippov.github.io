1. ⚖️ court meeting ("pretrial conference")

Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtraɪəl / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌtraɪəl / (BrE)
(Law) A meeting or conference held before a trial to clarify legal and factual issues, promote settlement, and outline discovery and trial procedures.
legal meeting before a trial

Examples

During the pretrial, the lawyers exchanged evidence and clarified the disputed issues.
The judge scheduled a pretrial to discuss settlement options with both parties.
The outcome of the pretrial often determines whether a case proceeds to a full trial.
