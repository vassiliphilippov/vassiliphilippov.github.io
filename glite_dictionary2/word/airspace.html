1. ✈️ sky above land ("airspace over a country")

Pronunciation: / ˈɛrˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈeəˌspeɪs / (BrE)
the part of the sky or atmosphere above a country or area that is controlled by that country
atmosphere above a nation's territory / space in the atmosphere above land or sea / the atmosphere above a region; especially for aviation

Examples

All commercial flights must obtain permission before entering a country's airspace.
Birds sometimes fly at altitudes higher than the airspace used by planes.
The government announced restrictions over the city’s airspace during the parade.
Military jets were scrambled when an unknown aircraft entered national airspace.
The pilot requested clearance before entering airspace controlled by another country.
There are strict regulations for foreign airlines flying over a country's airspace.
A country's airspace is protected by international law and cannot be entered without permission.
Commercial planes must receive clearance before entering another nation's airspace.
Satellite images can be used to monitor activity within restricted airspace.
