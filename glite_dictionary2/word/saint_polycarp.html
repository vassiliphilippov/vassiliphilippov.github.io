1. ✝️ Christian martyr ("Saint Polycarp of Smyrna")

Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈpɑliˌkɑrp / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈpɒlɪkɑːp / (BrE)
(circa 69–155 AD) A Greek bishop of Smyrna who was martyred for refusing to renounce his Christian faith.
Greek bishop and Christian martyr

Examples

According to tradition, Saint Polycarp was a disciple of the apostle John.
Many early Christian writings mention Saint Polycarp as a faithful leader and martyr.
The feast day of Saint Polycarp is celebrated on February 23 in many Christian traditions.
