1. ✍️ grammar shift ("enallage in poetry")

Pronunciation: / ɛnˈælədʒi / (AmE), / ɪˈnalədʒi / (BrE)
A rhetorical device involving the intentional substitution of one grammatical form for another, such as changing tense, part of speech, or number.
rhetorical substitution of grammatical forms

Examples

Rhetorical figures such as enallage can create vivid effects in literature.
The poet used enallage by switching from present to past tense mid-sentence.
