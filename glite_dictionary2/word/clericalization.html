Pronunciation: / klɛrɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / klɛrɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which the power or influence of a religious hierarchy is increased, often resulting in a greater distinction between clergy and laity
process of increasing religious hierarchy's power

Examples

During the reforms, some criticized the clericalization of religious leadership as contrary to community values.
Many historians suggest that clericalization contributed to increased division between the clergy and the laity.
The clericalization of the institution led to greater authority for the priests over daily affairs.
