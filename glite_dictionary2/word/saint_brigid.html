1. ⛪ Irish patron saint ("Saint Brigid of Ireland")

Pronunciation: / ˌseɪnt ˈbrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˌseɪnt ˈbrɪdɪd / (BrE)
An Irish abbess and one of the patron saints of Ireland, known for her piety and founding of several convents in the 5th and 6th centuries.
Irish patron saint and abbess

Examples

Legends say that Saint Brigid performed numerous miracles during her lifetime.
Many pilgrims visit the shrine of Saint Brigid in County Kildare each year.
The feast day of Saint Brigid is celebrated on February 1st in Ireland.
