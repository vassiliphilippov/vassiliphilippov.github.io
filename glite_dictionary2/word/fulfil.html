1. ✅ to complete ("to fulfil a promise")

Pronunciation: / fʊlˈfɪl / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪl / (BrE)
to do what is required, promised, or expected; to achieve or satisfy something
carry out or put into effect / meet or satisfy a requirement or need / to meet requirements or expectations

Examples

It is important to fulfil your promises if you want to build trust.
She fulfilled her duties as team leader with great dedication.
The government failed to fulfil its election pledges.
She was proud to fulfil her promise to her parents.
The candidate did not fulfil all the requirements for the scholarship.
The organization aims to fulfil the needs of the local community.
He hopes this new job will fulfil his professional ambitions.
Her performance did not fulfil the judges' expectations.
The company failed to fulfil its promise to improve working conditions.
