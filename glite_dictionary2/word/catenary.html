1. ➰ hanging curve ("catenary curve shape")

Pronunciation: / ˈkætəˌneri / (AmE), / kəˈtiːnəri / (BrE)
the curve formed by a perfectly flexible, uniform chain or cable hanging freely from two fixed points under its own weight
curve formed by a hanging chain or cable

Examples

A chain hanging between two poles naturally assumes the shape of a catenary.
He studied the properties of the catenary in his advanced mathematics class.
The cables of a suspension bridge follow a catenary curve, not a parabola.
