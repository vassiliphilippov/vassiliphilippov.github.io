1. ✝️ Christian doctrine ("Utraquism in communion")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈjuː.trəˌkwɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈjuː.trə.kwɪz.əm / (BrE)
The Christian doctrine and practice, held by the Hussite group called the Calixtins, advocating that communion should be administered in both bread and wine to all believers.
doctrine of offering communion in both bread and wine

Examples

Utraquism became a central issue in the negotiations between the Hussites and the Catholic Church.
Many Bohemian reformers identified with Utraquism during the early 15th century.
The historical significance of Utraquism lies in its challenge to traditional church practices.
