1. ⚖️ Roman civil law ("jus civile of Rome")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / jus sɪˈvaɪli / (AmE), / jus sɪˈviːliː / (BrE)
the body of civil law developed in ancient Rome, especially the code codified under Justinian, which became the foundation for many modern systems of civil law.
ancient Roman civil law code

Examples

Many elements of modern European law are derived from the jus civile of ancient Rome.
Scholars of legal history often compare the jus civile to contemporary concepts of civil law.
The reforms under Emperor Justinian codified the jus civile, greatly influencing legal systems worldwide.
