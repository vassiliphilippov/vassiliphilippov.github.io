1. ⚡ energy unit ("1000 eV energy")

Pronunciation: / i vi / (AmE), / iː viː / (BrE)
a unit of energy equal to the work done by an electron when it moves through an electric potential difference of one volt
unit of energy (electronvolt)

Examples

An x-ray photon can have energy in the range of several thousand eV.
Physicists often measure the energy of particles in eV rather than joules.
The binding energy of the hydrogen atom is about 13.6 eV.
