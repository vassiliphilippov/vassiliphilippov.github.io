1. ✍️ witness signer ("attestator signs document")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / əˈtɛs.teɪ.tər / (AmE), / əˈtɛs.teɪ.tə / (BrE)
(law) a person who verifies the authenticity of a document or signature by signing it themselves as a witness.
person witnessing and signing a document

Examples

Every contract must have an attestator present to witness the signatures.
The attestator signed at the bottom of the will to confirm its authenticity.
Without an attestator, the notarization process is not complete.
