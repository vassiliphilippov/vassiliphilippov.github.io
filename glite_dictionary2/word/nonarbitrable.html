1. ⚖️ not open to arbitration ("nonarbitrable legal dispute")

Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈɑːrbɪtrəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈɑːbɪtrəbl̩ / (BrE)
not suitable for or subject to arbitration; unable to be resolved through arbitration.
not subject to arbitration

Examples

Certain subject matters, like family law cases, may be nonarbitrable under national law.
The company argued that the claim was nonarbitrable and should instead be heard in a court of law.
The dispute was deemed nonarbitrable by the court because it involved criminal allegations.
