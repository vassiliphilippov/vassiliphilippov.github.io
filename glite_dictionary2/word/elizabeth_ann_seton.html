(1774–1821) The first person born in the United States to be canonized as a saint, and founder of the first Catholic girls' school in the United States.
first American-born canonized saint; religious leader

Examples

Elizabeth Ann Seton was canonized by the Catholic Church in 1975 for her contributions to education and the faith.
Many American schools and churches are named after Elizabeth Ann Seton in recognition of her legacy.
The Sisters of Charity, founded by Elizabeth Ann Seton, have played a major role in American Catholic history.
