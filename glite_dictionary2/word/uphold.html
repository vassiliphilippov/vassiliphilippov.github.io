1. ⚖️ to support ("to uphold the law")

VERB
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈhoʊld / (AmE), / ʌpˈhəʊld / (BrE)
to support, maintain, or defend a law, principle, decision, or idea, especially to keep it in effect or approve it
maintain or keep unchanged / support or defend a principle or idea / support or maintain, especially legally

Examples

A judge must uphold the law without personal bias or favoritism.

The judge promised to uphold the Constitution at all times.
We have a duty to uphold the values of honesty and integrity.
