NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪzəm, ˈsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪz.əm, ˈskɪz.əm / (BrE); / ˈskɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪzəm / (BrE)
a serious split or division within a group or organization, especially because of differing beliefs or opinions (often used about churches, but also about political or other groups)
division into opposing groups / formal split in a church

Throughout history, religious schisms have shaped entire civilizations.
