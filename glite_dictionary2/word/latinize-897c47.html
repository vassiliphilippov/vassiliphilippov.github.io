1. ⛪ to make Catholic ("to latinize a church")

Pronunciation: / ˈlæt.ən.aɪz / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to cause (a person, group, or institution) to adopt Roman Catholic practices, beliefs, or rites, especially those associated with the Latin Church
cause to adopt Catholicism or Latin Church rites

Examples

In the 19th century, there were efforts to latinize several Eastern Christian churches.
Some critics argued that the new liturgy would latinize traditional practices.
The missionaries sought to latinize the local community by introducing Catholic customs and ceremonies.
