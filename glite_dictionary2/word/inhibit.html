1. ⛔ to stop ("to inhibit growth")

Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (BrE)
to prevent, restrain, or make something less likely to happen or be expressed
consciously restrain or suppress / limit or decrease action or effect / make someone unable to act naturally / prevent or restrain

Examples

Parental supervision is needed so as not to inhibit children's creativity.
Some medications may inhibit the body's natural response to stress.
Strict laws can inhibit the growth of small businesses.
Being watched by the teacher can inhibit students from speaking freely in class.
His strict upbringing tended to inhibit his ability to express emotions.
Worrying about mistakes may inhibit your performance during the interview.
Certain medications can inhibit the absorption of essential nutrients.
Strong emotions may inhibit your ability to think clearly during difficult situations.
The scientist explained how cold temperatures inhibit the growth of bacteria.
His shyness tended to inhibit him from speaking up in class.
