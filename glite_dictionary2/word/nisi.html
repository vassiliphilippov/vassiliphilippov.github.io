1. ⚖️ not final ("nisi decree issued")

Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪsaɪ / (AmE), / ˈnaɪsaɪ / (BrE)
(of a decree or order) not final or absolute; effective unless something specified occurs to prevent it, often used in legal context.
not final or absolute (legal)

Examples

A judgment nisi may be made absolute if the conditions are not fulfilled.
The court issued a nisi decree, which would become final after six months if no appeal was made.
The divorce was not effective immediately, as the order was only nisi.
