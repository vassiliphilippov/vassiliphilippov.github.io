1. ⏪ affecting the past ("retroactive law change")

ADJ
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtroʊˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈæktɪv / (BrE)
(of a law, decision, or action) taking effect from a date in the past, so it affects things that happened before it was made
affecting the past; applying to earlier events / applying to a past period

Examples

Her promotion was made retroactive to January, so she received additional pay.
The court ruled that the new regulation would not be retroactive.
The government passed a retroactive tax law affecting income earned last year.
Employees will receive retroactive pay for the period before the wage increase was officially approved.
The court ruled that the tax increase could not be retroactive.
The new law is retroactive and will affect contracts signed years ago.

Related Terms

  • antonym – prospective: likely to happen or expected to happen in the future
  • synonym – retrospective: looking back on or dealing with past events or situations
