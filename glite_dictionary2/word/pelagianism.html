1. ✝️ Christian doctrine ("early Pelagianism debate")

Pronunciation: / pəˈleɪdʒiəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / pɪˈleɪdʒɪənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The theological doctrine advanced by Pelagius, denying original sin and affirming that humans can achieve righteousness by their own efforts; declared heretical by the Council of Ephesus in 431.
doctrine denying original sin (Christian theology)

Examples

His beliefs reflected elements of Pelagianism, emphasizing free will over predestination.
Many theologians debated the implications of Pelagianism for Christian doctrine.
The Council condemned Pelagianism as heresy in the fifth century.
