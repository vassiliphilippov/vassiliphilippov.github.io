monosyllabic

1. 1️⃣ one-syllable ("monosyllabic answer")

Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊsɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəsɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or utterance) consisting of only one syllable; (of a person or style of speaking) using very few words, especially so as to seem unfriendly or reluctant to engage.
having one syllable; using few words

Examples

Many Old English words are monosyllabic, reflecting the simplicity of the language's early roots.
The word "cat" is a monosyllabic noun that is easy for children to pronounce.
When asked about his plans, he gave only monosyllabic answers.
