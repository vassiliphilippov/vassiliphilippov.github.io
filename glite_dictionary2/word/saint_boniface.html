Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈbɑnɪfəs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈbɒnɪfəs / (BrE)
(c. 680–754) An Anglo-Saxon missionary and archbishop known for spreading Christianity in Germany and Frisia, and regarded as a martyr by the Roman Catholic Church.
Anglo-Saxon missionary and Christian saint

Examples

Saint Boniface played a crucial role in the Christianization of Germany during the 8th century.
Many churches across Europe are dedicated to Saint Boniface in honor of his missionary work.
The feast day of Saint Boniface is celebrated on June 5th by the Roman Catholic Church.
