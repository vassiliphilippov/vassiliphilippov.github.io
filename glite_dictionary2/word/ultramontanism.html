1. ⛪ pope's authority ("Catholic ultramontanism debate")

Pronunciation: / ˌʌltrəˈmɑːntəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈmɒntənɪzəm / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the policy or belief that supreme authority in church matters should belong to the pope.
the doctrine of papal supremacy

Examples

During the 19th century, ultramontanism influenced debates about church authority in many Catholic countries.
Opponents of ultramontanism argued for a more decentralized structure within the Church.
The council's decisions were shaped in part by proponents of ultramontanism.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conciliarism: belief that church councils have greater authority than the pope
  • synonym – papalism: support of papal authority or supremacy
