1. ♾️ unchanging over time ("ergodic process behavior")

ADJ
Pronunciation: / ɜːrˈɡɑːdɪk / (AmE), / ɜːˈɡɒdɪk / (BrE)
(of a process or system, especially in mathematics and physics) tending in probability to a limiting form that is independent of the initial conditions; having the property that, over time, its statistical behavior can be deduced from a single, sufficiently long sample of its evolution.
having time-averaging property (math/physics)

Examples

In an ergodic process, the initial conditions become irrelevant as time progresses.
Researchers assume the climate model behaves in an ergodic manner for their simulations to be valid.
The system is said to be ergodic if its statistical properties are the same when measured over time as they are across the ensemble.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aperiodic: not recurring at regular intervals; in statistics, not periodic
  • synonym – stationary: having statistical properties that do not change over time (math/statistics)
