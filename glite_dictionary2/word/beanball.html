NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːnˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈbiːnˌbɔːl / (BrE)
(in baseball) a pitch deliberately thrown at the batter's head, often as an act of intimidation or retaliation.
a baseball thrown at a batter's head

Examples

After a heated argument, a beanball nearly hit the batter, causing warnings from the umpire.
Intentional beanballs are strongly discouraged because of the dangers they pose to players.
The pitcher was ejected for throwing a beanball at the opposing team's star hitter.
