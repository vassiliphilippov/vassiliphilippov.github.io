1. ✅ to approve automatically ("to rubberstamp a decision")

Pronunciation: / ˈrʌbərˌstæmp / (AmE), / ˈrʌbəˌstæmp / (BrE)
to officially approve or mark something (often without real consideration), especially by using a rubber stamp
automatically approve (without scrutiny) / mark officially with a rubber stamp

Examples

Every proposal was rubberstamped without any real discussion.
She spent the morning rubberstamping attendance forms for her supervisor.
The manager must rubberstamp each invoice before payment is processed.
Congress cannot simply rubberstamp every proposal brought before it.
Critics accused the committee of rubberstamping decisions made by higher authorities.
The board tends to rubberstamp the CEO's recommendations without discussion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – approve: to officially accept or agree to something
  • synonym – endorse: to give support or approval to something officially
  • antonym – veto: to reject a decision or proposal officially
