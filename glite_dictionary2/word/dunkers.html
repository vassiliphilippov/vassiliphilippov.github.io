1. ⛪ Baptist sect ("Dunkers religious group")

Pronunciation: / ˈdʌŋkərz / (AmE), / ˈdʌŋkəz / (BrE)
A member of a Baptist religious denomination founded in 1708 by Americans of German descent, known for its opposition to military service and oaths, and for practicing trine immersion in baptism.
member of a German-American Baptist sect

Examples

Historically, the Dunkers practiced baptism by trine immersion as part of their religious rituals.
Several members of the congregation identified themselves as Dunkers during the town meeting.
The Dunkers were known for their commitment to pacifism and communal living.
