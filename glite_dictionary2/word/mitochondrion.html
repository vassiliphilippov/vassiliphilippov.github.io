1. ⚡ cell energy maker ("mitochondrion in a cell")

Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪtəˈkɑndriən / (AmE), / ˌmɪtəˈkɒndrɪən / (BrE)
A small organelle found in the cytoplasm of most cells, containing enzymes responsible for producing energy through cellular respiration.
energy-producing cell organelle

Examples

Each muscle cell contains more than one mitochondrion to meet its high energy demands.
Scientists discovered that some diseases are linked to mutations in the mitochondrion.
The mitochondrion is often called the powerhouse of the cell because it generates most of the cell's energy.
