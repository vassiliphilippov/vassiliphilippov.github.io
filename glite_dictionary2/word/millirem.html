1. ☢️ radiation unit ("a millirem of radiation")

Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪˌrɛm / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪˌrɛm / (BrE)
A unit of measurement for ionizing radiation equal to one thousandth of a rem.
unit of ionizing radiation (1/1000 of a rem)

Examples

A chest X-ray typically delivers a dose of about 10 millirems.
The annual background radiation in many areas is about 300 millirems.
Workers are monitored to ensure they do not exceed 5,000 millirems per year.
