1. ⏳ to decide before ("to predetermine the outcome")

Pronunciation: / ˌpriː.dɪˈtɝː.mən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˈtɜː.mɪn / (BrE); / ˌpriː.dɪˈtɝː.mɪn / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˈtɜː.mɪn / (BrE)
to decide or influence something in advance, often before all information is known
to decide or settle in advance / to influence or bias in advance

Examples

Cultural stereotypes can predetermine how people are perceived in society.
His early experiences seemed to predetermine his attitude toward authority.
The evidence should not predetermine the jury's verdict.
Some people believe that their fate is predetermined and cannot be changed.
The outcome of the experiment was predetermined by the initial conditions.
The schedule was predetermined months before the event took place.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bias: to influence, often unfairly, to produce a particular result
  • synonym – determine: to decide or establish something
  • antonym – improvise: to decide or act without prior planning
  • synonym – preordain: to decide or establish something in advance, especially by fate or divine will
