(c. 527–459 BC) An Athenian statesman who advocated for the expansion of the Athenian navy and led Athens to victory against the Persians.
Athenian statesman and naval leader (527–459 BC)

Examples

Many historians credit Themistocles with devising the naval strategy that saved Athens during the Persian Wars.
Modern politicians sometimes cite Themistocles as an example of strategic thinking and foresight.
The leadership of Themistocles at the Battle of Salamis was pivotal for the Greek alliance.
