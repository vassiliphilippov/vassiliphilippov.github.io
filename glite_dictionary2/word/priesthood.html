1. ⛪ group of priests ("the priesthood of the church")

NOUN
Pronunciation: / ˈpristˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈpriːsthʊd / (BrE)
all the priests or ordained religious practitioners as a group, especially in a particular religion or church.
the body of ordained priests

Examples

Many reforms were resisted by the established priesthood of the church.
The priesthood played a crucial role in medieval society, often acting as advisors to kings.
Women sought more prominent positions within the priesthood of several denominations.
