1. ✂️ sound removed ("aphaeresis in word change")

Pronunciation: / əˈfɪrəsɪs / (AmE), / əˈfɪərəsɪs / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The omission of one or more sounds or letters at the beginning of a word, as in 'coon' for 'raccoon' or 'till' for 'until'.
omission at beginning of a word

Examples

In Old English, aphaeresis often occurred as language evolved.
Linguists study aphaeresis to understand historical changes in word forms.
The contraction of 'until' to till is an example of aphaeresis.
