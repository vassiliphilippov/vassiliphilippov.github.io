1. ☁️ atmosphere layer ("in the stratosphere layer")

Pronunciation: / ˈstræt̬.əˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ə.sfiə / (BrE)
The layer of the Earth's atmosphere above the troposphere and below the mesosphere, characterized by a gradual increase in temperature with height and containing the ozone layer.
upper atmosphere layer above the troposphere

Examples

Jets typically fly in the lower stratosphere to avoid weather disturbances found in the troposphere.
Rising temperatures in the stratosphere can indicate changes in atmospheric conditions.
The ozone layer is found within the stratosphere, protecting life on Earth from harmful ultraviolet radiation.

Related Terms
