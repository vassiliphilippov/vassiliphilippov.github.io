1. ⛴️ French port town ("Calais ferry port")

PROPN
Pronunciation: / ˈkæleɪ / (AmE), / ˈkæleɪ / (BrE)
A port town in northern France on the Strait of Dover, historically notable as a ferry point to England and for its capture by the English in 1347 and recapture by the French in 1558.
port town in northern France

Examples

During his trip to France, he visited Calais to learn about its history.
The English controlled Calais for over two centuries before it was returned to France.
The ferry from Dover arrives in Calais several times a day.
