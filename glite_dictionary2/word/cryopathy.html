1. ❄️ cold tissue damage ("cryopathy after frostbite")

Pronunciation: / kraɪˈɑpəθi / (AmE), / kraɪˈɒpəθi / (BrE)
A pathological condition resulting from the destruction of tissue due to freezing, often characterized by tingling, blistering, and possible gangrene.
tissue damage from freezing

Examples

Doctors diagnosed the mountaineer with cryopathy after examining his discolored toes.
Early symptoms of cryopathy include tingling and numbness in the affected area.
Frostbite is a common form of cryopathy affecting people exposed to extreme cold.
