1. ✅ obviously true ("axiomatic truth")

Pronunciation: / ˌæksiəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌaksiəˈmatɪk / (BrE); / ˌæk.si.oʊˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæk.si.əˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE); / ˌæk.si.əˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæk.si.əˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
obviously true and not needing to be proved; accepted as a basic principle or fact
containing general truths or maxims / relating to or based on axioms / self-evident; not needing proof

Examples

He delivered axiomatic advice that everyone should treat others as they wish to be treated.
Her book is filled with axiomatic principles about success and happiness.
Many proverbs contain axiomatic truths that have stood the test of time.
Her argument rested on the axiomatic assumption that all people seek happiness.
It is axiomatic that democracy requires free speech to function well.
The notion that actions have consequences is almost axiomatic in philosophy.
In mathematics, the principles of geometry are considered axiomatic and provide the foundation for further reasoning.
It became axiomatic in the company that customer satisfaction was the top priority.
The scientist argued that the laws of physics should not be viewed as axiomatic but open to empirical testing.
