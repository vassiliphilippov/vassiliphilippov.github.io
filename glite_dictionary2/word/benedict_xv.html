(1854–1922) the pope from 1914 to 1922, known for his efforts to promote peace during World War I and for founding the Vatican service for prisoners of war.
pope from 1914 to 1922; promoted peace during WWI

Examples

During the chaos of World War I, Benedict XV appealed for an immediate ceasefire between the warring nations.
Many historians credit Benedict XV with establishing humanitarian efforts to aid prisoners of war.
The Vatican's neutrality during the conflict was maintained under the leadership of Benedict XV.
