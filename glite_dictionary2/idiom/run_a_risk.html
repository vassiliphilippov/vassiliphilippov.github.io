1. ⚠️ to take a chance ("to run a risk of injury")

Pronunciation: / rʌn ə rɪsk / (AmE), / rʌn ə rɪsk / (BrE)
to do something that might lead to danger, harm, or loss; to take a chance that something bad could happen
expose oneself to danger or loss / take a chance with possible bad outcome

Examples

Anyone who ignores the safety instructions is running a risk.
He knew he would run a risk by speaking out against the policy.
If you invest in that company, you run a risk of losing your money.
He doesn't like to run a risk when making important decisions.
If you don't wear a helmet while biking, you run a risk of injury.
They knew they would run a risk by investing in a new business.

Related Terms

  • antonym – play it safe: to avoid taking risks
  • synonym – take a chance: do something involving risk or uncertainty
  • synonym – take a risk: to do something despite possible danger or loss
