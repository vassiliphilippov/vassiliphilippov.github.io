1. ➕ not to mention ("to say nothing of extra problems")

Pronunciation: / tə seɪ ˈnʌθɪŋ əv / (AmE), / tə seɪ ˈnʌθɪŋ əv / (BrE)
used to introduce an additional thing that makes a situation even more difficult, surprising, or impressive, on top of what has already been mentioned; not to mention.
used to add another point; not to mention

Examples

He was late for the meeting, to say nothing of missing the important presentation.
She finished all her work before noon, to say nothing of helping her colleagues with theirs.
The hike was exhausting, to say nothing of the sudden downpour halfway up the mountain.
